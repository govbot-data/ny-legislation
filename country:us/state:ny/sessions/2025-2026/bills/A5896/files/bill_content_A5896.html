BILL NOA05896
 
SAME ASSAME AS S06879
 
SPONSORZinerman
 
COSPNSRShimsky
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Add Art 59 §§59-0101 - 59-0107, amd §§58-0303, 58-0503, 58-0703 & 58-0903, En Con L; amd §25-c, Lab L
 
Establishes the New York youth climate corps to support and grow climate-related service opportunities for young adults with the objective of building low-carbon and climate-resilient communities, ecosystems, and economies while providing education, workforce development, and career pathways to service members, with a focus on disadvantaged communities.

        AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law and the labor law, in
          relation to establishing the New York youth climate corps
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Legislative findings. 1. The  legislature  recognizes  that
     2  climate  change  and  environmental  degradation are two of the greatest
     3  challenges facing the state and the world today, and that we must  mobi-
     4  lize New York's workforce and youth to create a clean energy economy and
     5  strengthen  our  communities  and  ecosystems  in  the  face  of climate
     6  impacts.
     7    2. The legislature finds that service provides a unique opportunity to
     8  mobilize young adults to build clean energy and climate-resilient commu-
     9  nities, economies, and ecosystems. Growing equity-centered,  climate-re-
    10  lated  service programs and addressing critical gaps in service opportu-
    11  nities will broaden access to service, ensuring that young adults of all
    12  backgrounds, especially from  overburdened  communities  and  vulnerable
    13  populations,  can serve. Doing so will also ensure that service programs
    14  address the needs of communities  across  the  state,  especially  those
    15  communities  disproportionately  impacted  by  environmental  and health
    16  burdens.
    17    3. The legislature further finds that as our  state  transitions  away
    18  from  a  fossil  fuel-based economy, we must do so in a way that fosters
    19  innovation, investment, and growth in clean  energy  technology  sectors
    20  and  jobs  so  our businesses, workforce, and communities can thrive. As
    21  state, federal, local, and  tribal  governments  implement  policies  to
    22  mitigate  the destructive forces of climate change, there will be conse-
    23  quences for New York's businesses, workers, and communities. Accomplish-
    24  ing an equitable transition will require identification of future indus-
    25  try occupations and skill needs, the existing  workforce's  transferable

    23  § 59-0101. Definitions.
    24    As  used in this article, the following terms shall have the following
    25  meanings:
    26    1. "New York youth climate corps" shall mean  the  office  established
    27  under section 59-0103 of this article.
    28    2.  "Approved  host  sites"  shall  mean  not-for-profit corporations,
    29  public agencies,  local  governments,  educational  institutions,  labor
    30  organizations, Indian tribes, and any other entities approved by the New
    31  York youth climate corps and in compliance with the corps' minimum work-
    32  place requirements.
    33    3.  "Minimum workplace requirements" shall mean the minimum diversity,
    34  workplace safety, and wage requirements set for host sites  by  the  New
    35  York youth climate corps.
    36  § 59-0103. Establishment.
    37    The  New  York  youth climate corps is established to support and grow
    38  climate-related service opportunities for young adults with  the  objec-
    39  tive  of  building low-carbon and climate-resilient communities, ecosys-
    40  tems, and economies while providing  education,  workforce  development,
    41  and  career  pathways  to service members, with a focus on disadvantaged
    42  communities as defined in section 75-0111 of this chapter. The New  York
    43  youth climate corps shall be an office within the New York commission on
    44  national  and community service, a governmental entity created by execu-
    45  tive order one hundred seventy-nine, signed December thirtieth, nineteen
    46  hundred ninety-three.
    47  § 59-0105. Duties.
    48    The New York youth climate corps shall have the following duties:
    49    1. Seek funding from article fifty-eight of this  chapter  to  further
    50  the purposes of this article.
    51    2. Connect, amplify, and grow climate-related service opportunities to
    52  mobilize  and train young adults to build clean energy and climate-resi-
    53  lient communities, economies, and ecosystems, with a focus on  disadvan-
    54  taged communities as defined in section 75-0111 of this chapter.

    41  advisory board whose members shall serve without pay, meet not less than
    42  twice each year and whose sole purpose shall be  to  advise  as  to  the
