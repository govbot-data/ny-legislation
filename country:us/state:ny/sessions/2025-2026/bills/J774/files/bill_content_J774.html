J00774 Text:

 
Senate Resolution No. 774
 
BY: Senator HELMING
 
        CONGRATULATING  the  Hobart  Men's Ice Hockey Team
        upon  the  occasion   of   capturing   their   third
        consecutive  NCAA  Division  III  Men's  Ice  Hockey
        Championship on March 30, 2025
 
  WHEREAS, This Legislative Body has long valued  athletic  excellence
at   the   professional,  collegiate,  and  school  levels,  recognizing
exceptional  achievements,  outstanding  accomplishments,  and  national
championships; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Excellence  and  success  in  competitive  sports  can  be
achieved only through strenuous practice, team  play  and  team  spirit,
nurtured by dedicated coaching and strategic planning; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  Legislative Body is justly proud to congratulate the
Hobart Men's Ice Hockey Team upon the occasion of capturing their  third
consecutive  NCAA  Division  III  Men's Ice Hockey Championship, held on
Saturday, March 30, 2025, at the Adirondack Bank Center  in  Utica,  New
York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  To  the  praise  and  applause  of their excited fans, the
storied tradition of intercollegiate  athletic  success  at  Hobart  and
William  Smith  Colleges  has again risen to a new level when the Hobart
Men's Ice Hockey Team bested  Utica  University  in  a  hard-fought  2-1
victory in overtime; and
 
  WHEREAS, The achievement further cements Hobart College's reputation
as  one  of  the  most solid and consistent Division III programs in the
country, finishing the season with a 29-1-1 record, consistently  ranked
among the top Division III programs in the nation; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  Hobart Statesmen program has been built by Head Coach
Mark Taylor, who for the past 25 years,  has  brought  together  skilled
collegiate  hockey  players  to  create  a  team balanced by outstanding
defense, wise strategy, and teamwork, where every player was prepared to
get the job done one shift at a time; and
 
  WHEREAS, Hobart Hockey  took  advantage  of  home  ice  in  downtown
Geneva's  fabled "Cooler" with an undefeated 17-0-0 record, their second
undefeated home rink record in the program's history; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Among  Hobart  Hockey's  other  achievements  this  season
included  winning  their third consecutive NEHC Tournament Championship,
outscoring their opponents by  nearly  100  points  across  the  2024-25
season,  allowing  only 4 goals in the NCAA tournament, and becoming the
first team to capture three consecutive national titles since Middlebury
College in 2004-06; and
 
  WHEREAS, Celebration and recognition is shared by  the  current  and
past  parents, alumni players, college leadership, and boosters who have
supported Hobart Ice  Hockey  for  decades;  the  leadership  of  former
Athletic  Director Bill Stiles, who made the decision in 1977 to elevate
 
the decades-old Hobart Club Hockey Team to the NCAA Division III program
greatly inspired the success of the team; and
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
congratulate the members of the Hobart Men's Ice  Hockey  Team:  Mavrick
Goyer,  Nick  Dimatos, Bauer Morrissey, Josh Doolan, Cooper Swift, Ethan
Mulhearn, Tanner Daniels, Brandon Sacchetti, Dominic Schimizzi,  Matthew
Iasenza,  Luke  Aquaro,  Adam  Mendelson,  Heikki  Virtanen, Mason Mara,
Easton Ryan, Austin Mourar, Matthew Perryman, Shane Shell,  Calum  Chau,
Tristan  Fasig,  Conor  Lally,  Chris  Duclair,  Kahlil  Fontana, Connor
Shibley, Damon Beaver, Connor Elliot, Ryan Remick,  Ignat  Belov,  Logan
Gotinsky,  and  Tamas  Toth;  Coaching  Staff:  Head  Coach Mark Taylor;
Assistant Coaches:   Niko Kovachis and Derek  Berry;  Hockey  Operations
Assistants:  Joel  Stoneham  and  Victor  Cutting;  and  Support  Staff:
Athletic Fellow Emeritus John Halfman, Athletic  Fellow  Julie  Kingery,
Associate   Director   of   Athletic  Communications  Mackenzie  Larsen,
Assistant Athletic Director for Equipment and Operations Kevin McDonald,
and Assistant Athletic Trainer Sara Moore upon the occasion of capturing
their third consecutive NCAA Division III Men's Ice Hockey  Championship
on March 30, 2025; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to the Hobart Men's Ice Hockey Team.
