Abraham, whose years of faithful stewardship and exemplary guidance have left an indelible imprint upon the Chapter's growth and vitality; through her wisdom, mentorship, and unwavering dedication, she has upheld the highest tenets of the Eastern Star while fostering sisterly love and organizational excellence; and WHEREAS, Also recognized is Cynthia Settles, Director of the Falling Angels Sickle Cell Foundation, whose compassionate advocacy and public health leadership have brought hope, awareness, and critical resources to individuals and families impacted by sickle cell disease; her humanitarian service exemplifies the charitable mission and outreach embraced by the Order of the Eastern Star; and WHEREAS, Providing visionary leadership for this auspicious occasion is Worthy Matron Tania Hollensworth, whose administration has been marked by integrity, poise, and a steadfast commitment to strengthening the Chapter's charitable works, membership engagement, and community presence; under her guidance, Poinsettia Chapter No. 76 continues to flourish as a beacon of light, service, and sisterhood within the Third District and throughout the State of New York; and WHEREAS, Through events such as this Dinner Dance, Poinsettia Chapter No. 76 reaffirms its sacred bond to the principles of Prince Hall Masonry and the Order of the Eastern Star-promoting charity, cultivating fellowship, and advancing the moral and civic good of the communities it so faithfully serves; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to honor and commend Poinsettia Chapter No. 76 upon the occasion of hosting its 2026 Dinner Dance, and to recognize its enduring commitment to service, sisterhood, and charitable excellence; and be it further RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body proudly recognizes Junior Past Matron Linda Valentine, Past Matron Margo E. Abraham, and Cynthia Settles for their outstanding contributions and well-deserved honors bestowed upon this occasion; and be it further RESOLVED, That sincere appreciation is extended to Worthy Matron Tania Hollensworth for her distinguished leadership and devoted stewardship of Poinsettia Chapter No. 76; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Poinsettia Chapter No. 76 and the aforementioned honorees as a token of this Legislative Body's esteem and highest regard.
