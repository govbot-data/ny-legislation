SMITH, BRABENEC -- read once and referred to the Committee on Codes AN ACT to amend the penal law, in relation to unlawful operation of model or unmanned aircraft over a critical infrastructure facility The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The penal law is amended by adding a new section 240.80 to 2 read as follows: 3 § 240.80 Unlawful operation of model or unmanned aircraft over a crit- 4 ical infrastructure facility. 5 1. It is unlawful for a person to (a) knowingly operate or use a model 6 or unmanned aircraft over a critical infrastructure facility at an alti- 7 tude not higher than four hundred feet above ground level; or (b) allow 8 a model or unmanned aircraft system to make contact with a critical 9 infrastructure facility, including any person or object on the premises 10 of or within the facility. 11 2. This section does not apply to: 12 (a) the federal government; 13 (b) a public body; 14 (c) a law enforcement agency; 15 (d) a person under contract with or otherwise acting under the direc- 16 tion or on behalf of the federal government, a public body or a law 17 enforcement agency; 18 (e) an owner or operator of the critical infrastructure facility or a 19 person who has the prior written consent of the owner or operator of the 20 critical infrastructure facility; 21 (f) the owner or occupant of the property on which the critical 22 infrastructure facility is located or a person who has the prior written 23 consent of the owner or occupant of the property on which the critical 24 infrastructure facility is located; or EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. For the purposes of this section: 5 (a) (i) "Unmanned aircraft" means an aircraft, including an aircraft 6 commonly known as a drone, that is operated without the possibility of 7 direct human intervention from within or on the aircraft; and 8 (ii) "Unmanned aircraft system" means an unmanned aircraft and asso- 9 ciated elements, including any communication links and components that 10 control the unmanned aircraft. 11 (b) "Commercial purposes" means the use of an unmanned aircraft in 12 return for financial compensation and includes aerial photography, aeri- 13 al mapping or geospatial imaging. 14 (c) "Critical infrastructure facility" means any of the following 15 facilities, if completely enclosed by a fence or other physical barrier 16 that is obviously designed to exclude intruders, or if marked with a 17 sign conspicuously posted on the property that indicates that entry is 18 forbidden: 19 (i) a petroleum or alumina refinery; 20 (ii) an electrical power generating facility, substation, switching 21 station or electrical control center; 22 (iii) a chemical, polymer or rubber manufacturing facility; 23 (iv) a water intake structure, water treatment facility, wastewater 24 treatment plant or pump station; 30 (ix) a gas processing plant, including a plant used in the processing, 31 treatment or fractionation of natural gas; 32 (x) a transmission facility used by a federally licensed radio or 33 television station;
