O'PHARROW, RA -- read once and referred to the Committee on Governmental Employees AN ACT to amend the administrative code of the city of New York and the civil service law, in relation to health insurance coverage for surviving dependents following the death of a police officer by suicide The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Paragraph 2 of subdivision b of section 12-126 of the 2 administrative code of the city of New York is amended by adding a new 3 subparagraph (v) to read as follows: 4 (v) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, where 5 an active or retired member of the police department dies by suicide and 6 is enrolled in a health insurance plan, the surviving spouse or domestic 7 partner and any other dependents shall be afforded the right to such 8 health insurance coverage and health insurance coverage which is predi- 9 cated on the insured's enrollment in the hospital and medical program 10 for the aged and disabled under the social security act as is provided 11 for retirees and their dependents as set forth in subparagraph (i) of 12 this paragraph, for ninety days following such death. 13 § 2. Section 165 of the civil service law is amended by adding a new 14 subdivision 3 to read as follows: 15 3. Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, in the 16 event of death by suicide of an active or retired police officer having 17 coverage at the time of death for such police officer and such police 18 officer's dependents, the unremarried spouse of such police officer 19 covered at the time of such police officer's death and such police offi- 20 cer's covered dependents shall be eligible to continue full coverage 21 under the health benefit plan for ninety days after such police offi- 22 cer's death.
