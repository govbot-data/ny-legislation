        Introduced by M. of A. KAY -- read once and referred to the Committee on

        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  education  law, in relation to the provision of
          transportation after 4pm by school districts in Sullivan county
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section 1. The education law is amended by adding a new section 3627-a
     2  to read as follows:
     3    §  3627-a.  Transportation  after 4pm for Sullivan county. 1. Notwith-
     4  standing any other provisions of this section to the contrary,  for  the
     5  two thousand twenty-five--two thousand twenty-six school year and there-
     6  after, every school district located in the county of Sullivan providing
     7  transportation pursuant to this chapter shall be responsible for:
     8    (a) (i) providing transportation for every child attending a public or
     9  nonpublic  school who resides in an area containing at least fifty chil-
    10  dren within a five mile radius who remains at the same school for  which
    11  they  are  enrolled  for regularly scheduled academic classes from half-
    12  past nine o'clock in the morning or earlier until four  o'clock  in  the
    13  afternoon or later, on weekdays;
    14    (ii)  such  transportation shall be provided to all children attending
    15  grades kindergarten through eight who live more than two miles from  the
    16  school, which they legally attend, and for all children attending grades
    17  nine through twelve who live more than three miles from the school which
    18  they  legally  attend, and shall be provided for each such pupil up to a
    19  distance of fifteen miles, the distances in each case being measured  by
    20  the nearest available route from such child's home to school; or
    21    (b)  reimbursing the cost incurred by licensed transportation carriers
    22  pursuant to contracts with such school district for providing  transpor-
    23  tation for those children attending public and nonpublic schools attend-
    24  ing  grades kindergarten through eight who live more than two miles from
    25  the school, which they legally attend, and for  all  children  attending
