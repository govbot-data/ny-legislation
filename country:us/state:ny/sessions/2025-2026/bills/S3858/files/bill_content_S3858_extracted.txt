The public health law is amended by adding a new section 1392-b 6 to read as follows: 7 § 1392-b. Epinephrine devices required. 1. Definitions. As used in 8 this section, "emergency health care provider" means (a) a physician 9 with knowledge and experience in the delivery of emergency care; or (b) 10 a hospital licensed under article twenty-eight of this chapter that 11 provides emergency care. 12 2. Collaborative agreement. (a) Every person, firm, limited liability 13 company, association and corporation which operates a children's over- 14 night, summer day and traveling summer day camp, as defined in this 15 article, shall maintain a collaborative agreement with an emergency 16 health care provider and shall have on the premises an epinephrine 17 device. 18 (b) The collaborative agreement required pursuant to paragraph (a) of 19 this subdivision shall include a written agreement that incorporates 20 written practice protocols, and policies and procedures that shall 21 ensure compliance with the provisions of this section. The person, firm, 22 limited liability company, organization or entity operating the chil- 23 dren's overnight, summer day and traveling summer day camp shall file a 24 copy of the collaborative agreement with the department and with the 25 appropriate regional council prior to using any epinephrine device. 26 3. Possession and use of epinephrine devices. Possession and use of 27 epinephrine devices shall be limited as follows: 28 (a) No person shall use an epinephrine device unless such person shall 29 have successfully completed a training course in the use of epinephrine 30 devices approved by the commissioner pursuant to the rules of the 31 department. This section does not prohibit the use of an epinephrine 32 device (i) by a health care practitioner licensed or certified under 33 title eight of the education law acting within the scope of such practi- 34 tioner's practice, or (ii) by a person acting pursuant to a lawful 35 prescription. 36 (b) Every person, firm, organization and entity authorized to possess 37 and use epinephrine devices pursuant to this section shall use, maintain 38 and dispose of such devices pursuant to regulations of the department. 39 (c) Every use of an epinephrine device pursuant to this section shall 40 immediately be reported to the emergency health care provider. 41 4. Application of other laws. (a) Use of an epinephrine device pursu- 42 ant to this section shall be considered first aid or emergency treatment 43 for the purpose of any statute relating to liability. 44 (b) Purchase, acquisition, possession or use of an epinephrine device 45 pursuant to this section shall not constitute the unlawful practice of a 46 profession or other violation under title eight of the education law or 47 article thirty-three of this chapter. 48 (c) Any person otherwise authorized to sell or provide an epinephrine 49 device may sell or provide it to a person authorized to possess it 50 pursuant to this section. 51 § 3. This act shall take effect on the one hundred eightieth day after 52 it shall have become a law.
