        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  public service law, in relation to enacting the
          "build to need act"

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Short title. This act shall be known as and may be cited as
     2  the "build to need act".
     3    §  2.  Legislative  findings  and intent. The legislature hereby finds
     4  that in order to achieve the targets set forth in the climate leadership
     5  and community protection act, zero-emissions vehicle sales targets,  and
     6  regulations,  including the advanced clean truck and advanced clean cars
     7  II rules, zero-emissions school bus mandate, and other  relevant  goals,
     8  the interests of the people of the state would be served by:
     9    (a) expediting electric grid infrastructure upgrades to facilitate the
    10  charging of light-duty, medium-duty, and heavy-duty electric vehicles;
    11    (b)  aligning a proactive planning process for beneficial electrifica-
    12  tion with the current coordinated grid  planning  process  for  electric
    13  corporations;
    14    (c)  ensuring  the  full range of feasible distributed energy resource
    15  technologies, load management opportunities,  and  expected  electrified
    16  end uses are incorporated into electric utility investment plans;
    17    (d)  lowering costs to customers through efficient planning, including
    18  consideration and deployment of non-wires solutions that reduce or elim-
    19  inate the need for distribution system upgrades;
    20    (e) ensuring meaningful  benefits  to  communities  most  affected  by
    21  large-scale  proactive  utility projects developed under this framework;
    22  and

     1    (f) enhancing speed,  flexibility,  and  ease  of  customer  intercon-

     3    § 3. The public service law is amended by adding a new section 77-a to
     4  read as follows:
     5    §  77-a. Build to need act. 1. Within sixty days of the effective date
     6  of this section, the commission shall commence a proceeding to implement
     7  a grid planning process that shall direct electric corporations to:
     8    (a) align planning under this section  with  existing  planning  proc-
     9  esses,  including  the  coordinated  grid  planning  process implemented
    10  pursuant to the climate leadership and community protection act, to  the
    11  extent feasible;
    12    (b)  provide  annually  to  the  New  York independent system operator
    13  updated load projections consistent with the state's electrification and
    14  climate related policy mandates;
    15    (c) use a planning horizon of at  least  ten  years  for  transmission
    16  through  substation-level investments and a planning horizon of at least
    17  five years for other distribution investments;
    18    (d) proactively gather and incorporate data relevant to  future  elec-
    19  tric loads from sources including but not limited to customers operating
    20  fleets,  state  and  municipal  departments  of  transportation or their
    21  equivalents, other entities that may  reasonably  be  expected  to  have
    22  relevant  information concerning location, type, magnitude, or timing of
    23  major future loads, available vehicle telematics data, and other sources
    24  of information as directed by the commission;
    25    (e) consider customers' activities that may reduce, defer,  or  other-
    26  wise  affect  the  need  or  extent of grid upgrades, such as customers'
    27  reasonably foreseeable load management  activities,  including  but  not
    28  limited to implementation of automated load management technologies, and
    29  deployments  of  distributed  energy resources and ability to enter into
    30  flexible interconnections;
    31    (f) identify system needs where the electric corporation, planning  in
    32  accordance  with  the state's electrification and climate related policy
    33  mandates, finds (i) a reasonable expectation of new load in  a  specific
    34  area  based on inputs including fleet electrification commitments, prox-
    35  imity to  transportation  corridors,  ports,  airports,  other  existing
    36  transportation  or  freight  hubs  and  other relevant data sources that
    37  would substantiate the projections of new load and the associated  time-
    38  line;  and  (ii)  a reasonable likelihood of grid constraints that shall
    39  impair the electric corporations ability to  serve  the  load  absent  a
    40  solution to address the system need in a timely manner; and
    41    (g)  where  an  electric  corporation  identifies  a system need, such
    42  corporation shall develop solutions that proactively address such system
    43  need, and consider advanced technologies where appropriate and feasible.
    44    2. (a) The commission shall develop a test for  defining  larger  tier
    45  one solutions and smaller tier two solutions. Such test shall:
    46    (i)  be  applied  uniformly to all electric corporations, except where
    47  the commission determines such different  criteria  should  apply  to  a
    48  given electric corporation because unique circumstances or hardships, or
    49  impracticability  of  applying  the uniform test to such electric corpo-
    50  ration exist;
    51    (ii) prohibit electric corporations from segmenting solutions to avoid
    52  a tier one designation; and
    53    (iii) specify criteria such as, but not limited to, solution cost as a
    54  percentage of the electric corporation's  rate  base,  solution  nominal
    55  voltage, solution wattage and solution type.

     1    (b)  The  commission may update such test as appropriate to ensure the
     2  timely achievement of state electrification mandates and  the  efficient
     3  consideration of solutions development pursuant to this section.
     4    (c)  The commission shall establish separate requirements for tier one
     5  projects for which the electric corporation seeks approval under  subdi-
     6  vision four of this section and tier two projects.
     7    3.  The  commission  shall  develop  a  framework governing the use of
     8  advanced technologies as part of the solutions for  which  the  electric
     9  corporation  seeks approval under subdivision four of this section. Such
    10  framework shall: (a) define  advanced  technologies  to  include  energy
    11  storage,  demand  response, automated load management, other distributed
    12  energy resources, and any other categories as determined by the  commis-
    13  sion.    Such  framework  shall direct electric corporations to consider
    14  where appropriate and feasible at least one  alternative  technology  to
    15  address the identified system need fully or partially; and
    16    (b) such electric corporation shall demonstrate why advanced technolo-
    17  gies  were  or  were not included as part of the project. The commission
    18  may establish mandatory targets for deployment of advanced technologies,
    19  provide for earnings adjustment mechanisms  associated  with  utilities'
    20  deployment of advanced technologies, and include other metrics and guid-
    21  ance as appropriate.
    22    4.  In  approving tier one projects for which the electric corporation
    23  seeks approval under this subdivision, the commission shall require:
    24    (a) electric corporations file with  the  commission  a  petition  for
    25  approval.  Such  petition  shall  include  a description of project need
    26  including data sources to substantiate need, explanation of alternatives
    27  considered, description of its community benefit  plan  as  required  in
    28  subdivision  six  of  this  section,  projected  costs  and  benefits, a
    29  proposed cost recovery method, and any other requirements as established
    30  by the commission. Further, such petition shall include  an  explanation
    31  of  an  electric  corporation's  reasonable  efforts  to obtain state or
    32  federal funding sources to offset electric corporation customer costs of
    33  the project. If the electric corporation does not seek state or  federal
    34  funding sources, it shall explain why it did not seek such funding.
    35    (b) render a decision on such petition within one hundred twenty days,
    36  or  within the one hundred twenty day period, extend such time to render
    37  a decision by sixty days. Failure to render a decision within such time-
    38  line prescribed, shall deem such petition approved by operation of  law;
    39  and
    40    (c)  to the extent an electric corporation's infrastructure deployment
    41  or other costs attributable to this section  are  not  included  in  its
    42  current  rate  plan as of the effective date of this section, such elec-
    43  tric corporation may recover the incremental revenue requirement associ-
    44  ated with such costs through a surcharge mechanism until its base  rates
    45  are  reset.  A surcharge mechanism may only be used to the extent neces-
    46  sary between the effective date of this section and  the  conclusion  of
    47  the electric corporation's next rate case.
    48    5.  (a) All tier one projects for which the electric corporation seeks
    49  approval under subdivision four of this section shall include a communi-
    50  ty benefit plan. The commission shall develop a community  benefit  plan
    51  framework which shall:
    52    (i) define the term "affected communities," which shall include commu-
    53  nities  in  the vicinity of a tier one project as well as other criteria
    54  defined by the commission;
    55    (ii) consider separate requirements applicable to disadvantaged commu-
    56  nities within affected communities;

     1    (iii) specify criteria that a reasonable percentage of solution  bene-
     2  fits are demonstrated to be delivered to affected communities;
     3    (iv)  consider  achievable,  measurable workforce development programs
     4  and goals designed to expand high-quality job opportunities in  affected
     5  communities;
     6    (v)  require  such  electric  corporation provide benefits to affected
     7  communities; and
     8    (vi) require a description of such  electric  corporation's  past  and
     9  planned  efforts  concerning  community  engagement  and  its history of
    10  incorporating an affected communities feedback into its solution or  its
    11  willingness to incorporate an affected communities feedback.
    12    (b)  Reasonable  and prudently incurred costs in the implementation of
    13  the community benefit plan shall be recoverable by such electric  corpo-
    14  ration as part of solution costs.
    15    6.  The  commission  is authorized to promulgate rules and regulations
    16  necessary  to  determine  record  keeping  requirements  for  tier   two
    17  projects, including:
    18    (a) records demonstrating project need;
    19    (b) analysis of the chosen solution by the electric corporation;
    20    (c) alternatives considered;
    21    (d) project costs and benefits; and
    22    (e) any other requirements determined by the commission.
    23    7.  Within  two  hundred  eighty  days  of  the effective date of this
    24  section, electric corporations shall  offer  to  customers,  and  update
    25  tariffs  as  necessary  to  enable  both  hybrid  and flexible intercon-
    26  nections. For the purposes of this subdivision, hybrid  interconnections
    27  include  an  interconnection,  through  a  single application, of hybrid
    28  facilities.  Such hybrid facilities shall include those composed of more
    29  than one device of different technology types for such production, stor-
    30  age, and consumption of electricity that are located on  the  same  site
    31  and have a single point of interconnection. Further, for the purposes of
    32  this subdivision, flexible interconnections include electric corporation
    33  requirements  to  enable  expeditiously energizing new load or intercon-
    34  necting a distributed  energy  resource  to  an  electric  corporation's
    35  distribution  system,  including  an  agreement, supported by the use of
    36  software or hardware solutions such as automated  load  management,  for
    37  curtailing the import and export of electricity from and to the distrib-
    38  ution system.
    39    8.  Within one year of the effective date of this section, the commis-
    40  sion shall establish average and maximum target time  periods  for  load
    41  energizations  and  distributed generation interconnections. The commis-
    42  sion may establish targets that vary depending on the size and complexi-
    43  ty of such work, as well as factors beyond such  electric  corporation's
    44  control.    Further,  the  commission  shall  establish  a procedure for
    45  customers to  report  energization  or  interconnection  delays  to  the
    46  commission.  The  commission shall establish an enforcement mechanism to
    47  ensure timely interconnection and energization, including but not limit-
    48  ed to, earnings adjustment mechanisms with incentives and penalties.
    49    9. The commission, in consultation with  the  New  York  state  energy
    50  research  and  development  authority,  the  department of environmental
    51  conservation, the department of transportation, the power  authority  of
    52  New  York  state,  the  Long  Island power authority, and other agencies
    53  charged with administering electric  vehicle  programs,  incentives,  or
    54  procurements  may  include  provisions  for  the  use  of automated load
    55  management in such programs, incentives, and procurements when they  are
    56  determined  to be cost effective. For purposes of this subdivision auto-

     2  and also installation costs.
     3    10.  (a)  Within  one  year of the effective date of this section, the
     4  commission shall adopt reporting requirements under this section  for  a
     5  time  period  determined  by  the commission but no less than five years
     6  from the effective date of  this  section.  The  reporting  requirements
     7  shall include at a minimum:
     8    (i)  a  summary  of tier one and tier two projects deployed, including
     9  but not limited to a general categorization of projects, project  costs,
    10  and for projects that entered service between the effective date of this
    11  section  and the effective date of the electric corporation's subsequent
    12  rate plan, an identification of those projects not included in the elec-

    14    (ii) a comparison of projected and actual loads,  including  projected

    21    (v) energization and interconnection timelines.
    22    (b) To the extent an electric corporation's performance fails to  meet

    24  ures and shall provide a strategy for meeting the targets in the future.
    25  The commission shall  monitor  each  electric  corporation's  activities
    26  related to this section and take appropriate action as needed to address
    27  any deficiencies.
    28    § 4. This act shall take effect immediately.
