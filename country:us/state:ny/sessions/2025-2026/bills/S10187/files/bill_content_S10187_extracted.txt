The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Sections 629.0, 629.1 and 629.6 of chapter 273 of the laws 2 of 1939, constituting the Nassau county civil divisions act, as added by 3 chapter 71 of the laws of 1940, are amended to read as follows: 4 § 629.0 Incorporation; membership. A membership corporation by the 5 name of "Volunteer and Exempt [Firemen's] Firefighter's Benevolent Asso- 6 ciation of Bethpage, Nassau county, New York, Inc." is hereby created. 7 It shall be composed of such persons eligible for membership therein as 8 hereinafter provided as shall (a) notify the secretary of the Bethpage 9 fire department in writing prior to the organization meeting of such 10 corporation hereinafter provided for of their desire to become members, 11 or 12 (b) shall attend such meeting or an adjournment thereof and signify 13 their intention of becoming members at such meeting, or 14 (c) shall notify the secretary of the corporation hereby created in 15 writing within sixty days after [his] such secretary's election of their 16 desire to become members thereof, and also such persons so eligible as 17 may become members of such corporation pursuant to its by-laws. 18 § 629.1 Persons eligible to membership. All persons who are now, or 19 who have been, or who hereafter shall be active volunteer members of the 20 Bethpage fire department, and who have heretofore acquired or shall 21 hereafter acquire the status of exempt volunteer [firemen] firefighters 22 as defined in section two hundred of the general municipal law or any 23 other applicable law by virtue of having rendered service in the Beth- 24 page fire department, and all persons who are now active volunteer 25 members of the Bethpage fire department shall be eligible for membership 26 in the corporation created by this act, except that any [fireman] fire- 27 fighter who has been removed for cause, expelled or dropped from the EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
