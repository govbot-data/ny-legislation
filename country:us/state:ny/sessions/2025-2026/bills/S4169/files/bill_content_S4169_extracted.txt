PARKER -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Finance AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to creating the office of nutrition and fitness The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The executive law is amended by adding a new article 24-A 2 to read as follows: 3 ARTICLE 24-A 4 OFFICE OF NUTRITION AND FITNESS 5 Section 667. Statement of policy. 6 668. Definitions. 7 669. Office of nutrition and fitness; director. 8 670. Powers and duties. 9 671. Actions by other officers, departments, boards, commissions 10 or agencies. 11 § 667. Statement of policy. The legislature hereby reaffirms its poli- 12 cy to promote health and nutrition to protect the public from preventa- 13 ble diseases causally related to obesity by developing and implementing 14 a plan to educate and encourage health and nutrition in the state; and 15 recognizes that furtherance of such policy requires creation in the 16 executive department of an office of nutrition and fitness with staff 17 and skills requisite to act as the governor's agent in developing and 18 implementing healthy nutrition and fitness programs, to assure the prop- 19 er implementation and administration of such programs, and to assist the 20 governor and direct and coordinate the state's efforts with regard to 21 overall nutrition and fitness and the prevention of obesity. 22 § 668. Definitions. For the purposes of this article: 23 1. "Director" shall mean the director of nutrition and fitness. 24 2. "Office" shall mean the office of nutrition and fitness. Such office may have an office seal. 11 § 670. Powers and duties. The director shall assist the governor with 12 regard to developing policy and programs to improve the nutrition and 13 fitness of the people of New York. 14 § 671. Actions by other officers, departments, boards, commissions or 15 agencies. 1. Notwithstanding any inconsistent provision of law, any 16 state officer, department, board, commission or agency, shall, upon 17 written request from the director, take such administrative or other 18 action as is necessary to implement and administer the programs devel- 19 oped by the office. Such action may include, without limitation, the 20 adoption, repeal or amendment of rules, regulations or other procedures. 21 In particular, the director shall work closely with the education 22 department to develop and implement programs to improve the health and 23 nutrition of students in school and with the department of health to 24 develop and implement the findings and recommendations of the obesity 25 prevention act, as established in title five of article two of the 26 public health law, as added by chapter five hundred thirty-eight of the 27 laws of two thousand two. 28 2. All state officers, departments, boards, commissions and agencies 29 are authorized and directed to provide such other and further assist- 30 ance, services and data as may be necessary to allow the director prop- 31 erly to carry out their functions, powers and duties. 32 § 2.
