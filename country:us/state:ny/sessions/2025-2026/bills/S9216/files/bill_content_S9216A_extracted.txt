AN ACT in relation to directing the department of transportation and
certain public authorities to study certain bridges spanning bodies of
water connected to deep water ports in the state to determine whether
such bridges have the structural integrity to withstand an impact from
a large vessel; and providing for the repeal of such provisions upon
the expiration thereof
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. 1. The Department of Transportation, the Bridge Authority,
2 the Triborough Bridge and Tunnel Authority, the Thruway Authority, and
3 the Port Authority of New York and New Jersey, as applicable, shall each
4 conduct a study to determine the ability of certain bridges in the state
5 to withstand an impact to the support structures of such bridges from
6 large ships, including but not limited to, cargo vessels, oil tankers,
7 and passenger ships. Upon the conclusion of each such study, the commis-
8 sioner and the public authorities shall each prepare and issue a compre-
9 hensive report of their study findings and recommendations for improve-
10 ments to each bridge, if any.
11 a. The Department of Transportation shall study the following bridges:
12 i. Dunn Memorial Bridge; and
13 ii. Patroon Island Bridge.
14 b. The Bridge Authority shall study the following bridges:
15 i. Bear Mountain Bridge;
16 ii. Newburgh-Beacon Bridge;
17 iii. Mid-Hudson Bridge;
18 iv. Kingston-Rhinecliff Bridge;
19 v. Rip Van Winkle Bridge; and
20 vi. Walkway over the Hudson Pedestrian Bridge.
21 c. The Triborough Bridge and Tunnel Authority shall study the follow-
22 ing bridges:

17 2. Each such study shall include, at a minimum, an evaluation of the
18 following for each bridge:
19 a. the age of the bridge;
20 b. the number and type of vessels traversing the waters below the
21 bridge annually;
22 c. the current condition of the bridge;
23 d. any known or newly discovered chronic issue or problem with the
24 bridge;
25 e. the type, size, and construction of the support structures of the
26 bridge;
27 f. the type, size and construction of any barriers surrounding the
28 base of the support structures of the bridge and the distance between
29 such surrounding structures and the water channels occupied by vessels;
30 g. the width between the support structures of the bridge;
31 h. the height of the bridge;
32 i. the topography near the bridge, including but not limited to, sand
33 bars, shallows, and cliffs;
34 j. the area a vessel may safely access during a maneuver to avoid the
35 bridge in the event of a loss of power or propulsion or other emergency;
36 and
37 k. any other matters the commissioner or public authorities, as appli-
38 cable, deem necessary.
39 § 2. The reports required by this act shall be submitted to the gover-
40 nor, the speaker of the assembly, and the temporary president of the
41 senate no later than January 1, 2028. Each report shall include the
42 findings, conclusions, and recommendations of the commissioner or public
43 authorities, as applicable. Each report shall also be published on the
44 website of the Department of Transportation, the Bridge Authority, the
45 Triborough Bridge and Tunnel Authority, the Thruway Authority, and the
46 Port Authority of New York and New Jersey, as applicable, no later than
47 one month after it is completed.
48 § 3. The commissioner and the public authorities, as applicable, may
49 consult with any additional entities such commissioner or public author-
50 ities, as applicable, deem necessary to conducting the studies required
51 by this act.
