          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

    18  surrendered,  to  any natural person eighteen years of age or older, for

    20  § 280.00 Definitions.
    21    The following definitions are applicable to this article:
    22    1. "Animal" includes every living creature except a human being.
    23    2.  "Cruelty" includes every act, omission, or neglect, whereby unjus-
    24  tifiable physical pain, suffering or death is caused  or  permitted  and
    25  shall  include  but not be limited to, any act of overdriving, overload-
    26  ing, injuring, maiming, mutilating, or killing an animal.
    27    3. "Aggravated cruelty" shall mean an act of cruelty that is  done  or
    28  carried out in a depraved or sadistic manner.
    29    4. "Torture" means conduct that is intended to cause extreme pain.
    30    5. "Adoption" means the delivery of any animal, seized or surrendered,
    31  to  any  natural  person eighteen years of age or older, for the limited
    32  purpose of harboring such animal as a pet.
    33    6. "Farm animal" means  any  ungulate,  poultry,  species  of  cattle,
    34  sheep,  swine,  goats, llamas, horses or fur-bearing animals, as defined
    35  in section 11-1907 of the  environmental  conservation  law,  which  are
    36  raised  for  commercial  or  subsistence  purposes. "Fur-bearing animal"
    37  shall not include dogs or cats.
    38    7. "Companion animal" or "pet" means any dog or cat,  and  shall  also
    39  mean  any  other  domesticated animal normally maintained in or near the
    40  household of the owner or person who cares for such  other  domesticated
    41  animal. "Companion animal" or "pet" shall not include a "farm animal" as
    42  defined in this section.
    43    8.  "Animal  fighting"  shall  mean  any  fight between cocks or other
    44  birds, or between dogs, bulls, bears or any other  animals,  or  between
    45  any such animal and a person or persons, except in exhibitions of a kind
    46  commonly featured at rodeos.
    47    9.  "Abandons"  means any action taken that reflects willful departure
    48  from the ownership, possession, care, control, charge or custody  of  an
    49  animal, without making adequate provisions for the animal's future care.
    50    10.  "Restrain"  means to restrict an animal's movements intentionally
    51  and unlawfully in such manner as to  interfere  substantially  with  its
    52  liberty  by  moving  it  from  one  place to another, or by confining it
    53  either in the place where the restriction commences or  in  a  place  to
    54  which  it  has been moved, with knowledge that the restriction is unlaw-

    16  § 280.60 Appropriate shelter for dogs left outdoors.
    17    1. For purposes of this section:
    18    (a)  "Physical condition" shall include any special medical needs of a
    19  dog due to disease, illness, injury, age or breed about which the  owner
    20  or person with custody or control of the dog should reasonably be aware.
    21    (b)  "Inclement weather" shall mean weather conditions that are likely
    22  to adversely affect the health or safety of the dog, including  but  not
    23  limited to rain, sleet, ice, snow, wind, or extreme heat and cold.
    24    (c) "Dogs that are left outdoors" shall mean dogs that are outdoors in
    25  inclement  weather  without  ready access to, or the ability to enter, a
    26  house, apartment building,  office  building,  or  any  other  permanent
    27  structure  that  complies with the standards enumerated in paragraph (b)
    28  of subdivision three of this section.
    29    2. (a) Any person who owns or has custody or control of a dog that  is
    30  left  outdoors  shall  provide it with shelter appropriate to its breed,
    31  physical condition and climate. Any person who  knowingly  violates  the
    32  provisions of this section shall be guilty of a violation, punishable by
    33  a  fine of not less than fifty dollars nor more than one hundred dollars
    34  for a first offense, and a fine of not less than one hundred dollars nor
    35  more than two hundred fifty dollars for a second and subsequent offense.
    36  Beginning seventy-two hours after a charge of  violating  this  section,
    37  each  day  that a defendant fails to correct the deficiencies in the dog
    38  shelter for a dog that such defendant owns or that is in  their  custody
    39  or  control and that is left outdoors, so as to bring it into compliance
    40  with the provisions of this section shall constitute a separate offense.
    41    (b) The court may, in its discretion, reduce the amount  of  any  fine
    42  imposed  for a violation of this section by the amount which the defend-
    43  ant proves they have spent providing  a  dog  shelter  or  repairing  an
    44  existing  dog  shelter so that it complies with the requirements of this
    45  section. Nothing in this paragraph shall prevent the seizure  of  a  dog
    46  for  a  violation  of  this section pursuant to the authority granted in
    47  this article.
    48    3. Minimum standards for determining whether shelter is appropriate to
    49  a dog's breed, physical condition and the climate shall include:
    50    (a) For dogs that are restrained in  any  manner  outdoors,  shade  by
    51  natural  or  artificial means to protect the dog from direct sunlight at
    52  all times when exposure to sunlight is likely to threaten the health  of
    53  the dog.
    54    (b)  For all dogs that are left outdoors in inclement weather, a hous-
    55  ing facility, which must: (1) have a  waterproof  roof;  (2)  be  struc-
    56  turally  sound  with insulation appropriate to local climatic conditions
