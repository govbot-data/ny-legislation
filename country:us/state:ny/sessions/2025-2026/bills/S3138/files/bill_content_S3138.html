Add Art 26-C §§755 - 759, rpld §559, Exec L; amd §491, Soc Serv L
 
Establishes the commission of the deaf, deafblind and hard of hearing; requires the commission of the deaf, deafblind, and hard of hearing to transmit complaints in matters affecting the deaf, deafblind, and hard of hearing communities to the vulnerable persons' central register; repeals provisions relating to the New York state interagency coordinating council for services to persons who are deaf, deaf-blind, or hard of hearing.

        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  executive  law, in relation to establishing the
          commission of the deaf, deafblind and hard of hearing;  to  amend  the
          social  services  law,  in relation to requiring the commission of the
          deaf, deafblind, and hard of hearing to transmit complaints in matters
          affecting the deaf, deafblind, and hard of hearing communities to  the
          vulnerable persons' central register; and to repeal section 559 of the
          executive  law relating to the New York state interagency coordinating
          council for services to persons who are deaf, deaf-blind, or  hard  of
          hearing
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The executive law is amended by adding a new  article  26-C
     2  to read as follows:
     3                                ARTICLE 26-C
     4            COMMISSION OF THE DEAF, DEAFBLIND AND HARD OF HEARING
     5  Section 755. Definitions.
     6          756. Commission of the deaf, deafblind and hard of hearing.
     7          757. Executive director.
     8          758. Membership of the commission.
     9          759. Powers and duties of the commission.
    10    § 755. Definitions. When used in this article, the following words and
    11  terms shall have the meanings ascribed to them in this section:
    12    1.  "deaf"  shall  mean a person who has profound hearing loss and who
    13  primarily relies on visual communication, such as sign  language,  writ-
    14  ing,  lip  reading,  and  gestures,  which may be used exclusively or in
    15  combination, or who communicates orally and use sign language to support
    16  their understanding of the spoken language;

     1    2. "deafblind" shall mean a person with varying  degrees  of  combined
     2  hearing and vision loss; and
     3    3. "hard of hearing" shall mean a person with measurable hearing loss.
     4    § 756. Commission of the deaf, deafblind and hard of hearing. There is
     5  hereby  established  the  "commission of the deaf, deafblind and hard of
     6  hearing".
     7    § 757. Executive director. The governor shall appoint, at  the  recom-
     8  mendation of the commission, an executive director of the commission who
     9  shall  serve  at  the  pleasure  of the governor. The executive director
    10  shall receive an annual salary fixed by the governor within the  amounts
    11  appropriated  specifically  therefor and shall be entitled to reimburse-
    12  ment for actual expenses necessarily incurred in the discharge of  their
    13  official  duties.  The executive director shall assist the commission in
    14  carrying out the duties and responsibilities of the commission.
    15    § 758. Membership of  the  commission.  1.  The  commission  shall  be
    16  comprised  of  nine  voting  members  and  three non-voting members. All
    17  members must be New York residents.
    18    (a) Five members of the commission shall be appointed  by  the  gover-
    19  nor in the following manner:
    20    (i)  three  members,  one  whom is deaf, one whom is deafblind and one
    21  whom is hard of hearing;
    22    (ii) a licensed audiologist pursuant to article one hundred fifty-nine
    23  of the education law; and
    24    (iii) a representative of a state-supported school for the instruction
    25  of students who are deaf, deafblind or hard of hearing.
    26    (b) One member  of the commission shall be appointed by the  temporary
    27  president of the senate. Such member shall be hard of hearing.
    28    (c)  One member of the commission shall be appointed by the speaker of
    29  the assembly. Such member shall be deaf.
    30    (d) One member of the commission shall be appointed  by  the  minority
    31  leader of the senate. Such member shall be deafblind.
    32    (e)  One  member of the commission shall be appointed by the  minority
    33  leader of the assembly. Such member shall be a parent of a child who  is
    34  deaf, deafblind or hard of hearing.
    35    (f)  The  executive  director of the commission of the deaf, deafblind
    36  and hard of hearing shall be a  non-voting,  ex-officio  member  of  the
    37  commission.
    38    (g) The members of the commission shall appoint two non-voting members
    39  of  the  commission,  one whom shall be a qualified sign language inter-
    40  preter and one whom shall be a qualified real-time captioning provider.
    41    2. The identification and selection  of  candidates  for  appointments
    42  shall  be  done,  to  the greatest extent possible, in consultation with
    43  organizations and individuals representing the deaf, deafblind and  hard
    44  of hearing communities.
    45    3.  The  members  of the commission   shall   serve   terms  of  three
    46  years.  Subsequent appointments upon the expiration of such terms  shall
    47  be  filled in the same manner as the original appointments.  The members
    48  of the commission shall elect one of their members to  serve  as  chair-
    49  person  during  their  appointment  term or for a period of three years,
    50  whichever is shorter.
    51    4. Members of the commission shall  serve  without  compensation,  but
    52  shall  be  allowed  their  actual and necessary expenses incurred in the
    53  performance of their duties.
    54    5. The commission shall meet at least four times   per    year.    The
    55  meetings  shall  be  made  accessible to the deaf, deafblind and hard of
    56  hearing communities. A majority of the voting membership of the  commis-

     1  sion  shall   constitute  a  quorum  for  the  transaction of commission
     2  business.  Action may be taken and motions and  resolutions  adopted  by
     3  the  commission  at  any meeting thereof by the affirmative vote of five
     4  members of the commission.
     5    §  759.  Powers  and duties of the commission. 1. The commission shall
     6  advise the governor and the legislature on how the state can effectively
     7  meet the needs of the deaf, deafblind and hard of hearing communities.
     8    2. The duties and responsibilities of the commission are to:
     9    (a) create a statewide plan,  including  annual  updates,  to  deliver
    10  coordinated services and programs to persons who are deaf, deafblind and
    11  hard  of  hearing in a manner that best serves their needs including but
    12  not limited to accessibility, educational, medical, housing, and employ-
    13  ment services.  The commission shall coordinate with other  state  agen-
    14  cies,  and  the  Commission for the Blind, as appropriate, when creating
    15  their state plan to deliver coordinated services and programs;
    16    (b) develop a process to collect and maintain data on  the  number  of
    17  deaf, deafblind and hard of hearing individuals in New York state;
    18    (c)  regularly  maintain  a  website that serves as a clearinghouse of
    19  information, resources and services for persons who are deaf,  deafblind
    20  and hard of hearing;
    21    (d)  create  a  referral  system  that  receives complaints in matters
    22  affecting the deaf, deafblind and hard of hearing communities and refers
    23  such complaints to the appropriate agencies or organizations  if  neces-
    24  sary or appropriate, provided however, if such complaint is a reportable
    25  incident pursuant to article eleven of the social services law, it shall
    26  be  forwarded  to  the  vulnerable  persons' central register or if such
    27  complaint is a criminal matter, it shall be forwarded to the appropriate
    28  law enforcement agency;
    29    (e) coordinate with federal,  state  and  local  agencies  to  improve
    30  access to public safety and emergency services;
    31    (f)  develop strategies to increase employment opportunities for deaf,
    32  deafblind and hard of hearing individuals including, but not limited to,
    33  job training and career development services;
    34    (g) develop and recommend policies to the governor and legislature  as
    35  deemed  necessary  to  assist  the  deaf,  deafblind and hard of hearing
    36  communities;
    37    (h) develop a model policy  on  interpreter  and  captioning  services
    38  including  methods  to increase access to such services and licensing of
    39  qualified interpreters and captioners;
    40    (i) provide technical assistance to state  agencies  and  departments,
    41  local governments, agencies or other non-governmental entities on devel-
    42  oping  policies, providing services and increasing accessibility for the
    43  deaf, deafblind and hard of hearing communities;
    44    (j) improve and expand access to assistive  technology  including  but
    45  not  limited  to,  relay  services,  closed captioning, open captioning,
    46  hearing loops, telecommunication devices, transcription services, alter-
    47  native text or other communication services;
    48    (k) coordinate with the department of state regarding access to  hear-
    49  ing aids and other associated resources; and
    50    (l)  develop public awareness materials and resources on hearing pres-
    51  ervation and hearing loss prevention.
    52    3. Subject to an appropriation by the legislature, the commission  may
    53  hire personnel or enter into contracts deemed necessary to carry out the
    54  duties and responsibilities of the commission.
    55    4.  The  commission  shall submit an annual report to the governor and
    56  legislature detailing  the  operation  and  actions  of  the  commission

     2  statewide plan, policy recommendations, and actions taken in conjunction
     3  with state or local agencies.  The initial report shall be submitted  on
     4  September  first, two thousand twenty-six, with subsequent reports annu-
     5  ally thereafter.
     6    § 2. Section 559 of the executive law is REPEALED.
     7    § 3. Section 491 of the social services law is amended by adding a new
     8  subdivision 6 to read as follows:
     9    6. The commission of the deaf, deafblind and hard  of  hearing  estab-
    10  lished  pursuant  to  article twenty-six-C of the executive law shall be
    11  responsible for transmitting complaints in matters affecting  the  deaf,
    12  deafblind  and  hard  of  hearing communities to the vulnerable persons'
    13  central register, as appropriate, where such complaints involve  allega-
    14  tions of reportable incidents pursuant to this section.
    15    § 4. This act shall take effect on the one hundred eightieth day after
    16  it shall have become a law.
