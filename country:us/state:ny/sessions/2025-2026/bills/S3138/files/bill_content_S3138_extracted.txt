Powers and duties of the commission. 1. The commission shall 6 advise the governor and the legislature on how the state can effectively 7 meet the needs of the deaf, deafblind and hard of hearing communities. 8 2. The duties and responsibilities of the commission are to: 9 (a) create a statewide plan, including annual updates, to deliver 10 coordinated services and programs to persons who are deaf, deafblind and 11 hard of hearing in a manner that best serves their needs including but 12 not limited to accessibility, educational, medical, housing, and employ- 13 ment services. 18 (c) regularly maintain a website that serves as a clearinghouse of 19 information, resources and services for persons who are deaf, deafblind 20 and hard of hearing; 21 (d) create a referral system that receives complaints in matters 22 affecting the deaf, deafblind and hard of hearing communities and refers 23 such complaints to the appropriate agencies or organizations if neces- 24 sary or appropriate, provided however, if such complaint is a reportable 25 incident pursuant to article eleven of the social services law, it shall 26 be forwarded to the vulnerable persons' central register or if such 27 complaint is a criminal matter, it shall be forwarded to the appropriate 28 law enforcement agency; 29 (e) coordinate with federal, state and local agencies to improve 30 access to public safety and emergency services; 31 (f) develop strategies to increase employment opportunities for deaf, 32 deafblind and hard of hearing individuals including, but not limited to, 33 job training and career development services;
