MAY -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Investigations and Govern- ment Operations AN ACT to amend the public buildings law, in relation to induction loop listening systems The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 53 of the public buildings law, as amended by chap- 2 ter 23 of the laws of 1989, subdivision 1 as amended by chapter 221 of 3 the laws of 2018, is amended to read as follows: 4 § 53. [Assistive] Induction loop listening systems. 1. (a) All new 5 public buildings, construction of which commences after January first, 6 nineteen hundred ninety-one, [containing] which are or contain within an 7 [auditorium, theater, meeting hall, hearing room, amphitheater, or room 8 used in any similar capacity which are so designated by the appropriate 9 building and fire code] area of public assembly or service, shall have 10 equipped and installed an [assistive] induction loop listening system 11 for use by persons who are deaf or hard of hearing who require the use 12 of such a system to improve their reception of sound. 13 (b) The owner of any public building which is or contains within an 14 area of public assembly or service and has an induction loop listening 15 system installed and equipped shall post in a clear and conspicuous 16 manner permanently mounted signage to indicate to visitors that such 17 induction loop listening system is available. 18 (c) On a biennial basis, the owner of any public building which is or 19 contains within an area of public assembly or service and has an 20 induction loop listening system installed and equipped shall complete a 21 self-certification form and submit such form to the state fire 22 prevention and building code council, attesting that such induction loop 23 listening system continues to be functional. Standards for such systems shall be developed by the state fire 2 prevention and building code council upon receiving recommendations from 3 the advisory board on [assistive] induction loop listening systems in 4 places of public assembly. 5 3. For purposes of this section, the [term] following terms shall have 6 the following meanings: 7 (a) "[assistive] Induction loop listening system" shall mean [situa- 8 tional-personal acoustic communication equipment designed to improve the 9 transmission and auditory reception of sound] a hardwired assistive 10 listening system through which a loop of wire around an area of a build- 11 ing produces a signal, or a hardwired countertop version that produces a 12 signal, received directly by hearing aids and cochlear implants equipped 13 with telecoil features used by persons with hearing loss. 14 (b) "Area of public assembly or service" shall mean a building or 15 structure, or space within a building or structure, that is regularly 16 open for public gatherings, including an auditorium, theater, meeting 17 room or hall, courtroom, community center, amphitheater, library, phar- 18 macy counter, information desk at a medical facility, a waiting area for 19 a medical office, bank teller area, car rental business, restaurant, 20 bar, or other food or beverage counter service location, coat check 21 area, grocery store check-out area, ticket payment location, or any 22 other room used in any similar capacity which are so designated by the 23 appropriate building and fire code. 24 § 2. The state fire prevention and building code council shall update 25 and amend any applicable building and fire codes to carry out the 26 provisions of section 53 of the public buildings law. 27 § 3. This act shall take effect seven months after it shall have 28 become a law. Go to top Sitemap Request Information Directions and Maps
