S02657 Summary:

BILL NOS02657
 
SAME ASSAME AS A00334
 
SPONSORPARKER
 
COSPNSRCOONEY, JACKSON, LIU, MAY, WEBB
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Add §3615, Ed L
 
Establishes the bilingual teachers of tomorrow teacher recruitment and retention program to attract and retain bilingual, certified teachers in schools with a bilingual teacher shortage; provides for grants and procedures for applying for such grants and eligibility requirements for fund distribution.

        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  education  law, in relation to establishing the
          bilingual teachers  of  tomorrow  teacher  recruitment  and  retention
          program

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The education law is amended by adding a new  section  3615
     2  to read as follows:
     3    §  3615.  Bilingual  teachers  of  tomorrow  teacher  recruitment  and
     4  retention program. 1. Definitions. a. The term "school with a  bilingual
     5  teacher shortage" means a public school in which there was a shortage of
     6  bilingual, certified teachers in the previous school year and there is a
     7  projected  shortage  in  the  current  school  year as determined by the
     8  commissioner.
     9    b. The term "bilingual, certified teacher" means a teacher who holds a
    10  permanent or professional state teaching certificate appropriate to  the
    11  teaching  position,  including  the subject area if applicable, in which
    12  such teacher is to be employed and who is fully qualified to teach in  a
    13  bilingual education program.
    14    c.  The  term  "school  district"  shall  mean  a  common, union free,
    15  central, central high school, or city school district.
    16    d. The term "board of education" shall mean the governing board of any
    17  public school or public school district defined in paragraph c  of  this
    18  subdivision.
    19    e.  The  term  "year  of  service" shall mean ten months of continuous
    20  full-time service as a teacher during a school year, generally occurring
    21  between September first and June thirtieth.
    22    2. Application  and  funding.  a.  Grants  shall  be  made  to  school
    23  districts  for  bilingual,  certified  teacher recruitment and retention

     1  activities necessary to increase  the  number  of  bilingual,  certified
     2  teachers in schools with a bilingual teacher shortage.
     3    b. Such grants shall be awarded to school districts, within the limits
     4  of funds appropriated therefor, through a competitive process that takes
     5  into consideration the magnitude of any shortage of bilingual, certified
     6  teachers  in  the  school district, the fiscal capacity of the district,
     7  and the number of new bilingual, certified teachers the school  district
     8  intends  to  hire in the coming school year. Grants provided pursuant to
     9  this section shall be used only for  the  purposes  enumerated  in  this
    10  section.
    11    c. Not more than sixty percent of the funds allocated pursuant to this
    12  section shall be made available to any one school district.
    13    d.  Any school district receiving funds under this section shall main-
    14  tain any local effort in the current year equivalent to such  effort  in
    15  the  base  year  for  any  local  program  related to the recruitment or
    16  retention of teachers or any program that has purposes that are  similar
    17  to  any  purpose  of  this section or the district shall provide a local
    18  match to state funds received pursuant  to  this  section.  State  funds
    19  provided pursuant to this section shall not supplant local funds for any
    20  purpose similar to the purposes of this section.
    21    e.  Applications  by  a  school  district for funding pursuant to this
    22  section shall be filed with the commissioner, in a  form  determined  by
    23  the  commissioner,  by  June first of the base year and school districts
    24  shall be notified of their grant award by June  thirtieth  of  the  base
    25  year,  provided that in any year such application and award dates may be
    26  modified by the commissioner for the reasonable  administration  of  the
    27  program.  Such  application  shall include a plan by the school district
    28  for the expenditure of the funds in each category of allowable  expenses
    29  authorized  by  subdivision three of this section, and such expenditures
    30  shall be consistent with such plan and the provisions of this section.
    31    f. A person receiving an award pursuant to  any  subdivision  of  this
    32  section  shall not be eligible for an award pursuant to any other subdi-
    33  vision of this section in a school year.
    34    3.  Bilingual  teachers  of  tomorrow  teacher  recruitment  incentive
    35  program.    Bilingual teachers of tomorrow teacher recruitment incentive
    36  awards may be available to teachers pursuant to the provisions  of  this
    37  subdivision  to  attract teachers to employment in schools with a bilin-
    38  gual teacher shortage.
    39    a. Eligibility. To be eligible for an award pursuant to this  subdivi-
    40  sion, an applicant shall:
    41    (1) be a bilingual, certified teacher;
    42    (2)  agree  to a service obligation of one year of service in a school
    43  with a bilingual teacher shortage as a condition of receiving  an  award
    44  pursuant to this subdivision; and
    45    (3)  be  employed for the first time as a bilingual, certified teacher
    46  in the school district.
    47    b. Application and selection. (1) The board of education shall  deter-
    48  mine  the form of the application and the application process for teach-
    49  ers deemed eligible for the award, and shall in  its  discretion  select
    50  the  number of eligible applicants who will receive an award pursuant to
    51  this subdivision.
    52    (2) If the number of eligible applicants exceeds the number of  awards
    53  determined  by  the  board  pursuant to this subdivision, then the board
    54  shall use an objective competitive process based on  educational  quali-
    55  fications to determine which eligible applicants shall receive awards.

     1    c.  Payment  of  awards.  (1) Annual awards from state and local funds
     2  provided for this purpose shall be paid by the board of education to the
     3  award recipient. Award recipients shall be entitled  to  receive  awards
     4  pursuant  to the provisions of this subdivision and such awards shall be
     5  in addition to and not part of any award recipient's base salary.
     6    (2)  The  initial annual award shall be ten thousand dollars, and such
     7  initial award may be renewable each year, for  up  to  three  additional
     8  years,  provided  that  the recipient agrees to one year of service as a
     9  full-time bilingual, certified  teacher  in  schools  with  a  bilingual
    10  teacher  shortage  for  each  year  of  a renewed award and has provided
    11  satisfactory service in any prior year in which an  initial  or  renewed
    12  award  was  granted  to  the recipient. No awards shall be made for less
    13  than one school year of service and the sum of any initial  and  renewed
    14  awards shall not exceed forty thousand dollars.
    15    Awards  made  pursuant  to this subdivision shall be used to reimburse
    16  any educational costs, including those for which student loans may  have
    17  been  taken, incurred by the recipient prior to receipt of the award for
    18  certification as a teacher in the public schools of the state.
    19    (3) The board of education shall develop and  secure  from  the  award
    20  recipient  a  written  agreement  outlining the service obligation. Such
    21  agreement shall include provisions for the recipient to repay the amount
    22  of the award to the school district if the recipient fails  to  complete
    23  their  service  obligation in any year where an initial or renewed award
    24  is granted, and such repaid amounts shall be  deducted  from  any  grant
    25  amounts  due  to  the  district from the state pursuant to this section.
    26  Upon a written appeal by an award recipient, the board of education,  in
    27  its  discretion,  may  provide  a  waiver of repayment provisions for an
    28  award recipient who fails to  complete  their  service  requirement  for
    29  documented  reasons of health or other severe hardship, as determined by
    30  the commissioner.
    31    d. Notification. The board of education shall notify  both  successful
    32  and  unsuccessful  applicants  by September first of any school year for
    33  which an initial or renewed award is provided to  teachers  pursuant  to
    34  this subdivision.
    35    4.  Reporting.  By  November  first  following  the completion of each
    36  school year, the commissioner shall  report  to  the  governor  and  the
    37  legislature   regarding  the  bilingual  teachers  of  tomorrow  teacher
    38  recruitment and retention program. Such report shall list the amount  of
    39  each school district's total grant pursuant to this section, the uses of
    40  the  grant,  and  the  number of awards granted by type pursuant to this
    41  section, as well as an analysis of the effectiveness of the  program  in
    42  recruiting and retaining bilingual, certified teachers in schools with a
    43  bilingual teacher shortage in the state.
    44    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
