PARKER, COONEY, JACKSON, LIU, MAY -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Education AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to establishing the bilingual teachers of tomorrow teacher recruitment and retention program The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The education law is amended by adding a new section 3615 2 to read as follows: 3 § 3615. Bilingual teachers of tomorrow teacher recruitment and 4 retention program. 1. Definitions. a. The term "school with a bilingual 5 teacher shortage" means a public school in which there was a shortage of 6 bilingual, certified teachers in the previous school year and there is a 7 projected shortage in the current school year as determined by the 8 commissioner. 9 b. The term "bilingual, certified teacher" means a teacher who holds a 10 permanent or professional state teaching certificate appropriate to the 11 teaching position, including the subject area if applicable, in which 12 such teacher is to be employed and who is fully qualified to teach in a 13 bilingual education program. 14 c. The term "school district" shall mean a common, union free, 15 central, central high school, or city school district. 16 d. The term "board of education" shall mean the governing board of any 17 public school or public school district defined in paragraph c of this 18 subdivision. 19 e. The term "year of service" shall mean ten months of continuous 20 full-time service as a teacher during a school year, generally occurring 21 between September first and June thirtieth. 22 2. Application and funding. a. Grants shall be made to school 23 districts for bilingual, certified teacher recruitment and retention EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. Bilingual teachers of tomorrow teacher recruitment incentive 35 program. Bilingual teachers of tomorrow teacher recruitment incentive 36 awards may be available to teachers pursuant to the provisions of this 37 subdivision to attract teachers to employment in schools with a bilin- 38 gual teacher shortage. 39 a. Eligibility. To be eligible for an award pursuant to this subdivi- 40 sion, an applicant shall: 41 (1) be a bilingual, certified teacher; 42 (2) agree to a service obligation of one year of service in a school 43 with a bilingual teacher shortage as a condition of receiving an award 44 pursuant to this subdivision; and 45 (3) be employed for the first time as a bilingual, certified teacher 46 in the school district. 47 b. Application and selection. (1) The board of education shall deter- 48 mine the form of the application and the application process for teach- 49 ers deemed eligible for the award, and shall in its discretion select 50 the number of eligible applicants who will receive an award pursuant to 51 this subdivision. 52 (2) If the number of eligible applicants exceeds the number of awards 53 determined by the board pursuant to this subdivision, then the board 54 shall use an objective competitive process based on educational quali- 55 fications to determine which eligible applicants shall receive awards. Award recipients shall be entitled to receive awards 4 pursuant to the provisions of this subdivision and such awards shall be 5 in addition to and not part of any award recipient's base salary. 6 (2) The initial annual award shall be ten thousand dollars, and such 7 initial award may be renewable each year, for up to three additional 8 years, provided that the recipient agrees to one year of service as a 9 full-time bilingual, certified teacher in schools with a bilingual 10 teacher shortage for each year of a renewed award and has provided 11 satisfactory service in any prior year in which an initial or renewed 12 award was granted to the recipient. No awards shall be made for less 13 than one school year of service and the sum of any initial and renewed 14 awards shall not exceed forty thousand dollars. 15 Awards made pursuant to this subdivision shall be used to reimburse 16 any educational costs, including those for which student loans may have 17 been taken, incurred by the recipient prior to receipt of the award for 18 certification as a teacher in the public schools of the state. 19 (3) The board of education shall develop and secure from the award 20 recipient a written agreement outlining the service obligation. Such 21 agreement shall include provisions for the recipient to repay the amount 22 of the award to the school district if the recipient fails to complete 23 their service obligation in any year where an initial or renewed award 24 is granted, and such repaid amounts shall be deducted from any grant 25 amounts due to the district from the state pursuant to this section. 26 Upon a written appeal by an award recipient, the board of education, in 27 its discretion, may provide a waiver of repayment provisions for an 28 award recipient who fails to complete their service requirement for 29 documented reasons of health or other severe hardship, as determined by 30 the commissioner. 31 d. Notification. The board of education shall notify both successful 32 and unsuccessful applicants by September first of any school year for 33 which an initial or renewed award is provided to teachers pursuant to 34 this subdivision. 35 4. Reporting. By November first following the completion of each 36 school year, the commissioner shall report to the governor and the 37 legislature regarding the bilingual teachers of tomorrow teacher 38 recruitment and retention program. Such report shall list the amount of 39 each school district's total grant pursuant to this section, the uses of 40 the grant, and the number of awards granted by type pursuant to this 41 section, as well as an analysis of the effectiveness of the program in 42 recruiting and retaining bilingual, certified teachers in schools with a 43 bilingual teacher shortage in the state. 44 § 2.
