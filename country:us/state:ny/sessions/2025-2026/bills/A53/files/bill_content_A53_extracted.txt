The criminal procedure law is amended by adding a new 2 section 60.77 to read as follows: 3 § 60.77 Rules of evidence; admissibility of other crimes or acts. 4 1. In general, evidence of other crimes, wrongs, or acts is not admis- 5 sible to prove the character of a person in order to show action in 6 conformity therewith. It may, however, be admissible for other purposes, 7 such as proof of motive, opportunity, intent, preparation, plan, know- 8 ledge, state of mind of the defendant, state of mind of the victim or 9 other party, absence of mistake or accident, identity, modus operandi, 10 or common scheme or plan, provided that the prosecution in a criminal 11 case shall provide reasonable notice in advance of trial, or during 12 trial if the court excuses pretrial notice on good cause shown, of the 13 general nature of any such evidence it intends to introduce at trial. 14 2. In a criminal case in which the defendant is accused of a sexual 15 offense, evidence of the defendant's commission of another sexual 16 offense or offenses is admissible, and may be considered for its bearing 17 on any matter to which it is relevant. This rule shall not be construed EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
