LEVENBERG -- read once and referred to the Committee on Energy AN ACT to amend the public authorities law, in relation to enacting the "statewide building energy disclosure act" The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Short title. This act shall be known and may be cited as 2 the "statewide building energy disclosure act". 3 § 2. The public authorities law is amended by adding a new section 4 1886 to read as follows: 5 § 1886. Statewide building energy disclosure. 1. Definitions. For the 6 purposes of this section, the following terms shall have the following 7 meanings: 8 (a) "Building" means any structure in the state that consumes energy. 9 (b) "Gross floor area" means the total floor area contained within a 10 building, as determined pursuant to guidance by the authority. 11 (c) "Large building" means any building with a gross floor area of 12 twenty thousand square feet or greater. 13 (d) "Covered building" means a large building included on the annual 14 covered buildings list. 15 (e) "Covered buildings list" means the list issued annually by the 16 authority pursuant to subdivision two of this section. 17 (f) "Building owner" means the person or entity listed on the covered 18 buildings list as the responsible owner of a building. 19 (g) "Energy usage" means: 20 (i) for utilities, the amount of energy delivered to a covered build- 21 ing during the prior calendar year. 22 (ii) for building owners, all energy used at a covered building other 23 than energy used under subparagraph (i) of this paragraph, including but 24 not limited to delivered fuels, onsite generation, and other non-metered 25 sources. 26 (h) "Utility" means any gas corporation or electric corporation, as 27 such terms are defined by section two of the public service law, or any EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. Covered buildings list. (a) The authority shall issue an annual 8 covered buildings list identifying each covered building, its street 9 address, approximate gross floor area, primary use type, building owner 10 and business address, and any other information the authority determines 11 necessary. 12 (b) The authority shall notify building owners of their inclusion in 13 the covered buildings list. Failure to receive such notice shall not 14 relieve an owner of compliance obligations under this section. 15 (c) Newly constructed buildings shall be included in the covered 16 buildings list beginning with the first reporting year following issu- 17 ance of a certificate of occupancy. 18 (d) A building owner may request that multiple buildings be designated 19 and reported as a campus, subject to approval by the authority. 20 3. Utility reporting. (a) The public service commission shall promul- 21 gate regulations requiring utilities to provide annual building level 22 energy usage data for all covered buildings to the authority. Such util- 23 ity data shall be provided in a format and schedule established by the 24 public service commission in consultation with the authority. 25 (b) The public service commission may require utilities to use stand- 26 ardized data formats or platforms for transmission of the information 27 reported pursuant to paragraph (a) of this subdivision. 28 4. Building owner reporting. (a) The department of environmental 29 conservation shall promulgate regulations requiring building owners to 30 report all non-utility energy usage for each covered building. 31 (b) Building owner data shall be submitted to the authority in a form 32 and manner established by the department of environmental conservation 33 in consultation with the authority. 34 (c) A building owner may designate a reporting entity or a designated 35 building owner to act on its behalf. Such designation shall not relieve 36 the owner of legal responsibility for compliance. 37 (d) If a lessee or occupant is responsible for any portion of energy 38 usage, the building owner may request such usage from the lessee or 39 occupant. The regulations promulgated pursuant to paragraph (a) of this 40 subdivision shall specify timelines and required documentation for such 41 requests. 42 5. Required data elements. (a) The public service commission and the 43 department of environmental conservation shall require that all data 44 submitted pursuant to subdivisions three and four of this section 45 include, at a minimum: 46 (i) annual energy consumption by fuel type and meter, expressed in 47 units appropriate to each energy source; 48 (ii) the type of energy provided or consumed, including electricity, 49 natural gas, district steam, fuel oil, propane, on site generation, or 50 any other energy source identified by regulation; 51 (iii) building characteristics necessary for accurate reporting and 52 disclosure, including gross floor area, primary use type, and occupancy 53 information; and 54 (iv) any additional data elements necessary to support emissions 55 calculations or to ensure data quality, as determined by the authority A. 10530 3 1 in consultation with the public service commission and the department of 2 environmental conservation. 3 (b) The authority shall issue technical guidance specifying standard- 4 ized data definitions, units of measurement, and reporting formats. 5 6. Exemptions. (a) A building shall be exempt from reporting pursuant 6 to subdivision four of this section for any reporting year in which: 7 (i) it was vacant for the entire year; 8 (ii) it was demolished during such reporting year; 9 (iii) it was more than fifty percent vacant for such reporting year 10 due to circumstances beyond the control of the owner; 11 (iv) the owner is in bankruptcy or insolvency proceedings; or 12 (v) the department of environmental conservation determines that 13 special circumstances justify an exemption. 14 (b) The department of environmental conservation shall establish 15 procedures for requesting exemptions under this subdivision. 16 7. Disputes and corrections. (a) A building owner may dispute inclu- 17 sion on the covered buildings list or request corrections by submitting 18 evidence in a form established by the authority. 19 (b) Building owners may submit corrections to data provided by a util- 20 ity pursuant to subdivision three of this section to the authority 21 pursuant to procedures established by the department of environmental 22 conservation. 23 8. Public database and annual report. (a) The authority shall publish 24 an annual public database containing building level energy usage and 25 associated emissions calculations for each covered building. Such data- 26 base shall be searchable and sortable by municipality, zip code, build- 27 ing size, and use type, and shall include a map-based tool. 28 (b) The authority shall publish an annual report summarizing statewide 29 energy usage and emissions trends from covered buildings. 30 (c) No personally identifiable information relating to individuals 31 shall be published as part of the database under paragraph (a) of this 32 subdivision or the report under paragraph (b) of this subdivision, 33 except that an owner's or designee's name and business address may be 34 included. 35 9. Enforcement. (a) The public service commission shall enforce utili- 36 ty reporting obligations established under its regulations pursuant to 37 this section. Penalties for noncompliance shall be set by regulation and 38 may include daily fines. 39 (b) The department of environmental conservation shall enforce build- 40 ing owner reporting obligations established under its regulations pursu- 41 ant to this section. Penalties for noncompliance shall be set by regu- 42 lation and may include daily fines. 43 (c) The department of environmental conservation shall establish 44 penalties for lessees that fail to provide requested data when required 45 under regulation pursuant to this section. 46 (d) A building owner shall not pass any penalty assessed under this 47 section through to a lessee occupying less than five percent of the 48 building's gross floor area. 49 10. Inspection and audits. The department of environmental conserva- 50 tion may audit or inspect information submitted under this section and 51 may require supporting documentation for a period of five years follow- 52 ing any reporting year. The authority shall provide necessary data 53 access and cooperation to the department of environmental conservation. 54 11. Coordination with existing municipal benchmarking laws. (a) A 55 building owner shall be deemed in compliance with the reporting require- 56 ments of this section for any covered building located in a municipality A. 10530 4 1 that has adopted a local benchmarking law that requires annual energy 2 usage reporting and public disclosure, provided that: 3 (i) such building owner has submitted all required information to the 4 local jurisdiction in the form and manner required by such jurisdiction; 5 and 6 (ii) the authority has determined that such local benchmarking law is 7 at least as comprehensive as the requirements of this section with 8 respect to data elements, building coverage, and frequency of reporting. 9 (b) The authority, in consultation with the department of environ- 10 mental conservation and the public service commission, shall identify 11 municipalities with approved local benchmarking laws and shall publish a 12 list of such municipalities on its website. A municipality may request 13 approval by submitting its local law and reporting procedures to the 14 authority. 15 (c) For a covered building located in a municipality with an approved 16 local benchmarking law, a building owner may satisfy the reporting obli- 17 gations of this section by providing the authority with a certificate of 18 compliance or equivalent documentation issued by the local jurisdiction, 19 or by authorizing the local jurisdiction to transmit required data 20 directly to the authority. 21 (d) The authority may enter into written agreements with local juris- 22 dictions for the purpose of receiving data submissions, coordinating 23 data standards, aligning reporting deadlines, and avoiding duplication 24 of reporting requirements. Such agreements may include procedures for 25 data sharing, data quality control, and resolution of discrepancies. 26 (e) Nothing in this subdivision shall prevent the department of envi- 27 ronmental conservation or the public service commission from enforcing 28 compliance with their respective regulations if a building owner fails 29 to comply with the local benchmarking law or fails to provide documenta- 30 tion sufficient to demonstrate compliance. 31 12.
