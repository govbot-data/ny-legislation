ROSENTHAL -- read once and referred to the Committee on Health AN ACT to amend the public health law in relation to establishing a task force to handle preventive care in the state of New York if federal guidelines are changed or rescinded The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The public health law is amended by adding a new section 2 2804-b to read as follows: 3 § 2804-b. Preventive care task force. 1. (a) A state task force is 4 hereby created on clinical preventive practice guidelines and medical 5 technology assessment which shall consist of sixteen members. At least 6 half of the membership shall be physicians specializing in the fields of 7 behavioral health, family medicine, geriatrics, internal medicine, 8 pediatrics, obstetrics and gynecology, and nursing. Appointments shall 9 be made as follows: six shall be appointed by the governor; four shall 10 be appointed by the temporary president of the senate; four shall be 11 appointed by the speaker of the assembly; and one each shall be 12 appointed by the minority leaders of the senate and assembly. The 13 commissioner shall serve as an ex officio member of the task force. The 14 membership of the task force shall appoint a chairperson who shall be 15 selected by a majority vote of the task force membership. 16 (b) Task force members shall serve four year terms. 17 2. For the purposes of this section, the following terms shall have 18 the following meanings: (a) "clinical practice guidelines" shall mean 19 systematically developed statements to assist physician and patient 20 decisions about appropriate health care for specific clinical circum- 21 stances and accompanying preventive care; and 22 (b) "medical technology" shall mean an instrument or unit of equipment 23 or technique for use as a health-related treatment, testing or diagnos- 24 tic tool.
