        Introduced  by M. of A. DILAN -- read once and referred to the Committee

        AN ACT to amend the correction law, in relation  to  employment  of  sex
          offenders  in  a  position involving substantial contact with children
          while dressed as a children's character

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Section 168-w of the correction law, as renumbered by chap-
     2  ter 604 of the laws of 2005, is renumbered 168-x and a new section 168-w
     3  is added to read as follows:
     4    §  168-w.  Employment in a position involving substantial contact with
     5  children dressed as a children's character. 1.  No  sex  offender  shall
     6  apply  for  or accept a position which involves substantial contact with
     7  children while said person is dressed as a  children's  character.  Such
     8  position shall include any job, task or occupation, which by its nature,
     9  requires  a  person  to  be  dressed  as  a  children's character and in
    10  substantial contact with children in the regular  performance  of  their
    11  duties  or  dealings  in such position. This section shall also apply to
    12  any person seeking a permit or permission to  execute  any  activity  or
    13  performance while dressed as a children's character that would present a
    14  direct contact with children.
    15    2. As used in this section, the following terms shall have the follow-
    16  ing meanings:
    17    a. "Position" shall apply to any person seeking employment either paid
    18  or  unpaid,  any  person  seeking  to volunteer, or any person seeking a
    19  permit or permission that would present a substantial contact with chil-
    20  dren while dressed as a children's character.
    21    b. "Children's character" shall mean any costume worn by a  person  to
    22  depict  a  specific  character  to children and shall include but not be
    23  limited to:
    24    i. legendary figures such as Santa Claus or the Easter bunny;

     1    ii. mythical characters; and
     2    iii.  any such similar children's character that a person may dress as
     3  for a position which involves substantial contact with children.
     4    c. "Substantial contact with children" shall mean allowing children to
     5  sit on such person dressed as a character's  lap,  having  photos  taken
     6  with  children while such person is dressed as a children's character or
     7  any other activity that involves physical contact  with  children  while
     8  such person is dressed as a children's character.
     9    3. Examples of such employment shall include, but shall not be limited
    10  to, being dressed as a children's character at:
    11    a.  Any  shopping  mall or shopping center event specifically targeted
    12  toward children interacting with such  a  character,  such  as  but  not
    13  limited to photos with Santa;
    14    b.  Any  store  event  that  is  specifically targeted toward children
    15  interacting with such a character;
    16    c. Any such similar event that  promotes  activities  targeted  toward
    17  children interacting with such a character.
    18    4.  Employers, organizations and government entities shall have access
    19  to the statewide central registry of child abuse and  maltreatment,  the
    20  New  York  state  sex offender registry, the criminal history repository
    21  with the division of criminal justice services,  the  national  criminal
    22  repository with the federal bureau of investigation and the national sex
    23  offender  registry  using  the  national  crime  and information center,
    24  established under the Adam Walsh child protection and safety act of 2006
    25  (42 U.S.C. 16901 et seq) for the  purpose  of  performing  a  background
    26  check  for  any  convictions of sexual abuse of a child. Every employer,
    27  organization and government entity shall check any  potential  employees
    28  seeking  to  assume  a position that will be in substantial contact with
    29  children dressed as a children's character against the statewide central
    30  registry of child abuse and maltreatment, the New York state sex  offen-
    31  der registry, the criminal history repository with the division of crim-
    32  inal justice services, the national criminal repository with the federal
    33  bureau of investigation and the national sex offender registry using the
    34  national  crime and information center, established under the Adam Walsh
    35  child protection and safety act of 2006 (42  U.S.C.  16901  et  seq)  to
    36  ascertain if said person has a conviction for sexual abuse of a child.
    37    5.  a.  Any  sex  offender  who  applies  for or accepts employment in
    38  violation of this section shall be guilty of a class A misdemeanor  upon
    39  the first conviction thereof, and upon a second or subsequent conviction
    40  thereof shall be guilty of a class D felony.
    41    b.  Any  employer who knowingly employs a sex offender in violation of
    42  this section shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a class A misdemeanor.
    43    § 2. This act shall take effect on the first of November next succeed-
    44  ing the date on which it shall have become a law.
