SKOUFIS, HOYLMAN-SIGAL -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Civil Service and Pensions AN ACT to amend the civil service law, in relation to prohibiting elected officials from collecting retirement while still in an elected position The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 150 of the civil service law, as amended by chapter 2 211 of the laws of 1995, is amended to read as follows: 3 § 150. Suspension of pension and annuity during public employment. 4 Except as otherwise provided by sections one hundred one, two hundred 5 eleven, and two hundred twelve of the retirement and social security 6 law, section five hundred three of the education law, and except as now 7 provided by any local law or charter, if any person subsequent to [his 8 or her] their retirement from the civil service of the state or of any 9 municipal corporation or political subdivision of the state, shall 10 accept any office, position or employment in the civil service of the 11 state or of any municipal corporation or political subdivision of the 12 state to which any salary or emolument is attached, except jury duty or 13 the office of inspector of election, poll clerk or ballot clerk under 14 the election law, or the office of notary public or commissioner of 15 deeds, [or an elective public office,] any pension or annuity awarded or 16 allotted to [him or her] them upon retirement, and payable by the state, 17 by such municipal corporation or political subdivision, or out of any 18 fund established by or pursuant to law, shall be suspended during such 19 service or employment and while such person is receiving any salary or 20 emolument therefor except reimbursement for traveling expenses. 21 [Notwithstanding the foregoing, if any person, subsequent to his or her 22 retirement from an elective public office, accepts appointment, is re-e- 23 lected or takes a new oath of office to the same elective public office EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
