WEIK -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Higher Education AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to establishing the New York nurse expansion program The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The education law is amended by adding a new section 669-i 2 to read as follows: 3 § 669-i. New York nurse expansion program. 1. Undergraduate students 4 who are matriculated in an approved undergraduate program leading to a 5 career in nursing at a New York state public or private degree granting 6 institution of higher education are eligible for the New York nurse 7 expansion program, provided the applicant: (a) graduates from a high 8 school located in New York state during or after the two thousand twen- 9 ty-five--two thousand twenty-six school year; (b) graduates within the 10 top thirty-three percent of their high school class; (c) enrolls in 11 full-time study each term beginning in the fall term after their high 12 school graduation in an approved undergraduate nursing program, as 13 defined by the corporation, at a New York state institution of higher 14 education; (d) signs a contract with the corporation agreeing that their 15 award will be converted to a student loan in the event the student fails 16 to comply with the terms of this program as set forth in subdivision 17 four of this section; and (e) complies with the applicable provisions of 18 this article and all requirements promulgated by the corporation for the 19 administration of the program. 20 2. Awards shall be granted beginning with the two thousand twenty-six- 21 -two thousand twenty-seven academic year and thereafter to applicants at 22 New York state public or private degree granting institutions of higher 23 education that the corporation has determined are eligible to receive 24 such awards. The corporation shall grant such awards in an amount equal 25 to the amount of undergraduate tuition for residents of New York state EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. Upon notification of an award 14 under this program, the institution shall defer the amount of tuition 15 equal to the award. No award shall be final until the recipient's 16 successful completion of a term has been certified by the institution. 17 3. An eligible recipient shall not receive an award for more than four 18 academic years of full-time undergraduate study or five academic years 19 if the program of study normally requires five years, excluding any 20 allowable interruption of study. 21 4. The corporation shall convert to a student loan the full amount of 22 the award given pursuant to this section, plus interest, according to a 23 schedule to be determined by the corporation if: (a) a recipient fails 24 to complete an approved undergraduate nursing program; or (b) upon 25 completion of such undergraduate degree program a recipient fails to 26 either (i) complete five years of continuous full-time employment in the 27 field of nursing related to their studies with a public or private enti- 28 ty located within New York state, or (ii) maintain residency in New York 29 state for such period of employment; or (c) a recipient fails to respond 30 to requests by the corporation for the status of their academic or 31 professional progress. The terms and conditions of this subdivision 32 shall be deferred for individuals who graduate with a degree in an 33 approved undergraduate program and enroll on at least a half-time basis 34 in a graduate or higher degree program or other professional licensure 35 degree program until they are conferred a degree, and shall also be 36 deferred for any interruption in undergraduate study or employment as 37 established by the rules and regulations of the corporation. Any obli- 38 gation to comply with such provisions as outlined in this section shall 39 be cancelled upon the death of the recipient. Notwithstanding any 40 provisions of this subdivision to the contrary, the corporation is 41 authorized to promulgate rules and regulations to provide for the waiver 42 or suspension of any financial obligation which would involve extreme 43 hardship. 44 5. The corporation is authorized to promulgate rules and regulations, 45 and may promulgate emergency regulations, necessary for the implementa- 46 tion of the provisions of this section, including, but not limited to, 47 the rate of interest charged for repayment of the student loan and the 48 criteria for distributing the awards. 49 § 2. This act shall take effect on the sixtieth day after it shall 50 have become a law. Effective immediately, the addition, amendment and/or 51 repeal of any rule or regulation necessary for the implementation of 52 this act on its effective date are authorized to be made and completed 53 on or before such date.
