AN ACT in relation to authorizing the city of Little Falls, in the coun-
ty of Herkimer, to finance certain deficits by the issuance of bonds
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Short title. This act shall be known and may be cited as
2 the "city of Little Falls fiscal recovery act".
3 § 2. The city of Little Falls, county of Herkimer, is hereby author-
4 ized to issue serial bonds, subject to the provisions of section 10.10
5 of the local finance law, on or before October 1, 2026, in an aggregate
6 principal amount not to exceed three million four hundred thousand
7 dollars ($3,400,000) for the specific object or purpose of liquidating
8 deficits in its general, golf, and water funds expected at the close of
9 its fiscal year ending December 31, 2025, including any remaining prin-
10 cipal amount of any budget, deficiency, and/or revenue anticipation
11 notes outstanding at the time of such issuance of such serial bonds or
12 notes authorized pursuant to this act. In anticipation of the issuance
13 and sale of such serial bonds, bond anticipation notes are hereby
14 authorized to be issued.
15 § 3. Notwithstanding any provision of this act to the contrary, the
16 city of Little Falls shall not issue any bonds for the purpose of liqui-
17 dating the deficits described in section two of this act unless and
18 until the state comptroller shall first review and confirm the existence
19 of such deficits, as well as certify the amounts of such deficits. The
20 city of Little Falls shall prepare a report detailing the amount and
21 cause of such deficits and submit such report together with such city's
22 independent audit report for its last completed fiscal year, if any such
23 audit report has been or is to be prepared, and such other information
24 as the state comptroller may deem necessary, to the state comptroller,

20 § 4. It is hereby determined that the financing of the deficits
21 hereinbefore described is a purpose of the city of Little Falls for
22 which indebtedness may be incurred, the period of probable usefulness of
23 which is hereby determined to be ten years, computed from the date of
24 such bonds or from the date of the first bond anticipation note issued
25 in anticipation of the sale of such bonds, whichever date is earlier.
26 Such bonds and/or bond anticipation notes shall be general obligations
27 of the city of Little Falls to which the faith and credit of the city is
28 pledged, and the city shall make an annual appropriation sufficient to
29 pay the principal of and interest on such obligations as the same shall
30 become due.
31 § 5. (a) For each fiscal year occurring during the time bonds and/or
32 bond anticipation notes issued pursuant to this act are outstanding, the
33 mayor of the city of Little Falls shall prepare a quarterly report of
34 summarized budget data depicting overall trends of actual revenues and
35 budget expenditures for the entire budget rather than individual line
36 items. Such budgetary reports shall compare revenue estimates and appro-
37 priations as set forth in such budget with the actual revenues and
38 expenditures made to date. All such quarterly budgetary reports shall be
39 accompanied by recommendations of the mayor setting forth any remedial
40 action necessary or desirable to resolve any unfavorable budget variance
41 including the over-estimation of revenues and the under-estimation of
42 expenditures. Each such quarterly budgetary report shall be completed
43 within thirty days after the end of each quarter and shall be submitted
44 to each member of the common council of the city of Little Falls, the
45 director of the division of the budget, the state comptroller, and the
46 chairs of the senate finance committee and the assembly ways and means
47 committee.
48 (b) For each fiscal year occurring during the time bonds and/or bond
49 anticipation notes issued pursuant to this act are outstanding, the
50 mayor of the city of Little Falls shall also prepare a quarterly trial
51 balance of general and special fund ledger accounts. Each such quarterly
52 trial balance shall be prepared in accordance with generally accepted
53 accounting principles. Each such quarterly trial balance shall be
54 completed within thirty days after the end of each quarterly period and
55 shall be submitted to each member of the common council of the city of
56 Little Falls, the director of the division of the budget, the state

28 § 6. Severability clause. If any clause, sentence, paragraph, subdivi-

30 competent jurisdiction to be invalid, such judgment shall not affect,
31 impair, or invalidate the remainder thereof, but shall be confined in

33 or part thereof directly involved in the controversy in which such judg-
34 ment shall have been rendered. It is hereby the intent of the legisla-
