AN ACT to amend the public service law, in relation to prohibiting
termination of residential utility service during forecasts of extreme
temperature
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Paragraph (c) of subdivision 3 of section 32 of the public
2 service law, as added by chapter 713 of the laws of 1981, is amended to
3 read as follows:
4 (c) Special procedures for cold weather periods. [(i) The commission
5 shall establish procedures to be followed by a utility or municipality
6 supplying heat related service in cold weather periods. Such procedures
7 shall be designed to identify and assist, prior to termination of
8 service, those residents who may suffer serious impairment to health or
9 safety as a result of any such termination. The commission shall estab-
10 lish the applicable cold weather periods; specify criteria for identify-
11 ing residents who are likely to suffer serious impairments, and require
12 that such service not be terminated unless a representative of the util-
13 ity or municipality makes a diligent effort to contact by telephone or
14 in person an adult resident of the customer's premises at least seven-
15 ty-two hours prior to termination, makes a personal visit at the time of
16 termination and provides the customer with information regarding the
17 protections available under this article. The commission shall provide
18 for the manner in which such contacts and personal visits are made.
19 (ii)] The commission shall [also] require a utility or municipality
20 supplying service to continue service to customers where a serious

1 impairment to health or safety is likely to result from termination of
2 service and the person supplied is unable because of mental or physical
3 problems to manage [his or her] their own resources or to protect
4 [himself or herself] themself from neglect or hazardous situations with-
5 out the assistance of others. Doubts shall be resolved in favor of
6 continued service. Continuations of service shall be for a period of
7 time to be established by the commission. The commission shall consult
8 with the department of social services and the state office for the
9 aging in implementing the provisions of this paragraph.
10 § 2. Section 32 of the public service law is amended by adding a new
11 subdivision 3-a to read as follows:
12 3-a. (a) For the purposes of this subdivision, the term "forecast of
13 extreme temperature" means a national weather service forecast of ninety
14 degrees Fahrenheit or above on the national weather service heat index
15 (apparent temperature) during any time of day or night or thirty-two
16 degrees Fahrenheit or below during any time of day or night.
17 (b) A utility or municipality supplying residential heat or electric-
18 ity shall not terminate such heat or electricity the day of a forecast
19 of extreme temperature, or if such forecast of extreme temperature
20 precedes a holiday or weekend day, on any day during such holiday or
21 weekend day.
22 (c) Nothing in this subdivision shall be interpreted or otherwise
23 construed as preempting settlements filed with the commission or
24 department that are more strict than those contained in this subdivi-
25 sion.
26 § 3. This act shall take effect immediately.
