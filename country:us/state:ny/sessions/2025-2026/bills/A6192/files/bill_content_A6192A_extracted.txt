AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law, in relation to the
management of PFAS in biosolids and establishing the New York state
biosolids task force; to amend the agriculture and markets law and the
state finance law, in relation to establishing the PFAS agricultural
response program and the agricultural PFAS response fund; and provid-
ing for the repeal of certain provisions upon expiration thereof
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:

8 § 27-0801. Definitions.
9 As used in this title:
10 1. "Biosolids" means the accumulated semi-solids, solids or liquids
11 resulting from treatment of wastewaters from publicly or privately owned
12 or operated sewage treatment plants.
13 2. "Enterprise budget" means an estimation of the revenue, costs, and
14 profits for a farm.
15 3. "Monitoring" means sampling of biosolids from wastewater treatment
16 facilities, soil samples and/or water samples from agricultural land to
17 determine the concentration of PFAS present.

13 § 27-0803. Moratorium on land application of biosolids.
14 1. For the period commencing on the effective date of this title and
15 ending five years after such date, a moratorium shall be established on
16 the land application of:
17 (a) biosolids generated from a publicly or privately owned or operated
18 wastewater treatment plant;
19 (b) compost material that included in its production biosolids gener-
20 ated from a publicly or privately owned or operated wastewater treatment
21 plant;
22 (c) any other product or material that is intended for use as a ferti-
23 lizer, soil amendment, topsoil replacement or mulch, or for other simi-
24 lar agricultural purposes including parks, golf courses, or other non-
25 crop land applications, that is derived from or contains biosolids
26 generated from a publicly or privately owned or operated wastewater
27 treatment plant.
28 2. The moratorium shall apply to all existing permits, renewals, and
29 new applications.
30 3. The moratorium shall not apply to:
31 (a) the disposal or placement at a solid waste landfill of any of the
32 materials that are prohibited from application, spreading, sale or
33 distribution by this section; or
34 (b) the land application of or the sale or distribution of compost
35 materials or other agricultural products or materials derived from or
36 containing residuals generated as a result of the processing or culti-
37 vation of food, food waste, crops or vegetative material, or any other
38 product or material that is not derived from or does not contain bioso-
39 lids.
40 § 27-0805. Testing and reporting.
41 1. The department shall promulgate rules and regulations which add
42 perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances as a parameter of concern
43 for analysis within NYCRR Part 361.2.4.
44 (a) Within one year of the effective date of this title, the depart-
45 ment shall promulgate regulations establishing a perfluoroalkyl and
46 polyfluoroalkyl substances biosolids testing and reporting program that
47 shall, at a minimum, do the following:
48 (i) establish protocols for soil testing, requiring all permit holders
49 and previous permit holders to test lands where biosolids have been
50 applied at least quarterly for one year;
51 (ii) establish protocols for testing drinking water wells at least
52 quarterly for one year where such wells are within one half mile of
53 lands where biosolids have been applied;
54 (iii) establish protocols for testing surface water at least quarterly
55 for one year where such surface water is within one mile of lands where
56 biosolids have been applied;

48 biosolids for PFAS funded with public monies with New York state.

19 § 27-0601. New York State biosolids task force.
20 There is hereby established within the department the New York state
21 biosolids task force to evaluate the risks and benefits of various meth-
22 ods of biosolids disposal within the state with respect to the risks
23 posed by PFAS chemicals and to investigate a path forward for New York
24 state biosolid disposal that is maximally protective of human and
25 ecosystem health.
26 § 27-0603. Definitions.
27 As used in this title:
28 1. "Biosolids" means the accumulated semi-solids or solids resulting
29 from treatment of wastewaters from publicly or privately owned or oper-
30 ated sewage treatment plants.
31 2. "Enterprise budget" means an estimation of the planning and esti-
32 mation of revenue, costs, and profits for a farm.
33 3. "Monitoring" means sampling of biosolids from wastewater treatment
34 facilities, soil samples and/or groundwater samples from agricultural
35 land to determine the concentration of PFAS present.
36 4. "Perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances" or "PFAS" means a
37 class of fluorinated organic chemicals containing at least one fully
38 fluorinated carbon atom.
39 5. "Previous permit holder" means a permit holder who has a permit
40 that has expired within the five years previous to the effective date of
41 this article.
42 6. "Wastewater treatment facility" means any facility that treats
43 wastewater, including but not limited to municipal sewage treatment
44 plants, industrial wastewater treatment plants, and septage treatment
45 facilities.

53 (e) three members appointed by the governor as follows:
54 (i) one of whom shall represent wastewater treatment utilities;

22 § 27-0607. Powers and duties.
23 1. The task force shall:
24 (a) Review the draft "Sewage Sludge Risk Assessment for Perfluoroocta-
25 noic Acid (PFOA) CASRN 335-67-1 and Perfluorooctane Sulfonic Acid (PFOS)
26 CASRN 1763-23-1", dated January two thousand twenty-five, and monitor
27 comments provided to the United States environmental protection agency
28 and any revisions to the risk analysis;
29 (b) Analyze the pathways of human exposure to PFAS and how biosolids
30 applied to land contribute to such exposure;
31 (c) Support the department in any regulatory processes related to PFAS
32 in biosolids, prioritizing efficient processes and applying the best
33 available science;
34 (d) Evaluate mitigation strategies to reduce the risk of human,
35 ecosystem, and wildlife exposure from land application of biosolids;
36 (e) Review and summarize, including but not limited to the actions of
37 other states and regional and federal agencies to understand the risks
38 and consequences of such actions, the use and effects of PFAS and bioso-
39 lids, including, but not limited to:
40 (i) Continued land application;
41 (ii) Allowances for and use of biosolid derived fertilizer products;
42 (iii) Land application bans;
43 (iv) Landfilling;
44 (v) Incineration; and
45 (vi) Mitigation of PFAS through industrial pretreatment programs and
46 source identification;
47 (f) Work with the department to summarize current data and provide
48 recommendations, including recommendations for where more data may be
49 required;
50 (g) Review existing standards for PFAS contamination within soil,
51 water, air, and biosolids, both in New York and at the federal level;
52 (h) Review possible PFAS remediation strategies for PFAS contamination
53 within soil, water, air, and biosolids;
54 (i) Review and summarize the remaining capacity of landfills and
55 incineration facilities to accept biosolids. Such review shall consider
56 the impact across the different regions within the state and environ-
