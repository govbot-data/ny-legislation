WIEDER -- read once and referred to the Committee on Local Governments AN ACT in relation to dissolving the Rockland county drainage agency; and to repeal chapter 846 of the laws of 1975, relating to creating a stream control act for the county of Rock- land and defining its powers and duties The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. 1. Dissolution of the Rockland county drainage agency. The 2 Rockland county drainage agency is hereby dissolved and discontinued, 3 and all functions, duties, debts, and obligations are hereby transferred 4 to the Rockland county highway department in accordance with the 5 provisions of this act. 6 2. Transfer of functions. All of the functions and powers possessed 7 by, and all of the obligations and duties of the Rockland county drain- 8 age agency are transferred and assigned to, assumed by and devolved upon 9 the Rockland county highway department, in accordance with and pursuant 10 to the provisions of this act. 11 3. Transfer of employees. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, 12 rule, or regulation to the contrary, upon the transfer of functions from 13 the Rockland county drainage agency pursuant to this act, all employees 14 of the Rockland county drainage agency shall be transferred to the Rock- 15 land county highway department. Employees transferred pursuant to this 16 section shall be transferred without further examination or qualifica- 17 tion and shall retain their respective civil service classifications, 18 status and collective bargaining unit designations and collective 19 bargaining agreements. 20 4. Transfer of records. All books, papers, and property of the Rock- 21 land county drainage agency shall be provided to and deemed to be in the 22 possession of the Rockland county highway department. 23 5. Continuity of authority. For the purpose of succession of all func- 24 tions, powers, duties and obligations transferred and assigned to, 25 devolved upon and assumed by it pursuant to this act, the Rockland coun- EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
