S04690 Summary:

BILL NOS04690
 
SAME ASSAME AS A03933
 
SPONSORCLEARE
 
COSPNSR

Add §217-a, Transp L
 
Relates to human trafficking awareness and training for public transit employees; mandates displaying human trafficking resource materials created by the state public transportation safety board and the department of transportation in all public transit modes of transportation.

        AN ACT to amend the transportation law, in relation to human trafficking
          awareness and training  for  public  transit  employees  and  mandated
          displayed  materials created by the state public transportation safety
          board and the department of transportation
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The transportation law is amended by adding a new section
     2  217-a to read as follows:
     3    § 217-a. Human trafficking awareness and training. 1.  Human traffick-
     4  ing recognition training program.   (a) For purposes  of  this  section,
     5  "public  transit" shall mean any mode of transportation that is publicly
     6  available in the state and can accommodate for more than  eight  passen-
     7  gers or is funded by the state or any other local municipality.
     8    (b)  Every  public  transit system shall require all employees who are
     9  likely to interact or come into contact with guests to undergo  a  human
    10  trafficking  recognition  training  program  to  provide training in the
    11  recognition of a human trafficking victim as  defined  in  section  four
    12  hundred  eighty-three-aa  of  the  social  services  law.  Such training
    13  program shall be established or approved by  the  division  of  criminal
    14  justice  services  and the office of temporary and disability assistance
    15  in consultation with the New York state interagency task force on  human
    16  trafficking.  The training program may be developed by a federal, state,
    17  or non-profit organization, and may  be  incorporated  as  part  of  the
    18  public transit system's existing training programs or may be provided by
    19  organizations  or  providers identified by the commissioner of the divi-
    20  sion of criminal justice services or the commissioner of the  office  of
    21  temporary and disability assistance, provided that the training includes
    22  all  of the requirements of this section. Established or approved train-
    23  ing programs may be made available through methods  including,  but  not

     1  limited  to,  in-person instruction, electronic and video communication,
     2  or online programs.
     3    (c)  Any human trafficking recognition training program established or
     4  approved by the division of criminal justice services and the office  of
     5  temporary  and  disability  assistance in consultation with the New York
     6  state interagency task force on human trafficking as  required  in  this
     7  section shall address no less than the following issues:
     8    (i) the nature of human trafficking;
     9    (ii) how human trafficking is defined in law;
    10    (iii) how to identify victims of human trafficking;
    11    (iv) relief and recovery options for survivors; and
    12    (v) social and legal services available to victims.
    13    (d)  The commissioner of the division of criminal justice services and
    14  the commissioner of the office of temporary  and  disability  assistance
    15  shall make available a list of established or approved human trafficking
    16  recognition programs for use by a public transit system.
    17    (e)  All  new employees required to receive human trafficking recogni-
    18  tion training shall receive such training within their  first  month  of
    19  employment.
    20    (f)  The  training  shall  take  place at a location determined by the
    21  public transit system and shall be considered compensable time.
    22    2. Record keeping requirements of human trafficking recognition train-
    23  ing. Every keeper of each public transit system shall  maintain  records
    24  indicating  that  each  employee  required  to undergo an established or
    25  approved human trafficking recognition training program pursuant to this
    26  section has completed such training.  Such records shall be kept on file
    27  by the public transit system for the period during which the employee is
    28  employed by the public transit  system  and  for  one  year  after  such
    29  employment ends.
    30    3. Human trafficking prevention materials determined by the department
    31  in collaboration with the board shall be displayed in all public transit
    32  modes  of  transportation.  Such materials may include, but shall not be
    33  limited to, the human trafficking assistance hotline and where  to  find
    34  online or local resources.
    35    §  2. This act shall take effect one year after it shall have become a
    36  law; provided however that all applicable current employees of a  public
    37  transit  system  on  the  effective date of this act shall receive human
    38  trafficking recognition training within four  months  of  the  effective
    39  date of this act.
