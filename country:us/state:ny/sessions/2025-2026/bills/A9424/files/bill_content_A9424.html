        AN ACT to amend the New York state urban development corporation act, in
          relation to authorizing municipalities to purchase private residential
          real property located in a floodplain

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1. Section 1 of chapter 174 of the laws of 1968, constituting
     2  the New York state urban development  corporation  act,  is  amended  by
     3  adding a new section 15-a to read as follows:
     4    §  15-a. Special provisions relating to private residential real prop-
     5  erty located in a floodplain. (1) Notwithstanding any provision  of  law
     6  to  the contrary, but subject to any agreement with noteholders or bond-
     7  holders, any city, town or village  is  hereby  authorized  to  purchase
     8  private residential real property which is located in a floodplain.
     9    (2)  Notwithstanding  any provision of law to the contrary, any struc-
    10  ture which is located on private residential real property located in  a
    11  floodplain which is purchased by a city, town or village shall be demol-
    12  ished.
    13    (3)  Notwithstanding  any  provision  of  law to the contrary, private
    14  residential real property acquired pursuant to  this  section  shall  be
    15  deemed  public  property  and  such  property shall be dedicated as open
    16  space.
    17    (4) A city, town or village may offer to purchase private  residential
    18  real  property  which  is located in a floodplain for an amount up to or
    19  equal to the fair market value of such property as such property  is  or
    20  was valued prior to any flood damage.
    21    (5) A city, town, or village which elects to purchase private residen-
    22  tial  real property which is located in a floodplain shall pay all costs
    23  typically associated with the sale of private residential real property,
    24  including, but not limited to, fees for an appraisal, title  search,  or

     1  lot  survey and any associated closing costs. The seller of such private
     2  residential real property shall be responsible for the  payment  of  any
     3  mortgage,  lien or other financial encumbrance on such property, as well
     4  as  for  any moving costs or costs associated with renting or buying new
     5  property.
     6    (6) No owner of private residential real property which is located  in
     7  a  floodplain  shall  be  required  to  relinquish  their real property;
     8  provided, however, that owners of such private residential real property
     9  may need to take risk mitigation measures, such as elevating any  struc-
    10  tures on such property.
    11    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
