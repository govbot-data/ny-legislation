JACKSON -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Education AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to mandatory continuing education for teachers relating to immigrant focused trauma-informed practices The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Subdivision 2 of section 3006-a of the education law, as 2 added by section 2 of subpart C of part EE of chapter 56 of the laws of 3 2015, is amended to read as follows: 4 2. a. During each five-year registration period beginning on or after 5 July first, two thousand sixteen, an applicant for registration shall 6 successfully complete a minimum of one hundred hours of continuing 7 teacher and leader education, as defined by the commissioner. The 8 department shall issue rigorous standards for courses, programs, and 9 activities, that shall qualify as continuing teacher and leader educa- 10 tion pursuant to this section. For purposes of this section, a peer 11 review teacher, or a principal acting as an independent trained evalu- 12 ator, conducting a classroom observation as part of the teacher evalu- 13 ation system pursuant to section three thousand twelve-d of this article 14 may credit such time towards [his or her] their continuing teacher and 15 leader effectiveness requirements. 16 b. (i) Such continuing teacher and leader education required pursuant 17 to this section shall include, but not be limited to, a minimum of ten 18 hours of continuing education courses, programs and activities related 19 to immigrant focused trauma-informed practices to support students who 20 are dealing with adverse childhood or adolescent experiences as a result 21 of immigration status.
