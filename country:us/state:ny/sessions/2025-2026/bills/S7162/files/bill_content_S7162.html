Relates to the use of moneys received from foreign fire insurance companies by the Bohemia Volunteer Firefighter's Benevolent Association, and to modernization of certain language by making such language gender neutral.

        AN ACT to amend chapter 45 of the laws of 1963 relating to incorporating
          the Bohemia Volunteer Firemen's Benevolent Association, and  providing
          for  its  powers  and duties, in relation to the use of certain moneys
          received by such benevolent association and modernization  of  certain
          language
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Sections 1, 2, 6 and 7 of chapter 45 of the  laws  of  1963
     2  relating  to  incorporating  the  Bohemia Volunteer Firemen's Benevolent
     3  Association, and providing for its powers and  duties,  are  amended  to
     4  read as follows:
     5    Section  1. Incorporation; membership. A membership corporation by the
     6  name of "Bohemia Volunteer [Firemen's] Firefighter's Benevolent  Associ-
     7  ation"  is hereby created. It shall be composed of such persons eligible
     8  to membership therein as hereinafter provided as shall  (a)  notify  the
     9  secretary  of  the Bohemia volunteer fire department in writing prior to
    10  the organization meeting of such corporation hereinafter provided for of
    11  their desire to become members, or (b) shall attend such meeting  or  an
    12  adjournment  thereof,  and  also  such persons so eligible as may become
    13  members of such corporation pursuant to its by-laws.
    14    § 2. Persons eligible to membership. All persons who are now,  or  who
    15  have  been,  or  who  hereafter shall be active volunteer members of the
    16  Bohemia volunteer fire department, or of any  fire  corporation  in  the
    17  Bohemia  fire  district, and who have heretofore acquired or shall here-
    18  after acquire the status of exempt volunteer  [firemen]  firefighter  as
    19  defined in section two hundred of the general municipal law or any other
    20  applicable  law  by  virtue  of  having  rendered service in the Bohemia

     2  fire  district,  and all persons who are active volunteer members of the
     3  Bohemia volunteer fire department shall be eligible  for  membership  in

    13  man] firefighter as defined by section two hundred of the general munic-
    14  ipal  law  shall move from such fire district or from territory provided
    15  with fire protection as aforesaid or if the territory in which a  member

    17  such member's membership in such corporation shall cease.
    18    § 6. Control and disposal of  funds  and  property.  The  control  and
    19  disposal  of the funds, property and estate of the said association, the
    20  exercise of its powers, and the management and control  of  its  affairs
    21  shall  be  vested  in  and  exercised by a board of trustees which shall
    22  consist of [five] eight members of such corporation;  viz.:  the  presi-
    23  dent,  the  vice-president,  the secretary, the treasurer and [one] four
    24  other [member] members having the title of trustee.  Such  officers  and
    25  trustees  shall  be elected at the annual meeting of such corporation in
    26  the manner prescribed by its by-laws.
    27    § 7. Precept for payment of foreign fire insurance premium taxes. Such
    28  corporation shall collect and there  shall  be  paid  to  it  all  taxes
    29  imposed  by section [five hundred fifty-three] nine thousand one hundred
    30  four of the insurance law, for fire department  use  and  benefit,  upon
    31  premiums  for insurance against loss or damage by fire covering property
    32  situated within the territory protected by the  Bohemia  volunteer  fire
    33  department,  located  in the town of Islip, Suffolk county, New York, or
    34  within any duly organized territory in which the Bohemia volunteer  fire
    35  department is or may be obligated to render fire protection. The officer
    36  of said corporation designated by its by-laws to collect and receive the
    37  aforesaid  tax  shall  have  all  the  powers  and be subject to all the
    38  provisions of the insurance law, relating to treasurers of fire  depart-
    39  ments. Such corporation shall also be entitled to receive a share of the
    40  tax  imposed  by  section  [five  hundred  fifty-four] nine thousand one
    41  hundred five of the insurance law, based upon the  business  written  in
    42  the  territory  with  respect  to  which  it  is entitled to collect and
    43  receive the tax under section [five hundred fifty-three]  nine  thousand
    44  one  hundred  four  of  the insurance law. Such taxes shall only be used
    45  [for the care and relief of disabled or indigent  volunteer  and  exempt
    46  volunteer firemen and their families] in furtherance of its purposes set
    47  forth in section three of this act.
    48    §  2.  This  act shall take effect immediately; provided however, that
    49  the amendments to section 7 of chapter 45 of the laws of  1963  made  by
    50  section one of this act shall apply to taxes due on premiums received on
    51  and after January 1, 2026.
