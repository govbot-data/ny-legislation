A05472 Summary:

BILL NOA05472
 
SAME ASSAME AS S05938
 
SPONSORBores
 
COSPNSR
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Add §13, Exec L
 
Requires the governor to prepare an annual preliminary management report of the state and a final management report of the state which includes an assessment of performance goals and measures and other information pertaining to the actions of state agencies.

        Introduced  by M. of A. BORES -- read once and referred to the Committee

        AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to requiring the governor
          to prepare an annual management report of the state
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section  1.  This  act shall be known and may be cited as the "service
     2  delivery transparency act".
     3    § 2. The executive law is amended by adding a new section 13  to  read
     4  as follows:
     5    §  13.  State  management report. 1. Definitions. For purposes of this
     6  section:
     7    (a) "State agency" shall mean any  state  department,  board,  bureau,
     8  division, commission, committee, public authority, public benefit corpo-
     9  ration,  council,  office, or other governmental entity in the executive
    10  branch of government performing a governmental or  proprietary  function
    11  for the state.
    12    (b)  "Performance  goals"  shall  mean  goals  and  metrics which: (i)
    13  provide improved transparency to the public about the  state's  perform-
    14  ance  and quality of life in the state; (ii) are in a format that allows
    15  the public to better understand the agency's service delivery processes,
    16  including what occurs at each step of the process and how long each step
    17  takes to complete; and (iii) are updated as  frequently  as  practicable
    18  and shall provide the most recent data with a minimum of lag time.
    19    2.  Not  later than January thirtieth of each year, the governor shall
    20  make public and submit to the temporary president of the senate and  the
    21  speaker of the assembly a preliminary management report of the state and
    22  not  later than September fifteenth of each year the governor shall make
    23  public and submit to the temporary  president  of  the  senate  and  the
    24  speaker of the assembly a management report of the state.
 
         EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets
                              [ ] is old law to be omitted.

     1    3.  The  preliminary  management  report  shall contain for each state
     2  agency:
     3    (a)  a statement of actual performance for the first six months of the
     4  current fiscal year relative  to  the  performance  goals  and  measures
     5  established for such year;
     6    (b)  proposed performance goals and measures for the next fiscal year,
     7  reflecting budgetary decisions made as of the date of submission of  the
     8  proposed budget submitted annually by the governor to the legislature;
     9    (c)  an  explanation  in  narrative and/or tabular form of significant
    10  changes in the performance goals and measures from the current budget to
    11  the proposed budget submitted annually by the governor to  the  legisla-
    12  ture; and
    13    (d)  an  appendix  indicating the relationship between the performance
    14  goals and measures included in the management report and the correspond-
    15  ing appropriations contained in the proposed budget  submitted  annually
    16  by the governor to the legislature.
    17    4. The management report shall contain for each agency:
    18    (a)  performance goals for the current fiscal year and a statement and
    19  explanation of performance measures;
    20    (b) a statement of actual performance for the entire  previous  fiscal
    21  year relative to performance goals;
    22    (c)  a  statement of the status of the agency's internal control envi-
    23  ronment and systems, including a summary of any actions taken during the
    24  previous fiscal year, and any actions being  taken  during  the  current
    25  fiscal  year to strengthen the agency's internal control environment and
    26  system;
    27    (d) a summary of rulemaking actions undertaken by  the  agency  during
    28  the past fiscal year including:
    29    (i) the number of rulemaking actions taken;
    30    (ii)  the number of such actions which were not noticed in the regula-
    31  tory agenda prepared for such fiscal year, including a  summary  of  the
    32  reasons such rules were not included in such regulatory agenda; and
    33    (iii)  the  number  of such actions which were adopted under emergency
    34  rulemaking procedures;
    35    (e) a summary of the procurement actions  taken  during  the  previous
    36  fiscal year;
    37    (f) information regarding how well the agency is serving its customers
    38  including  timeliness  in  responding  to  emails,  letters  and service
    39  requests; and
    40    (g) an appendix indicating the relationship  between  the  performance
    41  goals  included  in the management report and the corresponding expendi-
    42  tures made pursuant to the adopted budget for the previous fiscal year.
    43    § 3. This act shall take effect immediately.
