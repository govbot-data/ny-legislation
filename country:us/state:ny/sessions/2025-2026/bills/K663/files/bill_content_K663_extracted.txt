W. Bush signed the historic Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) into law, marking a significant milestone in the ongoing struggle for disability rights and equality; and WHEREAS, A landmark piece of legislation, the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) prohibits discrimination against individuals with disabilities and promotes their full participation in all aspects of society, including employment, public accommodations, transportation, and telecommunications; and WHEREAS, The ADA has had a profound impact on the lives of millions of Americans, ensuring equal opportunities and removing barriers for individuals with disabilities, enabling them to fully engage in educational, employment, recreational, and social activities; and WHEREAS, Having fostered a more accessible and inclusive society, the ADA requires the implementation of reasonable accommodations, the removal of architectural barriers, and the promotion of accessibility standards in various sectors; and WHEREAS, Madison County has long been committed to upholding the principles enshrined in the ADA, advancing disability rights and fostering an environment where individuals with disabilities can thrive, contribute, and achieve their full potential; and WHEREAS, Let us celebrate the achievements of the ADA, reflect on the work that still lies ahead, and reaffirm our dedication to building a society that recognizes the inherent worth and abilities of every individual; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to commend the ADA Celebration Committee for Madison County, upon the occasion of hosting an esteemed event, commemorating the 35th Anniversary of the Signing of the Americans with Disabilities Act, on July 25, 2025; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the ADA Celebration Committee for Madison County.
