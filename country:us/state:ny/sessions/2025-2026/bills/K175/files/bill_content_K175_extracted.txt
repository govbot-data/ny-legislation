Cook HONORING Dr. Maria Santiago upon the occasion of her designation for special recognition at the Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Visionary Awards Gala on March 9, 2025 WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body to recognize and commend individuals whose leadership, compassion, and dedication to service have profoundly impacted the lives of others, particularly the most vulnerable members of our communities; and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and in full accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor Dr. Maria Santiago upon the occasion of her designation for special recognition at the Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Visionary Awards Gala, to be held on Sunday, March 9, 2025, in celebration of Women's History Month under the theme "Women of Destiny"; and WHEREAS, A visionary leader in healthcare and senior advocacy, Dr. Maria Santiago has dedicated her career to enhancing the well-being of elderly individuals, ensuring they receive the highest standard of care, dignity, and respect; and WHEREAS, Originally pursuing a career in broadcasting, Dr. Maria Santiago's journey took a transformative turn when she discovered her true calling--caring for seniors--a passion that has guided her work and defined her legacy in healthcare and philanthropy; and WHEREAS, After immigrating from Trinidad to the United States, Dr. Maria Santiago pursued her education at Hostos Community College in the Bronx, Lehman College, and the Connecticut School of Broadcasting, skillfully balancing her passion for media with her growing commitment to senior care; and WHEREAS, Dr. Maria Santiago worked her way through the healthcare system, rising from administrator to CEO and ultimately founding her own Home Health Aide Training Institute in New York, where she trains and empowers future healthcare workers, providing them with the skills and opportunities to experience the fulfillment of caring for others; and WHEREAS, Through her philanthropic endeavors, Dr. Maria Santiago has extended her humanitarian outreach globally, providing meals and essential resources to families in Ghana, Nigeria, and Trinidad, demonstrating her unwavering commitment to serving communities in need; and WHEREAS, During the COVID-19 pandemic, Dr. Maria Santiago honored and celebrated essential workers, recognizing their sacrifices by awarding over $100,000 in scholarships and life insurance policies, ensuring that those on the front lines of healthcare received the support and recognition they deserved; and WHEREAS, With each training program she oversees, job placement she facilitates, and act of service she performs, Dr. Maria Santiago remains steadfast in her mission to increase awareness of exemplary healthcare practices for seniors, ensuring they receive the highest quality of care; and WHEREAS, Guided by the belief that "there is a divine plan for all of us," Dr. Maria Santiago has committed herself to understanding and addressing the challenges faced by seniors, profoundly improving the lives of those in need, as well as the families and caregivers who serve them; and WHEREAS, Dr. Maria Santiago's tireless dedication to healthcare, philanthropy, and education exemplifies the principles of service, compassion, and advocacy, making her a fitting honoree at the Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Visionary Awards Gala; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to honor Dr. Maria Santiago upon the occasion of her designation for special recognition at the Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. Visionary Awards Gala, commending her for her extraordinary contributions to senior care, healthcare education, and global philanthropy; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Dr. Maria Santiago.
