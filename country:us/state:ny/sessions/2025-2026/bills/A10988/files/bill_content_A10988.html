A10988 Summary:

BILL NOA10988B
 
SAME ASSAME AS S09608-B
 
SPONSORShrestha
 
COSPNSR
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Add §4-c, Pub Serv L
 
Directs utility corporations to petition the public service commission for approval of electric grid utilization metrics; directs the public service commission to determine which electric grid utilization metrics shall be approved and how such metrics shall be applied; directs the public service commission to submit an annual report to the legislature and governor.

        AN ACT to amend the public service law, in relation to directing utility
          corporations to petition the public service commission for approval of
          electric grid utilization metrics
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The public service law is amended by adding a  new  section
     2  4-c to read as follows:
     3    §  4-c.  Utility corporation utilization metrics. 1. (a) No later than
     4  one hundred eighty days after the effective date of this  section,  each
     5  electric or combination electric and gas utility corporation shall peti-
     6  tion  the  commission for approval of electric grid utilization metrics.
     7  Such petition shall include an overview  of  electric  grid  utilization
     8  metrics,  including  industry standard metrics. Each utility corporation
     9  shall include  in  such  petition  an  assessment  of  current  electric
    10  distribution  and transmission system performance as compared with opti-
    11  mal utilization of existing electric grid assets, including any informa-
    12  tion necessary  for  the  commission  to  evaluate  the  efficiency  and
    13  performance of electric distribution and transmission system infrastruc-
    14  ture  owned  and operated by each such electric and combination electric
    15  and gas utility corporation.
    16    (b) Any electric grid utilization metrics proposed by a utility corpo-
    17  ration under paragraph (a) of this subdivision shall  include:  (i)  the
    18  ratio  of  distribution  system peak load to total distribution electric
    19  grid capacity; (ii) the ratio of  current  electric  load  delivered  to
    20  total  potential deliverable electric load over the distribution system;
    21  (iii) the percentage of kilowatt-hours of electricity  lost  during  the
    22  distribution  process or by the distribution system; (iv) an analysis of
    23  constrained circuits on the distribution system; and (v)  an  evaluation
    24  of the performance of the distribution system at system peaks.

     1    (c)  (i)  In  evaluating  petitions  made  under this subdivision, the
     2  commission shall determine which electric grid utilization metrics shall
     3  be approved, how such metrics may be reasonably applied  at  the  feeder
     4  and  substation  level,  and  how such metrics may be reported in future
     5  integrated resource plan filings, and shall include, as deemed appropri-
     6  ate  by  the commission, a requirement to submit seasonal assessments of
     7  electric grid utilization  and  implementation  of  such  electric  grid
     8  utilization metrics.
     9    (ii)  In  approving  any  electric grid utilization metrics under this
    10  paragraph, the commission shall include, as appropriate: (1) a  timeline
    11  for  each  utility  corporation to increase electric grid utilization in
    12  accordance with the approved metrics; and (2) a description of the  ways
    13  in  which  such  metrics  may  inform  the commission's consideration of
    14  future utility requests  for  approval  of  cost  recovery  for  capital
    15  investments.
    16    (iii) In subsequent integrated resource plan filings, each electric or
    17  combination  electric  and  gas  utility  corporation  shall  include an
    18  assessment of current  system  performance  relative  to  each  proposed
    19  metric and may propose new or modified electric grid utilization metrics
    20  for commission approval.
    21    2.  (a)  The commission shall produce an annual report to the legisla-
    22  ture and the governor on each utility corporation's assessment of  rele-
    23  vant grid utilization metrics and performance against such metrics rela-
    24  tive  to  the  requirements  of  this  section,  including  the timeline
    25  established by the commission for each such utility corporation.
    26    (b) In the report submitted under paragraph (a) of  this  subdivision,
    27  the  commission shall also analyze the potential for each utility corpo-
    28  ration to increase electric grid utilization through the  deployment  of
    29  non-wires alternatives, including: (i) virtual power plants; (ii) flexi-
    30  ble transmission and transmission static synchronous compensators; (iii)
    31  synchronous  condensers;  and  (iv)  power  quality monitoring equipment
    32  installed at the point of interconnection for all customers with a meas-
    33  ured or contracted demand of twenty-five megawatts or greater.
    34    (c) In conducting an analysis under paragraph (b) of this subdivision,
    35  the commission may request any information necessary from  each  utility
    36  corporation  to  properly evaluate such utility corporation's deployment
    37  of non-wires alternatives or other  methods  to  improve  electric  grid
    38  utilization.
    39    (d)  This section shall not apply to municipally-owned electric utili-
    40  ties.
    41    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
