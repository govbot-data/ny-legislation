        AN ACT to amend the public health law, in relation to  requirements  for
          health care proxies and death certificates
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The subdivision heading and paragraph (a) of subdivision  2
     2  of  section  2981 of the public health law, as amended by chapter 708 of
     3  the laws of 2019, are amended to read as follows:
     4    Health care proxy; execution[; witnesses]. (a) A competent  adult  may
     5  appoint  a health care agent by a health care proxy, signed and dated by
     6  the adult [in the presence of two adult witnesses who  shall  also  sign
     7  the proxy] and sworn to before a notary public.  Another person may sign
     8  and  date  the health care proxy for the adult if the adult is unable to
     9  do so, at the adult's direction and in the adult's presence, and in  the
    10  presence of [two adult witnesses who shall sign the proxy. The witnesses
    11  shall  state  that the principal appeared to execute the proxy willingly
    12  and free from duress. The person appointed as agent  shall  not  act  as
    13  witness to execution of the health care proxy] a notary public.
    14    §  2.  Subdivision  2  of  section  2982  of the public health law, as
    15  amended by chapter 619 of the laws  of  2024,  is  amended  to  read  as
    16  follows:
    17    2. Decision-making standard. After consultation with a licensed physi-
    18  cian,   registered   nurse,  physician  assistant,  nurse  practitioner,
    19  licensed psychologist, licensed master  social  worker,  or  a  licensed
    20  clinical  social worker, the agent shall make health care decisions: (a)
    21  in accordance with the principal's  wishes,  including  the  principal's
    22  religious  and  moral  beliefs; or (b) if the principal's wishes are not
    23  reasonably known and cannot with reasonable diligence be ascertained, in
    24  accordance with the principal's best interests; provided, however,  that
    25  if  the  principal's  wishes  regarding the administration of artificial

     1  nutrition and hydration are not reasonably known and cannot with reason-
     2  able diligence be ascertained, the agent shall not have the authority to
     3  make decisions regarding these measures; provided,  however,  the  agent
     4  may not make health care decisions which will eventually end the life of
     5  a person without receiving written opinion from two medical doctors, who
     6  are  not  the person's attending physician, stating that they agree with
     7  the health care decision.
     8    § 3. Subdivision 4 of section 4141 of the public health law is amended
     9  by adding a new paragraph (e) to read as follows:
    10    (e) Where a death is the result of  the  health  care  decision  of  a
    11  health care agent which caused the withdrawal of nutrition or hydration,
    12  it shall be stated on the death certificate.
    13    §  4.  This  act  shall take effect immediately; provided, however, if
    14  chapter 619 of the laws of 2024 shall not have taken effect on or before
    15  such date then section two of this act shall take  effect  on  the  same
    16  date  and  in the same manner as such chapter of the laws of 2024, takes
    17  effect.
