SAME ASSAME AS A03440
 
SPONSORHINCHEY
 
COSPNSR

Add §19, RP L
 
Relates to prohibiting the acquisition or transfer of agricultural land by foreign adversaries after January 1, 2026; provides that such prohibition will not apply to any interest in agricultural land held by a foreign adversary prior to such date or any federally recognized Indian tribe or its government units and enterprises; defines terms.

        AN ACT to amend the real property law, in relation  to  prohibiting  the
          acquisition or transfer of agricultural land by foreign adversaries
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section 1. The real property law is amended by adding a new section 19
     2  to read as follows:
     3    § 19. Foreign adversary acquisition or transfer of agricultural  land.
     4  1.  For the purposes of this section, the following terms shall have the
     5  following meanings:
     6    (a) "agricultural land" means real estate  used  for  an  agricultural
     7  operation in the state.
     8    (b)  "agricultural  operation" means any operation devoted to the bona
     9  fide production of crops, animals, or fowl, including the production  of
    10  fruits  and  vegetables  of any kind; meat, dairy, and poultry products;
    11  nuts, tobacco, nursery, and floral  products;  and  the  production  and
    12  harvest of products from silvicultural activity;
    13    (c)  "controlling interest" means either possession of more than fifty
    14  percent of the ownership interests in an entity or a  percentage  owner-
    15  ship  in  an  entity  of fifty percent or less if such owner directs the
    16  business and affairs of such entity without the requirement  or  consent
    17  of any other party;
    18    (d) "department" means the department of agriculture and markets;
    19    (e)  "foreign adversary" means any foreign government or nongovernment
    20  person determined by the United States secretary  of  commerce  to  have
    21  engaged  in  a long-term pattern or serious instances of conduct signif-
    22  icantly adverse to the national security of the  United  States  or  the
    23  security and safety of United States persons;
    24    (f)  "interest"  means any estate, remainder, or reversion, or portion
    25  of the estate, remainder, or reversion, or an option pursuant  to  which
    26  one  party has a right to cause legal or equitable title to agricultural
    27  land to be transferred.
 
         EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets
                              [ ] is old law to be omitted.

        S. 9665                             2
 
     1    2. (a) Notwithstanding any other provision of law, on and after  Janu-
     2  ary  first,  two thousand twenty-six, no foreign adversary shall acquire
     3  or transfer any interest in agricultural land in the state of New York.
     4    (b)  This  section shall not apply to (i) any interest in agricultural
     5  land held by a foreign adversary prior to January  first,  two  thousand
     6  twenty-six, or (ii) any federally recognized Indian tribe or its govern-
     7  ment units and enterprises.
     8    (c)  Any  transfer of an interest in land in violation of this section
     9  shall be void.
    10    (d) This section shall not be applied in a  manner  inconsistent  with
    11  any  provision of any treaty between the United States and another coun-
    12  try.
    13    3. (a) Based on the reports submitted to it pursuant  to  the  federal
    14  Agricultural Foreign Investment Disclosure Act, 7 U.S.C. § 3501 et seq.,
    15  and other information the department, at its discretion, deems appropri-
    16  ate,  the department shall compile an annual report in consultation with
    17  the appropriate  state  agencies  and  boards  for  each  calendar  year
    18  containing all of the following, if available:
    19    (i) the total amount of agricultural land that is under foreign owner-
    20  ship;
    21    (ii)  the  percentage change in foreign ownership of agricultural land
    22  in the state for each year over the prior ten years;
    23    (iii) the purpose for which foreign-owned  agricultural  land  in  the
    24  state  is being used currently. To the extent such information is avail-
    25  able, the department shall also include any significant  recent  changes
    26  or trends in the uses of foreign-owned agricultural land in the state;
    27    (iv)  with  the  assistance  of  relevant  state agencies, information
    28  regarding the extent of, and any recent changes in, foreign ownership of
    29  energy production, storage, or distribution facilities in the  state  to
    30  the extent such information is available; and
    31    (v)  any legislative, regulatory, or administrative policy changes the
    32  department recommends in light of the information in this report.
    33    (b) The department shall publish its inaugural report  no  later  than
    34  July  first, two thousand twenty-six and each subsequent report no later
    35  than July first of each year. The department shall also  deliver  copies
    36  of  such  report  to the governor, the temporary president of the senate
    37  and the speaker of the assembly.
    38    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
