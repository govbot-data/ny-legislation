DiPIETRO -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Transportation AN ACT to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to creating a distinctive plate for active duty military service members The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The vehicle and traffic law is amended by adding a new 2 section 404-ff to read as follows: 3 § 404-ff. Distinctive plates for active duty military service members. 4 1. Any active duty military service member serving in the armed forces 5 of the United States shall, upon request, be issued a license plate 6 bearing the words "U.S. Military". Application for said license plate 7 shall be filed with the commissioner in such form and detail as the 8 commissioner shall prescribe. 9 2. The distinctive plate authorized in subdivision one of this section 10 shall be issued upon proof, satisfactory to the commissioner, that the 11 applicant is an active duty military service member. The branch of mili- 12 tary service shall also be indicated on the license plate either in 13 words or by the insignia of the branch served in. 14 3. A distinctive plate issued pursuant to this section shall be issued 15 in the same manner as other number plates upon payment of the regular 16 registration fee prescribed by section four hundred one of this article 17 provided, however, that an additional one time service charge of ten 18 dollars shall be charged for such plate. 19 4. Nothing contained herein shall require the issuance of a special 20 number plate under this section unless the commissioner is satisfied 21 that the aggregate amount of annual service charges collected pursuant 22 to subdivision three of this section will be sufficient to recover the 23 costs of the design and manufacture of such special plates. 24 § 2. 1. A distinctive plate established pursuant to section 404-ff of 25 the vehicle and traffic law, as added by section one of this act, shall EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
