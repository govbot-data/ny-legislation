CARROLL -- read once and referred to the Committee on Environmental Conservation AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law, in relation to phas- ing out the sale of products that contain intentionally added PFAS The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Article 37 of the environmental conservation law is amended 2 by adding a new title 4 to read as follows: 3 TITLE 4 4 PRODUCTS CONTAINING PERFLUOROALKYL AND POLYFLUOROALKYL SUBSTANCES 5 Section 37-0401. Definitions. 6 37-0403. Notification. 7 37-0405. Exemptions. 8 37-0407. Prohibition on sale of products containing inten- 9 tionally added PFAS. 10 37-0409. Failure to provide notice. 11 37-0411. Violations. 12 37-0413. Rules and regulations. 13 § 37-0401. Definitions. 14 For the purposes of this title, the term: 1. "Currently unavoidable 15 use" means a use of PFAS that the department has determined to be essen- 16 tial for health, safety or the functioning of society and for which 17 alternatives are not reasonably available. 18 2. "Intentionally added chemical" shall have the same meaning as 19 subdivision eleven of section 37-0901 of this article. 20 3. "Manufacturer" means the person that manufactures a product or 21 whose brand name is affixed to the product. In the case of a product 22 imported into the United States, "manufacturer" includes the importer or 23 first domestic distributor of the product if the person that manufac- 24 tured or assembled the product or whose brand name is affixed to the 25 product does not have a presence in the United States. "Perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances" or "PFAS" means 2 substances that include any member of the class of fluorinated organic 3 chemicals containing at least one fully flourished carbon atom. 4 5. "Product" means an item manufactured, assembled, packaged or other- 5 wise prepared for sale to consumers, including its product components, 6 sold or distributed for personal, residential, commercial or industrial 7 use, including for use in making other products. 8 6. "Product component" means an identifiable component of a product, 9 regardless of whether the manufacturer of the product is the manufactur- 10 er of the component. 11 7. "Carpet or rug" means a fabric marketed or intended to be used as a 12 flooring covering. 13 8. "Cookware" means a durable houseware item that is used in homes and 14 restaurants to prepare, dispense, or store food, foodstuffs or beverag- 15 es. "Cookware" includes but is not limited to pots, pans, skillets, 16 grills, baking sheets, baking molds, trays, bowls and cooking utensils. 17 9. "Cosmetic product" shall have the same meaning as subdivision one 18 of section 37-0117 of this article. 19 10. "Personal care product" shall have the same meaning as subdivision 20 two of section 37-0117 of this article. 21 11. "Fabric treatment" means a substance applied to a fabric to give 22 the fabric one or more characteristics including but not limited to 23 stain or water resistance. 24 § 37-0403. Notification. 25 A manufacturer of a product for sale in the state that contains 26 perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) as intentionally 27 added chemicals shall comply with the requirements of this section. 28 1. Beginning January first, two thousand twenty-eight, a manufacturer 29 of a product for sale in the state that contains perfluoroalkyl and 30 polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) as intentionally added chemicals shall 31 submit to the department a written notification that includes: 32 (a) a brief description of the product; 33 (b) the purpose for which PFAS are used in the product, including any 34 product components; 35 (c) the amount of each of the PFAS, identified by its chemical 36 abstracts service registry number, in the product, reported as an exact 37 quantity determined using commercially available analytic methods or as 38 falling within a range approved for reporting purposes by the depart- 39 ment; 40 (d) the name and address of the manufacturer, and the name, address 41 and phone number of a contact person for the manufacturer; and 42 (e) any additional information established by the department by rule 43 as necessary to implement the requirements of this section. 44 2. With the approval of the department, a manufacturer may supply the 45 information required in subdivision one of this section for a category 46 or type of product rather than for each individual product. 47 3. A manufacturer shall update and revise the information in the writ- 48 ten notification required in subdivision one of this section whenever 49 there is significant change in the information or when requested to do 50 so by the department. 51 4. The department may extend the deadline for submission by a manufac- 52 turer of the information required under this section if deemed neces- 53 sary. 54 § 37-0405. Exemptions. 55 The following are exempt from this section: A. 8585 3 1 1. A product for which federal law governs the presence of PFAS in the 2 product in a manner that preempts state authority; and 3 2. Any product containing perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl 4 substances (PFAS) as intentionally added chemicals which have been 5 deemed by the department as a currently unavoidable use. 6 § 37-0407. Prohibition on sale of products containing intentionally 7 added PFAS. 8 1. Beginning January first, two thousand twenty-nine, a person shall 9 not distribute, sell or offer for sale in this state any carpet or rug, 10 cookware, cosmetic product, fabric treatment or personal care product 11 that contains perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) as 12 intentionally added chemicals, unless the department has determined that 13 the use of PFAS in such product is currently an unavoidable use. 14 2. The department may identify products by category or use that may 15 not be sold, offered for sale or distributed in this state if they 16 contain perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) as inten- 17 tionally added chemicals if the department determines that such products 18 are likely to cause contamination of land or water resources and are a 19 threat to human health or safety. 20 3. Beginning January first, two thousand thirty-four, a person shall 21 not distribute, sell or offer for sale in this state any product that 22 contains perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) as inten- 23 tionally added chemicals unless the department has determined that the 24 use of PFAS is a currently unavoidable use. 25 4. The department shall publish and maintain a list of products that 26 are exempt from the prohibition on distribution, sale or offer of sale 27 as determined to be a currently unavoidable use. 28 5. The prohibition on distribution, sale or offer of sale in this 29 section does not apply to the sale or resale of used products. 30 § 37-0409. Failure to provide notice. 31 1. (a) A person shall not distribute, sell or offer for sale in this 32 state a product that contains perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl 33 substances (PFAS) as intentionally added chemicals if the manufacturer 34 has failed to comply with requirements in section 37-0403 of this title 35 except where such product containing perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl 36 substances (PFAS) as intentionally added chemicals, which have been 37 deemed by the department as a currently unavoidable use. 38 (b) The prohibition in this section shall not apply to a retailer in 39 the state unless the retailer distributes, sells, or offers for sale a 40 product for which the retailer has received notification that the sale 41 of the product is prohibited. 42 2. If the department has reason to believe that a product contains 43 perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) as intentionally 44 added chemicals being distributed, sold or offered for sale in violation 45 of this section, the department may direct the manufacturer of the prod- 46 uct to, within thirty days: 47 (a) provide the department with a statement attesting that the product 48 does not contain perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) as 49 intentionally added chemicals; or 50 (b) notify persons who sell that product in this state that the sale 51 of that product is prohibited in this state and provide the department 52 with a list of the names and addresses of those notified. 53 § 37-0411. Violations. 54 1. A violation of any of the provisions of this title or any rule or 55 regulation promulgated pursuant thereto shall be punishable in the case 56 of a first violation, by a civil penalty not to exceed ten thousand A. 8585 4 1 dollars. In the case of a second and any further violation, the liabil- 2 ity shall be for a civil penalty not to exceed twenty-five thousand 3 dollars for each violation. 4 2. No manufacturer, distributor or retailer of a product shall be held 5 in violation of this title who can show that they relied in good faith 6 on the written assurance of the manufacturer of such covered product 7 that such product met the requirements of this title. Such written 8 assurance shall take the form of a certificate of compliance stating 9 that a product is in compliance with the requirements of this title, 10 provided however, where compliance is achieved because the product is 11 exempt pursuant to section 37-0405 of this title, the certificate shall 12 state the specific basis upon which the product is exempt. The certif- 13 icate of compliance shall be signed by an authorized official of the 14 manufacturing or distributing company. 15 § 37-0413. Rules and regulations. 16 The commissioner shall have the power to adopt such rules and regu- 17 lations to provide for the effective application and enforcement of this 18 title as deemed necessary. 19 § 2.
