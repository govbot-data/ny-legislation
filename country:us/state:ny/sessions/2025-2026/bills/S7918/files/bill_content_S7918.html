Add 3246 & 4331, Ins L; add 365-q, Soc Serv L
 
Relates to reimbursement for anesthesia services; provides that an insurer may use a time related reimbursement methodology for anesthesia services if such methodology is based upon criteria established by an independent organization.

        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  insurance  law  and the social services law, in
          relation to reimbursement for anesthesia services
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The insurance law is amended by adding a new section 3246
     2  to read as follows:
     3    § 3246. Reimbursement for anesthesia services. (a) An insurer  issuing
     4  a policy of hospital, medical, or surgical expense insurance pursuant to
     5  this section or any other section of law shall not impose arbitrary time
     6  caps  on reimbursement for anesthesia services provided during medically
     7  necessary procedures.
     8    (b) Reimbursement for anesthesia services shall be determined based on
     9  medical necessity as determined by the insurer,  taking  into  consider-
    10  ation  the complexity of the procedure as evidenced by the submission of
    11  medical records submitted by the attending anesthesiologist or  licensed
    12  anesthesia provider.
    13    (c)  (1) An insurer issuing a policy of hospital, medical, or surgical
    14  expense insurance pursuant to this section or any other section  of  law
    15  shall  be prohibited from denying payment for anesthesia services solely
    16  because the duration of care exceeded a pre-set time limit.
    17    (2) Notwithstanding paragraph one of this subsection, an  insurer  may
    18  use  a time related reimbursement methodology for anesthesia services if
    19  such methodology is based upon criteria established  by  an  independent
    20  organization,  including  the  criteria used by the centers for Medicare
    21  and Medicaid services to reimburse anesthesia services under title XVIII
    22  of the United States Social Security Act (Medicare). If an insurer  uses
    23  a  time related reimbursement methodology, it should have an established

     4    § 2. The insurance law is amended by adding a new section 4331 to read

     6    §  4331.  Reimbursement for anesthesia services. (a) Every corporation
     7  subject to  the  provisions  of  this  article  that  provide  hospital,
     8  medical,  or  surgical expense insurance coverage shall not impose arbi-
     9  trary time caps on reimbursement for anesthesia services provided during
    10  medically necessary procedures.
    11    (b) Reimbursement for anesthesia services shall be determined based on
    12  medical necessity as determined by the corporation, taking into  consid-
    13  eration  the  complexity of the procedure as evidenced by the submission
    14  of medical  records  submitted  by  the  attending  anesthesiologist  or
    15  licensed anesthesia provider.
    16    (c)(1)  A  corporation  issuing hospital, medical, or surgical expense
    17  insurance coverage shall be prohibited from denying  payment  for  anes-
    18  thesia  services  solely because the duration of care exceeded a pre-set
    19  time limit.
    20    (2) Notwithstanding paragraph one of this  subsection,  a  corporation
    21  may use a time related reimbursement methodology for anesthesia services
    22  if such methodology is based upon criteria established by an independent
    23  organization,  including  the  criteria used by the centers for Medicare
    24  and Medicaid services to reimburse anesthesia services under title XVIII
    25  of the United States Social Security Act (Medicare).  If  a  corporation
    26  uses  a time related reimbursement methodology, it should have an estab-
    27  lished process for the submission of additional medical records and  the
    28  sharing  of  electronic medical records to assess whether an increase to
    29  the reimbursement is warranted.
    30    § 3. The social services law is amended by adding a new section  365-q
    31  to read as follows:
    32    § 365-q. Reimbursement for anesthesia services. 1. Any medical assist-
    33  ance  provider  whose medical assistance includes the provision of anes-
    34  thesia, including such  assistance  furnished  through  a  managed  care
    35  program,  shall  not  be subject to arbitrary time caps on reimbursement
    36  when furnished during medically necessary procedures, and  such  payment
    37  shall  not  be denied for such assistance solely because the duration of
    38  such assistance exceeded a pre-set time limit.
    39    2. Notwithstanding subdivision one of this  section,  a  managed  care
    40  provider may use a time related reimbursement methodology for anesthesia
    41  services  if  such  methodology is based upon criteria established by an
    42  independent organization, including the criteria used by the centers for
    43  Medicare and Medicaid services to reimburse  anesthesia  services  under
    44  title  XVIII  of  the United States Social Security Act (Medicare). If a
    45  managed care provider uses a time related reimbursement methodology,  it
    46  should  have  an  established  process  for the submission of additional
    47  medical records and the sharing of electronic medical records to  assess
    48  whether an increase to the reimbursement is warranted.
    49    § 4. This act shall take effect January 1, 2026.
