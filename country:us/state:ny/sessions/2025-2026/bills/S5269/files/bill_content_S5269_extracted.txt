BAILEY, FERNANDEZ, RAMOS -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Finance AN ACT to amend the state finance law, in relation to authorizing the establishment of permanent COVID-19 memorials and establishing a COVID-19 permanent memorial fund The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The state of New York and any municipality therein are 2 authorized to, in collaboration with municipal parks departments and 3 under the guidance of the office of parks, recreation, and historic 4 preservation, designate and establish a permanent COVID-19 memorial 5 within its jurisdiction. Municipalities seeking to establish and main- 6 tain such a memorial may apply for funding under section 99-ss of the 7 state finance law as well as from other public and private sources, 8 including but not limited to grants, donations, and partnerships. The 9 final designs and locations of such memorials shall be selected through 10 a community-driven process overseen by municipal parks departments, with 11 specific guidance from the office of parks, recreation, and historic 12 preservation, and with financial oversight from the comptroller's 13 office. The final designs and locations of such memorials shall prior- 14 itize input from those most impacted, including but not limited to fami- 15 ly members and loved ones of victims of COVID-19. 16 § 2. The state finance law is amended by adding a new section 99-ss to 17 read as follows: 18 § 99-ss. COVID-19 permanent memorial fund. 1. There is hereby estab- 19 lished in the joint custody of the state comptroller and the commission- 20 er of parks, recreation and historic preservation a fund to be known as 21 the "COVID-19 permanent memorial fund". 22 2. The fund shall consist of all moneys appropriated therefor, grants, 23 gifts or bequests received by the state for the purposes of the fund, 24 and all other moneys credited or transferred thereto from any other fund EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
