FELDER -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Transportation AN ACT to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to requiring bicycles with electric assist and electric scooters be inspected and registered with the department of motor vehicles and carry liability insurance The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The vehicle and traffic law is amended by adding two new 2 sections 1244 and 1245 to read as follows: 3 § 1244. Registration and inspection of bicycles with electric assist. 4 1. Every bicycle with electric assist as defined in section one hundred 5 two-c of this chapter shall be registered and issued a number plate by 6 the department. No person shall operate a bicycle with electric assist 7 unless such bicycle with electric assist shall have a distinctive number 8 assigned to it by the commissioner and a number plate issued by the 9 commissioner with a number corresponding to that of the certificate of 10 registration. 11 2. Such number plates shall be of such material, form, design and 12 dimensions and contain or set forth such distinguishing number or other 13 identification marks as the commissioner shall prescribe, provided, 14 however, that there shall be at all times a marked contrast between the 15 color of the number plates and that of the numerals or letters thereon. 16 3. Each such number plate shall be conspicuously displayed on the rear 17 of the bicycle, securely fastened so as to prevent the same from swing- 18 ing. 19 4. The fee for such number plates shall be determined by the commis- 20 sioner. 21 5. The commissioner shall require that every bicycle with electric 22 assist registered in this state be inspected annually and shall 23 prescribe the standards and the mechanisms to be inspected; provided EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. A bicycle with electric assist regis- 9 tered under this article shall be covered by a policy of insurance, in 10 such language and form as shall be determined and established by the 11 superintendent of financial services, issued by an insurance carrier 12 authorized to do business in this state. Such policy shall provide for 13 coverage against loss from the liability imposed by law for damages, 14 including damages for care and loss of services, because of bodily inju- 15 ry to or death of any person and injury to or destruction of property 16 arising out of the ownership, maintenance, use, or operation of a bicy- 17 cle with electric assist as defined in section one hundred two-c of this 18 chapter, in such amounts as the superintendent shall prescribe. 19 2. Proof of insurance as required by this section shall be produced 20 and displayed by the owner or operator of such bicycle with electric 21 assist upon the request of any person having authority to enforce the 22 provisions of this chapter. The failure to produce such proof upon the 23 request of any such person shall not be an offense but shall be presump- 24 tive evidence that such bicycle with electric assist is being operated 25 without having such insurance in force and effect. 26 3. Proof of insurance as required by this section shall be produced 27 and displayed by the owner or operator of such bicycle with electric 28 assist to any person who has suffered or claims to have suffered either 29 personal injury or property damage as a result of the operation of such 30 bicycle with electric assist by the owner or operator, if such insurance 31 coverage was required under the circumstances of such operation. It 32 shall be an affirmative defense to any prosecution for a violation of 33 this subdivision that such proof was so produced or displayed within 34 twenty-four hours of receiving notice of such injury or damage, or the 35 claim of such injury or damage. 36 4. No owner of a bicycle with electric assist shall operate or permit 37 the same to be operated anywhere in this state other than on lands of 38 the owner of the bicycle with electric assist without having in full 39 force and effect the liability insurance coverage required by this 40 section, and no person shall operate a bicycle with electric assist 41 anywhere in this state other than on lands of the owner of the bicycle 42 with electric assist with the knowledge that such insurance is not in 43 full force and effect. 44 § 2.
