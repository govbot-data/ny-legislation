        Introduced  by  Sen.  CLEARE -- read twice and ordered printed, and when
          printed to be committed to the Committee on Aging
 
        AN ACT to amend the elder law, in relation to creating a temporary state
          commission to study and investigate the effects of  closures  of  long
          term  care  facilities  on  the residents of such facilities and their
          families; and providing for the repeal of such provisions upon expira-
          tion thereof
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1. The elder law is amended by adding a new article 4 to read
     2  as follows:
     3                                  ARTICLE IV
     4                          LONG TERM CARE FACILITIES
     5  Section 270. Definitions.
     6          271. Commission on long term care facilities.
     7    § 270. Definitions. As used in this article:
     8    1. "Director" shall mean the director of the office for the aging.
     9    2. "Long term care facilities"  shall  mean  residential  health  care
    10  facilities  as  defined  in  subdivision  three  of section twenty-eight
    11  hundred one of the public health law, and assisted living residences, as
    12  defined in article forty-six-B of the public health law, or any  facili-
    13  ties  which  hold  themselves  out  or advertise themselves as providing
    14  assisted living services and which are required to be licensed or certi-
    15  fied under the social services law or the public health  law  and  adult
    16  care  facilities  as defined in subdivision twenty-one of section two of
    17  the social services law.
    18    3. "State ombudsperson" shall mean the state long term care ombudsper-
    19  son appointed by the director pursuant to subdivision three  of  section
    20  two hundred eighteen of this chapter.
    21    §  271.  Commission on long term care facilities. 1. A temporary state
    22  commission, to be known as the commission on long term  care  facilities
 
         EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets
                              [ ] is old law to be omitted.
                                                                   LBD10965-01-5

        S. 6512                             2
 
     1  (hereinafter  the  "commission"),  is  hereby  created to study and make
     2  recommendations concerning the following:
     3    (a)  the effects of closures of long term care facilities on residents
     4  including, but not limited to, the disruption of any established  commu-
     5  nities  within  the  facility  and the physical and mental health impli-
     6  cations related to moving a resident to a new facility;
     7    (b) the effects of closures of long term  care  facilities  on  family
     8  members  of  residents  including,  but not limited to, ability to visit
     9  residents based on new distance of facility; availability of appropriate
    10  placement for a resident; and, ensuring the transfer of legal  documents
    11  and special care instructions;
    12    (c)  the  availability  of  long term care facility placements for New
    13  Yorkers who need skilled nursing care or who have Alzheimer's disease or
    14  other dementia;
    15    (d) the feasibility of arranging comparable placements, as opposed  to
    16  appropriate placements, in other facilities within the same geographical
    17  area when a long term care facility serves fifty or more residents;
    18    (e)  the effectiveness of current laws, rules, and regulations govern-
    19  ing the process of closing a long term care facility,  including  notice
    20  to residents and assistance to relocate residents; and
    21    (f) such other matters as the commission deems appropriate.
    22    2.  The  commission  shall  make recommendations for additional legis-
    23  lation and/or regulations to govern the closing of long term care facil-
    24  ities and facilitate the relocation of long term care facility residents
    25  in a manner that is in the best interests of the residents in the  event
    26  of  a closure of a long term care facility. The commission shall further
    27  study the need, if any, to devise a notification system to  alert  resi-
    28  dents  and  families not less than one year in advance of the closure or
    29  potential closure of a long term care facility.
    30    3. (a) The commission shall consist of eleven members to be  appointed
    31  as  follows:  five  members shall be appointed by the governor and shall
    32  include the commissioner of health, the director of the office  for  the
    33  aging, the state attorney general, the state ombudsperson, and one addi-
    34  tional  member  who  shall be a resident of the state with expertise and
    35  experience in the fields of long term care  and  advocacy;  two  members
    36  shall be appointed by the temporary president of the senate; two members
    37  shall  be  appointed by the speaker of the assembly; one member shall be
    38  appointed by the minority leader of the senate; and one member shall  be
    39  appointed  by  the  minority leader of the assembly.  All of the members
    40  appointed by the temporary president of the senate, the speaker  of  the
    41  assembly,  the  minority leader of the senate and the minority leader of
    42  the assembly shall be residents of the state with expertise and  experi-
    43  ence  in the fields of long term care and advocacy. No person shall be a
    44  member of such commission while such person is a member of the senate or
    45  assembly. Any vacancy on such commission shall be  filled  in  the  same
    46  manner  as  the  original  appointment was made. A chairperson and vice-
    47  chairperson of such commission shall be elected by the majority  of  its
    48  members, all members being present.
    49    (b)  Except  as  provided  in  paragraph  (a)  of this subdivision, no
    50  member, officer or employee of the commission shall be disqualified from
    51  holding any other public office or employment, nor  shall  they  forfeit
    52  any  such office or employment by reason of their appointment hereunder,
    53  notwithstanding the provisions of any general,  special  or  local  law,
    54  ordinance or city charter.
    55    (c) All members of the commission shall be appointed within sixty days
    56  of  the effective date of this section. The first meeting of the commis-

        S. 6512                             3
 
     1  sion shall take place  within  thirty  days  after  appointment  of  all
     2  members of the commission.
     3    4.  The  members  of  the commission shall receive no compensation for
     4  their services.
     5    5. The commission may employ and at pleasure remove such personnel  as
     6  it may deem necessary for the performance of its functions and fix their
     7  compensation  within  the amounts made available by appropriation there-
     8  for, if any, or by donation, if any. The commission may  meet  and  hold
     9  public  and/or  private  hearings within or without the state, and shall
    10  have all the powers of a legislative committee pursuant to the  legisla-
    11  tive law.
    12    6.  For  the  accomplishment  of its purposes, the commission shall be
    13  authorized and empowered to undertake any studies, inquiries, surveys or
    14  analyses it may deem relevant through its own personnel  or  in  cooper-
    15  ation with or by agreement with any other public or private agency.
    16    7. The commission may request and shall receive from any agency in the
    17  state  and  from any subdivision, department, board, bureau, commission,
    18  office, agency or other instrumentality of the state or of any political
    19  subdivision thereof such facilities, assistance and  data  as  it  deems
    20  necessary or desirable for the proper execution of its powers and duties
    21  and to effectuate the purposes set forth in this section.
    22    8. The commission is hereby authorized and empowered to enter into any
    23  agreements and to do and perform any acts that may be necessary, desira-
    24  ble or proper to carry out the purposes and objectives of this section.
    25    9.  The  commission  may  administer  oaths  or affirmations, subpoena
    26  witnesses, compel their attendance, examine them under oath or  affirma-
    27  tion  and  require  the  production  of any books, records, documents or
    28  other evidence it may deem relevant or material to an investigation.
    29    10. The commission shall make a report of its findings and recommenda-
    30  tions and shall submit such report, including  any  recommendations  for
    31  legislative  action  as  it  may  deem necessary and appropriate, to the
    32  governor, the temporary president of the  senate,  the  speaker  of  the
    33  assembly,  the  minority leader of the senate and the minority leader of
    34  the assembly one year after the first meeting of the commission.
    35    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately and shall continue in full
    36  force and effect until one year after the report and recommendations  of
    37  the commission on long term care facilities is delivered to the governor
    38  and the legislature when upon such date the provisions of this act shall
    39  be  deemed repealed; provided that the office for the aging shall notify
    40  the legislative bill drafting commission  upon  the  occurrence  of  the
    41  enactment  of the legislation provided for in section one of this act in
    42  order that the commission may maintain an accurate and timely  effective
    43  data  base  of the official text of the laws of the state of New York in
    44  furtherance of effectuating the provisions of section 44 of the legisla-
    45  tive law and section 70-b of the public officers law.
