STECK -- read once and referred to the Committee on Local Governments AN ACT to amend the general municipal law, in relation to implementing a dangerous dog owner alert system The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. This act shall be known as "Templeton's Law". 2 § 2. Subdivision 6 of section 209-cc of the general municipal law is 3 renumbered subdivision 7 and a new subdivision 6 is added to read as 4 follows: 5 6. (a) The clerk of each city, town or village shall provide necessary 6 data to the clerk of the county where such city, town or village is 7 located and such county shall maintain a web page listing the block of 8 all residences where such dogs are kept within such municipality. Such 9 web page shall provide, for each block listed, a link to a mapping 10 service site that utilizes global positioning system technology to 11 provide street maps and satellite imagery of such blocks. The county 12 clerk shall post on such web page notifications of the block where the 13 owner resides with a conviction for an offense involving a dangerous 14 dog. Posting such notification on the web page shall not preclude a 15 city, town or village from using any other specific information such as 16 a resident's name, address, dog's name or any other additional means of 17 notifying local residents of such convictions. 18 (b) Upon the death of a dangerous dog, the owner of such dangerous dog 19 shall notify the clerk of the municipality in which the owner resides. 20 The clerk, in turn, shall forward such notification to the county clerk 21 of the county where such municipality is located. 22 (c) Upon a change of residence, the owner of a dangerous dog shall 23 notify the clerk in the municipality which the owner is moving from and 24 shall notify the clerk of the municipality in which the owner shall 25 reside that the owner is the owner of a dangerous dog and shall provide 26 the owner's address. The clerks, in turn, shall forward such notifica- 27 tion to the county clerk of the county where such municipalities are 28 located. 29 § 3. This act shall take effect on the thirtieth day after it shall 30 have become a law. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
