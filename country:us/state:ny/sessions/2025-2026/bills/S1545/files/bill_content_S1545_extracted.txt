The public service law is amended by adding a new section 231 to 32 read as follows: 33 § 231. Internet service neutrality. 1. For purposes of this section, 34 "network management practice" shall mean a practice that has a primarily 35 technical network management justification, but does not include other 36 business practices. A "reasonable network management practice" shall 37 mean a network management practice that is primarily used for and 38 tailored to achieving a legitimate network management purpose, taking 39 into account the particular network architecture and technology of the 40 broadband internet access service. 41 2. The commission shall certify annually that any broadband internet 42 service provider qualified to do business in this state, does not: 43 (a) block lawful content, applications, services, or non-harmful 44 devices, subject to reasonable network management. 45 (b) impair or degrade lawful internet traffic on the basis of internet 46 content, application, or service, or use of a non-harmful device, 47 subject to reasonable network management. 48 (c) engage in paid prioritization, including, but not limited to, 49 traffic shaping, prioritization, resource reservation, or other forms of 50 preferential traffic management, either (i) in exchange for any form of 51 consideration from a third party, or (ii) to benefit an affiliated enti- 52 ty, unless the broadband internet service provider demonstrates that the 53 practice would provide a significant public interest benefit and would 54 not harm the open nature of the internet. 55 3.
