Amd Art 11 Art Head, §§212 & 215, add §231, Pub Serv L; add §149, St Fin L
 
Relates to instituting internet service neutrality; provides the public service commission with jurisdiction over monitoring broadband internet service providers; requires a certification for internet service neutrality in certain state contracts.

        AN  ACT  to  amend  the public service law and the state finance law, in
          relation to instituting internet service neutrality

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The  article  heading of article 11 of the public service
     2  law, as added by chapter 83 of the laws of 1995, is amended to  read  as
     3  follows:
     4       PROVISIONS RELATING TO CABLE TELEVISION COMPANIES AND BROADBAND
     5                         INTERNET SERVICE PROVIDERS
     6    §  2.  Section  212 of the public service law is amended by adding two
     7  new subdivisions 15 and 16 to read as follows:
     8    15. "Broadband internet  access  service"  shall  mean  a  mass-market
     9  retail  service  that  provides  the  capability to transmit data to and
    10  receive data from all or substantially all internet endpoints, including
    11  any capabilities that are incidental to and enable the operation of  the
    12  communications  service,  but  shall not include dial-up internet access
    13  service.
    14    16. "Broadband internet service provider" shall mean any person, busi-
    15  ness or organization qualified to do business in this  state,  including
    16  municipal  broadband providers, that provides individuals, corporations,
    17  or other entities with broadband internet access service.
    18    § 3. The section heading of section 215 of the public service law,  as
    19  added  by  chapter 83 of the laws of 1995, is amended and a new subdivi-
    20  sion 14 is added to read as follows:
    21    Duties of the commission in respect to cable television companies  and
    22  broadband internet service providers.
    23    14. Develop and maintain a statewide plan for the monitoring of broad-
    24  band internet service providers, including the annual certification that

     6    § 149. Internet service neutrality requirements in certain procurement
     7  contracts. Notwithstanding any other provision of law to  the  contrary,
     8  where  a contract that includes broadband internet access services is to

    19  services are compliant with the internet service neutrality requirements
    20  established in section two hundred thirty-one of the public service law.
    21  Provided, however, the entity awarding  such  contract  may  award  such
    22  contract  to  any broadband internet service provider that is not certi-

    25  entity demonstrates to the public service  commission  that  either  (i)

    30  service provider not certified by the public service commission.
    31    §  5. The public service law is amended by adding a new section 231 to
    32  read as follows:
    33    § 231. Internet service neutrality. 1. For purposes of  this  section,
    34  "network management practice" shall mean a practice that has a primarily
    35  technical  network  management justification, but does not include other
    36  business practices. A "reasonable  network  management  practice"  shall
    37  mean  a  network  management  practice  that  is  primarily used for and
    38  tailored to achieving a legitimate network  management  purpose,  taking
    39  into  account  the particular network architecture and technology of the
    40  broadband internet access service.
    41    2. The commission shall certify annually that any  broadband  internet
    42  service provider qualified to do business in this state, does not:
    43    (a)  block  lawful  content,  applications,  services,  or non-harmful
    44  devices, subject to reasonable network management.
    45    (b) impair or degrade lawful internet traffic on the basis of internet
    46  content, application, or  service,  or  use  of  a  non-harmful  device,
    47  subject to reasonable network management.
    48    (c)  engage  in  paid  prioritization,  including, but not limited to,
    49  traffic shaping, prioritization, resource reservation, or other forms of
    50  preferential traffic management, either (i) in exchange for any form  of
    51  consideration from a third party, or (ii) to benefit an affiliated enti-
    52  ty, unless the broadband internet service provider demonstrates that the
    53  practice  would  provide a significant public interest benefit and would
    54  not harm the open nature of the internet.
    55    3. The commission shall annually  prepare  a  report  that  lists  the
    56  certification status for every broadband internet service provider qual-
