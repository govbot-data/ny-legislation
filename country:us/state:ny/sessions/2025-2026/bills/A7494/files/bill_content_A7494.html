Rpld & add Art 9 Title 4 §§960 - 967, RPT L
 
Establishes provisions for real property tax lien transfers to assist homeowners facing foreclosure based on unpaid taxes; repeals existing provisions relating to acceptance of taxes from certain loan corporations.

        AN ACT to amend the real property tax  law,  in  relation  to  tax  lien
          transfers; and to repeal title 4 of article 9 of the real property tax
          law relating to acceptance of taxes from certain loan corporations

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Title 4 of article 9  of  the  real  property  tax  law  is
     2  REPEALED and a new title 4 is added to read as follows:
     3                                   TITLE 4
     4                      TAX LIEN TRANSFER AUTHORIZATIONS
     5  Section 960. Legislative intent.
     6          961. Definitions.
     7          962. Tax lien transfer.
     8          963. Tax lien transfer authorization.
     9          964. Tax lien transfer certificate.
    10          965. Property tax lien payment agreement.
    11          966. Foreclosure.
    12          967. Redemption.
    13    §  960.  Legislative  intent. The Legislature finds that local govern-
    14  ments are struggling under the weight of increased costs, reduced reven-
    15  ues and increased stresses on their respective  municipal  budgets.  The
    16  Legislature  also  finds  that  property owners are struggling under the
    17  weight of high property taxes and need some assistance in  paying  those
    18  taxes in order to prevent foreclosure and the sale of their property tax
    19  liens  to  third parties involuntarily. The Legislature finds that it is
    20  in the best interests of the residents of the state of New York and  the
    21  local  governments  to  allow  property owners to voluntarily enter into
    22  agreements with private entities to provide for  the  payment  of  their
    23  property  taxes.  Currently,  property  owners are required to pay their
    24  taxes in full. Allowing property owners to  increase  the  payment  term

     1  will  help  alleviate the pressure on these already financially stressed

     4  taxes,  such  as  tax  lien sales and tax lien foreclosures, because the

     6    § 961. Definitions. As used in this title the  following  terms  shall
     7  have the following meanings:
     8    1.  "Property  tax  payor"  means  a person who advances money for the
     9  payment of real property taxes directly to a municipal government at the
    10  request of a property owner. Also referred to as a "transferee."
    11    2. "Property tax payment" means the amount of money advanced  for  the
    12  payment  of  property  taxes  pursuant  to  a property tax lien transfer
    13  authorization and property tax lien payment agreement.
    14    3. "Tax lien transfer authorization" shall have  the  meaning  as  set
    15  forth in section nine hundred sixty-three of this title.
    16    4. "Tax lien transfer certificate" shall have the meaning as set forth
    17  in section nine hundred sixty-four of this title.
    18    5. "Property tax lien payment agreement" shall have the meaning as set
    19  forth in section nine hundred sixty-five of this title.
    20    6. "Referee's deed" shall mean the deed issued to the purchaser at the
    21  foreclosure sale at the courthouse steps.
    22    7.  "Transferee"  means a person who advances money for the payment of
    23  real property taxes directly to a municipal government at the request of
    24  a property owner to whom  a  property  tax  lien  is  transferred.  Also
    25  referred to as a "property tax payor."
    26    § 962. Tax lien transfer. 1. A property owner may execute an affidavit
    27  in  the  form  of a tax lien transfer authorization that consents to and
    28  authorizes the transfer of the  property  tax  liens  on  such  property
    29  owner's  property from a municipal government to a property tax payor if
    30  the property tax payor pays the property tax payment on  behalf  of  the
    31  property owner.
    32    2. Upon receipt by the taxing jurisdiction of the property owner's tax
    33  lien  transfer authorization and the property tax payment from the prop-
    34  erty tax payor, the real property tax lien shall be transferred from the
    35  taxing jurisdiction to the property tax payor as reflected in a tax lien
    36  transfer certificate. The tax lien transfer  certificate  shall  certify
    37  that  the  taxing  jurisdiction  received written authorization from the
    38  property owner to transfer the taxes owed; that the property  tax  payor
    39  paid  the proper amount owed by the  property owner; and that the taxing
    40  jurisdiction's liens are transferred to the property tax  payor  in  the
    41  amounts listed in the tax lien transfer certificate.
    42    3.  The  creation  of  the  tax lien transfer certificate shall not be
    43  deemed to create a new tax  lien.  The  transferee,  or  its  heirs  and
    44  assigns,  shall  have  the  same rights, remedies, and priorities as the
    45  taxing jurisdiction, subject to the terms and conditions of the property
    46  tax lien payment agreement.   The creation  of  the  tax  lien  transfer
    47  certificate shall not be deemed to allow the property tax payor to fore-
    48  close  or  in  any  manner act upon the tax lien covered by the tax lien
    49  transfer certificate except for the collection of taxes, as provided for
    50  in the property tax lien payment agreement.
    51    4. The tax lien transfer certificate shall be recorded in  the  county
    52  clerk's  office  where  the  property  is situated, and the property tax
    53  payor shall pay any statutory fee for said recording.
    54    § 963. Tax lien transfer authorization. A sworn affidavit of a proper-
    55  ty owner authorizing the transfer of tax liens must contain the  follow-
    56  ing information:

    23    §  965.  Property  tax  lien payment agreement. 1. A property owner is
    24  permitted to enter into a property tax lien  payment  agreement  with  a
    25  property tax payor.
    26    2. Said agreement shall contain terms and conditions of repayment.
    27    3. The property tax payor shall be allowed to collect from the proper-
    28  ty  owner  an application fee not to exceed two hundred fifty dollars, a
    29  fee for title charges not to exceed four hundred dollars, reasonable and
    30  customary attorney's fees  not  to  exceed  one  thousand  dollars,  and
    31  reasonable  and  customary  closing  costs,  not  to exceed five hundred
    32  dollars, all of which may, at the property owner's request  and  written
    33  consent,  be  contained in and made a part of the amounts owed under the
    34  property tax lien payment agreement  and  the  amounts  secured  by  the
    35  transferred tax lien.
    36    4.  The property tax payor shall be allowed to collect interest not to
    37  exceed 1.5% per month on the amount  owed  according  to  the  tax  lien
    38  payment agreement.
    39    5.  Nothing  contained within this section shall be deemed to create a
    40  mortgage, note or other instrument governed by any law, state or  feder-
    41  al,  regulating  mortgage agreements, and the tax lien payment agreement
    42  shall be deemed a contract for the repayment  of  monies  and  shall  be
    43  governed  by applicable state laws relating to contracts, except for the
    44  foreclosure of said agreement, which is subject to the rules  and  regu-
    45  lations as governing mortgage foreclosures as detailed in the real prop-
    46  erty actions and proceedings law.
    47    6.  The  property  owner  or a prior recorded first lien mortgagee may
    48  obtain a release of the transferred tax lien by paying the property  tax
    49  payor all amounts due under the property tax lien payment agreement.
    50    §  966.  Foreclosure. 1. If at anytime the property tax payor files to
    51  foreclose upon the terms and conditions of the property  tax  agreement,
    52  the  property  tax  payor  shall foreclose pursuant to the real property
    53  actions and proceedings law as if foreclosing a mortgage.
    54    2. The property tax payor is entitled to recover foreclosure fees  for
    55  the following costs:

     2  competent jurisdiction; and
     3    (b)  Reasonable and customary title charges not to exceed four hundred

     5    (c) Reasonable and customary fees for service of  process  and  adver-
     6  tisement  as  approved  by a court of competent jurisdiction, so long as

    16  the lien in the manner provided by subdivision one of this section.
    17    4.  Nothing  in this section shall be deemed to affect the rights of a
    18  taxing jurisdiction under article eleven of this chapter  or  affect  or
    19  extend the redemption period, subject to section nine hundred sixty-sev-
    20  en of this title.
    21    § 967. Redemption. 1. If a property tax payor forecloses on a tax lien
    22  transferred as provided by section nine hundred sixty-two of this title,
    23  beginning  on  the date the referee's deed is recorded, the person whose
    24  property is sold as provided by section nine hundred sixty-six  of  this
    25  title  or  the mortgage servicer of a prior recorded first mortgage lien
    26  against the property is entitled to redeem the foreclosed property  from
    27  the  purchaser  or  the purchaser's successor by paying the purchaser or
    28  such purchaser's successor:
    29    (a) One hundred twenty percent of the amount paid by the purchaser  at
    30  the foreclosure sale; and
    31    (b)  The  amount  reasonably spent by the purchaser in connection with
    32  the upkeep of the property plus the legal judgment  rate  of  return  on
    33  that  amount.  The  property purchaser shall only be entitled to recover
    34  the costs contained within this  paragraph  if  the  purchaser  provides
    35  written proof of any and all costs incurred in the upkeep of the proper-
    36  ty prior to redemption.
    37    2.  The  right of redemption provided by this section may be exercised
    38  not later than the one hundred eightieth day after the date on which the
    39  referee's deed is filed in the office of the county clerk of the  county
    40  where the property is situated.
    41    §  2.  This  act shall take effect on the ninetieth day after it shall
    42  have become a law.
