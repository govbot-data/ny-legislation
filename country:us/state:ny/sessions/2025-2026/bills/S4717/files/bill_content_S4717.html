        AN ACT to amend the state finance law and the public authorities law, in
          relation to the purchase of personal protective equipment and  medical
          supplies produced or manufactured in the state of New York

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Subdivisions 1 and 3 of section 148 of  the  state  finance
     2  law, as added by section 2 of part BB of chapter 55 of the laws of 2021,
     3  are amended to read as follows:
     4    1.  Notwithstanding  any  other  provisions of law, all contracts over
     5  fifty thousand dollars in value made and awarded by  any  department  or
     6  agency of the state for the purchase of personal protective equipment or
     7  medical supplies shall require that the personal protective equipment or
     8  medical supply items be produced or made in whole or substantial part in
     9  the  [United States] state of New York; and shall utilize, to the extent
    10  practicable, preferred sources in accordance with  section  one  hundred
    11  sixty-two  of the state finance law, for at least thirty percent of such
    12  personal protective equipment or medical supply items.
    13    3. The provisions of this section shall not apply if the head  of  the
    14  department  or  agency  purchasing  the personal protective equipment or
    15  medical supplies, in [his or her] the head's sole discretion, determines
    16  that such provisions would not be in the public interest; that obtaining
    17  such personal protective equipment or medical supplies produced or  made
    18  in the state of New York or in the United States would increase the cost
    19  of the contract by an unreasonable amount; that such personal protective
    20  equipment or medical supplies cannot be produced or made in the state of
    21  New  York or in the United States in sufficient and reasonably available
    22  quantities and of satisfactory quality or design  to  meet  the  depart-
    23  ment's  or agency's requirements; or that purchasing personal protective
    24  equipment or medical supplies manufactured outside of the state  of  New

     9  law, as added by section 3 of part BB of chapter 55 of the laws of 2021,
    10  are amended to read as follows:
    11    1.  Notwithstanding  any  other  provisions of law, all contracts over
    12  fifty thousand dollars in value made and awarded by any state  authority
    13  for  the  purchase  of personal protective equipment or medical supplies
    14  shall require that the personal protective equipment or  medical  supply
    15  items  be  produced  or made in whole or substantial part in the [United
    16  States] state of New York; and shall utilize, to the extent practicable,
    17  preferred sources in accordance with section one  hundred  sixty-two  of
    18  the  state  finance  law,  for  at least thirty percent of such personal
    19  protective equipment or medical supply items.
    20    3. The provisions of this section shall not apply if the head  of  the
    21  state  authority purchasing the personal protective equipment or medical
    22  supplies, in [his or her] the head's sole  discretion,  determines  that
    23  such provisions would not be in the public interest; that obtaining such
    24  personal  protective  equipment  or medical supplies in the state of New
    25  York or outside of the United States would  increase  the  cost  of  the
    26  contract by an unreasonable amount; that such personal protective equip-
    27  ment  or medical supplies cannot be produced or made in the state of New
    28  York or in the United States  in  sufficient  and  reasonably  available
    29  quantities  and  of  satisfactory  quality  or  design to meet the state
    30  authority's requirements; or that purchasing personal protective  equip-
    31  ment  or  medical supplies manufactured outside of the state of New York
    32  or outside of the United States is necessary to avoid  a  delay  in  the
    33  delivery  of critical services that could compromise the public welfare.
    34  In the event a New York state source or a preferred source is  not  able
    35  to  supply  personal  protective  equipment or medical supply items, any
    36  other personal protective equipment or medical supply items produced  or
    37  made  in  whole or substantial part in the United States may be utilized
    38  to satisfy the requirements of this subdivision.
    39    § 3. This act shall take effect on the sixtieth  day  after  it  shall
    40  have become a law and shall apply to any state contracting opportunities
    41  advertised  on  or after such date and shall exclude contracts for which
    42  an invitation for bid, request for proposal, or similar solicitation has
    43  been issued prior to this proposal becoming effective.
