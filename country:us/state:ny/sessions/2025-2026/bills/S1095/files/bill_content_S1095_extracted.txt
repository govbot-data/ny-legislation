RAMOS, COMRIE, HOYLMAN-SIGAL, JACKSON, KRUEGER, RIVERA, SALAZAR, SEPULVEDA -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Codes AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to creating a written policy on the use of sustained auditory dispersal tools The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 837 of the executive law is amended by adding a new 2 subdivision 24 to read as follows: 3 24. (a) Establish a written policy prohibiting police agencies from 4 using sustained auditory dispersal tools. 5 (b) For the purposes of this subdivision, the term "sustained auditory 6 dispersal tool" shall mean any long range acoustic device, sonic weapon, 7 sonic cannon, or any high powered sound magnifier, using piezoelectric 8 transducer or other technology, to emit sustained tones, beeps, chirps, 9 or any deterrent tone or other non-verbal communication at decibels 10 shown to cause hearing loss or other dangerous effects for a sustained 11 period. Any such tone or non-verbal communication over ninety decibels 12 shall be considered presumptively unreasonable. The term "sustained 13 auditory dispersal tool" shall not include any device designed to magni- 14 fy information or orders via verbal communication at ninety decibels or 15 below, and properly administered according to health and safety stand- 16 ards, by properly trained personnel, provided, however, that such use of 17 any such device as a deterrent and not as a mode of verbal communication 18 shall cause such device to be a "sustained auditory dispersal tool". 19 (c) (i) Any individual who has been subjected to a sustained auditory 20 dispersal tool by a law enforcement officer in violation of this section 21 or the written policy of the division of criminal justice services 22 promulgated under this section may institute a civil action against the 23 employing agency of such law enforcement officer for any of the follow- 24 ing: EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
