Simon) -- read once and referred to the Committee on Governmental Operations AN ACT to amend the public buildings law, in relation to requiring state properties which utilize irrigation for landscaped areas to be equipped with smart irrigation systems The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The public buildings law is amended by adding a new section 2 148 to read as follows: 3 § 148. Smart irrigation systems required for state property. 1. For 4 purposes of this section, the following terms shall have the following 5 meanings: 6 (a) "Smart irrigation system" shall mean an in-ground, automated 7 watering system that uses digital technology to create a custom map of a 8 property and uses real-time weather data or other environmental condi- 9 tions to optimize water use and limit unnecessary irrigation. 10 (b) "State property" shall mean any real property owned, leased, or 11 managed by the state of New York, including but not limited to build- 12 ings, facilities, grounds, lawns, and landscaped areas. 13 2. All state property that utilizes irrigation for landscaped areas, 14 including lawns and grass, shall be equipped with a smart irrigation 15 system in accordance with the provisions of this section. 16 3. (a) All new construction and all projects involving the installa- 17 tion or substantial rehabilitation of irrigation systems on or after the 18 effective date of this section shall comply with the requirements of 19 this section. 20 (b) All existing irrigation systems on state property shall be retro- 21 fitted or replaced to comply with this section no later than five years 22 after the effective date of this section. 23 4. The commissioner of general services shall promulgate rules and 24 regulations necessary to implement the provisions of this section, 25 including but not limited to: 26 (a) minimum standards for smart irrigation systems; 27 (b) water efficiency benchmarks; EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
