SCARCELLA-SPANTON -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Codes AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to establishing the police training council; and to repeal certain provisions of the exec- utive law related thereto The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Sections 839, 840, and 841 of the executive law are 2 REPEALED and three new sections 839, 840, and 841 are added to read as 3 follows: 4 § 839. Police training council. 1. There is hereby created within the 5 office of public safety within the division, a police training council, 6 to be administered by the deputy commissioner for criminal justice 7 services, and composed of nine members who are law enforcement profes- 8 sionals representing academic, legal, supervisory, law enforcement labor 9 organizations as well as community organizations in order to elicit a 10 wide cross section of views in determining an appropriate annual train- 11 ing curriculum for all law enforcement agencies established with police 12 officer or peace officer status throughout the state. Such members shall 13 be selected as follows: 14 (a) three members appointed by the governor; 15 (b) two members appointed by the temporary president of the senate; 16 (c) two members appointed by the speaker of the assembly; 17 (d) one member appointed by the minority leader of the senate; and 18 (e) one member appointed by the minority leader of the assembly. 19 2. The governor shall designate from among the members of the council 20 a chairperson who shall serve at the pleasure of the governor and who 21 shall be compensated for such position. 22 3. All members of the council shall be appointed for terms of two 23 years, such terms to commence on April first, and expire on March thir- 24 ty-first. Functions, powers and duties of council. The council shall 32 recommend to the commissioner rules and regulations with respect to 33 establishing a statewide mandatory training curriculum for all law 34 enforcement agencies established with police officer or peace officer 35 status throughout the state. Such training curriculum shall include but 36 not be limited to updates in legislative changes related to laws, rules 37 and regulations involving the law enforcement profession as well as 38 police interaction with civilians, use of force, powers and procedures 39 for arrest, civil rights, policing tactics and any issue or subject 40 deemed relevant by the council that will enhance police professional 41 standards. 42 § 841. Functions, powers and duties of the commissioner with respect 43 to the council. In addition to the functions, powers and duties other- 44 wise provided by this article, the commissioner shall, with the general 45 advice of the council approve a statewide mandatory training curriculum 46 for all law enforcement agencies throughout the state. 47 § 2.
