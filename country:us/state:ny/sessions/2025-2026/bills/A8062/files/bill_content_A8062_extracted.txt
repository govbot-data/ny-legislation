McDONALD -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Economic Development AN ACT to amend the alcoholic beverage control law, in relation to the sale of private-label liquor at retail for consumption off the prem- ises The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The alcoholic beverage control law is amended by adding a 2 new section 106-c to read as follows: 3 § 106-c. Sale of private-label liquor at retail for consumption off 4 the premises. 1. For the purposes of this section, the following terms 5 shall have the following meanings: 6 (a) "Private-label liquor" means liquor distilled by a licensed 7 distiller and bottled by a bottler in containers bearing a brand name or 8 trademark for sale at retail directly by the owner or licensee of such 9 brand name or trademark, or through retail dealers affiliated with such 10 owner or licensee by a cooperative or franchise agreement. 11 (b) "Holder of a license to sell liquor at retail for consumption on 12 the premises" shall mean any person or entity holding a valid license 13 issued under section sixty-four of this chapter permitting the sale of 14 alcoholic beverages for on-premises consumption at a restaurant. 15 (c) "Licensed distiller" shall mean any person or entity holding a 16 valid license to produce distilled spirits in New York state issued 17 under section sixty-one of this chapter. 18 2. (a) Notwithstanding any other provision of this chapter, a holder 19 of a license to sell liquor at retail for consumption on the premises 20 may sell bottles of private-label liquor at retail for off-premises 21 consumption provided that such private-label liquor is: 22 (i) distilled within the borders of the state of New York by a 23 licensed distiller; and EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
