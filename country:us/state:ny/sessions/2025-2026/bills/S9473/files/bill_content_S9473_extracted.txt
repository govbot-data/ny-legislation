HARCKHAM -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Environmental Conservation AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law and the agriculture and markets law, in relation to enacting the "lead-free game donation act" The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Short title. This act shall be known and may be cited as 2 the "lead-free game donation act". 3 § 2. Legislative findings. The legislature finds that: 4 1. Lead is a potent neurotoxin; no level of exposure is considered 5 safe for humans. Children and pregnant people are most at risk. 6 2. Scientific studies and public health reviews have demonstrated that 7 game taken with lead-based ammunition contains small, widely dispersed 8 lead fragments. 9 3. Recipients of charitable food donations, often the neediest New 10 Yorkers, should not bear the risk of lead contamination in donated game. 11 4. Chapter 358 of the laws of 2025 expanded lawful participation in 12 archery seasons, including through the use of crossbows. 13 5. Encouraging the continuing growth of the use of non-lead ammunition 14 is consistent with existing department of environmental conservation 15 outreach programs, and aligns with New York's broader wildlife and 16 public health goals. 17 § 3. Paragraph d of subdivision 9 of section 11-0917 of the environ- 18 mental conservation law, as amended by chapter 468 of the laws of 2011, 19 is amended to read as follows: 20 d. Game legally taken may be donated to, and possessed, prepared and 21 distributed by a charitable or not-for-profit organization which serves 22 or distributes food without cost to the poor or needy. This act shall take effect two years after it shall have become a 34 law.
