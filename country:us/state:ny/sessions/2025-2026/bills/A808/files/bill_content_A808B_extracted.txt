AN ACT to amend the public health law, in relation to establishing a
drug checking program including requirements for enhanced drug check-
ing service delivery and public health surveillance
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The public health law is amended by adding a new section
2 507 to read as follows:
3 § 507. Drug checking services program. 1. For purposes of this
4 section, the following terms shall have the following meanings:
5 (a) "Enhanced drug checking services" means the utilization of all
6 forms of drug testing equipment including complex technology or equip-
7 ment designed to analyze substances and provide results at point of
8 testing or point of care, which may include but is not limited to, chem-
9 ical screening devices such as infrared spectrophotometers, raman spec-
10 trophotometers, or ion mobility spectrometers.
11 (b) "Public health surveillance" means the continuous and systematic
12 collection, analysis, and interpretation of data needed for the plan-
13 ning, implementation, and evaluation of public health initiatives.
14 Public health surveillance may be used for the following purposes:
15 (i) as an early warning system for impending public health emergen-
16 cies;
17 (ii) to document the impact of an intervention;
18 (iii) to track progress towards specific goals;

1 (iv) to monitor and clarify the epidemiology of health outcomes; and
2 (v) to inform the public health policy and practices.
3 2. The department shall:
4 (a) establish a program to authorize a county or municipality or other
5 entity to provide enhanced drug checking services to assist individuals
6 in determining whether a drug or controlled substance contains contam-
7 inants, toxic substances, or hazardous compounds; and
8 (b) establish public health surveillance of the unregulated drug
9 supply to monitor trends and the impact on health outcomes and increase
10 public awareness of new substances in the unregulated drug supply.
11 3. The commissioner shall promulgate rules and regulations for the
12 provision of enhanced drug checking services which shall include but not
13 be limited to:
14 (a) providing information and harm reduction advice to help individ-
15 uals make informed decisions about drug use;
16 (b) testing any drug or substance that an individual presents for
17 checking to ascertain the composition and likely identity of such drug
18 or substance;
19 (c) advising the individual who presented a drug or substance for
20 checking of the results of the testing;
21 (d) disposing of or arranging for the disposal of, any sample of a
22 drug or substance used in testing and any drug or substance surrendered
23 by any individual for disposal; and
24 (e) if necessary, arranging for a sample of a drug or substance to be
25 tested by a department approved laboratory.
26 4. The department shall develop policies and procedures to:
27 (a) utilize enhanced drug checking technology for public health
28 surveillance;
29 (b) develop requirements for technician training to ensure accurate
30 point-of-testing and point-of-care results;
31 (c) identify appropriate equipment to use; and
32 (d) establish reporting processes.
33 5. Employees, contractors, and volunteers of the department, direc-
34 tors, managers, employees, contractors, and volunteers of an entity
35 providing drug checking services, owners of properties where drug check-
36 ing services occur, counties, municipalities or other entities author-
37 ized by the department, and individuals presenting drugs or substances
38 for checking, acting in the course and scope of employment or engaged in
39 good faith in the provision of enhanced drug checking services, in
40 accordance with established protocols, shall not be subject to:
41 (a) arrest, charges, or prosecution pursuant to article thirty-three
42 of this chapter or any violation or misdemeanor, including for attempt-
43 ing, aiding and abetting, or conspiracy to commit a violation or misde-
44 meanor pursuant to article two hundred twenty of the penal law; or
45 (b) a civil or administrative penalty or liability of any kind, or
46 disciplinary action by a professional licensing board, for conduct
47 relating to the provision of authorized drug checking services unless
48 such conduct was performed in a negligent manner or in bad faith.
49 6. The department and entities authorized by the department shall not
50 collect, maintain, use, or disclose any personal information relating to
51 an individual from whom the department or other authorized entity
52 receives any drug or substance for checking or disposal.
53 7. The result of any test carried out by the department or an author-
54 ized entity in relation to any drug or substance presented for checking
55 shall not be admissible as evidence in any criminal or civil proceedings
