AN ACT to amend the public health law, in relation to establishing a
drug checking program including requirements for enhanced drug check-
ing service delivery and public health surveillance
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The public health law is amended by adding a new section
2 507 to read as follows:
3 § 507. Drug checking services program. 1. For purposes of this
4 section, the following terms shall have the following meanings:
5 (a) "Enhanced drug checking services" means the utilization of all
6 forms of drug testing equipment including complex technology or equip-
7 ment designed to analyze substances and provide results at point of
8 testing or point of care, which may include but is not limited to, chem-
9 ical screening devices such as infrared spectrophotometers, raman spec-
10 trophotometers, or ion mobility spectrometers.
11 (b) "Public health surveillance" means the continuous and systematic
12 collection, analysis, and interpretation of data needed for the plan-
13 ning, implementation, and evaluation of public health initiatives.
14 Public health surveillance may be used for the following purposes:
15 (i) as an early warning system for impending public health emergen-
16 cies;
17 (ii) to document the impact of an intervention;
18 (iii) to track progress towards specific goals;
19 (iv) to monitor and clarify the epidemiology of health outcomes; and

1 (v) to inform the public health policy and practices.
2 2. The department shall:
3 (a) establish a program to authorize a county or municipality or other
4 entity to provide enhanced drug checking services to assist individuals
5 in determining whether a drug or controlled substance contains contam-
6 inants, toxic substances, or hazardous compounds; and
7 (b) establish public health surveillance of the unregulated drug
8 supply to monitor trends and the impact on health outcomes and increase
9 public awareness of new substances in the unregulated drug supply.
10 3. The commissioner shall promulgate rules and regulations for the
11 provision of enhanced drug checking services which shall include but not
12 be limited to:
13 (a) providing information and harm reduction advice to help individ-
14 uals make informed decisions about drug use;
15 (b) testing any drug or substance that an individual presents for
16 checking to ascertain the composition and likely identity of such drug
17 or substance;
18 (c) advising the individual who presented a drug or substance for
19 checking of the results of the testing;
20 (d) disposing of or arranging for the disposal of, any sample of a
21 drug or substance used in testing and any drug or substance surrendered
22 by any individual for disposal; and
23 (e) if necessary, arranging for a sample of a drug or substance to be
24 tested by a department approved laboratory.
25 4. The department shall develop policies and procedures to:
26 (a) utilize enhanced drug checking technology for public health
27 surveillance;
28 (b) develop requirements for technician training to ensure accurate
29 point-of-testing and point-of-care results;
30 (c) identify appropriate equipment to use; and
31 (d) establish reporting processes.
32 5. Employees, contractors, and volunteers of the department, direc-
33 tors, managers, employees, contractors, and volunteers of an entity
34 providing drug checking services, owners of properties where drug check-
35 ing services occur, counties, municipalities or other entities author-
36 ized by the department, and individuals presenting drugs or substances
37 for checking, acting in the course and scope of employment or engaged in
38 good faith in the provision of enhanced drug checking services, in
39 accordance with established protocols, shall not be subject to:
40 (a) arrest, charges, or prosecution pursuant to article thirty-three
41 of this chapter or any violation or misdemeanor, including for attempt-
42 ing, aiding and abetting, or conspiracy to commit a violation or misde-
43 meanor pursuant to article two hundred twenty of the penal law; or
44 (b) a civil or administrative penalty or liability of any kind, or
45 disciplinary action by a professional licensing board, for conduct
46 relating to the provision of authorized drug checking services unless
47 such conduct was performed in a negligent manner or in bad faith.
48 6. The department and entities authorized by the department shall not
49 collect, maintain, use, or disclose any personal information relating to
50 an individual from whom the department or other authorized entity
51 receives any drug or substance for checking or disposal.
52 7. The result of any test carried out by the department or an author-
53 ized entity in relation to any drug or substance presented for checking
54 shall not be admissible as evidence in any criminal or civil proceedings
55 against the individual from whom the drug or substance was received,
56 unless submitted by such individual.

2 amended by chapter 547 of the laws of 1981, paragraph (d) as amended by
3 chapter 635 of the laws of 1997, is amended to read as follows:
4 1. The provisions of this article restricting the possession and
5 control of controlled substances and official New York state
6 prescription forms shall not apply:
7 (a) to common carriers or to [warehousemen] warehouse worker while
8 engaged in lawfully transporting or storing such substances, or to any

10 or
11 (b) to public officers or their employees in the lawful performance of
12 their official duties requiring possession or control of controlled
13 substances; or
14 (c) to temporary incidental possession by employees or agents of
15 persons lawfully entitled to possession, or by persons whose possession
16 is for the purpose of aiding public officers in performing their offi-
17 cial duties[.]; or
18 (d) to individuals providing enhanced drug checking services author-
19 ized under section five hundred seven of this chapter, and individuals
20 presenting drugs or substances for checking in the provision of such
21 authorized drug checking services; or
22 (e) to a duly authorized agent of an incorporated society for the
23 prevention of cruelty to animals or a municipal animal control facility
24 for the limited purpose of buying, possessing, and dispensing to regis-
25 tered and certified personnel, ketamine hydrochloride to anesthetize

28 public interest, register such duly authorized agent and such agent
29 shall file, on a quarterly basis, a report of purchase, possession, and
30 use of ketamine hydrochloride and/or sodium pentobarbital, which report
31 shall be certified by the society for the prevention of cruelty to
32 animals or municipal animal control facility as to its accuracy and
33 validity. This report shall be in addition to any other record keeping
34 and reporting requirements of state and federal law and regulation. The
35 department shall adopt rules and regulations providing for the registra-
36 tion and certification of any individual who, under the direction of the

38 prevention of cruelty to animals, or municipal animal control facility,

40 barbital to euthanize animals, including but not limited to dogs and

44 of cruelty to animals and animal control facilities. Nothing in this

46 incorporated societies for the prevention of cruelty to animals and

48 § 3. This act shall take effect immediately; provided, however, that
49 subdivisions one, two, three and four of section 507 of the public
50 health law, as added by section one of this act, shall take effect on
51 the first of April next succeeding the fiscal year in which this act
52 shall have become a law. Effective immediately, the addition, amendment,
53 and/or repeal of any rule or regulation necessary for the implementation
54 of this act on its effective date are authorized to be made and
55 completed on or before such effective date.
