Labor. Legacy.," the Summit blends physical activation, health screenings, real conversations, and actionable resources to move participants from awareness to sustained engagement, serving as a catalyst for long-term health behavior change and connecting attendees to care, community, and a clear path toward improved well-being and lasting impact; and WHEREAS, The Summit's programming encompasses youth activations, health screenings and labs, panel discussions, breakout sessions, Barbershop Talk, an art showcase, and direct access to Black physicians, residents, and trainees across specialties for real conversations and real mentorship; and WHEREAS, The ManKave Summit operates from the foundational understanding that the health disparities facing Black men and boys in this country are not inevitable but are the product of systemic barriers to care, cultural mistrust born of historical harm, and the persistent failure of mainstream health institutions to meet Black men where they are; and that the most powerful interventions are those that come from within the community, led by people who look like, live like, and love the men they are trying to reach; and WHEREAS, This Legislative Body is proudly moved to honor Maven Community Health, Inc., its leadership, its medical partners, and every Black man and boy who will walk through the doors of The ManKave Summit 2026 in pursuit of a healthier, longer, and more intentional life; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to commend Maven Community Health, Inc. upon the occasion of hosting The ManKave Summit 2026 on June 13, 2026, and to extend congratulations and deep appreciation for the life-saving work this organization does every day; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Dr. Victor Ukwu and the staff of Maven Community Health, Inc., in recognition of their unwavering commitment to the health, legacy, and dignity of Black men and boys in Brooklyn and beyond.
