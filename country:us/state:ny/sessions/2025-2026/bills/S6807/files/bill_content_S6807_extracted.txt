Section 902 of the education law, as amended by chapter 477 of 51 the laws of 2004, paragraph a of subdivision 2 as amended by chapter 376 52 of the laws of 2015, paragraph b of subdivision 2 as amended by chapter 53 501 of the laws of 2005, is amended to read as follows: 54 § 902. Employment of health and mental health professionals. 1. a. As 55 used in this article "health professionals" means persons duly licensed 56 or otherwise authorized to practice a health profession pursuant to S. 6807 3 1 applicable law, including, but not limited to, physicians, registered 2 professional nurses, nurse practitioners, physicians assistants, optome- 3 trists, dentists, dental hygienists, dietitians and nutritionists, and 4 audiologists. 5 b. As used in this article, "mental health professionals" shall mean 6 certified school psychologists and certified or licensed school social 7 workers duly authorized to provide mental health services pursuant to 8 applicable law. 9 2. a. The board of education, and the trustee or board of trustees of 10 each school district, shall employ, at a compensation to be agreed upon 11 by the parties, a qualified physician, or a nurse practitioner to the 12 extent authorized by the nurse practice act and consistent with subdivi- 13 sion three of section six thousand nine hundred two of this chapter, to 14 perform the duties of the director of school health services, including 15 any duties conferred on the school physician or school medical inspector 16 under any provision of law, to perform and coordinate the provision of 17 health services in the public schools and to provide health appraisals 18 of students attending the public schools in the city or district. The 19 physicians or nurse practitioners so employed shall be duly licensed 20 pursuant to applicable law. 21 b. Any such board or trustees may employ one or more school nurses, 22 who shall be registered professional nurses, as well as other health 23 professionals, as may be required. Such registered professional nurses 24 and other health professionals shall aid the director of school health 25 services of the district and shall perform such duties, including health 26 instruction for the benefit of the public schools as may be prescribed 27 by such board or trustees, in compliance with each such health profes- 28 sional's practice act. 29 c. Any such board or trustees shall employ one or more school psychol- 30 ogist and school social worker, as may be required. Such professional 31 shall perform such duties as may be prescribed by such board or trus- 32 tees, in compliance with such professional's authorization. 33 3. Health and mental health professionals may be employed by the trus- 34 tees or boards of education of two or more school districts, and the 35 compensation of such health and mental health professionals, and the 36 expenses incurred in providing school health and mental health services 37 for students as provided in this article, shall be borne jointly by such 38 districts, and be apportioned among them in any manner agreed to by such 39 districts in a sharing agreement entered pursuant to section one hundred 40 nineteen-o of the general municipal law. The trustees or boards of 41 education of two or more school districts in a supervisory district may 42 enter into an agreement, or agreements as may be required, with a board 43 of cooperative educational services for the provision of the services of 44 one or more mental health professional or registered professional nurs- 45 es, and other health professionals to perform health and mental health 46 services, including health and mental health instruction in such 47 districts. 48 4. The mental health professionals required herein shall be in addi- 49 tion to such personnel as necessary to implement the provisions of the 50 federal Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), as amended. 51 § 3. This act shall take effect on the first of September next 52 succeeding the date on which it shall have become a law. Effective imme- 53 diately, the addition, amendment and/or repeal of any rule or regulation 54 necessary for the implementation of this act on its effective date is 55 authorized to be made and completed on or before such effective date.
