The penal law is amended by adding a new section 240.28 to 2 read as follows: 3 § 240.28 Harassment of a first responder. 4 1. A person is guilty of harassment of a first responder when, after 4 receiving a verbal warning not to approach from a first responder such 5 person knows or reasonably should know is a first responder, where such 6 first responder is engaged in the lawful performance of a legal duty, to 7 knowingly and willfully violate such warning and approach or remain 8 within twenty-five feet of the first responder with the intent to: 9 (a) Impede or interfere with the first responder's ability to perform 10 such duty; 11 (b) Threaten the first responder with physical harm; or 12 (c) engage in a course of conduct directed at a first responder which 13 causes substantial emotional distress to the first responder and which 14 serves no legitimate purpose. 15 Harassment of a first responder is a class A misdemeanor. 16 § 2.
