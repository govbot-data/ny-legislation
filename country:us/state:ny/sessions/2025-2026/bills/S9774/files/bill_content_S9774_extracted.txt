SEPULVEDA -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Finance AN ACT to amend the executive law and the administrative code of the city of New York, in relation to requiring annual reports of legal settlement payments related to law enforcement activity The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The executive law is amended by adding a new section 838-c 2 to read as follows: 3 § 838-c. Settlement reporting. 1. The comptroller shall make an annual 4 report which details all payments made for legal settlements relating to 5 law enforcement activity. Such report shall include, but not be limited 6 to, monetary amounts, plaintiffs and named parties, and shall be 7 published on the website of the comptroller, provided, however, that 8 every plaintiff whose name would be included in the report shall have 9 the option to have a pseudonym used in place of such plaintiff's name. 10 Any named party in a legal settlement relating to law enforcement activ- 11 ity who is or was a defendant in such action shall be named in the 12 report and shall not have the option to use a pseudonym in place of such 13 defendant's name. As used in this section, "law enforcement" means 14 every local police department, local correctional facility, each county 15 sheriff, the division of the state police, the department of corrections 16 and community supervision, and every agency that employs a peace officer 17 in this state. 18 2. The administrative code of the city of New York is amended by 18 adding a new section 14-198.1 to read as follows: 19 § 14-198.1 Settlement reporting. The comptroller shall make an annual 20 report which details all payments made for legal settlements relating to 21 law enforcement activity. Such report shall include, but not be limited 22 to, monetary amounts, plaintiffs and named parties, and details and 23 descriptions of any allegations or misconduct involved in each case, and 24 shall be published on the website of the comptroller, provided, however, 25 that the comptroller shall provide every plaintiff whose name would be 26 included in the report the option to have a pseudonym used in place of 27 such plaintiff's name. Any named party in a legal settlement relating 28 to law enforcement activity who is or was a defendant in such action 29 shall be named in the report and shall not have the option to use a 30 pseudonym in place of such defendant's name. Notwithstanding any 31 provision of law to the contrary, failure to report all information 32 required pursuant to the provisions of this section by a law enforcement 33 agency to the comptroller shall result in a loss of both eligibility for 34 city funding and accreditation for such agency. Such eligibility for 35 funding and accreditation shall be restored once such law enforcement 36 agency is in compliance with the provisions of this section. 37 § 3.
