        Introduced  by  M.  of  A.  CUNNINGHAM  -- read once and referred to the

        AN ACT to amend the economic development law, in relation to  establish-
          ing  the  "save our small-business grant program" to aid certain small
          businesses impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic; and  providing  for  the
          repeal of such provisions upon expiration thereof
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1.  The economic development law is amended by  adding  a  new
     2  section 139-a to read as follows:
     3    §  139-a.  Save our small-business grant program.  1. For the purposes
     4  of this section, the "save our small-business grant program" shall  mean
     5  the program established pursuant to subdivision two of this section.
     6    2.  The  division  for small-business, in conjunction with the state's
     7  regional economic development councils, shall develop  and  implement  a
     8  save  our  small-business  grant program to aid small-businesses in this
     9  state  impacted  by  the  COVID-19  pandemic,  in  accordance  with  the
    10  provisions of this section.
    11    3.  The  state's  regional economic development councils shall provide
    12  the funds necessary to operate the save our small-business grant program
    13  in accordance with the provisions of this section.
    14    4. The division for small-business, in conjunction  with  the  state's
    15  regional  economic  development  councils,  shall develop an application
    16  process whereby small-businesses located in this  state  may  submit  an
    17  application  for  a  grant to the save our small-business grant program.
    18  The save our small-business grant program shall process and approve such
    19  applications in a manner to be determined by the division for  small-bu-
    20  siness  in  conjunction  with  the state's regional economic development
    21  councils.  Such application process shall be published conspicuously  on
    22  the  regional  economic development councils' website. For a small-busi-
    23  ness to be eligible for such approval, such small-business shall  demon-

     3    5.  The division for small-businesses, in conjunction with the state's
     4  regional economic development councils, shall develop a list of qualify-
     5  ing hardships that shall satisfy the requirement under subdivision  four

     7  conspicuously on the regional economic development councils' website.

    22  the  commission  may maintain an accurate and timely effective data base
    23  of the official text of the laws of the state of New York in furtherance
    24  of effectuating the provisions of section 44 of the legislative law  and
    25  section 70-b of the public officers law.
