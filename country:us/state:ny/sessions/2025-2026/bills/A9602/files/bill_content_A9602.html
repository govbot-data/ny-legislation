Add §29-f, amd §3, Veterans' Services L
 
Establishes a voluntary veterans' registry for the purpose of providing information on federal, state, and local governmental benefits, programs, and services available to veterans, and to link veterans with resources that can provide assistance.

        Introduced  by M. of A. BRONSON -- read once and referred to the Commit-
          tee on Veterans' Affairs
 
        AN ACT to amend the veterans' services law, in relation to  establishing
          a voluntary veterans' registry
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The veterans' services law  is  amended  by  adding  a  new
     2  section 29-f to read as follows:
     3    §  29-f.  Voluntary veterans' registry. 1. The department shall estab-
     4  lish a voluntary veterans' registry for the purpose of providing  infor-
     5  mation on federal, state, and local governmental benefits, programs, and
     6  services available to veterans, and to link veterans with resources that
     7  can provide assistance.
     8    2.  For  the  purposes of this section, the following terms shall have
     9  the following meanings:
    10    (a) "State agency" means  any  department,  board,  bureau,  division,
    11  commission, council, or committee within the executive branch, the state
    12  university  of  New York, the city university of New York, or any public
    13  authority under control of the executive branch  of  the  state  of  New
    14  York.
    15    (b)  "Voluntary  veterans' registry" or "registry" means the voluntary
    16  veterans' registry established  pursuant  to  subdivision  one  of  this
    17  section.
    18    (c)  "State  veterans' service agency" means a state veterans' service
    19  agency established pursuant to section thirteen of this article.
    20    (d) "Local veterans' service agency" means a local  veterans'  service
    21  agency established pursuant to section fourteen of this article.
    22    3.  Upon  a veteran's registration with the registry, if permission is
    23  granted by such veteran, such veteran's information shall be shared with
    24  other state agencies, state veterans' service agencies, and local veter-
    25  ans' service agencies.
    26    4. The department shall coordinate with other state agencies that have
    27  contact with veterans to establish a paper and online  voluntary  veter-
    28  ans'  registry form to enable state agencies to assist the department in

     1  registering veterans who wish  to  be  included  in  the  registry.  The
     2  department  shall  provide  access  to  such  paper and online voluntary
     3  veterans' registry form which shall contain a statement  indicating  the
     4  purpose  of the registry and that filling out such form does not guaran-
     5  tee that a veteran is eligible for any benefits. Such form shall include
     6  but not be limited to the following information:
     7    (a) name;
     8    (b) address;
     9    (c) gender;
    10    (d) branch of military service;
    11    (e) dates of military service;
    12    (f) contact information, including phone or email;
    13    (g) a signed statement, or for the  online  version,  a  box  that  is
    14  checked, indicating that the veteran grants permission to the department
    15  to  store  and share such veteran's information with the local veterans'
    16  service agencies' directors and with the other state agencies to  ensure
    17  that  the  state  provides  such  veteran assistance in receiving earned
    18  benefits; and
    19    (h) any other information that the department may request.
    20    5. Each state agency shall:
    21    (a) utilize the paper or online voluntary veterans' registry  form  to
    22  develop  a  mechanism  to  identify  veterans  the agency serves for the
    23  purpose of expanding the registry;
    24    (b) for each veteran who wishes to be included in the voluntary veter-
    25  ans' registry, the agency shall provide assistance in  filling  out  the
    26  paper or online registry form for the department to add each such veter-
    27  an to the registry; and
    28    (c)  submit  a  veteran's paper registry form to the department within
    29  sixty days from the date the veteran opts in to the voluntary  veterans'
    30  registry.
    31    6.  Information collected under this section shall not be sold or used
    32  for commercial purposes or used for purposes not  specified  under  this
    33  section.  Information regarding a veteran's status shall be protected as
    34  personal confidential material and shall  only  be  used  to  assist  in
    35  providing  information  about  benefits  and  entitlements  for veterans
    36  residing in the state on the federal, state, and local government level.
    37  Data collected under this section may  be  used  by  the  department  to
    38  assist  in  formulating  future  policy  initiatives and shared with the
    39  veterans' services commission pursuant to section three of this  article
    40  in its role of assisting the commissioner in the formulation of policies
    41  affecting veterans.
    42    7.  On or before December thirty-first, two thousand twenty-seven, and
    43  annually thereafter, the commissioner  shall  submit  a  report  to  the
    44  governor,  the  speaker  of the assembly, and the temporary president of
    45  the senate. Such report shall include, but not be limited to, the  total
    46  number of voluntary veterans' registry forms that were completed for the
    47  calendar  year  and  a  breakdown  of  registered veterans by gender and
    48  location.
    49    § 2. Subdivision 5 of section 3  of  the  veterans'  services  law  is
    50  amended to read as follows:
    51    5.  The  commission  shall  have  power,  and it shall be its duty, to
    52  assist the state commissioner in the formulation of  policies  affecting
    53  veterans,  including  using  data  collected pursuant to section twenty-
    54  nine-f of this article, and in the coordination  of  all  operations  of
    55  state agencies relating to veterans' services.
