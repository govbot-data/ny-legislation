BAILEY, MAY, MYRIE -- read twice and ordered print- ed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Energy and Telecommunications AN ACT to require the public service commission to undertake a study on various approaches to increasing broadband access within this state; and providing for the repeal of such provisions upon expiration there- of The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The public service commission shall, within thirty days of 2 the effective date of this act, undertake a study on various approaches 3 to increasing broadband access within this state. The public service 4 commission shall study and make determinations on issues including but 5 not limited to: (i) current broadband capacity within this state, 6 including average internet access speeds statewide and in various 7 regions of the state; (ii) regulatory and statutory barriers to improv- 8 ing broadband speeds; (iii) comparative state policies that have aided 9 the increase in broadband speeds in other states, and whether such meas- 10 ures would be similarly effective in this state; (iv) whether the use of 11 public-private partnerships would aid in the goal of increasing broad- 12 band speeds, and whether relevant businesses would be open to such part- 13 nerships; and (v) best practices recommended within the National Broad- 14 band Plan issued by the Federal Communications Commission and whether 15 the public service commission can readily adopt and put into place any 16 such recommendations. 17 § 2. The public service commission may conduct any hearings or take 18 any written testimony as it deems necessary in order to aid such study, 19 and shall take all other steps necessary to provide a thorough analysis 20 of all issues related to the provisions listed in section one of this 21 act.
