CUNNINGHAM, SHIMSKY, LEVENBERG, MAHER, BURROUGHS -- read once and referred to the Committee on Transportation -- recom- mitted to the Committee on Transportation in accordance with Assembly Rule 3, sec. 2 -- committee discharged, bill amended, ordered reprinted as amended and recommitted to said committee AN ACT to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to the accept- ance of electronic signatures on form MV-82, application for registration/title The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The vehicle and traffic law is amended by adding a new 2 title 11-A to read as follows: 3 TITLE 11-A 4 ELECTRONIC SIGNATURES 5 ARTICLE 48-D 6 ELECTRONIC SIGNATURES 7 Section 2500. Definitions. 8 2501. Acceptance of electronic signatures. 9 2502. Requirements and limitations. 10 2503. Utilization. 11 2504. Conflict. 12 § 2500. Definitions. For the purposes of this article: 13 (a) "Document" means form MV-82, application for registration/title, 14 as prescribed by the commissioner, and shall not include any other form, 15 certificate, record, or instrument required under this chapter. 16 (b) "Electronic signature" means an electronic sound, symbol, or proc- 17 ess, attached to or logically associated with form MV-82 (application 18 for registration/title) and executed or adopted by a person with the 19 intent to sign, agree, or otherwise acknowledge the information and 20 facts to be accurate and correct. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. LBD03600-03-6 A. 4066--A 2 1 (c) "Electronic" means relating to technology having electrical, 2 digital, magnetic, wireless, optical, electromagnetic, or similar capa- 3 bilities as used solely in connection with form MV-82 (application for 4 registration/title). 5 § 2501. Acceptance of electronic signatures. (a) The commissioner may 6 accept electronic signatures on form MV-82. 7 (b) An electronic signature affixed to form MV-82, when permitted by 8 the commissioner, shall have the same force and legal effect as a hand- 9 written "wet ink" signature. 10 § 2502. Requirements and limitations. (a) The commissioner: 11 (1) may require that form MV-82 containing an electronic signature to 12 include an indication that the signature is electronic; 13 (2) may establish procedures for the acceptance and processing of form 14 MV-82 executed with an electronic signature, including procedures for 15 invalidating any previously submitted paper version of such form when 16 replaced by an electronic submission; 17 (3) may not refuse to accept form MV-82 executed with an electronic 18 signature solely because such signature is electronic, provided the 19 electronic signature complies with standards established by the commis- 20 sioner; 21 (4) may establish standards for electronic signature processes and 22 vendors used for form MV-82, including security, authentication, and 23 fraud prevention requirements; 24 (5) shall not require a form MV-82 executed with an electronic signa- 25 ture to be converted to physical format by printing for any purpose, 26 except when submission by physical means is required because the commis- 27 sioner does not have an electronic process or system available to accept 28 such form MV-82, or where an applicant does not have access to the elec- 29 tronic process or system; 30 (6) shall not impose any additional requirement upon an electronic 31 signature on form MV-82 except as authorized by this section; and 32 (7) may require reasonable authentication or verification measures for 33 electronic signatures on form MV-82, including such methods as deter- 34 mined necessary by the commissioner to ensure integrity and prevent 35 fraud. 36 (b) The commissioner may establish security, verification, and record- 37 keeping standards for electronic signatures on form MV-82, including 38 measures necessary to ensure the integrity of the submission and prevent 39 fraud. 40 (c) This section shall not be construed to require, limit, prohibit, 41 or otherwise hinder the commissioner from providing electronic services 42 or systems for form MV-82, including electronic signature capturing or 43 identity verification related solely to such form. 44 § 2503. Utilization. Electronic signatures used on form MV-82 shall be 45 used solely for the purpose of executing such form as indicated by the 46 signer and shall be associated with the electronic submission of such 47 form in a manner sufficient to verify authenticity and prevent unauthor- 48 ized alteration. 49 § 2504. Conflict. In the event of a conflict between this section and 50 any other provision of this chapter or article three of the state tech- 51 nology law, this section shall apply solely with respect to form MV-82. 52 § 2.
