AN ACT in relation to authorizing the commissioner of education to
conduct a survey regarding instruction on Asian American, Native Hawa-
iians and Pacific Islander history within the state; and to amend the
education law, in relation to establishing an Asian American, Native
Hawaiians and Pacific Islander history advisory committee; and provid-
ing for the repeal of such provisions upon the expiration thereof
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. (a) The commissioner of education is authorized and
2 directed to conduct a survey regarding instruction on Asian American,
3 Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander (AANHPI) history within the state.
4 Such survey shall focus on identifying which school districts provide
5 instruction on Asian American, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander
6 (AANHPI) history. Such survey shall include as appropriate, questions
7 on how the district is providing such instruction, including, but not
8 limited to, the following:
9 (1) how do the department's learning standards inform the school
10 district's teaching of AANHPI history;
11 (2) which grade levels are being taught about AANHPI topics;
12 (3) how much instructional time is devoted to AANHPI topics;
13 (4) what are the school district's social studies curricula that are
14 used in the district, and who publishes curricula;
15 (5) what challenges and/or barriers do educators face in incorporating
16 AANHPI topics into the curriculum;
17 (6) do educators receive any training or professional development
18 regarding AANHPI topics; and

11 § 2. The education law is amended by adding a new section 805 to read
12 as follows:
13 § 805. Asian American, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander history
14 advisory committee; appointment; powers and duties. 1. Contingent upon
15 appropriation for such, the commissioner shall establish an Asian Ameri-
16 can, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander (AANHPI) history advisory
17 committee.
18 2. (a) The committee shall be comprised of four members, to be
19 appointed as follows:
20 (i) one member appointed by the governor;
21 (ii) one member appointed by the temporary president of the senate;
22 (iii) one member appointed by the speaker of the assembly; and
23 (iv) one member appointed by the commissioner.
24 (b) All members appointed to the committee shall be experts in Asian
25 American, Native Hawaiians and/or Pacific Islander studies and/or have a
26 background in educational instruction and or educational administration.
27 (c) All members of the committee shall be appointed within ninety days
28 of the effective date of this section.
29 3. Vacancies in the membership of the committee shall be filled in the
30 same manner provided for original appointments.
31 4. The committee shall meet no less than four times per year.
32 5. The committee shall have the powers and duties to:
33 (a) evaluate the extent to which AANHPI content is incorporated into
34 current state learning standards;
35 (b) evaluate the current ways AANHPI history is taught in classrooms;
36 and
37 (c) make recommendations on how to include AANHPI content into the
38 state learning standards, key aspects of the subject matter that should
39 be included in the instruction, and ways to integrate AANHPI history
40 into existing courses.
41 6. The committee shall submit a written report to the commissioner no
42 later than two years after the effective date of this section detailing
43 its findings and recommendations.
44 7. Committee members shall receive no compensation for their services,
45 but shall be allowed their actual and necessary expenses incurred in the
46 performance of their duties.
47 8. To effectuate the purposes of this section, the committee may
48 request from any department, board, bureau, commission or other agency
49 of the state, and the same are authorized to provide, such cooperation
50 and assistance, services and data as will enable the committee to prop-
51 erly carry out its functions pursuant to this section.
52 § 3. This act shall take effect immediately; and shall expire and be
53 deemed repealed three years after such effective date.
