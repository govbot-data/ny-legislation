AN ACT in relation to authorizing the commissioner of education to
conduct a survey regarding instruction on Asian American, Native Hawa-
iians and Pacific Islander history within the state; to amend the
education law, in relation to establishing an Asian American, Native
Hawaiians and Pacific Islander history advisory committee; and provid-
ing for the repeal of such provisions upon the expiration thereof
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. This act shall be known as the "New York AANHPI Education
2 Equity Act".
3 § 2. (a) Contingent upon appropriation for such, the commissioner of
4 education is authorized and directed to conduct a survey regarding
5 instruction on Asian American, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander
6 (AANHPI) history within the state. Such survey shall focus on identify-
7 ing the extent to which school districts provide instruction on Asian
8 American, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander (AANHPI) history. Such
9 survey shall include as appropriate, questions on how the district is
10 providing such instruction, including, but not limited to, the follow-
11 ing:
12 (1) how the department's learning standards inform the school
13 district's teaching of AANHPI history;
14 (2) which grade levels are being taught about AANHPI history;
15 (3) how much instructional time is devoted to AANHPI history;
16 (4) what are the school district's social studies curricula that are
17 used in the district, and who publishes curricula;

2 AANHPI history into the curriculum;
3 (6) do educators receive any training or professional development

5 (7) are there assessments or evaluations to measure student under-

7 (b) Findings shall include detailed results of the survey, and if
8 needed, recommendations to implement or strengthen such instruction. A
9 report of the findings of such survey shall be delivered to the gover-
10 nor, the temporary president of the senate, the speaker of the assembly,
11 the minority leader of the senate and the minority leader of the assem-
12 bly the first of January following the effective date of this act or one
13 hundred twenty days after the effective date of this act, whichever is
14 later.
15 § 3. The education law is amended by adding a new section 805 to read
16 as follows:
17 § 805. Asian American, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander history
18 advisory committee; appointment; powers and duties. 1. Contingent upon
19 appropriation for such, the commissioner shall establish an Asian Ameri-
20 can, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander (AANHPI) history advisory
21 committee.
22 2. (a) The committee shall be comprised of four members, to be
23 appointed as follows:
24 (i) one member appointed by the governor;
25 (ii) one member appointed by the temporary president of the senate;
26 (iii) one member appointed by the speaker of the assembly; and
27 (iv) one member appointed by the commissioner.
28 (b) All members appointed to the committee shall be experts in Asian
29 American, Native Hawaiians and/or Pacific Islander studies and/or have a
30 background in educational instruction and/or educational administration.
31 (c) All members of the committee shall be appointed within ninety days
32 of the effective date of this section.
33 3. Vacancies in the membership of the committee shall be filled in the
34 same manner provided for original appointments.
35 4. The committee shall meet no less than four times per year.
36 5. The committee shall provide recommendations on the following:
37 (a) high-quality K-12 curricular and instructional resources on AANHPI
38 history; and
39 (b) strategies for strengthening and deepening public K-12 education
40 programs in the areas of AANHPI history, including analysis of the
41 survey referenced in section two of the chapter of the laws of two thou-
42 sand twenty-five that added this section.
43 6. The committee shall submit their recommendations to the commission-
44 er no later than two years after the effective date of this section and
45 be disbanded thereafter.
46 7. Committee members shall receive no compensation for their services,
47 but shall be allowed their actual and necessary expenses incurred in the
48 performance of their duties subject to an appropriation therefor.
49 8. To effectuate the purposes of this section, the committee may
50 request from any department, board, bureau, commission or other agency
51 of the state, and the same are authorized to provide, such cooperation
52 and assistance, services and data as will enable the committee to prop-
53 erly carry out its functions pursuant to this section.
54 § 4. This act shall take effect immediately and shall expire and be
55 deemed repealed 3 years after such date.
