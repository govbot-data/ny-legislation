CASHMAN -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Corporations, Authorities and Commissions AN ACT to amend the public service law, in relation to requiring broad- band service providers to adhere to repair timelines for service interruptions The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Broadband 2 Service Repair Timeline Act". 3 § 2. The public service law is amended by adding a new section 231 to 4 read as follows: 5 § 231. Broadband service repair timeline. 1. For the purposes of this 6 section, the following terms shall have the following meanings: 7 (a) "Broadband service provider" means any entity providing fixed or 8 wireless broadband internet access service to residential or business 9 customers within the state. 10 (b) "Service interruption" means a complete cessation of broadband 11 service or a material degradation that renders the service unusable for 12 its ordinary intended purposes. 13 2. Broadband service providers shall: 14 (a) commence corrective actions on any service interruptions within 15 twenty-four hours of receiving notice of such service interruption. 16 (b) restore broadband service within seventy-two hours of receiving 17 notice of such service interruption, unless such provider demonstrates 18 that broadband service cannot be restored within such period due to: 19 (i) severe weather; 20 (ii) a natural disaster; 21 (iii) damage to third-party infrastructure outside the provider's 22 control; or 23 (iv) other extraordinary circumstances as determined by the commis- 24 sion. 25 (c) provide timely notice to affected customers of the service inter- 26 ruption including, but not limited to: 27 (i) estimated restoration times; and EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
