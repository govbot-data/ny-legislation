For the purposes of this section, the term "mask or face covering" 9 shall mean any face covering, face mask, face shield, or other material 10 worn whether standalone or part of another item of clothing or equipment 11 that conceals a person's identity, either partially or wholly, whereby 12 the face or voice is disguised by being masked or in any manner is 13 disguised by unusual or unnatural attire or facial alteration. 14 § 498.05 Sentencing. 15 1. When a person is convicted of the crime of aggravated criminal 16 concealment of identity pursuant to this article, and the specified 17 offense is a violent felony offense, as defined in section 70.02 of this 18 chapter, the crime of aggravated criminal concealment of identity shall 19 be deemed a violent felony offense. 20 2. When a person is convicted of the crime of aggravated criminal 21 concealment of identity pursuant to this article and the specified 22 offense is a misdemeanor or a class C, D or E felony, the crime shall be 23 deemed to be one category higher than the specified offense the defend- 24 ant committed, or one category higher than the offense level applicable 25 to the defendant's conviction for an attempt or conspiracy to commit a 26 specified offense, whichever is applicable. 27 3. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, when a person is 28 convicted of the crime of aggravated criminal concealment of identity 29 pursuant to this article and the specified offense is a class B felony: 30 (a) the maximum term of the indeterminate sentence must be at least 31 six years if the defendant is sentenced pursuant to section 70.00 of 32 this chapter; 33 (b) the term of the determinate sentence must be at least eight years 34 if the defendant is sentenced pursuant to section 70.02 of this chapter; 35 (c) the term of the determinate sentence must be at least twelve years 36 if the defendant is sentenced pursuant to section 70.04 of this chapter; 37 (d) the maximum term of the indeterminate sentence must be at least 38 four years if the defendant is sentenced pursuant to section 70.05 of 39 this chapter; and 40 (e) the maximum term of the indeterminate sentence or the term of the 41 determinate sentence must be at least ten years if the defendant is 42 sentenced pursuant to section 70.06 of this chapter. 43 4. Notwithstanding any other provision of law, when a person is 44 convicted of the crime of aggravated criminal concealment of identity 45 pursuant to this article and the specified offense is a class A-1 felo- 46 ny, the minimum period of the indeterminate sentence shall be not less 47 than twenty years. 48 § 2.
