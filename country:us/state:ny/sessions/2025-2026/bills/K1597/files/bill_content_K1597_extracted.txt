Miller CONGRATULATING Joseph William Stehlik upon the occasion of receiving the distinguished rank of Eagle Scout, the most prestigious Scouting honors, to be celebrated on June 14, 2026 WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to recognize and commend those young people whose dedication, perseverance, leadership, and service to others exemplify the highest ideals of citizenship and community involvement; and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and fully in accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor Joseph William Stehlik of Verona, New York, upon the occasion of achieving the prestigious rank of Eagle Scout, the highest honor attainable in the Boy Scouts of America; and WHEREAS, Joseph William Stehlik successfully fulfilled all requirements for the rank of Eagle Scout and passed his Eagle Board of Review on October 27, 2025, and will be formally recognized for this outstanding achievement at his Eagle Court of Honor on June 14, 2026; and WHEREAS, The rank of Eagle Scout is awarded to only a small percentage of Scouts and represents years of dedication, personal growth, leadership development, and service to others; and WHEREAS, Joseph William Stehlik began his Scouting journey as a Tiger Scout in the first grade and remained actively involved in Scouting throughout his elementary, middle school, and high school years, demonstrating an unwavering commitment to the principles of the Scout Oath and Scout Law; and WHEREAS, During his 12 years as a member of Troop 25 of the Leatherstocking Council, Joseph William Stehlik participated in numerous service projects and community activities, including food drives, Memorial Day cemetery flag placement, fire department cleanup efforts, and volunteer service at a local food pantry, while also participating in the Order of the Arrow and attending many summer camps; and WHEREAS, As part of his Eagle Scout requirements, Joseph William Stehlik planned, organized, and completed an ambitious Eagle Scout Service Project focused on preserving local history through the restoration of a building more than 100 years old located at the town cemetery; and WHEREAS, This significant undertaking included replacing clapboard siding, scraping and painting exterior surfaces, performing concrete repairs, coordinating the safe relocation of honeybees, and organizing hundreds of volunteer service hours while raising thousands of dollars in materials and support; and WHEREAS, Through his leadership and determination, Joseph William Stehlik ensured that an important historical structure would be preserved and maintained for future generations of residents and visitors to appreciate; and WHEREAS, In addition to his many contributions through Scouting, Joseph William Stehlik has demonstrated a deep respect for community traditions and patriotism, including his participation in Memorial Day observances, most recently serving as the "Echo" during the performance of Echo Taps at the Memorial Day Parade in Sherrill, New York; and WHEREAS, Joseph William Stehlik's commitment to service, leadership, citizenship, and community involvement reflects great credit upon himself, his family, Troop 25, the Leatherstocking Council, and the State of New York; and WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body that those individuals who demonstrate such exceptional character and dedication to serving others are worthy of the highest praise and recognition; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to honor Joseph William Stehlik upon attaining the rank of Eagle Scout and to commend him for his years of dedication to Scouting, leadership, community service, and commitment to preserving the history and traditions of his community; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Joseph William Stehlik in recognition of this remarkable achievement and with sincere best wishes for continued success in all his future endeavors.
