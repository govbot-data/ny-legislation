26 chiropractic only under the supervision of a licensed chiropractor and
27 only in a licensed chiropractic office or clinical setting.

29 permit may be renewed annually at the discretion of the department.

32 § 7. The education law is amended by adding a new section 6557 to read
33 as follows:
34 § 6557. Special provisions. 1. Non-liability of licensed chiropractors
35 for first aid or emergency treatment. Notwithstanding any inconsistent
36 provision of any general, special or local law, any licensed chiroprac-
37 tor who voluntarily and without the expectation of monetary compensation
38 renders first aid or emergency treatment at the scene of an accident or
39 other emergency, outside a hospital, doctor's office or any other place
40 having proper and necessary chiropractic equipment, to a person who is
41 unconscious, ill or injured, shall not be liable for damages for inju-
42 ries alleged to have been sustained by such person or for damages for
43 the death of such person alleged to have occurred by reason of an act or
44 omission in the rendering of such first aid or emergency treatment
45 unless it is established that such injuries were or such death was
46 caused by gross negligence on the part of such chiropractor. Nothing in
47 this section shall be deemed or construed to relieve a licensed chiro-
48 practor from liability for damages for injuries or death caused by an
49 act or omission on the part of a chiropractor while rendering profes-
50 sional services in the normal and ordinary course of their practice.
51 2. This article shall not be construed to affect or prevent the
52 following:
53 a. The furnishing of any assistance in an emergency;
54 b. The chiropractor from being a primary portal of entry healthcare
55 provider.
