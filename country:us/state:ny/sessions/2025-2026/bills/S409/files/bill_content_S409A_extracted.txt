AN ACT to amend the alcoholic beverage control law, in relation to
permitting certain retail licensees to purchase wine and liquor from
certain other retail licensees
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Section 106 of the alcoholic beverage control law is
2 amended by adding a new subdivision 2-b to read as follows:
3 2-b. Notwithstanding any provision of this chapter to the contrary, a
4 retail licensee for on-premises consumption shall be authorized to
5 purchase up to six bottles of wine or liquor in the aggregate per week
6 from an off-premises retail licensee, and may resell any wine and liquor
7 so purchased for consumption on the premises licensed therefor.
8 § 2. Section 105 of the alcoholic beverage control law is amended by
9 adding a new subdivision 2 to read as follows:
10 2. (a) Notwithstanding any provision of this chapter to the contrary,
11 a retail licensee to sell liquor and/or wine for consumption off the
12 premises shall be authorized to sell up to six bottles of wine or liquor
13 in the aggregate per week to a retail licensee for on-premises consump-
14 tion.
15 (b) Both the retail licensee for on-premises consumption and the
16 retail licensee for off-premises consumption shall retain evidence of
17 each purchase of wine and liquor from a retailer licensed to sell liquor
18 and/or wine for consumption off the premises in the form of a purchase
19 receipt showing the name of the retailer, the date of purchase, a
