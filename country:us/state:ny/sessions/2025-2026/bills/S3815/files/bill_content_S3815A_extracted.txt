18 § 3. An individual who would have been eligible for benefits under
19 section 208-f of the general municipal law, as amended by section one of
20 this act, as a child of a deceased member who was under the age of 21
21 when the death of the member occurred and has previously aged out, where
22 such member died before the effective date of this act and was a member
23 of the police department established pursuant to chapter 18 of the New
24 York city charter shall be eligible to receive such benefit if such
25 individual files an application with the New York City police pension
26 fund established pursuant to chapter 2 of title 13 of the administrative
27 code of the city of New York within one year following the effective
28 date of this act. Any application received for such benefit which
29 reduces the benefit of another beneficiary shall not be accepted unless
30 such other beneficiary affirmatively consents to a reduction of benefits
31 in the manner and form prescribed by such pension fund. Such benefits
32 shall not be paid retroactively. No individual shall be eligible under
33 this section if (a) they have reached the age of thirty at the time of
34 application or (b) the current beneficiary or beneficiaries is or are
35 the surviving spouse or the parent or parents of such deceased member
36 who died before the effective date of this act.
37 § 4. This act shall take effect immediately.
