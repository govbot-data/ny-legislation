        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  real property law, in relation to requiring the
          disclosure of lead-based paint test  reports  in  real  estate  trans-
          actions
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Legislative findings. a.  The legislature hereby finds  and
     2  declares  that  lead  poisoning  of children persists as one of the most
     3  prevalent and preventable environmental  diseases  in  New  York  state.
     4  Nearly  100,000  children  were  newly identified with levels of lead in
     5  their blood at five micrograms per deciliter (mcg/dL) in New York  state
     6  between 2011 and 2015.
     7    b. Medical research indicates that children can suffer permanent brain
     8  damage  at  blood  levels even lower than 5 mcg/dL, and that there is no
     9  level of lead ingestion that is without adverse impact; indeed, in  2021
    10  the federal Centers for Disease Control and Prevention revised the blood
    11  lead reference level downward to 3.5 mcg/dL.
    12    c. Black children and children from low-income households are consist-
    13  ently  found  to  have  higher  levels of lead in their blood than their
    14  white peers or their peers from higher income households.
    15    d. The predominant cause of lead poisoning in young  children  is  the
    16  ingestion  of  lead  particles  from deteriorating or abraded lead-based
    17  paint from older and poorly maintained residences.   Although  New  York
    18  state banned the sale of lead-based paint in 1970, (l.1970, ch. 338) 74%
    19  of New York's housing stock was constructed prior to 1970 and lead-based

     1  paint  was available outside of the state until 1978. New York state has
     2  both the nation's greatest number (over 4 million  units),  the  highest
     3  percentage  (55.08%)  of  pre-1960 and pre-1950 (41.0%) housing, and the
     4  oldest housing inventory among the fifty states. At least ninety percent
     5  of  lead-based paint still exists in occupied housing built before 1960.
     6  New York state's older housing stock places residents at great  risk  of
     7  exposure to lead hazards, with low-income children living in older hous-
     8  ing having the highest risk of lead poisoning.
     9    e.  Knowledge  of lead-based paint hazards, their control, mitigation,
    10  abatement, and risk avoidance is not sufficiently widespread.  In  addi-
    11  tion, while federal law requires the disclosure by sellers of real prop-
    12  erty  of  knowledge  of the existence of lead-based paint and lead-based
    13  paint hazards, and encourages potential buyers  to  conduct  inspections
    14  for  lead-based  paint,  these  mechanisms  neither  mandate  that  such
    15  inspections take place either by sellers or buyers. This gap in  disclo-
    16  sure  requirements  results  in  residential  property being transferred
    17  without any knowledge of the potential for such property to  cause  lead
    18  poisoning and the attendant liabilities.
    19    f.  Local  county health departments lack sufficient information as to
    20  which housing contains lead-based paint and the locations of such  lead-
    21  based paint, resulting in less cost-effective prevention of lead poison-
    22  ing, avoidable harm to children's health, and wasted public resources.
    23    g.  The  purposes  of this act are to assure that properties that have
    24  not been previously tested for lead-based paint are  not  simply  trans-
    25  ferred  to  new  owners without knowledge of whether there is lead-based
    26  paint present, and to better utilize  the  existing  federal  laws  that
    27  mandate  disclosure of lead-based paint and lead-based paint hazards and
    28  to aid in the prevention of lead poisoning.  This act is not intended to
    29  and does not diminish the responsibility of buyers to carefully  examine
    30  the property which they intend to purchase and public records pertaining
    31  to the property. This act is not intended to and does not limit existing
    32  responsibilities by a seller, buyer or agent concerning the condition of
    33  the  property or potential liabilities or remedies at law, statute or in
    34  equity.
    35    This act will significantly improve the transfer  process  and  better
    36  serve  the interests of all parties to a home purchase. It will increase
    37  clarity regarding the nature of the property and  will  provide  greater
    38  certainty  to contracts entered into by better informed buyers and sell-
    39  ers. As well, it will provide incentive to owners  to  voluntarily  test
    40  their property prior to sale.
    41    h.  This  act  will  complement  existing state and local laws on lead
    42  poisoning prevention. Section 1377 of the public health law, enacted  in
    43  2023,  requires  the  department  of health to develop a registry of all
    44  rental residential dwellings with two or more units  built  before  1980
    45  within  communities  of concern outside the city of New York, which will
    46  also require such dwellings be inspected for lead hazards at least every
    47  three years. This act will ensure equity of information  for  purchasers
    48  of  all  residential property built prior to 1978 regardless of location
    49  or number of units by ensuring that they receive information  about  the
    50  presence of lead-based paint in dwellings they purchase.
    51    i.  This  act  will  require  inspection reports that are developed to
    52  comply with this act to be included on the registry of lead  inspections
    53  that  the  department  of health is creating pursuant to section 1377 of
    54  the public health law so that future renters and  purchasers  can  learn
    55  about lead-based paint in homes they are considering renting or purchas-
    56  ing.

     4  paint  by  August  2025.  This  act will not require re-testing of these

    26    2.  "Binding  contract  of  sale"  shall  mean  a real estate purchase

    35  Indian tribes, and nonprofit organizations.
    36    5. "Lead-based paint" shall mean paint or other similar surface  coat-
    37  ing  material containing .50 milligrams of lead per square centimeter or
    38  greater, as determined by laboratory analysis of paint samples with  all
    39  layers  of  paint  present,  or by an x-ray fluorescence analyzer. If an
    40  x-ray fluorescence analyzer is used, readings  shall  be  corrected  for
    41  substrate bias when necessary as specified by the performance character-
    42  istic  sheets  released  by  the  United States environmental protection
    43  agency and the United States department of housing and urban development
    44  for the specific x-ray fluorescence analyzer  used.  X-ray  fluorescence
    45  readings  shall  be  classified as positive, negative or inconclusive in
    46  accordance with the United States department of housing and urban devel-
    47  opment guidelines for the evaluation and  control  of  lead-based  paint
    48  hazards in housing (July 2012) or successor guidelines, and the perform-
    49  ance  characteristic  sheets released by the United States environmental
    50  protection agency and the United States department of housing and  urban
    51  development  for  the  specific  x-ray fluorescence analyzer used. X-ray
    52  fluorescence readings that fall within the inconclusive zone, as  deter-
    53  mined  by  the  performance characteristic sheets, shall be confirmed by
    54  laboratory analysis of paint chips, the measure of such laboratory anal-
    55  ysis shall be definitive.  Where a laboratory analysis of paint chips is
    56  performed (including because an x-ray fluorescence reading is  inconclu-

    13    § 532. Inspection of residential real property  for  lead-based  paint
    14  prior  to transfer of title. 1. (a) Effective August first, two thousand
    15  twenty-six, every seller of residential real property pursuant to a real
    16  estate purchase contract shall deliver to a buyer or buyer's agent prior
    17  to the signing by the buyer of a binding contract of sale a  certificate
    18  that such property has been tested for lead-based paint, and provide the
    19  report  of such test.  The seller shall attach a copy of the certificate
    20  containing the signature of the seller and any  report  of  a  test  for
    21  lead-based paint to the real estate purchase contract.  The seller shall
    22  submit a copy of such certificate and report of such test and any subse-
    23  quent reports of such tests to the office authorized under section three
    24  hundred  seventy-two  of  this  chapter  to be registrar of title in the
    25  county where such real property is located, and such  office  shall  not
    26  accept  for filing an instrument of transfer of title unless accompanied
    27  by such certificate where applicable.  The seller shall also  file  such
    28  certificate  and  report with the registry established by the department
    29  of health pursuant to subdivision one of section thirteen hundred seven-
    30  ty-seven of the public health law. The registry shall be publicly acces-
    31  sible online, translated consistent with section two  hundred  two-a  of
    32  the  executive  law,  and  easy  to  navigate and read by people with an
    33  eighth grade education or less.  The department of health  shall  notify
    34  the  county  department  of  health in the location of the property sale
    35  about new entries in the registry coming from property sales.
    36    (b) The presentation of a certificate of such test by a prior owner of
    37  such property and evidence of filing such certificate  and  report  with
    38  the department of health in the county where such residential real prop-
    39  erty  is located, and with the registry established by the department of
    40  health pursuant to subdivision one of section thirteen hundred  seventy-
    41  seven of the public health law, shall be deemed to be in compliance with
    42  the provisions of this subdivision.
    43    (c)  In  the  event  the  seller has not received from a prior owner a
    44  certification and report of such tests as set forth in this subdivision,
    45  the costs of testing for lead-based  paint  and  the  preparation  of  a
    46  certificate  and report thereof as provided in this subdivision shall be
    47  deductible by the transferor or  grantor,  up  to  the  amount  of  five
    48  hundred dollars, or in a building with more than one dwelling unit up to
    49  four hundred dollars per dwelling unit tested, from the taxes imposed by
    50  sections fourteen hundred two and fourteen hundred two-a of the tax law.
    51  The transferor or grantor shall not be reimbursed for costs in excess of
    52  the  total  taxes  imposed by sections fourteen hundred two and fourteen
    53  hundred two-a of the tax law.

    34    § 533. Duty of agent. An agent representing a  seller  of  residential
    35  real  property as a listing broker, or, if the seller is not represented
    36  by an agent, the agent representing the buyer of residential real  prop-
    37  erty and dealing with a prospective seller, shall have the duty to time-
    38  ly  (in  any  event,  before the buyer signs a binding contract of sale)
    39  inform each seller of the seller's obligations under  this  article.  An
    40  agent  representing  a  buyer  of  residential real property, or, if the
    41  buyer is not represented by an agent, the agent representing a seller of
    42  residential real property and dealing with a  prospective  buyer,  shall
    43  have  the duty to timely (in any event, before the buyer signs a binding
    44  contract of sale) inform such buyer of  the  buyer's  rights  and  obli-
    45  gations  under  this  article. If an agent performs the duties and obli-
    46  gations imposed upon such agent pursuant  to  this  section,  the  agent
    47  shall  have no further duties under this article and shall not be liable
    48  to any party for a violation of this article. The  department  of  state
    49  may,  pursuant  to  section  four  hundred  forty-one-c of this chapter,
    50  revoke or suspend the license of an agent who violates this article.
    51    § 534. Liability. Nothing contained in this article shall be construed
    52  as limiting any existing legal cause of action  or  remedy  at  law,  in
    53  statute or in equity.
