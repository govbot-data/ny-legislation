Add §33-f, Nav L; amd §3-0301, En Con L
 
Relates to the regulation of exhaust gas cleaning systems on vessels and provides enforcement mechanisms and remedies for violations; relates to the powers of the department of environmental conservation.

        Introduced  by Sen. HOYLMAN-SIGAL -- read twice and ordered printed, and

        AN ACT to amend the navigation law, in relation  to  the  regulation  of
          exhaust  gas  cleaning  systems  on vessels; and to amend the environ-
          mental conservation law, in relation to the powers of  the  department
          of environmental conservation
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The navigation law is amended by adding a new section  33-f
     2  to read as follows:
     3    §  33-f.  Regulating  exhaust gas cleaning systems. 1. As used in this
     4  section:
     5    (a) "exhaust gas cleaning  systems"  means  a  mechanism  intended  to
     6  remove  particulate  matter from exhaust gasses generated as a result of
     7  the combustion process in marine engines, whose output is the  discharge
     8  of wash water as water, spray, exhaust or other form of discharge; and
     9    (b) "department" means the state department of environmental conserva-
    10  tion.
    11    2.  (a)  No exhaust gas cleaning system on any vessel used or operated
    12  upon navigable waters of this state shall be operated so as to discharge
    13  into said waters or air directly or indirectly except  as  required  for
    14  safety reasons.
    15    (b)  Exhaust  gas  cleaning system overboard discharge valves shall be
    16  sealed while the vessel is in navigable waters of the state.
    17    3. No exhaust gas cleaning system shall be used, sold,  or  physically
    18  offered for sale in this state.
    19    4.  The department shall require persons making application for a boat
    20  registration certificate for a vessel pursuant to the vehicle and  traf-
    21  fic  law  to disclose whether such vessel has within or on it an exhaust
    22  gas cleaning system and if so, to certify that such device shall not  be

     1  used  in  the  waters  of  this  state  as required by this section. The
     2  department is further empowered to direct that the issuance  of  a  boat
     3  registration certificate or a renewal thereof be withheld if such device
     4  is used in violation of this section.
     5    5.  All  vessels located upon waters of this state shall be subject to
     6  boarding and inspection by the department  or  any  lawfully  designated
     7  agents  or  inspectors  thereof,  for the purpose of determining whether
     8  such vessel is in compliance with this section.
     9    6. The department is hereby authorized and empowered to  make,  adopt,
    10  promulgate, amend and repeal such standards and rules and regulations as
    11  are  necessary,  or  convenient for the carrying out of duties and obli-
    12  gations and powers conferred on the department by this section.
    13    7. A copy of the regulations adopted pursuant to this section and  any
    14  of  the  amendments thereto, shall be filed in the office of the depart-
    15  ment, the water resources commission, and in the office of the secretary
    16  of state. Rules, regulations and standards shall  be  published  by  the
    17  department in convenient form.
    18    8. Any person who violates this section, shall be guilty of an offense
    19  and  upon  conviction shall be punished with a fine of up to twenty-five
    20  thousand dollars per violation; provided, however, that in the event any
    21  person violates this section more than twice during  the  same  calendar
    22  year  and  is  convicted of more than two such violations, the third and
    23  each subsequent violation shall be deemed a misdemeanor.
    24    9. Any person who violates this  section  shall  be  strictly  liable,
    25  without  regard  to  fault,  for  all  cleanup and removal costs and all
    26  direct and indirect damages, no matter by whom sustained for the follow-
    27  ing:
    28    (a) the cost of restoration and replacement, where  possible,  of  any
    29  natural resource damaged or destroyed by a discharge;
    30    (b)  the  cost  of  restoring,  repairing,  or  replacing  any real or
    31  personal property damaged or destroyed by a discharge, any  income  lost
    32  from  the  time  such  property  is damaged to the time such property is
    33  restored, repaired or replaced, any reduction in value of such  property
    34  caused by such discharge by comparison with its value prior thereto;
    35    (c)  loss of income or impairment of earning capacity due to damage to
    36  real or personal property,  including  natural  resources  destroyed  or
    37  damaged  by  a  discharge; provided that such loss or impairment exceeds
    38  ten percent of the amount which claimant derives, based upon  income  or
    39  business  records, exclusive of other sources of income, from activities
    40  related to the particular real or personal property or natural resources
    41  damaged or destroyed by such discharge during the week,  month  or  year
    42  for which the claim is filed;
    43    (d) loss of tax revenue by the state or local governments for a period
    44  of  one  year  due  to  damage  to real or personal property proximately
    45  resulting from a discharge; and
    46    (e) interest on loans obtained or  other  obligations  incurred  by  a
    47  claimant  for  the  purpose  of  ameliorating  the  adverse effects of a
    48  discharge pending the payment of a claim in full  as  provided  by  this
    49  article.
    50    10.  If any court of competent jurisdiction shall find any subdivision
    51  or subdivisions of this section  to  be  unconstitutional  or  otherwise
    52  invalid,  such  findings  shall  not affect the validity of this section
    53  which can be given effect.
    54    § 2. Subdivision 1 of section 3-0301 of the environmental conservation
    55  law is amended by adding a new paragraph ii to read as follows:
