        AN ACT to amend the correction law, in relation to requiring the commis-
          sioner of corrections and community supervision to assess  and  update
          the  security camera, land-mobile radio, and public address systems in
          use in correctional facilities

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  Section  112 of the correction law is amended by adding a
     2  new subdivision 7 to read as follows:
     3    7. (a) Not later than ninety days after the  effective  date  of  this
     4  subdivision, the commissioner shall:
     5    (i)  evaluate  the  security  camera,  land-mobile  radio,  and public
     6  address systems in use in correctional facilities as  of  the  effective
     7  date of this subdivision; and
     8    (ii)  submit  to  the governor and the legislature a plan for ensuring
     9  that all correctional facilities have the security  camera,  land-mobile
    10  radio, and public address system coverage and capabilities necessary to:
    11    (1)  ensure  the  health and safety of staff and incarcerated individ-
    12  uals; and
    13    (2) ensure the documentation and accessibility of video evidence  that
    14  may  pertain  to misconduct by staff or incarcerated individuals, negli-
    15  gent or abusive  treatment  of  incarcerated  individuals,  or  criminal
    16  activity within correctional facilities.
    17    (b)  The  plan  required pursuant to paragraph (a) of this subdivision
    18  shall:
    19    (i) identify and include plans to  address  any  deficiencies  in  the
    20  security  camera  system  in  use  at correctional facilities, including
    21  those related to:
    22    (1) an insufficient number of cameras;
    23    (2) inoperable or malfunctioning cameras;

         EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets

     1    (3) blind spots;
     2    (4) poor quality video; and
     3    (5) any other deficits in the security camera system;
     4    (ii)  identify  and  include  plans to adopt and maintain any security
     5  camera system upgrades needed to achieve the purposes described in para-
     6  graph (a) of this subdivision, including:
     7    (1) conversion of all analog cameras to digital surveillance  systems,
     8  with corresponding infrastructure and equipment upgrade requirements;
     9    (2)  upgrades to ensure the secure storage, logging, preservation, and
    10  accessibility of recordings such that the recordings  are  available  to
    11  investigators or courts at such time as may be reasonably required; and
    12    (3) additional camera system capabilities needed to enhance the safety
    13  and security of incarcerated individuals and staff;
    14    (iii)  include  an  assessment of operational and logistical consider-
    15  ations in implementing the plan required pursuant to  paragraph  (a)  of
    16  this subdivision, including:
    17    (1) a prioritization of facilities for needed upgrades, beginning with
    18  high security institutions;
    19    (2) the personnel and training necessary to implement the changes; and
    20    (3) ongoing repair and maintenance requirements; and
    21    (iv)  include a two-year strategic plan and cost projection for imple-
    22  menting the changes and upgrades to  the  security  camera,  land-mobile
    23  radio,  and  public  address  systems identified under subparagraphs (i)
    24  through (iii) of this paragraph.
    25    (c) Not later than two years after the  date  on  which  the  plan  is
    26  submitted  pursuant to subparagraph (ii) of paragraph (a) of this subdi-
    27  vision, and subject to appropriations therefor, the  commissioner  shall
    28  complete implementation of the submitted plan.
    29    (d)  Beginning  one year after the date on which the plan is submitted
    30  pursuant to subparagraph (ii) of paragraph (a) of this subdivision,  and
    31  each   year  thereafter  until  full  implementation  of  such  plan  is
    32  completed, the commissioner shall issue  a  public  report  to  be  made
    33  available  on  the  website  of  the  department on the progress of such
    34  implementation.
    35    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
