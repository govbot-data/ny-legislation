Add §226, Ec Dev L
 
Directs the commissioner of economic development to expand Global NY to establish and administer a global NY reparative economic inclusion program to foster international economic partnerships, trade, investment and workforce development opportunities between New York state and nations, regions, communities and organizations representing or comprised of people of African descent.

        Introduced by M. of A. ZINERMAN -- read once and referred to the Commit-
          tee on Economic Development
 
        AN  ACT  to amend the economic development law, in relation to expanding
          Global NY to promote reparative economic inclusion and diaspora  part-
          nerships with nations and peoples of African descent

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The economic development law is amended  by  adding  a  new
     2  section 226 to read as follows:
     3    §  226.  Global  NY  reparative  economic  inclusion  program. (a) The
     4  commissioner shall expand Global NY to establish and administer a global
     5  NY reparative economic inclusion program to foster international econom-
     6  ic partnerships, trade, investment, and workforce  development  opportu-
     7  nities  between  New  York  state and nations, regions, communities, and
     8  organizations representing or comprised of people  of  African  descent,
     9  including but not limited to:
    10    (i) the African Union and its member states;
    11    (ii) the Caribbean Community and its member states;
    12    (iii) the Organization of African, Caribbean and Pacific States;
    13    (iv) nations with significant African diaspora populations; and
    14    (v) international diaspora organizations.
    15    (b)  The  purposes  of  the  Global  NY  reparative economic inclusion
    16  program shall include:
    17    (i) supporting reparative trade agreements and commercial partnerships
    18  that advance economic opportunity for African-descended peoples;
    19    (ii) facilitating bilateral and multilateral trade missions focused on
    20  sectors including healthcare, green energy, infrastructure, agriculture,
    21  technology, arts and culture, education, and financial services;
    22    (iii) developing diaspora investment zones and  enterprise  assistance
    23  programs  for  African-descended  entrepreneurs  in  New  York state and
    24  abroad;

     7    (i) the division of minority and women's business development;
