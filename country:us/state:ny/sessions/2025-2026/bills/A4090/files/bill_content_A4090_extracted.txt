CUNNINGHAM -- read once and referred to the Committee on Transportation AN ACT to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to providing for the suspension of a commercial driver's license if a driver has been convicted of a violation of local laws relating to a designated system of truck routes The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Subdivision 3 of section 510-a of the vehicle and traffic 2 law is amended by adding a new paragraph (f) to read as follows: 3 (f) A commercial driver's license shall be suspended by the commis- 4 sioner: 5 (i) for a period of sixty days where the holder is convicted of a 6 violation of a local law, ordinance, order, rule or regulation enacted 7 by the legislative body of any city, village or town, pursuant to the 8 provisions of paragraph ten of subdivision (a) of section sixteen 9 hundred forty or paragraph ten of subdivision (a) of section sixteen 10 hundred sixty of this chapter. 11 (ii) for a period of one hundred twenty days where the holder is 12 convicted of a second violation of a local law, ordinance, order, rule 13 or regulation enacted by the legislative body of any city, village or 14 town, pursuant to the provisions of paragraph ten of subdivision (a) of 15 section sixteen hundred forty or paragraph ten of subdivision (a) of 16 section sixteen hundred sixty of this chapter, both of which were 17 committed within a three-year period. 18 (iii) for a period of one year where the holder is convicted of a 19 third violation of a local law, ordinance, order, rule or regulation 20 enacted by the legislative body of any city, village or town, pursuant 21 to the provisions of paragraph ten of subdivision (a) of section sixteen 22 hundred forty or paragraph ten of subdivision (a) of section sixteen 23 hundred sixty of this chapter, all of which were committed within a 24 three-year period. 25 § 2. This act shall take effect on the ninetieth day after it shall 26 have become a law. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
