The general business law is amended by adding a new section 2 394-j to read as follows: 3 § 394-j. Policy for the use of automatic license plate reader systems. 4 1. As used in this section, the following words and phrases shall have 5 the following meanings: 6 (a) "Automatic license plate reader system" shall mean a system of one 7 or more mobile or fixed high-speed cameras used in combination with 8 computer algorithms to convert images of license plates into computer- 9 readable data. "Automated license plate reader system" shall include but 10 not be limited to such systems that are subject to multi-agency or 11 vendor agreements that allow the sharing of automatic license plate 12 reader information collected in New York. 13 (b) "Captured plate data" shall mean the GPS coordinates, date and 14 time, photograph, license plate number, and any other data captured by 15 or derived from an automatic license plate reader system. 16 (c) "Automatic license plate reader user" means a person or entity 17 that owns or operates an automatic license plate reader device. (a) An automatic license plate reader user shall not sell, share, 9 allow access to, or transfer captured plate data to any state or local 10 jurisdiction for the purpose of investigating or enforcing a law that 11 denies or interferes with a person's right to choose or obtain reproduc- 12 tive health care services or any lawful health care services. 13 (b) Any automatic license plate reader user in this state, including 14 any law enforcement agency of this state that uses automatic license 15 plate reader systems, shall not share automatic license plate reader 16 information with an out-of-state law enforcement agency without first 17 obtaining a written declaration from the out-of-state law enforcement 18 agency that it expressly affirms that the captured plate data obtained 19 shall not be used in a manner that violates paragraph (a) of this subdi- 20 vision. If a written declaration of affirmation is not executed, the law 21 enforcement agency shall not share the captured plate data with the 22 out-of-state law enforcement agency. 23 (c) Captured plate data shall be maintained confidentially to the 24 fullest extent permitted by law. 25 3.
