A07675 Summary:

BILL NOA07675A
 
SAME ASSAME AS S07217-A
 
SPONSORStern
 
COSPNSRRamos, Brown K, Griffin
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Amd §125, Gen Muni L
 
Authorizes a city, town or village to establish a program whereby a building permit may be issued based upon certification by a registered architect or professional engineer.

        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  general  municipal law, in relation to building
          permits; and providing for the repeal of such provisions upon  expira-
          tion thereof
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Section 125 of the general municipal law, as added by chap-
     2  ter 439 of the laws of 1998, is amended to read as follows:
     3    § 125. Issuance of building permits. 1. No city, town or village shall
     4  issue a building permit without  obtaining  from  the  permit  applicant
     5  either:
     6    [1.]  (a)  proof  duly subscribed that workers' compensation insurance
     7  and  disability  benefits  coverage  issued by an insurance carrier in a
     8  form satisfactory to the chair of the   workers' compensation  board  as
     9  provided  for in section fifty-seven of the workers' compensation law is
    10  effective; or
    11    [2.] (b) an affidavit that such permit applicant has  not  engaged  an
    12  employer  or  any employees as those terms are defined in section two of
    13  the  workers' compensation law to perform work relating to such building
    14  permit.
    15    2. (a) Notwithstanding any law, rule or regulation to the  contrary  a
    16  city,  town  or village in a county with a population of one million two
    17  hundred thousand or more, not wholly contained within a city,  or  in  a
    18  city  with  a  population  of  between two hundred nine thousand and two
    19  hundred ten thousand and in a county with a population of  between  nine
    20  hundred ninety thousand and one million, may establish a program whereby
    21  a  building  permit may be issued based upon a certification by a regis-

     1  tered architect licensed to practice in the  state  of  New  York  or  a
     2  professional engineer licensed to practice in the state of New York that
     3  the  intended work as set forth in the building permit application meets
     4  the  requirements  of  the  Uniform  Fire  Prevention and Building Code,
     5  excluding the approval of fire service features and compliance with fire
     6  protection and life safety systems as defined by the fire  code  of  New
     7  York  state,  or  any more restrictive local provisions relating to fire
     8  services, fire protection or life safety systems.   Such  program  shall
     9  include the following requirements:
    10    (i)  no  later  than  forty-five  days  after submission of a building
    11  permit application with a certification as provided for  herein  to  the
    12  municipal  building department such application shall be deemed approved
    13  unless denied;
    14    (ii) in the event of a denial of a building permit application with  a
    15  certification  the  building  department shall set forth the reasons for
    16  such denial and shall cite the applicable provisions of the law or regu-
    17  lation upon which such denial is predicated; and
    18    (iii) a random audit of applications for a building permit  to  ensure
    19  compliance.   Provided, however, a city, town, or village establishing a
    20  program pursuant to this section shall be prohibited  from  reducing  or
    21  eliminating  the  position  of  any  employee involved with the building
    22  permit application process during the  effectiveness  of  this  section.
    23  Any  program  established  by  a  city, town or village pursuant to this
    24  paragraph  shall  be  referred  to  as  a  "professional   certification
    25  program".
    26    (b) The wrongful or negligent certification by a licensed professional
    27  pursuant  to  this  section  may constitute professional misconduct, and
    28  referral can be  made  to  the  state  education  department  office  of
    29  professions  upon  a finding of wrongdoing or negligence by the licensed
    30  professional by the  building  department  with  jurisdiction  over  the
    31  project.
    32    §  2.  This  act shall take effect immediately and shall expire and be
    33  deemed repealed three years after such effective date.
