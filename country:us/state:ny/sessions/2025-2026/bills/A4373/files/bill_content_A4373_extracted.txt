TAPIA, GALLAGHER, SHIMSKY -- read once and referred to the Committee on Environmental Conservation AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law, in relation to regu- lation of PFAS as a toxic air pollutant The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Legislative findings and intent. Per- and polyfluorinated 2 substances (PFAS) are a class of persistent and highly toxic chemicals 3 responsible for a pollution crisis impacting communities across the 4 globe. Exposure to these chemicals is wide-ranging, from the water we 5 drink to the food we eat to the clothes we wear to the products we use 6 in the home. New York communities have been impacted through contam- 7 inated drinking water supplies, and much of the regulatory focus has 8 been on establishing drinking water quality standards for these chemi- 9 cals and prohibiting these substances in products, such as food packag- 10 ing, apparel, and firefighting foam. However, our biggest polluting 11 facilities, including incinerators, landfills, and manufacturing facili- 12 ties, are not required to address PFAS entering the environment through 13 air pathways. Communities, particularly those within the vicinity of a 14 polluting facility, may be experiencing additional and compounded expo- 15 sure to PFAS through air and soil as a result of unregulated air emis- 16 sions. These air emissions are also likely playing a role in surface 17 water contamination across the state. 18 § 2. The environmental conservation law is amended by adding a new 19 section 19-0333 to read as follows: 20 § 19-0333. PFAS as a toxic air pollutant. 21 1. Definitions. For the purposes of this section, the following terms 22 shall have the following meanings: 23 a. "Adjacent to" means within a radius of one mile. 24 b. "Disadvantaged community" shall have the same meaning as defined by 25 subdivision five of section 75-0101 of this chapter. Promulgation of PFAS as a high toxicity air contaminant. 16 a. Within ninety days of the effective date of this section, the 17 department shall promulgate rules and/or regulations defining PFAS as a 18 high toxicity air contaminant. 19 b. The department shall ensure that process operations not undergoing 20 permit modification or renewal on the effective date of this section are 21 assessed as to whether PFAS emissions are occurring to the outdoor 22 atmosphere. If such PFAS emissions are found to be occurring, the 23 department shall ensure such process operation is subject to appropriate 24 control technology. 25 c. The department may adopt any rules and regulations it deems neces- 26 sary to implement the provisions of this section. 27 3. Fence line monitoring. No later than six months after the effective 28 date of this section, the department shall establish a PFAS air emis- 29 sions fence line monitoring program. In developing such program, the 30 department shall: 31 a. Prioritize monitoring facilities located in disadvantaged and/or 32 impacted communities; 33 b. Consider the feasibility of monitoring for other toxic air pollu- 34 tants; 35 c. Include a notification system for the public, and publicly provide 36 quarterly reports summarizing the measurements; 37 d. Include any other measures the department deems necessary to devel- 38 op such a program. 39 § 3. This act shall take effect on the one hundred eightieth day after 40 it shall have become a law.
