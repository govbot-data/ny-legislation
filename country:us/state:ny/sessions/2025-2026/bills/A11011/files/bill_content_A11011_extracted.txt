BLUMENCRANZ -- read once and referred to the Committee on Governmental Employees -- committee discharged, bill amended, ordered reprinted as amended and recommitted to said commit- tee AN ACT to amend the civil service law, in relation to providing addi- tional credits to Nassau county law enforcement explorers The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Short title. This act shall be known and may be cited as 2 the "Nassau future protectors act". 3 § 2. The civil service law is amended by adding a new section 85-e to 4 read as follows: 5 § 85-e. Additional credit allowed for Nassau county law enforcement 6 explorers in competitive examinations; preference in retention upon 7 abolition of positions. 1. Additional credits shall be allowed for 8 participants of the Nassau county law enforcement exploring program in 9 competitive examinations for original appointment. The Nassau county law 10 enforcement exploring program shall be defined as a youth police train- 11 ing program which provides youths between the ages of fourteen and twen- 12 ty with training and on the purpose, mission and objectives of the 13 Nassau county police department, public safety and law enforcement. 14 (a) On all eligible lists resulting from competitive examinations, the 15 names of eligible persons shall be entered in the order of respective 16 final earned ratings on examinations, with the name of the eligible 17 person with the highest final earned ratings at the head of such list, 18 provided, however, that for the purpose of determining final earned 19 ratings, Nassau county law enforcement explorers shall be entitled to 20 receive additional points in a competitive examination for original 21 appointment in Nassau county. An eligible person who served as a Nassau 22 county law enforcement explorer for a minimum of four years shall 23 receive an additional five points. An eligible person who served as a 24 Nassau county law enforcement explorer for less than four years shall 25 receive an additional two points. Any candidate, believing themselves to be entitled to additional 6 credit in a competitive examination as provided in this section, may 7 make application for such additional credit at any time between the date 8 of application for examination and the date of the establishment of the 9 resulting eligible list. A candidate who fails to 19 establish, by appropriate documentary proof, the eligibility to receive 20 additional credit by the time an eligible list is established shall not 21 thereafter be granted additional credit on such eligible list. 22 3. (a) Except as otherwise provided in this subdivision, no person who 23 has received a permanent original appointment in the civil service of 24 Nassau county thereof from an eligible list on which such person was 25 allowed the additional credit granted by this section as a Nassau county 26 law enforcement explorer, shall thereafter be entitled to any additional 27 credit under this section. 28 (b) Where, at the time of establishment of an eligible list, the posi- 29 tion of a Nassau county law enforcement explorer on such list has not 30 been affected by the addition of credits granted under this section, the 31 appointment of such Nassau county law enforcement explorer from such 32 eligible list shall not be deemed to have been made from an eligible 33 list on which they were allowed the additional credit granted by this 34 section. 35 (c) If, at the time of appointment from an eligible list, a Nassau 36 county law enforcement explorer is in the same relative standing among 37 the eligible persons who are willing to accept appointment as if such 38 explorer had not been granted the additional credits as provided by this 39 section, the appointment from such eligible persons shall not be deemed 40 to have been made from an eligible list on which they were allowed such 41 additional credits. 42 (d) Where a Nassau county law enforcement explorer has been originally 43 appointed from an eligible list on which such explorer was allowed such 44 additional credit, but such appointment is thereafter terminated either 45 at the end of the probationary term or by resignation at or before the 46 end of the probationary term, such explorer shall not be deemed to have 47 been appointed, as the case may be, from an eligible list on which such 48 explorer is allowed additional credit, and such appointment shall not 49 affect such explorer's eligibility for additional credit in other exam- 50 inations. 51 4. An application for additional credit in a competitive examination 52 under this section may be withdrawn by the applicant at any time prior 53 to the establishment of the resulting eligible list.
