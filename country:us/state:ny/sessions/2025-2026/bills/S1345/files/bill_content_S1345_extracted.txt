PARKER -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Environmental Conservation AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law, in relation to requiring the department of environmental conservation to conduct a study on sea level rise in cities with a population of one million or more The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The environmental conservation law is amended by adding a 2 new section 3-0320 to read as follows: 3 § 3-0320. Study on sea level rise; cities with a population of one 4 million or more. 5 1. The department, in consultation with the department of state shall 6 conduct a study on the ongoing and impending sea level rise in cities 7 with a population of one million or more and its effect on the environ- 8 ment and coastal lands. The study shall include: 9 (a) The cause of the sea level rises around the city; 10 (b) A risk assessment that analyzes issues related to the impact of 11 sea level rise; 12 (c) The rates of change in sea level; 13 (d) The effects of sea level rise on flooding and flood mitigation; 14 (e) The effects of the rise in sea level on biodiversity; 15 (f) The effects of sea level on health and vitality of the lands and 16 water fronts; 17 (g) A determination of coastal lands in the state that are and will be 18 impacted by sea level rise; 19 (h) A risk assessment that analyzes issues related to the ownership of 20 state coastal lands, including potential legal challenges related to 21 title from the need to transfer ownership of coastal lands and stranded 22 assets due to sea level rise; EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
