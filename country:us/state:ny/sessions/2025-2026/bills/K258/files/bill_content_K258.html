Assembly Resolution No. 258
 
BY: M. of A. Miller
 
        HONORING  the  Willowvale Fire Company, Inc., upon
        the occasion  of  its  75th  Anniversary  and  long,
        distinguished history of service to the community
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is the sense of this Legislative Body to recognize and
commend fire departments of the State of New York that have exhibited  a
long  and  sustained  commitment  to  excellence  in fire protection and
emergency  services,  significantly  contributing  to  the  safety   and
well-being of their communities; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern, and fully in accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
the  Willowvale  Fire  Company,  Inc.,  upon   the   occasion   of   its
distinguished   history   of  service,  recognizing  its  commitment  to
protecting life and property in the Town of New Hartford and beyond; and
 
  WHEREAS, The Willowvale Fire Company traces its origins back to 1950
when residents of the Willowvale Bleachery and surrounding  areas  voted
to  form  a  fire protection district and enter into a contract for fire
services; through their determination and community spirit,  members  of
the  Company successfully raised funds for a new fire truck and laid the
foundation for what would become a vital public safety institution; and
 
  WHEREAS, Over the past seven decades, the  Willowvale  Fire  Company
has  grown  from  its  humble  beginnings  into  a well-equipped, highly
trained fire  and  emergency  response  organization,  adapting  to  the
evolving needs of its community and continuously improving its services;
and
 
  WHEREAS,   The  Company  has  played  a  pivotal  role  in  numerous
significant moments in the history of the Sauquoit Valley, including its
1953  construction  of  a  four-bay  firehouse  on  Oneida  Street,  the
establishment of Station No. 2 in Washington Mills in 1970, and the 2005
construction  of  a  modern  $1.5  million  fire  station,  ensuring its
continued ability to provide effective and  timely  emergency  response;
and
 
  WHEREAS,   The   members   of   the  Willowvale  Fire  Company  have
consistently demonstrated selfless dedication, not only in  firefighting
and  rescue  operations  but also in community outreach, youth programs,
and local training initiatives;  from  responding  to  devastating  barn
fires  and  tragic  incidents to pioneering local fire training programs
and mutual aid agreements, the Company has  remained  steadfast  in  its
mission to protect and serve; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  Company  has  also shown a commitment to honoring its
legacy and leadership, recognizing individuals, such as Chief  James  L.
Harrigan  (1957-1974),  Chief  William  J. Hughes (1981-2012), and Chief
Wayne R.  Smoulcey (2018-2019), whose unwavering service and  leadership
shaped the Company into the esteemed institution it is today; and
 
  WHEREAS, Throughout its history, the Willowvale Fire Company has not
only  responded  to  fires but has also taken an active role in disaster
 
response, rescue operations, and community  safety  education,  ensuring
that  residents  of  Chadwicks,  Washington Mills, and surrounding areas
remain safe and prepared in times of emergency; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The strength and success of the Willowvale Fire Company is
built upon the  dedication  of  its  volunteer  firefighters,  past  and
present,  who  have  demonstrated bravery, resilience, and an unwavering
commitment to duty in the face of adversity; and
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to recognize  the
achievements  of  organizations  that  uphold  the  highest standards of
public service and self-sacrifice, ensuring  the  continued  safety  and
prosperity of their communities; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor the Willowvale Fire Company, Inc., for its  75th  Anniversary  and
historic  and  distinguished  service,  commemorating  its achievements,
dedication, and the vital role it plays in the Town of New Hartford  and
Oneida County; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to the Willowvale Fire Company, Inc.
