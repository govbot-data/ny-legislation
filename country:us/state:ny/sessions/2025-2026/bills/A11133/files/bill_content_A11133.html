        AN ACT to amend chapter 10 of the law of 1958, relating to incorporating
          the Islip Terrace Volunteer Exempt Firemen's  Benevolent  Association,
          and providing for its powers and duties, in relation to making techni-
          cal amendments thereto
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Sections 1, 2 and 7 of chapter 10  of  the  laws  of  1958,
     2  relating  to  incorporating the Islip Terrace Volunteer Exempt Firemen's
     3  Benevolent Association, and providing for its  powers  and  duties,  are
     4  amended to read as follows:
     5    Section  1. Incorporation; membership. A membership corporation by the
     6  name of "Islip Terrace Volunteer Exempt [Firemen's] Firefighter's Benev-
     7  olent Association" is hereby created.  It  shall  be  composed  of  such
     8  persons  eligible to membership therein as hereinafter provided as shall
     9  (a) notify the secretary of the Islip Terrace fire department in writing
    10  prior to  the  organization  meeting  of  such  corporation  hereinafter
    11  provided  for  their  desire to become members, or (b) shall attend such
    12  meeting or an adjournment thereof, and also such persons so eligible  as
    13  may become members of such corporation pursuant to its by-laws.
    14    §  2. Persons eligible to membership. All persons who are now, or have
    15  been, or who hereafter shall be volunteer members of the  Islip  Terrace
    16  fire  district  fire department, or of any fire corporation in such fire
    17  district, and all persons resident in such fire district or in territory
    18  provided with fire protection by contract with such  fire  district  who
    19  are  exempt  volunteer  [firemen] firefighters as defined by section two
    20  hundred of the general municipal law, shall be eligible to membership in
    21  the corporation hereby created; provided that any person  who  has  been
    22  expelled  or  removed  for  cause from membership in any fire company or
    23  department shall not be eligible  to  membership  in  such  corporation.
    24  Whenever  a member who is not an exempt [fireman] firefighter as defined
    25  by section two hundred of the general municipal law shall move from such

     1  fire district or from territory provided with fire protection as  afore-

     5    §  7. Precept for the payment of foreign fire insurance premium taxes.
     6  Such corporation shall collect and there shall be paid to it  all  taxes
     7  imposed  by section [five hundred fifty-three] nine thousand one hundred
     8  four of the insurance law for fire  department  use  and  benefit,  upon
     9  premiums  for insurance against loss or damage by fire covering property
    10  situated within the  territory  protected  by  the  Islip  Terrace  fire
    11  department,  located  in the town of Islip, Suffolk county, New York, or
    12  within any duly organized territory in  which  the  Islip  Terrace  fire
    13  department is or may be obligated to render fire protection. The officer
    14  of said corporation designated by its by-laws to collect and receive the
    15  aforesaid  tax  shall  have  all  the  powers  and be subject to all the
    16  provisions of the insurance law, relating to treasurers of fire  depart-
    17  ments. Such corporation shall also be entitled to receive a share of the
    18  tax  imposed  by  section  [five  hundred  fifty-four] nine thousand one
    19  hundred five of the insurance law, based upon the  business  written  in
    20  the  territory  with  respect  to  which  it  is entitled to collect and
    21  receive the tax under section [five hundred fifty-three]  nine  thousand
    22  one  hundred  four  of  the insurance law. Such taxes shall only be used
    23  [for the care and relief of disabled or indigent  volunteer  and  exempt
    24  volunteer  firemen and their families] in furtherance of its purposes as
    25  set forth in section three of this act.
    26    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately; provided,  however,  that
    27  the  amendments  to  section 7 of chapter 10 of the laws of 1958 made by
    28  section one of this act shall apply to taxes due on premiums received on
    29  or after January 1, 2027.
