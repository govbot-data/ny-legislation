BROWN, SIMON, SEAWRIGHT, OTIS, EACHUS -- read once and referred to the Committee on Transporta- tion AN ACT directing the New York State Department of Transportation, in conjunction with the New York State Energy Research and Development Authority and the New York Power Authority, to promulgate rules and regulations for the standardization of public charging stations for electric vehicles The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Purpose. The New York State Department of Transportation, 2 in conjunction with the New York State Energy Research and Development 3 Authority and the New York Power Authority, shall promulgate rules and 4 regulations for the standardization of public charging stations for 5 electric vehicles in accordance with the provisions of this act. 6 § 2. Definitions. As used in this act, the following terms shall have 7 the following meanings: 8 (a) "Electric vehicle" means a motor vehicle subject to registration 9 pursuant to subdivision 6 of section 401 of the vehicle and traffic law 10 which is: (i) powered by an electric motor drawing current from 11 rechargeable storage batteries or other portable electrical energy stor- 12 age devices; and (ii) capable of being recharged from an external source 13 of electricity. 14 (b) "Plug-in hybrid electric vehicle" means an electric vehicle that 15 has an internal combustion engine or other propulsion system. 16 (c) "Public electric vehicle charging station complex" means an area 17 which contains electric vehicle chargers to supply electricity to elec- 18 tric vehicles and is open to the general public. 19 (d) "Non-public vehicle charging station complex" means an area which 20 contains electric vehicle chargers to supply electricity to electric 21 vehicles and which is not open to the general public, including but not EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. Level 3 chargers are commonly known as the fast 10 chargers. 11 (g) "Electric vehicle service equipment" means equipment, both above 12 and below ground, installed for the purpose of transmitting electricity 13 between the electric vehicle charging station's charging coupler and the 14 electric vehicle's connector. 15 (h) "Electric vehicle service equipment software" means the software 16 programs and other information technologies used to operate the electric 17 vehicle charging equipment. 18 § 3. Charging spaces. (a) Each public charging station complex shall 19 provide space for no less than eight vehicles to be charged. All public 20 charging station complexes shall offer the two most used level 3 fast 21 charger technologies on all of the electric vehicle chargers for all of 22 the eight spaces. 23 (b) No person shall park in a space equipped with a charger at a 24 public electric vehicle charging station, unless such person is charging 25 a plug-in hybrid electric vehicle or battery electric vehicle. 26 § 4. Owners or operators of public electric vehicle charging stations 27 shall be prohibited from requiring a subscription, application, a 28 membership in any club, association, or organization as a condition of 29 using the chargers at the public electric vehicle charging complex. Each 30 electric vehicle charger shall accept all major credit cards as defined 31 in section 521 of the general business law and not require any special 32 credit card solely distributed by the public charging station. Other 33 options to pay for charging services may be available as well. 34 § 5. (a) Owners and operators of public electric vehicle charging 35 station complexes are prohibited from charging customers by-the-minute 36 and must charge per kilowatt hours for the purpose of charging an elec- 37 tric vehicle. The unit price of the kilowatt hours shall be displayed on 38 the face of each charging station prior to and at any point during a 39 customer's transaction. 40 (b) Notwithstanding subdivision (a) of this section, the owners and 41 operators of public electric vehicle charging complexes may impose 42 restrictions on the amount of time that an electric vehicle may be 43 charged at the charging station. Upon the vehicle becoming fully- 44 charged, the owner/operator may charge an additional fee of $10.00 for 45 each half hour or part thereof that the vehicle is parked beyond the 46 time necessary to charge the vehicle in addition to the cost of elec- 47 tricity. 48 § 6. Electric vehicle charging stations as described in this act are 49 exempt from being defined as an electric corporation as defined in 50 section 2 of the public service law. 51 § 7. (a) Owners and operators of public electric vehicle charging 52 stations must maintain signage at the electric vehicle charging station 53 that displays the status of each electric vehicle charger, the total 54 number of chargers and the number of available chargers (total number 55 less those being used), and how long a wait for the next charger to be 56 available, the name and address and phone number of the operator and A. 5038 3 1 owner, the cost of charging including any additional fees that may be 2 charged beyond the cost of electricity for non-charging vehicles, and 3 any rules or regulations customers must follow at the charging station 4 complex when using the chargers. 5 (b) All publicly-owned highway signs or signs at exits of highways in 6 the state that display information about the availability and location 7 of gas stations shall also provide information regarding the availabili- 8 ty and location of public electric vehicle charging stations, including 9 the number of available chargers available and the distance to the 10 charging station complex. 11 § 8. The provisions of this act shall not apply to non-public charging 12 complexes which are not for use by the general public are not subject to 13 the requirements of this law provided signage is clearly displayed that 14 no electric vehicle chargers are available to the general public except 15 for customers of the business or those vehicles owned by the business. 16 § 9.
