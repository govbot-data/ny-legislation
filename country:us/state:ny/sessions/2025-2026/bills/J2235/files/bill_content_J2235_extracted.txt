and WHEREAS, Thomas Patrick McMahon, a resident of Croton-on-Hudson, New York, passed away on March 23, 2026, at the age of 79; and WHEREAS, Born on October 25, 1946, in Manhattan, New York, Thomas Patrick McMahon was the son of the late Peter and Bessie McMahon; and WHEREAS, Thomas Patrick McMahon proudly served his country in the United States Navy for four years, demonstrating honor, discipline, and a steadfast commitment to service; and WHEREAS, Following his military service, Thomas Patrick McMahon pursued a skilled trade career as a union carpenter, contributing to the building and strengthening of his community through his craftsmanship, diligence, and dedication to quality work; and WHEREAS, Thomas Patrick McMahon was a man of varied interests and rich appreciation for life's simple pleasures, including classical music, older sports cars, cartoons, and classic film genres such as Westerns and science fiction; and WHEREAS, He was known for his kindness, generosity, depth of knowledge, and his ability to connect with others through humor, warmth, and sincerity; and WHEREAS, Above all, Thomas Patrick McMahon shared a deep and enduring bond with his devoted wife of 56 years, Agatha McMahon, whose companionship and love were central to his life; and WHEREAS, He is survived by his beloved wife, Agatha McMahon; his brother, Donald McMahon; and his sister, Catherine McMahon, together with extended family members and friends who will forever cherish his memory; and WHEREAS, Thomas Patrick McMahon's life was marked by service, hard work, loyalty, and devotion to family, leaving behind a legacy of love, craftsmanship, and kindness that will continue to inspire all who knew him; and WHEREAS, The passing of Thomas Patrick McMahon represents a profound loss to his family, friends, and community, and his memory will be forever treasured by those whose lives he touched; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of Thomas Patrick McMahon, honoring his service in the United States Navy, his contributions as a union carpenter, and his enduring devotion to his family and community; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Thomas Patrick McMahon.
