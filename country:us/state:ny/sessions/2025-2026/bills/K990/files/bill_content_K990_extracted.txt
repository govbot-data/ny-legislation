Berger MEMORIALIZING the life of Rachel Aliza Nisanov a"h, and honoring her enduring legacy of faith, kindness, and unity WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to honor the memory of cherished members of our communities whose lives left an enduring and meaningful impact upon all who knew them; and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and in full accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body pauses to memorialize and honor the life of Rachel Aliza Nisanov a"h, a young soul whose kindness, humility, and profound faith inspired family, friends, and community alike; and WHEREAS, Rachel Aliza Nisanov was the beloved daughter of Rabbi Shlomo and Rebbetzin Ora Nisanov of Kehilat Sephardim Ahavat Achim in Queens, New York, and the cherished youngest of eight children in a home distinguished by Torah, chessed, and boundless hospitality; and WHEREAS, At just 13 years of age, Rachel Aliza was known for her gentle spirit, deep sensitivity, and unwavering love of peace; she strove to bring harmony among siblings and friends, modeling empathy, patience, and understanding in all her interactions; and WHEREAS, Teachers and classmates alike described her as a "quiet leader," a source of positivity and growth who encouraged others to daven with greater intention, to take on kabbalos for the sake of others, and to pursue personal improvement with humility and sincerity; and WHEREAS, Without seeking recognition, she undertook private commitments of spiritual growth, embodying the timeless teaching that true greatness is measured not by public acclaim but by quiet consistency and integrity; and WHEREAS, Rachel Aliza shared a uniquely close bond with her mother and cherished her family deeply, maintaining a special connection with her siblings and lovingly caring for her nieces and nephews; through heartfelt conversations and simple acts of kibbud av, she expressed her devotion with warmth, wisdom beyond her years, and quiet pride; and WHEREAS, Rachel Aliza embraced life's simple joys, music, dancing, laughter, and friendship, and used every opportunity to strengthen relationships and foster genuine connection; and WHEREAS, Though her time in this world was heartbreakingly brief, the impact of Rachel Aliza Nisanov a"h has been vast, moving thousands to greater achdus, chessed, and strengthened faith, and serving as a lasting reminder that even the quietest light can illuminate an entire community; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to honor the life and memory of Rachel Aliza Nisanov a"h, whose legacy of kindness, connection, humility, and faith will continue to inspire all who learn of her story; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Rabbi Shlomo and Rebbetzin Ora Nisanov and the Nisanov family as an expression of the deepest sympathy and respect of this Legislative Body.
