AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to degree-granting
programs offered by SUNY's office of higher education in prison
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The education law is amended by adding a new section 355-f
2 to read as follows:
3 § 355-f. Office of higher education in prison. 1. Within amounts
4 appropriated therefor, there shall be created within the state universi-
5 ty of New York an office of higher education in prison with the purpose
6 of promoting and coordinating access to higher education for individuals
7 during and after incarceration in New York state. Such office shall:
8 (a) no later than January first, two thousand twenty-nine, develop and
9 implement a plan, approved by the chancellor of the state university of
10 New York or such chancellor's designee, to establish and operate a
11 degree-granting program in every correctional facility, as defined by
12 section two of the correction law, that is located in the state and
13 determined to be viable by such office; and
14 (b) enroll a total of at least two thousand students in degree-grant-
15 ing programs established under paragraph (a) of this subdivision as soon
16 as practicable.
17 2. The commissioner of the department of corrections and community
18 supervision shall work with the state university of New York and the
19 office of higher education in prison to maximize educational opportu-
20 nities for students in programs established under paragraph (a) of
21 subdivision one of this section, including access to federal and state
22 financial aid.
23 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
