the Eagle Court of Honor ceremony will be held on Sunday, May 31, 2026, at Mineola High School, Garden City Park, New York; and WHEREAS, Andrew Leiter is a member of Scouts BSA Troop 45, Mineola, New York; and WHEREAS, Andrew Leiter has achieved this high honor through his steadfast commitment to the principles of Scouting, including leadership, service, citizenship, and personal responsibility; and WHEREAS, A resident of Williston Park, New York, Andrew Leiter was born on September 1, 2007, and has demonstrated excellence both academically and within his community; and WHEREAS, Andrew Leiter attended Mineola High School, where he distinguished himself through participation in numerous extracurricular and leadership activities, including Jazz Ensemble, Marching Band, Varsity Lacrosse, Track & Field, National Honor Society, and Wind Ensemble; and WHEREAS, He has continued his educational pursuits at the University at Buffalo, where he is involved with the Men's Club Lacrosse Team and the Nursing Student Organization, while pursuing a future career in advanced nursing practice; and WHEREAS, Andrew Leiter was the recipient of the Andrew Desiderio Leadership Award, recognizing his exemplary character, dedication, and leadership abilities; and WHEREAS, Andrew Leiter completed a meaningful and lasting Eagle Scout service project involving the design and construction of a meditation garden in the courtyard of NYU Langone Medical Center, featuring handcrafted benches, flowers, and a raked Zen sand garden, while also beautifying and restoring the surrounding area to create a peaceful and welcoming environment for patients, staff, and visitors alike; and WHEREAS, Andrew Leiter's Scouting career began with Cub Scout Pack 246 in Mineola, New York, where he earned the Arrow of Light Award on January 5, 2018, before continuing his Scouting journey with Troop 45 in Mineola; and WHEREAS, Through years of perseverance and commitment, Andrew Leiter advanced through the ranks of Scout, Tenderfoot, Second Class, First Class, Star Scout, and Life Scout, ultimately completing his Eagle Scout project on July 13, 2025, successfully passing his Eagle Scout Board of Review on January 6, 2026, and earning the rank of Eagle Scout; and WHEREAS, Throughout his Scouting journey, Andrew Leiter served in numerous leadership roles including Den Chief, Patrol Leader, Assistant Senior Patrol Leader, and Junior Assistant Scoutmaster, thereby exemplifying the ideals of leadership and mentorship central to the Scouting movement; and WHEREAS, Andrew Leiter earned an impressive array of merit badges, including all required badges necessary for the rank of Eagle Scout, among them Citizenship in the Community, Citizenship in the Nation, Citizenship in the World, Citizenship in Society, Communications, Environmental Science, First Aid, Lifesaving, Personal Fitness, Personal Management, Family Life, and Swimming, in addition to elective merit badges such as Space Exploration, Robotics, Astronomy, Canoeing, Kayaking, and Small Boat Sailing; and WHEREAS, Andrew Leiter's dedication to service, leadership, and personal excellence has earned him the admiration of his family, friends, fellow Scouts, and community members; and WHEREAS, With his future aspirations of becoming either a Nurse Practitioner or Certified Registered Nurse Anesthetist, Andrew Leiter exemplifies the values of compassion, perseverance, and civic responsibility that define the finest traditions of Scouting and citizenship; and WHEREAS, This new Eagle Scout is a credit to his family, his school, his community, his State and his Nation, and no doubt his future will be filled with accomplishments of equal importance; and WHEREAS, This Legislative Body pauses in its deliberations to congratulate Andrew Leiter upon the occasion of receiving the rank of Eagle Scout, the highest honor bestowed by the Boy Scouts of America, and to commend him for his dedication to service, leadership, and excellence; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to congratulate Andrew Leiter upon the occasion of receiving the rank of Eagle Scout, the highest honor bestowed by the Boy Scouts of America, and to commend him for his dedication to service, leadership, and excellence; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Andrew Leiter.
