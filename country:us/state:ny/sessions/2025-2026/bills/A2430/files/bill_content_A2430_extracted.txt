BRAUNSTEIN -- read once and referred to the Committee on Corporations, Authorities and Commissions AN ACT to amend the public service law, in relation to requiring the public service commission to develop a formula for the calculation of a residential water cost index; requiring each water-works corporation and municipal water system serving one thousand customers or more to calculate and submit to the public service commission its residential water cost index; and requiring the public service commission to publish a report on the residential water cost index of each applica- ble water-works corporation and municipal water system The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The public service law is amended by adding a new section 2 89-k to read as follows: 3 § 89-k. Residential water cost index. 1. For the purposes of this 4 section, "residential water cost index" means the annual cost of water 5 for an average single family household served by a water-works corpo- 6 ration or municipal water system. The water cost index shall be calcu- 7 lated using uniform factors developed by the commission including, but 8 not limited to, usage, fees, applicable taxes, meter size and seasonal 9 rate structures. 10 2. Within six months of the effective date of this section, the 11 commission shall develop a formula for the calculation of a residential 12 water cost index. Such formula shall include the uniform factors to be 13 used by any water-works corporation or municipal water system in calcu- 14 lating its water cost index. The commission shall publish such formula 15 on its website, and shall provide such formula to each water-works 16 corporation and municipal water system serving one thousand customers or 17 more. The commission may, from time to time, update such formula. 18 3. Annually, each water-works corporation and municipal water system 19 serving one thousand customers or more shall calculate and submit to the EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
