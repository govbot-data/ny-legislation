AN ACT to amend chapter 636 of the laws of 1995 relating to incorporat-
ing the Volunteer and Exempt Firemen's Benevolent Association of
Coldenham Inc., and providing for its powers and duties, in relation
to the purposes and duties of such corporation and the use of foreign
fire insurance premium taxes
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Sections 1, 2, 3 and 7 of chapter 636 of the laws of 1995
2 relating to incorporating the Volunteer and Exempt Firemen's Benevolent
3 Association of Coldenham Inc., and providing for its powers and duties,
4 are amended to read as follows:
5 Section 1. Incorporation; membership. A not-for-profit corporation by
6 the name of the Volunteer and Exempt [Firemen's] Firefighter's Benevo-
7 lent Association of Coldenham, Inc., is hereby created. It shall be
8 composed of such persons eligible to membership therein as hereinafter
9 provided as shall (a) notify the secretary of the Volunteer [Firemen's]
10 Firefighter's Benevolent Association of Coldenham, Inc., in writing
11 prior to the organization meeting of such corporation hereinafter
12 provided for of their desire to become members, or (b) shall attend such
13 meeting or an adjournment thereof, and also such persons so eligible as
14 may become members of such corporation, pursuant to its by-laws.
15 § 2. Persons eligible to membership. All persons who are now or have
16 been, or hereafter shall be active volunteer members of the fire company
17 or corporation of Coldenham and who have heretofore acquired or shall
18 hereafter acquire the status of exempt volunteer [firemen] firefighter
19 as defined in section 200 of the general municipal law or any other
20 applicable law by virtue of having rendered service in said fire corpo-
21 ration or fire company in such district, and all persons who are now or
22 hereafter shall be active volunteer members of said fire company or
23 corporation shall be eligible for membership in the corporation hereby

33 § 7. Precept for payment of foreign fire insurance premium taxes. Such
34 corporation shall collect and there shall be paid to it all taxes
35 imposed by section 9104 of the insurance law for fire department use and
36 benefit upon premiums for insurance against loss or damage by fire
37 covering property situated within the Coldenham fire district within the
38 towns of Newburgh and Montgomery located in Orange county, New York. The
39 officers of said corporation designated by its by-laws to collect and
40 receive the aforesaid tax shall have all the powers and be subject to
41 all the provisions of the insurance law, relating to treasurers of fire
42 departments. Such corporation shall also be entitled to receive a share
43 of the tax distributed pursuant to the provisions of section 9105 of the
44 insurance law, based upon the business written in the territory with
45 respect to which it is entitled to collect and receive the tax under
46 section 9104 of the insurance law. Such taxes shall only be used [for
47 the care and relief of indigent volunteer and exempt firefighters and
48 their families] by the corporation in furtherance of its purposes as set
49 forth in section three of this act.
50 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately; provided, however, that
