SAME ASNo Same As

Amd §14, Pub Hous L
 
Provides for including siblings of parents and children of siblings in the definition of "family member" in relation to regulations pertaining to rights of family members to succeed in certain cases to the rights of certain tenants.

        AN ACT to amend the public housing law, in relation to the definition of
          "family member"
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section 1. Paragraph (c) of subdivision 4 of section 14 of the  public
     2  housing  law, as added by chapter 116 of the laws of 1997, is amended to
     3  read as follows:
     4    (c) that for the purposes of such  regulations:  (i)  "family  member"
     5  shall  be  defined  as  a  [husband, wife, son, daughter, stepson, step-
     6  daughter,  father,  mother,  stepfather,  stepmother,  brother,  sister,
     7  grandfather,   grandmother,  grandson,  granddaughter,  daughter-in-law,
     8  son-in-law, mother-in-law or father-in-law]  spouse,  child,  stepchild,
     9  parent,  stepparent,  sibling,  sibling of a parent, child of a sibling,
    10  grandparent, grandchild, spouse of a child, or parent of a spouse of the
    11  tenant; or any other person residing with  the  tenant  in  the  housing
    12  accommodation  as a primary residence who can prove emotional and finan-
    13  cial commitment, and interdependence between such person and the tenant.
    14  Although no single factor shall be solely determinative, evidence  which
    15  is  to be considered in determining whether such emotional and financial
    16  commitment and interdependence existed, may include, without limitation,
    17  such factors as listed below.  In no event would evidence  of  a  sexual
    18  relationship between such persons be required or considered.
    19    (A) longevity of the relationship;
    20    (B)  sharing of or relying upon each other for payment of household or
    21  family expenses, or other common necessities of life;
    22    (C) intermingling of finances as evidenced  by,  among  other  things,
    23  joint  ownership  of  bank  accounts, personal and real property, credit
    24  cards, loan obligations, sharing a  household  budget  for  purposes  of
    25  receiving  government  benefits,  or such other factors as may be deter-
    26  mined by regulation;
