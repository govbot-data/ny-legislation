        Introduced  by  M.  of  A.  ROSENTHAL  --  read once and referred to the
          Committee on Tourism, Parks, Arts and Sports Development
 
        AN ACT to establish a temporary state commission to rename any projects,
          infrastructure, facilities or sites within the state which  are  named
          after  or include any reference to Robert Moses; and providing for the
          repeal of such provisions upon expiration thereof
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The  commissioner  of the New York state office of parks,
     2  recreation and historic preservation, is hereby authorized to  create  a
     3  temporary  state commission, hereafter referred to as the commission, to
     4  rename any projects, infrastructure,  facilities  or  sites  within  the
     5  state  which  are  named after or include any reference to Robert Moses,
     6  including, but not limited to,  statues,  schools,  parks,  playgrounds,
     7  roadways and beaches.
     8    §  2.  (a)  The  commission  shall  consist  of  seven  members, to be
     9  appointed as follows: one member to be appointed by the governor;  three
    10  members  to  be  appointed  by  the  temporary  president of the senate,
    11  consisting of two members from the majority party and  one  member  from
    12  the  minority party; and three members to be appointed by the speaker of
    13  the assembly, consisting of two members from the majority party and  one
    14  member  from  the minority party. Each member appointed pursuant to this
    15  section shall be one of a local official, civic leader or park official.
    16  Vacancies in the membership of the commission  and  among  its  officers
    17  shall  be  filled  in  the  manner provided for original appointments or
    18  designations. Such appointments shall be made within six months  of  the
    19  effective date of this act.
    20    (b)  All  departments or agencies of the state or subdivisions thereof
    21  shall, at the  request  of  the  commission,  provide  such  facilities,
    22  assistance,  and  data  as  will  enable the commission to carry out its
    23  powers and duties.

     1    § 3. (a) For the accomplishment of its purposes,  the  commission  may
     2  meet and hold public and/or private hearings within or without the state
     3  and shall have all the powers of a legislative committee pursuant to the
     4  legislative  law. The commission is authorized to consult with community
     5  leaders  and  empowered  to undertake any studies, inquiries, surveys or
     6  analyses it may deem relevant through its own personnel  or  in  cooper-
     7  ation  with or by agreement with any other public or private agency. The
     8  commission shall consult with other departments or agencies of the state
     9  or subdivisions thereof as necessary when the  renaming  of  a  project,
    10  piece  of  infrastructure,  facility or site involves such other depart-
    11  ments or agencies of the state or subdivisions thereof.
    12    (b) To the maximum extent feasible, the  commission  may  request  and
    13  shall be entitled to receive and shall utilize and be provided with such
    14  facilities,  resources and data from any court in the state and from any
    15  subdivision, department, board, bureau, commission,  office,  agency  or
    16  other instrumentality of the state or of any political subdivision ther-
    17  eof  as  it deems necessary or desirable for the proper execution of its
    18  powers and duties and to effectuate the purposes set forth in this act.
    19    (c) The commission is hereby authorized and empowered  to  enter  into
    20  any  agreements  and  to  do and perform any acts that may be necessary,
    21  desirable or proper to carry out the purposes  and  objectives  of  this
    22  act.
    23    §  4.  (a) The commission shall make and submit their name recommenda-
    24  tions to the commissioner of the New York state office of parks,  recre-
    25  ation  and  historic preservation, the temporary president of the senate
    26  and the speaker of the assembly.   Such recommendations  shall  be  made
    27  within  six  months  after  all  members  of  the  commission  have been
    28  appointed pursuant to paragraph (a) of section two of this act.
    29    (b) Once such recommendations  are  received  and  decided  upon,  the
    30  commissioner  of  the  New  York  state  office of parks, recreation and
    31  historic preservation shall take all necessary  steps  to  proceed  with
    32  such renaming. Where applicable, the commissioner shall work in conjunc-
    33  tion  with  other  departments  or agencies of the state or subdivisions
    34  thereof to proceed with such renaming.
    35    § 5. This act shall take effect immediately and shall  expire  and  be
    36  deemed repealed on the first date next succeeding the date of the renam-
    37  ing  of  all  projects,  infrastructure,  facilities or sites within the
    38  state which are named after or include any reference to Robert Moses  as
    39  required under this act; and provided further, however, that the commis-
    40  sioner  of  the  New York state office of parks, recreation and historic
    41  preservation shall notify the legislative bill drafting commission  upon
    42  the renaming of all projects, infrastructure, facilities or sites within
    43  the state which are named after or include any reference to Robert Moses
    44  as  required under this act in order that the commission may maintain an
    45  accurate and timely effective data base of the official text of the laws
    46  of the state of New York in furtherance of effecting the  provisions  of
    47  section  44  of the legislative law and section 70-b of the public offi-
    48  cers law.
