and WHEREAS, Robert Galazin of Croton-on-Hudson, New York, died on Friday, April 25, 2025, at the age of 71; and WHEREAS, Born on December 13, 1953, to the late Mary Balitchik and Stanley Galazin, Robert Galazin grew up with a strong commitment to community and family; and WHEREAS, On October 12, 1974, Robert Galazin married the love of his life, Marlene Seelke and they shared a blissful life full of love, laughter and mutual respect; and WHEREAS, Robert Galazin distinguished himself in his profession and by his sincere dedication and substantial contribution to the welfare of his community; and WHEREAS, Possessing a deep commitment to his community, Robert Galazin worked for the U.S. Postal Service for 38 years until he retired; he also served his community by working with the Croton Police Department for 15 years; and WHEREAS, Robert Galazin's commitment to excellence, and his spirit of humanity, carried over into all fields of enterprise, including charitable and civic endeavors; and WHEREAS, With a generous spirit, a strong work ethic, profound love for his family and a caring nature Robert Galazin was a well-respected figure in his community; and WHEREAS, Through his love of the Green Bay Packers, the New York Mets and hunting, Robert Galazin embraced life by taking joy from his interests; and WHEREAS, Robert Galazin is survived by his devoted wife Marlene Galazin; his cherished son Stephen; daughter-in-law Azalia; his adored granddaughter Beatrice, who brought immense joy to his life; his brother Stanley and sister-in-law Mary; his sister Barbara; his brother-in-law Kurt Seelke and his wife Sheila; several nieces, nephews, cousins, aunts, and uncles; and WHEREAS, A man of integrity, kindness, and humor, Robert Galazin's presence was a comfort to those who knew him; he leaves behind a legacy of devotion to his family and his community, and a life full of meaningful contributions; and WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of compassion, Robert Galazin leaves behind a legacy which will long endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all he served and befriended; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of Robert Galazin, distinguished citizen and devoted member of his community; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Robert Galazin.
