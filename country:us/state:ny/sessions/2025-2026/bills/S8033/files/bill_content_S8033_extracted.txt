FERNANDEZ -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Crime Victims, Crime and Correction AN ACT to amend the correction law, in relation to prohibiting incarcer- ated individuals suffering from a substance use disorder from being prescribed medications for a different disorder The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Paragraph (a) of subdivision 2 of section 626 of the 2 correction law, as separately amended by chapters 147 and 486 of the 3 laws of 2022, is amended to read as follows: 4 (a) The commissioner, in conjunction with the office of addiction 5 services and supports, shall establish a program to be administered at 6 correctional facilities within the department in the state, for the 7 purpose of employing medication assisted treatment for incarcerated 8 individuals in such facilities who are undergoing treatment for a 9 substance use disorder. Such program shall include all forms of medica- 10 tion assisted treatments approved for the treatment of a substance use 11 disorder by the Federal Food and Drug Administration for the duration of 12 an incarcerated individual's incarceration and shall provide an individ- 13 ualized treatment plan for each participant. After a medical screening, 14 incarcerated individuals who are determined to suffer from a substance 15 use disorder, for which FDA approved addiction medications exist shall 16 be offered placement in the medication assisted treatment program. 17 Placement in such program shall not be mandatory. Each participating 18 incarcerated individual shall work with an authorized specialist to 19 determine an individualized treatment plan, including an appropriate 20 level of counseling.
