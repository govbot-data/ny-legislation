MARTINEZ -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Civil Service and Pensions AN ACT to amend the civil service law, in relation to the compensation paid to persons employed in positions requiring foreign language skills and directing the department of civil service to conduct a study on certain job titles that require proficiency in a language other than English The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 130 of the civil service law is amended by adding a 2 new subdivision 15 to read as follows: 3 15. Whenever the director finds that under prevailing wage practices 4 in private or other public employment in the state, employees in a given 5 occupation who are required to be proficient in a language other than 6 English receive a higher rate of pay than employees in the same occupa- 7 tion who are not required to be proficient in a language other than 8 English, the director may, subject to the approval of the director of 9 the budget, establish a pay differential for titles that require profi- 10 ciency in a language other than English. Such differential shall be in 11 addition to an employee's basic salary and shall not affect, impair or 12 impede any performance advance payments, performance awards, longevity 13 payments or other rights or benefits to which an employee may be enti- 14 tled under the provisions of this chapter, provided, however, that any 15 differential payable pursuant to this subdivision shall be included as 16 compensation for retirement purposes. A pay differential shall be termi- 17 nated for any employee when the employee ceases to be employed in the 18 position for which such pay differential was authorized. A pay differen- 19 tial shall remain in effect until terminated by the director of the 20 classification and compensation division with the consent of the direc- 21 tor of the budget or until a new pay differential is authorized pursuant EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. The department of civil service is hereby directed to conduct a 7 study and publish a report on titles in the state service that require 8 proficiency in a language other than English. The study and report shall 9 include, but not be limited to: 10 1. the number of employees in titles that require proficiency in a 11 language other than English and the appointment type held by such 12 employees; 13 2. the jurisdictional classification of such titles; 14 3. the salary grades and allocations of such titles; 15 4. a determination of whether the current salary allocations for each 16 title adequately reflect the title's responsibilities and duties; 17 5. whether any changes should be made to the current descriptions of 18 such titles to reflect more accurately the actual duties of such titles; 19 6. the gender and race/ethnicity identification of the employees in 20 such titles; 21 7. a list of state offices and/or departments with employees in such 22 titles and the location of such titles; 23 8. the number of times that examinations are held for appointment or 24 promotion into titles that require proficiency in a language other than 25 English in the past five years; 26 9. the number of individuals that have taken examinations for appoint- 27 ment or promotion into titles that require proficiency in a language 28 other than English in the past five years; 29 10. the number of times that existing employees in titles that require 30 proficiency in a language other than English transferred or were 31 promoted to a different title within the past five years; and 32 11. a determination of whether there are sufficient career mobility 33 opportunities in the state service for individuals employed in titles 34 that require proficiency in a language other than English. 35 § 3. For the purposes of this act, every public employer as defined in 36 this section shall provide all requested assistance and information to 37 the department of civil service in conducting the study required by this 38 act. It shall be the duty of each officer of the public employer to 39 comply with and aid in all proper ways in carrying into effect the 40 provisions of this study. For the purposes of this act, the term "public 41 employer" shall mean those employers with offices and positions in the 42 classified service of the state and the term "classified service" shall 43 have the same meaning as in section 40 of the civil service law. 44 § 4.
