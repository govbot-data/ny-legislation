RIVERA -- read once and referred to the Committee on Labor AN ACT to amend the labor law, in relation to requiring reporting on merit determinations relating to unfair labor practices and a certif- icate of compliance to be eligible for state economic incentives The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The labor law is amended by adding a new section 45 to read 2 as follows: 3 § 45. Unfair labor practice reporting. 1. Any employer applying for 4 any economic incentive, or a renewal of such incentive, authorized under 5 the tax law, economic development law, this chapter, public housing 6 law, general municipal law, general city law, insurance law, real prop- 7 erty tax law, or any other laws of the state of New York shall provide 8 a certificate of compliance as part of its application. 9 2. Any employer applying for or receiving economic incentives from the 10 state shall file an annual report with the department detailing any 11 merit determinations relating to unfair labor practices in violation of 12 article twenty of this chapter or the National Labor Relations Act (29 13 U.S.C. 151 et seq.) and the outcome of such determinations. 14 3. After a review of the materials submitted pursuant to subdivision 15 two of this section by the department, employers shall receive a certif- 16 icate of compliance deeming them eligible to receive economic incentives 17 authorized under state law. 18 § 2. This act shall take effect on the one hundred eightieth day after 19 it shall have become a law. Effective immediately, the addition, amend- 20 ment and/or repeal of any rule or regulation necessary for the implemen- 21 tation of this act on its effective date are authorized to be made and 22 completed on or before such effective date.
