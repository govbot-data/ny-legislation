NOVAKHOV -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Judiciary AN ACT to amend the judiciary law, in relation to increasing the allow- ances provided to trial and grand jurors The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Subdivision (a) of section 521 of the judiciary law, as 2 amended by chapter 302 of the laws of 2002, is amended to read as 3 follows: 4 (a) Except as provided in subdivision (b) of this section, trial and 5 grand jurors in each court of the unified court system shall be entitled 6 to an allowance equal to the sum of [forty] sixty dollars per day for 7 the first ten days and one hundred dollars for each and every day there- 8 after of physical attendance wherein the court convenes, except that no 9 person who is employed shall be entitled to receive such allowance if, 10 pursuant to section five hundred nineteen of this article, [his or her] 11 such juror's employer is prohibited from withholding [the first forty 12 dollars] such applicable amount of wages of such person during such 13 applicable period and such person's daily wages equal or exceed [forty 14 dollars] such applicable amounts. If such person's daily wages are less 15 than [forty dollars] the applicable amount, [he or she] such juror shall 16 be entitled to receive an allowance hereunder equal to the difference 17 between [forty dollars] such amount and the amount of [his or her] such 18 juror's daily wages. Such fees and those expenses actually and necessar- 19 ily incurred in providing food and lodging for jurors shall be a state 20 charge payable out of funds appropriated to the office of court adminis- 21 tration for that purpose. 22 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
