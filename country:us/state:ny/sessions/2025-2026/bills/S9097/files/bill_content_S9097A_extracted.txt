AN ACT to amend the general business law, in relation to banned members
of social referral services
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Paragraph (d) of subdivision 1 of section 394-c of the
2 general business law, as amended by chapter 13 of the laws of 2025, is
3 amended and two new paragraphs (h) and (i) are added to read as follows:
4 (d) "banned member" shall mean the member whose account or profile is
5 the subject of a fraud ban or content ban.
6 (h) "content ban" shall mean when a member's account or profile is
7 barred from an online dating service because, in the judgment of such
8 online dating service, such member was found to have engaged in or is
9 substantially likely to have engaged in conduct or share content that
10 violates law, breaches such online dating service's terms or policies,
11 or poses a significant risk of harm to users on such online dating
12 service's platform.
13 (i) "contract and customer service records" shall include the contract
14 between a user of a social referral service's platform and such social
15 referral service, including any contract amendments or ancillary agree-
16 ments, evidence of such user's acceptance of such contract's terms, such
17 user's name and contact information, and any notices provided by either
18 such party including, but not limited to, notices of cancellation,
19 consent, or withdrawal of consent, customer service records, and/or
20 purchase and billing records, except that such records shall exclude any
21 sensitive payment information.
22 § 2. Subdivision 6 of section 394-c of the general business law, as
23 amended by chapter 13 of the laws of 2025, is amended to read as
24 follows:

1 6. (a) Every contract for social referral service shall provide that
2 at the expiration of the contract or at the expiration of services

6 photographs or background information shall be [promptly] returned by
7 the seller to the purchaser by certified mail or destroyed and deleted
8 from any electronic storage devices within thirty days, with certif-
9 ication of destruction or deletion promptly provided to the purchaser
10 upon request, unless the retention of such information and material is
11 required [(a)]:
12 (i) by federal, state, or local laws, rules or regulations [or (b)];
13 (ii) to comply with a judicial court order; or
14 (iii) by such social referral service to maintain enforcement of a
15 fraud ban or content ban.
16 (b) Information and material retained to effectuate and enforce a
17 fraud ban or content ban pursuant to subparagraph (iii) of paragraph (a)
18 of this subdivision shall not be used for any other purpose except for
19 identifying the purchaser in order to effectuate and enforce such fraud
20 ban or content ban. Such information and material retained for such
21 purpose shall be limited to the information and material collected by
22 the social referral service in order for the purchaser to create an
23 account and profile. A social referral service may retain contract and
24 customer service records for a period not to exceed six years, or for a
25 period for which a purchaser may be authorized to bring action under
26 section two hundred thirteen of the civil practice law and rules, which-
27 ever is longer.
