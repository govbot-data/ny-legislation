        Introduced  by M. of A. STECK -- read once and referred to the Committee
          on Transportation
 
        AN ACT to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to  prohibiting
          certain entities from engaging in the business of securing motor vehi-
          cle registrations or giving instructions as to procuring motor vehicle
          registrations
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Section 395 of the vehicle and traffic law, as  amended  by
     2  chapter 124 of the laws of 1974, is amended to read as follows:
     3    § 395. Certain  private  service  bureaus  to be licensed.   Except as
     4  otherwise provided herein, no person, firm, association  or  corporation
     5  shall, engage in the business of assisting for hire in securing licenses
     6  to  drive  motor  vehicles or registrations or titles of motor vehicles,
     7  nor shall any person, firm, association or corporation for  compensation
     8  give  instructions  as  to procuring licenses to drive motor vehicles or
     9  registrations or titles of motor vehicles, without being the holder of a
    10  license for such purpose issued by the commissioner of  motor  vehicles.
    11  An  applicant  for  a  license  shall furnish the commissioner with such
    12  information and such references as  to  moral  character  as  [he]  such
    13  commissioner may reasonably require. Every application shall be accompa-
    14  nied  by  a  fee  of  twenty-five dollars, which shall be regarded as an
    15  application fee and shall in no event be refunded. If an application  be
    16  approved by the commissioner, the applicant upon the payment of an addi-
    17  tional fee of twenty-five dollars shall be granted a license which shall
    18  expire  on the thirtieth day of June following the date of its issuance.
    19  The commissioner shall issue a  license  certificate  to  each  licensee
    20  which certificate shall be conspicuously displayed in the place of busi-
    21  ness of the licensee, or, if the licensee has no place of business, such
    22  certificates shall be exhibited at the request of any person. In case of

     1  the  loss,  mutilation or destruction of a certificate, the commissioner
     2  shall issue a duplicate upon proof of the facts and the payment of a fee
     3  of one dollar. The refusal to issue a  license  may  be  reviewed  by  a
     4  proceeding  under  article  seventy-eight  of the civil practice law and
     5  rules. Such license shall be renewed annually upon the payment of a  fee
     6  of  twenty-five dollars, such renewal to take effect on the first day of
     7  July in each year.  Upon renewal, the commissioner may, in [his] at such
     8  commissioner's discretion, issue a license which shall be  valid  for  a
     9  two  year  period.  The fee for any such two year renewal shall be fifty
    10  dollars.
    11    No license shall be issued under this section nor shall any renewal of
    12  a license issued under this section be made for conducting business in a
    13  city having a population of fifty thousand or  more,  according  to  the
    14  latest  federal  census  if  the  place  of business of the licensee, or
    15  branch thereof, is within fifteen hundred feet of a building,  owned  or
    16  leased  by  the state, a county or a city, in which motor vehicle regis-
    17  trations or licenses to drive motor vehicles are issued to  the  public.
    18  The  said  distance  of fifteen hundred feet shall be measured along the
    19  public streets by the nearest route from  such  place  of  business,  or
    20  branch thereof, to such building. The provisions of this paragraph shall
    21  not  apply  to a holder of a certificate of registration issued pursuant
    22  to section four hundred fifteen of this chapter.
    23    No license shall be issued under this section nor shall any renewal of
    24  a license issued under this section be made for a person, firm,  associ-
    25  ation  or  corporation who engages in the business of assisting for hire
    26  in securing motor vehicle registrations or who, for  compensation,  give
    27  instructions as to procuring motor vehicle registrations. The provisions
    28  of this paragraph shall not apply to a holder of a license issued pursu-
    29  ant  to  section three hundred ninety-four of this article or the holder
    30  of a certificate of registration issued pursuant to section four hundred
    31  fifteen of this chapter.
    32    A licensee shall be subject to such reasonable regulations  concerning
    33  the  business  conducted  under  [his]  such  licensee's  license as the
    34  commissioner may prescribe and  [he]  such  licensee  shall  permit  the
    35  commissioner,  or  [his] such commissioner's representatives, to inspect
    36  [his] such licensee's place of business on any business  day  and  shall
    37  furnish  to  the  commissioner,  or  [his] such commissioner's represen-
    38  tative, such information concerning the conduct of the business  as  may
    39  be reasonably required.
    40    The  provisions  of subdivisions five and six of section three hundred
    41  ninety-four of this [chapter] article shall be applicable  with  respect
    42  to this section.
    43    The holder of a license issued pursuant to section three hundred nine-
    44  ty-four  of  this  [chapter]  article  or the holder of a certificate of
    45  registration issued pursuant to section four  hundred  fifteen  of  this
    46  chapter  shall not be required to secure a license under this section in
    47  order to conduct a business for which a  license  is  required  by  this
    48  section  but  any  such  licensee, or registrant, who shall conduct such
    49  business, shall be subject to the same visitation and regulation by  the
    50  commissioner with reference to such business as provided in this section
    51  with respect to a licensee under this section.
    52    As  used in this section the phrase "licenses to drive motor vehicles"
    53  includes the term "learners' permits".
    54    A violation of any of the provisions of this section shall  constitute
    55  a misdemeanor.
    56    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
