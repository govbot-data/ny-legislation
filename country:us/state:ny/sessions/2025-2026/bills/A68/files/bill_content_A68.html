SPONSORHevesi

Rpld §§331.1 - 331.7, amd Fam Ct Act, generally; amd §325, Judy L
 
Relates to the discovery provisions applicable to juvenile delinquency proceedings in family court with regard to the timing of discovery, disclosure prior to certain guilty pleas, DNA evidence, court orders, and the admissibility of discovery.

     2  or sanction. (a) When material or information is discoverable under this
     3  part  but  is disclosed belatedly, the court shall impose an appropriate
     4  remedy or sanction if the party entitled to disclosure shows that it was
     5  prejudiced.  Regardless  of a showing of prejudice the party entitled to
     6  disclosure shall be given reasonable time to prepare and respond to  the
     7  new material.
     8    (b)  When  material or information is discoverable under this part but
     9  cannot be disclosed because it has been lost  or  destroyed,  the  court
    10  shall  impose an appropriate remedy or sanction if the party entitled to
    11  disclosure shows that the lost or destroyed material may have  contained
    12  some  information  relevant to a contested issue. The appropriate remedy
    13  or sanction is that which is proportionate  to  the  potential  ways  in
    14  which  the lost or destroyed material reasonably could have been helpful
    15  to the party entitled to disclosure.
    16    2. Available remedies or sanctions. For failure  to  comply  with  any
    17  discovery  order  imposed or issued pursuant to this part, the court may
    18  make a further order for discovery, grant a continuance,  order  that  a
    19  hearing be reopened, order that a witness be called or recalled, draw an
    20  adverse  inference  regarding  the  non-compliance, preclude or strike a
    21  witness's testimony or a portion of  a  witness's  testimony,  admit  or
    22  exclude  evidence,  order a mistrial, order the dismissal of all or some
    23  of the charges, or make such other order as  it  deems  just  under  the
    24  circumstances;  except  that  any  sanction against the respondent shall
    25  comport with the respondent's constitutional right to present a defense,
    26  and precluding a witness from testifying on  behalf  of  the  respondent
    27  shall  be  permissible only upon a finding that the respondent's failure
    28  to comply with the discovery obligation or order was willful  and  moti-
    29  vated by a desire to obtain a tactical advantage.
    30    3.  Consequences  of non-disclosure of statement of witness testifying
    31  for the presentment agency. The failure of  the  presentment  agency  to
    32  disclose  any written or recorded statement made by a witness testifying
    33  on the agency's behalf, which relates  to  the  subject  matter  of  the
    34  witness's testimony, shall not constitute grounds for any court to order
    35  a new pre-trial hearing or set aside an adjudication, or reverse, modify
    36  or vacate an adjudication, in the absence of a showing by the respondent
    37  that  there  is  a  reasonable possibility that the non-disclosure mate-
    38  rially contributed to the result  of  the  trial  or  other  proceeding;
    39  provided,  however,  that  nothing in this section shall affect or limit
    40  any right the respondent may have to a reopened pre-trial  hearing  when
    41  such statements were disclosed before the close of evidence at the fact-
    42  finding hearing.
    43    §  331.13. Admissibility of discovery. The fact that a party has indi-
    44  cated during the discovery  process  an  intention  to  offer  specified
    45  evidence or to call a specified witness is not admissible in evidence or
    46  grounds for adverse comment at a pre-trial or fact-finding hearing.
