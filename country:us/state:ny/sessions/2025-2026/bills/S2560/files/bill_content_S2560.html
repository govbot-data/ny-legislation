        AN ACT to amend the tax law, in relation to  processing  fees  resulting
          from  credit card or other non-cash payments selected by passengers of
          taxicabs and for-hire transportation

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  Subdivision (c) of section 1283 of the tax law is amended
     2  by adding a new paragraph 4 to read as follows:
     3    (4) The surcharge imposed by this article  must  be  passed  along  to
     4  passengers and separately stated on any receipt that is provided to such
     5  passengers,  and may include, if permitted by the regulatory agency, any
     6  processing fees resulting from a credit card or other  non-cash  payment
     7  option  selected  by such passenger. The passing along of such surcharge
     8  shall not be construed by any court or administrative body as the  impo-
     9  sition  of  the surcharge on the person or entity that pays for the for-
    10  hire transportation trip.  All regulatory agencies must adjust any fares
    11  that are authorized by them to include the  surcharge  imposed  by  this
    12  article,  and  may adjust such authorized fare to include any processing
    13  fees imposed on the payment of such surcharge, and must require that any
    14  meter or other instrument used in any for-hire vehicle regulated  by  it
    15  to  calculate fares be adjusted to include the surcharge and any attend-
    16  ant fees where authorized. Any processing  fees  charged  to  passengers
    17  shall also be separately stated on any receipt provided to passengers.
    18    §  2. Paragraph 1 of subdivision (b) of section 1299-b of the tax law,
    19  as added by section 2 of part NNN of chapter 59 of the laws of 2018,  is
    20  amended to read as follows:
    21    (1)  The  surcharge  imposed  by  this article must be passed along to
    22  passengers and separately stated on any receipt that is provided to such
    23  passengers, and may include, if permitted by the regulatory agency,  any

     1  processing  fees  resulting from a credit card or other non-cash payment
     2  option selected by such passenger. The passing along of  such  surcharge
     3  shall  not be construed by any court or administrative body as the impo-
     4  sition  of  the surcharge on the person or entity that pays for the for-
     5  hire transportation trip. All regulatory agencies must adjust any  fares
     6  that  are  authorized  by  them to include the surcharge imposed by this
     7  article, and may adjust such authorized fare to include  any  processing
     8  fees imposed on the payment of such surcharge, and must require that any
     9  meter  or  other instrument used in any for-hire vehicle regulated by it
    10  to calculate fares be adjusted to include the surcharge and any  attend-
    11  ant  fees  where  authorized.  Any processing fees charged to passengers
    12  shall also be separately stated on any receipt provided to passengers.
    13    § 3. (a) For purposes of this  section,  the  term  "surcharge"  shall
    14  mean:
    15    (i) the taxicab improvement surcharge, the rush hour surcharge and the
    16  nighttime  surcharge  imposed on taxicab fares pursuant to section 58-26
    17  of the rules of the city of New York; and
    18    (ii) any other surcharge imposed on taxicab or other  for-hire  trans-
    19  portation  fares  which is not specifically set forth in section 1283 or
    20  1299-b of the tax law, or in paragraph (i) of this subdivision.
    21    (b) Notwithstanding any law, rule or regulation to the  contrary,  any
    22  surcharge imposed upon a taxicab or for-hire transportation fare must be
    23  passed  along to passengers and separately stated on any receipt that is
    24  provided to such passengers,  and  may  include,  if  permitted  by  the
    25  authorizing  regulatory  agency,  any  processing  fees resulting from a
    26  credit card or other non-cash payment option selected by such passenger.
    27  The passing along of any such surcharge shall not be  construed  by  any
    28  court  or administrative body as the imposition of any such surcharge on
    29  the person or entity that pays for the taxicab or  for-hire  transporta-
    30  tion  trip.  All  regulatory  agencies  must  adjust  any fares that are
    31  authorized by them to include any surcharges imposed by the state or any
    32  other governmental body, and may adjust such authorized fare to  include
    33  any  processing  fees  imposed  on  the  payment  of  such  surcharge or
    34  surcharges, and must require that any meter or other instrument used  in
    35  any  taxicab  or  for-hire vehicle regulated by it to calculate fares be
    36  adjusted to include such surcharge or surcharges and any attendant  fees
    37  where  authorized.  Any processing fees charged to passengers shall also
    38  be separately stated on any receipt provided to passengers.
    39    § 4. This act shall take effect on the ninetieth day  after  it  shall
    40  have become a law.
