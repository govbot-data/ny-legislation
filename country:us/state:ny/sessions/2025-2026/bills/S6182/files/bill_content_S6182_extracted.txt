PARKER -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Judiciary CONCURRENT RESOLUTION OF THE SENATE AND ASSEMBLY proposing an amendment to article 5 of the constitution, in relation to reducing a police officer's pension to satisfy judgments in a wrongful death action 1 Section 1. Resolved (if the Assembly concur), That section 7 of arti- 2 cle 5 of the constitution be amended by adding a new subdivision (e) to 3 read as follows: 4 (e) (i) Notwithstanding subdivision (a) of this section, the public 5 pension of a police officer, as defined in paragraph (ii) of this subdi- 6 vision, who is found liable in a wrongful death action arising out of 7 the performance of such officer's duties, may be reduced to no less than 8 twenty thousand dollars to satisfy the judgment against such police 9 officer in such wrongful death action prior to the payment of any public 10 funds, following notice and a hearing by an appropriate court, as 11 provided by law. The court determination whether to reduce such pension 12 shall be based on the consideration of factors including the severity of 13 such police officer's actions and the proportionality of a reduction of 14 such pension to such actions. When a court issues an order to reduce 15 such pension, the court shall consider and determine specific findings 16 as to the amount of such reduction, if any, and whether the reduction 17 would result in undue hardship or other inequity upon any dependent 18 children, spouse or other dependents; and other factors as provided by 19 law. The legislature shall enact legislation to implement this amend- 20 ment. 21 (ii) For the purposes of paragraph (i) of this subdivision, the term 22 "police officer" shall mean any member of a duly organized police force 23 or department of any county, city, town, village, municipality, authori- 24 ty, police district, regional state park commission when employed full 25 time in the enforcement of the general criminal laws of the state, or EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
