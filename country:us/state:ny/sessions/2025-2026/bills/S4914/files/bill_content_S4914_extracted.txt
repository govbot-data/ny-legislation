Legislative findings and intent. The legislature declares 2 that it is the public policy of New York state that every individual 3 possesses a fundamental right of privacy and equality with respect to 4 their personal medical decisions, and should be able to safely effectu- 5 ate those decisions, including by seeking and obtaining reproductive 6 health care or gender-affirming care, free from the threat that they may 7 be penalized for such legally protected care. 8 The legislature finds and reaffirms that the state has a paramount 9 interest in protecting access to health care, including reproductive 10 health care and gender-affirming health care, as such health care is 11 fundamental to each person's exercise of bodily autonomy, dignity, and 12 equal citizenship. The state has enshrined the fundamental right to EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. LBD00432-09-5 S. 4914--B 2 1 bodily autonomy and reproductive decision-making in its constitution. It 2 has furthered codified in numerous statutes the principle that every 3 individual has the right to privacy and equality with respect to their 4 personal medical decisions, including reproductive and gender-affirming 5 health care. 6 The legislature further finds that, despite the vital importance of 7 reproductive and gender-affirming health care, those who seek, provide, 8 or facilitate such health care are facing an unprecedented risk that 9 they will be targeted or penalized for doing so. The state has a strong 10 public policy of promoting access to these vital forms of health care 11 for residents and non-residents alike, and of maintaining New York's 12 status as a beacon of reproductive freedom, health care access, and 13 equality for all. Efforts to penalize or restrict reproductive or 14 gender-affirming care have a chilling effect on access to this important 15 health care for all individuals, and accordingly threaten each person's 16 health and bodily autonomy. 17 To secure the fundamental right to bodily autonomy and ensure access 18 to health care, the legislature finds it is critical to enact 19 protections to shield those who seek, provide, or facilitate reproduc- 20 tive or gender-affirming health care from efforts to restrict or penal- 21 ize them for doing so. 22 In order to fully effectuate this purpose, the legislature finds and 23 affirms that this act's protections must apply not just to those who 24 seek or provide health care, but also to those who facilitate it, such 25 as organizations that offer financial assistance to people in need, 26 internet service providers that host websites offering accurate health 27 information, or a friend who drives their loved one to the clinic. 28 Facilitators play an important role in ensuring access to health care, 29 and efforts to restrict or penalize them because of their assistance in 30 the exercise of these important rights have a chilling effect on access 31 to care and thereby impinge upon the fundamental rights to privacy, 32 equality, and bodily autonomy. 33 § 2.
