Relates to permitting certain not-for-profit corporations engaged in engineering for certain conservation efforts to do business or provide professional engineering, land surveying, or professional geology services in the state.

        AN ACT to amend the education law, in  relation  to  permitting  certain
          not-for-profit corporations engaged in engineering for certain conser-
          vation  efforts  to  do  business or provide professional engineering,
          land surveying, or professional geology services in the state
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  Section  7209 of the education law is amended by adding a
     2  new subdivision 10 to read as follows:
     3    10. Nothing in this article shall prohibit  a  foreign  not-for-profit
     4  corporation, as defined in section one hundred two of the not-for-profit
     5  corporation law, engaged in engineering for conservation efforts associ-
     6  ated  with  (a)  tidal wetland, as defined in subdivision one of section
     7  25-0103 of the environmental conservation law, restoration, (b) freshwa-
     8  ter wetland, as defined in subdivision one of  section  24-0107  of  the
     9  environmental  conservation  law,  restoration, (c) habitat restoration,
    10  (d) groundwater recharge, or (e) natural infrastructure from obtaining a
    11  certificate of authority to do  business  in  the  state  of  New  York,
    12  provided  that  the  business proposed to be done by such not-for-profit
    13  corporation  within  this  state  relates  to  the  stated  conservation
    14  resources  and shall be conducted by a professional engineer pursuant to
    15  this article.
    16    § 2. Subdivisions 1, 2, and 3 of section 7210 of  the  education  law,
    17  subdivision 1 as amended by chapter 475 of the laws of 2014 and subdivi-
    18  sions 2 and 3 as amended by chapter 483 of the laws of 2001, are amended
    19  to read as follows:
    20    1.  Domestic  or  foreign  professional  service  corporations, design
    21  professional service corporations, professional service limited  liabil-
    22  ity companies, foreign professional service limited liability companies,
    23  registered  limited  liability partnerships, New York registered foreign

    38  New  York  including the power to discipline and impose penalties in the
    39  same manner and to the same extent as is provided with respect to  indi-
