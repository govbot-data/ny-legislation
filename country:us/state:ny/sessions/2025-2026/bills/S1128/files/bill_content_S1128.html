SAME ASNo Same As

Add §14-o, Transp L
 
Establishes a bicycle and pedestrian planning assistance program within the department of transportation to provide county and municipal governments with planning assistance for locally driven projects and activities that lead to bicycle and pedestrian friendly infrastructure; establishes criteria for approval for assistance under such program.

        AN ACT to amend the transportation law, in relation  to  establishing  a
          bicycle and pedestrian planning assistance program
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The transportation law is amended by adding a  new  section
     2  14-o to read as follows:
     3    § 14-o. Bicycle and pedestrian planning assistance program. 1. Program
     4  established.  There is hereby established within the department a "bicy-
     5  cle  and  pedestrian  planning  assistance program". The purposes of the
     6  program shall be to provide county and municipal governments with  plan-
     7  ning  assistance for locally driven projects and activities that lead to
     8  bicycle and pedestrian friendly infrastructure.
     9    2. Application to program. (a) The commissioner shall create a compet-
    10  itive application process under which county and  municipal  governments
    11  may  apply  for  assistance  under  the  program. The commissioner shall
    12  develop evaluation methods for approval or denial of county and  munici-
    13  pal  governments  that  have  applied  to the program for assistance, in
    14  accordance with the criteria under paragraph (b) of this subdivision.
    15    (b) In order for a county or municipal  government's  application  for
    16  assistance  to be approved under paragraph (a) of this subdivision, such
    17  county or municipal  government's  application  shall  include  evidence
    18  that:
    19    (i)  sincere interest exists in the local community within such county
    20  or municipality in improving bicycle and pedestrian friendly infrastruc-
    21  ture;
    22    (ii) safety issues exist within such county or municipality due  to  a
    23  significant  need  for  improved bicycle and pedestrian friendly infras-
    24  tructure, and that such safety issues can be addressed if assistance  is
    25  provided under the program;

     1    (iii)  if  such  application  is  approved,  planning  efforts will be
     2  directed towards the needs of special  populations  including,  but  not
     3  limited  to,  senior citizens, children and/or people with disabilities;

     5    (iv)  such  county  or  municipal  government has an existing complete
     6  streets policy under section three hundred  thirty-one  of  the  highway
     7  law, or an existing commitment to adopting such a policy.
     8    (c)  To the extent practicable, at least thirty-five percent of appli-
     9  cations approved pursuant to this subdivision shall be for  projects  in
    10  disadvantaged  communities,  or  that the county or municipal government
    11  demonstrates will provide a beneficial impact to one or  more  disadvan-
    12  taged communities.
    13    3.  Assistance  under  program.  (a) All planning efforts for improved
    14  bicycle and pedestrian friendly infrastructure under the  program  shall
    15  be  locally  driven, including the commitment of personnel and/or finan-
    16  cial resources by the county or municipal government,  public  outreach,
    17  and participation by local officials and citizens.
    18    (b)  The program shall provide support to county and municipal govern-
    19  ments approved under subdivision two of this section, at  no  cost,  for
    20  the  following  planning  activities  to  improve bicycle and pedestrian
    21  friendly infrastructure, including, but not limited to:
    22    (i) Evaluation of bicycle and/or  pedestrian  safety  issues  and  the
    23  development of potential solutions;
    24    (ii)  Comprehensive  bicycle  and/or  pedestrian  plans or circulation
    25  elements;
    26    (iii) Bicycle and/or pedestrian circulation studies  focusing  on  key
    27  corridors  or activity centers in the county or municipality, including,
    28  but not limited to schools, parks, shopping centers,  transit  stations,
    29  employment centers, and other community facilities;
    30    (iv)  Planning  location  or  feasibility  assessments  for shared-use
    31  paths, including rail trails and rail with trails;
    32    (v) Complete streets policy development and implementation plans under
    33  section three hundred thirty-one of the highway law;
    34    (vi) Sidewalk inventories;
    35    (vii) Walkability audits;
    36    (viii) Inventories or audits of bicycle compatible roadways and desig-
    37  nated bicycle facilities;
    38    (ix) Bicycle suitability mapping;
    39    (x) Deficiency analyses, in terms of bicycle and/or pedestrian  travel
    40  along  selected  routes  to schools, transit facilities, downtown areas,
    41  and other frequently commuted routes,  along  with  recommendations  for
    42  mitigating such deficiencies;
    43    (xi) Residential neighborhood or downtown traffic calming; and/or
    44    (xii)  Safe routes to school travel projects under subdivision thirty-
    45  five of section fourteen of this article.
    46    4. Funding. The commissioner shall, subject to  legislative  approval,
    47  appropriate  any  funds  necessary for the administration of the program
    48  from those received by the state under the federal department of  trans-
    49  portation's congestion mitigation and air quality improvement program.
    50    5.  Rules  and  regulations.  The  commissioner shall be authorized to
    51  promulgate any rules and/or regulations necessary for the implementation
    52  of the provisions of this section.
    53    6. Definitions. For the purposes of this section, the following  terms
    54  shall have the following meanings:
    55    (a) "Disadvantaged communities" means communities that bear burdens of
    56  negative  public  health  effects,  environmental  pollution, impacts of
