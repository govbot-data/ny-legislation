Short title. This act shall be known and may be cited as 2 the "toll payer advocacy and relief act". 3 § 2. Legislative findings and intent. Since the adoption of cashless 4 tolling and tolls by mail collection in New York state, many drivers 5 have been complaining of excessive fines and charges resulting in oner- 6 ous debt. Oftentimes, penalties are applied before a driver is aware of 7 a missed toll. Many drivers have complained of insufficient notifica- 8 tion, poor communication with the toll collecting authorities, and lack 9 of advocacy to dispute tolls and fines. This legislation is necessary to 10 make improvements to the cashless tolling and tolls by mail collection 11 systems that will provide relief to drivers from excessive fines, while 12 providing them adequate time to pay the original amount of tolls owed, 13 without penalty.
