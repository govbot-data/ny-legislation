BILL NOS00143
 
SAME ASSAME AS A06190
 
SPONSORPALUMBO
 
COSPNSRBORRELLO, GALLIVAN, MURRAY, OBERACKER, STEC
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Add §837-f-3, Exec L
 
Establishes a coordinated law enforcement adult rescue "teal alert" for missing adults; provides that local law enforcement agencies notify the division of criminal justice services about missing adults; provides that such division shall issue an alert about such missing adult.

        AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to enacting "Gabby's law"
          establishing a coordinated law enforcement adult rescue  "teal  alert"
          for missing adults
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Short title. This act shall be known and may  be  cited  as
     2  "Gabby's law" establishing a "teal alert".
     3    §  2.  The executive law is amended by adding a new section 837-f-3 to
     4  read as follows:
     5    § 837-f-3. Coordinated law enforcement adult rescue alert for  missing
     6  adults. 1. Definitions. As used in this section:
     7    (a)  "Adult"  means a person who is eighteen years of age or older but
     8  younger than sixty-five years of age.
     9    (b) "Alert" means the  statewide  coordinated  law  enforcement  adult
    10  rescue  alert for missing adults that is developed and implemented under
    11  this section.
    12    (c) "Bodily injury" means physical pain, illness, or any impairment of
    13  physical condition.
    14    (d) "Local law enforcement agency" means a local law enforcement agen-
    15  cy with jurisdiction over the investigation of a missing adult.
    16    2. With the cooperation  of  the  department  of  transportation,  the
    17  office  of  the governor, and other appropriate law enforcement agencies
    18  in this state, the division shall develop  and  implement  a  system  to
    19  allow a statewide alert to be activated on behalf of a missing adult.
    20    3.  (a)  The  commissioner  shall  be the statewide coordinator of the
    21  alert system.

     1    (b) The commissioner shall adopt rules and issue directives as  neces-
     2  sary  to ensure proper implementation of the alert system. The rules and
     3  directives shall include:
     4    (i)  the  procedures  to  be used by a local law enforcement agency to
     5  verify whether an adult is missing and  whether  circumstances  indicate
     6  that:
     7    (A) the missing adult is in imminent danger of bodily injury or death;
     8  or
     9    (B)  the  disappearance  of the missing adult may not have been volun-
    10  tary, including cases of abduction or kidnapping; and
    11    (C) the missing adult's disappearance poses a credible threat  to  the
    12  safety  and  health of the individual or another identifiable person and
    13  under such circumstances also deemed appropriate by law enforcement;
    14    (ii) a description of  the  circumstances  under  which  a  local  law
    15  enforcement  agency  is  required to report a missing adult to the divi-
    16  sion; and
    17    (iii) the procedures to be used by an individual or entity  to  report
    18  information  about  a  missing adult to designated media outlets in this
    19  state.
    20    (c) The commissioner shall  prescribe  forms  for  use  by  local  law
    21  enforcement agencies in requesting activation of the alert system.
    22    4.  The  division  shall  recruit public and commercial television and
    23  radio broadcasters, private commercial entities, state or local  govern-
    24  mental  entities, the public, and other appropriate persons to assist in
    25  developing and implementing the alert system.
    26    5. (a) A state agency participating in the alert system shall:
    27    (i) cooperate with the division and assist in  developing  and  imple-
    28  menting the alert system; and
    29    (ii)  establish a plan for providing relevant information to its offi-
    30  cers, investigators, or employees, as appropriate, once the alert system
    31  has been activated.
    32    (b) In addition to its duties as a state agency under paragraph (a) of
    33  this subdivision, the department of  transportation  shall  establish  a
    34  plan  for providing relevant information to the public through an exist-
    35  ing system of dynamic message signs located across the state.
    36    6. (a) A local law enforcement agency shall notify the division if the
    37  agency:
    38    (i) receives a report regarding a missing adult;
    39    (ii) verifies that at the time the adult is reported missing:
    40    (A) the person reported missing is eighteen years of age or older  but
    41  younger than sixty-five years of age;
    42    (B) the adult's location is unknown; and
    43    (C) the adult has been missing for less than seventy-two hours;
    44    (iii)  confirms  that a preliminary investigation has taken place with
    45  respect to the disappearance and that, as  a  result  of  that  investi-
    46  gation,  the  agency  believes  that  the adult is missing under circum-
    47  stances described by subparagraph (i) of paragraph  (b)  of  subdivision
    48  three of this section; and
    49    (iv)  believes  sufficient  information is available to disseminate to
    50  the public that could assist in locating the adult, a  person  suspected
    51  of  abducting  or  kidnapping the adult, or a vehicle suspected of being
    52  used by the adult or in any abduction or kidnapping of the adult.
    53    (b) The division may modify the criteria described by paragraph (a) of
    54  this subdivision as necessary for the proper implementation of the alert
    55  system.

     1    7. (a) When a local  law  enforcement  agency  notifies  the  division
     2  pursuant  to subdivision six of this section, the division shall confirm
     3  the accuracy of the information and, if confirmed, immediately issue  an
     4  alert  under  this  section  in accordance with the division's rules and
     5  directives under subdivision three of this section.
     6    (b)  The  division  may issue the alert on its own initiative, without
     7  receiving the notification described by paragraph (a) of  this  subdivi-
     8  sion,  if  the  issuance conforms to the division's rules and directives
     9  and if the criteria described by paragraph (a)  of  subdivision  six  of
    10  this section are satisfied.
    11    (c)  In issuing the alert, the division shall send the alert to desig-
    12  nated media outlets in this  state.  Following  receipt  of  the  alert,
    13  participating  radio  stations and television stations and other partic-
    14  ipating media outlets may issue the alert  at  designated  intervals  to
    15  assist in locating the missing adult.
    16    (d) The division shall also send the alert to:
    17    (i) any appropriate law enforcement agency;
    18    (ii) the department of transportation;
    19    (iii) the New York lottery; and
    20    (iv) the independent bankers association of New York state.
    21    8. The alert shall include:
    22    (a)  all appropriate information that may lead to the safe recovery of
    23  the missing adult, as determined by the division; and
    24    (b) a statement instructing any person with information related to the
    25  missing adult to contact a local or state law enforcement agency.
    26    9. (a) The commissioner shall terminate any activation  of  the  alert
    27  with respect to a particular missing adult not later than the earlier of
    28  the date on which:
    29    (i)  the  missing  adult  is  located  or  the  situation is otherwise
    30  resolved; or
    31    (ii) the notification period ends, as determined by division rule.
    32    (b) A local law enforcement agency that locates a missing adult who is
    33  the subject of an alert under this section shall notify the division  as
    34  soon as possible that the missing adult has been located.
    35    10.  (a)  Notwithstanding  paragraph  (b)  of subdivision five of this
    36  section, the department of transportation is not  required  to  use  any
    37  existing  system  of  dynamic  message signs in a statewide alert system
    38  created under this section if such department receives notice  from  the
    39  United  States  department  of  transportation  federal highway adminis-
    40  tration that the use of such signs would result in the loss  of  federal
    41  highway  funding  or other punitive actions taken against this state due
    42  to noncompliance with federal laws, regulations, or policies.
    43    (b) Not later than one year after the effective date of this  section,
    44  the  division  shall  submit to the governor, the temporary president of
    45  the senate, and the speaker of the  assembly  a  report  containing  the
    46  results  of the study conducted pursuant to paragraph (a) of this subdi-
    47  vision, including any recommendations for proposed legislation.
    48    § 3. This act shall take effect immediately.
