PALUMBO, BORRELLO, GALLIVAN, OBERACKER, STEC -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Codes AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to enacting "Gabby's law" establishing a coordinated law enforcement adult rescue "teal alert" for missing adults The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Short title. This act shall be known and may be cited as 2 "Gabby's law" establishing a "teal alert". 3 § 2. The executive law is amended by adding a new section 837-f-3 to 4 read as follows: 5 § 837-f-3. Coordinated law enforcement adult rescue alert for missing 6 adults. 1. Definitions. As used in this section: 7 (a) "Adult" means a person who is eighteen years of age or older but 8 younger than sixty-five years of age. 9 (b) "Alert" means the statewide coordinated law enforcement adult 10 rescue alert for missing adults that is developed and implemented under 11 this section. 12 (c) "Bodily injury" means physical pain, illness, or any impairment of 13 physical condition. 14 (d) "Local law enforcement agency" means a local law enforcement agen- 15 cy with jurisdiction over the investigation of a missing adult. 16 2. With the cooperation of the department of transportation, the 17 office of the governor, and other appropriate law enforcement agencies 18 in this state, the division shall develop and implement a system to 19 allow a statewide alert to be activated on behalf of a missing adult. 20 3. (a) The commissioner shall be the statewide coordinator of the 21 alert system. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. The rules and 3 directives shall include: 4 (i) the procedures to be used by a local law enforcement agency to 5 verify whether an adult is missing and whether circumstances indicate 6 that: 7 (A) the missing adult is in imminent danger of bodily injury or death; 8 or 9 (B) the disappearance of the missing adult may not have been volun- 10 tary, including cases of abduction or kidnapping; and 11 (C) the missing adult's disappearance poses a credible threat to the 12 safety and health of the individual or another identifiable person and 13 under such circumstances also deemed appropriate by law enforcement; 14 (ii) a description of the circumstances under which a local law 15 enforcement agency is required to report a missing adult to the divi- 16 sion; and 17 (iii) the procedures to be used by an individual or entity to report 18 information about a missing adult to designated media outlets in this 19 state. 20 (c) The commissioner shall prescribe forms for use by local law 21 enforcement agencies in requesting activation of the alert system. 22 4. (a) A state agency participating in the alert system shall: 27 (i) cooperate with the division and assist in developing and imple- 28 menting the alert system; and 29 (ii) establish a plan for providing relevant information to its offi- 30 cers, investigators, or employees, as appropriate, once the alert system 31 has been activated. 32 (b) In addition to its duties as a state agency under paragraph (a) of 33 this subdivision, the department of transportation shall establish a 34 plan for providing relevant information to the public through an exist- 35 ing system of dynamic message signs located across the state. 36 6. (a) A local law enforcement agency shall notify the division if the 37 agency: 38 (i) receives a report regarding a missing adult; 39 (ii) verifies that at the time the adult is reported missing: 40 (A) the person reported missing is eighteen years of age or older but 41 younger than sixty-five years of age; 42 (B) the adult's location is unknown; and 43 (C) the adult has been missing for less than seventy-two hours; 44 (iii) confirms that a preliminary investigation has taken place with 45 respect to the disappearance and that, as a result of that investi- 46 gation, the agency believes that the adult is missing under circum- 47 stances described by subparagraph (i) of paragraph (b) of subdivision 48 three of this section; and 49 (iv) believes sufficient information is available to disseminate to 50 the public that could assist in locating the adult, a person suspected 51 of abducting or kidnapping the adult, or a vehicle suspected of being 52 used by the adult or in any abduction or kidnapping of the adult. 53 (b) The division may modify the criteria described by paragraph (a) of 54 this subdivision as necessary for the proper implementation of the alert 55 system.
