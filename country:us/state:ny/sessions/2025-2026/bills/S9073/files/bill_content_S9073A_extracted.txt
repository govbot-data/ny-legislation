AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law, in relation to
prohibiting the sale of certain products that contain regulated
perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The environmental conservation law is amended by adding a
2 new section 37-0123 to read as follows:
3 § 37-0123. Prohibition against the sale of perfluoroalkyl and polyfluo-
4 roalkyl substances in consumer and household products.
5 1. For purposes of this section, the following terms have the follow-
6 ing definitions:
7 (a) "Covered product" means textile articles, rugs, fabric treatments,
8 cookware, ski waxes, architectural paints, cleaning products, dental
9 floss, or a component thereof.
10 (b) "Regulated perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances" or
11 "regulated PFAS" means PFAS that are any of the following:
12 (i) an intentionally added chemical as defined in subdivision four of
13 section 37-0121 of this title; or
14 (ii) present in a product or product component at or above a level
15 that the department shall establish by regulation, as measured in total
16 organic fluorine, that is the lowest level that can feasibly be
17 achieved; provided, however, that the department shall review such level
18 at least every five years to determine whether it should be lowered.
19 (c) "Architectural paint" means interior and exterior architectural
20 coatings; provided, however, that "architectural paint" shall not
21 include industrial, original equipment or specialty coatings.
22 (d) "Cleaning product" means a finished product that is an air care
23 product, automotive product, general cleaning product, or a polish or
24 floor maintenance product used primarily for janitorial, domestic, or

1 institutional cleaning purposes. "Cleaning product" shall not mean any
2 of the following:
3 (i) foods, drugs, cosmetics and personal care products as defined in
4 section 37-0117 of this title; or
5 (ii) industrial products specifically manufactured for, and exclusive-

7 heavy industry manufacturing; industrial water treatment; industrial
8 textile maintenance and processing other than industrial laundering;
9 food and beverage processing and packaging; or other industrial manufac-

11 (e) "Air care product" means a chemically formulated consumer product
12 labeled to indicate that the purpose of the product is to enhance or
13 condition the indoor environment by eliminating unpleasant odors or
14 freshening the air.
15 (f) "Automotive product" means a chemically formulated consumer prod-
16 uct labeled to indicate that the purpose of the product is to maintain
17 the appearance of a motor vehicle, as defined in section one hundred
18 twenty-five of the vehicle and traffic law, including products for wash-
19 ing, waxing, polishing, cleaning, or treating the exterior or interior
20 surfaces of motor vehicles. "Automotive product" does not include auto-
21 motive paint or paint repair products.
22 (g) "General cleaning product" means a soap, detergent, or other chem-
23 ically formulated consumer product labeled to indicate that the purpose
24 of the product is to clean, disinfect, or otherwise care for fabric,
25 dishes, or other wares; surfaces including, but not limited to, floors,
26 furniture, countertops, showers, and baths; or other hard surfaces, such
27 as stovetops, microwaves, and other appliances.
28 (h) "Polish or floor maintenance product" means a chemically formu-
29 lated consumer product, such as polish, wax, a stripper, or a restorer,
30 labeled to indicate that the purpose of the product is to polish,
31 protect, buff, condition, temporarily seal, strip, or maintain furni-
32 ture, floors, metal, leather, or other surfaces.
33 (i) "Cookware" refers to durable items used in homes, restaurants,
34 institutional settings, and commercial kitchens for preparing, dispens-
35 ing, or storing food, foodstuffs, or beverages. These items are designed
36 for indoor or outdoor use and include pots, pans, skillets, grills,
37 baking sheets, molds, trays, bowls, camping gear, and cooking utensils.
38 (j) "Fabric treatment" means a substance applied to a fabric for
39 stain, grease, or water resistance.
40 (k) "Manufacturer" means a person, firm, association, partnership or
41 corporation:
42 (i) that produces or manufactures a covered product or whose brand
43 name is affixed to the covered product; or
44 (ii) in the case of a covered product imported into the United States,
45 "manufacturer" means the importer or first domestic distributor of the
46 product if the person that manufactured or assembled the covered product
47 or whose brand name is affixed to the product does not have a presence
48 in the United States.
49 (l) "Rugs" means any consumer products made from natural or synthetic
50 fabric intended to be used as a floor covering, other than carpets, and
51 includes handmade rugs, area rugs, or mats.
52 (m) "Ski wax" means a lubricant applied to the bottom of snow runners,
53 including skis and snowboards, to improve their grip and glide proper-
54 ties.
55 (n) "Textile" means any item made in whole or in part from a natural,
56 human-made, or synthetic fiber, yarn, or fabric. Textile includes, but

1 is not limited to, the following: leather, cotton, silk, jute, hemp,
2 wool, viscose, nylon, or polyester. "Textile" does not include single-
3 use paper hygiene products, including, but not limited to, toilet paper,
4 paper towels or tissues, or single-use absorbent hygiene products. For
5 the purposes of this subdivision, "single-use" means conventionally
6 disposed of after a single use or not sufficiently durable or washable
7 to be, or not intended to be, reusable or refillable.
8 (o) "Textile articles" means textile goods customarily used in house-
9 holds and businesses including, but not limited to, handbags, luggage,
10 backpacks, footwear, costumes and accessories, draperies, shower
11 curtains, furnishings, upholstery, beddings, towels, napkins, and table-
12 cloths. For the purposes of this section, "textile articles" does not
13 include (i) rugs; (ii) personal protective equipment, including for
14 medical purposes; or (iii) items covered by section 37-0121 of this
15 article.
16 (p) "Dental floss" means a thread, cord, string or similar material
17 that is used for cleaning between teeth. Dental floss includes floss
18 picks and other flossing tools.
19 2. (a) No person shall sell or offer for sale in the state any covered
20 product that contains regulated perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl
21 substances pursuant to subparagraph (i) of paragraph (b) of subdivision
22 one of this section.
23 (b) Commencing on January first, two thousand twenty-eight, no person
24 shall sell or offer for sale in the state any covered product that
25 contains regulated perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances pursu-
26 ant to subparagraph (ii) of paragraph (b) of subdivision one of this
27 section.
28 (c) The prohibition, sale or offer of sale under this section does not
29 apply to the sale or resale of used products.
30 3. (a) A manufacturer of a covered product sold into the state shall
31 provide persons that offer the product for sale in the state with a
32 certificate of compliance. The certificate of compliance shall provide
33 assurance, at a minimum, that the product does not contain any regulated
34 PFAS. Any certificate of compliance provided under this paragraph shall
35 be signed by an authorized official of the manufacturer.
36 (b) In addition to any other applicable penalties, it shall be a
37 violation of this section to provide a certificate of compliance under
38 paragraph (a) of this subdivision when a product is knowingly in
39 violation of the requirements of this section.
40 4. If the department has reason to believe that a covered product
41 contains regulated perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances and is
42 being sold or offered for sale in violation of this section, the
43 manufacturer of the covered product within thirty days shall:
44 (a) provide the department with independent, third-party laboratory
45 test results demonstrating that the covered product does not contain
46 regulated PFAS; or
47 (b) notify persons who sell that covered product in this state that
48 the sale of that covered product is prohibited in this state and provide
49 the department with a list of the names and addresses of those notified.
50 5. A retailer of a product, who is not also the manufacturer of the
51 product, shall not be held in violation of this section if it can show
52 that such retailer relied in good faith on the certificate of compliance
53 provided for in paragraph (a) of subdivision three of this section.
