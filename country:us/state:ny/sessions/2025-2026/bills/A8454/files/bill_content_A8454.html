A08454 Summary:

BILL NOA08454A
 
SAME ASSAME AS S08857
 
SPONSORBichotte Hermelyn
 
COSPNSR
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Add §230-a, RP L
 
Requires a notice informing tenants of their rights to be provided to tenants at the time they execute a residential lease and to be posted in certain buildings.

        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  real  property law, in relation to creating the
          tenants' bill of rights

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  This act shall be known and may be cited as the "tenants'
     2  bill of rights".
     3    § 2. The real property law is amended by adding a new section 230-a to
     4  read as follows:
     5    § 230-a. Tenants' bill of rights. 1. Any tenant who occupies a  rental
     6  property as their primary residence shall have the following rights:
     7    (a)  the  right  to  premises  that  are  fit  for human habitation as
     8  provided by the warranty of habitability provided by section two hundred
     9  thirty-five-b of this article;
    10    (b) the right to form, join  or  participate  in  tenants'  groups  as
    11  provided in section two hundred thirty of this article;
    12    (c)  the  right  to  be  free from retaliation by landlord against the
    13  tenant as provided in section two hundred twenty-three-b of  this  arti-
    14  cle;
    15    (d)  in  buildings  with  four or more residential units, the right to
    16  sublease or assign the tenant's rights to the unit  in  accordance  with
    17  the provisions of section two hundred twenty-six-b of this article;
    18    (e)  the  right to notice of rent increase or non-renewal of the resi-
    19  dential tenancy as provided in section two hundred twenty-six-c of  this
    20  article;
    21    (f) the right to terminate the lease in accordance with the provisions
    22  of section two hundred twenty-seven-a, two hundred twenty-seven-b or two
    23  hundred twenty-seven-c of this article;

     1    (g) the right to offset payments made to a utility company against the
     2  rent  due as provided in section two hundred thirty-five-a of this arti-
     3  cle, applicable to any residential unit;
     4    (h) the right to receive a receipt for the payment of rent as provided
     5  in section two hundred thirty-five-e of this article;
     6    (i)  the  right  not  to  utilize an electronic billing and/or payment
     7  system as the only method for the payment of rent as provided in section
     8  two hundred thirty-five-g of this article; and
     9    (j) the rights pertaining to security  depositor  advance  payment  of
    10  rent  as  provided  in  sections 7-103, 7-105, 7-107 and/or 7-108 of the
    11  general obligations law.
    12    2. Subject to the provisions of subdivision  three  of  this  section,
    13  each landlord renting a residential unit in this state shall:
    14    (a) attach a copy of the tenants' bill of rights to each written lease
    15  and provide the same to the tenant with the executed lease;
    16    (b)  provide  a  copy  of  the  tenants' bill of rights to each tenant
    17  entering into an oral lease at the time the tenant agrees to the  lease;
    18  and
    19    (c) for units containing more than six rental units, affix a notice to
    20  tenants of the rights described in this section to the front entrance to
    21  the  rental  premises or elsewhere in the building in a location readily
    22  available to the tenants.
    23    3. (a) Except as provided in paragraph (b) of  this  subdivision,  the
    24  provisions of this section apply to written and/or oral leases for resi-
    25  dential  property,  including  leases  for subsidized, rent-regulated or
    26  rent stabilized units.
    27    (b) The following properties are exempt from the  provisions  of  this
    28  section:
    29    (i) units in owner-occupied buildings with six or fewer units;
    30    (ii) units in hotels, motels, rooming houses, unless rent is paid on a
    31  monthly basis and such unit is occupied for more than thirty-two days;
    32    (iii) school dormitory rooms;
    33    (iv) shelters;
    34    (v) employees' quarters;
    35    (vi) non-residential rental properties; and
    36    (vii)  owner-occupied  cooperative  apartments where the tenant is the
    37  unit owner or shareholder of a cooperative housing  corporation,  or  is
    38  the owner-occupied condominium apartment.
    39    §  3.  This  act shall take effect on the ninetieth day after it shall
    40  have become a law.
