This act shall be known and may be cited as the "tenants' 2 bill of rights". 3 § 2. The real property law is amended by adding a new section 230-a to 4 read as follows: 5 § 230-a. Tenants' bill of rights. 1. Any tenant who occupies a rental 6 property as their primary residence shall have the following rights: 7 (a) the right to premises that are fit for human habitation as 8 provided by the warranty of habitability provided by section two hundred 9 thirty-five-b of this article; 10 (b) the right to form, join or participate in tenants' groups as 11 provided in section two hundred thirty of this article; 12 (c) the right to be free from retaliation by landlord against the 13 tenant as provided in section two hundred twenty-three-b of this arti- 14 cle; 15 (d) in buildings with four or more residential units, the right to 16 sublease or assign the tenant's rights to the unit in accordance with 17 the provisions of section two hundred twenty-six-b of this article; 18 (e) the right to notice of rent increase or non-renewal of the resi- 19 dential tenancy as provided in section two hundred twenty-six-c of this 20 article; 21 (f) the right to terminate the lease in accordance with the provisions 22 of section two hundred twenty-seven-a, two hundred twenty-seven-b or two 23 hundred twenty-seven-c of this article; EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. Subject to the provisions of subdivision three of this section, 13 each landlord renting a residential unit in this state shall: 14 (a) attach a copy of the tenants' bill of rights to each written lease 15 and provide the same to the tenant with the executed lease; 16 (b) provide a copy of the tenants' bill of rights to each tenant 17 entering into an oral lease at the time the tenant agrees to the lease; 18 and 19 (c) for units containing more than six rental units, affix a notice to 20 tenants of the rights described in this section to the front entrance to 21 the rental premises or elsewhere in the building in a location readily 22 available to the tenants. 23 3. (a) Except as provided in paragraph (b) of this subdivision, the 24 provisions of this section apply to written and/or oral leases for resi- 25 dential property, including leases for subsidized, rent-regulated or 26 rent stabilized units. 27 (b) The following properties are exempt from the provisions of this 28 section: 29 (i) units in owner-occupied buildings with six or fewer units; 30 (ii) units in hotels, motels, rooming houses, unless rent is paid on a 31 monthly basis and such unit is occupied for more than thirty-two days; 32 (iii) school dormitory rooms; 33 (iv) shelters; 34 (v) employees' quarters; 35 (vi) non-residential rental properties; and 36 (vii) owner-occupied cooperative apartments where the tenant is the 37 unit owner or shareholder of a cooperative housing corporation, or is 38 the owner-occupied condominium apartment. 39 § 3.
