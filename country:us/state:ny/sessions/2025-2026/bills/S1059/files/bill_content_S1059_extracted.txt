SERRANO, ADDABBO, HOYLMAN-SIGAL -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Environmental Conservation -- committee discharged, bill amended, ordered reprinted as amended and recommitted to said committee AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law, in relation to prohibiting the aerial and ground application of malathion and certain pyrethroid-based insecticides The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Article 37 of the environmental conservation law is amended 2 by adding a new title 12 to read as follows: 3 TITLE 12 4 PROHIBITIONS ON THE USE OF MALATHION AND PYRETHROID-BASED INSECTICIDES 5 Section 37-1201. Definitions. 6 37-1203. Prohibition on the aerial and ground application of 7 malathion and certain pyrethroid-based insecticides. 8 37-1205. Controlling standard. 9 37-1207. Emergency exception. 10 37-1209. Regulations. 11 § 37-1201. Definitions. 12 For the purposes of this section: 13 1. "Day care center" means all child day care centers and head start 14 day care centers, as defined in section three hundred ninety of the 15 social services law; 16 2. "Park" means any state, county, or municipal park; and 17 3. "School" means any public school district or private or parochial 18 school or board of cooperative educational services. 19 § 37-1203. Prohibition on the aerial and ground application of malathion 20 and certain pyrethroid-based insecticides. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. LBD01636-02-5 S. 1059--A 2 1 No person, firm, partnership, association, limited liability company, 2 corporation, municipality or government entity shall allow or implement 3 the aerial or ground application of malathion or pyrethroid-based insec- 4 ticides: 5 1. within a park; or 6 2. within one hundred feet of any school, day care center or park. 7 § 37-1205. Controlling standard. 8 If a more restrictive standard is set forth in the information 9 contained on the label of the pesticide, or by other regulations, 10 including county or local regulations, the more restrictive standard 11 shall apply. 12 § 37-1207. Emergency exception. 13 1. Notwithstanding the prohibitions of subdivision two of this 14 section, an application of the least toxic pyrethroid pesticide avail- 15 able, as determined by the department, may be made in the case of an 16 emergency. Determination of an emergency shall be made by the county 17 health department, or for a county not having a health department such 18 authority as the county legislature shall designate, the commissioner of 19 health or their designee, the commissioner of environmental conservation 20 or their designee, or, in the case of a public school, the school board. 21 2. Any such application shall be: 22 (a) conducted at a time that minimizes exposure to children; 23 (b) preceded by adequate public notice; and 24 (c) accompanied by appropriate signage. 25 § 37-1209. Regulations. 26 The department is authorized to promulgate such rules and regulations 27 as it shall deem necessary to implement the provisions of this title. 28 § 2.
