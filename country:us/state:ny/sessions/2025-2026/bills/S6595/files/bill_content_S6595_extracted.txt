"Eligible carbon-to-value application" shall mean the application 30 of carbon-to-value equipment at facilities for the purposes of mitigat- 31 ing carbon dioxide emissions that are: (a) generated as a result of the 32 operation of that facility; and/or (b) the manufacture of materials that 33 are prepared or produced at that facility, by technologies that remove, 34 capture and/or beneficially use carbon dioxide, resulting in a net 35 reduction of carbon dioxide emissions. 36 10. "Eligible building" shall mean class four real property located 37 within a city having a population of one million or more persons. Desig- 38 nated agencies shall be empowered to exclude property tax abatement 39 eligibility of certain building types on the basis of carbon dioxide 40 emissions reduction and/or environmental justice considerations if the 41 latter are determined to contradict the intent of existing local laws 42 that have been established to reduce the carbon dioxide emissions of 43 such buildings. No building shall be eligible for the property tax 44 abatement, under this provision, if the designated agencies empowered to 45 administer such abatement, deem that such building has not exhausted 46 other viable methods to reduce the building's carbon emissions in align- 47 ment with rules, objectives and programs established pursuant to exist- 48 ing local laws. No building shall be eligible for more than one tax 49 abatement pursuant to this title. 50 11. "Facility-integrated carbon-to-value equipment" refers to technol- 8 ogies placed in service at buildings within a city of one million or 9 more persons that remove carbon dioxide from the ambient air, capture 10 carbon dioxide from emissions point sources located at the property, 11 and/or utilize carbon dioxide in the production of goods and materials. 12 Qualified carbon-to-value equipment must perform functions that result 13 either in verifiable carbon dioxide removal and storage or constitute a 14 verifiable carbon dioxide beneficial use that results in reduced or 15 avoided carbon dioxide emissions. 16 § 498-a. Tax abatement terms and amounts. 1. If the facility-integrat- 17 ed carbon-to-value equipment is placed in service on or after January 18 first, two thousand twenty-five, and not after December thirty-first, 19 two thousand thirty, for each year of the compliance period such tax 20 abatement shall be the lesser of: (a) five percent of eligible facili- 21 ty-integrated carbon-to-value equipment expenditures; (b) the amount of 22 taxes payable in such tax year; or (c) one hundred thousand dollars. 23 2. For facility-integrated carbon-to-value equipment that captures 24 carbon dioxide from boiler systems that combust fossil-based hydrocarbon 25 fuels eligibility for the property tax abatement shall be restricted to 26 properties that meet the following conditions: 27 (a) Boiler systems that are located at the property were placed in 28 service between January first, two thousand nineteen and April twenty- 29 second, two thousand twenty-four. 30 (b) The carbon dioxide captured at the property by the proposed 31 carbon-to-value application shall: 32 (i) be utilized subsequent to capture within the physical jurisdiction 33 of the city with a population of one million or more people; and 34 (ii) result in the storage of carbon dioxide in materials for periods 35 of no less than one hundred years in duration. 36 (c) The carbon-to-value application at the property shall demonstrate 37 net carbon dioxide reductions as verified by a life cycle assessment in 38 compliance with International Standard ISO 14040. 39 (d) The equipment shall not be located at buildings located within 40 designated environmental justice areas as defined by a city of one 41 million or more persons pursuant to local law. 42 § 498-b.
