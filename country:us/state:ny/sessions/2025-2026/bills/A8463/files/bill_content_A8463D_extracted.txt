AN ACT in relation to authorizing the commissioner of education to
conduct a survey regarding instruction on Asian American, Native Hawa-
iians and Pacific Islander history within the state; to amend the
education law, in relation to establishing an Asian American, Native
Hawaiians and Pacific Islander history advisory committee; and provid-
ing for the repeal of such provisions upon the expiration thereof
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. This act shall be known as the "New York AANHPI Education
2 Equity Act".
3 § 2. (a) Contingent upon appropriation for such, the commissioner of
4 education is authorized and directed to conduct a survey regarding
5 instruction on Asian American, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander
6 (AANHPI) history within the state. Such survey shall focus on identify-
7 ing the extent to which school districts provide instruction on Asian
8 American, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander (AANHPI) history. Such
9 survey shall include as appropriate, questions on how the district is
10 providing such instruction, including, but not limited to, the follow-
11 ing:
12 (1) how the department's learning standards inform the school
13 district's teaching of AANHPI history;

19 § 3. The education law is amended by adding a new section 805 to read
20 as follows:
21 § 805. Asian American, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander history
22 advisory committee; appointment; powers and duties. 1. Contingent upon
23 appropriation for such, the commissioner shall establish an Asian Ameri-
24 can, Native Hawaiians and Pacific Islander (AANHPI) history advisory
25 committee.
26 2. (a) The committee shall be comprised of four members, to be
27 appointed as follows:
28 (i) one member appointed by the governor;
29 (ii) one member appointed by the temporary president of the senate;
30 (iii) one member appointed by the speaker of the assembly; and
31 (iv) one member appointed by the commissioner.
32 (b) All members appointed to the committee shall be experts in Asian
33 American, Native Hawaiians and/or Pacific Islander studies and/or have a
34 background in educational instruction and/or educational administration.
35 (c) All members of the committee shall be appointed within ninety days
36 of the effective date of this section.
37 3. Vacancies in the membership of the committee shall be filled in the
38 same manner provided for original appointments.
39 4. The committee shall meet no less than four times per year.
40 5. The committee shall provide recommendations on the following:
41 (a) high-quality K-12 curricular and instructional resources on AANHPI
42 history; and
43 (b) strategies for strengthening and deepening public K-12 education
44 programs in the areas of AANHPI history, including analysis of the
45 survey referenced in section two of the chapter of the laws of two thou-
46 sand twenty-five that added this section.
47 6. The committee shall submit their recommendations to the commission-
48 er no later than two years after the effective date of this section and
49 be disbanded thereafter.
50 7. Committee members shall receive no compensation for their services,
51 but shall be allowed their actual and necessary expenses incurred in the
52 performance of their duties subject to an appropriation therefor.
53 8. To effectuate the purposes of this section, the committee may
54 request from any department, board, bureau, commission or other agency
55 of the state, and the same are authorized to provide, such cooperation
