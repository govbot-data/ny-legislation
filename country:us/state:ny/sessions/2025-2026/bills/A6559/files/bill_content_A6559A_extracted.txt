AN ACT to amend the veterans' services law, in relation to the estab-
lishing a veterans' bill of rights
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The veterans' services law is amended by adding a new
2 section 29-e to read as follows:
3 § 29-e. Veterans' bill of rights. 1. The department shall establish,
4 print, publish and distribute a veterans' bill of rights. Such bill of
5 rights shall include the following or substantially similar text:
6 "VETERANS' BILL OF RIGHTS
7 You as a veteran have a right:
8 1. To apply for veterans' benefits;
9 2. To receive assistance from your local state veterans' service agen-
10 cy and all offices of the New York state department of veterans'
11 services;
12 3. To receive an explanation of the services and benefits available
13 under federal and New York state laws, rules and regulations;
14 4. To receive written notice and an explanation of the approval or
15 denial of your application for benefits available under New York state
16 law;
17 5. To be treated with dignity and respect;
18 6. To appeal any adverse determination or denial of your application
19 for services or benefits available under New York state law;
20 7. To expect confidentiality;
21 8. To receive fair and equal treatment without regard to sex, race,
22 religion, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or national origin; and
23 9. To be informed of assistance provided to veterans in the state of
24 New York including but not limited to: legal services; educational
