and WHEREAS, Robert Violante of Somers, New York, died on Wednesday, May 14, 2025, at the age of 45; and WHEREAS, Born on December 21, 1979, in Yonkers, New York, to loving parents Frank and Martha Violante, Robert Violante was the middle child of three sons; he shared a close bond with his beloved brothers, Chris and Brian; and WHEREAS, A gifted athlete from a young age, Robert Violante shined at Somers High School as a standout in both football and basketball; his talents and fierce determination led him to Iona University, where he played collegiate football; and WHEREAS, Robert Violante continued his prolific athletic career at the University of Delaware; he then earned a degree in Special Education at Mercy College and later obtained a master's degree in Educational Administration; and WHEREAS, Robert Violante distinguished himself in his profession and by his sincere dedication and substantial contribution to the welfare of his community; and WHEREAS, Dedicating his life to education and leadership, Robert Violante began his professional journey as a special education teacher and rose to the role of Dean of Students at Bergenfield High School in New Jersey; and WHEREAS, Taking his passion and talent for sports, Robert Violante diligently coached both varsity football and track where he inspired countless students on and off the field; and WHEREAS, Robert Violante's commitment to excellence, and his spirit of humanity, carried over into all fields of enterprise, including charitable and civic endeavors; and WHEREAS, Known for his warmth, leadership, and unwavering commitment to those around him, Robert Violante is remembered as a beloved son, brother, uncle, and coach; his presence touched the lives of many and leaves behind a legacy of kindness, strength, and dedication; and WHEREAS, Robert Violante is survived by his parents, Frank and Martha; his brothers, Chris (Hoda) and Brian (Laura); his cherished nephews, Andrew, Cameron, Dylan, and Jordan; his precious niece, Natalie; and his longtime loving girlfriend, Dana Brescia; and WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of compassion, Robert Violante leaves behind a legacy which will long endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all he served and befriended; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of Robert Violante, distinguished citizen and devoted member of his community; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Robert Violante.
