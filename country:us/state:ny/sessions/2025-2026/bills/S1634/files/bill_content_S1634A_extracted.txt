AN ACT to amend the insurance law and the social services law, in
relation to primary care investment
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The insurance law is amended by adding a new section 3217-k
2 to read as follows:
3 § 3217-k. Primary care spending. (a) Definitions. As used in this
4 section, the following terms shall have the following meanings:
5 (1) "Overall healthcare spending" means the total cost of care for the
6 patient population of a payor or provider entity for a given calendar
7 year, where cost is calculated for such year as the sum of (A) all
8 claims-based spending paid to providers by public and private payors and
9 (B) all non-claim payments for such year, including, but not limited to,
10 incentive payments and care coordination payments.
11 (2) "Plan or payor" means every insurance entity providing managed
12 care products, individual comprehensive accident and health insurance or
13 group or blanket comprehensive accident and health insurance, as defined
14 in this chapter, corporation organized under article forty-three of this
15 chapter providing comprehensive health insurance, entity licensed under
16 article forty-four of this chapter providing comprehensive health insur-
17 ance, every other plan over which the department has jurisdiction, and
18 every third-party payor providing health coverage.
19 (3) "Primary care" means integrated, accessible healthcare, provided
20 by clinicians accountable for addressing most of a patient's healthcare

7 (4) "Primary care services" means services provided in an outpatient,
8 non-emergency setting by or under the supervision of a physician, nurse
9 practitioner, physician assistant, or midwife, who is practicing general
10 primary care in the following fields, including as evidenced by billing
11 and reporting codes: family practice; general pediatrics; primary care
12 internal medicine; primary care obstetrics; or primary care gynecology.
13 Behavioral or mental health and substance use disorder services are
14 included in primary care services when integrated into a primary care
15 setting, including when provided by a behavioral healthcare psychia-
16 trist, social worker or psychologist. Primary care services shall not
17 include inpatient services, emergency department services, ambulatory
18 surgical center services, or services provided in an urgent care setting
19 that are billed with non-primary care billing and reporting codes.
20 (5) "Primary care spending" means any expenditure of funds made by
21 third party payors, public entities, or the state, for the purpose of
22 paying for primary care services directly or paying to improve the
23 delivery of primary care. Primary care spending includes all payment
24 methods, such as fee-for-service, capitation, incentives, value-based
25 payments or other methodologies, and all non-claim payments including
26 but not limited to incentive payments and care coordination payments.
27 For payees that own and/or operate facilities, entities, or other
28 providers, such as health systems or hospital systems, that provide
29 other medical services in addition to primary care, only those funds
30 that are separately documented as funds designated for primary care
31 services shall be considered primary care spending. Any spending shall
32 be adjusted appropriately to exclude any portion of the expenditure that
33 is reasonably attributed to inpatient services or other non-primary care
34 services.
35 (b) Reporting. (1) Beginning on April first, two thousand twenty-sev-
36 en, each plan or payor as defined in this section shall annually report
37 to the department the percentage of the plan or payor's overall annual
38 healthcare spending that constituted primary care spending.
39 (2) Nothing herein shall require any plan or payor to report or
40 publicly disclose any specific rates of reimbursement for any specific
41 primary care services.
42 (3) No plan or payor shall require any healthcare provider to provide
43 additional data or information in order to fulfill this reporting
44 requirement.
45 (c) Regulation and publication. (1) The commissioner of health and the
46 superintendent shall each promulgate consistent regulations to carry out
47 the provisions of this section, including but not limited to setting
48 deadlines for the reporting required in this section, and adopting
49 further specific definitions of the primary care services for which
50 costs must be reported under this section, including specific billing
51 and reporting codes.
52 (2) The department of health and the department shall together provide
53 an annual report to the legislature with a summary of the primary care
54 spending data required in this section, and shall also make the report
55 publicly available on both agencies' websites, no later than three
56 months after the data has been collected. The first annual report shall

5 (d) Primary care spending. (1) Beginning on April first, two thousand

12 and on April first of every subsequent year after such plan has been
13 submitted, and until such time as the plan or payor's reported primary
14 care spending is equal to or more than twelve and one-half percent of
15 that plan or payor's overall healthcare spending, the plan or payor's
16 annual reporting shall include information regarding steps that have
17 been taken to increase its proportion of primary care spending.
18 (2) The commissioner of health and the superintendent may jointly
19 issue guidelines or promulgate regulations regarding the areas on which
20 primary care spending could be increased, including but not limited to:
21 (A) reimbursement;

23 (C) upgrading technology, including electronic health record systems
24 and telehealth capabilities;
25 (D) incentive payments, including but not limited to per-member-per-
26 month, value-based-payment arrangements, shared savings, quality-based
27 payments, risk-based payments; and
28 (E) transitioning to value-based-payment arrangements.
29 (e) Limits on premium increases. Plans or payors shall adopt strate-
30 gies that improve value and quality of care and shift current spending
31 without increasing total medical expenditures. Spending shifts resulting
32 from compliance with this section shall not result in higher premiums or
33 cost-sharing requirements for insured individuals.
