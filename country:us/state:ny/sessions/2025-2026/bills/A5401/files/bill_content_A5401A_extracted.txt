AN ACT to amend the private housing finance law, in relation to requir-
ing housing owned by limited-profit housing companies to be inspected
on an annual basis by a housing management representative
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Section 32-a of the private housing finance law is amended
2 by adding a new subdivision 15 to read as follows:
3 15. (a) Assign an employee of the commissioner or supervising agency,
4 as the case may be, as a housing management representative to each
5 company. Such housing management representative shall perform a physical
6 inspection of each building owned or maintained by a housing company
7 pursuant to this article. The physical inspection shall be conducted
8 according to the national standards for the physical inspection of real
9 estate as established by the United States department of housing and
10 urban development. The housing management representative shall notify
11 the housing company, including managing agents, duly constituted
12 tenants' association, or duly constituted cooperators' advisory council,
13 of any deficiencies found as a result of such inspection within five
14 business days of such inspection; provided, however, that if any life-
15 threatening deficiency is found as a result of such inspection then such
16 notification shall be provided to the housing company, including manag-
17 ing agents, duly constituted tenants' association, or duly constituted
18 cooperators' advisory council, immediately. The commissioner or super-
19 vising agency shall require that the housing company certify correction
20 of such violation within a reasonable timeframe, as determined by the
21 commissioner or the supervising agency in accordance with the national

17 (c) In addition to a physical inspection of all buildings in the
18 development, the grounds, common facilities and central systems for
19 general construction, an inspection may include, but is not limited to:
20 a review of all records the commissioner or supervising agency deems
21 relevant; interviews of shareholders, board members, housing company
22 employees and managing agent employees and any other information which
23 the commissioner or supervising agency deems relevant.
24 (d) The commissioner or supervising agency shall review any local
25 health, safety or building code violations reports or notices retained
26 by the housing company and shall determine whether the buildings and
27 units satisfy the uniform physical condition standards established by
28 the United States department of housing and urban development (24 CFR
29 5.703). The United States department of housing and urban development
30 physical condition standards shall not supersede or preempt local

32 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
