        AN  ACT to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to authorizing
          persons who transport severely disabled persons to apply for  disabled
          person plates
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Section 404-a of the vehicle and traffic law, as  added  by
     2  chapter 181 of the laws of 1980, subdivision 1 as amended by chapter 596

     4  of  1991,  subdivision  3  amended  by  chapter 445 of the laws of 2024,
     5  subdivision 4 and paragraph (b) of subdivision 5 as amended  by  chapter
     6  376  of the laws of 2015, subdivision 4-a as added by chapter 686 of the
     7  laws of 1994, subdivision 4-b as added by chapter 277  of  the  laws  of
     8  2012,  subdivisions  5 and 6 as renumbered by chapter 601 of the laws of

    12    § 404-a. Registration of motor vehicles  [of]  for  severely  disabled
    13  persons.  1.   Legislative intent. It is the sense of the legislature to
    14  facilitate the usual and special uses of the highways  and  other  motor
    15  vehicle  facilities  accorded  to  severely  disabled  residents of this
    16  state. The commissioner is hereby empowered to issue to [such]  eligible
    17  persons,  upon  their  application, special motor vehicle registrations,
    18  [one] and a set of vehicle identification plates for each  motor  [vehi-

    20  the transportation of [such] a severely disabled person [and one set  of
    21  vehicle  identification  plates  for  each  motor  vehicle owned by such
    22  persons and used by a severely disabled member of the owner's family].
    23    1-a. Definitions. For the purposes  of  this  section,  the  following

     1    (a) "Severely disabled person" shall mean any person having any one or
     2  more  of the following impairments, disabilities or conditions which are
     3  permanent in nature:
     4    (i) Has limited or no use of one or both lower limbs;
     5    (ii) Has a neuro-muscular dysfunction which severely limits mobility;
     6    (iii)  Has a physical or mental impairment or condition which is other
     7  than those specified above, but is of such nature as to  impose  unusual
     8  hardship  in  utilization  of  public transportation facilities and such
     9  condition is certified by a  physician,  physician  assistant  or  nurse
    10  practitioner, to the extent authorized by law and consistent with subdi-
    11  vision  three  of  section  sixty-nine hundred two of the education law,
    12  duly licensed to practice in this state,  or,  pursuant  to  subdivision
    13  four-a  of this section, a podiatrist duly licensed to practice podiatry
    14  in this state or, pursuant to subdivision four-b  of  this  section,  an
    15  optometrist  duly  licensed  to  practice  optometry  in  this state, as
    16  constituting an equal degree of disability  (specifying  the  particular
    17  condition)  so  as  to  prevent  such person from getting around without
    18  great difficulty in accordance with subdivision two of this section;
    19    (iv) A blind person; or
    20    (v) A deaf person.
    21    (b) "Eligible person" shall mean a severely  disabled  person  or  any
    22  parent,  spouse, guardian, caregiver, or any other family member respon-
    23  sible for the transportation of a severely disabled person  or  severely
    24  disabled veteran by motor vehicle.
    25    2.  Registration  of  vehicles  owned  by  [severely disabled persons]
    26  eligible persons. The commissioner shall assign to such motor  vehicles,
    27  including  any  van  or pick-up truck used for transporting persons with
    28  disabilities which is not used for commercial purposes,  owned  by  such
    29  eligible  persons,  a  distinctive  number and issue and deliver in such
    30  manner as the commissioner may select to the owner  a  certification  of
    31  registration,  in  such form as the commissioner shall prescribe and two
    32  number plates, called  disabled  person  plates.  Said  disabled  person
    33  plates  shall conform to the requirements of section four hundred one of
    34  this [chapter] article, but shall bear distinctive marks to  distinguish
    35  them  from number plates to be issued to other persons, qualifying under
    36  this  chapter.  The  commissioner,  [in  his]  at  such   commissioner's
    37  discretion,  may  issue,  for any registration year, only one plate as a
    38  set for a motor vehicle, in which event a set of disabled person  plates
    39  for  a motor vehicle shall consist of one plate. Where [a severely disa-
    40  bled] an eligible person owns more than one vehicle and such vehicle  or
    41  vehicles  is  or are used by a severely disabled [members of the owner's
    42  family who reside with the owner] person, the commissioner  shall  issue
    43  one  set  of  plates  for  each additional vehicle used by such severely
    44  disabled person, provided that such user qualifies as a  severely  disa-
    45  bled person in the manner required by this section.
    46    3.  Registration  of vehicles owned by severely disabled veterans. The
    47  commissioner shall assign to such motor vehicles, including any vans  or
    48  pick-up  trucks used for transporting handicapped veterans which are not
    49  used for commercial purposes and which are owned by such veterans  or  a
    50  not-for-profit  corporation  serving such veterans, a distinctive number
    51  and issue and deliver in such manner as the commissioner  may  prescribe
    52  to  the  owner  a  certification  of  registration,  in such form as the
    53  commissioner shall prescribe and  two  number  plates,  called  disabled
    54  veteran  plates.  Said severely disabled veteran plates shall conform to
    55  the requirements of section four hundred one of this article, but  shall
    56  bear  distinctive  marks  to  distinguish  them from number plates to be
