BURKE -- read once and referred to the Committee on Governmental Operations AN ACT to amend the state finance law, in relation to establishing the upstate cities preparedness and resiliency fund; making an appropri- ation therefor; and providing for the repeal of such provisions upon expiration thereof The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The state finance law is amended by adding a new section 2 99-uu to read as follows: 3 § 99-uu. Upstate cities preparedness and resiliency fund. 1. For the 4 purposes of this section, the following terms shall have the following 5 meanings: 6 (a) "major upstate cities" shall mean cities with a population of more 7 than one hundred twenty-five thousand and less than one million. 8 (b) "mid-sized upstate cities" shall mean cities with a population of 9 more than twenty-five thousand and less than or equal to one hundred 10 twenty-five thousand. 11 (c) "small upstate cities" shall mean cities with a population of 12 twenty-five thousand or less. 13 (d) "population" shall mean the population of a city according to the 14 United States census bureau's two thousand twenty-three American commu- 15 nity survey one-year estimates. 16 2. There is hereby established in the joint custody of the commission- 17 er of taxation and finance and the state comptroller a special fund to 18 be known as the "upstate cities preparedness and resiliency fund". 19 3. Such fund shall consist of all moneys appropriated thereto and all 20 other moneys credited or transferred thereto from any other fund or 21 source pursuant to law. Nothing contained in this section shall prevent 22 the state from receiving grants, gifts or bequests for the purposes of 23 the fund as defined in this section and depositing them into the fund 24 according to law. 25 4. The fund shall be governed and administered by the comptroller. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. LBD14013-02-6 A. 9612 2 1 5. The moneys in such fund shall be awarded in the form of grants by 2 the comptroller to support cities in counties other than Rockland, West- 3 chester, Bronx, New York, Richmond, Kings, Queens, Nassau, and Suffolk 4 that face recurring fiscal stress and operational challenges caused by 5 extreme winter weather, aging infrastructure, high poverty rates, and a 6 large share of tax-exempt property that limits local revenue capacity. 7 Such grants shall be used for emergency preparedness, response and resi- 8 lience measures. Eligible uses may include, but shall not be limited 9 to: emergency operations and communications improvements, hazard miti- 10 gation and resilience planning, stormwater, culvert or winter weather 11 resilience improvements, equipment and fleet winterization, infrastruc- 12 ture improvements. 13 6. Grants awarded under this section shall be distributed: 14 (a) within ninety days of the effective date of this section to major 15 upstate cities in an amount equal to eighty million on a per-capita 16 basis according to the most recent census. 17 (b) within two years of the effective date of this section to mid- 18 sized upstate cities under a competitive grant program which shall be 19 administered by the division of homeland security and emergency 20 services, in collaboration with the senate committee on cities and the 21 assembly committee on cities, for projects which demonstrate measurable 22 improvements in preparedness, resilience or emergency coordination. A 23 competitive grant awarded under this section to a mid-sized upstate city 24 shall be between five hundred thousand and three million dollars. 25 (c) within two years of the effective date of this section to small 26 upstate cities under a competitive grant program which is to be adminis- 27 tered by the division of homeland security and emergency services, in 28 collaboration with the senate committee on cities and the assembly 29 committee on cities, for projects which demonstrate measurable improve- 30 ments in preparedness, resilience or emergency coordination. A compet- 31 itive grant awarded under this section to a small upstate city shall be 32 between fifty thousand and two hundred fifty thousand dollars. 33 7. Moneys shall be payable from the fund on the audit and warrant of 34 the comptroller, disbursed as advances, progress payments, reimburse- 35 ments, or a combination thereof on vouchers approved and certified by 36 the division of homeland security and emergency services. 37 § 2. The sum of one hundred million dollars ($100,000,000), or so much 38 thereof as may be necessary, is hereby appropriated from any moneys in 39 the state treasury in the general fund not otherwise appropriated to the 40 credit of the upstate cities preparedness and resiliency fund as estab- 41 lished under section 99-uu of the state finance law for the purposes of 42 granting awards and carrying out the provisions of this act. Such sum 43 shall be payable on the audit and warrant of the state comptroller on 44 vouchers certified or approved in the manner provided by law. 45 § 3.
