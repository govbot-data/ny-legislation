BURDICK -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Transportation -- committee discharged, bill amended, ordered reprinted as amended and recommitted to said committee AN ACT to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to limiting the suspension or revocation of an individual's driver's license when participating in a treatment court program The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Paragraph (e) of subdivision 2 of section 1193 of the vehi- 2 cle and traffic law is amended by adding a new subparagraph 8 to read as 3 follows: 4 (8) Treatment court program. Notwithstanding any law, rule, or regu- 5 lation to the contrary, the suspension or revocation of a driver's 6 license by the department shall not result from a defendant taking a 7 plea, where: 8 a. the defendant was a participant in a designated treatment court 9 program recognized and established by the chief administrator of the 10 courts or their designee pursuant to sections 170.15, 180.20, 230.11, or 11 230.21 of the criminal procedure law; 12 b. the defendant pleaded guilty in accordance with a conditional plea 13 agreement before such a treatment court; 14 c. as required under such a conditional plea agreement, the defendant 15 successfully completed a treatment program and complied with any other 16 requirements or conditions; 17 d. the court allowed the defendant to withdraw their previously 18 entered plea of guilty, and the defendant pleaded guilty to lesser 19 charges in accordance with such a conditional plea agreement; and 20 e. the department revoked or suspended the defendant's driver's 21 license upon the defendant initially having pleaded guilty and at least 22 six months have elapsed since such initial plea. 23 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
