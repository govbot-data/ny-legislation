AN ACT in relation to authorizing the village of New Berlin to alienate
and discontinue the use of certain parklands
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Subject to the provisions of this act, the village of New
2 Berlin is hereby authorized, acting by and through its governing body,
3 and upon such terms and conditions as determined by such governing body,
4 to discontinue the use as parkland and alienate the lands described in
5 section three of this act, and to sell and convey such lands at fair
6 market value to Good Shepherd Communities for the purpose of the
7 construction of a skilled nursing and rehabilitation facility.
8 § 2. The authorization contained in section one of this act shall only
9 be effective on the condition that the village of New Berlin dedicates
10 an amount equal to or greater than the fair market value of the park-
11 lands being discontinued towards the acquisition of new parklands and/or
12 capital improvements to existing parklands and recreational facilities
13 within the village of New Berlin.
14 § 3. The parklands authorized by section one of this act to be discon-
15 tinued as parkland is described as follows:

45 § 4. In the event that the village of New Berlin received any funding
46 support or assistance from the federal government for the purchase,
47 maintenance, or improvement of the parklands set forth in section three
48 of this act, the discontinuance and alienation of such parklands author-
49 ized by the provisions of this act shall not occur until the village of
50 New Berlin has complied with any federal requirements pertaining to the
51 alienation or conversion of parklands, including satisfying the secre-
52 tary of the interior that the alienation or conversion complies with all
53 conditions which the secretary of the interior deems necessary to assure
54 the substitution of other lands shall be equivalent in fair market value
55 and usefulness to the lands being alienated or converted.
56 § 5. This act shall take effect immediately.
