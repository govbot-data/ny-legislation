MATTERA -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Environmental Conservation AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law, in relation to establishing a climate action cost council; and to amend the environ- mental conservation law, the public service law and the public author- ities law, in relation to limiting the number of rules and regulations that may be promulgated annually regarding certain climate goals The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The environmental conservation law is amended by adding a 2 new section 75-0104 to read as follows: 3 § 75-0104. Climate action cost council. 4 1. There is hereby established a climate action cost council, which 5 shall consist of nine members to be appointed as follows: 6 a. one member appointed by the governor; 7 b. two members appointed by the temporary president of the senate; 8 c. two members appointed by the speaker of the assembly; 9 d. two members appointed by the minority leader of the senate; and 10 e. two members appointed by the minority leader of the assembly. 11 2. The members shall always include individuals with expertise in 12 issues relating to energy, environmental conservation, labor, public 13 health and regulated industries, including, but not limited to housing, 14 agriculture, construction, transportation and manufacturing. 15 3. Council members shall receive no compensation for their services 16 but shall be reimbursed for actual and necessary expenses incurred in 17 the performance of their duties. 18 4. Each member of the council shall be entitled to one vote. The coun- 19 cil's approval of regulatory action pursuant to this article shall 20 require a two-third vote of the council. No action shall be taken by the 21 council unless there is a quorum, which shall at all times be a majority 22 of the members of the council. Any vacancies on the council shall be filled in the manner provided 2 for in the initial appointment. 3 6. Prior to promulgation of any regulatory measure taken to ensure the 4 attainment of the statewide greenhouse gas emissions limits established 5 pursuant to section 75-0107 of this article taking effect, such measure 6 shall be approved by a two-thirds vote of the council. 7 7. Each state agency shall notify the council of any regulatory meas- 8 ure taken to ensure the attainment of the statewide greenhouse gas emis- 9 sions limits established pursuant to section 75-0107 of this article no 10 less than sixty days before the final adoption of a rule, regulation, or 11 code. The council shall vote on such regulatory measure as soon as prac- 12 ticable and no later than its next quarterly meeting after the council 13 has been notified. No regulatory measures pursuant to this article shall 14 take effect unless approved by the council. 15 8. When determining whether to vote to approve a regulatory measure, 16 council members shall consider: 17 a. the cost, including, but not limited to, the cost to ratepayers and 18 businesses across the state; 19 b. the impact to the electrical grid, including, but not limited to, 20 reliability and safety; and 21 c. the reduction in greenhouse gas emissions associated with the regu- 22 latory measure. 23 9. The council shall meet quarterly. Such meetings shall be open to 24 the public and subject to article seven of the public officers law. No 25 less than sixty days prior to a meeting of the council, the council 26 shall publish a list of draft regulations, rules, or codes to be consid- 27 ered by the council, and the council shall make meaningful public 28 comment available prior to the meeting. 29 10. The council shall submit an annual report to the governor and the 30 legislature beginning on January first, two thousand twenty-six, and 31 annually thereafter. The report shall include a summary of actions taken 32 by the council, the total costs imposed on ratepayers and businesses, 33 and the amount of reduction in greenhouse gas emissions. The report 34 shall be made publicly available on the department's, the New York state 35 energy research and development authority's, and the department of 36 public service's websites. 37 11. The council may request information related to attaining the cost 38 of regulatory measures on consumers and businesses from third parties 39 and state agencies. 40 12. A vote of the council may be overridden by a roll call vote of 41 two-thirds of each chamber of the legislature. 42 § 2.
