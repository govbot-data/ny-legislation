JACKSON -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Environmental Conservation AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law, in relation to including comprehensive climate change risk analysis as part of every environmental assessment statement and requiring updates to rules and regulations regarding the state environmental quality review The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Paragraphs (k) and (l) of subdivision 2 of section 8-0109 2 of the environmental conservation law, as amended by chapter 49 of the 3 laws of 2023, are amended and three new paragraphs (m), (n) and (o) are 4 added to read as follows: 5 (k) effects of any proposed action on disadvantaged communities, 6 including whether the action may cause or increase a disproportionate 7 pollution burden on a disadvantaged community; [and] 8 (l) such other information consistent with the purposes of this arti- 9 cle as may be prescribed in guidelines issued by the commissioner pursu- 10 ant to section 8-0113 of this [chapter.] article; 11 (m) measurement and analysis of all impacts of climate change includ- 12 ing, but not limited to, surface air temperature changes, atmospheric 13 chemistry changes, extreme precipitation changes, drought risk changes, 14 sea level changes, such measurement and analysis shall be done for every 15 aspect of an action; 16 (n) the carbon footprint per person per building block prior to the 17 proposed action and at the conclusion of a reasonable worst case devel- 18 opment scenario for such proposed action, as applicable; and 19 (o) a reasonable worst case development scenario period of one hundred 20 years for assessing potential flooding risks and environmental impacts 21 for the life of any new building which is part of a proposed action. 22 § 2. Section 8-0113 of the environmental conservation law is amended 23 by adding a new subdivision 5 to read as follows: EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
