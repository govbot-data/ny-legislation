and WHEREAS, Patricia Rathschmidt of Mahopac, New York died on July 12, 2025 at the age of 69; and WHEREAS, Born on July 1, 1956, in The Bronx, New York to Bill and Bridie VanSlyck, Patricia Rathschmidt grew up in Yonkers, New York, in a large and loving family alongside her siblings: MaryJane Begley, Bill VanSlyck, Denise O'Neill, Peter VanSlyck, Jimmy VanSlyck, and Kim VanSlyck; and WHEREAS, Patricia Rathschmidt distinguished herself in her profession and by her sincere dedication and substantial contribution to the welfare of her community; and WHEREAS, With a warm, compassionate heart, Patricia Rathschmidt made caring for others her professional career, first as a nurse, then later as a nursing manager in various nursing homes; and WHEREAS, Patricia Rathschmidt was known as a devoted wife, mother, grandmother, sister, niece, daughter, friend, advocate, and leader; and WHEREAS, Patricia Rathschmidt's commitment to excellence, and her spirit of humanity, carried over into all fields of enterprise, including charitable and civic endeavors; and WHEREAS, Alongside her husband, Patricia Rathschmidt co-founded United For The Troops, a nonprofit organization that has touched the lives of thousands of service members across the globe, sending care packages and spreading love and gratitude to those far from home; and WHEREAS, Patricia Rathschmidt is survived by her beloved husband, James Rathschmidt; her children, Denise Iarussi (Dan), Kathy Moberg (Robert), Michael Kelly (Jen), Tracy Barrows (Michael), Tracy Hascup, James Rathschmidt, and Teresa Reuter (Brian); her cherished 12 grandchildren, Brandt, Rori, Maci, Addisyn, John, Charlotte, Sophie, Connor, Jaelynn, Danny, Katie, and Dillon; and her many nieces, nephews, extended family, and dear friends; she is predeceased by her beloved son, Luke Rathschmidt; and her parents, Bill and Bridie VanSlyck; and WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of compassion, Patricia Rathschmidt leaves behind a legacy which will long endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all she served and befriended; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of Patricia Rathschmidt, distinguished citizen and devoted member of her community; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Patricia Rathschmidt.
