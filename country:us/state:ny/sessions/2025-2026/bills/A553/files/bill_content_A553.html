Add §1225-e, V & T L
 
Establishes optional pilot programs for the county of Westchester, for local police departments to use electronic scanning devices to determine whether or not the operator of a vehicle was using a mobile phone or portable electronic device near the time of an accident or collision.

        AN  ACT  to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to creating a
          pilot program for the field testing of mobile telephones  or  portable
          electronic  devices by local police departments in Westchester county;
          and providing for the repeal of such provisions upon expiration there-
          of
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The  vehicle  and  traffic law is amended by adding a new
     2  section 1225-e to read as follows:
     3    § 1225-e. Pilot program for field testing  of  mobile  telephones  and
     4  portable  electronic devices. 1. Purpose. For the county of Westchester,
     5  the commissioner shall authorize a two year pilot program for such coun-
     6  ty to allow local police departments to field test mobile telephones and
     7  portable electronic devices following an accident or collision involving
     8  damage to real or personal property, personal  injury  or  death.    The
     9  commissioner  shall  not require any police department to participate in
    10  such program.
    11    2. Definitions. For the purposes of this section:
    12    (a) "Field testing" shall mean  the  use  of  an  electronic  scanning
    13  device:
    14    (1) approved and utilized in accordance with rules jointly promulgated
    15  by the commissioner and the commissioner of criminal justice services;
    16    (2) approved and certified for use by Westchester county;
    17    (3) used only to scan the log records on the mobile telephone or port-
    18  able electronic device of the operator of the motor vehicle to determine
    19  whether  or not the operator of a motor vehicle was using a mobile tele-
    20  phone or a portable electronic device in  violation  of  section  twelve
    21  hundred  twenty-five-c  or twelve hundred twenty-five-d of this article,

     1  provided, however, that such use of an electronic scanning device  shall
     2  be limited to a search of log records that have been activated by typing
     3  or  swiping  actions on a mobile telephone or electronic portable device
     4  in  the  prior  sixty  minutes  in determining whether the operator of a
     5  motor vehicle was using a mobile telephone or portable electronic device
     6  in violation of either such section at or near the time of the  accident
     7  or collision which provides the grounds for such testing.  No such elec-
     8  tronic  scan  shall access, collect or store user data or the content or
     9  origin of any communication or game conducted, or  image  or  electronic
    10  data viewed, on a mobile telephone or portable electronic device.
    11    (b) "Log records" shall mean the log records of keyboard usage, activ-
    12  ity  logs and applications logs for mobile telephones and portable elec-
    13  tronic devices. For the purposes of  this  section,  log  records  shall
    14  include  only log records which have been activated by typing or swiping
    15  a mobile telephone or portable electronic device, and shall not  include
    16  any  log  records  that  were activated by Bluetooth or voice activation
    17  technology.
    18    (c) "Mobile telephone" shall mean a mobile  telephone  as  defined  in
    19  paragraph (a) of subdivision one of section twelve hundred twenty-five-c
    20  of this article.
    21    (d)  "Portable  electronic  device"  shall  mean a portable electronic
    22  device as defined in paragraph (a) of subdivision two of section  twelve
    23  hundred twenty-five-d of this article.
    24    (e)  "User  data"  shall  mean  text messages, contacts, call history,
    25  pictures, videos, files, contents of  databases,  contents  of  applica-
    26  tions, names of applications or deleted data.
    27    (f) "Using" shall mean:
    28    (1) for the purposes of mobile telephones, as defined in paragraph (c)
    29  of subdivision one of section twelve hundred twenty-five-c of this arti-
    30  cle; and
    31    (2)  for  the  purposes  of portable electronic devices, as defined in
    32  paragraph (b) of subdivision two of section twelve hundred twenty-five-d
    33  of this article.
    34    3. Scope of field testing. (a) Every person operating a motor  vehicle
    35  which  has been involved in an accident or collision involving damage to
    36  real or personal property, personal injury or death, and who has in such
    37  person's possession at or near the time of such accident or collision, a
    38  mobile telephone or personal electronic device, shall at the request  of
    39  a  police officer, submit such person's mobile telephone and/or portable
    40  electronic device to the police officer solely for the purpose of  field
    41  testing such mobile telephone and/or portable electronic device. If such
    42  field  testing  determines  that  the  operator of the motor vehicle was
    43  using their mobile telephone or portable electronic device in  violation
    44  of  section twelve hundred twenty-five-c or twelve hundred twenty-five-d
    45  of this article, the results of such testing shall  constitute  evidence
    46  of any such violation.
    47    (b)  The department, Westchester county and any police departments and
    48  their respective agents participating in the pilot program authorized by
    49  this section may store log records collected during electronic scans and
    50  use such log records only to determine the accuracy of field testing and
    51  the prevalence of violations of section twelve hundred twenty-five-c  or
    52  twelve  hundred  twenty-five-d  of  this  article.  No  user data may be
    53  collected or stored by the department, Westchester county or any  police
    54  departments  or  their  respective  agents  participating  in  the pilot
    55  program authorized by this section.

     2  in consultation with the commissioner of criminal justice services shall
     3  assess the program and submit a report to the legislature and the gover-
