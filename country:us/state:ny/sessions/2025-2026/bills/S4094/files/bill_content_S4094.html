S04094 Summary:

BILL NOS04094
 
SAME ASNo Same As
 
SPONSORPARKER

Amd §§903 & 904, Ed L
 
Includes that health certificates furnished by each student upon their entrance into school shall include an assessment of the student for eating disorders pursuant to the assessment standards established by the commissioner of health.

        AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to screening of  students
          for eating disorders
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section 1. Subdivision 1 of section  903  of  the  education  law,  as
     2  amended  by  chapter  183  of  the  laws  of 2017, is amended to read as
     3  follows:
     4    1. A health certificate shall be furnished  by  each  student  in  the
     5  public schools upon [his or her] their entrance in such schools and upon
     6  [his  or her] their entry into the grades prescribed by the commissioner
     7  in regulations,  provided  that  such  regulations  shall  require  such
     8  certificates  at  least  twice during the elementary grades and twice in
     9  the secondary grades. An examination and health history of any child may
    10  be required by the  local  school  authorities  at  any  time  in  their
    11  discretion  to  promote  the  educational  interests of such child. Each
    12  certificate shall be signed by  a  duly  licensed  physician,  physician
    13  assistant,  or  nurse practitioner, who is authorized by law to practice
    14  in this state, and consistent with  subdivision  three  of  section  six
    15  thousand  nine hundred two of this chapter, or by a duly licensed physi-
    16  cian, physician assistant, or nurse practitioner, who is  authorized  to
    17  practice  in  the  jurisdiction  in  which  the  examination  was given,
    18  provided that the commissioner has determined that such jurisdiction has
    19  standards of licensure and practice comparable to  those  of  New  York.
    20  Each  such  certificate shall describe the condition of the student when
    21  the examination was made, which shall not be  more  than  twelve  months
    22  prior to the commencement of the school year in which the examination is
    23  required,  and shall state whether such student is in a fit condition of
    24  health to permit [his or her] their attendance at  the  public  schools.
    25  The  examination may include a diabetes risk analysis. Each such certif-

    32  the  square  of  height in meters or the weight in pounds divided by the
    33  square of height in inches multiplied by a  conversion  factor  of  703.
    34  Weight  status  categories  for  children  and  adolescents  shall be as
    35  defined by the commissioner of health. In  all  school  districts,  such
    36  physician,  physician  assistant  or  nurse practitioner shall determine
    37  whether a one-time test for sickle cell anemia is necessary or desirable
    38  and [he or she] they shall conduct such tests and the certificate  shall
    39  state  the results. If it should be ascertained, upon such test or exam-
    40  ination, that any of such students have defective sight or  hearing,  an
    41  eating  disorder,  or  other  physical disability, including sickle cell
    42  anemia, as above described, the principal or [his or her]  such  princi-
