The vehicle and traffic law is amended by adding two new sections 15 417-d and 417-e to read as follows: 16 § 417-d. Credit applications for purchase of motor vehicles. 1. Every 17 dealer which applies for or grants eligibility for the provision of 18 credit for the purchase of a motor vehicle shall review and confirm with 19 the applicant for such credit all information included in the applica- 20 tion therefor. Such review and confirmation shall include specific 21 consideration of each item of information as to its accuracy to the best 22 of the knowledge of the parties. No such application shall be deemed 23 complete nor shall it be submitted unless the dealer and the credit 24 applicant shall have signed their signatures attesting that all informa- 25 tion in the application is accurate and true to the best of their know- 26 ledge. 27 2. The failure of any dealer to comply with the provisions of this 28 section, shall grant any credit applicant, who takes delivery of a motor 29 vehicle purchased on the basis of credit acquired for them by such deal- 30 er, the option to void the sale of such motor vehicle within thirty days 31 of the delivery of such vehicle. Upon the voiding of any sale pursuant 32 to this subdivision the dealer shall immediately repay to the purchaser 33 all costs incurred by such purchaser as a consequence of the voided 34 sale, including any financing charges and interest, and the fair market 35 private sale value of any motor vehicle traded-in in the course of such 36 sale. Furthermore, in the event a purchaser commences a cause of action 37 to enforce the provisions of this subdivision, a prevailing purchaser 38 shall be entitled to court costs and reasonable attorney's fees. 39 3. The provisions of this section shall not be deemed to limit any 40 other right or remedy of a plaintiff in law or equity. 41 § 417-e. Conditional deliveries; prohibited. 1. No motor vehicle sold 42 by a dealer shall be delivered to the possession of a purchaser subject 43 to any condition to be complied with after delivery of possession of the 44 motor vehicle. 45 2. All terms and conditions of the sale of a motor vehicle shall be 46 fully and clearly disclosed, in writing, to the purchaser prior to the 47 delivery of possession of such vehicle, and shall be signed by both the 48 dealer and the purchaser. 49 3. Every dealer shall provide a copy of the written disclosures 50 required by subdivision two of this section, and shall also maintain a 51 copy thereof for a period of not less than seven years. 52 § 4.
