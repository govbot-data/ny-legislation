AN ACT to amend the banking law, in relation to the use of automated
lending decision-making tools to make lending decisions
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The banking law is amended by adding a new section 103-a to
2 read as follows:
3 § 103-a. Use of automated lending decision-making tools to make lend-
4 ing decisions. 1. For the purposes of this section, the following terms
5 shall have the following meanings:
6 (a) "Automated lending decision-making tool" means any software that
7 uses algorithms, computational models, or artificial intelligence tech-
8 niques, or a combination thereof, to materially automate or replace
9 human decision-making regarding lending decisions that impact natural
10 persons. "Automated lending decision-making tool" shall not include any
11 software used primarily for basic computerized processes, such as calcu-
12 lators, spellcheck tools, autocorrect functions, spreadsheets, electron-
13 ic communications, or any tool that relates only to internal management
14 affairs such as ordering office supplies or processing payments, and
15 that do not materially impact any lending decisions relating to natural
16 persons.
17 (b) "Lending decision" means any determination made by a covered enti-
18 ty or its agent, whether automated, manual or a combination thereof,
19 that affects the approval, denial, offer, counteroffer, or modification
20 of the terms or conditions of a loan or credit application, including
21 decisions regarding creditworthiness, loan amounts, interest rates,
22 collateral requirements, repayment, or any other material term, and any

15 2. No less than annually, each covered entity that uses automated
16 lending decision-making tools shall conduct an impact assessment
17 substantially completed and bearing the signature of one or more indi-
18 viduals responsible for meaningful human review for the lawful applica-
19 tion and use of such automated lending decision-making tools. An impact
20 assessment shall be conducted prior to any material change to any auto-
21 mated lending decision-making tool that may change the outcome or effect
22 of such tool. An impact assessment final report summarizing the conclu-
23 sions of the initial impact assessment shall be posted on such covered
24 entity's website prior to the implementation and use of such automated
25 lending decision-making tool and updated on the entity's website follow-
26 ing each subsequent assessment. Such impact assessment final report
27 shall include:
28 (a) a description of the objectives of the automated lending deci-
29 sion-making tool;
30 (b) an evaluation of the ability of the automated lending decision-
31 making tool to achieve its stated objectives;
32 (c) a description and evaluation of the objectives and development of
33 the automated lending decision-making tool including:
34 (i) a summary of the underlying algorithms, computational modes, and
35 artificial intelligence tools that are used within the automated lend-
36 ing decision-making tool; and
37 (ii) the design and training data used to develop the automated lend-
38 ing decision-making tool process;
39 (d) testing for:
40 (i) accuracy, fairness, bias and discrimination, and an assessment of
41 whether the use of the automated lending decision-making tool produces
42 discriminatory results on the basis of a consumer's or a class of
43 consumers' actual or perceived race, color, ethnicity, religion,
44 national origin, sex, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, fami-
45 lial status, biometric information, lawful source of income, age, or
46 disability and, outlines mitigations for any identified performance
47 differences in outcomes across relevant groups impacted by such use;
48 (ii) any cybersecurity vulnerabilities and privacy risks resulting
49 from the deployment and use of the automated lending decision-making
50 tool, and the development or existence of safeguards to mitigate the
51 risks;
52 (iii) any public health or safety risks resulting from the deployment
53 and use of the automated lending decision-making tool; and
54 (iv) any reasonably foreseeable misuse of the automated lending deci-
55 sion-making tool and the development or existence of safeguards
56 against such misuse;

24 ing tool to screen applicants for a loan shall notify each such appli-

26 (i) That an automated lending decision-making tool will be used in

30 (iii) Information about the type of data collected for such automated
31 lending decision-making tool, the source of such data, and the covered
32 entity's data retention policy; and
33 (iv) If an application for a loan is denied through use of the auto-
34 mated lending decision-making tool, to the extent practicable, the

46 denial.
