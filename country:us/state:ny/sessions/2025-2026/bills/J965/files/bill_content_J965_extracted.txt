Monrose upon the occasion of his 20th Pastoral Anniversary on May 3, 2025 WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to recognize and commend those individuals who have devoted their lives to serving the spiritual needs of their communities, thereby contributing to the moral and social fabric of the State of New York; and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and in full accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor Pastor Gilford T. Monrose upon the occasion of his 20th Pastoral Anniversary, celebrated on Saturday, May 3, 2025; and WHEREAS, A native of the U.S. Virgin Islands, Pastor Monrose moved to New York in 1999 to pursue a Bachelor of Arts degree in Theology and Bible Studies at Nyack College and Alliance Theological Seminary, and to marry the love of his life, Dianne Thomas; and WHEREAS, Pastor Monrose began his ministry as Youth Director at the historic Mt. Zion Church of God 7th Day, where he developed programs for young people, demonstrating a deep understanding of their struggles and a tireless commitment to enhancing their spiritual maturity; and WHEREAS, In May 2005, Pastor Monrose was ordained to the ministry and has since continued to show unwavering love and dedication to his church and its members; and WHEREAS, Beyond the pulpit, Pastor Monrose has made significant contributions to the broader community; in 2002, he established the Catch The Vision (CTV) Youth Conferences, empowering youth, leaders, and marriages, and attracting participants from around the world; and WHEREAS, He initiated the annual Unity Gospel Fest at Kingsbrook Jewish Medical Center to promote spiritual and physical wellness, and was instrumental in the development of the East Flatbush Ready When the Time Comes Disaster Team, providing disaster-relief training through the American Red Cross; and WHEREAS, Pastor Monrose has served as a chaplain for the Metropolitan Transportation Authority, and in various leadership roles including as director of the Faith-Based and Clergy Initiative at the Brooklyn Borough President's Office, board member of the Flatbush YMCA, and chair of the Community Leadership Council of Kingsbrook Jewish Medical Center; and WHEREAS, Pastor Gilford T. Monrose is the founding President of the 67th Precinct Clergy Council, known as "The GodSquad," serving as a liaison between the police and the community to address gun violence and promote peace; and WHEREAS, Pastor Monrose's dedication extends internationally, having partnered with groups across the Caribbean, Africa, Brazil, Moscow, England, and Israel, empowering individuals to become active and responsible citizens; and WHEREAS, As the loving husband of Dianne Monrose and father to Gianne and Joshua, Pastor Monrose is a passionate advocate for families and children, residing in Weeksville, Brooklyn; and WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body that when individuals of such noble aims and accomplishments are brought to our attention, they should be recognized by all the citizens of the great State of New York; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to honor Pastor Gilford T. Monrose upon the occasion of his 20th Pastoral Anniversary, and to commend him for his dedicated service to his faith and community; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Pastor Gilford T.
