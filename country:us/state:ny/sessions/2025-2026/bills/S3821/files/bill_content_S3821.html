    41    § 22-101. Purpose. In furtherance of the policy set forth by  the  New
    42  York state climate leadership and community protection act, the legisla-
    43  ture hereby directs that building energy and emissions limits be adopted
    44  to  protect  the  health, safety and security of the people of the state
    45  and to assure that clean energy is used in design  and  construction  of
    46  all public and private buildings in the state, as well as to further the
    47  economic  development  of  the  state,  including  the creation of good,
    48  career-supporting jobs and to lower utility bills through  energy  effi-
    49  ciency.
    50    §  22-103.  Emissions  limits  in  buildings under ten thousand square
    51  feet. 1. After December thirty-first, two  thousand  thirty,  no  person
    52  shall  be permitted to replace systems using combustion of any substance
    53  for heating or cooling or providing hot water for a building  under  ten
    54  thousand  square  feet  with  anything that causes the combustion of any
    55  substance that emits twenty-five kilograms or more of carbon dioxide per

        S. 3821                             5
 
     1  million British thermal units of energy, as  determined  by  the  United
     2  States energy information administration.
     3    2. Notwithstanding the prohibition in subdivision one of this section,
     4  combustion  of  a  substance  that emits twenty-five kilograms of carbon
     5  dioxide per million British thermal units of energy  or  more  shall  be
     6  permitted  for  use  within such a building where the combustion of such
     7  substance occurs in connection with a device that contains no connection
     8  to a building's gas supply line or fuel oil piping system, is used on an

    12  larger. 1. After December thirty-first,  two  thousand  thirty-five,  no

    15  ten  thousand  square  feet  or  larger  with  anything  that causes the
    16  combustion of any substance that emits twenty-five kilograms or more  of
    17  carbon  dioxide  per  million British thermal units of energy, as deter-
    18  mined by the United States energy information administration.

    23  substance occurs in connection with a device that contains no connection

    27    § 22-107. Exemptions. 1. Notwithstanding the provisions of this  arti-
    28  cle,  the  state  fire  prevention  and building code council may exempt
    29  systems for emergency back-up heating, but in doing  so  shall  seek  to

    32  shall limit the infrastructure, building systems, or equipment used  for
    33  the  combustion of fossil fuels to the system and area of a building for
    34  which a prohibition on infrastructure, building  systems,  or  equipment
    35  used  for  the  combustion of fossil fuels is infeasible. To the fullest
    36  extent feasible, the code shall require that the area or service  within
    37  the  project  where  infrastructure, building systems, or equipment used
    38  for the combustion of fossil fuels are installed shall  be  all-electric
    39  ready.  Financial considerations shall not be sufficient basis to deter-
    40  mine  physical  or  technical  infeasibility.    Exemptions  or  waivers
    41  provided under this subdivision shall be reviewed during each major code
    42  update cycle to determine whether they are still needed.
    43    2.  The  provisions  set  forth  in sections 22-103 and 22-105 of this
    44  article shall not be construed as applying to  generation  of  emergency

    52  prevention and building code council.

    31    § 260-c. Eligibility. To be eligible for subsidies under this program,
    32  projects shall:
    33    1.  be  built  at an average of forty percent of the AMI, adjusted for

    39  projects funded under this  action  shall  receive  not  less  than  the
    40  prevailing  rate  of  wage  and  benefits for the classification of work
    41  performed by each upon such project pursuant to the labor  law.  Nothing
    42  under  this  paragraph  shall  limit  the ability of labor agreements to
    43  afford a wage higher than the prevailing wage for all workers, laborers,
    44  and mechanics employed on projects funded under this action;
    45    4. follow tenant protections that prevent  eviction  except  in  situ-
    46  ations  of  non-payment  of  rent; the tenant is violating a substantial
    47  obligation of the tenancy and has failed to cure such  violation  within
    48  ten  days;  or  the tenant is committing or permitting a nuisance in the

    51  has  unreasonably  refused the landlord's access to the housing accommo-

    53    5. utilize a project labor agreement. For purposes of this  paragraph,
