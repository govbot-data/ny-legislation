The legislature finds and 14 declares that there exists a serious shortage of safe and habitable 15 affordable housing in New York state for persons and families of low 16 income. A portion of the affordable, multifamily housing stock is in 17 operational and financial distress, threatening to exacerbate this shor- 18 tage. Rent collection losses, rising interest rates and insurance costs, 19 and other factors have caused significant increases in operating 20 expenses, which may jeopardize the availability to tenants of affordable 21 housing units or even the viability of some buildings. Existing preser- 22 vation program funding is limited, and distressed affordable housing 23 properties may not be eligible for such funding. Definitions. For the purposes of this article, the following 5 terms shall have the following meanings: 6 1. Affordable housing relief fund program. Within amounts appro- 32 priated or otherwise available therefor, the housing trust fund corpo- 33 ration shall develop and administer an affordable housing relief fund 34 program which shall provide aid in the form of payments and grants for 35 reasonable and necessary expenses to eligible applicants for the preser- 36 vation or improvement of affordable housing properties. 37 § 1303. Program criteria. The corporation shall develop procedures, 38 criteria and requirements related to the competitive application for and 39 award of aid pursuant to this section which shall include: eligibility, 40 market demand, feasibility and funding criteria; the funding determi- 41 nation process; supervision and evaluation of contracting applicants; 42 reporting, budgeting and record-keeping requirements; provisions for 43 modification and termination of contracts; and such other matters not 44 inconsistent with the purposes and provisions of this article as the 45 corporation shall deem necessary or appropriate. The corporation may 46 prioritize properties in the greatest distress. 47 § 1304. Funding criteria. Up to fifty thousand dollars per income-res- 48 tricted housing unit may be awarded for residential affordable housing 49 preservation or rehabilitation. Assistance shall be in the form of a 50 zero percent interest rate loan, with no monthly payments, that shall be 51 forgiven after ten years. All or a portion of program funds may be 52 required to be repaid if the project is sold within ten years or fails 53 to comply with the regulatory agreement. The amount subject to possible 54 repayment shall decrease by 1/120 per month each month the property 55 remains in compliance with affordability terms. S. 3219 3 1 § 1305. Affordability protection. Funding. Subject to appropriations for this program, the 7 corporation in its sole discretion shall authorize all funding decisions 8 and make all award determinations. 9 § 1307. Annual report. The corporation shall, on or before January 10 thirty-first in each year submit a report to the legislature on the 11 implementation of this article. Such report shall include, but not be 12 limited to, the number of applications received by the end of the 13 preceding calendar year, the total dollar amount of such applications, 14 and the disposition of such applications; and for each award made to a 15 grantee under this article, a description of such award, the contract 16 amount and cumulative total, the specific activities performed by such 17 grantee, the amounts of monies received by the grantee from sources 18 other than payments made pursuant to this article, and such other infor- 19 mation as the corporation deems pertinent. 20 § 2.
