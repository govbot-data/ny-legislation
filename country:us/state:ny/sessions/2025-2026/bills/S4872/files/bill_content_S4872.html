        AN ACT to amend the general municipal  law,  in  relation  to  providing
          notice  of health insurance contracts for retired officers, employees,
          and their families

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Subdivision 2 of section 92-a of the general municipal law,
     2  as  amended by chapter 805 of the laws of 1984 and as further amended by
     3  section 104 of part A of chapter 62 of the laws of 2011, is amended  and
     4  a new subdivision 2-a is added to read as follows:
     5    2.  [A]  Consistent  with  the provisions of subdivision two-a of this
     6  section, a public corporation may contract with a non-profit  membership
     7  corporation,  organized  under  article forty-three of the insurance law
     8  and approved by the superintendent of financial services and  the  state
     9  board  of social welfare, or with any insurance company authorized to do
    10  business in this state for the purpose of furnishing medical and  surgi-
    11  cal  services  and  hospital  service  as defined in such article forty-
    12  three, or medical and surgical and hospital  insurance  to  persons  who
    13  contract with such non-profit membership corporation or insurance compa-
    14  ny,  or  who  subscribe to a plan or plans, as hereinafter provided. Any
    15  such contract entered into by a  public  corporation  shall  permit  any
    16  officer  or  employee  or group of officers or employees of an agency or
    17  department of the public corporation voluntarily to subscribe to a  plan
    18  or  plans providing for medical and surgical and hospital insurance for,
    19  or medical and surgical services and hospital service to, such  officers
    20  or  employees  and  their  families. Any such contract entered into by a
    21  public corporation may, if authorized by the governing board or body and
    22  subject to such conditions, limitations and eligibility requirements  as
    23  may  be  fixed  by  such  board or body, permit any retired officers and
    24  employees or group of retired officers and employees  of  an  agency  or

     1  department  of the public corporation voluntarily to subscribe to such a
     2  plan or plans to provide such insurance for or service to themselves and
     3  their families. The comptroller  or  other  disbursing  officer  of  the
     4  public corporation, or of any fund out of which officers or employees of
     5  such public corporation are paid, is authorized to deduct from the wages
     6  or  salary  of such contracting or subscribing officer or employee, with
     7  [his] such officer or employee's prior consent,  in  writing,  the  sums
     8  required  to  be  paid  by  such  officer or employee to such non-profit
     9  membership corporation or insurance company. Such public corporation, if
    10  such contract or plan provides that  the  employer  shall  contribute  a
    11  share  of  the  cost  of such medical and surgical services and hospital
    12  service, or medical and surgical and hospital insurance, for  its  offi-
    13  cers  and  employees or retired officers and employees, is authorized to
    14  appropriate a sum required to be paid under such contract by the  public
    15  corporation  as  employer. The sum to be paid by it under such contract,
    16  in the discretion of such public corporation, may be any  percentage  of
    17  the  total  cost  including the whole thereof. Where the compensation of
    18  any officer or employee whose position is covered by medical and  surgi-
    19  cal  services  and hospital service or medical and surgical and hospital
    20  insurance pursuant to this section is paid from a special or administra-
    21  tive fund provided for by law, the contributions required to be paid  by
    22  the public corporation for such coverage shall be paid from such special
    23  or  administrative  fund.  The public corporation shall be authorized to
    24  pay directly to such non-profit membership corporation or to such insur-
    25  ance company, the total of such appropriation and of  such  officer  and
    26  employee deductions.
    27    2-a.  Forty-five  days prior to the governing board of a public corpo-
    28  ration authorizing or approving a contract or plan of health  insurance,
    29  or  an  amendment  thereto,  for retired officers, retired employees, or
    30  their families pursuant to subdivision two of this section,  the  public
    31  corporation shall give written notice of such proposed contract, plan or
    32  amendment, to the retired officers, retired employees, or their families
    33  who are covered under such contract or plan. Where the contract, plan or
    34  amendment  is  contracted  for  as part of negotiations between a public
    35  corporation and a recognized and certified employee organization  pursu-
    36  ant  to  article  fourteen of the civil service law, such written notice
    37  shall be given at the time such health insurance contract is binding  on
    38  the covered retired officers, retired employees, or their families. Such
    39  written  notice shall contain either: (a) the full text of such proposed
    40  contract, plan or  amendment  and  any  relevant  financial  information
    41  including, but not limited to the cost of the proposed contract, plan or
    42  amendment  to the public corporation and the cost to the covered retired
    43  officers, retired employees, or their families; or (b) the general terms
    44  of the proposed contract, plan or  amendment  along  with  the  physical
    45  location  and  web address to a secure website where the covered retired
    46  officers, retired employees or their family members can obtain the  full
    47  text  of  such  proposed  contract,  plan  or amendment and any relevant
    48  financial information including, but not limited  to  the  cost  of  the
    49  proposed  contract,  plan or amendment to the public corporation and the
    50  cost to the covered retired officers, retired employees, or their  fami-
    51  lies.
    52    §  2.  This  act  shall take effect immediately and shall apply to any
    53  health insurance contract or plan entered into,  renewed,  modified,  or
    54  amended on or after such effective date.
