Section 8-502 of the election law, as amended by chapter 2 481 of the laws of 2023, is amended to read as follows: 3 § 8-502. Challenges; generally. 1. Before [his] their vote is cast at 4 an election any person may be challenged as to [his] their right to 5 vote, or [his] their right to vote by early mail, absentee, military, 6 special federal or special presidential ballot. Such challenge may be 7 made by an inspector or clerk, by any duly appointed watcher, or by any 8 registered voter properly in the polling place. An inspector shall chal- 9 lenge every person offering to vote, whom [he] the inspector shall know 10 or suspect is not entitled to vote in the district, and every person 11 whose name appears on the list of persons to be challenged on election 12 day which is furnished by the board of elections. 13 2. Prior to making a challenge to a voter as provided in this section, 14 the person challenging the voter's right to vote shall: 15 (a) take a verbal oath in the presence of an inspector, clerk or duly 16 appointed watcher affirming that the person is making the challenge in 17 good faith and is aware that a bad faith challenge could lead to penal- 18 ties and consequences; and 19 (b) complete a written document in such form as is approved by the 20 state board of elections stating the challenger's name and address and 21 the name of the voter who was challenged. 22 § 2.
