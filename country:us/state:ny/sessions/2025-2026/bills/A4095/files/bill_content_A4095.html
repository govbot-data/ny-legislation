Amd §§8-502 & 8-504, El L
 
Requires persons administering challenge oaths to voter applicants to attest that the challenge is being made in good faith and to provide certain identifying information; requires challenge oaths to be presented to a challenged voter applicant in writing.

        AN ACT to amend to the election law, in relation to  the  administration
          of challenge oaths to voter applicants
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Section 8-502 of the election law, as  amended  by  chapter
     2  481 of the laws of 2023, is amended to read as follows:
     3    §  8-502. Challenges; generally. 1. Before [his] their vote is cast at
     4  an election any person may be challenged as  to  [his]  their  right  to
     5  vote,  or  [his]  their right to vote by early mail, absentee, military,
     6  special federal or special presidential ballot. Such  challenge  may  be
     7  made  by an inspector or clerk, by any duly appointed watcher, or by any
     8  registered voter properly in the polling place. An inspector shall chal-
     9  lenge every person offering to vote, whom [he] the inspector shall  know
    10  or  suspect  is  not  entitled to vote in the district, and every person
    11  whose name appears on the list of persons to be challenged  on  election
    12  day which is furnished by the board of elections.
    13    2. Prior to making a challenge to a voter as provided in this section,
    14  the person challenging the voter's right to vote shall:
    15    (a)  take a verbal oath in the presence of an inspector, clerk or duly
    16  appointed watcher affirming that the person is making the  challenge  in
    17  good  faith and is aware that a bad faith challenge could lead to penal-
    18  ties and consequences; and
    19    (b) complete a written document in such form as  is  approved  by  the
    20  state  board  of elections stating the challenger's name and address and
    21  the name of the voter who was challenged.
    22    § 2. Section 8-504 of the election law, subdivisions 1, 2, 3, and 4 as
    23  renumbered by chapter 373 of the laws of 1978, subdivision 4 as  amended
    24  by chapter 9 of the laws of 1978, subdivision 5 as amended by chapter 82
