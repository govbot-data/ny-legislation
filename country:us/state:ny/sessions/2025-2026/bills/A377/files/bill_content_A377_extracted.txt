VANEL -- read once and referred to the Committee on Consumer Affairs and Protection AN ACT to amend the general business law, in relation to requirements for unsolicited private government-related registration service commu- nications The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The general business law is amended by adding a new section 2 350-f-2 to read as follows: 3 § 350-f-2. Unsolicited private government-related registration service 4 communications. 1. Any person or entity, whether located within the 5 state or sending physical or electronic mail to a person within the 6 state, sending an unsolicited private government-related registration 7 service communication that mentions any fees, renewal charges, requests 8 for payment, or requests to enter into a contract, shall include a 9 notice that is reasonably calculated to apprise the recipient of the 10 following message: 11 "(COMPANY NAME) IS NOT AFFILIATED WITH NOR IS A GOVERNMENT ENTITY. 12 YOU ARE NOT REQUIRED TO PAY OR ENTER INTO ANY AGREEMENT WITH US." 13 For purposes of this section, "unsolicited private government-related 14 registration service communication" shall mean any letter, notice, 15 invoice, bill, or statement of account that is sent to a person or enti- 16 ty without prior request or agreement, where no existing business 17 relationship or contract exists between the sender or recipient, 18 purporting to charge for or offer any service related to any govern- 19 mental registration made with any state or federal government entity 20 within the United States. 21 2. Any violation of subdivision one of this section shall constitute a 22 deceptive act or practice within the meaning of section three hundred 23 forty-nine of this article. 24 § 2. This act shall take effect on the thirtieth day after it shall 25 have become a law. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
