AN ACT to amend the general business law, in relation to prohibiting
online retailers from basing prices on certain criteria
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The general business law is amended by adding a new section
2 219 to read as follows:
3 § 219. Online retailer pricing. 1. For the purposes of this section,
4 the following terms shall have the following meanings:
5 (a) "Bona fide discount" means a genuine reduction in price that is
6 referred to as such to consumers. Entities may reasonably interpret the
7 term "bona fide discount" to mean a genuine reduction from a contempora-
8 neous or recent reference price that is established absent of deceptive,
9 abusive and unfair practices as required by section three hundred
10 forty-nine of this chapter and 16 CFR 233.
11 (b) "Consumer price" means the reference price with adjustments based
12 on bona fide discounts, including sales, coupons, promotions or other
13 discounts.
14 (c) "Reference price" means the actual amount a consumer must pay for
15 any good or service, including mandatory fees or charges necessary to
16 receive such good or service except taxes or fees imposed by a govern-
17 ment, that is openly and actively offered to the public in the regular
18 course of business for a reasonably substantial and recent period in
19 time.
20 (d) "Hardware state" shall mean the condition or mode of existence of
21 a system, component, or simulation, including, but not limited to,
22 battery life, number of wireless connections detected, and age of the

1 device, data that is erased when an online device is restarted or

4 (e) "Online device" shall mean a physical object that has built-in
5 resources that allow it to communicate through the internet or a short-

7 but not limited to, a laptop computer, a desktop computer, a tablet, a

9 (f) "Personal data" means any data that identifies or could reasonably
10 be linked, directly or indirectly, with a specific consumer or device.
11 2. (a) A reference price offered to a consumer through such consumer's
12 online device shall not be generated by any online retailer or retail
13 mercantile establishment, in whole or in part, based on any of the
14 following input data, to the extent that it is not used or processed as
15 personal data:
16 (i) The hardware or hardware state of such online device;
17 (ii) The presence or absence of any software on such online device.
18 Provided, however, a consumer price may be offered to a consumer through
19 the consumer's online device if the consumer has downloaded an applica-
20 tion or software and opted into such application or software to purchase
21 a product or service; or
22 (iii) Geolocation data of the online device.
23 (b) Notwithstanding subparagraph (iii) of paragraph (a) of this subdi-
24 vision, the geolocation data of the online device may be used, and the
25 consumer's location may be accounted for, to generate a reference price
26 for the following reasons:
27 (i) To account for the differences in the costs or demands associated
28 with providing a good or service to different consumers;
29 (ii) A surcharge based on the real-time demand for the product or
30 service in the consumer's vicinity, if the product or service is
31 provided immediately upon request; or
32 (iii) Pricing based on price variances at different physical
33 locations, due to legitimate cost or demand differentials or state or
34 local government taxes, duties, or other fees.
35 (c) This section shall not apply to any bona fide discounts or any
36 other bona fide rebates, sales, or promotions that reduce the price of
37 goods or services, that are offered or made available to the general
38 public on the same terms, and do not incorporate input data as specified
39 in paragraph (a) of this subdivision.
40 3. The duties and obligations pursuant to this section are cumulative
41 with any other duties or obligations imposed under local, state, and
42 federal law and shall not be construed to relieve any party from any
43 duties or obligations imposed under any applicable laws.
44 4. Any person in violation of this article shall be subject to the
45 remedies and penalties available pursuant to section three hundred
46 forty-nine of this chapter and, in addition, shall be subject to a
47 supplemental civil penalty of not more than five thousand dollars for
48 the first violation, and twenty thousand dollars for each subsequent
49 violation, or the profits earned by violating the section, whichever is
50 greater, which may be recovered in a civil action brought by the attor-
51 ney general. Whenever a court determines that a violation of this
52 section has occurred, the court may impose any other civil penalty
53 and/or remedy deemed appropriate by the court. Civil penalties imposed
54 under this section and recovered by the attorney general shall be used
55 to enforce consumer protection and data privacy.
