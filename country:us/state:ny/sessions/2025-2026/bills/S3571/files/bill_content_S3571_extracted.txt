CLEARE, WEBB -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Disabilities AN ACT in relation to naming a certain state facility after Willie Mae "Goody" Goodman The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Legislative intent: The legislature recognizes the contrib- 2 utions of Willie Mae Goodman, a mother and tireless advocate who has 3 devoted her life to ensure that people with developmental disabilities 4 can have "full" lives in the community. Since, 1961, Ms. Goodman's 5 daughter has been under the care of the state department of mental 6 hygiene, first at the Willowbrook State school in Staten Island and then 7 at Gouverneur Hospital in lower Manhattan. Throughout her daughter's 8 life, Ms. Goodman has fought not only for Margaret's rights, but for the 9 rights of other developmentally disabled individuals and their families. 10 At Gouverneur Hospital she led the first group of parents in a court 11 action to block the transfer of their loved ones back to Willowbrook. 12 She then fought to obtain legal affirmation that Gouverneur residents 13 were members of the Willowbrook Class and entitled to the same benefits 14 and protection. Ms. Goodman continues to advocate for people with disa- 15 bilities as part of the Gouverneur Parents Association. In honor of her 16 tireless work for the developmentally disabled community, the 119th 17 street facilities will bear her name. 18 § 2.
