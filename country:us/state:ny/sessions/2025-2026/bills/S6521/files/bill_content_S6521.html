S06521 Summary:

BILL NOS06521
 
SAME ASNo Same As

Add §20-a, Pub Hous L
 
Establishes a housing succession rights task force to conduct a state-wide assessment of succession rights in rent stabilized, public housing and city or municipal owned buildings.

        AN  ACT  to  amend the public housing law, in relation to establishing a
          housing succession rights task force; and providing for the repeal  of
          such provisions upon expiration thereof

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The public housing law is amended by adding a  new  section
     2  20-a to read as follows:
     3    §  20-a.  Housing  succession  rights  task  force. 1. There is hereby
     4  established within the division of housing and community renewal a hous-
     5  ing succession rights task force which shall meet at least once annually
     6  in different geographical areas of  the  state.  All  members  shall  be
     7  provided  with  notice  reasonably in advance of each meeting with date,
     8  time and location of such meeting. Task force members may use  videocon-
     9  ferencing for attendance and participation. The task force shall consist
    10  of  ten  members  and  shall  include representatives from the following
    11  state agencies: the division  of  housing  and  community  renewal,  the
    12  office  for  the  aging, the department of economic development, and the
    13  New York city department of housing preservation and development. Of the
    14  six remaining members, two shall be appointed  by  the  speaker  of  the
    15  assembly,  two  shall  be  appointed  by  the temporary president of the
    16  senate, one shall be appointed by the minority leader  of  the  assembly
    17  and  one  shall  be  appointed by the minority leader of the senate, and
    18  shall broadly represent housing groups from all geographic areas of  the
    19  state including service providers, associations, advocates, proprietors,
    20  and  residents  of rent stabilized, public housing and city or municipal
    21  owned  buildings.  Members  shall  receive  no  compensation  for  their
    22  services  but shall be reimbursed for travel and other expenses actually
    23  and necessarily incurred in the performance of their  duties.  The  task
