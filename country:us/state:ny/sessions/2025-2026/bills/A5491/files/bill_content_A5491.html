BILL NOA05491B
 
SAME ASSAME AS S02284-A
 
SPONSORBichotte Hermelyn
 
COSPNSRRaga, Gonzalez-Rojas, Levenberg, Hyndman, Taylor, Novakhov, Santabarbara
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Add §149, St Fin L
 
Requires contractors receiving public funds to pay amounts owed to small businesses and minority-owned business enterprises within 10 days of receipt of an undisputed invoice generated by a small business, minority-owned business enterprise or vendor management service and managed service provider on behalf of the small business or minority-owned business enterprise.

        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  state finance law, in relation to providing for
          prompt payment by contractors receiving public funds of  amounts  owed
          to small businesses and minority-owned business enterprises
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The state finance law is amended by adding  a  new  section
     2  149 to read as follows:
     3    § 149.  Prompt contracting and payments for small business and minori-
     4  ty-owned business enterprise subcontractors. 1. For the purposes of this
     5  section:
     6    (a)  "Covered project" shall mean a project which is paid for in whole
     7  or in part out of public funds as such term is defined in  this  section
     8  where  the amount of all such public funds, when aggregated, is at least
     9  thirty percent of the total project costs and where such  project  costs
    10  are  over  twenty-five  thousand  dollars.   "Covered project" shall not
    11  include a project which entails constructing, reconstructing,  rehabili-
    12  tating, or repairing buildings or highways for, or on behalf of, a state
    13  agency.
    14    (b)  "Paid  for  in whole or in part out of public funds" means any of
    15  the following:
    16    (i) the payment of money, by a public entity, or a third party  acting
    17  on  behalf  of and for the benefit of a public entity, directly to or on
    18  behalf of the contractor, subcontractor, developer or owner that is  not
    19  subject to repayment; or

     1    (ii)  the  savings achieved from fees, rents, interest rates, or other
     2  loan costs, or insurance costs that are lower than  market  rate  costs;
     3  savings  from  reduced taxes as a result of tax credits, tax abatements,
     4  tax exemptions or tax increment financing; savings from payments in lieu
     5  of  taxes; and any other savings from reduced, waived, or forgiven costs
     6  that would have otherwise been at a higher or market rate  but  for  the
     7  involvement of the public entity.
     8    (c)  "Contract"  means  an  enforceable  agreement  entered  into by a
     9  contractor and a subcontractor.
    10    (d) "Contractor" means any person, partnership, firm, corporation,  or
    11  association:  (i)  selling  materials, equipment, or supplies or leasing
    12  property or equipment to a state agency; or (ii) rendering or  providing
    13  services pursuant to a contract with a state agency.  "Contractor" shall
    14  not  mean  any  person  constructing, reconstructing, rehabilitating, or
    15  repairing buildings or highways for, or on behalf of, a state agency.
    16    (e) "Subcontractor" means a person engaged by a contractor or  another
    17  subcontractor to perform a portion of the contractor's obligations under
    18  a  contract.  "Subcontractor" shall not include any person constructing,
    19  reconstructing, rehabilitating, or repairing buildings or highways  for,
    20  or on behalf of, a state agency.
    21    (f)  "Small  business  concern"  or  "small business" means a business
    22  which is resident in this state, independently owned and  operated,  not
    23  dominant in its field and employs one hundred or less persons.
    24    (g)  "Minority-owned business enterprise" has the meaning set forth in
    25  subdivision seven of section three hundred ten of the executive law.
    26    (h) "Entity" means a public entity or third party acting on behalf  of
    27  and for the benefit of a public entity.
    28    (i)  "Public entity" means any organization, body, or institution that
    29  operates on behalf of the  government  and  is  involved  in  a  covered
    30  project.
    31    2.  Upon  receipt  of an undisputed invoice generated by a small busi-
    32  ness, minority-owned business enterprise or  vendor  management  service
    33  and  managed service provider on behalf of the small business or minori-
    34  ty-owned business enterprise for services rendered, the entity receiving
    35  payment for a covered project shall issue  payment  no  later  than  ten
    36  business  days  from  receipt  of  such invoice. Date of invoice service
    37  period shall not exceed ten business days. Failure by the contractor  to
    38  make  any  payment,  including  any  remaining  amounts  of the contract
    39  balance to any small business  or  minority-owned  business  enterprise,
    40  shall  result in the commencement and accrual of interest on amounts due
    41  to such subcontractors. Such interest shall be the  sole  responsibility
    42  of  the  entity,  and shall be paid at the rate of interest in effect on
    43  the date payment is made by the entity.
    44    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
