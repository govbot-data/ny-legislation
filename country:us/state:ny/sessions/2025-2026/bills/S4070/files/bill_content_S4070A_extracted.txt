AN ACT to amend the labor law, in relation to enacting the "trapped at
work act"
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Short title. This act shall be known and may be cited as
2 the "trapped at work act".
3 § 2. The labor law is amended by adding a new article 37 to read as
4 follows:
5 ARTICLE 37
6 TRAPPED AT WORK ACT
7 Section 1050. Definitions.
8 1051. Scope.
9 1052. Prohibiting the use of employment promissory notes and
10 other similar provisions.
11 1053. Enforcement.
12 1054. Rules.
13 1055. Savings clause.
14 § 1050. Definitions. For purposes of this article:
15 1. "Employer" means an individual, partnership, association, corpo-
16 ration, limited liability company, trust, government or government
17 subdivision, or any organized group that hires or contracts with a work-
18 er to work for the employer. For the purposes of this article, this term
19 shall also include any subsidiary of an employer and any individual,
20 partnership, association, corporation, limited liability company, trust,
21 government or government subdivision, or any organized group associated
22 with an employer that provides training to workers.
23 2. "Worker" means an individual who is permitted to work for or on
24 behalf of an employer. The term "worker" includes an employee, independ-
25 ent contractor, extern, intern, volunteer, apprentice, sole proprietor
26 who provides a service or services to an employer or to a client or

1 customer of an employer on behalf of such employer, and an individual
2 who provides service through a business or nonprofit entity or associ-
3 ation. "Worker" does not include an individual, even if the individual
4 performs incidental service for the employer, whose sole relationship
5 with the employer is as a vendor of goods.
6 3. "Employment promissory note" means any instrument, agreement, or
7 contract provision that requires a worker to pay the employer, or the
8 employer's agent or assignee, a sum of money if the worker leaves such
9 employment before the passage of a stated period of time. "Employment
10 promissory note" includes any such instrument, agreement, or contract
11 provision which states such payment of moneys constitutes reimbursement
12 for training provided to the worker by the employer or by a third party.
13 § 1051. Scope. The rights and remedies provided pursuant to this arti-
14 cle shall not supersede any other rights and remedies provided by stat-
15 ute or common law.
16 § 1052. Prohibiting the use of employment promissory notes and other
17 similar provisions. 1. Beginning on the effective date of this article,
18 no employer may require, as a condition of employment, any worker or
19 prospective worker to execute an employment promissory note. The
20 execution of an employment promissory note as a condition of employment
21 is unconscionable, against public policy, and unenforceable, and any
22 such note shall be void. If any such note is part of a larger agreement,
23 the invalidity of such note shall not affect the other provisions of
24 such agreement.
25 2. Nothing in this section shall prohibit or render void or unenforce-
26 able any agreement between a worker and an employer that:
27 (a) requires the worker to repay to the employer any sums advanced to
28 such worker by the employer, unless such sums were used to pay for
29 training related to the worker's employment with the employer;
30 (b) requires the worker to pay the employer for any property it has
31 sold or leased to such worker;
32 (c) requires educational personnel to comply with any terms or condi-
33 tions of sabbatical leaves granted by their employers; or
34 (d) is entered into as part of a program agreed to by the employer and
35 its workers' collective bargaining representative.
36 § 1053. Enforcement. 1. Any employer found to have violated this arti-
37 cle shall be fined by the commissioner not less than one thousand
38 dollars and not more than five thousand dollars for each violation. Each
39 worker or prospective worker whom an employer required to execute an
40 employment promissory note or against whom an employer seeks to enforce
41 such a note shall constitute a separate violation of this article.
42 2. Any worker or prospective worker for whom an employer requires
43 execution of an employment promissory note as a condition of employment
44 or against whom an employer seeks to enforce such a note may bring an
45 action against such employer to recover actual damages or five thousand
46 dollars, whichever is greater, and injunctive relief. In the event of a
47 successful action, the court shall award any plaintiff reasonable costs
48 and attorney fees.
49 § 1054. Rules. The commissioner may promulgate rules and regulations
50 necessary to carry out the provisions of this article.
51 § 1055. Savings clause. If any portion of this article or the applica-
52 tion thereof to any person, occupation or circumstance is held invalid,
53 the remainder of the article and the application of such provision to
54 other persons, employees, occupations, or circumstances shall not be
55 affected thereby.
56 § 3. This act shall take effect immediately.
