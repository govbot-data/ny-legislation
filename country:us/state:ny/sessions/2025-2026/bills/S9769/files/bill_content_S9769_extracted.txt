SEPULVEDA -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Judiciary AN ACT to amend the general obligations law, in relation to the liabil- ity of electronic marketplaces for compensation for damages for defec- tive goods The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The general obligations law is amended by adding a new 2 section 11-108 to read as follows: 3 § 11-108. Liability of electronic marketplaces for compensation for 4 damages for defective goods. 1. As used in this section: 5 (a) "Electronic marketplace provider" means a person who, pursuant to 6 an agreement with a marketplace seller, sells or facilitates sales of 7 tangible personal property by such marketplace seller or sellers via an 8 internet website, online catalog, mobile application, or similar forum. 9 "Electronic marketplace provider" includes any subsidiaries or related 10 party companies. A person "facilitates sales of tangible personal prop- 11 erty" for purposes of this paragraph when the person meets both the 12 following conditions: 13 (i) such person provides the forum in which, or by means of which, the 14 sale takes place or the offer of sale is accepted, including an internet 15 shop, store, booth, application, website, catalog, or similar forum; and 16 (ii) such person or an affiliate of such person collects the receipts 17 paid by a customer to a marketplace seller for a sale of tangible 18 personal property, or contracts with a third party to collect such 19 receipts. 20 (b) "Handmade" means a product that conforms to all applicable state 21 and federal consumer health and safety laws and is made by the market- 22 place seller in the primary residence of the marketplace seller, so long 23 as the marketplace seller's sales of handmade products generate less 24 than fifty thousand dollars per year in revenues. 25 (c) "Marketplace seller" means any person who has an agreement with an 26 electronic marketplace provider under which the electronic marketplace EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. LBD08360-01-5 S. 9769 2 1 provider will facilitate sales of tangible personal property by such 2 person within the meaning of paragraph (a) of this subdivision. 3 (d) "Product" means a tangible good that is subject to strict product 4 liability law. 5 2. An electronic marketplace provider shall be deemed to be a retailer 6 for purposes of New York strict liability law and shall be strictly 7 liable for all damages caused by defective products placed into the 8 stream of commerce to the same extent that a retailer of that defective 9 product would be liable. The liability of an electronic marketplace 10 provider shall be equal to, but not greater than, the liability of a 11 retailer, and all defenses to strict liability that are available under 12 New York law, except as modified by this section, shall be preserved for 13 electronic marketplace providers. 14 3. This section shall apply: 15 (a) without regard to fault; 16 (b) regardless of whether the electronic marketplace provider or 17 marketplace seller has a physical presence in the state; and 18 (c) regardless of whether the electronic marketplace provider ever 19 took physical possession of, or title to, the product. 20 4. An electronic marketplace provider shall not be liable as described 21 in subdivision two of this section if any of the following conditions 22 are met: 23 (a) The product that caused the damage was one of the following: 24 (i) preowned or used and prominently described or prominently adver- 25 tised in the electronic marketplace provider as preowned or used at the 26 time it was purchased by the consumer; or 27 (ii) handmade; or 28 (iii) custom-made. 29 (b) The electronic marketplace provider did not receive a direct or 30 indirect financial benefit from the sale of the defective product that 31 caused the injury. A fee that is exclusively for an advertisement is not 32 a "financial benefit from the sale of the defective product." 33 5. Notwithstanding the provisions of subdivision four of this section, 34 an electronic marketplace provider shall be strictly liable for the sale 35 of preowned, used, handmade, or custom-made defective products if the 36 application of strict liability to the electronic marketplace provider 37 is consistent with the policy considerations underlying strict product 38 liability. 39 6. This section shall not be deemed to limit or impair any cause of 40 action authorized pursuant to any other provision of law available to a 41 person injured as a result of a product. The provisions of this section 42 shall be in addition to any other right of action or recovery otherwise 43 available under the law. 44 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
