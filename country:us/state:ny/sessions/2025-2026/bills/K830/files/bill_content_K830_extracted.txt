Levenberg MEMORIALIZING Governor Kathy Hochul to proclaim January 15, 2026, as Bagel Day in the State of New York WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to recognize official days that are set aside to celebrate the spirit of New York; and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and in full accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to memorialize Governor Kathy Hochul to proclaim January 15, 2026 as Bagel Day in the State of New York, in conjunction with the observance of National Bagel Day; and WHEREAS, National Bagel Day is an annual celebration commemorating the delectable doughnut-shaped yeast-leavened roll that is characterized by a crisp, shiny crust and a dense interior celebrated across the country; and WHEREAS, The bagel was brought to the United States largely by the growing Eastern European and Jewish-immigrant communities in New York City in the early 1900s; and WHEREAS, The bagel grew in popularity in Manhattan's Lower East Side in neighborhoods populated by Jewish immigrants from Poland, where it became a staple starting in the 1600s; and WHEREAS, An entire union was created in the early 1900s called Bagel Bakers Local 338 to support the growing, immigrant-led industry; and WHEREAS, The hundreds of bagel shops in New York have a long history of being immigrant-owned small businesses that contribute to New York's vibrant and distinctive local culinary scene; and WHEREAS, Bagel Day is an opportunity to reflect on the immigrant roots of our great state, support small businesses, and enjoy this delicious cuisine; therefore be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to memorialize Governor Kathy Hochul to proclaim January 15, 2026, as Bagel Day in the State of New York; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to The Honorable Kathy Hochul, Governor of the State of New York.
