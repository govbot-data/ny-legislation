New York photovoltaic module stewardship and take-back 53 program. 54 1. The department shall develop guidance for a photovoltaic module 55 stewardship and take-back program to guide manufacturers in preparing 56 and implementing a self-directed program to ensure the convenient, safe, S. 1346 3 1 and environmentally sound take-back and recycling of photovoltaic 2 modules and their components and materials. By December thirty-first, 3 two thousand twenty-six, the department shall establish a process to 4 develop guidance for photovoltaic module stewardship plans by working 5 with manufacturers, stewardship organizations, and other stakeholders on 6 the content, review, and approval of stewardship plans. 7 2. A manufacturer shall annually review and amend, if necessary, its 8 stewardship plan. The department shall approve the amendment if it meets 9 the requirements for plan approval outlined in the department's guid- 10 ance. 11 3. A stewardship organization may be designated to act as an agent on 12 behalf of a manufacturer or manufacturers in implementing and operating 13 the stewardship program required under this title. The department shall 14 develop rules and guidance for adherence to such designation. 15 § 27-3405. Manufacturer stewardship plans. 16 1. Each manufacturer shall prepare and submit a stewardship plan to 17 the department by the later of January first, two thousand twenty-eight, 18 or within thirty days of its first sale of a photovoltaic module in or 19 into the state. A manufacturer shall implement the stewardship plan. 20 2. A stewardship plan shall, at a minimum: 21 (a) describe how manufacturers will finance the take-back and recycl- 22 ing system, and include an adequate funding mechanism to finance the 23 costs of collection, management, and recycling of photovoltaic modules 24 and residuals sold in or into the state by the manufacturer with a mech- 25 anism that ensures that photovoltaic modules can be delivered to take- 26 back locations without cost to the last owner or holder; 27 (b) describe how the program will minimize the release of hazardous 28 substances into the environment and maximize the recovery of other 29 components, including rare earth elements and commercially valuable 30 materials; 31 (c) provide for the take-back of photovoltaic modules at locations 32 that are within the region of the state in which the photovoltaic 33 modules were used and are as convenient as reasonably practicable, and 34 if no such location within the region of the state exists, include an 35 explanation for the lack of such location; 36 (d) provide a process for relevant stakeholders to receive information 37 required in order to participate in the program; and 38 (e) establish performance goals. 39 § 27-3407. Annual report. 40 Beginning December first, two thousand twenty-nine, and annually ther- 41 eafter, a manufacturer, or its designated stewardship organization, 42 shall provide to the department a report for the previous calendar year 43 that documents implementation of the plan, recommendations to the 44 department, and an assessment of its achievement of its performance 45 goals. Such report shall be posted on the manufacturer's publicly acces- 46 sible website. 47 § 27-3409. Enforcement. 48 The department may adopt rules as necessary for the purpose of imple- 49 menting, administering, and enforcing this title. 50 § 27-3411. Administrative fee. 51 The department may collect a flat fee from participating manufacturers 52 to recover costs associated with stewardship program and plan guidance, 53 review, and approval processes. All fees collected from manufacturers 54 under this title shall be deposited in the photovoltaic module recycling 55 account established pursuant to section ninety-nine-ss of the state 56 finance law.
