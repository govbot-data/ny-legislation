DILAN -- read once and referred to the Committee on Corporations, Authorities and Commissions AN ACT to amend the public service law, in relation to requiring cable television companies to issue credits for service outages The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The public service law is amended by adding a new section 2 224-d to read as follows: 3 § 224-d. Credit for service outage. 1. When a service outage affecting 4 any subscriber exists due to a particular service interruption cause, a 5 cable television company shall issue a credit allowance to such 6 subscriber pursuant to the provisions of subdivisions two, three and 7 four of this section. 8 2. If interruption continues for twenty-four hours or more, one thir- 9 tieth of the monthly rate for all services and facilities furnished and 10 billed by the service provider that are rendered useless or substantial- 11 ly impaired for each twenty-four-hour period or fraction thereof. 12 3. If a service interruption is for less than twenty-four hours, and 13 if credit is requested by the subscriber on the grounds that service for 14 the day was substantially impaired by such interruption or inter- 15 ruptions, then, a credit allowance will be issued for such day at one 16 thirtieth of the monthly rate. 17 4. If the service provider reasonably determines that the interruption 18 was in part caused by the loss of commercial power to the service 19 provider's network, the credit allowance period will begin twenty-four 20 hours after commercial power is restored. 21 5. The minimum credit for a service outage shall be equal to one thir- 22 tieth times the applicable monthly charge for each twenty-four-hour 23 period during which a service outage continues for at least four hours. 24 6. A cable television company shall be responsible for every service 25 outage except for interruption of programming to provide emergency EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
