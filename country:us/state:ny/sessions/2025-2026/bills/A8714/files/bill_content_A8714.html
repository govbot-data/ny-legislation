        AN ACT to amend the insurance law, in relation to requiring  the  super-
          intendent  of financial services to audit certain reports submitted by
          insurers  to ensure such insurers are in full compliance with  federal
          and state mental health and substance use disorder parity requirements
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Subsection (a) of section 343  of  the  insurance  law,  as
     2  added by chapter 207 of the laws of 2019, is amended to read as follows:
     3    (a)  (i)  Beginning  July  first,  two thousand nineteen and every two
     4  years thereafter, each insurer providing managed care products, individ-
     5  ual comprehensive accident and health  insurance  or  group  or  blanket
     6  comprehensive  accident and health insurance, each corporation organized
     7  pursuant to article forty-three of this chapter providing  comprehensive
     8  health insurance and each entity licensed pursuant to article forty-four
     9  of  the  public  health law providing comprehensive health service plans
    10  shall submit to the superintendent, in a form and manner  prescribed  by
    11  the  superintendent,  a  report  detailing  the entity's compliance with
    12  federal and state mental health and substance use disorder  parity  laws
    13  based  on  the  entity's record during the preceding two calendar years.
    14  The superintendent shall publish  on  the  department's  website  on  or
    15  before  October first, two thousand nineteen, and every two years there-
    16  after, the reports submitted pursuant to this section.  The  superinten-
    17  dent  shall  also publish on the department's website a summary of these
    18  reports to assist stakeholders and  the  general  public  in  reviewing,
    19  understanding  and  evaluating  any  data  compiled as part of the audit
    20  process including but not limited to a summary of the data  as  required

     1  by  paragraph  one  of subsection (b) of this section, comparison of the
     2  data provided by each insurer providing managed care products,  individ-
     3  ual  comprehensive accident and health  insurance  or  group  or blanket
     4  comprehensive  accident and health insurance, each corporation organized
     5  pursuant  to article forty-three of this chapter providing comprehensive
     6  health insurance and each entity licensed pursuant to article forty-four
     7  of  the  public  health law providing comprehensive health service plans
     8  for each, overall ranking for each insurer, major findings, any  correc-
     9  tive  actions  required  to be taken, any fines or penalties levied as a
    10  result, and recommendations to enhance compliance with federal and state
    11  mental health and substance use disorder  parity  laws.  The  department
    12  shall provide a copy of the report to the governor, the temporary presi-
    13  dent  of  the  senate,  the  speaker of the assembly, and members of the
    14  legislature.
    15    (ii) The superintendent shall audit each report submitted pursuant  to
    16  this section.  Such audit shall include, but not be limited to, an exam-
    17  ination  of each such report submitted by the insurer for the purpose of
    18  determining if such insurer is in full compliance with federal and state
    19  mental health and substance use disorder parity requirements.  In  addi-
    20  tion,  the  superintendent shall investigate all credible allegations of
    21  insurer improprieties and noncompliance. Where the superintendent  makes
    22  a  determination that an insurer has willfully violated federal or state
    23  mental health and substance use disorder parity requirements, the super-
    24  intendent shall provide such findings, including the reason  or  reasons
    25  for  making  such  determination, to the office of the attorney general.
    26  The attorney general shall consider the superintendent's  findings  and,
    27  upon  agreement  with the superintendent's determination, shall commence
    28  an action in the name of the people of the state to enforce an insurer's
    29  required compliance with federal and state mental health  and  substance
    30  use disorder parity requirements.
    31    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
