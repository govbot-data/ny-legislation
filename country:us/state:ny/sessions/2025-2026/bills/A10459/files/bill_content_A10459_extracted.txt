HYNDMAN -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Ways and Means AN ACT to amend the tax law, in relation to the donation of a human organ The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Paragraph 38 of subsection (c) of section 612 of the tax 2 law, as amended by section 2 of part S of chapter 59 of the laws of 3 2025, is amended to read as follows: 4 (38) For taxable years beginning before January first, two thousand 5 twenty-five, an amount of up to ten thousand dollars if a taxpayer, 6 while living, donates one or more of the taxpayer's human organs to 7 another human being for human organ transplantation. For purposes of 8 this paragraph, "human organ" means all or part of a liver, pancreas, 9 kidney, intestine, lung, or bone marrow. A subtract modification allowed 10 under this paragraph shall be claimed in the taxable year in which the 11 human organ transplantation occurs. Provided, however, that this 12 deduction shall not apply to any donation for which the taxpayer has 13 received benefits under section forty-three hundred seventy-one of the 14 public health law. 15 (A) A taxpayer shall claim the subtract modification allowed under 16 this paragraph only once and such subtract modification shall be claimed 17 for only the following unreimbursed expenses which are incurred by the 18 taxpayer or spouse of the taxpayer and related to the taxpayer's organ 19 donation: 20 (i) travel expenses; 21 (ii) lodging expenses; [and] 22 (iii) lost wages[.]; and 23 (iv) child care costs. 24 (B) The subtract modification allowed under this paragraph shall not 25 be claimed by a part-year resident or a non-resident of this state. 26 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately and shall be deemed to 27 have been in full force and effect on the same date as chapter 814 of 28 the laws of 2022 took effect.
