Blankenbush HONORING Trooper Nicole M. Duvall upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of the Superintendent's Commendation Award on May 7, 2025 WHEREAS, Law enforcement officers from the State of New York put their lives on the line every day for the citizens of this great Empire State; and WHERESA, The quality and sanctity of community life are cherished values, worthy of safeguarding, and this Legislative Body is moved to honor those individuals who serve to preserve and protect such values, lauding particularly their unwavering courage and dedication in the daily performance of their duties; and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and in full accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor Trooper Nicole M. Duvall upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of the Superintendent's Commendation Award to be celebrated at the New York State Police 49th Annual Superintendent's Commendation and Awards Day Ceremony on Thursday, May 7, 2025, at the State Police Academy in Albany, New York; and WHEREAS, This prestigious award was established to honor outstanding individuals, dedicated to their service to law enforcement, whose valiant efforts protect the people of New York State; and WHEREAS, Trooper Nicole M. Duvall of Rodman, New York, has been a member of the New York State Police since September 13, 2021; and WHEREAS, On March 28, 2024, Trooper Nicole M. Duvall along with fellow Trooper Benjamin Freeman responded to a structure fire in the Town of Richland, New York; upon arriving at the scene they found a 14-year-old girl in the front yard, who advised them that her mother was still unresponsive at the rear of the residence in a locked room; and WHEREAS, Filled with bravery, courage, and a strong sense of duty, Trooper Nicole M. Duvall and Trooper Benjamin Freeman entered the smoke-filled home, checking each room as they navigated through the residence; and WHEREAS, Halted by a locked door, the Troopers could hear a woman in the bedroom moaning, but attempts to direct her were unsuccessful; they proceeded to knock down the door and together they traversed through the room, blinded by smoke, until they located the unresponsive woman with their hands; and WHEREAS, Despite the conditions, Trooper Nicole M. Duvall and Trooper Benjamin Freeman managed to physically remove the woman from the bed and assisted her out of the residence, leading her to be evaluated by Emergency Medical Services and transported to the hospital for further treatment; and WHEREAS, The quick thinking, calm demeanor under pressure, bravery, and exemplary performance of duty of Trooper Nicole M. Duvall when faced with a chaotic scene, such as the one on March 28, 2024, is in the highest traditions and standards of the New York State Police and is worthy of both praise and honor; and WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body that those dedicated public servants who unselfishly devote their lives to the preservation of order and the protection of others are worthy and due full praise for their commitment and noble endeavors; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to honor Trooper Nicole M. Duvall upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of the Superintendent's Commendation Award; and be it further RESOVLED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Trooper Nicole M. Duvall. Go to top Sitemap Request Information Directions and Maps
