and WHEREAS, It is with profound sorrow and deep regret that this Legislative Body records the passing of Richard Brian Cassolino of Peekskill, New York, a beloved husband, father, grandfather, veteran, and friend, who died on Thursday, April 30, 2025, at the age of 74; and WHEREAS, Richard Brian Cassolino was born on May 30, 1950, in Southington, Connecticut, the cherished son of Elizabeth (McGinn) Cassolino and the late Richard Cassolino; and WHEREAS, Known to many as "Rick," he brought joy and vitality to all who knew him through his musical talents, adventurous spirit, and unwavering enthusiasm; and WHEREAS, A proud veteran of the United States Coast Guard, Rick Cassolino's years of service were marked by a commitment to duty and a love for the sea that continued throughout his life, including memorable trips aboard his beloved sailboat, the Outlandish, to Block Island and beyond; and WHEREAS, Richard Brian Cassolino was the devoted husband of Virginia Cassolino, the loving father of David Cassolino, Kathleen Barnett, and Christine Cassolino, and the adored grandfather of Eliza Barnett; he is also survived by his brother, Brent Cassolino, and a host of extended family members and friends whose lives were touched by his guidance, humor, and generous spirit; and WHEREAS, Richard Brian Cassolino's infinite selflessness and benevolence will shine on through his family's vivid and happy memories; his musicality, storytelling, and sense of leadership brought people together and left an indelible mark on his family and community; and WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of compassion, Richard Brian Cassolino leaves behind a legacy which will long endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all who were privileged to have known and loved such an amazing man; he will be deeply missed and truly merits the grateful tribute of this Legislative Body; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of Richard Brian Cassolino, and to express its deepest condolences to his family; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Richard Brian Cassolino.
