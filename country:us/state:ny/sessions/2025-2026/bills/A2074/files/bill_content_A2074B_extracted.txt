AN ACT to amend the executive law and the public officers law, in
relation to the sealing of certain claims against law enforcement
officers
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Legislative intent. The legislature finds and declares that
2 it is of critical importance that law enforcement officers who dutifully
3 serve our communities shall always have available to them the opportu-
4 nity to, among other things, secure loans for homeownership, pursue
5 further employment, and participate fully in the life of the community
6 in which they reside. The legislature further finds that the prospect of
7 their being able to do the aforementioned is dampened by the fact that
8 currently, an unfounded or exonerated claim made against a law enforce-
9 ment officer remains in their respective personnel record, and, absent
10 language in their applicable collective bargaining agreement, there
11 exists no method of recourse for them to have such claims removed from
12 their record. Unfounded claims are those claims that are made against
13 law enforcement officers that, upon investigation, are determined to
14 lack factual basis and are dismissed without disciplinary actions or
15 criminal charges; exonerated claims are claims that are made against a
16 law enforcement officer where the alleged conduct occurred but was
17 determined to be lawful. It is, therefore, the intent of the legislature

1 to strike a delicate balance between maintaining transparency regarding
2 previous conduct of law enforcement officers, how police departments
3 investigate and adjudicate claims of misconduct, and policing procedures
4 more generally, and ensuring that unfounded and exonerated claims made
5 against law enforcement officers do not unjustly cast a permanent shadow
6 over an otherwise admirable career in public service.
7 § 2. The executive law is amended by adding a new section 845-f to
8 read as follows:
9 § 845-f. Sealing of certain claims against law enforcement officers.
10 1. Definitions. For purposes of this section, the following terms shall
11 have the following meanings:
12 (a) "Unfounded claim" shall mean any complaint or allegation made
13 against a law enforcement officer that, upon investigation, is deter-
14 mined to lack factual basis and is dismissed without disciplinary action
15 or criminal charges.
16 (b) "Exonerated claim" shall mean any complaint or allegation made
17 against a law enforcement officer where the alleged conduct occurred but
18 was deemed lawful and proper.
19 (c) "Pending claim" shall mean any complaint or allegation made
20 against a law enforcement officer for which no final determination has
21 been made regarding such complaint or allegation.
22 (d) "Law enforcement officer" shall mean a police officer as defined
23 in subdivision thirty-four of section 1.20 of the criminal procedure
24 law.
25 (e) "Seal" or "sealing" shall mean the process by which records of
26 unfounded claims are rendered inaccessible to the public, with limited
27 exceptions as set forth in this section.
28 2. Sealing of unfounded claims, exonerated claims, and pending claims.
29 (a) Any unfounded claim made against a law enforcement officer shall be
30 automatically sealed upon final determination of unfounded status.
31 (b) Any exonerated claim made against a law enforcement officer shall
32 be automatically sealed upon final determination of exonerated status.
33 (c) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, pend-
34 ing claims shall be sealed.
35 3. Exceptions and access to sealed records. (a) Sealed records of
36 unfounded, exonerated, or pending claims shall remain accessible to:
37 (i) law enforcement agencies for the purpose of internal investi-
38 gations or audits;
39 (ii) the New York state attorney general's office and district attor-
40 neys for prosecutorial purposes, if relevant to a criminal investi-
41 gation; and
42 (iii) the civilian complaint review board or other authorized over-
43 sight bodies during an active investigation involving an officer.
44 (b) Sealed records shall not be disclosed or made available to the
45 public, media, or any non-authorized entities.
46 4. Implementation and responsibilities. (a) The division shall be
47 responsible for overseeing the implementation of this section, including
48 but not limited to:
49 (i) ensuring that all eligible unfounded, exonerated, or pending
50 claims are sealed in accordance with this section; and
51 (ii) maintaining a secure and confidential system for storing and
52 accessing sealed records in accordance with this section.
53 (b) Law enforcement agencies shall be required to update their records
54 management systems to comply with the provisions of this section and to
55 report compliance to the division annually.
