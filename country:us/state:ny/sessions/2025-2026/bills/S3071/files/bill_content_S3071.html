        AN  ACT  to  amend the education law, in relation to establishing a task
          force on safety in school transportation; and providing for the repeal
          of such provisions upon expiration thereof
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The education law is amended by adding a new section 3639
     2  to read as follows:
     3    § 3639. Task force on safety in school  transportation.  1.  There  is
     4  hereby  created a task force on safety in school transportation with the
     5  purpose of reviewing and promoting safety in school transportation.
     6    2. The task force shall  consist  of  eighteen  members  appointed  as
     7  follows:  five members appointed by the governor, four members appointed
     8  by  the temporary president of the senate, and four members appointed by
     9  the speaker of the assembly, one member appointed by the minority leader
    10  of the senate, and one member appointed by the minority  leader  of  the
    11  assembly.    The  task  force shall also include the commissioner or the
    12  commissioner's designee; the  commissioner  of  transportation  or  such
    13  commissioner's designee; and the commissioner of the department of motor
    14  vehicles  or such commissioner's designee.  The members appointed to the
    15  task force shall include, but not be limited to:  education and communi-
    16  ty stakeholders; parents; teachers; administrators; and school bus driv-
    17  ers and other school bus personnel who are members of a labor union.
    18    3. It shall be the duty of the task force to study and issue recommen-
    19  dations related to student safety in  school  transportation.  The  task
    20  force shall review and make recommendations on:

     1    a.  existing state laws, regulations, and programs that address school
     2  bus safety, and make recommendations for  their  improvement,  including
     3  any recommendations necessary to ensure pedestrian safety and the safety
     4  of students;
     5    b. examining the possibility of requiring a state department or office
     6  to  conduct a biannual review of all aspects of school bus safety and to
     7  issue recommendations for additional action or oversight,  as  appropri-
     8  ate;
     9    c. developing a plan to phase in any school bus safety measures recom-
    10  mended  by  the  task force through retrofitting and prospective vehicle
    11  purchases, and identify the  potential  application  of  vehicle  sensor
    12  technology to improve school bus safety;
    13    d. identifying best practices for the designation of school bus stops;
    14    e.  the  current safety status of students who walk to and from school
    15  and  identify  strategies  that  support  safe  pedestrian  behavior  by
    16  students  between their homes and schools, including as they walk to and
    17  from bus stops and board and exit buses;
    18    f. identifying safety protocols to be followed by school  bus  drivers
    19  and  school bus aides, including safeguards to ensure that protocols are
    20  followed when a substitute driver or school bus aide is  assigned  to  a
    21  school bus;
    22    g. developing recommendations to curb illegal passing of school buses;
    23  and
    24    h.  determining  if  there  is  a need for additional public education
    25  programs that promote school bus safety.
    26    4.  Vacancies in the membership of the task force shall be  filled  in
    27  the  same  manner as the original appointments were made. The members of
    28  the task force shall serve without compensation but  may  be  reimbursed
    29  for necessary expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.
    30    5.  The  task  force  shall  organize as soon as is practical upon the
    31  appointment of a majority of its members.  The chair of the  task  force
    32  shall be designated by a simple majority vote of all members of the task
    33  force.
    34    6.  The department shall provide staff and related support services to
    35  the task force. The task force shall also be entitled to call  upon  the
    36  services  of  any state, county, or municipal department, board, commis-
    37  sion or agency, as may be available to it for  these  purposes,  and  to
    38  incur  such  traveling  and  other miscellaneous expenses as it may deem
    39  necessary for the proper execution of its duties and as  may  be  within
    40  the  limit  of  funds appropriated or otherwise made available to it for
    41  these purposes. The task force may meet and hold hearings at such places
    42  as it shall designate.
    43    7.  The task force shall create a final report regarding all  findings
    44  and  recommendations to be delivered no later than eighteen months after
    45  the effective date of this section, to the governor, the temporary pres-
    46  ident of the senate, the speaker of  the  assembly,  the  chair  of  the
    47  senate  education committee, the chair of the assembly education commit-
    48  tee, the chair of the senate transportation committee and the  chair  of
    49  the assembly transportation committee.
    50    §  2.  This  act shall take effect immediately and shall expire and be
    51  deemed repealed twenty-four months after it shall have become a law.
