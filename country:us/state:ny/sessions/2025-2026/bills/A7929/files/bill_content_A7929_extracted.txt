CRUZ, FALL, RIVERA, LUNSFORD, MEEKS, FORREST, JACKSON, CLARK, GONZALEZ-ROJAS, EPSTEIN, ZINERMAN, GALLAGHER, ROSEN- THAL -- read once and referred to the Committee on Consumer Affairs and Protection -- committee discharged, bill amended, ordered reprinted as amended and recommitted to said committee AN ACT to amend the general business law, in relation to prohibiting food stores and retail establishments from refusing to accept payment in cash The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The general business law is amended by adding a new section 2 396-ii to read as follows: 3 § 396-ii. Cashless policies prohibited. a. As used in this section, 4 the following terms shall have the following meanings: 5 (i) "Cash" means United States coins and currency, including federal 6 reserve notes. Cash does not include foreign currency; any paper instru- 7 ment other than a federal reserve note, including, but not limited to, 8 any check, bond, or promissory note; or any foreign metal coin. 9 (ii) "Consumer commodity" means any article, good, merchandise, prod- 10 uct or commodity of any kind or class produced, distributed or offered 11 for retail sale for consumption by individuals, or for personal, house- 12 hold or family purposes. 13 (iii) "Food store" means an establishment which gives or offers for 14 sale food or beverages to the public for consumption or use on or off 15 the premises, or on or off a pushcart, stand or vehicle. 16 (iv) "Retail establishment" means an establishment wherein consumer 17 commodities are sold, displayed or offered for sale, or where services 18 are provided to consumers at retail. This definition does not include 19 banks or trust companies as such terms are defined in section two of the 20 banking law. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. LBD00727-02-5 A. 7929--A 2 1 b. It shall be unlawful for a food store or a retail establishment to 2 refuse to accept payment in cash from consumers, provided that such food 3 store or retail establishment may refuse to accept payment: 4 (i) In cash bills denominated above twenty dollars; or 5 (ii) In cash for any telephone, mail, or internet-based transaction, 6 unless the payment for such transaction takes place on the premises of 7 such food store or retail establishment. 8 c. No food store or retail establishment shall charge a higher price 9 for the same consumer commodity to a consumer who pays in cash than to a 10 consumer who pays for such commodity through a cash-less transaction. 11 d. A food store or retail establishment that violates this section is 12 liable for a civil penalty of not more than one thousand dollars for the 13 first violation and a civil penalty of not more than one thousand five 14 hundred dollars for each succeeding violation. 15 e. (i) This section shall not apply to a food store or retail estab- 16 lishment that provides a device on premises that converts cash, without 17 charging a fee or requiring a minimum deposit amount greater than one 18 dollar, into a prepaid card that allows a consumer to complete a trans- 19 action at such food store or retail establishment. 20 (ii) Upon request, such device shall provide each consumer with a 21 receipt indicating the amount of cash such consumer deposited onto the 22 prepaid card. 23 (iii) Cash deposits onto such a prepaid card shall not be subject to 24 an expiration date and there shall be no limit on the number of trans- 25 actions that may be completed on such prepaid card. 26 (iv) In the event that such device malfunctions, the food store or 27 retail establishment where such device is located shall accept payment 28 in cash from consumers throughout the time period in which such device 29 does not function in accordance with the standards set forth in this 30 section. Such food store or retail establishment shall place a conspic- 31 uous sign on or immediately adjacent to such device indicating that such 32 food store or retail establishment is required by law to accept cash if 33 such device malfunctions, and that consumers may report any violation to 34 the department of state division of consumer protection. The department 35 of state may establish by rule additional requirements relating to such 36 signs and the display of such signs. 37 § 2.
