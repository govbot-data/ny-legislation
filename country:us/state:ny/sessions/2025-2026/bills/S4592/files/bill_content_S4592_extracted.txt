GIANARIS, HOYLMAN-SIGAL, SCARCELLA-SPANTON, SEPULVE- DA -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Codes AN ACT to amend the criminal procedure law, in relation to allowing evidence of a defendant's prior crimes or acts to be admissible in a criminal case for certain purposes The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The criminal procedure law is amended by adding a new 2 section 60.77 to read as follows: 3 § 60.77 Rules of evidence; admissibility of other crimes or acts. 4 1. In general, evidence of other crimes, wrongs, or acts is not admis- 5 sible to prove the character of a person in order to show action in 6 conformity therewith. It may, however, be admissible for other purposes, 7 such as proof of motive, opportunity, intent, preparation, plan, know- 8 ledge, state of mind of the defendant, state of mind of the victim or 9 other party, absence of mistake or accident, identity, modus operandi, 10 or common scheme or plan, provided that the prosecution in a criminal 11 case shall provide reasonable notice in advance of trial, or during 12 trial if the court excuses pretrial notice on good cause shown, of the 13 general nature of any such evidence it intends to introduce at trial. 14 2. In a criminal case in which the defendant is accused of a sexual 15 offense, evidence of the defendant's commission of another sexual 16 offense or offenses is admissible, and may be considered for its bearing 17 on any matter to which it is relevant. This rule shall not be construed 18 to limit the admission or consideration of evidence under any other 19 rule. 20 3. The court, in its discretion, may exclude evidence described in 21 this section if its probative value is outweighed by the probability 22 that its admission will create undue prejudice to the defendant.
