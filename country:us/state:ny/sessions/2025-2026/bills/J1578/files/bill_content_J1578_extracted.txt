Bill No.: Summary   Actions   Committee&nbspVotes   Floor&nbspVotes   Memo   Text   LFIN   Chamber&nbspVideo/Transcript J01578 Summary:BILL NOJ01578  SAME ASNo Same As SPONSORGRIFFO COSPNSR MLTSPNSR  Go to topJ01578 Text: Senate Resolution No. 1578 BY: Senator GRIFFO MEMORIALIZING Governor Kathy Hochul to proclaim April, 2026, as Parkinson's Awareness Month in the State of New York WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to set aside official days established to increase awareness of serious health issues that affect the lives of New Yorkers; and WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body to memorialize Governor Kathy Hochul to proclaim April, 2026, as Parkinson's Awareness Month in the State of New York; and WHEREAS, Parkinson's disease is a progressive neurological disorder that affects millions of individuals worldwide, including thousands of New Yorkers, causing a wide range of debilitating symptoms that impact the ability to move, communicate, and perform basic daily activities; and WHEREAS, According to the New York State Department of Health, the Parkinson's Foundation, and the World Health Organization, Parkinson's disease affects more than 65,000 people in New York State, one million people in the United States, and over 10 million people around the world; and WHEREAS, Parkinson's disease does not discriminate by age, race, gender, or socioeconomic status, but can affect people of all walks of life, and those living with the disease often face challenges that require medical care, family support, and community engagement; and WHEREAS, April is recognized annually as Parkinson's Awareness Month, a month to raise awareness, promote understanding, and celebrate the resilience of individuals living with Parkinson's disease; and WHEREAS, Acknowledging Parkinson's disease and raising public awareness is an essential part of reducing the stigma surrounding the condition, promoting research for better treatments, and supporting those affected by it, including their families and caregivers; and WHEREAS, The State of New York joins Community Wellness Partners and other organizations throughout the State in committing to supporting those affected by Parkinson's disease and their families; and WHEREAS, During the month of April, 2026, the global Parkinson's community will unite through the 'Spark the Night' campaign, a worldwide initiative dedicated to raising awareness for Parkinson's disease by lighting up buildings, bridges, and landmarks in blue; and WHEREAS, Increased research, education, and community support services are crucial to improving the lives of people with Parkinson's and finding a cure; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to memorialize Governor Kathy Hochul to proclaim April, 2026, as Parkinson's Awareness Month in the State of New York; and be it further RESOLVED, That copies of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to The Honorable Kathy Hochul, Governor of the State of New York; and Community Wellness Partners.
