A06936 Summary:

BILL NOA06936A
 
SAME ASNo Same As
 
SPONSORFall

MLTSPNSR
 
Amd §§3, 64 & 106, ABC L
 
Authorizes a certain property operating as a private membership establishment to sell alcohol for on-premises consumption to members and their guests; defines "private membership establishment"; provides that such provisions shall not be interpreted to authorize any form of discrimination.

        AN ACT to amend the alcoholic  beverage  control  law,  in  relation  to
          authorizing  a  certain  property  operating  as  a private membership
          establishment to sell alcohol for on-premises consumption  to  members
          and their guests
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Section 3 of the alcoholic beverage control law is  amended
     2  by adding a new subdivision 39 to read as follows:
     3    39.  "Private  membership establishment" means an association operated
     4  by an individual, corporation, partnership, company, or any  other  for-
     5  profit entity which is the owner, lessee, or occupant of a premises used
     6  exclusively for such association's purposes, and which is operated sole-
     7  ly  for a recreational, social, communal workspace or athletic purposes.
     8  A "member" of a private membership establishment  shall  mean  a  person
     9  who,  whether a charter member or admitted in agreement with the by-laws
    10  of such private membership establishment, has become a bona fide  member
    11  thereof,  who  maintains  the private membership by the payment of their
    12  annual dues in a bona fide manner in accordance  with  the  by-laws  and
    13  whose  name and address is entered on such private membership establish-
    14  ment's list of members. A private membership establishment must have one
    15  hundred or more members and  shall  be  subject  to  the  provisions  of
    16  section  one  hundred ten of this chapter. Private membership establish-
    17  ments may have ticketed events for their members or members' guests, but
    18  a business or facility that sells tickets to the  general  public  shall
    19  not be considered a private membership establishment.
    20    §  2.  Subdivision  5  of section 64 of the alcoholic beverage control
