BILL NOS01473

SPONSORBORRELLO

MLTSPNSR
 
Amd §§35.15, 35.05, 35.10 & 120.14, Pen L; amd §§140.10 & 180.10, CP L; amd R3211, add §3012-c, CPLR
 
Relates to the justified use of physical force; removes requirement that a person facing an imminent physical threat must retreat; provides immunity from civil and criminal liability.

        AN  ACT to amend the penal law, the criminal procedure law and the civil
          practice law and rules, in relation to the justified use  of  physical
          force

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Section 35.15 of the penal law, as added by chapter  73  of
     2  the  laws of 1968, subdivisions 1 and 2 as amended by chapter 511 of the
     3  laws of 2004, paragraph (b) of subdivision 2 as amended by chapter 23 of
     4  the laws of 2024, is amended to read as follows:
     5  § 35.15 Justification; use of physical force in defense of a person.
     6    1. A person may, subject to the provisions of subdivision two of  this
     7  section,  use  physical force upon another person when and to the extent
     8  [he or she] such person reasonably believes  such  to  be  necessary  to
     9  defend  [himself,  herself]  themself or a third person from what [he or
    10  she] such person reasonably believes to be the use or  imminent  use  of
    11  unlawful physical force by such other person, unless:
    12    (a)  The  latter's  conduct  was  provoked by the actor with intent to
    13  cause physical injury to another person; or
    14    (b) The actor was the initial aggressor; except that in such case  the
    15  use of physical force is nevertheless justifiable if the actor has with-
    16  drawn from the encounter and effectively communicated such withdrawal to
    17  such  other person but the latter persists in continuing the incident by
    18  the use or threatened imminent use of unlawful physical force; or
    19    (c) The physical force involved is the product of a combat  by  agree-
    20  ment not specifically authorized by law.
    21    2.  A  person  may  not  use deadly physical force upon another person
    22  under circumstances specified in subdivision one of this section unless:

    48    4.  No person shall be arrested pursuant to this section for a use  of

     3    §  7.  Rule  3211  of  the  civil practice law and rules is amended by

    18    §  8.  The  civil  practice  law  and rules is amended by adding a new
    19  section 3012-c to read as follows:
    20    § 3012-c. Certificate of merit in certain actions to  recover  damages
    21  for  personal  injury,  injury to property or wrongful death. (a) In any
    22  action to recover damages for personal injury,  injury  to  property  or
    23  wrongful  death  brought  by an individual who committed a crime against
    24  the respondent, where the injuries complained of resulted from a use  of
    25  physical force or deadly physical force by the respondent at the time of
    26  the  commission  of  the  crime, the complaint shall be accompanied by a
    27  certificate, signed by the attorney for the plaintiff,  certifying  that
    28  the attorney has reviewed the facts of the case and that, to the best of
    29  such  attorney's knowledge, information and belief there is a reasonable
    30  basis for the commencement of such action and that  the  respondent  was
    31  not  justified  in  the  use  of physical force or deadly physical force
    32  against the plaintiff.
    33    (b) Where a certificate is required pursuant to this section, a single
    34  certificate shall be filed for each action even if more than one defend-
    35  ant has been named in the complaint or is subsequently named.
    36    (c) If the attorney for the plaintiff fails to provide the certificate
    37  as required by subdivision (a) of this section the court may dismiss the
    38  complaint or make such final or conditional order with  regard  to  such
    39  failure as is just including but not limited to denial of the accrual of
    40  any  interest,  costs,  attorneys'  fees and other fees, relating to the
    41  underlying mortgage debt. Any such dismissal shall be without  prejudice
    42  and shall not be on the merits.
