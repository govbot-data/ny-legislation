and WHEREAS, GianRaul Moreno Olivo of White Plains, New York, died on Sunday, January 25, 2026, at the age of 13; and WHEREAS, GianRaul Moreno Olivo was born on May 18, 2012, to his adoring parents, Denise Olivo and Raul Moreno, in East Meadow, New York; and WHEREAS, In the 13 years he graced this earth, GianRaul Moreno Olivo touched the lives of everyone he met; he filled every space he entered with connection, determination, and an unmistakable presence; and WHEREAS, GianRaul Moreno Olivo was an adored 8th grade student at Highland Middle School, and he was a proud member of the Purchase College Men's Lacrosse Team through Team IMPACT, an active participant at Breakthrough Physical Therapy, a two-year participant in the Run to Breakthrough 100-mile run, and a participant in the St. Pius X Catechism program; and WHEREAS, Through his involvements, GianRaul Moreno Olivo found belonging and friendship in the activities he enjoyed; and WHEREAS, GianRaul Moreno Olivo found happiness in the simple parts of life, and he was happiest when he was spending time with people he loved, watching his favorite movies, sliding, swimming in wave pools, splashing at splash pads, visiting the beach, and going on long walks; and WHEREAS, Above all, GianRaul Moreno Olivo loved to be around people, and he was known to cherish the friendships he made at school and enjoy the close bonds he had with friends such as Siena, Kevin, and his brothers; and WHEREAS, GianRaul Moreno Olivo will be remembered for his strong personality and clear expectations; despite obstacles, he always made sure his voice was heard, and his meaning, emotions, and presence were always unmistakably clear; and WHEREAS, Loving and caring for GianRaul Moreno Olivo taught his family how to listen without hearing words, how to smile through obstacles, how to move through hardship with grace, and how to love through all experiences in life; and WHEREAS, GianRaul Moreno Olivo is survived by his loving parents, Denise Olivo and Raul Moreno; his four brothers, Alexander Moreno-Crestejo, Collin Moreno-Crestejo, Luis-Antonio Moreno Olivo, and Rafael Moreno Olivo; his grandmother, Elsa Olivo; his aunts and uncles, Steve Yozcik, Linette Olivo, Elizabeth Richards, Jessica Adkisson, Hassan Alyassin, and Edward Alyassin; his cousins, Andrew Yozcik, Makaiya Adkisson, Christian Yozcik, Steven Yozcik, Marcos Valazques, Morgan Richards, Lilianna Valazques, and Jax Richards; his devoted caregivers and friends, Kevin Emanuel and Ada Eze; and many friends and classmates; and WHEREAS, GianRaul Moreno Olivo's infinite kindness and joy will shine on through the memories his family and friends have of him; his strength and resilience will forever serve as a beacon of love, light, and hope to the countless lives he touched; and WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of compassion, GianRaul Moreno Olivo leaves behind a legacy which will long endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all who were privileged to have known and loved such an amazing young man; he will be deeply missed and truly merits the grateful tribute of this Legislative Body; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the untimely death of GianRaul Yiango Moreno Olivo, and to express its deepest condolences to his family; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of GianRaul Yiango Moreno Olivo.
