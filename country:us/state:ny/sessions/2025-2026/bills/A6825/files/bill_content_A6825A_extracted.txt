AN ACT to amend the agriculture and markets law, in relation to fees
related to liquified petroleum
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Section 192-e of the agriculture and markets law is amended
2 by adding five new subdivisions 10, 11, 12, 13 and 14 to read as
3 follows:
4 10. A seller shall not misrepresent the nature of any fee, nor collect
5 from a consumer:
6 (a) a special usage fee that is greater than a nominal value. For the
7 purposes of this section, "special usage fee" shall mean a fee charged
8 to a consumer for using less than the minimum amount of liquified petro-
9 leum gas as established by the seller. No special usage fee shall be
10 charged except for when: (i) the consumer's usage of liquified petroleum
11 gas is low enough that the seller would otherwise be unable to make a
12 reasonable return on investment for providing liquified petroleum gas
13 service to that consumer; (ii) the special usage fee shall be disclosed
14 in the contract between the seller and the consumer; and (iii) a
15 description of the fee shall be provided on the consumer's bill pursuant
16 to subdivision twelve of this section;
17 (b) a fee for liquified petroleum gas that is not actually delivered
18 to a consumer;
19 (c) a fee that is not disclosed in the contract between the seller and
20 the consumer related to termination of liquified petroleum gas service
21 at the agreed upon end of contract between the seller and the consumer
22 that is greater than the cost of labor associated with the termination
23 of service, including a fee that is greater than the cost of labor to:

1 (i) remove the seller's liquified petroleum gas tank from the prem-
2 ises;
3 (ii) pump out or restock liquified petroleum gas; or
4 (iii) terminate service;
5 (d) a fee related to termination of liquified petroleum gas service
6 prior to the agreed upon end of a contract between the seller and the
7 consumer, that is (i) greater than a nominal value, or (ii) greater than
8 the cost of labor;
9 (e) a tank rental fee for any period of time after the service has
10 been disconnected by the seller or the consumer has notified the seller
11 that the seller's equipment is no longer connected;
12 (f) any amounts for the repair of equipment or for any special trip
13 related to the repair of equipment if the equipment owned by a seller
14 malfunctions through no fault of a consumer or other person authorized
15 to be on the premises;
16 (g) a delivery fee if the seller fails to make a delivery through no
17 fault of the consumer and the consumer runs out of liquified petroleum
18 gas as a result; or
19 (h) a fee, the basis of which is not compliant with any applicable
20 statute or rule.
21 11. A seller shall provide a rebate to a consumer equal to the price
22 paid by that consumer for liquified petroleum gas that is removed from a
23 liquified petroleum gas tank by the seller and resold.
24 12. A seller of liquified petroleum gas shall provide on each bill a
25 description of the nature and purpose of each fee that is being charged.
26 13. The fiscal impact of being compliant with any governmental, regu-
27 latory, or environmental requirement shall not be borne by liquified
28 petroleum gas consumers and shall not be added to bills rendered by a
29 company selling liquified petroleum gas; provided, however, that this
30 subdivision shall not limit the per-gallon price charged by sellers or
31 prohibit collection of any tax allowable under state law.
32 14. The attorney general shall have jurisdiction to enforce the
33 provisions of this section in accordance with the powers granted to the
34 attorney general by section sixty-three of the executive law.
35 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
