Section 606 of the tax law is amended by adding a new 2 subsection (b-1) to read as follows: 3 (b-1) Renters' and small homeowners' credit in a city with a popu- 4 lation of one million or more. 5 (1) For the purposes of this subsection: 6 (A) "Qualified taxpayer" means a resident individual who lives in a 7 city with a population of one million or more who has occupied and paid 8 rent for such individual's primary residence in such city for six months 9 or more of the taxable year, is required or chooses to file a return 10 under this article, and (i) is sixty-five years of age or older, (ii) is 11 filing a joint return with a spouse who is sixty-five years of age or 12 older, (iii) is a head of household, (iv) is a married individual filing 13 a joint return with a spouse and has at least one dependent, (v) is a 14 married individual filing a separate return and has at least one depend- 15 ent, or (vi) is a surviving spouse and has at least one dependent. For 16 purposes of this subsection "qualified taxpayer" shall also include the 17 owner of any dwelling with six units or less in a city with a population 18 of one million or more who occupies such dwelling as their primary resi- 19 dence for six months or more of the taxable year and who is required or 20 chooses to file a return under this article. An individual cannot be a 21 qualified taxpayer if such individual is an individual with respect to 22 whom a deduction under subsection (c) of section 151 of the internal 23 revenue code is allowable to another taxpayer for the taxable year or 24 pays rent for such individual's primary residence to a family member 25 sharing the same primary residence.
