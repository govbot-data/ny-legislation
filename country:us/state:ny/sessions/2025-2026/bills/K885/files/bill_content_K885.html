Assembly Resolution No. 885
 
BY: M. of A. Simpson
 
        CONGRATULATING  Lake  Luzerne Town Supervisor Gene
        Merlino upon the  occasion  of  his  retirement  and
        honoring  him  for  his distinguished service to the
        Town of Lake Luzerne and  the  people  of  New  York
        State
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is the sense of this Legislative Body to recognize and
commend  outstanding  public  servants  who  have  devoted  their  time,
energies,  and  expertise  to  improving  the  quality  of life in their
communities and who have faithfully served the State of  New  York  with
dedication and commitment; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This Legislative Body is justly proud to congratulate Lake
Luzerne  Town  Supervisor  Gene  Merlino  upon  the  occasion   of   his
retirement,  and to honor him for his exemplary record of service to the
Town of Lake Luzerne, Warren County, and the State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Gene Merlino first took office as Supervisor of the Town of
Lake Luzerne in 2006, following earlier service as a Town Councilman and
as a member of the Town Planning Board; throughout his  tenure,  he  has
been  known  for  his  steady leadership, practical vision, and constant
dedication to the needs of his community; and
 
  WHEREAS, Furthermore, Gene Merlino  brought  to  public  office  the
discipline and devotion to duty instilled during his service as a United
States Marine Corps Veteran; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Simultaneously with his responsibilities to the Town, Gene
Merlino was the innkeeper of the former Lamplight Inn Bed and Breakfast,
further  contributing  to  local  tourism,  hospitality,  and   economic
vitality; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Over  the course of nearly two decades as Supervisor, Gene
Merlino led  and  supported  numerous  transformative  initiatives  that
strengthened  the  Town  of  Lake  Luzerne,  improved  public  services,
enhanced community life, and ensured responsible stewardship  of  public
resources; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Among  his many accomplishments, Gene Merlino has cited as
one   of   his   proudest   achievements   the   renovation    of    the
19,000-square-foot  former bowling alley into a modern municipal complex
housing the Town Offices, Town Court, Senior Center, and  Warren  County
Meal  Site-completed  without  incurring  debt  and  creating a unified,
efficient home for essential services; and
 
  WHEREAS, Supervisor Merlino worked in  collaboration  with  the  New
York State Department of Environmental Conservation, the Adirondack Park
Agency,  the  Department  of  Health,  and the Town of Lake Luzerne Lake
Association to reduce and control invasive milfoil,  thereby  protecting
and preserving one of the Town's most vital natural resources; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Under  his  leadership,  the  construction of a cell tower
provided enhanced communications coverage, improving cell phone  service
 
and   strengthening   fire,   police,  and  emergency  medical  response
capabilities; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Supervisor  Merlino also oversaw the revitalization of the
former Town Hall by facilitating its long-term tenancy by the Adirondack
Folk School, which now provides cultural arts education and  contributes
significantly to local tourism and economic development; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Gene  Merlino  guided  the  renovation of the fire-damaged
Harmon House, transforming  it  into  a  welcoming  tourist  information
center  and  public  restroom  facility  in  the  downtown area, thereby
supporting local businesses and enhancing the visitor experience; and
 
  WHEREAS, Throughout his tenure, Gene Merlino remained a  responsible
fiscal  steward,  consistently  committed  to conservative budgeting and
careful tax management on behalf of Town residents; and
 
  WHEREAS, Gene Merlino  has  exemplified  the  highest  standards  of
public  service, generously devoting his time, experience, and steadfast
commitment to the  betterment  of  his  community;  his  leadership  has
produced  a  meaningful  and enduring impact on the Town of Lake Luzerne
and serves as a commendable example of dedicated municipal service; now,
therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
congratulate   Supervisor   Gene   Merlino  upon  the  occasion  of  his
retirement, to thank him for his many years of exemplary service, and to
extend to him its best wishes for health, happiness, and fulfillment  in
all his future endeavors; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Gene Merlino.
