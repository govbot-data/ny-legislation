    27  to  subdivision  two of this section shall be utilized for the following
    28  purposes:
    29    (a) the provision of financial assistance to first-time homebuyers who
    30  are residents of the town for the purchase of a first home. Such  finan-
    31  cial assistance may be in the form of a grant or a loan;
    32    (1)  A  town  may  provide  financial assistance for the purchase of a
    33  first home to a first-time homebuyer who is a resident of  the  town  or
    34  who  is  employed  in  the  town. A resident of the town shall include a
    35  person who is currently a resident of the town or a non-resident who has
    36  been a resident within the past five years.
    37    (2) Such financial assistance shall not exceed fifty  percent  of  the
    38  purchase price of the home.
    39    (3)  If  such financial assistance is in the form of a loan, such loan
    40  shall be repayable to the town pursuant to the terms agreed  to  between
    41  the recipient and the town, provided that any loan shall be fully repaid
    42  by the recipient upon the resale of the home.
    43    (4)  For the purposes of calculating town tax liability for such prop-
    44  erty, only, the dollar  amount  of  any  financial  assistance  for  the
    45  purchase of a first home made by the town pursuant to this section shall
    46  be subtracted from the full equalized assessed value of such property.
    47    (5)  All  revenues  received  by the town from the repayment of a loan
    48  shall be deposited in the fund.
    49    (6) A town may provide financial assistance for community  housing  in
    50  conjunction  with  a  public/private  partnership  for employer assisted
    51  housing.
    52    (b) the actual production of community housing for  sale  to  eligible
    53  individuals by the town;
    54    (c)  the  actual  production of community housing for sale to eligible
    55  individuals in conjunction with a public/private partnership, where  the
    56  private  partner  agrees to comply with the profit guidelines of the New

    20  the fund be transferred to any other account. Nothing contained in  this

    31  the construction, rehabilitation, purchase or rental of housing pursuant
    32  to  this  section.  All  revenues  received by the town from the sale or
    33  rental of community homes, or the repayment of loans shall be  deposited
    34  in the fund.

    36  community housing fund pursuant to this section shall create an advisory
    37  board to review and make recommendations regarding the town's  community
    38  housing  plan  required by subdivision seven of this section. Such board
    39  shall consist of not less than seven nor more than fifteen  legal  resi-
    40  dents  of  the  municipality  who  shall  serve without compensation. No
    41  member of the local legislative body shall serve on the board. The board
    42  shall include a representative of: (a) the  construction  industry;  (b)
    43  the  real estate industry; (c) the banking industry; and three represen-
    44  tatives of local housing advocacy or human services organizations. Where
    45  a village or villages, located within the town, have elected to  partic-
    46  ipate in the fund, as provided in subdivision seven of this section, the
    47  board  shall include at least one resident of a participating village or
    48  villages. Where an Indian nation is located within the boundaries  of  a
    49  town,  the board shall include at least one member from such nation. The
    50  board shall act in an advisory capacity to the town board.
    51    7. Adoption of housing plan. (a) Before the town may expend any  funds
    52  pursuant  to this section, the town board shall first adopt a town hous-
    53  ing plan which establishes an implementation plan for the  provision  of
    54  community  housing opportunities by the fund. Said plan shall be adopted
    55  by local law. Such plan shall adhere to the following smart growth prin-
