The social services law is amended by adding a new section 2 498 to read as follows: 3 § 498. Aversive conditioning. Notwithstanding any provision of law to 4 the contrary: 5 1. No program, agency or facility that is funded, operated, licensed, 6 or approved by the state or an agency or political subdivision of the 7 state shall administer or cause to be administered to a vulnerable 8 person any procedure which uses aversive conditioning. 9 2. No program, agency or facility that is funded, operated, licensed, 10 or approved by the state or an agency or political subdivision of the 11 state shall use any form of physical contact or punishment that is 12 otherwise prohibited by law, or would be prohibited if used on a person 13 who is not vulnerable. 14 3. Paragraph (e) of subdivision 1 of section 488 of the social 14 services law, as added by section 1 of part B of chapter 501 of the laws 15 of 2012, is amended to read as follows: 16 (e) "Use of aversive conditioning," which shall mean the application 17 of a physical stimulus that is intended to induce pain or discomfort in 18 order to modify or change the behavior of a person [receiving services 19 in the absence of a person-specific authorization by the operating, 20 licensing or certifying state agency pursuant to governing state agency 21 regulations]. Aversive conditioning may include but is not limited to, 22 the use of physical stimuli such as noxious odors, noxious tastes, 23 blindfolds, the withholding of meals and the provision of substitute 24 foods in an unpalatable form [and], movement limitations used as punish- 25 ment, including but not limited to helmets and mechanical restraint 26 devices and any procedure which causes obvious signs of physical pain, 27 including but not limited to hitting, pinching and electric shock. 28 § 3.
