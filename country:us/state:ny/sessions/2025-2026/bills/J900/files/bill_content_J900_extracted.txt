and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and in full accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to commemorate the 125th Anniversary of the New York State College of Ceramics (NYSCC) at Alfred University, to be celebrated with a myriad of events, including an exhibit opening reception entitled "History: A Legacy in Motion" on Thursday, May 8, 2025; and WHEREAS, This monumental exhibit will be on display from May 8, 2025, through December 14, 2025, displaying the works of over 23 artists who taught and continue to teach at this remarkable educational institution, showcasing works from 1900 through the current day; and WHEREAS, The New York State College of Ceramics at Alfred University has been a unique public/private partnership in higher education since its inception in 1900; the program attracts renowned artists and scientists as faculty in ceramics, glass, and other materials, while producing world-class alumni in engineering and groundbreaking artists across all media; and WHEREAS, 125 years ago, Charles Fergus Binns was invited to become the first director of a new initiative at Alfred University, the New York School of Clay-Working and Ceramics; the new director, along with his initial core of instructors, launched American studio ceramics and simultaneously transformed Alfred University into a renowned hub of creative ceramic activity that has persisted to this day; and WHEREAS, With a core understanding that interdisciplinary collaboration is pivotal to the modern student, the New York State College of Ceramics at Alfred University works closely with The School of Art & Design, the Inamori School of Engineering, and the Scholes Library to uphold the tradition of offering cutting-edge programs in a myriad of areas of study; and WHEREAS, Interdisciplinary education offers valuable benefits by fostering critical thinking, problem-solving, and a comprehensive understanding of complex issues; it encourages students to explore diverse viewpoints, evaluate conflicting perspectives and develop their own informed opinions, as well as exercise creativity and imaginative thinking in all facets of life; and WHEREAS, From groundbreaking ceramic, glass, and video art to advancements in materials science used in aerospace, medicine, and electronics, the NYSCC remains a global leader in its disciplines; and WHEREAS, Those dedicated educators, past and present, who have given of their time and energies to the field of education and to those in pursuit of such knowledge are most worthy of the highest praise and recognition of all citizens of this great State and Nation; and WHEREAS, Ceramics and art are at the root of human expression, connecting humanity to its earliest histories, bridging the gap between countless generations; the arts continue to develop and evolve, shaping new expressions of culture, identity, emotion, and our shared experiences, and are most worthy of our devout safeguarding and uplifting for years to come; and WHEREAS, Steeped in a proud and distinguished history, the New York State College of Ceramics at Alfred University may look back with pride upon its humble beginnings, find just satisfaction in its purposeful growth and hold true of purpose in its commitment to serving the educational needs of the citizens of New York; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to commemorate the 125th Anniversary of the New York State College of Ceramics at Alfred University; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the New York State College of Ceramics at Alfred University.
