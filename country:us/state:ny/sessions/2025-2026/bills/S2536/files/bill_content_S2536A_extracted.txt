AN ACT to amend the labor law, in relation to inclusion of certain off-
site custom fabrication as public work for the purposes of payment of
prevailing wage
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Subdivision 3 of section 220 of the labor law is amended
2 by adding a new paragraph (f) to read as follows:
3 (f) (i) For the purposes of this subdivision contractors or subcon-
4 tractors engaged in custom fabrication shall not be regarded as material
5 suppliers. For the purposes of this subdivision, a legal day's work for
6 which the prevailing rate of wages for the New York county in which the
7 public works project is situated shall be paid to laborers, workers and
8 mechanics shall include custom fabrication regardless of whether the
9 custom fabrication occurs on-site, off-site, or in another jurisdiction
10 outside the state where the custom fabrication constitutes a significant
11 portion of the building or work, it shall be a condition of the contract
12 concerning all such custom fabrication work that the contractor or
13 employer awarded the public work contract, except where the project
14 includes federal funding that triggers federal rules for wage require-
15 ments, and any subcontractor thereto shall pay the on-site prevailing
16 wage required for workers at the site of the public work project to
17 those workers performing the custom fabrication. To perform a public
18 works contract each contractor or subcontractor who employs workers

6 subdivision the term "custom fabrication" shall include, but not be

11 repair or renovation of a building which is the subject of a contract to

16 portions or modules of the building or work, as opposed to smaller
17 prefabricated components, are delivered to the place where the building
18 or work will remain, with minimal construction work remaining other than
19 the installation and/or assembly of the portions or modules.
20 (ii) The department of jurisdiction, as defined in subdivision three-a
21 of this section and including any entity to whom such department of
22 jurisdiction delegates their authority, shall be required to report to
23 the fiscal officer the intention to contract for custom, off-site fabri-
24 cation of any materials on all contracts, regardless of the amount,
25 which may include the employment of laborers, workers or mechanics in
26 the application to the fiscal officer for the approval of such contract.
27 The department of jurisdiction's report shall state the name and address
28 of the off-site, custom fabricator, identify the custom materials that
29 are being fabricated off-site and the quantity thereof, the estimated
30 and actual cost of the off-site fabrication, and the estimated number of
31 workers employed to complete the custom fabrication at the off-site
32 location.
33 § 2. This act shall take effect on the one hundred eightieth day after
34 it shall have become a law and shall apply only to those contracts put
35 out to bid on or after such effective date. Effective immediately, the
36 addition, amendment and/or repeal of any rule or regulation necessary
37 for the implementation of this act on its effective date are authorized
38 to be made and completed on or before such effective date.
