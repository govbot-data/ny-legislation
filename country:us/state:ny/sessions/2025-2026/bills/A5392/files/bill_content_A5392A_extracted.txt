rine nasal sprays

23 2. Possession and use. (a) Any eligible person or entity may purchase,

26 toms, under this section.

28 uals who have completed the training required by paragraph (c) of this
29 subdivision to be responsible for the storage, maintenance, control, and
30 general oversight of the epinephrine [auto-injectors] devices acquired
31 by the eligible person or entity.

33 an eligible person or entity unless [he or she has] they have success-
34 fully completed a training course in the use of epinephrine [auto-injec-

36 enced in training laypersons in emergency health treatment or by an
37 entity or individual approved by the commissioner, or is directed in a
38 specific instance to use an epinephrine [auto-injector] device by a
39 health care practitioner. The training required by this paragraph shall
40 include (i) how to recognize signs and symptoms of severe allergic
41 reactions, including anaphylaxis; (ii) recommended dosage for adults and
42 children; (iii) standards and procedures for the storage and adminis-
43 tration of an epinephrine [auto-injector] device; and (iv) emergency
44 follow-up procedures.
45 (d) This section does not prohibit the use of an epinephrine [auto-in-
46 jector] device (i) by a health care practitioner or (ii) by a person
47 acting pursuant to a lawful patient-specific prescription.
48 (e) Every eligible person and entity authorized to possess and use
49 epinephrine [auto-injector] devices pursuant to this section shall use,
50 maintain and dispose of such devices pursuant to regulations of the
51 department.
52 (f) Nothing in this section shall require any eligible person or enti-
53 ty to acquire, possess, store, make available, or administer an epineph-
54 rine [auto-injector] device.
