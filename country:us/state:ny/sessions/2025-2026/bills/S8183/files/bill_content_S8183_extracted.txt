MARTINS -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Local Government AN ACT to amend the village law and the public officers law, in relation to residency requirements for clerk-treasurer or deputy clerk in the village of Baxter Estates The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 3-300 of the village law is amended by adding a new 2 subdivision 36 to read as follows: 3 36. Notwithstanding any provisions of this chapter or any other gener- 4 al law, local law, ordinance or special act, the village board of trus- 5 tees of the incorporated village of Baxter Estates, Nassau county, may, 6 by resolution, provide that the office of village clerk-treasurer or 7 deputy clerk of such village may be held by a person who is not a resi- 8 dent of such village, provided that such person resides in the county of 9 Nassau or in an adjoining county within the state of New York. 10 § 2. Section 3 of the public officers law is amended by adding a new 11 subdivision 76 to read as follows: 12 76. In the village of Baxter Estates, in the county of Nassau, the 13 provisions of this section requiring a person to be a resident of the 14 political subdivision or municipal corporation of the state for which 15 such person shall be chosen or within which such person's official func- 16 tions are required to be exercised, shall not prevent such person from 17 holding the office of village clerk-treasurer or deputy clerk of the 18 village of Baxter Estates, provided that such person resides in Nassau 19 county or an adjoining county within the state of New York; provided, 20 however, any person performing such functions of village clerk-treasurer 21 or deputy clerk in any other village shall be a resident of such 22 village, unless otherwise provided by law. 23 § 3. This act shall take effect immediately. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
