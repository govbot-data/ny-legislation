BORRELLO -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Education AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to permitting schools located within the state to purchase certain New York milk The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The education law is amended by adding a new section 915-a 2 to read as follows: 3 § 915-a. Milk options. 1. As used in this section, the following terms 4 shall have the following meanings: 5 (a) "New York milk" shall mean raw milk produced by the milking of 6 cows physically located within the geographic boundaries of the state, 7 transported to a dairy processing facility located within the geographic 8 boundaries of the state and processed as fluid milk into containers 9 intended for distribution to consumers. 10 (b) "Reduced fat New York milk" shall mean New York milk containing 11 two percent milk fat. The term includes New York milk that has been 12 flavored. 13 (c) "Whole New York milk" shall mean New York milk containing at least 14 three percent milk fat. The term includes New York milk that has been 15 flavored. 16 2. Notwithstanding any other provision of law or regulation to the 17 contrary, a school board or other governing entity of a school located 18 in the state may elect to utilize funds from state or local sources to 19 obtain whole New York milk or reduced fat New York milk to provide or 20 sell at such school. 21 3. The commissioner of education shall notify the superintendent or 22 chief administrator of each school in the state of the provisions of 23 this section. 24 4. The attorney general shall, on behalf of a school located within 25 the state, bring a civil action against the federal government or any EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. This act shall take effect on the thirtieth day after it shall 19 have become a law and shall expire and be deemed repealed upon the 20 occurrence of either of the following: 21 1. The amendment or repeal of section 9(a)(2)(A) of the National 22 School Lunch Act (60 Stat. 230, 42 U.S.C. § 1758(a)(2)(A)), which 23 results in the availability of whole milk or reduced fat milk, flavored 24 or unflavored, in elementary and secondary schools in the state. 25 2. An update to the Dietary Guidelines for Americans published under 26 section 301 of the National Nutrition Monitoring and Related Research 27 Act of 1990 (Public Law 101-445, 7 U.S.C. § 5341), which permits the 28 availability of whole milk or reduced fat milk, flavored or unflavored, 29 in elementary and secondary schools in the state. 30 Provided, that the commissioner of education shall notify the legisla- 31 tive bill drafting commission upon the occurrence of either event 32 described in subdivisions one or two of this section in order that the 33 commission may maintain an accurate and timely effective data base of 34 the official text of the laws of the state of New York in furtherance of 35 effectuating the provisions of section 44 of the legislative law and 36 section 70-b of the public officers law.
