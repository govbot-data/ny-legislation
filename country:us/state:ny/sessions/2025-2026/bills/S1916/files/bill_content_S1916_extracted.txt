RIVERA -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Health AN ACT to amend the public health law, in relation to qualified group practices The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The public health law is amended by adding a new section 2 2998-g to read as follows: 3 § 2998-g. Qualified group practice. 1. For the purposes of this 4 section, "qualified group practice" means a medical, dental or podiatric 5 group practice or other lawful combination of such health care practi- 6 tioners, licensed or certified pursuant to title eight of the education 7 law, that meets the standards set forth in paragraphs (a) through (h) of 8 subdivision one of section twenty-eight hundred five-j of this chapter, 9 other than the governing board requirements of such paragraph (a). 10 2. A qualified group practice may operate a malpractice prevention 11 program independently, or through an otherwise lawful collaborative 12 arrangement with a hospital or accountable care organization program 13 operated pursuant to section twenty-eight hundred five-j or twenty-nine 14 hundred ninety-nine-r of this chapter, in which case the qualified group 15 practice and the hospital or accountable care organization may share 16 confidential information with each other for purposes of such practice 17 without waiving confidentiality with respect to others. 18 3. A qualified group practice shall be deemed a hospital, and its 19 malpractice prevention program shall be deemed a medical, dental and 20 podiatric malpractice prevention program, for the purposes of subdivi- 21 sion two of section twenty-eight hundred five-j, subdivision four of 22 section twenty-eight hundred five-k and section twenty-eight hundred 23 five-m of this chapter, and subdivision three of section sixty-five 24 hundred twenty-seven of the education law. Such provisions of law shall 25 apply to its information, records, documentation and committee actions, 26 and to participants in committee proceedings. 27 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
