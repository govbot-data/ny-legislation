BROWN, DAVILA -- read once and referred to the Committee on Economic Develop- ment AN ACT to amend the general business law, in relation to requiring private investigators to notify the state police when they are employed by a foreign government or foreign national and requiring the state police to maintain a registry of all private investigators who have been employed by a foreign government or foreign national The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 70 of the general business law is amended by adding 2 a new subdivision 3-a to read as follows: 3 3-a. Upon the issuance of a private investigator license or the 4 renewal of a private investigator license pursuant to this article, the 5 department of state shall notify the licensee that such licensee is 6 required to inform the state police when they are employed by a foreign 7 government or foreign national. Such notification shall be in addition 8 to any requirement under federal law regarding an individual operating 9 at the direction of a foreign government. 10 § 2. The general business law is amended by adding a new section 88-a 11 to read as follows: 12 § 88-a. Private investigator registry. The superintendent of the state 13 police shall maintain a registry of private investigators licensed in 14 the state pursuant to this article who have been employed by foreign 15 nationals and foreign governments. Such registry shall only be open to 16 the attorney general and members of the state police. Such registry 17 shall include: 18 1. the name of the private investigator; 19 2. the business name associated with the private investigator; 20 3. the address at which such business is registered; 21 4. the name and business name of the client; and EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
