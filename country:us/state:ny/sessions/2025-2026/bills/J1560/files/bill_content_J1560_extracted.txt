Bragman Athletic Complex and led throughout the final game of the eight-player season, following Frankfort-Schuyler as the section's second consecutive intersectional champion; and WHEREAS, Head Coach Don Johnson and 10 seniors completed their run with an impressive victory; the Morrisville-Eaton Mavericks led the Eldred Yellowjackets 16-0 just over three minutes into the game; and WHEREAS, Retirement from coaching is emotional for Don Johnson, but he has plans and leaves his son, Ethan, as an assistant and likely replacement; three years ago, he retired from his position at Colgate University; there is a possibility that he will return to Morrisville-Eaton as an assistant or volunteer; and WHEREAS, Don Johnson and his wife, who retired from teaching, will take time to travel; the couple has plans to enjoy Ireland this year; and WHEREAS, The many contributions of which Don Johnson has played a vital role are a testament to his legacy which will inspire future students and athletes of Morrisville-Eaton High School for generations to come; and WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body that those dedicated individuals who have shown a long and sustained commitment to excellence unquestionably have earned the recognition and applause of all the citizens of this great Empire State; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to commend Head Coach Don Johnson upon the occasion of his retirement and to wish him well in all his future endeavors; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Head Coach Don Johnson.
