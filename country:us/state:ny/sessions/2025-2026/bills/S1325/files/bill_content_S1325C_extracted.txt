AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to the submission of
requests to the committee on special education for the educational
services mandated by an individualized education services program
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Section 3602-c of the education law is amended by adding
2 two new subdivisions 2-d and 2-e to read as follows:
3 2-d. For education provided to students with disabilities beginning in
4 the two thousand twenty-five--two thousand twenty-six school year and
5 each school year thereafter, the school district of location shall annu-
6 ally provide written notice to parents or persons in parental relation
7 to all students with disabilities who have been parentally-placed in and
8 are attending nonpublic schools within their district regarding the
9 process for requesting special education services. Such notice shall:
10 (i) be provided in any of the twelve most common non-English languages
11 spoken by limited-English proficient individuals in the state, based on
12 the data in the most recent American community survey published by the
13 United States census bureau, except that any city school district in a
14 city having a population of one million or more inhabitants shall
15 provide translations in accordance with that district's established
16 policies and practices for parent-facing translation services; (ii) be

2 requests for special education services must be submitted; (iii) specify

9 city school district in a city having a population of one million or

12 b. On or before the first Friday of October, two thousand twenty-
13 five, a parent or person in parental relation to a student who had an
14 individualized education services program, pursuant to subdivision two-b
15 of this section, for the two thousand twenty-four--two thousand twenty-
16 five school year and who was obligated to file a request for services on
17 or before the first day of June, two thousand twenty-four, pursuant to
18 subdivision two of this section, but failed to do so, and did not
19 receive some of or all of the services recommended on the student's
20 individualized education services program because the school district
21 did not make them available, may contact the committee on special educa-
22 tion of the school district of location and request an expedited annual
23 review of the individualized education services program, which will be
24 conducted within sixty calendar days of such request. In advance of such
25 review, the parent or person in parental relation to the student shall
26 have the opportunity to provide the committee with any information or
27 documentation related to how such missed services impacted the student's
28 educational progress during the two thousand twenty-four--two thousand
29 twenty-five school year. At such review, the committee and the parent or
30 person in parental relation to the student shall discuss any such infor-
31 mation and documentation and determine whether the failure to receive
32 such services significantly and negatively impacted the student's educa-
33 tional progress during the two thousand twenty-four--two thousand twen-
34 ty-five school year. The committee shall incorporate any such determi-
35 nation of impact into the services recommended on any individualized
36 education services program for the student for the two thousand twenty-
37 five--two thousand twenty-six school year.
38 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
