        AN  ACT  to  amend the education law, in relation to registered pharmacy
          technicians
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  Subdivision  1  of  section 6841 of the education law, as
     2  amended by chapter 543 of the laws  of  2025,  is  amended  to  read  as
     3  follows:
     4    1.  A  registered pharmacy technician may, under the direct [personal]
     5  supervision of a licensed pharmacist, assist such  licensed  pharmacist,
     6  as directed, in compounding, preparing, labeling, or dispensing of drugs
     7  used to fill valid prescriptions or medication orders or in compounding,
     8  preparing, and labeling in anticipation of a valid prescription or medi-
     9  cation  order  for a patient to be served by the pharmacy, in accordance
    10  with article one hundred thirty-seven of this  title  where  such  tasks
    11  require  no professional judgment. Such professional judgment shall only
    12  be exercised by a licensed pharmacist.  A registered pharmacy technician
    13  may only practice under the direct [personal] supervision of a  licensed
    14  pharmacist employed in such a pharmacy. A registered pharmacy technician
    15  may  administer  the  same  immunizations  as  licensed  pharmacists are
    16  authorized to administer under the  direct  supervision  of  a  licensed
    17  pharmacist consistent with the training and other requirements in  arti-
    18  cle  one hundred thirty-seven of this title as determined by the depart-
    19  ment.  Training shall include certification in cardiopulmonary  resusci-
