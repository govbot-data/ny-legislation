S07951 Summary:

BILL NOS07951B
 
SAME ASSAME AS A09620
 
SPONSORMAYER

Add §99-uu, St Fin L; add §67, Hway L; add §404-ff, V & T L
 
Establishes the native/pollinator planting fund and the pollinator highway program; creates a distinctive native plant and pollinator license plate with the goal of promoting the cultivation and maintenance of native flowering plants, grasses and shrubs on land bordering state highways and on other public lands.

        AN ACT to amend the state finance law, the highway law and  the  vehicle
          and  traffic  law,  in  relation to establishing the native/pollinator
          planting fund, the pollinator highway program and creating distinctive
          native plant or pollinator license plates
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The  state finance law is amended by adding a new section
     2  99-uu to read as follows:
     3    § 99-uu. Native/pollinator planting fund. 1. There  is  hereby  estab-
     4  lished  in the joint custody of the commissioner of taxation and finance
     5  and  the  state  comptroller  a  special  fund  to  be  known   as   the
     6  native/pollinator planting fund. It shall consist of amounts credited to
     7  the fund in accordance with this section.
     8    2. The fund shall consist of: (a) revenue from appropriations or other
     9  money authorized by the state legislature and specifically designated to
    10  be  credited  to the fund; (b) interest earned on money in the fund; (c)
    11  state and federal grants for  green  infrastructure  projects;  and  (d)
    12  revenue generated from the sale of a dedicated distinctive license plate
    13  featuring  native  plant  or pollinator imagery. Amounts credited to the
    14  fund shall not be subject to further appropriation and any money remain-
    15  ing in the fund at the end of a fiscal year  shall  not  revert  to  the
    16  general fund.
    17    3.  Money  in  the fund shall be expended to support the establishment
    18  and implementation of the program established under section  sixty-seven

    10    §  2. The highway law is amended by adding a new section 67 to read as
    11  follows:
    12    § 67. Pollinator highway program. 1. The department shall establish  a
    13  program  to  promote the cultivation and maintenance of native flowering
    14  plants, grasses and shrubs on land bordering state highways and on other
    15  public lands. The department shall establish objectives for the  program
    16  including:  (a) incorporating native plantings into green infrastructure
    17  solutions, such as rain gardens, bio-swales and permeable landscapes  to
    18  reduce  runoff,  filter pollutants and improve water quality; (b) estab-
    19  lishing and enhancing habitats for pollinators, including bees, butterf-
    20  lies and other beneficial insects by prioritizing plant  species  native
    21  to  the  region;  and  (c)  utilizing  native vegetation to improve soil
    22  health, sequester carbon and reduce  the  need  for  emissions-intensive
    23  maintenance activities such as mowing and pesticide applications.
    24    2.  The department, in consultation with the department of agriculture
    25  and markets, the department of environmental conservation  and  relevant
    26  environmental organizations, shall: (a) identify and prioritize suitable
    27  locations  and areas that would benefit from improved stormwater manage-
    28  ment, reduced maintenance or enhanced biodiversity; (b)  develop  stand-
    29  ards for selecting and maintaining plant species to ensure their ecolog-
    30  ical   suitability,   resilience  and  effectiveness  in  achieving  the
    31  program's goals; (c) encourage the use of innovative stormwater  manage-
    32  ment  techniques  that  integrate  native vegetation; and (d) coordinate
    33  with municipalities, regional planning agencies and environmental groups
    34  to implement pilot projects and community-based initiatives.
    35    3. The department shall support the program's implementation by estab-
    36  lishing: (a) grants to municipalities for designing and installing green
    37  infrastructure projects utilizing  native  plantings;  (b)  partnerships
    38  with local nurseries and landscapers to supply native plants and support
    39  job  creation;  (c)  public-private  partnerships  to  reduce  costs and
    40  enhance the program's outreach; and (d) in consultation with the depart-
    41  ment of motor vehicles, a distinctive  license  plate  featuring  native
    42  plant  or pollinator imagery, with the proceeds supporting the program's
    43  activities and grants to municipalities.
    44    4. The department shall promote community engagement and education by:
    45  (a) developing and disseminating educational materials about  the  envi-
    46  ronmental, economic and health benefits of native plantings and stormwa-
    47  ter  management;  (b)  encouraging citizen involvement through volunteer
    48  programs, local workshops and monitoring initiatives;  and  (c)  collab-
    49  orating with school and community organizations to promote environmental
    50  stewardship and awareness.
    51    5.  The  department  shall  establish  measurable goals and metrics to
    52  assess the program's effectiveness,  including:  (a)  the  reduction  in
    53  stormwater  runoff  and  improvement  in water quality; (b) increases in
    54  pollinator populations and biodiversity; (c) cost savings in maintenance
    55  and operations; and (d) annual reporting to the general public  and  the
    56  legislature on the program's progress.

     1    6.  The department shall promulgate rules and regulations to carry out
     2  the purposes of this section within one year of its effective date.
     3    §  3.  The  vehicle and traffic law is amended by adding a new section
     4  404-ff to read as follows:
     5     § 404-ff. Distinctive native plant or pollinator license  plates.  1.
     6  Any  person  residing  in  this  state  shall, upon request, be issued a
     7  distinctive native  plant  or  pollinator  license  plate  of  a  design
     8  approved  by  the commissioner, after consultation with the commissioner
     9  of environmental conservation and the  commissioner  of  transportation.
    10  Application  for such license plate shall be filed with the commissioner
    11  in such form and detail as the commissioner shall prescribe.
    12    2. A distinctive native plant or pollinator plate issued  pursuant  to
    13  this  section  shall be issued in the same manner as other number plates
    14  upon the payment of the regular registration fees prescribed by  section
    15  four  hundred one of this article; provided, however, that an additional
    16  annual service charge of twenty-five dollars shall be charged  for  such
    17  plate.  All monies received as annual service charges under this section
    18  shall be deposited to the credit of the native/pollinator planting  fund
    19  established pursuant to section ninety-nine-uu of the state finance law;
    20  and shall be used to support the establishment and implementation of the
    21  pollinator  highway program established under section sixty-seven of the
    22  highway law.
    23    § 4. 1. A distinctive plate established pursuant to section 404-ff  of
    24  the  vehicle and traffic law as added by section three of this act shall
    25  only be designed, produced and issued upon the delivery to  the  depart-
    26  ment  of  motor vehicles of a surety bond in the amount of $6,000, which
    27  shall be executed by a surety company authorized by  the  department  of
    28  financial  services to transact business in this state. Provided, howev-
    29  er, that if the commissioner of motor vehicles shall have received prior
    30  to plate design, production and issuance at least two hundred orders for
    31  such distinctive plate  together  with  the  additional  annual  service
    32  charge  applicable to each such order, which shall be non-refundable, no
    33  such surety bond shall  be  required.  Any  additional  service  charges
    34  collected  pursuant  to  this section shall be deposited pursuant to the
    35  provisions of section ninety-nine-uu of the state finance law.
    36    2. If, upon the expiration of two years following the date upon  which
    37  distinctive  plates  in  the  series are first available for sale 200 or
    38  more sets of such plates are sold, a bond  delivered  pursuant  to  this
    39  section shall be discontinued. If fewer than 200 sets of such plates are
    40  sold by such time, the department of motor vehicles shall be entitled to
    41  recover against the bond in an amount proportionate to such shortfall.
    42    § 5. This act shall take effect immediately.
