The highway law is amended by adding a new section 67 to read as 11 follows: 12 § 67. Pollinator highway program. 1. The department shall establish a 13 program to promote the cultivation and maintenance of native flowering 14 plants, grasses and shrubs on land bordering state highways and on other 15 public lands. The department shall establish objectives for the program 16 including: (a) incorporating native plantings into green infrastructure 17 solutions, such as rain gardens, bio-swales and permeable landscapes to 18 reduce runoff, filter pollutants and improve water quality; (b) estab- 19 lishing and enhancing habitats for pollinators, including bees, butterf- 20 lies and other beneficial insects by prioritizing plant species native 21 to the region; and (c) utilizing native vegetation to improve soil 22 health, sequester carbon and reduce the need for emissions-intensive 23 maintenance activities such as mowing and pesticide applications. 24 2. The department, in consultation with the department of agriculture 25 and markets, the department of environmental conservation and relevant 26 environmental organizations, shall: (a) identify and prioritize suitable 27 locations and areas that would benefit from improved stormwater manage- 28 ment, reduced maintenance or enhanced biodiversity; (b) develop stand- 29 ards for selecting and maintaining plant species to ensure their ecolog- 30 ical suitability, resilience and effectiveness in achieving the 31 program's goals; (c) encourage the use of innovative stormwater manage- 32 ment techniques that integrate native vegetation; and (d) coordinate 33 with municipalities, regional planning agencies and environmental groups 34 to implement pilot projects and community-based initiatives. 35 3. The department shall support the program's implementation by estab- 36 lishing: (a) grants to municipalities for designing and installing green 37 infrastructure projects utilizing native plantings; (b) partnerships 38 with local nurseries and landscapers to supply native plants and support 39 job creation; (c) public-private partnerships to reduce costs and 40 enhance the program's outreach; and (d) in consultation with the depart- 41 ment of motor vehicles, a distinctive license plate featuring native 42 plant or pollinator imagery, with the proceeds supporting the program's 43 activities and grants to municipalities. 44 4. The department shall promote community engagement and education by: 45 (a) developing and disseminating educational materials about the envi- 46 ronmental, economic and health benefits of native plantings and stormwa- 47 ter management; (b) encouraging citizen involvement through volunteer 48 programs, local workshops and monitoring initiatives; and (c) collab- 49 orating with school and community organizations to promote environmental 50 stewardship and awareness. 51 5. The department shall establish measurable goals and metrics to 52 assess the program's effectiveness, including: (a) the reduction in 53 stormwater runoff and improvement in water quality; (b) increases in 54 pollinator populations and biodiversity; (c) cost savings in maintenance 55 and operations; and (d) annual reporting to the general public and the 56 legislature on the program's progress.
