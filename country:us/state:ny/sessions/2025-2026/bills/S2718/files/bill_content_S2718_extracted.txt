ORTT -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Energy and Telecommuni- cations AN ACT to amend the public authorities law, in relation to adding two trustees to the power authority to represent Niagara county and St. Lawrence county The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 1003 of the public authorities law, as amended by 2 chapter 766 of the laws of 2005, is amended to read as follows: 3 § 1003. Trustees. The authority shall consist of [seven] nine trus- 4 tees, five of whom shall serve respectively for terms of one, two, 5 three, four and five years, to be appointed by the governor, by and with 6 the advice and consent of the senate. The sixth and seventh trustees 7 shall be appointed by the governor, by and with the advice and consent 8 of the senate, and shall serve initial terms of one and two years 9 respectively. The eighth and ninth trustees shall be appointed by the 10 governor, by and with the advice and consent of the senate, and shall 11 serve initial terms of one and two years respectively and shall be resi- 12 dents of Niagara county and St. Lawrence county, respectively. Each 13 trustee shall hold office until a successor has been appointed and qual- 14 ified. At the expiration of the term of each trustee and of each 15 succeeding trustee the governor shall, by and with the advice and 16 consent of the senate, appoint a successor, who shall hold office for a 17 term of five years, or until a successor has been appointed and quali- 18 fied. In the event of a vacancy occurring in the office of the trustee 19 by death, resignation or otherwise, the governor shall, by and with the 20 advice and consent of the senate, appoint a successor, who shall hold 21 office for the unexpired term. Four trustees shall constitute a quorum 22 for the purpose of organizing the authority and conducting the business 23 thereof. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
