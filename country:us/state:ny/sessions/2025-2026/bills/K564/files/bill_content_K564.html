Assembly Resolution No. 564
 
BY: M. of A. Angelino
 
        MOURNING    the   death   of   Terry   L.   Yoder,
        distinguished citizen, public servant,  and  devoted
        member of her community
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to pay tribute to
citizens  of  the  State  of  New York whose lifework and civic endeavor
served to enhance the quality of life in their communities and the great
State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Terry L. Yoder of Unadilla, New York died on Friday,  April
25, 2025, at the age of 65; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Terry L. Yoder distinguished herself in her profession and
by her sincere dedication and substantial contribution to the welfare of
her community; and
 
  WHEREAS, An admired public servant for over a decade, Terry L. Yoder
was appointed to the position of Town Clerk for the Town of Unadilla  on
May  1,  2013,  and began her first elected four-year term on January 1,
2014; she earned the public's trust with her reliable and  knowledgeable
service,  going  on  to  secure her re-election to the position multiple
times; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 2019, Terry L. Yoder was voted in  by  her  peers  as  a
District  3  Director  for the New York State Town Clerks Association--a
coveted position held by few Town  Clerks  across  the  State;  in  this
position  which she held proudly for three years, she worked as a mentor
to  and  liaison   between   Albany,   Columbia,   Greene,   Rensselaer,
Schenectady, Schoharie, Otsego, and Delaware Counties; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  July  2020,  Terry  L. Yoder received a Certificate of
Appreciation for outstanding performance as District 3 Director; and
 
  WHEREAS, With a commitment to excellence in  her  career,  Terry  L.
Yoder  completed  the  Certified  Town Official Program in 2019; she was
also awarded a Certificate of Completion by the New York State Office of
the State  Comptroller  for  taking  the  Introduction  to  Governmental
Accounting course in 2023; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  her position as Town Clerk, Terry L. Yoder also served
as Deputy Tax Collector and Deputy Court  Clerk  and  completed  several
training  programs offered by the New York State Unified Court System in
this capacity; and
 
  WHEREAS, Truly making an impact in her community, Terry L. Yoder was
instrumental in procuring the HUD Community Development Block Grant  for
the  town's  Community  Development  Housing  Rehabilitation Program; to
date, the Town of Unadilla has received more than one million dollars in
grants to help those in need  of  home  repairs  due  to  her  steadfast
efforts; and
 
  WHEREAS,  In  her role as Town Clerk, Terry L. Yoder was responsible
for marrying countless couples, always ensuring they  felt  special  and
comfortable during the process; and
 
  WHEREAS, Outside of work, Terry L. Yoder had a love for children and
always  helped  those  who  were  less  fortunate;  she  had been both a
volunteer as well as an advocate for  grant  funding  for  the  Unadilla
Community    Food   Pantry's   annual   Christmas   Toy   Giveaway   for
underprivileged children; and
 
  WHEREAS, The many hobbies and interests of Terry L.  Yoder  included
home  decorating,  furniture  refinishing, gardening, cooking, shopping,
loving her horses, and entertaining family and friends; and
 
  WHEREAS, Terry L. Yoder's commitment to excellence, and  her  spirit
of  humanity,  carried  over  into  all  fields of enterprise, including
charitable and civic endeavors; and
 
  WHEREAS, Terry L. Yoder  is  survived  by  her  children,  her  life
partner, and her many dear friends and colleagues; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Armed  with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of
compassion, Terry L. Yoder leaves behind a legacy which will long endure
the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory  to  all  she
served and befriended; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
mourn the  death  of  Terry  L.  Yoder,  distinguished  citizen,  public
servant, and devoted member of her community; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to the family of Terry L. Yoder.
