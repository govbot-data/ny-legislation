AN ACT to amend the election law and the vehicle and traffic law, in
relation to joining multistate voter list maintenance organizations
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Legislative findings and intent. As the official record of
2 all eligible voters in a state, a state's voter registration rolls are
3 the foundation of free, fair, and accurate elections. Illegal voting is
4 exceedingly rare but maintaining accurate voter rolls reduces the oppor-
5 tunity for such behavior and it helps build confidence in election
6 outcomes. However, keeping voter rolls up to date is a challenge
7 because, every day, voters move or die. Voters do not always remember to
8 update their registration when they move. These challenges are especial-
9 ly acute in states with large and highly mobile populations. Partic-
10 ipation in a multistate voter list maintenance organization can improve
11 the accuracy of New York's voter registration rolls. A multistate voter
12 list maintenance organization is a coordinating entity between states
13 that aids them in identifying voters who may no longer be eligible to
14 vote, who have moved, or who have died. A voter list maintenance organ-
15 ization may also offer other information useful to improving voter
16 registration or enhancing the integrity of election administration.
17 § 2. Article 5 of the election law is amended by adding a new title 10
18 to read as follows:

7 § 5-1000. Joining a multistate voter list maintenance organization.
8 The state board of elections shall join the state as a member in one or
9 more multistate voter list maintenance organizations including, but not
10 limited to, the electronic registration information center or its
11 successor. The state board of elections shall expend funds for member-
12 ship fees, dues and other expenses related to such membership.
13 § 5-1001. Designating a member representative. The state board of
14 elections shall designate the chief election official of the state of
15 New York as the representative to the board of directors of any multi-
16 state voter list maintenance organization that the state is a member of
17 including, but not limited to, the electronic registration information
18 center or its successor.
19 § 5-1002. Authorization to share registration records with multistate
20 voter list maintenance organization. 1. Notwithstanding any other
21 provision of law, the member representative designated under section
22 5-1001 of this title may provide to the multistate voter list mainte-
23 nance organization that they are a member of the following information
24 from the statewide voter registration list as defined in section 5-614
25 of this article:
26 (a) all name fields;
27 (b) all address fields;
28 (c) date of birth;
29 (d) state voter identification number;
30 (e) voter's last registration date;
31 (f) activity dates as defined by the multistate voter list maintenance
32 organization; and
33 (g) voter history.
34 2. The member representative designated under section 5-1001 of this
35 title may also provide any information to the multistate voter list
36 maintenance organization provided to such member representative from the
37 department of motor vehicles pursuant to section two hundred nineteen-a
38 of the vehicle and traffic law.
39 3. The state board of elections shall not provide any information on
40 any person in the confidential voter program pursuant to section 5-508
41 of this article for any purpose under this title. Nothing in this title
42 shall be construed to require the department of motor vehicles to
43 provide any information on any person in such program.
44 4. The state board of elections is authorized to promulgate any rule
45 or regulation necessary to effectuate the provisions of this title.
46 § 5-1003. Use of information from a multistate voter list maintenance
47 organization. 1. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary,
48 the state board of elections shall use any information provided by a
49 multistate voter list maintenance organization that the state has joined
50 pursuant to section 5-1001 of this title, including but not limited to,
51 the use of such information in list maintenance activities performed
52 pursuant to section 5-614 of this article.
53 2. To avoid inadvertent or accidental registration of persons ineligi-
54 ble to register to vote, any mailing to any potentially eligible but
55 unregistered person shall state the voter registration criteria, includ-
56 ing but not limited to, any citizenship requirements.

8 § 219-a. State membership in a multistate voter list maintenance

10 contrary, the commissioner is authorized to provide to the state's

12 designated under section 5-1001 of the election law, the following
13 information: (a) all name fields; (b) all address fields; (c) DMV ID
14 number; (d) last four digits of the DMV ID holder's social security
15 number; (e) date of birth; (f) current record status; (g) phone number;
