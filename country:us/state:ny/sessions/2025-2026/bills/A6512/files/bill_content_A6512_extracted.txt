VANEL -- read once and referred to the Committee on Racing and Wagering AN ACT to amend the racing, pari-mutuel wagering and breeding law, in relation to prohibiting certain limits or bans on players The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The racing, pari-mutuel wagering and breeding law is 2 amended by adding a new section 912 to read as follows: 3 § 912. Limiting or banning players. 1. a. No entity licensed under 4 this chapter to conduct any gaming activity shall limit, prohibit, or 5 require certain bets or ban a player based, in any part, on the player's 6 success, except in cases where the player exceeds the threshold win and 7 loss caps permitted under this section. 8 b. For purposes of this section, a player is deemed limited, prohibit- 9 ed, or required to place certain bets or banned based, in any part, on 10 the player's success where the player's success is deemed a motivating 11 factor behind the action, irrespective of any other act the player 12 engaged in. 13 c. For purposes of this section, "success" shall mean a player's abil- 14 ity to win or lose a game or games operated by an entity licensed under 15 this chapter through lawful means. 16 2. An entity licensed under this chapter to conduct any gaming activ- 17 ity may implement an identifiable win cap under the following circum- 18 stances: 19 a. The licensee implements an identifiable loss cap, expressed as a 20 negative value, that shall not exceed two percent of the win cap; and 21 b. Both the win and loss caps are strictly enforced by the licensee 22 except with respect to players who opt out in accordance with subdivi- 23 sion three of this section. A win or loss cap is deemed to be strictly 24 enforced against a player where a player who exceeds either cap is 25 prohibited from further play through the use of reasonable means by the EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
