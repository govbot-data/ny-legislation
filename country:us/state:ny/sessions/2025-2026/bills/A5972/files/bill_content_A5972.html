        Introduced  by  M.  of  A.  REYES, OTIS -- read once and referred to the
          Committee on Governmental Operations
 
        AN ACT to amend the executive law, in  relation  to  providing  language
          translation services to accommodate census self-reporting
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The executive law is amended by adding a new section 154 to
     2  read as follows:
     3    § 154. Census-related  translation  services  to  state  agencies  and
     4  localities. 1.  Census-related translation services shall be extended to
     5  those  who  express  a  need  beyond the services provided by the United
     6  States Census Bureau. The department of state, by and through the secre-
     7  tary of state or duly authorized officers or employees, shall  have  the
     8  following  state  agency  and locality oversight functions, in regard to
     9  census-related translation services:
    10    (a) The department shall identify existing translation  services,  and
    11  shall  work with state agencies and other relevant state institutions to
    12  determine if such translation services may be utilized as census-related
    13  translation services. In addition to existing translation services,  the
    14  department  shall  work  to identify expanded translation services which
    15  shall include as many languages as possible, provided however that, such
    16  expanded translation services, at a minimum, shall be made available  in
    17  all languages used within census self-reporting forms.
    18    (b)  The  department shall connect state agencies, municipalities, and
    19  other relevant state or local institutions with  all  identified  trans-
    20  lation  services  for  the  purposes of providing quality census-related
    21  translation services to communities throughout the state of New York.
    22    (c) To ensure the services listed in paragraph (a) of this subdivision
    23  are effectively provided to the public, state  agencies  and  localities
    24  shall  be  required  to  notify the public of the availability of trans-
    25  lation services identified for self-reporting in the  decennial  census.

     1  Such  notice  shall include posting information online, working with any
     2  relevant organizations to reach limited or non-proficient English speak-
     3  ers, and any other means identified as effectively providing notice.
     4    2. On or before April first of each year following a decennial census,
     5  commencing  after April first, two thousand twenty-eight, the department
     6  shall submit a report to the governor, the director of the  budget,  the
     7  speaker of the assembly and the temporary president of the senate on the
     8  prior  year's  census-related  translation  services for limited English
     9  proficiency speakers. The report shall identify the translation services
    10  or programs utilized by state agencies, localities, and  other  relevant
    11  state  or  local institutions for census-related translation services in
    12  that year. In addition, the report shall include recommendations for the
    13  improvement of these services for the following decennial census.
    14    § 2. This act shall take effect April 1, 2027. Effective  immediately,
    15  the  addition,  amendment and/or repeal of any rule or regulation neces-
    16  sary for the implementation of  this  act  on  its  effective  date  are
    17  authorized  to  be  made  and  completed by the secretary of state on or
    18  before such effective date.
