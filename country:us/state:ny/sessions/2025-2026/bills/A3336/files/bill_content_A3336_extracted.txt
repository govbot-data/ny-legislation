DINOWITZ, PAULIN, KELLES, MAGNARELLI, ZINERMAN -- read once and referred to the Committee on Election Law AN ACT to amend the election law, in relation to prohibiting recurring campaign donations without affirmative consent The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The election law is amended by adding a new section 14-115 2 to read as follows: 3 § 14-115. Recurring contributions. 1. A candidate, committee, not-for- 4 profit entity or for-profit entity shall not solicit or accept a recur- 5 ring contribution from a person unless the candidate, committee, not- 6 for-profit entity or for-profit entity receives the affirmative consent 7 of the person to make a recurring contribution at the time of the 8 initial contribution. Passive action by the contributor, such as fail- 9 ing to uncheck a pre-checked box authorizing a recurring contribution, 10 shall not meet the requirement of affirmative consent under this 11 section. 12 2. A candidate, committee, not-for-profit entity or for-profit entity 13 that accepts a recurring contribution described in subdivision one shall 14 do all of the following: 15 a. provide a receipt to the contributor that clearly and conspicuously 16 discloses all terms of the recurring contribution within three days of 17 receipt of the initial contribution or each recurring contribution; 18 b. provide all necessary information to cancel the recurring contrib- 19 ution in each communication with the contributor that concerns the 20 contribution; and 21 c. immediately cancel a recurring contribution upon request of the 22 contributor. 23 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
