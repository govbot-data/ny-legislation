HOOKS -- read once and referred to the Committee on Consumer Affairs and Protection AN ACT to amend the general business law, in relation to requiring certain retail stores to accept flexible benefit cards The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The general business law is amended by adding a new article 2 38-C to read as follows: 3 ARTICLE 38-C 4 FLEXIBLE BENEFIT CARDS 5 Section 840. Definitions. 6 841. Acceptance of flexible benefit cards. 7 842. Enforcement. 8 843. Education and outreach. 9 § 840. Definitions. As used in this article, the following terms shall 10 have the following meanings: 11 1. "Covered store" means a retail store that primarily sells food for 12 off-site consumption or a retail store that includes a pharmacy. 13 2. "Eligible item" means a product that may be purchased with a flexi- 14 ble benefit card, as determined by the issuer of the flexible benefit 15 card. 16 3. "Flexible benefit card" means a card provided by a health insurance 17 provider to an insured member that may be used to purchase items covered 18 by the insured member's health insurance policy. 19 § 841. Acceptance of flexible benefit cards. Every covered store that 20 accepts credit or debit card payment shall accept flexible benefit cards 21 as a form of payment for eligible items. 22 § 842. Enforcement. Any person that violates the provisions of this 23 article or any rules or regulations promulgated pursuant thereto shall 24 be liable for a civil penalty of two hundred fifty dollars for a first 25 violation and five hundred dollars for any subsequent violation within 26 eighteen months of a previous violation; provided, however, that the 27 superintendent of financial services shall issue a warning instead of a 28 notice of violation for any violation that occurs within one year of the EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
