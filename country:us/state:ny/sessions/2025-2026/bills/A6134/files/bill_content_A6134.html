        AN  ACT to amend the judiciary law, in relation to creating a fourteenth
          judicial district
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section  1. Section 70 of the judiciary law, as amended by chapter 690
     2  of the laws of 2007, is amended to read as follows:
     3    § 70. Judicial departments. The state  is  hereby  divided  into  four
     4  judicial departments. The first department shall consist of the counties
     5  embraced  within  the  first  and twelfth judicial districts; the second
     6  department shall consist of the counties  embraced  within  the  second,
     7  ninth,   tenth,  eleventh  [and],  thirteenth  and  fourteenth  judicial
     8  districts; the third department shall consist of the  counties  embraced
     9  within  the  third,  fourth  and  sixth  judicial  districts; the fourth
    10  department shall consist of the  counties  embraced  within  the  fifth,
    11  seventh and eighth judicial districts.
    12    §  2.  The  opening  paragraph and the ninth undesignated paragraph of
    13  section 140 of the judiciary law, the opening paragraph  as  amended  by
    14  chapter  690 of the laws of 2007 and the ninth undesignated paragraph as
    15  amended by chapter 10 of the laws of 1948, are amended and a  new  four-
    16  teenth undesignated paragraph is added to read as follows:
    17    The   state  is  hereby  divided  into  [thirteen]  fourteen  judicial
    18  districts, pursuant to the provisions of the first section of the  sixth
    19  article  of  the  constitution,  which  districts  shall  be arranged as
    20  follows:
    21    The ninth judicial district shall consist of the [counties] county  of
    22  Westchester[, Putnam, Dutchess, Orange and Rockland];
    23    The  fourteenth  judicial  district  shall  consist of the counties of
    24  Putnam, Dutchess, Orange, and Rockland. The creation of such  fourteenth
    25  district  shall  not  affect or impair the rights, privileges or compen-
