POWERS -- read once and referred to the Committee on Health AN ACT to amend the public health law, in relation to requiring tobacco product retailers to offer nicotine replacement therapy products for sale The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The public health law is amended by adding a new section 2 1399-lll to read as follows: 3 § 1399-lll. Tobacco product retailers; nicotine replacement therapy 4 products required. 1. Any entity, other than a cigar shop, that sells, 5 offers for sale, or distributes for commercial purpose any tobacco prod- 6 uct within the state of New York shall maintain a stock of, and offer 7 for retail sale, at least one type of nicotine replacement therapy drug, 8 device, or combination product that has been approved by the federal 9 Food and Drug Administration for cessation of tobacco use. 10 2. An entity that is subject to the requirements of subdivision one of 11 this section shall have the discretion to determine: 12 (a) the number and type of nicotine replacement therapy products that 13 the entity will stock and offer for sale; 14 (b) the quantity of each nicotine replacement therapy product that is 15 stocked and offered for sale; and 16 (c) whether the entity will stock and offer for sale more than one 17 type of nicotine replacement therapy product. 18 3. An entity that is subject to the requirements of subdivision one of 19 this section that sells out of the entity's full stock of nicotine 20 replacement therapy products shall have five business days to place an 21 order for a new stock of a nicotine replacement therapy product, and 22 shall have fourteen days from the date the entity sells its last nico- 23 tine replacement therapy product to again stock and offer for retail 24 sale a nicotine replacement therapy product. 25 4. An entity that is subject to the requirements of subdivision one of 26 this section shall: EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. The commissioner may establish requirements concerning how and 9 where materials described in paragraphs (b) and (c) of subdivision four 10 of this section shall be displayed, as well as requirements concerning 11 the size and other characteristics of such materials. 12 6. For the purposes of this section, the following terms shall have 13 the following meanings: 14 (a) "Cigar shop" means a retail establishment wherein the only tobacco 15 products and products related to tobacco use that are available for sale 16 or commercial distribution are cigars and cigar accessories, including 17 lighters, cigar cutters, humidors, and cigar carrying tubes. An estab- 18 lishment shall be considered a cigar shop for the purposes of this 19 section regardless of whether the establishment offers other, non-tobac- 20 co products for sale or commercial distribution. 21 (b) "Tobacco product" means any product containing, made of, or 22 derived from tobacco or nicotine that is intended for human consumption 23 or is likely to be consumed, whether inhaled, absorbed, or ingested by 24 other means, including, but not limited to, a cigarette, cigar, pipe 25 tobacco, chewing tobacco, snuff, or snus; any vapor product; and any 26 component, part, or accessory of a product containing, made of, or 27 derived from tobacco or nicotine or a vapor product, regardless of 28 whether the component, part, or accessory contains tobacco or nicotine. 29 A tobacco product includes, but is not limited to, filters, rolling 30 papers, blunt or hemp wraps, hookahs, and pipes. 31 § 2.
