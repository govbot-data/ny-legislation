The social services law is amended by adding a new section 2 461-v to read as follows: 3 § 461-v. Access to criminal history records. 1. For the purposes of 4 this section, the following terms shall have the following meanings: 5 (a) "Maintenance employee" shall mean any individual to be employed or 6 used by a provider, including those persons employed through a temporary 7 employment or staffing agency, and who provide supportive maintenance 8 services to such facility, included, but not limited to beautician, 9 security, janitorial, laundry, recreational and groundskeeping services. 10 Such term shall not include volunteers. 11 (b) "Provider" shall mean an assisted living facility, residential 12 health care facility, skilled nursing facility or any other type of 13 facility that provides residential or institutional care to the elderly 14 or disabled. 15 2. Subject to the rules and regulations of the division of criminal 16 justice services, the department of health shall have access to criminal 17 history records maintained by such division pertaining to any mainte- 18 nance employee or such persons as the department of health at any time 19 deems necessary to determine their criminal histories. 20 3. Every court in which a maintenance employee of a provider is 21 convicted of a crime while performing their intended duties at such 22 facility shall, within seven days after either the entry of a plea of 23 guilty, or the verdict of the court or a jury, notify the department of 24 health in writing of such conviction.
