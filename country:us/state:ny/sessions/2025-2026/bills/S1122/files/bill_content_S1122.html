        AN  ACT to amend the real property tax law, in relation to extending the
          benefits of the STAR program to small businesses

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1. Subdivision 3 of section 425 of the real property tax law,
     2  as added by section 1 of part B of chapter 389  of  the  laws  of  1997,
     3  paragraph  (a)  as amended by chapter 264 of the laws of 2000, paragraph
     4  (b-1) as amended by section 1 of part RR of chapter 59 of  the  laws  of
     5  2019,  paragraph  (d)  as amended by chapter 564 of the laws of 2015 and
     6  paragraph (e) as added by section 2 of part W of chapter 57 of the  laws
     7  of 2008, is amended to read as follows:
     8    3.   Eligibility  requirements.  (a)  Property  use.  To  qualify  for
     9  exemption pursuant to this section, the property must be a one,  two  or
    10  three  family  residence, a farm dwelling, small business or residential
    11  property held in condominium or cooperative form of  ownership.  If  the
    12  property is not an eligible type of property, but a portion of the prop-
    13  erty is partially used by the owner as a primary residence, that portion
    14  which  is  so  used  shall be entitled to the exemption provided by this
    15  section; provided that in  no  event  shall  the  exemption  exceed  the
    16  assessed value attributable to that portion.
    17    (b)  Primary  residence.  The property must serve as the primary resi-
    18  dence of one or more of the owners  thereof,  unless  such  property  is
    19  owned  by  a small business as defined in paragraph (f) of this subdivi-
    20  sion.
    21    (b-1) Income. For final assessment rolls to be used for  the  levy  of
    22  taxes  for the two thousand eleven-two thousand twelve through two thou-
    23  sand eighteen-two thousand nineteen school years,  the  parcel's  affil-
    24  iated  income  may  be no greater than five hundred thousand dollars, as

     1  determined by the commissioner pursuant to subdivision fourteen of  this
     2  section or section one hundred seventy-one-u of the tax law, in order to
     3  be  eligible  for the basic exemption authorized by this section. Begin-
     4  ning with the two thousand nineteen-two thousand twenty school year, for
     5  purposes  of  the  exemption  authorized  by  this section, the parcel's
     6  affiliated income may be no greater  than  two  hundred  fifty  thousand
     7  dollars, as so determined.  As used herein, the term "affiliated income"
     8  shall  mean  the  combined income of all of the owners of the parcel who
     9  resided primarily thereon on the applicable taxable status date, and  of
    10  any  owners' spouses residing primarily thereon. For exemptions on final
    11  assessment rolls to be used for the levy of taxes for the  two  thousand
    12  eleven-two  thousand  twelve  school  year,  affiliated  income shall be
    13  determined based upon the parties'  incomes  for  the  income  tax  year
    14  ending  in two thousand nine. In each subsequent school year, the appli-
    15  cable income tax year shall be advanced by one year. The  term  "income"
    16  as  used  herein  shall  have the same meaning as in subdivision four of
    17  this section.
    18    (c) Trusts. If legal title to the property is  held  by  one  or  more
    19  trustees,  the  beneficial  owner  or  owners shall be deemed to own the
    20  property for purposes of this subdivision.
    21    (d) Farm dwellings not owned by the resident. (i) If  legal  title  to
    22  the farm dwelling is held by an S-corporation or by a C-corporation, the
    23  exemption  shall  be granted if the property serves as the primary resi-
    24  dence of a shareholder of such corporation.
    25    (ii) If the legal title to the farm dwelling is held by a partnership,
    26  the exemption shall be granted if the property  serves  as  the  primary
    27  residence of one or more of the partners.
    28    (iii)  If  the  legal  title to the farm dwelling is held by a limited
    29  liability company, the exemption shall be granted if the property serves
    30  as the primary residence of one or more of the owners.
    31    (iv) Any information deemed necessary to establish shareholder,  part-
    32  ner  or owner status for eligibility purposes shall be considered confi-
    33  dential and exempt from the freedom of information law.
    34    (e) Dwellings owned by limited partnerships. (i) If legal title  to  a
    35  dwelling is held by a limited partnership, the exemption shall be grant-
    36  ed if the property serves as the primary residence of one or more of the
    37  partners, provided that the limited partnership which holds title to the
    38  property  does  not  engage in any commercial activity, that the limited
    39  partnership was lawfully created to hold title solely for  estate  plan-
    40  ning and asset protection purposes, and that the partner or partners who
    41  primarily  reside  thereon personally pay all of the real property taxes
    42  and other costs associated with the property's ownership.
    43    (ii) Any information deemed necessary to establish partner status  for
    44  eligibility  purposes  shall  be considered confidential and exempt from
    45  the freedom of information law.
    46    (f) For the purposes of this subdivision, the  term  "small  business"
    47  shall mean a business which employs one hundred persons or less.
    48    §  2.  This  act  shall take effect immediately and shall apply to all
    49  taxable years beginning on and after January 1, 2025.
