COMRIE, RAMOS, ROLISON -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Corpo- rations, Authorities and Commissions AN ACT to amend the New York state urban development corporation act, in relation to assistance for certain small businesses The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Subdivision 1 of section 16-m of section 1 of chapter 174 2 of the laws of 1968, constituting the New York state urban development 3 corporation act, is amended by adding a new paragraph (p) to read as 4 follows: 5 (p) Assistance to small businesses engaged in dry cleaning or appear- 6 ance enhancement businesses which practice nail specialty to make capi- 7 tal improvements through grants and flexible financing programs, includ- 8 ing, but not limited to, loan loss reserve and revolving loan programs, 9 working capital loans, working capital loan guarantees, or other flexi- 10 ble financing programs that leverage traditional financing. Such finan- 11 cial assistance shall be used to assist: (i) dry cleaning businesses 12 for the purchase and installation of non-perchlorethylene machines and 13 sprinkler systems to comply with state and local codes, rules and regu- 14 lations; and (ii) appearance enhancement businesses which practice nail 15 specialty to make capital improvements and upgrades to mechanical venti- 16 lation systems that are necessary to comply with federal, state and 17 local indoor air quality codes, rules and regulations. The corporation, 18 in consultation with the environmental facilities corporation, the 19 department of environmental conservation and the department of health, 20 shall promulgate all necessary rules and regulations to facilitate and 21 administer such assistance program. For the purposes of this paragraph, 22 "small businesses" shall have the same meaning as defined in section one 23 hundred thirty-one of the economic development law.
