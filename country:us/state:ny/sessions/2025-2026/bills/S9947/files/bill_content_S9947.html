        Introduced  by  Sen. MATTERA -- read twice and ordered printed, and when

        AN ACT to amend chapter  63  of  the  laws  of  1971  incorporating  the
          "Centerport  Volunteer  Exempt  Firemen's  Benevolent Association" and
          providing for its powers and duties, in relation to the collection and
          use of foreign fire insurance premium taxes
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  Sections  1,  2  and  7 of chapter 63 of the laws of 1971
     2  incorporating the  "Centerport  Volunteer  Exempt  Firemen's  Benevolent
     3  Association"  and  providing  for  its powers and duties, are amended to
     4  read as follows:
     5    Section 1. Incorporation: membership. A not-for-profit corporation  by
     6  the  name  of  "Centerport  Volunteer  Exempt  [Firemen's] Firefighter's
     7  Benevolent Association" is hereby created. It shall be composed of  such
     8  persons  eligible to membership therein as hereinafter provided as shall
     9  (a) notify the secretary of the Centerport Fire  Department  Company,  a
    10  volunteer  fire company, in writing prior to the organization meeting of
    11  such corporation hereinafter provided for  of  their  desire  to  become
    12  members, or (b) shall attend such meeting or an adjournment thereof, and
    13  also  such  persons so eligible as may thereafter become members of such
    14  corporation, pursuant to its by-laws.
    15    § 2. Persons eligible to membership. All persons who are now, or  have
    16  been,  or  hereafter shall be active volunteer members of the Centerport
    17  Fire Department Company, in the town of Huntington, County  of  Suffolk,
    18  or  of any fire corporation in the Centerport Fire District in such town
    19  and who have acquired or shall hereafter acquire the  status  of  exempt
    20  volunteer [fireman] firefighter as defined in section two hundred of the
    21  general  municipal  law  by  virtue  of  having  rendered service in the
    22  Centerport Fire Department Company or  in  a  fire  corporation  in  the
    23  Centerport Fire District, and all persons who are now or hereafter shall
    24  be  active  volunteer  members of the Centerport Fire Department Company

     1  shall be eligible for membership in the corporation created by this act,
     2  except that any [fireman] firefighter who has been  removed  for  cause,
     3  expelled  or  dropped from the rolls of any fire company or fire depart-
     4  ment, and who has not been reinstated therein, shall not be eligible for
     5  membership  in  such  corporation.  The membership of any person in such
     6  corporation shall terminate when [his] their membership as an active  or
     7  volunteer  member of the Centerport Fire Department Company shall termi-
     8  nate before such person shall have acquired the status of exempt  volun-
     9  teer  [fireman]  firefighter  as  defined  in section two hundred of the
    10  general municipal law by  virtue  of  having  rendered  service  in  the
    11  Centerport Fire Department Company.
    12    §  7.  Precept  for  payment  of foreign fire insurance premium taxes.
    13  Notwithstanding any contrary provisions of any general, special or local
    14  law, or of any city charter  or  ordinance,  heretofore  or  hereinafter
    15  enacted,  such  corporation  shall collect and there shall be paid to it
    16  all taxes imposed by section [five hundred  fifty-three]  nine  thousand
    17  one  hundred four of the insurance law for fire department use and bene-
    18  fit, upon premiums for insurance against loss or damage by fire covering
    19  property situated in the Centerport Fire District in the Town  of  Hunt-
    20  ington,  County  of  Suffolk,  or within any duly organized territory in
    21  which the Centerport Fire Department Company is or may be  obligated  to
    22  render  fire  protection.  The officer of said corporation designated by
    23  its by-laws to collect and receive the  aforesaid  tax  shall  have  the
    24  powers and be subject to all the provisions of the insurance law, relat-
    25  ing  to  treasurers  of fire departments. Such corporation shall also be
    26  entitled to receive a share of the tax imposed by section [five  hundred
    27  fifty-four]  nine  thousand one hundred five of the insurance law, based
    28  upon the business written in the territory with respect to which  it  is
    29  entitled  to  collect  and  receive  the tax under section [five hundred
    30  fifty-three] nine thousand one hundred four of the insurance  law.  Such
    31  taxes  shall  only be used [for the care and relief of disabled or indi-
    32  gent volunteer and exempt volunteer firemen and their families]  by  the
    33  corporation in furtherance of its purposes as set forth in section three
    34  of this act.
    35    §  2.  This act shall take effect immediately; provided, however, that
    36  the amendments to section 7 of chapter 63 of the laws of  1971  made  by
    37  section one of this act shall apply to taxes due on premiums received on
    38  or after January 1, 2027.
