CARROLL -- read once and referred to the Committee on Education AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to requiring that the New York state board of regents hold at least two meetings a year in the city of New York The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 204 of the education law is amended by adding two 2 new subdivisions 5 and 6 to read as follows: 3 5. (a) The regents shall hold at least two meetings in each calendar 4 year in a city having a population of one million or more. Such meetings 5 shall be held at a time to ensure parental attendance and participation. 6 (b) (i) The regents shall announce the location, time and agenda of 7 each meeting to be held in a city having a population of one million or 8 more whenever such meeting is scheduled, but not later than three weeks 9 before the meeting date to allow adequate time for public review. 10 Provided, however, that if an agenda for any such meeting is not avail- 11 able, such agenda shall be announced as soon as it becomes available. 12 (ii) Such announcements shall be made available on the regents' public 13 website and the New York city department of education website in the 14 same manner as other public meetings are announced. The New York city 15 department of education shall inform parent education advocates, teach- 16 ers, principals and other school administrators of the location, time 17 and agenda of such meetings held in a city having a population of one 18 million or more. For the purposes of this subdivision, the term "parent 19 education advocates" shall include, but not be limited to parent associ- 20 ations, parent-teacher associations, school leadership teams, presidents 21 councils, district leadership teams and citywide and communitywide 22 education councils. 23 (c) At each meeting of the regents held in a city having a population 24 of one million or more, the public shall be allotted a period of time, EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
