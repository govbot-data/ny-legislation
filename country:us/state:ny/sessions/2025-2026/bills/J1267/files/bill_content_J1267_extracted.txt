and WHEREAS, Louis Coppola of Mahopac, New York, died on Saturday, June 21, 2025, at the age of 76; and WHEREAS, Born in New York City on July 31, 1948, Louis Coppola was the son of William V. and Mary Coppola; and WHEREAS, Louis Coppola served in the United States Army from 1968-1970, then in the Army Reserves until 1974; and WHEREAS, Louis Coppola distinguished himself in his profession and by his sincere dedication and substantial contribution to the welfare of his community; and WHEREAS, A carpenter with the Carpenters Union, Louis Coppola primarily worked on bridges; and WHEREAS, In his spare time, Louis Coppola enjoyed fishing, boating, hunting, gardening, and in his retirement, he was routinely seen driving around town checking in with family and hanging out with his friends at the bait shop; and WHEREAS, Louis Coppola's commitment to excellence, and his spirit of humanity, carried over into all fields of enterprise, including charitable and civic endeavors; and WHEREAS, Louis Coppola is survived by his brother, William; his sister, Marie Olivier; nieces, nephews, grandnieces and grandnephews that he deeply cherished; he was predeceased by his parents and older brother, Anthony; and WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of compassion, Louis Coppola leaves behind a legacy which will long endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all he served and befriended; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of Louis Coppola, distinguished citizen and devoted member of his community; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Louis Coppola.
