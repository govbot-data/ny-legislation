S00371 Summary:

BILL NOS00371
 
SAME ASSAME AS A04604
 
SPONSORSKOUFIS
 
COSPNSRJACKSON, LIU
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Add §280-d, Pub Health L; amd §6808, Ed L
 
Creates a wholesale prescription drug importation program which complies with federal standards and regulations for the purpose of generating substantial savings for consumers.

        AN ACT to amend the public health law and the education law, in relation
          to creating a wholesale prescription drug importation program

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The  public health law is amended by adding a new section
     2  280-d to read as follows:
     3    § 280-d. Wholesale prescription drug importation program.  1. As  used
     4  in this section,  the following terms shall have the following meanings:
     5    (a)  "Wholesale  prescription  drug  importation program" or "program"
     6  means the wholesale prescription drug importation program created  under
     7  this section.
     8    (b)  "Prescription  drug  wholesaler"  means  an  entity authorized to
     9  acquire prescription drugs and sell or distribute them wholesale in  the
    10  state.
    11    (c) "Approved wholesaler" means a prescription drug wholesaler author-
    12  ized to participate in the importation program under this section pursu-
    13  ant  to  approval by the state education department under section sixty-
    14  eight hundred eight of the education law.
    15    2. The commissioner, in consultation with the commissioner  of  educa-
    16  tion  and  other  appropriate federal and state agencies, shall design a
    17  wholesale prescription drug importation program for the wholesale impor-
    18  tation of prescription drugs  from  Canada.  The  program  design  shall
    19  comply  with applicable federal requirements, including 21 U.S.C. § 384,
    20  and requirements regarding safety and cost savings.  Under  the  program
    21  design:
    22    (a)   prescription   drugs   shall  only  be  acquired  from  Canadian
    23  prescription drug suppliers regulated and authorized under the  laws  of
    24  Canada or one or more Canadian provinces, or both;

     1    (b) only prescription drugs meeting the federal Food and Drug Adminis-
     2  tration's  safety,  effectiveness,  misbranding,  adulteration and other
     3  standards shall be imported under the program;
     4    (c)  only  prescription drugs expected to generate substantial savings
     5  for consumers in the state shall be imported;
     6    (d) the prescription drug is not:
     7    (i) a controlled substance as defined in 21 U.S.C. § 802;
     8    (ii) a biological product as defined in 42 U.S.C. § 262;
     9    (iii) an infused drug, including a peritoneal dialysis solution;
    10    (iv) an intravenously injected drug;
    11    (v) a drug that is inhaled during surgery;
    12    (vi) a drug which is a parenteral drug, the importation  of  which  is
    13  determined  by  the United States Secretary of Health and Human Services
    14  to pose a threat to public health;
    15    (e) any approved wholesaler shall at all times comply with the  track-
    16  ing  and tracing requirements of 21 U.S.C. §§ 360eee and 360eee-1 to the
    17  extent practicable prior to imported prescription drugs coming into  the
    18  possession  of  the  approved  wholesaler,  and  fully comply with those
    19  requirements after imported prescription drugs are in the possession  of
    20  the approved wholesaler;
    21    (f)  an  approved  wholesaler  shall  not sell, distribute or dispense
    22  prescription drugs imported under the program outside of the state;
    23    (g) the commissioner may impose an annual fee on approved wholesalers,
    24  which may be based in whole or in part  on  the  value  of  prescription
    25  drugs  imported by the approved wholesaler under the program, to support
    26  the operation of the program;
    27    (h) every approved wholesaler shall provide the commissioner  and  the
    28  commissioner  of  education with information on its participation in the
    29  program as required by such commissioners including but not limited to:
    30    (i) the name and quantity of the active ingredient of the drug;
    31    (ii) a description of the dosage form of the drug;
    32    (iii) the date on which the drug is received;
    33    (iv) the quantity of the drug that is received;
    34    (v) the point of origin and destination of the drug; and
    35    (vi) the price paid by the approved wholesaler for the drug
    36    (i) the commissioner  shall  provide  for  auditing  of  the  program,
    37  including  making  sure  that  prescription  drugs are made available at
    38  substantial savings to consumers as a result of  the  program,  ensuring
    39  that  the  prescription  drugs  are approved for marketing in the United
    40  States, meet all labeling requirements under state and federal laws  and
    41  regulations,  and  is  not  adulterated or misbranded, and ensuring that
    42  prescription drugs are authentic and in compliance with the federal Food
    43  and Drug Administration's approved drug specifications and standards.
    44    3. The department, in consultation with the  state  education  depart-
    45  ment,  shall  promulgate  rules and regulations to design the program in
    46  accordance with subdivision two of this section.
    47    4. (a) The commissioner, in  consultation  with  the  commissioner  of
    48  education,  shall  seek all necessary approvals and certification by the
    49  secretary of the U.S. Department of Health  and  Human  Services  and/or
    50  other  appropriate  federal  officials  or  agencies  for  the wholesale
    51  prescription drug importation program designed under this section.
    52    (b) The commissioner shall seek  the  appropriate  federal  approvals,
    53  waivers,  exemptions, or agreements, or a combination thereof, as needed
    54  to enable all covered entities enrolled in  or  eligible  for  the  drug
    55  discount program authorized by section 340B of the federal public health
    56  service  act  (42  U.S.C.  §  256b)  to  participate  in  the  wholesale

     1  prescription drug importation program to  the  fullest  extent  possible
     2  without jeopardizing their eligibility for such drug discount program.
     3    5.  Upon receipt of federal approval and certification under paragraph
     4  (a) of subdivision four of this section, the commissioner  shall  imple-
     5  ment the program pursuant to this section.
     6    6.  The  commissioner  shall  immediately suspend the importation of a
     7  specific prescription drug or the importation of prescription  drugs  by
     8  an  approved  wholesaler  if the commissioner discovers that any drug or
     9  activity is in violation of this section or any federal or state law  or
    10  regulation,  and  shall immediately notify the commissioner of education
    11  of such suspension.   Furthermore, the  commissioner  shall  inform  the
    12  commissioner of education of all facts and circumstances leading to such
    13  suspension  as soon as practicable, and shall cooperate with the commis-
    14  sioner of education in any disciplinary investigation or action pursuant
    15  to title eight of the education law related to such wholesaler.
    16    7. Nothing in this section shall be construed as affecting or  in  any
    17  way interfering with the commissioner of education's oversight of whole-
    18  salers.
    19    8.  The commissioner shall annually report to the governor, the tempo-
    20  rary president of the senate, and the speaker of the assembly  regarding
    21  the  implementation  of a federally approved wholesale prescription drug
    22  importation program.  The report shall include, at a minimum:
    23    (a) a list of the prescription drugs imported under the program;
    24    (b) a list of all participating Canadian prescription drug  suppliers,
    25  approved wholesalers, and other participating entities;
    26    (c) estimated cost savings during the previous fiscal year;
    27    (d) information regarding audit findings; and
    28    (e) any other relevant information.
    29    §  2.  Section  6808  of  the education law is amended by adding a new
    30  subdivision 10 to read as follows:
    31    10. Prescription drug importation program wholesalers. a. A wholesaler
    32  shall not participate in the  wholesale  prescription  drug  importation
    33  program  under  section  two  hundred  eighty-d of the public health law
    34  without prior application and approval by the department.
    35    b. Such application shall be made on a form prescribed by the  depart-
    36  ment.
    37    c.  Such  application  shall be accompanied by a fee determined by the
    38  department.
    39    d. All approvals shall be renewed on dates set by the department.
    40    e. All approvals shall meet applicable federal  laws  and  regulations
    41  including under 21 U.S.C. § 384, as amended, and any regulations promul-
    42  gated thereunder.
    43    §  3.  This  act shall take effect eighteen months after it shall have
    44  become a law. Effective  immediately,  the  addition,  amendment  and/or
    45  repeal  of  any  rule  or regulation necessary for the implementation of
    46  this act on its effective date are authorized to be made  and  completed
    47  on or before such effective date.
