CLEARE -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Corporations, Authorities and Commissions AN ACT to amend chapter 154 of the laws of 1921, relating to the port authority of New York and New Jersey, in relation to the operation of a youth services unit within the port authority police department The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 1 of chapter 154 of the laws of 1921, relating to 2 the port authority of New York and New Jersey, is amended by adding a 3 new article VII-F to read as follows: 4 ARTICLE VII-F 5 1. The port authority police department shall operate a youth services 6 unit at any bus terminal and airport operated by the authority, at all 7 port authority Trans-Hudson stations and at the World Trade Center. 8 2. Such youth services unit shall be tasked with aiding youths in 9 danger of exploitation, namely those who are runaways and who may be in 10 danger of becoming human and sex trafficking victims through measures 11 including but not limited to intercepting runaway youths; collaborating 12 with undercover plain clothed police officers and social workers; iden- 13 tifying vulnerable youths before they become victimized; identifying and 14 stopping perpetrators; assisting in state and federal prosecution of 15 offenders; reuniting at-risk youths with their families; and providing 16 services referrals to at-risk youths and victims of trafficking. 17 3. The port authority is hereby authorized to make and enforce such 18 rules and regulations necessary for the implementation of this article. 19 § 2. This act shall take effect upon the enactment into law by the 20 state of New Jersey of legislation having an identical effect; but if 21 the state of New Jersey shall have already enacted such legislation, 22 then this act shall take effect immediately and provided that the state 23 of New Jersey shall notify the legislative bill drafting commission upon EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
