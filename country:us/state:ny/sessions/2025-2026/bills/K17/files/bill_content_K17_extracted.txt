Bologna MOURNING the death of Flora Leamer, distinguished citizen and devoted member of her community WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to pay tribute to citizens of the State of New York whose lifework and civic endeavor served to enhance the quality of life in their communities and this great Empire State; and WHEREAS, Flora Leamer of Clarence, New York, died on Wednesday, October 16, 2024, at the age of 76; and WHEREAS, Flora Leamer distinguished herself in her profession and by her sincere dedication and substantial contribution to the welfare of her community; and WHEREAS, A native of Akron, New York, Flora Leamer began living in Clarence in 1971, and married her beloved husband, John, in in 1985, after meeting him at church; and WHEREAS, With a passion for seeing the world, Flora Leamer often enjoyed taking mission trips to Nicaragua, as well as traveling with her cherished husband; and WHEREAS, Flora Leamer lent 48 years to the profession of accounting; she was also one of the original committee members of the Clarence Hollow Farmers Market, and was its accountant, as well as a dedicated volunteer; and WHEREAS, Regarded for her unwavering commitment to local committees and organizations, Flora Leamer was also involved with the Meet in the Center merchant's association, Rock the Barn music festival, the Clarence Clubhouse Restoration Committee, the Garden Friends of Clarence, the former Clarence Woman's Club, the Clarence Contemporary Club, Bark in the Park, Winterfest, Clarence and Newstead's historical societies, Akron High School alumni, and the Clarence Lions Club as an honorary Lion; and WHEREAS, Flora Leamer additionally served the Clarence Chamber of Commerce in multiple roles; throughout her time serving the organization, she held the titles of membership chair, officer and chair of the gift certificate program, and additionally served as a member of its trade fair and membership committee, board of directors, Annual Awards banquet committee, and annual golf outing committee; and WHEREAS, Known widely for her love of photographing community events and for her spirited participation in the local parades, Flora Leamer's energy and love for her fellow citizens and the Town of Clarence was radiant; she will be remembered for her kindness, warmth, and selflessness; and WHEREAS, To further honor Flora Leamer, the Clarence Chamber of Commerce, in partnership with the Eastern Hills Sunrise Rotary Foundation, established the Flora B. Leamer Young Entrepreneur Grant to support young entrepreneurs as they take their first steps into business, or expand and develop their already existing business ventures; and WHEREAS, Flora Leamer's commitment to excellence and her spirit of humanity carried over into all fields of enterprise, including all charitable and civic endeavors in which she partook; and WHEREAS, Flora Leamer is survived by her husband, John Leamer; two sisters, Kathleen Kline and Marilyn Judge; one brother, John Babel; and many nieces and nephews; she was predeceased by her parents, Chester and Elizabeth Babel; one sister, Phyllis Graf; and one brother, Lenard Babel; and WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of compassion, Flora Leamer leaves behind a legacy which will long endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all she served and befriended; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of Flora Leamer, distinguished citizen and devoted member of her community; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Flora Leamer.
