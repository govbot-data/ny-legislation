Carmel Catholic Church in The Bronx; and WHEREAS, Salvatore Colatruglio distinguished himself in his profession and by his sincere dedication and substantial contribution to the welfare of his community; and WHEREAS, Owner of Colatruglio Construction, Salvatore Colatruglio specialized his trade as a bricklayer; he also worked as a school bus driver and a maintenance worker in the Mahopac Central School District; and WHEREAS, Salvatore Colatruglio's commitment to excellence, and his spirit of humanity, carried over into all fields of enterprise, including charitable and civic endeavors; and WHEREAS, Committed to serving his community, Salvatore Colatruglio was a life member of the Mahopac Volunteer Fire Department where he took on many roles, including that of Department Quartermaster; he was also a member of the Putnam County Fire Police Association and the Putnam County Volunteer Firemen's Association; and WHEREAS, Embracing a strong connection to his heritage, Salvatore Colatruglio was a member of the Italian American Clubs of both Mahopac, where he had been honored as an Honorary Grand Marshall of the Columbus Day Parade, and Seaside Park, New Jersey; and WHEREAS, Salvatore Colatruglio is predeceased by his wife Carmela, who passed away on July 12, 2020, and is survived by his son, Joseph and his wife, Amy; his daughter, Maria Hughes and her husband, Colin; his four beloved grandchildren, Samantha, Joseph Jr., Sydney and Riley; and WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of compassion, Salvatore Colatruglio leaves behind a legacy which will long endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all he served and befriended; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of Salvatore Colatruglio, distinguished citizen and devoted member of his community; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Salvatore Colatruglio.
