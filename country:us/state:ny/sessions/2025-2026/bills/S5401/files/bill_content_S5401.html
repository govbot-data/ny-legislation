Add §2424, Pub Health L
 
Establishes the clinical trial access and education fund to provide grants to eligible applicants to provide certain services for patients with life-threatening or disabling conditions or diseases.

        AN  ACT  to amend the public health law, in relation to establishing the
          clinical trial access and education fund
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The  public health law is amended by adding a new section
     2  2424 to read as follows:
     3    § 2424. Clinical trial access and education fund. 1. Definitions.  For
     4  the purposes of this section, the following terms shall have the follow-
     5  ing meanings:
     6    a. "Eligible applicant" means (i) a medical  school  that  sponsors  a
     7  clinical  trial,  (ii) a not-for-profit organization with experience and
     8  expertise working  with  patients  with  life-threatening  or  disabling
     9  conditions or diseases, (iii) a health care provider organization, asso-
    10  ciation  or  society, (iv) a general hospital defined in article twenty-
    11  eight of this chapter, (v) a county or city health department, or (vi) a
    12  municipality.
    13    b. "Clinical trial" shall have the  same  meaning  as  in  subdivision
    14  two-b of section forty-nine hundred of this chapter.
    15    c.  "Ancillary  costs"  means costs associated with participation in a
    16  clinical trial, including but not limited to costs for:
    17    (i) travel;
    18    (ii) lodging;
    19    (iii) parking and tolls;
    20    (iv) a travel companion to  assist  patients  who  are  elderly,  very
    21  young, or too ill to travel alone; and
    22    (v)  other costs considered appropriate jointly by the department, the
    23  service providing organization and the trial site.
    24    2. Establishment of fund.  There  is  hereby  established  within  the
    25  department a clinical trial access and education fund. Subject to appro-

    23    (ii) a detailed description of the services to be provided;
    24    (iii) applicant's experience working with patients with life-threaten-

    26    (iv) applicant's ability to provide patient outreach or clinical trial
    27  education  and navigation services, or coordinate or provide transporta-
    28  tion and lodging for patients; and

    30  appropriate.

    32    (i)  have  experience  and  expertise working with patients with life-
    33  threatening or disabling conditions or diseases;
    34    (ii) provide patient outreach, education and  health  care  navigation
    35  services;
    36    (iii) collaborate with physicians, health care providers, and clinical
    37  trial sponsors to notify a prospective subject about the program when:
    38    (1) the prospective subject consents to a clinical trial; and
    39    (2) funding is available to provide the program for the clinical trial
    40  in which the prospective subject participates; and
    41    (iv)  reimburse  subjects  based  on  financial need to subjects whose
    42  income is at or below seven  hundred  percent  of  the  federal  poverty
    43  level, which shall include reimbursement for reasonable ancillary costs.
    44    4.  Institutional  review  board requirements. A reimbursement program
    45  under this section shall:
    46    a. be approved by the institutional review board associated  with  the
    47  clinical trial; and
    48    b. comply with applicable federal and state laws.
    49    5.  Reimbursement. Reimbursement to a subject of ancillary costs under
    50  the program:
    51    a. does not constitute an undue inducement to participate in  a  clin-
    52  ical trial;
    53    b.  is  not  considered coercion or the exertion of undue influence to
