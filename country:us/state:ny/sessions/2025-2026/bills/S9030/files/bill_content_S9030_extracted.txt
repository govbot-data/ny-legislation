ASHBY -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Procurement and Contracts AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to establishing a New York state child care expansion program within the office of general services The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The executive law is amended by adding a new section 204 to 2 read as follows: 3 § 204. New York state child care expansion program. 1. For the 4 purposes of this section, the following terms shall have the following 5 meanings: 6 (a) "Child day care center" shall have the same meaning as defined by 7 section three hundred ninety of the social services law. 8 (b) "State agency" means any department, division, bureau, commission, 9 agency, office, board or other subunit of any public authority, the 10 executive, the judiciary, or the legislature. 11 (c) "State office" means any office building or facility in the state 12 wherein one hundred or more employees, in aggregate, of a state agency 13 are located, whether or not such office building or facility is owned, 14 rented, or leased by the state. 15 2. There shall be created within the office of general services a "New 16 York state child care expansion program". The purpose of such program 17 shall be to assist state agencies with establishing a physical location 18 whereby each state office shall allow a public or private child day care 19 center to operate within such state office. Such program shall require 20 within the contractual agreement with a public or private child day care 21 center, an acceptance lottery system with first preference given to 22 state employees employed in the same state office as such child day care 23 center, and any remaining available spots offered to the general public. 24 3. Each state office, with assistance from the New York state child 25 care expansion program, shall designate a room or rooms as dedicated 26 child care space whereby such state office shall allow a public or 27 private child day care center to operate.
