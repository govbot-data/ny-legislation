BROUK -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Health AN ACT to establish a work group to set reimbursement rates for doulas in the state Medicaid program and address other criteria related to their practice The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The legislature acknowledges the excluded history that 2 healthcare workers and doulas have as members of historically excluded 3 communities, and acknowledges the exclusionary forces these professions 4 have had in gaining the recognition, certification, and equitable 5 compensation for the critical work they do in pregnancy, maternal 6 health, maternal mental health, and childcare. The legislature declares 7 that it is the purpose of this work group to examine and recommend the 8 best practices for integrating doulas into New York state's Medicaid 9 healthcare system and ensuring the state is prepared and ready to 10 elevate the critical work doulas perform across historically excluded 11 communities across the state of New York. 12 § 2. There is hereby established in the department of health, the 13 doula Medicaid reimbursement work group, hereinafter referred to as the 14 "work group." The majority of the members of the work group shall be 15 composed of doulas, as well as multi-disciplinary experts in the field 16 of doula services and maternal health who serve and are representative 17 of the racial, ethnic, geographic and socioeconomic diversity of birth- 18 ing people in communities across the state served by the state's Medi- 19 caid program. 20 § 3. The work group shall be composed of fourteen members, which shall 21 include eight members to be appointed by the governor as follows: 22 a. the commissioner of the department of health or their designee; 23 b. five representatives who may either be practicing doulas, or doulas 24 in training, or individuals with expertise in the field of doula 25 services; 26 c. one representative serving maternal health needs; 27 d. one representative from the medical insurance industry; EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. The governor shall designate the commissioner of the department 5 of health or their designee, as chair of the board. Members of the work 6 group shall receive no compensation for their services, but shall be 7 reimbursed for actual expenses incurred during the performance of their 8 duties on the work group. Reimbursement shall allow for historically 9 excluded communities to participate wholly in the performance of their 10 duties on the work group by providing, if necessary, reimbursements for 11 reasonable expenses incurred that may include, but not be limited to, 12 travel, meals and lodging. 13 § 5. Appointments shall be made within ninety days of the effective 14 date of this act. Vacancies in the work group shall be filled in the 15 same manner provided for original appointments. The appointee makeup of 16 the work group shall be majority doulas, as well as multi-disciplinary 17 experts in the field of doula services and maternal health who serve and 18 are representative of the racial, ethnic, geographic, and socioeconomic 19 diversity of birthing people in communities across the state. 20 § 6. The work group shall conduct a study and evaluation of the costs, 21 benefits and issues that may be associated with Medicaid reimbursement 22 for doulas and for providing doula care to Medicaid recipients. The work 23 group shall consider factors including but not limited to: 24 a. identifying evidence-based practices related to when and how doula 25 care results in improvements to maternal and infant mortality rates; 26 b. identifying successful Medicaid doula programs and initiatives in 27 other states and recommend programs, tools, and funding sources that are 28 needed to implement similar programs and initiatives in New York state; 29 c. establishing a criteria for adequate and equitable Medicaid 30 reimbursement rates for a primary doula; 31 d. establishing a criteria for adequate and equitable Medicaid 32 reimbursement for a substitute doula, in the event the primary doula is 33 unavailable to provide doula services to Medicaid patients during deliv- 34 ery; 35 e. considering the appropriate quantity and selection of antepartum, 36 intrapartum, or postpartum doula visits to qualify for Medicaid 37 reimbursement; 38 f. examining the need for doula liability coverage and insurance; 39 g. considering the need for continuing education for doulas; and 40 h. recommending the state file an amendment to the Medicaid state plan 41 to include payment for doula services rendered for antepartum, intrapar- 42 tum, or postpartum doula services provided to a birthing person or to a 43 person at a reasonable time postpartum for labor and delivery support by 44 a doula. 45 § 7. The work group, on or before December 31, 2026, shall submit a 46 final report containing all findings and recommendations to the gover- 47 nor, the temporary president of the senate, and the speaker of the 48 assembly. 49 § 8.
