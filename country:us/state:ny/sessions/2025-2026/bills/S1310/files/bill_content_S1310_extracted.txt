CANZONERI-FITZPATRICK, BORRELLO, OBERACKER, RHOADS -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Education AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to creating a school mental health services program coordinator The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The education law is amended by adding a new section 2801-c 2 to read as follows: 3 § 2801-c. New York state school mental health services program coordi- 4 nator. 1. For purposes of this section, the term "school mental health 5 services program coordinator" shall mean a mental health services 6 professional, with qualifications determined by regulation by the 7 commissioner, whose role and responsibility shall be to work with 8 students, faculty and other mental health and health care professionals 9 to identify, report and address mental health issues of students, facul- 10 ty and administration at any public or non-public school that could pose 11 a risk to public safety. 12 2. Any public or non-public school may employ a mental health services 13 professional, in either the classified or unclassified service, deter- 14 mined in regulation by the commissioner to be qualified, as a mental 15 health services program coordinator. Any such public or non-public 16 school may also contract with the state of New York, a county, city, 17 town or village, or with a private mental health services provider, for 18 the provision of a mental health services professional, determined in 19 regulation by the commissioner to be qualified, to serve as a mental 20 health services program coordinator. 21 3. The commissioner shall, by regulation, determine the qualifications 22 for a mental health services program coordinator. A school district 23 shall be authorized to employ or contract for as many mental health 24 services program coordinators, as such district deems necessary.
