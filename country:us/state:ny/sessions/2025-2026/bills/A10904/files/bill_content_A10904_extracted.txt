MOLITOR -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Insurance AN ACT to amend the insurance law, in relation to enacting the "small business health plan act" The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Short title. This act shall be known and cited as the 2 "small business health plan act". 3 § 2. Subsection (h) of section 3216 of the insurance law is amended by 4 adding a new paragraph 7 to read as follows: 5 (7) any policy or contract offered to owners or employees of a small 6 business, as such term is defined in section one hundred thirty-one of 7 the economic development law, as a benefit of owning or being employed 8 by such business. 9 § 3. Section 3221 of the insurance law is amended by adding a new 10 subsection (w) to read as follows: 11 (w) This section shall not apply to any policy or contract offered to 12 owners or employees of a small business, as such term is defined in 13 section one hundred thirty-one of the economic development law, as a 14 benefit of owning or being employed by such business. 15 § 4. The insurance law is amended by adding a new section 3217-k to 16 read as follows: 17 § 3217-k. Small business owner and employee insurance exemption. (a) 18 For the purposes of this section, the term "small business" shall have 19 the same meaning as such term is defined in section one hundred thirty- 20 one of the economic development law. 21 (b) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, insur- 22 ers may offer to the owners or employees of a small business individual 23 and/or small group accident and health insurance policies which do not 24 provide for coverages required under this chapter where such small busi- 25 ness is subsidizing such policies. Such policies may include riders for 26 any coverages, including coverages required for other similar policies 27 under this chapter.
