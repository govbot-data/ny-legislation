Statewide resilience plan. 1. To coordinate and strengthen 45 efforts to reduce losses from future disasters across the state, the 46 office shall contribute to all statewide planning efforts related to 47 resilience and risk mitigation and shall develop a strategic statewide 48 resilience plan to protect the state from multiple climate threats. 49 2. Such plan shall include, but not be limited to, the following: 50 (a) Articulation of the state's resilience goals and objectives; 51 (b) Utilization of the best available science, including a range of 52 future projections, to identify, implement, or reform policies, 53 projects, and programs to achieve the state's resilience goals and 54 objectives; 55 (c) Recommended agency-specific strategic actions, including criteria 56 for prioritization based on a vulnerability assessment of the risks from A. 8088--B 4 1 multiple environmental threats to agency mission areas, assets, 2 services, and populations served; 3 (d) Prioritization of natural, nature-based, and non-structural 4 approaches to mitigating climate threats, wherever possible including, 5 without limitation, use of living shorelines, riparian restoration, 6 permeable surfaces, rain gardens, green roofs, tree canopy expansion, 7 wetland restoration, removing, altering, or right-sizing dams, natural 8 area conservation, waste-water and stormwater infrastructure upgrades, 9 alteration of structures, buyouts, and other flood and extreme heat 10 prevention, mitigation and resiliency strategies or projects; 11 (e) Set goals and resilience indicators that shall be tracked and 12 reported to the public over time in an annual progress report; and 13 (f) A framework for resilience project development, funding, and 14 implementation.
