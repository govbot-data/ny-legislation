Enacts the climate resilient New York act; establishes the office of resilience and a resilience task force to assess and identify climate related threats and develop a statewide resilience plan.

    15  "climate resilient New York act of 2026".
    16    § 931. Declaration of purpose. The  legislature  recognizes  that  the
    17  state is particularly vulnerable to adverse impacts from climate change.
    18  In  less than 15 years, the state has experienced sixteen climate disas-
    19  ter declarations. These rising risks  pose  economic,  social,  environ-
    20  mental,  and public health and safety challenges. A coordinated approach
    21  is necessary to effectively,  efficiently,  and  equitably  address  and

    11    § 932. Office of resilience. 1. There is hereby created in the  execu-
    12  tive  department  an  office  of resilience, hereinafter in this article
    13  referred to as the "office".
    14    2. The office shall have the following functions, powers and duties:
    15    (a) Coordinate the resilience task force and provide strategic  direc-
    16  tion  for governmental resilience initiatives to build long-term climate
    17  resilience for a robust, vibrant economy, sustainable  natural  environ-
    18  ment,  healthy  communities,  and  an  equitable  and just transition to
    19  future climate;
    20    (b) Establish an interagency resilience coordination team;
    21    (c) Establish, in collaboration with the interagency resilience  coor-
    22  dination  team,  a statewide resilience plan and framework to facilitate
    23  coordination across resilience plans at all levels of government;
    24    (d) Provide technical guidance and assistance or support  to  agencies
    25  and  local and regional jurisdictions, to integrate statewide resilience
    26  goals into future projects, plans, and programs, and  to  foster  inter-
    27  municipal cooperation;
    28    (e)  Establish  a means of tracking progress toward statewide goals on
    29  climate resilience;
    30    (f) Identify and develop policies necessary to implement  a  statewide
    31  resilience plan and risk reduction strategy;
    32    (g) Establish and maintain a website which shall facilitate the satis-
    33  faction of the functions and duties of the office;
    34    (h)  Establish  and maintain a principal office and such other offices
    35  within the state as it may deem necessary;
    36    (i) Appoint a secretary, counsel, clerks and such other employees  and
    37  agents as it may deem necessary, fix their compensation within the limi-
    38  tations provided by law, and prescribe their duties; and
    39    (j)  Require  that  state  agencies  and  any other state or municipal
    40  department, agency, public authority, task force, commission,  or  other
    41  state  or  municipal  government  body,  provide and the same are hereby
    42  authorized to provide, such assistance,  documents,  and  data  as  will
    43  enable the office to carry out its functions and duties.
    44    §  933.  Chief  resilience officer. 1. The head of the office shall be
    45  the chief resilience officer who shall be appointed by the governor  and
    46  who shall hold office at the pleasure of the governor.
    47    2.  The  chief  resilience officer shall have the following functions,
    48  powers and duties:
    49    (a) Employ or allocate the necessary staff and request the  assistance
    50  of  personnel  of  any state department or agency to carry out the func-
    51  tions, powers and duties  provided  in  this  article  or  as  otherwise
    52  provided by law;
    53    (b)  Manage  the office, the budget for such office, and related func-
    54  tions as provided by law;

     2  resilience  and  risk  mitigation  activities  to develop and present an

     4    (d)  Represent  the policy and consensus viewpoint of the state at the

     6  and risk mitigation;
     7    (e) Monitor and seek available funds to support the state's resilience

    10    (f) Provide strategic direction for interagency and cross-disciplinary
    11  initiatives  to  build resilience, in collaboration with the other rele-
    12  vant resilience task force and entities as the chief resilience  officer
    13  deems  appropriate,  for the purposes of climate resilience planning and
    14  goal development, tracking and reporting progress on climate  resilience
    15  goals, and public engagement on climate resilience issues;
    16    (g)  Appraise  the adequacy of statutory and administrative mechanisms
    17  for coordinating the state's policies and programs at  both  the  intra-
    18  state  and  interstate  levels,  and  between  federal, state, and local

    20    (h) Develop, where appropriate, intrastate or intergovernmental agree-
    21  ments to formalize coordination roles for regional resilience  projects,
    22  such as the New York-New Jersey harbor and tributaries project;
    23    (i)  Appraise  policy  barriers  to  meet  the goals of the state with

    25    (j) Serve as subject-matter expert for the state on issues related  to
    26  resilience and mitigation and provide recommendations to the legislature
    27  and  federal congress with respect to policies, programs, and coordinat-
    28  ing mechanisms relative to resilience and risk mitigation;
    29    (k) Assist with the state's planning efforts, including but not limit-
    30  ed to a statewide resilience plan, the state hazard mitigation plan, and
    31  other relevant state and regional plans  for  which  there  is  a  state
    32  interest, to ensure the incorporation and alignment of the state's resi-
    33  lience  goals  and  objectives  into  a unified, proactive, pre-disaster
    34  approach to adaptation and near-, mid-, and long-term resilience;
    35    (l) To serve as a clearinghouse  for  the  benefit  of  municipalities
    36  regarding information relating to flooding, extreme heat, and other risk
    37  prevention  and  mitigation,  including impact prevention and mitigation
    38  project funding programs, and other information relating to their common
    39  problems with respect  to  these  hazards  and  the  state  and  federal
    40  services available to assist in solving such problems;
    41    (m)  Take other actions consistent with law as deemed necessary by the
    42  chief resilience officer to carry out such officer's duties,  functions,
    43  and responsibilities.
    44    §  934.  Statewide  resilience  plan.  1. To coordinate and strengthen
    45  efforts to reduce losses from future disasters  across  the  state,  the
    46  office  shall  contribute  to  all statewide planning efforts related to
    47  resilience and risk mitigation and shall develop a  strategic  statewide
    48  resilience plan to protect the state from multiple climate threats.
    49    2. Such plan shall include, but not be limited to, the following:
    50    (a) Articulation of the state's resilience goals and objectives;
    51    (b)  Utilization  of  the best available science, including a range of
    52  future  projections,  to  identify,  implement,  or   reform   policies,
    53  projects,  and  programs  to  achieve  the  state's resilience goals and
    54  objectives;
    55    (c) Recommended agency-specific strategic actions, including  criteria
    56  for prioritization based on a vulnerability assessment of the risks from

     1  multiple   environmental   threats  to  agency  mission  areas,  assets,
     2  services, and populations served;
     3    (d)   Prioritization  of  natural,  nature-based,  and  non-structural
     4  approaches to mitigating climate threats, wherever  possible  including,
     5  without  limitation,  use  of  living  shorelines, riparian restoration,
     6  permeable surfaces, rain gardens, green roofs,  tree  canopy  expansion,
     7  wetland  restoration,  removing, altering, or right-sizing dams, natural
     8  area conservation, waste-water and stormwater  infrastructure  upgrades,
     9  alteration  of  structures,  buyouts,  and  other flood and extreme heat
    10  prevention, mitigation and resiliency strategies or projects;
    11    (e) Set goals and resilience indicators  that  shall  be  tracked  and
    12  reported to the public over time in an annual progress report; and
    13    (f)  A  framework  for  resilience  project  development, funding, and
    14  implementation.  Such framework shall include, but not  be  limited  to,
    15  the following:
    16    (i)  Spatial analysis of projected climate threat exposure and vulner-
    17  ability, including but not limited to flood, extreme heat  and  precipi-
    18  tation,  storm  events, and wildfire, and other risks. Such analysis and
    19  resulting maps should delineate the geography and the social and ecolog-
    20  ical vulnerability of the risk, using the state's environmental  justice
    21  and  disadvantaged  community  layers  and  including climate-vulnerable
    22  ecosystems, leveraging existing information  from  the  New  York  state
    23  climate  impacts  assessment, the New York city panel on climate change,
    24  and other regional, peer-reviewed,  best  available  scientific  source,
    25  wherever feasible;
    26    (ii)  An accessible, updated database or inventory of critical infras-
    27  tructure vulnerable to current and future flooding, developed in collab-
    28  oration with municipalities.  This includes those that are essential for
    29  critical government and business functions, national security, transpor-
    30  tation, utilities, public health and  safety,  the  economy,  flood  and
    31  storm protection, water quality management, and wildlife habitat manage-
    32  ment;
    33    (iii) Maps or accessible, visual representation of federal, state, and
    34  local  municipal and county projects planned to reduce such risks, along
    35  with the federal, state, or local agencies leading  those  projects  and
    36  the funding source; and
    37    (iv)  A strategic plan for developing, funding, and financing projects
    38  that address such risks  through  federal,  state,  local,  and  private
    39  sources. Such strategic plan shall:
    40    (1)  Include  a strategy for how to make every effort practicable that
    41  disadvantaged communities, as identified pursuant to section 75-0111  of
    42  the  environmental  conservation  law, receive at least forty percent of
    43  the benefits of proposed plans and projects; provided,  however,  disad-
    44  vantaged  communities  shall receive no less than thirty-five percent of
    45  such benefits; and
    46    (2) Seeks to build alignment and efficiencies  across  agency  vulner-
    47  ability assessments and resilience strategies.

    55    (b) The commissioner of the department of environmental  conservation,

    22    §  936.  State  agency  resilience  coordinators.  Each  state  agency
    23  included  in  the  resilience  task  force  and any other agencies to be
    24  included in resilience planning as designated by  the  chief  resilience
    25  officer  or resilience task force shall appoint a resilience coordinator
    26  to work with the chief resilience officer to ensure resilience is  inte-
    27  grated  into  agency  missions and priorities, and  otherwise coordinate
    28  with the chief resilience officer.  Such coordinators shall serve on the
    29  interagency resilience coordination team established pursuant to section
    30  nine hundred thirty-seven of this article. Each such  coordinator  shall
    31  be  appointed  by  a state agency with the exclusive role of focusing on
    32  climate resilience with such agency's mission and activities.
    33    § 937. Interagency resilience coordination team. 1.  There  is  hereby
    34  established  within  the  office  an interagency resilience coordination
    35  team to maintain awareness, communication, and alignment with regard  to
    36  the  state's resilience and risk mitigation needs, progress, and priori-
    37  ties and to oversee development of the statewide resilience plan.
    38    2. Such team shall:
    39    (a) Be comprised of resilience coordinators  from  each  state  agency
    40  included in this article or otherwise designated by the chief resilience
    41  officer  or  resilience task force and the chief resilience officer, who
    42  shall serve as chair;
    43    (b) Meet upon the call of the chair, with a minimum of  four  meetings
    44  annually;
    45    (c) Develop strategic plans for agencies and collaborate in the devel-
    46  opment of a statewide resilience plan; and
    47    (d)  Develop and implement a plan for public engagement, review of key
    48  products of the statewide resilience  plan,  and  track  and  report  on
    49  progress of such plan over time.
    50    3. The chief resilience officer shall convene the first meeting of the
    51  interagency  resilience coordination team on or before the ninetieth day
    52  after the effective date of this section.
    53    § 938. Public engagement and reporting. 1. Public engagement. A state-
    54  wide resilience plan shall be developed and the  resilience  task  force
    55  shall  hold  at  least six regional public comment hearings on the draft
    56  plan, including three meetings in the upstate region and three  meetings

     1  in  the  downstate  region,  and shall allow at least one hundred twenty
     2  days for the submission of public comment. The task force shall  provide
     3  meaningful  opportunities  for  public  comment from all segments of the
     4  population  that  will be impacted by the plan, including persons living
     5  in disadvantaged communities as identified pursuant to  section  75-0111
     6  of the environmental conservation law.
     7    2.  Reporting. No later than one year after the effective date of this
     8  section, and every five years thereafter, the office shall complete  and
     9  submit  an updated statewide resilience plan to the legislature and make
    10  such plan publicly available.
