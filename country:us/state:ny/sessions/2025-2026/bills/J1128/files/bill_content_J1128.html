Senate Resolution No. 1128
 
BY: Senator MARTINS
 
        CONGRATULATING Marianno Sarro upon the occasion of
        celebrating his 100th Birthday
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the custom of this Legislative Body to commemorate
and recognize certain milestones celebrated by citizens  of  this  great
Empire State; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Senior citizens bring a wealth of experience and knowledge
to the increasingly active roles they play  in  today's  society;  their
past  contributions  and  future  participation are a vital part of, and
valuable asset to, the fabric of community life and activity; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing  traditions,  this  Legislative  Body  is  justly proud to
congratulate Marianno Sarro upon the occasion  of  his  100th  Birthday,
celebrated on Saturday, May 24, 2025; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Marianno  Sarro,  affectionately known as "Mud" by all who
knew him, was born a century ago on May 24, 1925 to Louis and  Crucifisa
Sarro  in  Oyster Bay, New York where he grew up and attended Oyster Bay
High School with his three brothers and three sisters; and
 
  WHEREAS, Showing an affinity  for  music,  Marianno  Sarro  found  a
particular talent and passion for playing the drums; and
 
  WHEREAS,  After  his graduation from high school, Marianno Sarro was
drafted into the military, and proudly served three years  in  the  Navy
where he was later stationed in Guam; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Upon  his return from his service, Marianno Sarro's father
bought him a set of drums and his passion for music grew with his talent
for the drums; wanting to follow his growing love for music he  attended
Hartnett   Music   School  in  Manhattan  where  he  studied  piano  and
percussion; and
 
  WHEREAS, While attending music school, Marianno Sarro met  the  love
of his life Catherine (Kate) Yannucci; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Marianno  and  Kate  Sarro married on February 9, 1946 and
remained in Oyster Bay  where  he  worked  for  Sunset  Meat  Market,  a
long-standing  butcher  shop  owned  by  his  dad  that served the local
community; and
 
  WHEREAS, The couple started their family in Oyster  Bay  having  two
sons,  Louis  and  Ronald and then, in 1955, they moved to Bethpage, New
York where, in 1956 and 1963, they were blessed with two more sons, Gary
and James, respectively; and
 
  WHEREAS, With a growing family to  support,  Marianno  Sarro  worked
many  years  for  the Long Island Lighting Company and Northrop Grumman,
and retired from the Nassau County Highway Department in 1990; and
 
  WHEREAS, Throughout his working life, Marianno  Sarro  continued  to
play  the drums professionally on the weekends while giving drum lessons
at night during the week as a secondary source of  income;  one  of  his
students  eventually became the long-standing drummer for the well-known
1960's pop group called "The Brooklyn Bridge"; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Marianno  Sarro,  with his zest for life, has inspired and
enhanced the lives of his family and friends; and
 
  WHEREAS, Following in their  father's  footsteps,  Marianno  Sarro's
four  sons  learned  to play the drums, two of whom earned music degrees
and also played professionally; and
 
  WHEREAS, This exceptional centenarian has experienced the incredible
joys and sorrows characteristic of  and  reserved  for  those  with  the
stamina and courage to savor a full life; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Filled  with  an  incredible  sense  of love and devotion,
Marianno Sarro remained by the side of his beloved wife throughout their
70-year marriage, caring for her until she passed in 2016; and
 
  WHEREAS, In addition to being a wonderful father to his  four  boys,
Marianno   Sarro   is   a   doting  grandfather  to  his  six  cherished
grandchildren  and  great  grandfather  to  his   nine   beloved   great
grandchildren; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Marianno  Sarro's  undying  love  for  his family, healthy
lifestyle, and razor sharp humor undoubtedly keeps him  young  at  heart
and contributes to his longevity; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Marianno  Sarro has enriched the lives of those around him
through his joyous and sincere love for others and through the  profound
charm and wisdom which comes only from a fullness of years; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  intent  of  this Legislative Body to publicly
recognize those who have reached such a  remarkable  age  and  who  have
witnessed  and  celebrated  the  innovations,  cultural developments and
awesome achievements of this country  during  the  last  century,  while
themselves  contributing  to  the  growth  and  excellence of this great
Empire State; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
congratulate  Marianno  Sarro upon the occasion of celebrating his 100th
Birthday; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Marianno Sarro.
