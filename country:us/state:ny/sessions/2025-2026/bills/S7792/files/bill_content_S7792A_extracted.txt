AN ACT to amend the mental hygiene law, in relation to protecting the
communication rights of individuals with disabilities
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The mental hygiene law is amended by adding a new section
2 13.45 to read as follows:
3 § 13.45 New York state communication bill of rights for individuals with
4 disabilities.
5 In order to ensure that each person with a disability is able to lead
6 a life of dignity, all persons with a disability shall have the right to
7 communicate in their preferred manner and all staff and providers under
8 this section, shall have the obligation to ensure and support such
9 rights, which shall include, but not be limited to:
10 (a) the right to utilize any communication method that meets their
11 needs, including but not limited to:
12 (1) augmentative and alternative communication (AAC) techniques and
13 devices;
14 (2) spelling boards, letterboards and typing-based communication;
15 (3) sign language and non-verbal gestural systems; and
16 (4) speech-generating devices or any other assistive technology;
17 (b) the right to have such individual's communication method recog-
18 nized and supported in schools, community residences, and public insti-
19 tutions;
20 (c) the right to speech therapy and communication support by duly
21 licensed professionals without arbitrary restrictions;

1 (d) the right to have at least one trained staff member available in

4 (e) the right to receive appropriate communication supports from
