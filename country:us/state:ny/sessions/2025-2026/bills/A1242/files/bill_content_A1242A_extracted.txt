AN ACT to establish a task force to identify evidence-based and evidence
informed solutions to reduce exposure to adverse experience
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. A task force on adverse experiences and trauma prevention
2 is hereby established to examine, evaluate, accept public comment and
3 make recommendations concerning the impact of adverse experiences and
4 preventive and trauma-informed policy and practices.
5 § 2. The task force shall consist of 10 members to be appointed as
6 follows:
7 a. One member appointed by the department of health;
8 b. One member appointed by the office of children and family services;
9 c. One member appointed by the state education department;
10 d. One member appointed by the department of criminal justice
11 services;
12 e. One member appointed by the temporary president of the senate;
13 f. One member appointed by the speaker of the assembly; and
14 g. Four members appointed by the governor.
15 § 3. The members of the task force shall each have experience in or
16 represent the following areas: juvenile justice, social services, public
17 health, childhood development, minority and ethnic groups, education
18 prekindergarten through grade 12. The governor shall designate the chair
19 of the task force which shall be filled in the manner provided for
20 original appointments.

1 § 4. The members of the task force shall receive no compensation for
2 their services, but shall be allowed their actual and necessary expenses
3 incurred in the performance of their duties pursuant to this act.
4 § 5. The task force shall perform the following duties:
5 (1) Study the impacts of various types of adverse experiences with the
6 goal of developing a comprehensive understanding of these experiences
7 and their lifelong impacts on individuals. These traumas shall include,
8 but not be limited to:
9 a. Community violence: violence in neighborhoods, such as shootings,
10 gang violence, and other forms of community trauma.
11 b. Systemic racism and discrimination: the chronic stress and trauma
12 experienced by individuals and communities due to racism, discrimi-
13 nation, and oppression.
14 c. Climate-related trauma: natural disasters, such as hurricanes,
15 wildfires, and floods, as well as the anxiety and distress associated
16 with climate change.
17 d. Technological trauma: cyberbullying, online harassment, and expo-
18 sure to disturbing virtual content.
19 e. Intergenerational trauma: trauma that is passed down through gener-
20 ations as the result of war, genocide, or other large-scale traumatic
21 events.
22 f. Complex trauma: trauma that is repeated, or prolonged.
23 (2) Engage social services, office of children and family services,
24 education, health, and criminal justice systems in creation of trauma-
25 informed policy and practices in each of these systems to prevent
26 adverse childhood experiences from occurring and support the health and
27 well-being of all families.
28 (3) Identify social determinants of health and well-being and recom-
29 mend solutions to eliminate racial and ethnic disparities in the state
30 in relation to the occurrence of adverse experiences.
31 § 6. To the maximum extent feasible, the task force shall be entitled
32 to request and receive, and shall utilize and be provided with such
33 facilities, resources and data of any department, division, board,
34 bureau, commission, district or agency of the state or any political
35 subdivision thereof as it may reasonably request to properly carry out
36 its powers and duties pursuant to this act.
37 § 7. The task force shall submit, within 2 years of the effective date
38 of this act, a report of its findings, conclusions and recommendations
39 to the governor, temporary president of the senate, speaker of the
40 assembly, minority leader of the senate, minority leader of the assem-
41 bly, chair of senate education committee, chair of assembly education
42 committee, chair of senate health committee, chair of assembly health
43 committee, chair of senate social services committee, chair of assembly
44 social services committee, chair of senate children and families commit-
45 tee and chair of assembly children and families committee.
46 § 8. This act shall take effect immediately.
