Senate Resolution No. 1153
 
BY: Senator HARCKHAM
 
        MOURNING  the  death  of  Neil  Francis  Gollogly,
        distinguished citizen  and  devoted  member  of  his
        community
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to pay tribute to
citizens  of  the  State  of  New York whose lifework and civic endeavor
served to enhance the quality of life in their communities and the great
State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, Neil Francis Gollogly, of Yorktown Heights, New York,  died
on Sunday, June 1, 2025, at the age of 92; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 1932, Neil Francis Gollogly was born to Neil T. Gollogly
and Anna Daly, hard-working Scottish immigrants, in Tarrytown, New York;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  As  a  first-generation  Scottish  American,  Neil Francis
Gollogly lived a life defined by selflessness, service, and devotion  to
both  family  and  community;  when  he  was a young child, he delivered
newspapers and served as an altar boy at St. Theresa's Parish  where  he
also  attended  grammar  school;  he graduated from North Tarrytown High
School where he excelled as a member of the track team; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 1951,  Neil  Francis  Gollogly  was  drawn  to  military
service  and  entered  the  United  States  Navy  where he trained as an
electrician and developed a lifelong love for the sea;  after  his  Navy
days, he settled in Millwood, New York, with his beloved wife, Katherine
(Kae), where the couple raised five children; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Neil  Francis  Gollogly  was  an  Electrician  Foreman  at
Reader's Digest for many years; in 1958, he  joined  the  Millwood  Fire
Company,  moving  his  way  up the ranks, and in 1969, he was named Fire
Chief; tragically, that same year, he became a widower and single father
to his five young children; and
 
  WHEREAS, In 1976, Neil Francis Gollogly found love again and married
Nan; in 1983, he welcomed his sixth child; and
 
  WHEREAS, Neil Francis Gollogly never  stopped  volunteering,  rising
through  the  ranks of community leadership and eventually becoming Fire
Commissioner of the Millwood Fire District; and
 
  WHEREAS, Neil Francis Gollogly played a  pivotal  role  in  securing
plans  for a new firehouse, laying the groundwork for future generations
of first responders; he was also a Charter  Member  of  the  Westchester
County  Parade Judges Association, enjoying the fellowship and tradition
of the region's fire service; and
 
  WHEREAS, Neil Francis Gollogly is survived by his devoted wife of 49
years, Nan; children,  Neil  (Beth)  Gollogly,  Thomas  Gollogly,  Kathy
(Stan)    Heist,   Marianne   Reba,   and   Douglas   (Kate)   Gollogly;
daughter-in-law, Sandy  Gollogly;  grandchildren,  Kate,  Kelly,  Kevin,
Taylor,  Neil,  Kim,  Scott,  Erin,  and  Grace;  and  great-grandchild,
 
one-year-old Pippen; he was predeceased  by  his  sister,  Mary  (Frank)
Galgano, and his beloved son, Kevin Gollogly; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Neil  Francis  Gollogly always acted with conviction and a
deep commitment to his community; his strong values and faith shaped the
family he raised and the legacy he leaves  behind;  he  will  always  be
remembered  for  his  integrity,  fierce work ethic, sense of humor, and
friendly smile; and
 
  WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a  sense  of
compassion, Neil Francis Gollogly leaves behind a legacy which will long
endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all
he served and befriended; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
mourn the death of Neil Francis Gollogly; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to the family of Neil Francis Gollogly.
