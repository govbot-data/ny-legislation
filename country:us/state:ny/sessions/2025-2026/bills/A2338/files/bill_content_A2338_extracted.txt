It is hereby found and declared that many 14 New York residents have served our nation honorably in the armed forces 15 of the United States, and that many such New Yorkers have sacrificed 16 considerably during their service. It is in the public interest both to 17 demonstrate our appreciation for that service by providing state finan- 18 cial assistance to such veterans; in order to encourage home ownership 19 and the rehabilitation of deteriorating housing, stabilize communities 20 and improve the local tax base. 21 § 1115-a. "Person" means an honorably discharged veteran of the Armed Forces 24 of the United States, or their surviving unmarried spouse. There is hereby established a "veteran's home and land ownership 37 loan fund". Such fund shall be used to make loans authorized by this 38 article and for expenses incurred by the agency in the implementation of 39 the program established by this article. 40 § 1115-c. Purpose and terms of loan. 1. The agency, acting on behalf 41 of the state may in its discretion, enter into a contract with an eligi- 42 ble person to provide a loan to assist in the purchase of a dwelling or 43 the purchase and rehabilitation of a dwelling containing up to four 44 residential units, provided such person shall reside in at least one of 45 such units. Such loan may also be made for the purchase of unimproved 46 real property when such property shall be used for the construction of a 47 new dwelling. 48 2. Such loan shall not exceed twenty percent of the value for the 49 purchase of a home, or forty thousand dollars for the purchase of unim- 50 proved real property. Such value shall be determined from the appraisal, 51 if any, required by the lending institution granting the first mortgage 52 loan on such dwelling, and if no such appraisal has been made at the 53 time that a contract for loan is entered into pursuant to this article, 54 the agency shall cause such appraisal to be made. 55 § 1115-d. Loan repayment. 1. Any loan contracted for pursuant to this 56 article shall be secured by a second mortgage on the dwelling or unim- A. 2338 3 1 proved real property purchased by the recipient of such loan if the 2 recipient of such loan assigns, transfers or otherwise conveys the 3 recipient's interest in such dwelling or ceases to occupy such dwelling, 4 the unpaid principal balance of said second mortgage, together with 5 interest thereon, shall become due and payable. If the recipient of any 6 loan is unable to repay the loan, the agency, at its discretion, may 7 adjust the interest rate, terms and conditions of the loan to facilitate 8 repayment. 9 2. Repayment of any loan provided in accordance with this article 10 shall be subject to an interest rate to be determined in accordance with 11 terms and conditions as the agency may establish. In no case shall the 12 term exceed the term of the first mortgage obtained for the purpose of 13 purchasing such dwelling except, in the case of a graduated payment 14 mortgage loan, the term of the loan made pursuant to this article may 15 exceed the expected term of such mortgage loan provided. The term of the 16 loan made pursuant to this article does not exceed the lesser of: 17 (a) The term indicated by the limited amortization schedule of the 18 graduated payment mortgage loan; or 19 (b) Thirty years. Payments by homeowners shall be paid to the agency 20 and deposited in the veteran's home and land ownership loan fund. 21 § 1115-e. Issuance of bonds. 1.
