AN ACT to amend the public health law, in relation to authorizing insti-
tutional dispensers to dispense controlled substances for use off
premises in an emergency situation
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Subdivision 2 of section 3342 of the public health law, as
2 amended by chapter 466 of the laws of 2024, is amended and a new subdi-
3 vision 4 is added to read as follows:
4 2. An institutional dispenser may dispense controlled substances for
5 use off its premises only pursuant to a prescription, prepared and filed
6 in conformity with this title, provided, however, that, in an emergency
7 situation as defined by rule or regulation of the department, a practi-
8 tioner in a hospital [without a full-time pharmacy] may dispense
9 controlled substances to a patient in a hospital [emergency room] for
10 use off the premises of the institutional dispenser for a period not to
11 exceed twenty-four hours, unless the federal drug enforcement adminis-
12 tration has authorized a longer time period for the purpose of initiat-
13 ing maintenance treatment, [detoxification treatment] withdrawal manage-
14 ment, or both.
15 4. For the purpose of initiating maintenance treatment, withdrawal
16 management, or both, an institutional dispenser may dispense controlled
17 substances for use off its premises in an emergency situation for a
18 period not to exceed three days for one person's use in accordance with
19 federal laws and regulations, unless the federal drug enforcement admin-
20 istration has authorized a longer time period.
21 § 2. Section 3350 of the public health law, as added by chapter 878 of
22 the laws of 1972, is amended to read as follows:

1 § 3350. Dispensing prohibition. Controlled substances may not be
2 prescribed for, or administered or dispensed to [addicts or habitual
3 users of controlled substances] individuals who suffer or are suspected
4 of suffering from substance use disorder, except as provided by this
5 title or title [III] three of this article.
6 § 3. Subdivisions 2 and 3 of section 3351 of the public health law, as
7 added by chapter 878 of the laws of 1972, are amended and a new subdivi-
8 sion 6 is added to read as follows:
9 2. Controlled substances may be ordered for use by an [addict or
10 habitual user] individual by a practitioner and administered by a prac-
11 titioner or registered nurse to relieve acute withdrawal symptoms.
12 3. Methadone, or such other controlled substance designated by the
13 commissioner as appropriate for such use, may be ordered for use of an
14 [addict] individual by a practitioner and dispensed or administered by a
15 practitioner or [his] such practitioner's designated agent as interim
16 treatment [for an addict on a waiting list for admission to an author-
17 ized maintenance program].
18 6. For the purpose of initiating maintenance treatment, withdrawal
19 management, or both, a practitioner may order and dispense but not
20 prescribe methadone or such other controlled substance designated by the
21 commissioner as appropriate for such use for use off premises in an
22 emergency situation for a period not to exceed three days for one
23 person's use in accordance with federal laws and regulations, unless the
24 federal drug enforcement administration has authorized a longer time
25 period.
26 § 4. The title heading of title 5 of article 33 of the public health
27 law, as added by chapter 878 of the laws of 1972, is amended to read as
28 follows:
29 DISPENSING TO [ADDICTS AND HABITUAL USERS]
30 INDIVIDUALS WITH SUBSTANCE USE DISORDER
31 § 5. This act shall take effect on the ninetieth day after it shall
32 have become a law; provided, however, that if chapter 466 of the laws of
33 2024 shall not have taken effect on or before such date then section one
34 of this act shall take effect on the same date and in the same manner as
35 such chapter of the laws of 2024 takes effect.
