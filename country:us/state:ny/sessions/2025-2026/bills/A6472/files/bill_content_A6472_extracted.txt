DAIS -- read once and referred to the Committee on Mental Health AN ACT to establish a Black youth suicide prevention task force; and providing for the repeal of such provisions upon expiration thereof The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Black youth suicide prevention task force. (a) There is 2 hereby created the Black youth suicide prevention task force to examine, 3 evaluate and determine how to improve mental health and suicide 4 prevention for Black New York residents aged 5 though 18 years, consist- 5 ing of seven members, each to serve until two years after the effective 6 date of this act. 7 (b)(1) Such members shall be appointed as follows: one member shall be 8 appointed by the governor; one member shall be appointed by the tempo- 9 rary president of the senate; one member shall be appointed by the 10 minority leader of the senate; one member shall be appointed by the 11 speaker of the assembly; one member shall be appointed by the minority 12 leader of the assembly; and two members shall be appointed by the 13 commissioner of mental health. Appointments shall be made within sixty 14 days of the effective date of this act. Vacancies in the task force 15 shall be filled in the same manner provided for original appointments. 16 (2) All appointees shall have expertise in fields or disciplines 17 related to mental health suicide prevention services, supports and know- 18 ledge related to the Black community. All appointments shall be coordi- 19 nated to ensure geographic representation from the entire state. 20 (3) The task force shall be chaired by the governor's appointee. The 21 task force shall elect a vice-chair by majority vote and other necessary 22 officers from among all appointed members. 23 (4) The task force shall meet at least quarterly at the call of the 24 chair. Meetings may be held via teleconference. Special meetings may be EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. Such committees shall be chaired by a task force member, but 16 may be composed of task force members as well as other individuals 17 selected by the task force to provide expertise of interest specific to 18 the charge of such committees. 19 (e) The task force may, as it deems appropriate, request that studies, 20 surveys and analyses relating to the task force's powers and duties be 21 performed by any state department, commission, agency or public authori- 22 ty. All state departments, commissions, agencies or public authorities 23 shall provide information and advice in a timely manner and otherwise 24 assist the task force with its work; provided however, any information 25 or records otherwise confidential and privileged in accordance with 26 state or federal law that are provided to the task force pursuant to 27 this subdivision shall remain confidential as provided by such state or 28 federal law and provided, further, that reports of the task force shall 29 not identify the name or related identifying information concerning 30 individual persons who are deceased as a result of suicide. 31 (f) The task force shall provide a preliminary report to the governor 32 and the legislature of its findings, conclusions, recommendations and 33 activities already undertaken by the task force, not later than thirteen 34 months after the effective date of this act, and a final report of its 35 findings, conclusions, recommendations and activities already undertaken 36 by the task force, not later than twenty-two months after the effective 37 date of this act and shall submit with its reports legislative proposals 38 as it deems necessary to implement its recommendations. 39 § 2. This act shall take effect on the sixtieth day after it shall 40 have become a law and shall expire 2 years after such effective date 41 when upon such date the provisions of this act shall be deemed repealed.
