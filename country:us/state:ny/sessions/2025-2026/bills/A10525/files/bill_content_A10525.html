Provides an exemption for a certain parcel of land in Manhattan from selling alcohol within a certain distance from a church; provides for the repeal of certain provisions upon expiration thereof.

A10525 Text:




 
                STATE OF NEW YORK
        ________________________________________________________________________
 
                                          10525
 
                   IN ASSEMBLY
 
                                      March 6, 2026
                                       ___________
 
        Introduced  by M. of A. BORES -- read once and referred to the Committee
          on Economic Development
 
        AN ACT to amend the alcoholic beverage control law, in  relation  to  an
          exemption  for  certain property from the prohibition of alcohol sales
          within a certain distance from a church; and providing for the  repeal
          of such provisions upon expiration thereof

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Subdivision 7 of  section  64  of  the  alcoholic  beverage
     2  control  law  is  amended  by  adding  a new paragraph (e-18) to read as
     3  follows:
     4    (e-18) Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph (a) of this  subdi-
     5  vision,  the  authority  may  issue  a  retail  license  for on-premises
     6  consumption for a premises which shall be within two hundred feet  of  a
     7  building  occupied  exclusively  as a school, church, synagogue or other
     8  place of worship, provided such premises constitutes a premises for  the
     9  sale  of  food  or  beverages  at retail for consumption on the premises
    10  located wholly within the boundaries of the borough of Manhattan, county
    11  of New York, city and state  of  New  York,  bounded  and  described  as
    12  follows:
    13    Any  licensed  retailer located on the first floor and basement of all
    14  that certain plot, piece or  parcel  of  land  with  the  buildings  and
    15  improvements thereon erected, situate, lying and being in the Borough of
    16  Manhattan,  City, County and State of New York, bounded and described as
    17  follows:
    18    BEGINNING at a point formed by the intersection of the  westerly  side
    19  of  Second Avenue with the northerly side of 53rd Street, running thence
    20  Northerly along the westerly side of Second Avenue, 25  feet  5  inches,
    21  thence  Westerly and parallel with 53rd Street and through the center of
    22  a party wall, 61 feet 5 inches, thence Southerly  parallel  with  Second
    23  Avenue,  25  feet  5  inches  to  the northerly side of 53rd Street, and
    24  thence Easterly along the northerly side of 53rd Street, 61 feet 5 inch-
    25  es to the point of place of beginning.
 
         EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets
                              [ ] is old law to be omitted.

        A. 10525                            2
 
     1    SAID PREMISES also being known as  and  by  the  street  address  1003
     2  Second  Avenue,  New York, New York and designated as Block 1327, Lot 21
     3  as shown on the tax map of the City of New York, County of New York.
     4    §  2.  This act shall take effect immediately; provided, however, that
     5  upon the sale or transfer of the parcel detailed in paragraph (e-18)  of
     6  subdivision  7  of  section 64 of the alcoholic beverage control law, as
     7  added by section one of this act, such paragraph  shall  expire  and  be
     8  deemed repealed unless the licensee immediately obtains a leasehold upon
     9  such sale or transfer of such parcel as part of the sale or transfer. If
    10  the  licensee obtains such a leasehold, such paragraph (e-18) pertaining
    11  to such parcel shall expire and be  deemed  repealed  upon  the  end  or
    12  termination  of  such lease. The state liquor authority shall notify the
    13  legislative bill drafting commission upon notification by  the  licensee
    14  of the sale, transfer or termination of the leasehold, or non-renewal of
    15  the  leasehold in order that the commission may maintain an accurate and
    16  timely effective data base of the official text of the laws of the state
    17  of New York in furtherance of effectuating the provisions of section  44
    18  of the legislative law and section 70-b of the public officers law.
