CASHMAN -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Corporations, Authorities and Commissions AN ACT to amend the public authorities law, in relation to establishing a commission on hosting the olympic games The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The public authorities law is amended by adding a new 2 section 2611-a to read as follows: 3 § 2611-a. Commission on hosting the olympic games. 1. When used in 4 this section the following terms shall have the following meanings: 5 (a) "commission" shall mean the commission established under this 6 section. 7 (b) "olympics" shall mean the olympic games. 8 2. There is hereby established within the New York state olympic 9 regional development authority a commission on hosting the olympic games 10 which shall consist of thirteen members and shall have and exercise the 11 powers and duties set forth in this section. 12 3. The commission shall consist of thirteen members of whom three 13 shall be appointed by the governor, two each by the speaker of the 14 assembly and the temporary president of the senate, one each by the 15 minority leader of the senate and the minority leader of the assembly, 16 and four by the authority. Any vacancy on such commission shall be 17 filled in the same manner as the original appointment was made. Members 18 of the commission shall be residents of the state. 19 4. Members of the commission shall designate a chair from the member- 20 ship thereof for a term of one year. Any member of the commission may 21 call a meeting. Seven members shall constitute a quorum. The commission 22 may meet and hold public and/or private hearings within or without the 23 state, and shall have all the powers of a legislative committee pursuant 24 to the legislative law. 25 5. The members of the authority shall not receive a salary or other 26 compensation for their services as members of the commission but each 27 member shall be allowed reimbursement for the necessary and actual EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
