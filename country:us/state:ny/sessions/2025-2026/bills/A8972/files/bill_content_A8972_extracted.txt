The history and civic impact of the September eleventh, two 15 thousand one terrorist attacks advisory committee; appointment; powers 16 and duties. 1. The commissioner shall establish a history and civic 17 impact of the September eleventh, two thousand one terrorist attacks 18 advisory committee. 19 2. (a) The committee shall be comprised of four members, to be 20 appointed as follows: 21 i. one member appointed by the governor; 22 ii. one member appointed by the temporary president of the senate; 23 iii. one member appointed by the speaker of the assembly; and 24 iv. one member appointed by the commissioner. 25 (b) All members appointed to the committee shall be experts in the 26 history and civic impact of the September eleventh, two thousand one 27 terrorist attacks and/or have a background in educational instruction 28 and/or educational administration. 29 (c) All members of the committee shall be appointed within ninety days 30 of the effective date of this section. 31 3. Vacancies in the membership of the committee shall be filled in the 32 same manner provided for original appointments. 33 4. The committee shall meet no less than four times per year. 34 5. The committee shall provide recommendations on the following: 35 (a) high-quality K-12 curricular and instructional resources on the 36 history and civic impact of the September eleventh, two thousand one 37 terrorist attacks; and 38 (b) strategies for strengthening and deepening public K-12 education 39 programs in the areas of the history and civic impact of the September 40 eleventh, two thousand one terrorist attacks, including analysis of the 41 survey referenced in section two of the chapter of the laws of two thou- 42 sand twenty-five that added this section. 43 6. The committee shall submit their recommendations to the commission- 44 er no later than two years after the effective date of this section and 45 be disbanded thereafter. 46 7. Committee members shall receive no compensation for their services, 47 but shall be allowed their actual and necessary expenses incurred in the 48 performance of their duties. 49 8. To effectuate the purposes of this section, the committee may 50 request from any department, board, bureau, commission or other agency 51 of the state, and the same are authorized to provide, such cooperation 52 and assistance, services and data as will enable the committee to prop- 53 erly carry out its functions pursuant to this section. 54 § 4.
