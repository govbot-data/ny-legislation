COLTON, RIVERA, WILLIAMS, DE LOS SANTOS -- read once and referred to the Committee on Election Law AN ACT to amend the election law, in relation to providing bilingual voting materials The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Legislative findings and declaration of policy. The legis- 2 lature finds that, through the use of various practices and procedures, 3 citizens of language minorities have been effectively excluded from 4 participation in the electoral process. Among other factors, the denial 5 of the right to vote of such minority group citizens is ordinarily 6 directly related to the unequal educational opportunities afforded them 7 resulting in high illiteracy and low voting participation. The legisla- 8 ture declares that, in order to enforce the guarantees of the United 9 States Constitution, it is necessary to eliminate such discrimination by 10 prohibiting these practices, and by prescribing other remedial devices. 11 § 2. The election law is amended by adding a new section 3-505 to read 12 as follows: 13 § 3-505. Bilingual voting materials requirement. 1. Generally. Before 14 August sixth, two thousand twenty-six, no covered state or political 15 subdivision shall provide voting materials only in the English language. 16 2. Covered states and political subdivisions. (a) Generally. A state 17 or political subdivision is a covered state or political subdivision for 18 the purposes of this section if the director of the census determines, 19 based on census data, that: 20 (1) more than five percent of the citizens of voting age of such state 21 or political subdivision are members of a single language minority and 22 are limited-English proficient; or 23 (2) more than ten thousand of the citizens of voting age of such poli- 24 tical subdivision are members of a single language minority and are 25 limited-English proficient. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. LBD03907-01-5 A. 2521 2 1 (b) Exception. The prohibitions of this section do not apply in any 2 political subdivision that has less than five percent voting age limit- 3 ed-English proficient citizens of each language minority which comprises 4 over five percent of the statewide limited-English proficient population 5 of voting age citizens, unless the political subdivision is a covered 6 political subdivision independently from its state. 7 3. Definitions. As used in this section: 8 (a) The term "voting materials" means registration or voting notices, 9 forms, instructions, assistance, or other materials or information 10 relating to the electoral process, including ballots; 11 (b) The term "limited-English proficient" means unable to speak or 12 understand English adequately enough to participate in the electoral 13 process; 14 (c) The term "citizens" means citizens of the United States; and 15 (d) The term "illiteracy" means the failure to complete the fifth 16 primary grade. 17 4. Special rule. The determinations of the director of the census 18 under this section shall be effective upon publication in the Federal 19 Register and shall not be subject to review in any court. 20 5. Requirement of voting notices, forms, instructions, assistance, or 21 other materials and ballots in minority language. Whenever any state or 22 political subdivision subject to the prohibition of this section 23 provides any registration or voting notices, forms, instructions, 24 assistance, or other materials or information relating to the electoral 25 process, including ballots, it shall provide them in the language of the 26 applicable minority group as well as in the English language. Provided, 27 that where the language of the applicable minority group is oral or 28 unwritten or in the case of Alaskan natives and American Indians, if the 29 predominant language is historically unwritten, the state or political 30 subdivision is only required to furnish oral instructions, assistance, 31 or other information relating to registration and voting. 32 6. Action for declaratory judgment permitting English-only materials. 33 Any state or political subdivision subject to the prohibition of this 34 section, which seeks to provide English-only registration or voting 35 materials or information, including ballots, may file an action against 36 the United States in the United States District Court for a declaratory 37 judgment permitting such provision. The court shall grant the requested 38 relief if it determines that the illiteracy rate of the applicable 39 language minority group within the state or political subdivision is 40 equal to or less than the national illiteracy rate. 41 § 3.
