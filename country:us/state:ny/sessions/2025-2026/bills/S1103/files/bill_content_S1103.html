Add §15-0519, En Con L
 
Establishes the New York state cooperative watershed management program within the department of environmental conservation under which watershed networks may apply for grants for watershed network development and watershed network professionalization, subject to appropriation of funds from the legislature.

        AN ACT to amend the  environmental  conservation  law,  in  relation  to
          establishing  the  New  York  state  cooperative  watershed management
          program

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The environmental conservation law is amended by adding a
     2  new section 15-0519 to read as follows:
     3  § 15-0519. Cooperative watershed management program.
     4    1. There is hereby established within the department a "New York state
     5  cooperative watershed management program" for the purpose  of  providing
     6  funding  to  watershed  networks to encourage such watershed networks to
     7  form local or regional solutions to address their  water  and  watershed
     8  management needs.
     9    2. For the purposes of this section the following terms shall have the
    10  following meanings:
    11    (a)  "Program"  means the New York state cooperative watershed manage-
    12  ment program established pursuant to subdivision one of this section.
    13    (b)  "Watershed  network"  means  a  self-sustaining,  non-regulatory,
    14  consensus-based  network  that  is composed of a diverse array of stake-
    15  holders, which may include but shall not be  limited  to,  lake  associ-
    16  ations  and  other  watershed-related  non-profit organizations, munici-
    17  palities, federal, state, or  local  agencies,  and  tribal  nations.  A
    18  "watershed  network"  may  be specific to a defined watershed devoted to
    19  one water body or may represent an array of such groups  working  across
    20  connected  watersheds  in  a region. A "watershed network" may be housed
    21  within a watershed management council, a lake association,  intermunici-
    22  pal  body,  or  a municipal government, but shall represent a variety of
    23  stakeholders in such watershed or region.

     1    (c) "Watershed network development"  means  activities  to  develop  a
     2  watershed  network, including, but not limited to, development of bylaws
     3  or mission statements, performing stakeholder outreach, pursuing  recog-
     4  nition as a nonprofit, or other similar organization activities.
     5    (d)   "Watershed  network  professionalization"  means  activities  to
     6  professionalize a watershed network,  including,  but  not  limited  to,
     7  hiring  professional  staff  for  watershed management or grant writing,
     8  developing watershed restoration plans, or designing  watershed  manage-
     9  ment projects.
    10    3.  (a) The program shall develop a process whereby watershed networks
    11  may apply to the program for grants for watershed network development or
    12  watershed network professionalization.
    13    (b) The program shall develop criteria for a competitive award process
    14  for approval or denial of grant  applications  from  watershed  networks
    15  pursuant  to  paragraph  (a)  of  this  subdivision. Such criteria shall
    16  include, but not be limited to, the following for approval  of  a  grant
    17  application:
    18    (i)  The applicant watershed network shall be comprised of two or more
    19  public or private stakeholders; and
    20    (ii) The applicant  watershed  network  has  demonstrated  stakeholder
    21  buy-in to the watershed network development or watershed network profes-
    22  sionalization  plan  by  submitting  a complete budget proposal for such
    23  plan, and by submitting letters of support from such stakeholders.
    24    4. (a) A watershed network selected for a watershed  network  develop-
    25  ment  grant  pursuant  to  this  section shall be awarded a grant not to
    26  exceed twenty thousand dollars. The  same  watershed  network  shall  be
    27  eligible  to  receive  a watershed network development grant pursuant to
    28  this section no more frequently than once every five years.
    29    (b) A watershed network selected for a watershed network professional-
    30  ization grant pursuant to this section shall be awarded a grant  not  to
    31  exceed  forty  thousand  dollars.  The  same  watershed network shall be
    32  eligible to receive a watershed network professionalization grant pursu-
    33  ant to this section annually for up to three consecutive years.
    34    (c) A watershed network shall not be eligible to receive  a  watershed
    35  network  development  grant and a watershed professionalization grant in
    36  the same year.
    37    5. Funds awarded by the program pursuant  to  this  section  shall  be
    38  subject to appropriation therefor by the legislature.
    39    §  2.  This  act  shall take effect on the sixtieth day after it shall
    40  have become a law. Effective immediately, the addition, amendment and/or
    41  repeal of any rule or regulation necessary  for  the  implementation  of
    42  this  act  on its effective date are authorized to be made and completed
    43  on or before such effective date.
