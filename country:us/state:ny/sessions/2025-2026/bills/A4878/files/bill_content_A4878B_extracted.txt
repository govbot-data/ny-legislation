AN ACT to amend the mental hygiene law, in relation to the establishment
of mental health clubhouses
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The mental hygiene law is amended by adding a new section
2 7.49 to read as follows:
3 § 7.49 Mental health clubhouses.
4 (a) Definitions. For the purposes of this section, the term "mental
5 health clubhouse" or "clubhouse" means a facility that:
6 1. is accredited by a recognized body or bodies approved by the
7 office;
8 2. addresses psychosocial deficits and social determinants of health;
9 3. uses a therapeutic approach that emphasizes purposeful engagement,
10 relationship development, and mutual support for people with serious
11 mental illness;
12 4. has members and program staff work side by side to build and manage
13 a non-clinical therapeutic community;
14 5. offers members a community-based rehabilitative environment that
15 both addresses psychosocial needs of people living with serious mental
16 illness and provides structures that offer support in natural, community
17 environments;

1 6. offers support services for employment, education, health care,
2 housing, and other services to broadly address health-related social
3 care needs;
4 7. is an essential part of the continuum of care and works in tandem
5 with other medical and behavioral health services;
6 8. relies on the restorative power of work and work-mediated relation-
7 ships to build a firm foundation for growth and individual achievement
8 for members;
9 9. emphasizes strengths-based teamwork and opportunities to contribute
10 to operations through a work-ordered day; and
11 10. offers social and community-based recreational opportunities to
12 contribute to a member's path and success in recovery.
13 (b) Mental health clubhouses; establishment. 1. Clubhouses shall be
14 established within reasonable proximity to public transportation, if
15 practicable. If feasible and within available resources, virtual club-
16 house programming shall be implemented by an accredited clubhouse to
17 ensure accessibility to clubhouse support services in rural areas or
18 other parts of the state without access to a physical clubhouse.
19 2. The office shall develop a statewide implementation plan through
20 technical advising and consultation with established clubhouses to
21 assist with the accreditation process for any clubhouse in the state and
22 with implementation of the provisions under this section within one year
23 of the effective date of this article.
24 (c) Operational standards and core services. Membership of clubhouses
25 shall be:
26 1. voluntary, free of cost, and open to any individual with a history
27 of serious mental illness; and
28 2. shall receive accreditation or be in the process of becoming
29 accredited within the first three years of operation by a recognized
30 body.
31 (d) Oversight, compliance, and reporting. 1. Each clubhouse shall
32 submit a comprehensive report every two years to the office detailing:
33 (i) clubhouse active membership statistics and daily service utiliza-
34 tion;
35 (ii) employment placements and workforce development outcomes;
36 (iii) housing placements and homelessness prevention efforts; and
37 (iv) crisis intervention cases and emergency responses.
38 2. The office shall conduct biennial audits of all clubhouses to
39 ensure compliance with regulations promulgated by the office consistent
40 with this section and shall be research-based, utilizing information
41 from sources with expertise in the operation and the accreditation of a
42 clubhouse.
43 § 2. This act shall take effect on the ninetieth day after it shall
44 have become a law.
