AN ACT to amend the public health law, in relation to the establishment
of mental health clubhouses
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The public health law is amended by adding a new article
2 29-L to read as follows:
3 ARTICLE 29-L
4 MENTAL HEALTH CLUBHOUSES
5 Section 2999-nn. Definitions.
6 2999-oo. Mental health clubhouses; establishment.
7 2999-pp. Operational standards and core services.
8 2999-qq. Oversight, compliance, and reporting.
9 § 2999-nn. Definitions. For the purposes of this article, the term
10 "mental health clubhouse" or "clubhouse" means a facility that:
11 1. is accredited or is in the process of becoming accredited, by a
12 recognized body or bodies approved by the office of mental health;
13 2. addresses psychosocial deficits and social drivers of health;
14 3. uses a therapeutic approach that emphasizes purposeful work,
15 engagement, relationship development, and mutual support for people with
16 serious mental illness;
17 4. uses blended leadership of professional mental health staff and
18 individuals with lived experience to create and manage the therapeutic
19 community and its services in a nonclinical setting;
20 5. offers members a community-based rehabilitative environment that
21 both addresses often unmet psychosocial needs of people living with
22 serious mental illness and provides structures that offer support in
23 natural, community environments;

1 6. offers support services for employment, education, health care,
2 housing, and more to broadly address health-related social care needs;
3 7. is an essential part of the continuum of care and works in tandem
4 with other medical and behavioral health services;
5 8. relies on the restorative power of work and work-mediated relation-
6 ships to build a firm foundation for growth and individual achievement
7 for members;
8 9. emphasizes strengths-based teamwork and opportunities to contribute
9 to operations through a work-ordered day; and
10 10. offers naturalistic social and community-based recreational oppor-
11 tunities to contribute to a member's path and success in recovery.
12 § 2999-oo. Mental health clubhouses; establishment. 1. Clubhouses
13 shall be established within reasonable proximity to public transporta-
14 tion. In rural areas, virtual clubhouse programming shall be imple-
15 mented to ensure accessibility.
16 2. The department and the office of mental health shall develop a
17 statewide implementation plan through technical advising and consulta-
18 tion with established clubhouses for the provisions of this article
19 within one year of the effective date of this article.
20 § 2999-pp. Operational standards and core services. 1. Membership of
21 clubhouses shall be voluntary, free of cost, and open to any individual
22 with a history of serious mental illness.
23 2. Clubhouses shall receive accreditation or be in the process of
24 becoming accredited within the first three years of operation by a
25 recognized body.
26 § 2999-qq. Oversight, compliance, and reporting. 1. Each clubhouse
27 shall submit a comprehensive report to the department and the office of
28 mental health detailing:
29 (a) clubhouse active membership statistics and daily service utiliza-
30 tion;
31 (b) employment placements and workforce development outcomes;
32 (c) housing placements and homelessness prevention efforts; and
33 (d) crisis intervention cases and emergency responses.
34 2. The department shall conduct biennial audits of all clubhouses to
35 ensure compliance with state regulations.
36 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
