        Introduced  by  Sen.  LIU  --  read  twice and ordered printed, and when

        AN ACT in relation to providing a World War II service bonus for certain
          veterans
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section  1.  Legislative  intent.  The legislature finds that many New
     2  York residents served honorably in the armed forces of the United States
     3  after September 2, 1945, the date  recognized  in  the  New  York  State
     4  Constitution  for  the  purposes  of the World War II service bonus, but
     5  before the cutoff date of December 31, 1946 for  World  War  II  veteran
     6  status as recognized by the federal government. Due to this discrepancy,
     7  there  are  many  service members who were conferred WWII Veteran status
     8  and benefits by the federal government, but not  by  the  state  of  New
     9  York. It is the intent of this legislature to fully honor these unrecog-
    10  nized  individuals,  or  their next of kin, under terms similar to those
    11  provided in section 18 of Article VII of the New York State Constitution
    12  to honor their service to the United States and the state of New York by
    13  means of a bonus similar to what was provided under section 18 of  Arti-
    14  cle  VII  of the New York State Constitution.  The bonus would go a long
    15  way in acknowledging the inequity these veterans faced, restoring parity
    16  with their fellow service members, and offering a lasting expression  of
    17  gratitude from the people of New York.
    18    § 2. 1. Definitions. For the purposes of this act:
    19    (a)  "Qualifying Member of the Armed Forces" shall mean members of the
    20  Army, Navy, Marine Corps, Coast Guard, Army Air Forces, Merchant Marines
    21  of the United States as well as members of the public health service  or
    22  coast and geodetic survey while engaged in active service with the armed
    23  forces  of  the United States, including the Army, Navy, Marine Corps or
    24  Coast Guard, who did not receive a bonus pursuant to Chapter 547 of  the
    25  laws of 1947.

     1    (b)  "Qualifying  service"  means active duty with the armed forces of
     2  the United States at any time during the period  beginning  December  7,
     3  1941  and  ending  December  31, 1946, and separation or discharge under
     4  honorable conditions.
     5    (c)  "Resident"  means  an  individual  who was a resident of New York
     6  state for a period of at least six months as of December 31, 1946.
     7    (d) "Next of Kin" means the lawful surviving spouse, child, parent  or
     8  sibling of the member of the armed forces, in that order of precedence.
     9    2. Bonus eligibility. A bonus of $250 shall be paid to surviving qual-
    10  ifying  members  of  the armed forces who performed a qualifying service
    11  and was a  resident  of  New  York  state  prior  to  their  enlistment,
    12  induction,  or  call  to  active duty. The next of kin of any qualifying
    13  member of the armed forces shall be  entitled  to  such  bonus  if  such
    14  member:
    15    (a)  performed  a  qualifying  service  and was a resident of New York
    16  state prior to their enlistment, induction, or call to active  duty  and
    17  died after their separation or discharge under honorable conditions; and
    18    (b)  was  a  resident  of  New  York  state prior to their enlistment,
    19  induction, or call to active duty and died while on active  duty  during
    20  the period December 7, 1941 and ending December 31, 1946.
    21    3.  Administration. (a) The Department of Veterans' Services is desig-
    22  nated as the agency to receive, examine and pay bonus claims filed here-
    23  under, and the New York State  Commissioner  of  Veterans'  Services  is
    24  authorized  to  adopt  rules  and regulations to perform such duties and
    25  functions as may be necessary for the proper administration of this act,
    26  including the establishment of a "WWII  Veterans'  Equity  bonus  fund".
    27  Funding  for  the  bonus  shall  come from existing funds, not otherwise
    28  obligated, of the Department of Veterans' Services.
    29    (b) The Department of Veterans' Services shall assist persons entitled
    30  to any bonus in the preparation of claims and all agencies of the  state
    31  shall  cooperate  with  the commissioner to effectuate the provisions of
    32  this act.
    33    (c) Applications for such bonus shall be accepted for 5 years from the
    34  effective date of this act, and no bonus payment shall be made on appli-
    35  cations filed subsequent to such date by any person, unless such  person
    36  can  show  that  they were incapable of making such application prior to
    37  such date because of physical or mental incapacity.
    38    § 3. This act shall take effect immediately.
