Arnold, affectionately known as Bob, led a life defined by devotion to family, a tireless work ethic, and deep compassion for others; and WHEREAS, After honorably serving for two years in the United States Merchant Marines, Bob Arnold joined New York State Electric and Gas (NYSEG) in 1960, where he built a respected 39-year career as a lineman, retiring in 1999; and WHEREAS, Demonstrating a lifelong commitment to learning and service, Bob Arnold earned a bachelor's degree in sociology from Pace University in 1999 and began a second career as an addiction treatment counselor at St. Christopher's Inn in Garrison, New York, where he worked until 2005 and continued to volunteer through 2008; and WHEREAS, A man of deep and abiding faith, Bob Arnold was a devoted parishioner of St. James the Apostle Church in Carmel, New York, and lived out his values through service to others and unconditional love for his family; and WHEREAS, On August 18, 1962, Bob Arnold married the love of his life, the former Carol Frances Dolan, at Saint Francis of Assisi Church in Mount Kisco, New York; together they shared 57 years of marriage and raised three children: Melissa, Noreen, and Matthew; and WHEREAS, Bob and Carol Arnold treasured traveling together, visiting destinations near and far including Cape Cod, Bermuda, Ireland, Italy, and France, and shared a special tradition of visiting The Red Lion Inn in Stockbridge, Massachusetts every December; and WHEREAS, Bob Arnold found joy in exploring his family's genealogy, celebrating his heritage, and cherishing time with his grandchildren; and WHEREAS, Predeceased by his beloved wife Carol in 2020, Robert J. Arnold is survived by his loving children, Melissa Della Posta (Joseph), Noreen Gill, and Matthew Arnold; his seven grandchildren, Natalie, Daniel, and Ethan Della Posta, Nyla and Emma Gill, and Hannah and Jacob Arnold; his brother, Richard (Kathy) Arnold and sister, Deborah Arnold; and WHEREAS, Robert J. Arnold's life was a testament to selfless service, the strength of family, and a faithful heart, and his memory will live on in the hearts of all those whose lives he touched; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of Robert John Arnold, noting his purposeful life and generous spirit; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Robert John Arnold.
