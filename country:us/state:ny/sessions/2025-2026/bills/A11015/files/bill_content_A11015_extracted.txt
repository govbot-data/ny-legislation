Article 27 of the environmental conservation law is amended 2 by adding a new title 34 to read as follows: 3 TITLE 34 4 STATE COMPOSTING PROGRAMS 5 Section 27-3401. State composting programs. 6 § 27-3401. State composting programs. 7 1. For the purposes of this section, the following terms shall have 8 the following meanings: 9 (a) "agency" means any department, agency, board, public benefit 10 corporation, public authority, or commission; and 11 (b) "compostable" means all the materials in the product will (i) 12 undergo degradation by biological processes during composting to yield 13 carbon dioxide, water, inorganic compounds, and biomass at a rate 14 consistent with other known compostable materials; and (ii) leave no 15 visible, distinguishable or toxic residue, including no adverse impact 16 on the ability of composts to support plant growth once the finished 17 compost is placed in soil. 18 2. Beginning one year after the effective date of this section, all 19 state agencies shall establish a composting program in buildings owned, 20 occupied or operated by such agencies that shall, at a minimum: 21 (a) require that all compostable waste including but not limited to 22 food scraps, plant trimmings, food-soiled paper and certified composta- 23 ble products from garbage and other recyclables be separated and placed 24 in labeled containers; 25 (b) post and maintain signs with instructions on identifying and sepa- 26 rating compostable waste from garbage and recyclables; EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. Each state agency may arrange for collection of compostable waste 9 by a private carter, transport such waste itself or process such waste 10 on-site. 11 4. Beginning one year after the establishment of the program pursuant 12 to subdivision two of this section, and annually thereafter, each state 13 agency shall report to the department on such program, including, but 14 not limited to (i) an estimate of the amount of compostable waste 15 collected; and (ii) the costs associated with such program. 16 5. (a) An agency need not comply with subdivisions two and three of 17 this section with respect to a particular building if it provides the 18 department with a written explanation that compliance for such building 19 would be infeasible or would impose unreasonable costs because: (i) the 20 building generates only a de minimis quantity of compostable waste; (ii) 21 the building is located in a municipality or region with limited compost 22 management infrastructure, or insufficient service availability neces- 23 sary to support compliance; or (iii) geographic, climatic, or environ- 24 mental conditions such as high elevation, extreme temperatures, or other 25 factors that interfere with biological decomposition processes, make the 26 establishment or maintenance of composting operations impracticable. 27 (b) An agency must reevaluate each building for which it is not in 28 compliance every five years and submit a new non-compliance explanation, 29 as applicable, at such time. 30 (c) The provisions of subdivisions two and three of this section shall 31 not apply to buildings located within a municipality which establishes 32 and implements a composting program by adopting a local law or ordi- 33 nance. 34 Buildings required to comply with a municipal composting program 35 enacted by a municipal law or ordinance are exempt from the requirements 36 of subdivisions two and three of this section. 37 6. By December first, two thousand thirty, and annually thereafter, 38 the department shall post a report on its website detailing state agency 39 composting programs in the state. Such report shall include an evalu- 40 ation of the effectiveness of such composting programs, information on 41 costs and collection rates, and compliance exemptions pursuant to subdi- 42 vision five of this section. 43 § 2.
