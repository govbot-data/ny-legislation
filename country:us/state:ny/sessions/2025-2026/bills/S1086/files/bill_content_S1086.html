Add Art 4-D §§49 - 49-b, Civ Rts L; amd §89, Pub Off L; amd §5, Tax L; amd §3-503, Gen Ob L; amd Ed L, generally; amd §§110 & 126, rpld §126 sub 3, ABC L; amd §10, Gen City L; rpld §3421 sub 2 ¶(a), Pub Health L; amd §41, Gen Bus L; amd §341, Lab L; amd §460, Judy L
 
Enacts the empire state licensing act to provide all New Yorkers with access to professional, occupational, commercial, or business licenses, permits, certificates, or related registrations regardless of an applicant's citizenship or immigration status.

     3  requires otherwise:
     4    1. "License" shall mean any state or local government  issued  profes-

     9  ment, board, bureau, commission, or  other  public  entity  that  issues
    10  licenses,  permits,  certificates or any other state or local government
    11  issued permission to operate.
    12    § 49-a. Nondiscrimination based on citizenship or immigration  status.
    13  1. Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, no other-
    14  wise  qualified  applicant  for  a  license or renewal thereof, shall be
    15  denied to any individual or entity based on the immigration or  citizen-
    16  ship status of such applicant.
    17    2.  Any  existing  requirement  based  on United States citizenship or
    18  permanent residence shall be deemed null and void  for  the  issuing  or
    19  renewal of a license, as defined in section forty-nine of this article.
    20    3.  No  issuing entity shall inquire into, request or require proof of
    21  an applicant's citizenship, lawful immigration status, country of  birth
    22  or national origin.
    23    4.  If an issuing entity does not currently have a policy in place for
    24  reviewing and analyzing  relevant  experience  obtained  in  the  United
    25  States or abroad, such issuing entity shall create a policy for evaluat-
    26  ing experience for substantial equivalency to existing requirements in a
    27  relevant  field.  Provided, however, that such policy shall be available
    28  to the public and posted on the entity's website, if any.
    29    5.  When English-language proficiency is determined to not  be  neces-
    30  sary  by  an issuing entity to issue or maintain a license, the applica-
    31  tion process, including any related examination under the issuing  enti-
    32  ty, shall be made accessible in an applicant's primary language.
    33    §  49-b.  Application; confidentiality. 1.  Every issuing entity shall
    34  create and/or maintain processes for the protection from  disclosure  of
    35  identifying  information  for  any  purpose other than those required by
    36  law, and to comply with the confidentiality requirements herein.
    37    2. The issuing  entity shall not retain originals  or  copies  of  any
    38  documents  that  may  be  probative  of  an  applicant's place of birth,
    39  including, but not limited to, an applicant's birth certificate,  consu-
    40  lar  identification  card, passport, social security card, or individual
    41  taxpayer identification number card, for longer than the time needed  to
    42  review  the  application.  Nothing in this subdivision shall prevent the
    43  issuing or registering entity from retaining documents where such  docu-
    44  ments  are  required  by  law  to be preserved for the purposes of liti-
    45  gation.
    46    3. The issuing entity shall  not  disclose  information  or  documents
    47  collected  that  may  be  probative  of  an  applicant's place of birth,
    48  including, but not limited  to,  country  of  passport  issuance,  birth
    49  certificate,  consular  identification  card,  passport, social security
    50  number or social security card, or  individual  taxpayer  identification
    51  number or individual taxpayer identification card unless such disclosure
    52  is:
    53    (a)  authorized  in writing by the individual to whom such information
    54  pertains, or if such individual is a minor or is otherwise  not  legally
    55  competent, by such individual's parent or legal guardian; or
