A03784 Summary:

BILL NOA03784
 
SAME ASSAME AS S02411
 
SPONSORJones
 
COSPNSRMcDonald, Lunsford, Clark, Buttenschon
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Amd §19-0327, En Con L
 
Relates to bioheating fuel requirements; allows B100 or B99 biofuel and R100 or R99 renewable fuel to be used to blend with or replace fossil heating oil in order to create bioheating fuel.

        Introduced  by M. of A. JONES -- read once and referred to the Committee

        AN ACT to amend the  environmental  conservation  law,  in  relation  to
          bioheating fuel requirements
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section 1. Section 19-0327 of the environmental conservation  law,  as
     2  amended by chapter 750 of the laws of 2021, paragraph (b) of subdivision
     3  1  and  subdivision  9  as  amended by chapter 5 of the laws of 2022, is
     4  amended to read as follows:
     5  § 19-0327. Bioheating fuel requirements.
     6    (1) Definitions. For the purpose of this section, the following  terms
     7  shall have the following meanings:
     8    (a)  ["Biodiesel"] "Biofuel" shall mean a fuel, designated one hundred
     9  percent B100 or a blend of ninety-nine percent B99 biodiesel by  volume,
    10  that  meets  the  requirements  of  the ASTM international specification
    11  D6751 or the requirements of the ASTM international specification  D975;
    12  provided,  however, that biofuel shall include R100 or R99 produced from
    13  hydroprocessing renewable biomass feedstocks.
    14    (b) "Renewable Hydrocarbon Diesel" shall mean a  [North  American-pro-
    15  duced]  fuel derived from vegetable oils, animal fats, and other renewa-
    16  ble feedstocks that meet the requirements of ASTM international specifi-
    17  cation D975. Renewable hydrocarbon  shall  not  include  any  fuel  from
    18  co-processed biomass with a feedstock that is not biomass.
    19    (c)  "Bioheating  fuel"  shall  mean  a  fuel, designated B100 or B99,
    20  comprised of [biodiesel] biofuel blended with petroleum heating oil that
    21  meets the requirements of the ASTM international specification  D396,  a
    22  [fuel  comprised  of domestically-produced renewable hydrocarbon diesel]
    23  biofuel, designated R100 or R99 with petroleum heating  oil  that  meets
    24  the  specifications  of  ASTM international specification D975, or other
    25  specifications as determined by the commissioner.

     1    (d) "Heating oil" shall mean petroleum oil refined for the purpose  of
     2  use  as  fuel for combustion in a space and/or water heating system that
     3  meets the requirements of the [ATSM]  ASTM  international  specification
     4  D396 or other specifications as determined by the commissioner.
     5    (e)  "Feedstock"  shall mean soybean oil, oil from annual cover crops,
     6  algal oil, biogenic waste oils, fats or greases, or non-food grade  corn
     7  oil, provided that the commissioner may, by rules and regulations, modi-
     8  fy  the definition of feedstock based on the vegetable oils, animal fats
     9  or cellulosic biomass listed in table 1 of 40 C.F.R. § 80.1426.
    10    (2) On and after July first, two thousand eighteen,  all  heating  oil
    11  sold  for use in any building within the counties of Nassau, Suffolk and
    12  Westchester shall be bioheating fuel that contains at least five percent
    13  [biodiesel] biofuel.
    14    (3) On or after July first, two thousand twenty-two, all  heating  oil
    15  sold  for  use in any building within the state shall be bioheating fuel
    16  that contains at least five percent [biodiesel] biofuel.
    17    (4) On or after July first, two thousand twenty-five, all heating  oil
    18  sold  for  use in any building within the state shall be bioheating fuel
    19  that contains at least ten percent [biodiesel] biofuel.
    20    (5) On or after July first, two thousand thirty, all heating oil  sold
    21  for  use  in any building within the state shall be bioheating fuel that
    22  contains at least twenty percent [biodiesel] biofuel.
    23    (6) The minimum content requirements of this section shall  not  apply
    24  to kerosene.
    25    (7)  The  governor  may,  by  issuing  an executive order, temporarily
    26  suspend the applicability of this section  at  any  time  based  on  the
    27  governor's determination, after consulting with the New York state ener-
    28  gy  research  and development authority and the department, that meeting
    29  the requirement of subdivision two of this section is not  feasible  due
    30  to  lack  of  adequate supply of [biodiesel] biofuel or that meeting the
    31  requirement would result in financial hardship to consumers.
    32    (8) The requirements of this section shall not: (a) prohibit the  sale
    33  and  use  of  bioheating fuel with the same or any greater percentage of
    34  [biodiesel] biofuel blended with heating oil, or the sale and use of one
    35  hundred percent [biodiesel] biofuel,  for  space  and/or  water  heating
    36  purposes  in the state; and (b) preempt any laws of the city of New York
    37  with respect to mandated percentage levels of [biodiesel] biofuel blends
    38  with heating oil provided that such laws require  the  same  or  greater
    39  percentage  of  [biodiesel] biofuel blended with heating oil as required
    40  by this section.
    41    (9) No bioheating fuel shall be sold to a customer that would void the
    42  manufacturer's warranty for  that  burner  or  boiler  type  unless  the
    43  [bioheat] bioheating fuel provider warranties in writing to the customer
    44  that  the  use of such bioheating fuel will be covered to the extent and
    45  time limits of the warranty then covering such burner or boiler.
    46    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
