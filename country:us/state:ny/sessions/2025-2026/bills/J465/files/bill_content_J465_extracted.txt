Arthur Klamka was one such individual; and WHEREAS, It is with profound sorrow and deep regret that this Legislative Body records the passing of Arthur Klamka, noting the significance of his purposeful life and accomplishments; and WHEREAS, Arthur "Artie" Klamka, a cherished religious advisor and firefighter who spent decades serving local fire departments and his community, died on Wednesday, January 1, 2025, at the age of 85; and WHEREAS, Known for his unwavering dedication and compassionate spirit, Arthur Klamka touched countless lives during his 45 years of service; and WHEREAS, Arthur Klamka joined the Goldens Bridge Fire Department in 1979 after moving to the area with his wife, Judy; he brought with him a wealth of experience from his time serving in the Ossining and Croton Falls Fire Departments; and WHEREAS, Over the years, Arthur Klamka became a central figure in Northern Westchester's firefighting community, not only as an active department member but also as a trusted chaplain; and WHEREAS, In addition to his service as chaplain, Arthur Klamka participated in numerous fire department committees and activities, truly embodying the spirit of community service; and WHEREAS, Throughout his meritorious life, Artie was known for his selflessness, generosity, and kindness, leaving a lasting impact on the countless lives of all whose lives he touched; and WHEREAS, Arthur Klamka's infinite selflessness and benevolence will shine on through his family's vivid and happy memories; his insight and strength will forever serve as a beacon of love, light and hope to the countless lives he touched; and WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of compassion, Arthur Klamka leaves behind a legacy which will long endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all who were privileged to have known and loved such an amazing man; he will be deeply missed and truly merits the grateful tribute of this Legislative Body; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of Arthur Klamka, and to express its deepest condolences to his family; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Arthur Klamka.
