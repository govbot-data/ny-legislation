        Introduced  by  Sen. JACKSON -- read twice and ordered printed, and when

        AN ACT to amend the retirement and social security law, in  relation  to
          making  certain  benefits  available to members of the Dutchess county
          sheriff's  department;  and  providing  for  the  repeal  of   certain
          provisions upon the expiration thereof
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Subdivision k of section 89-p of the retirement and  social
     2  security  law  is  relettered  subdivision  l and a new subdivision k is
     3  added to read as follows:
     4    k. (1) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary,  Dutchess
     5  county  may elect to make available to the members described in subdivi-
     6  sions a and d of this section the twenty-year retirement  plan  provided
     7  by  section five hundred sixty-one of this chapter; provided that Dutch-
     8  ess county shall pay the cost attributable therefor.
     9    (2) The benefits provided by this subdivision shall be available  only
    10  to  those  members defined in paragraph one of this subdivision upon the
    11  adoption of a  resolution  specifically  stating  that  Dutchess  county
    12  elects  to  offer the benefit provided by section five hundred sixty-one
    13  of this chapter, as authorized by this subdivision and filing  a  certi-
    14  fied copy thereof with the comptroller. Such resolution may also contain
    15  an  election  that any past service cost be paid over either a five-year
    16  or ten-year period. Such resolution shall be accompanied by the  affida-
    17  vit  of  the chief executive officer of Dutchess county that such county
    18  has received an estimate from the retirement system of the cost  of  the
    19  benefit provided by section five hundred sixty-one of this chapter.
    20    (3)  Such  resolution  shall apply to all members defined in paragraph
    21  one of this subdivision, except those already subject  to  a  retirement
    22  plan  which permits immediate retirement with a benefit upon a specified
    23  period of service of twenty years or less without regard to age.

     1    § 2. The retirement and social security law is amended by adding a new
     2  section 561 to read as follows:
     3    §  561.  Optional retirement plan for Dutchess county deputy sheriffs.
     4  a. A member of the retirement system employed  by  Dutchess  county  may
     5  elect  to  become  a  member  pursuant to the provisions of this section
     6  within one year after such member becomes a member, if  Dutchess  county
     7  has  elected  to make the benefits provided herein available to members,
     8  or within one year after Dutchess county elects  to  make  the  benefits
     9  provided herein available to its members.
    10    b.  Elections  made  pursuant  to this section shall be in writing and
    11  shall be duly acknowledged and filed with the  comptroller.  Any  member
    12  who  files  such  an  election  pursuant to this section may withdraw it
    13  after it has been filed for at least one year. Such withdrawal shall  be
    14  by written notice duly acknowledged and filed with the comptroller.
    15    c.  A member participating on the basis of this section at the time of
    16  retirement, shall be entitled to retire after the completion  of  twenty
    17  years  of  total creditable service or upon the attainment of age sixty-
    18  two, by filing an application therefor  in  a  manner  similar  to  that
    19  provided in this chapter.
    20    1.  Upon  completion  of twenty years of such service and upon retire-
    21  ment, each such member shall receive a  pension  sufficient  to  provide
    22  such  member  with  a retirement allowance equal to one-fortieth of such
    23  member's final average salary for each year of total creditable  service
    24  for  which  such  member  is otherwise entitled but not exceeding in the
    25  aggregate one-half of such  member's  final  average  salary;  provided,
    26  however,  if upon retirement, such member shall have credited service in
    27  excess of twenty years, such member shall  receive,  for  each  year  of
    28  service  in excess of twenty, an additional pension which shall be equal
    29  to one-sixtieth of such member's  final  average  salary;  and  provided
    30  further  that the total allowance payable pursuant to this section shall
    31  not exceed three-quarters of such member's final average salary.
    32    2. Upon attainment  of  age  sixty-two  and  upon  retirement  without
    33  completion  of  twenty  years  of  such  service, each such member shall
    34  receive a pension sufficient to provide such member  with  a  retirement
    35  allowance  equal  to one-fortieth of their final average salary for each
    36  year of creditable service. Every such member shall also be entitled  to
    37  an  additional  pension  equal  to  the pension for any other creditable
    38  service rendered as otherwise provided for in this chapter. This  latter
    39  pension  shall not increase the total allowance to more than one-half of
    40  such member's final average salary.
    41    d. Dutchess county may elect to provide any service credit earned with
    42  a public employer prior to services performed as a sheriff, undersheriff
    43  or deputy sheriff towards the one-sixtieth allowance provided  by  para-
    44  graph one of subdivision c of this section.
    45    e.  The  increased pension, as provided by this section, shall be paid
    46  from additional contributions made  by  the  participating  employer  on
    47  account  of  such  members.  The  actuary of the retirement system shall
    48  compute the additional contribution required for each member who  elects
    49  to receive the special benefits provided under this section.  Such addi-
    50  tional  contributions  shall  be  computed on the basis of contributions
    51  during the prospective service of  such  member  which  will  cover  the
    52  liability  of  the  retirement  system  for  such  extra  pensions. Upon
    53  approval of the comptroller,  such  additional  contributions  shall  be
    54  certified  by  the comptroller to the Dutchess county chief fiscal offi-
    55  cer. The amount thereof shall be included in the annual appropriation of
    56  Dutchess county. Such amount shall be paid on the warrant of  the  chief

     1  fiscal  officer  of  Dutchess county to the pension accumulation fund of
     2  the retirement system.
     3    f.  In  computing  the  twenty years of completed service of a member,
     4  full credit shall be given for military service as defined  in  subdivi-
     5  sions  twenty-nine-a  and  thirty  of  section three hundred two of this
     6  chapter.
     7    g. Every member participating on the basis of this  section  shall  be
     8  separated  from  the  service on the last day of the calendar month next
     9  succeeding the calendar month in which such member  attains  age  sixty-
    10  two,  provided,  however,  that  such  a  member who attained the age of
    11  sixty-two before the  Dutchess  county  elected  to  make  the  benefits
    12  provided  herein  available  to  such  member, or who attains the age of
    13  sixty-two within one month after the Dutchess county makes such benefits
    14  available, to be eligible for a pension computed in accordance with  the
    15  provisions  of  this section, shall be separated from the service within
    16  three months after the Dutchess county makes such benefits available.
    17    h. The provisions of this section shall be controlling notwithstanding
    18  any provision in this article to the contrary.
    19    i. The benefits of this section shall be available upon Dutchess coun-
    20  ty electing to provide such benefits by adopting a  resolution  to  such
    21  effect and filing a certified copy thereof with the comptroller.
    22    j.  The  benefits  hereinabove  provided shall be payable to a member,
    23  unless at the date of retirement, such member would otherwise  be  enti-
    24  tled  to  a  greater  benefit under other provisions of this chapter had
    25  such member withdrawn from this section, in  which  event  such  greater
    26  benefits shall be payable.
    27    k.  1. As used in this section "creditable service" shall include, any
    28  and all services performed as a sheriff, undersheriff or deputy sheriff,
    29  provided, however, that criminal law enforcement service shall  only  be
    30  creditable  when it aggregates fifty per centum or more of their service
    31  as a deputy sheriff.
    32    2. Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph one  of  this  subdivi-
    33  sion, Dutchess county may elect on a form filed with the comptroller for
    34  that  purpose  to provide that creditable service shall also include any
    35  and all service performed by a deputy sheriff who is  a  police  officer
    36  pursuant  to  subdivision  thirty-four  of  section 1.20 of the criminal
    37  procedure law as certified by the municipal police council or as  certi-
    38  fied  by the police department of the city of New York upon satisfactory
    39  completion of the basic training program and  graduation  from  the  New
    40  York city police academy.
    41    3. Credit for service as a member or officer of the state police or as
    42  a  paid  firefighter,  police  officer  or officer of any organized fire
    43  department or police force or department of any county,  city,  village,
    44  town,  fire  district  or police district and that credit therefor shall
    45  not exceed five years, shall also be deemed to be creditable service and
    46  shall be included in computing years of  total  service  for  retirement
    47  pursuant  to  this  section,  provided such service was performed by the
    48  member while contributing to  the  retirement  system  pursuant  to  the
    49  provisions of this article or article eight of this chapter.
    50    §  3.  Dutchess county is authorized to file an election in accordance
    51  with subdivision k of section eighty-nine-p of the retirement and social
    52  security law electing to offer the provisions of  section  five  hundred
    53  sixty-one  of  the  retirement  and  social  security law to its members
    54  defined in subdivisions a and d of section eighty-nine-p of the  retire-
    55  ment  and  social  security  law  within  one hundred eighty days of the

     1  effective date of this act.  All  past  service  costs  associated  with
     2  section two of this act shall be borne by Dutchess county.
     3    §  4. Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, none
     4  of the provisions of this act shall  be  subject  to  the  appropriation
     5  requirement of section 25 of the retirement and social security law.
     6    § 5. This act shall take effect immediately; provided however, that if
     7  the  county  of  Dutchess fails to file the election pursuant to section
     8  three of this act with the comptroller within one hundred eighty days of
     9  the effective date of this act, on  the  one  hundred  eighty-first  day
    10  following the effective date of this act, sections two and three of this
    11  act  shall  expire and be deemed repealed; provided that the comptroller
    12  shall notify the legislative bill drafting commission  upon  the  occur-
    13  rence  of  the  filing of such election provided for in section three of
    14  this act, or lack thereof, in order that the commission may maintain  an
    15  accurate and timely effective data base of the official text of the laws
    16  of  the  state of New York in furtherance of effectuating the provisions
    17  of section 44 of the legislative law and  section  70-b  of  the  public
    18  officers law.
