Add Art 19 Title 13 §§19-1301 - 19-1307, En Con L
 
Establishes an indoor air quality inspection and evaluation program which shall: promulgate regulations for inspections, evaluations, acceptable levels of hazardous air pollutants, ventilation standards, notifications and best practices to improve indoor air quality in covered entities; enter into any necessary interagency agreements to coordinate the indoor air quality inspection and evaluation program; inspect a covered entity, upon their own initiative or upon complaint to the department regarding indoor air quality; provide results of an inspection with any appropriate local health authority, and any other person or department the department deems necessary; assist covered entities in developing reasonable plans to improve indoor air quality conditions including but not limited to those found during inspection; and develop and implement community outreach programs to educate the public on indoor air quality and risk reduction.

     3                                  TITLE 13

     5  Section 19-1301. Definitions.
     6          19-1303. Indoor air quality inspection and evaluation program.

     8          19-1307. Rules and regulations.
     9  § 19-1301. Definitions.
    10    For  the  purposes  of  this title, the following terms shall have the
    11  following meanings:
    12    1. "Covered entity" shall include any public building  as  defined  in
    13  subdivision two of this section.
    14    2.  "Public  building"  shall  include  a  factory building, an office
    15  building, a mercantile building, a hotel building, a theatre building, a
    16  warehouse building, an apartment building, a state or  municipal  build-
    17  ing, a school, a college or university building, a building containing a
    18  place of public assembly maintained or leased for pecuniary gain, or any
    19  other  building  more  than  one story high except a dwelling house less
    20  than three stories high or occupied by fewer than three families.
    21    3. "Indoor air quality" means air quality within and around a  covered
    22  entity  which  affects  the  health and comfort of individuals within or
    23  near such building.

     1    4.  "Hazardous  air  pollutants"  means  any  substance  listed  as  a
     2  substance  hazardous  to the public health, safety or the environment in
     3  regulations promulgated pursuant to article thirty-seven of the environ-
     4  mental  conservation  law  which  may  affect  indoor  air  quality  and
     5  includes,  but  is  not  limited to lead, radon, asbestos, formaldehyde,
     6  volatile  organic  compounds,   mold,   excess   moisture,   pesticides,
     7  substances from fuel-burning combustion appliances, tobacco products and
     8  any  substance  known  to cause health effects which exceeds a specified
     9  level as designated by the commissioner.
    10    5. "Health effects" means those that are immediate including, but  not
    11  limited to irritation of the eyes, nose, or throat, headaches, dizziness
    12  and  fatigue  and those that are long-term including, but not limited to
    13  respiratory diseases, heart disease, asthma and lung cancer.
    14  § 19-1303. Indoor air quality inspection and evaluation program.
    15    1. Within one year of the effective date of this title, the department
    16  shall establish an indoor air quality inspection and evaluation program.
    17    2. The department shall, in order  to  effectuate  the  provisions  of
    18  subdivision one of this section:
    19    (a)  promulgate  regulations  for inspections, evaluations, acceptable
    20  levels of hazardous air pollutants, ventilation standards, notifications
    21  and best practices to improve indoor air quality in covered entities;
    22    (b) enter into any necessary interagency agreements to coordinate  the
    23  indoor air quality inspection and evaluation program;
    24    (c)  inspect  a  covered  entity,  upon  their  own initiative or upon
    25  complaint to the department regarding indoor air quality;
    26    (d) provide results of an inspection with any appropriate local health
    27  authority, and any other  person  or  department  the  department  deems
    28  necessary;
    29    (e)  assist covered entities in developing reasonable plans to improve
    30  indoor air quality conditions including but not limited to  those  found
    31  during inspection; and
    32    (f)  develop  and implement community outreach programs to educate the
    33  public on indoor air quality and risk reduction.
    34    3. After each inspection, the department shall prepare a report that:
    35    (a) describes the department's findings;
    36    (b) describes whether any test results exceed  acceptable  levels  for
    37  any  hazardous air pollutants established by the department or the occu-
    38  pational safety and health  administration  guidelines  for  indoor  air
    39  quality;
    40    (c)  identifies any conditions that are contributing or could contrib-
    41  ute to poor indoor air quality, including, but not limited to inadequate
    42  ventilation; and
    43    (d) provides guidance on steps the covered entity may take to  improve
    44  indoor air quality.
    45    4.  A  complaint  regarding the indoor air quality of a covered entity
    46  shall be in writing and sent to the department in a manner to be  deter-
    47  mined  by  the  department.  The  department  shall determine whether an
    48  inspection of such covered entity pursuant to paragraph (c) of  subdivi-
    49  sion  two  of  this  section  is necessary. If necessary, the department
    50  shall conduct such inspection  within  sixty  days  of  receipt  of  the
    51  complaint.
    52  § 19-1305. Guidance; best practices.
    53    The  department,  in consultation with the department of health, shall
    54  distribute a guidance document or documents of best practices for manag-
    55  ing indoor air quality as described in this title,  including,  but  not
    56  limited  to best practices for ventilation. The department may utilize a
