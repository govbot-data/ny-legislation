AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law and the state finance
law, in relation to food service item waste reduction
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Article 27 of the environmental conservation law is amended
2 by adding a new title 34 to read as follows:
3 TITLE 34
4 FOOD SERVICE ITEM WASTE REDUCTION
5 Section 27-3401. Definitions.
6 27-3403. Restriction on providing excess food service items.
7 27-3405. Violations.
8 27-3407. Preemption.
9 § 27-3401. Definitions.
10 As used in this title, the following terms shall have the following
11 meanings:
12 1. "Condiment" means an individual, single-use container, sealed by
13 the manufacturer, containing a sauce or other substance used to enhance
14 the flavor of food, including but not limited to, ketchup, mustard,
15 mayonnaise, hot sauce, salt, or pepper.
16 2. "Customer" means an individual who receives or purchases food or
17 beverages from a public food service establishment on a take-out, drive-
18 thru, or delivery basis.
19 3. "Eating container" means a tool used for holding food or beverages,
20 including but not limited to, a plate, bowl, cup, or lid.
21 4. "Napkin" means a piece of cloth or paper used to wipe a person's
22 hands or face or protect garments while eating.
23 5. "Public food service establishment" means a premises or part of a
24 premises where food is provided directly to a person, whether such food

12 § 27-3403. Restriction on providing excess food service items.
13 1. No public food service establishment operating in the state shall
14 provide utensils, condiments, napkins or extra eating containers to a
15 customer, whether directly to such customer or through a third-party
16 food delivery service, except upon, and in accordance with, the express
17 request of such customer. The provisions of this subdivision shall apply
18 regardless of whether the customer's meal order is placed in person,
19 over the phone, online, or using other available means or methods.
20 2. Any public food service establishment that elects to supply custom-
21 ers with utensils, condiments, napkins or extra eating containers upon
22 request, as authorized by subdivision one of this section, shall provide
23 each customer with only those types and amounts of utensils, condiments,
24 napkins or extra eating containers that have been expressly requested
25 thereby.
26 3. Nothing in this section shall be deemed to require a public food
27 service establishment to provide any utensils, condiments, napkins or
28 extra eating containers to customers, upon their request, as authorized
29 by subdivision one of this section.
30 4. A public food service establishment maintaining a self-service
31 station at which utensils, condiments, napkins or extra eating contain-
32 ers are offered shall not be a violation of subdivision one of this
33 section.
34 § 27-3405. Violations.
35 1. Any public food service establishment that violates any provision
36 of this title shall receive a warning notice for the first such
37 violation and a civil penalty of up to one hundred dollars for any
38 subsequent violation in the same calendar year. For purposes of this
39 section, each commercial transaction shall constitute no more than one
40 violation. A hearing or opportunity to be heard shall be provided prior
41 to the assessment of any civil penalty.
42 2. The department, the department of agriculture and markets, and the
43 attorney general are hereby authorized to enforce the provisions of this
44 title, and all monies collected shall be deposited to the credit of the
45 environmental protection fund established pursuant to section ninety-
46 two-s of the state finance law.
47 § 27-3407. Preemption.
48 This title shall not apply in a city with a population of one million
49 or more that has a local law, ordinance or regulation in place that
50 restricts the provision of utensils, condiments, napkins and extra
51 eating containers by a public food service establishment to a customer
52 and is not in conflict with this title.
