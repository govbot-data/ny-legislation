SAYEGH, RAMOS, DeSTEFANO, COLTON, STECK, SHIMSKY, LEMONDES -- read once and referred to the Committee on Governmental Operations -- recommitted to the Committee on Governmental Operations in accordance with Assembly Rule 3, sec. 2 -- committee discharged, bill amended, ordered reprinted as amended and recommitted to said committee AN ACT to amend the state finance law and the tax law, in relation to establishing the bullet-proof vests for officers fund The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The state finance law is amended by adding a new section 2 99-uu to read as follows: 3 § 99-uu. Bullet-proof vests for officers fund. 1. A special fund to be 4 known as the "bullet-proof vests for officers fund" is hereby estab- 5 lished in the custody of the state comptroller and the commissioner of 6 taxation and finance. 7 2. The fund shall consist of all monies transferred to such fund 8 pursuant to law, all monies required by any provision of law to be paid 9 into or credited to the fund, all monies from gifts pursuant to section 10 six hundred thirty-m of the tax law and any interest earnings which may 11 accrue from the investment of monies in the fund. Nothing contained 12 herein shall prevent the state from receiving grants, gifts or bequests 13 for the purposes of the fund as defined in this section and depositing 14 them in the fund according to law. 15 3. Monies of the fund, when allocated, shall be available to the divi- 16 sion of criminal justice services for the purpose of providing police 17 officers and canines serving in canine units with bullet-proof vests for 18 use in the line of duty. Bullet-proof vests shall be distributed at the 19 discretion of the commissioner of criminal justice services, pursuant to 20 guidelines established by such commissioner for statewide distribution 21 of such bullet-proof vests. The tax law is amended by adding a new section 630-m to read as 5 follows: 6 § 630-m. Gift for the bullet-proof vests for officers fund. Effective 7 for any tax year commencing on or after January first, two thousand 8 twenty-six, an individual in any taxable year may elect to contribute to 9 the bullet-proof vests for officers fund. Such contribution shall be in 10 any whole dollar amount and shall not reduce the amount of state tax 11 owed by such individual. The commissioner shall include space on the 12 personal income tax return to enable a taxpayer to make such contrib- 13 ution. Notwithstanding any other provision of law all revenues collected 14 pursuant to this section shall be credited to the bullet-proof vests for 15 officers fund and used only for those purposes enumerated in section 16 ninety-nine-uu of the state finance law. 17 § 3.
