FAHY -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Education AN ACT to amend the New York state archives partnership trust act, in relation to authorizing the New York state archives partnership trust fund to be used for the benefit of the New York state museum and the New York state library The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 1 of chapter 758 of the laws of 1992, constituting 2 the New York state archives partnership trust act, is amended by adding 3 a new section 9 to read as follows: 4 § 9. 1. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the 5 purposes, powers and authorized expenditures of the trust board 6 prescribed in this act may be exercised for the benefit of the New York 7 state museum and the New York state library, in addition to the New York 8 state archives. 9 2. In addition to the powers of the trust board as prescribed in 10 section five of this act and expenditures authorized by section six of 11 this act, the powers and authorized expenditures of the trust board as 12 they relate to the New York state museum and the New York state library 13 shall include, but not be limited to: 14 a. the acquisition, preservation, conservation, management, and acces- 15 sibility of collections, records, objects, artifacts, specimens and 16 other materials of enduring historical, cultural, educational, scientif- 17 ic or governmental value held by, or intended for, the New York state 18 museum or the New York state library; 19 b. the support of research, exhibitions, interpretive initiatives, 20 educational programming, publications, and public programs related to 21 the collections, holdings, functions, and statutory missions of the New 22 York state museum and the New York state library; and 23 c. the acceptance, administration, investment and expenditure of 24 gifts, grants, donations, bequests and other assistance, including those 25 designated for specific purposes or projects.
