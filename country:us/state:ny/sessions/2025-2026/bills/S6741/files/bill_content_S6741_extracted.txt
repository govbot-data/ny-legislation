SANDERS -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Corporations, Authorities and Commissions AN ACT to amend the New York state urban development corporation act, in relation to creating the "NYS entrepreneurial training act" The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as the "NYS entre- 2 preneurial training act". 3 § 2. Section 1 of chapter 174 of the laws of 1968, constituting the 4 New York state urban development corporation act, is amended by adding 5 two new sections 16-jj and 16-kk to read as follows: 6 § 16-jj. Entrepreneurial training grant program. 1. There is hereby 7 established within the empire state development corporation an entrepre- 8 neurial training grant program. The program shall be formulated by such 9 corporation and administered by individual participating cities and 10 towns. The purpose of the program shall be to provide grants to eligi- 11 ble applicants to support and train entrepreneurial candidates. 12 2. In order for an eligible applicant to receive an award of an annual 13 grant an applicant shall submit with its application a gradable business 14 plan for the use of the grant money. 15 3. A minimum of twenty percent of the participants of the program 16 shall be New York state certified minority and women's business enter- 17 prises and a minimum of ten percent shall be veterans of the United 18 States military. 19 § 16-kk. Entrepreneurial training grant program awards. 1. Within 20 amounts appropriated therefor, the empire state development corporation 21 shall be authorized to grant awards for the support of approved entre- 22 preneurial training grant programs. The empire state development corporation shall, from within amounts 5 appropriated to such corporation, undertake all activities necessary to 6 plan for and preliminarily provide for the timely implementation of the 7 entrepreneurial training grant program authorized by section sixteen-jj 8 of this act for the state fiscal year commencing one year following the 9 effective date of this section. 10 4. Participating cities having a population of one million or more 11 shall be allocated five million dollars. One million dollars shall be 12 used for the operation of the program. Four million dollars shall be 13 used to grant four hundred ten thousand dollar grants to successful 14 graduates of the program. 15 5. One hundred thousand dollars shall be used for the 18 operation of the program. Four hundred thousand dollars shall be used to 19 grant forty ten thousand dollar grants to successful graduates of the 20 program. 21 § 3. This act shall take effect immediately.
