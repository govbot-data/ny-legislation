Senate Resolution No. 1447
 
BY: Senator MARTINS
 
        COMMENDING  HEARTS PW upon the occasion of hosting
        its Red Party  on  February  27,  2026,  and  paying
        tribute to its distinguished honorees
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is the sense of this Legislative Body to recognize and
commend  those  organizations  and  individuals  whose   commitment   to
community enrichment, education, and the arts significantly enhances the
quality of life for the residents of the State of New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  HEARTS PW (Helping Enrich the Arts of Port Washington) has
long been dedicated to supporting, promoting, and  sustaining  arts  and
cultural  programming  throughout the Port Washington community, thereby
fostering creativity, access, and engagement for residents of all  ages;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  HEARTS  PW  will  host  its  annual  Red  Party on Friday,
February 27, 2026, a signature fundraising and  celebratory  event  that
highlights  the  organization's  mission  and  honors  individuals whose
service and leadership have had a profound impact on the  arts  in  Port
Washington; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  year's Red Party proudly honors Eric Gewirtz, Lauren
Foster-Holzer, and Christine Nelson, three exceptional individuals whose
dedication to arts leadership  and  education  exemplifies  the  highest
ideals of public service and community commitment; and
 
  WHEREAS,   Eric  Gewirtz  has  demonstrated  exceptional  leadership
through his service on the HEARTS PW Board  since  2017,  including  his
tenure  as  Board President from 2020 through 2025, during which time he
guided the organization through the challenges of the Covid-19  pandemic
while strengthening its governance and expanding its impact; and
 
  WHEREAS, During his presidency, Eric Gewirtz worked closely with the
Board  to recruit new members, significantly increase total grant awards
year over  year,  and  elevate  the  organization's  visibility  through
strategic   and   engaging   storytelling  within  the  Port  Washington
community; and
 
  WHEREAS, A lifelong advocate for the arts, Eric Gewirtz has built  a
distinguished  career  in arts communications, supporting major cultural
institutions including the New York Philharmonic and Lincoln Center, and
now serves as Senior Vice President at Rubenstein Communications,  while
continuing  to  serve  HEARTS  PW  through  leadership  of  PortFest and
oversight of communications; and
 
  WHEREAS, Lauren Foster-Holzer  is  a  dedicated  educator  and  arts
leader   who   has  directed  musical  productions  and  produced  drama
productions for the Schreiber Theatre Company for  the  past  17  years,
creating  inclusive  and  inspiring  theatrical  experiences that foster
artistic growth and personal development among students; and
 
  WHEREAS, A proud alumna and  resident  of  Port  Washington,  Lauren
Foster-Holzer  has  deep  roots  in  the  community  and serves the Port
 
Washington School District as a Special Education  teacher  at  Paul  D.
Schreiber  High School, as well as an advisor to the Student Council and
a coach of the Portettes; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Through  her  commitment to collaboration, creativity, and
student-centered learning, Lauren Foster-Holzer  has  shaped  meaningful
educational  and artistic experiences that resonate long after the final
curtain call, and she remains grateful to be honored  by  HEARTS  PW,  a
steadfast supporter of the Schreiber Theatre Company; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Christine  Nelson  is  a dedicated educator, director, and
theater professional who has served the Paul D.  Schreiber  High  School
community  for  many  years  as  both  a  Science Teacher and a creative
co-leader of the Schreiber Theatre Company; and
 
  WHEREAS, Christine Nelson brings extensive  professional  experience
to  her  work,  including  prior  service  as  Lead  Teacher for Arts in
Education for Nassau County BOCES, performance and directing experience,
and advanced training in educational theater, acting, and directing from
respected institutions; and
 
  WHEREAS, Christine  Nelson  is  being  recognized  alongside  Lauren
Foster-Holzer   for  their  nearly  two-decade  partnership  defined  by
collaboration and artistic excellence, through which they have  directed
and  produced  an  extensive repertoire of stage plays and musicals that
have enriched the cultural life of Port  Washington  and  supported  the
creative journeys of countless students; and
 
  WHEREAS,  The collective dedication and achievements of the honorees
exemplify the mission of HEARTS PW and  underscore  the  essential  role
that  arts  organizations  and  arts  educators  play  in  strengthening
communities and enriching public life; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its  deliberations  to
commend HEARTS PW upon the occasion of hosting its Red Party on February
27, 2026, and to honor Eric Gewirtz, Lauren Foster-Holzer, and Christine
Nelson   for   their   outstanding  service,  leadership,  and  enduring
contributions to the arts and education in Port Washington;  and  be  it
further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  copies  of  this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to HEARTS PW and this year's aforementioned honorees.
