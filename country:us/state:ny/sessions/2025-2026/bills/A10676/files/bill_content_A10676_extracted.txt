Section 353 of the agriculture and markets law, as amended 2 by chapter 458 of the laws of 1985, the opening paragraph as amended by 3 chapter 523 of the laws of 2005, is amended to read as follows: 4 § 353. Overdriving, torturing and injuring animals; failure to provide 5 proper sustenance or shelter. A person who overdrives, overloads, 6 tortures or cruelly beats or unjustifiably injures, maims, mutilates or 7 kills any animal, whether wild or tame, and whether belonging to 8 [himself] such person or to another, or deprives any animal of necessary 9 sustenance, food or drink, or shelter, or neglects or refuses to furnish 10 it such sustenance or drink, or shelter or causes, procures or permits 11 any animal to be overdriven, overloaded, tortured, cruelly beaten, or 12 unjustifiably injured, maimed, mutilated or killed, or to be deprived of 13 necessary food or drink, or shelter, or who wilfully sets on foot, 14 instigates, engages in, or in any way furthers any act of cruelty to any 15 animal, or any act tending to produce such cruelty, is guilty of a class 16 A misdemeanor and for purposes of paragraph (b) of subdivision one of 17 section 160.10 of the criminal procedure law, shall be treated as a 18 misdemeanor defined in the penal law. 19 Nothing herein contained shall be construed to prohibit or interfere 20 with any properly conducted scientific tests, experiments or investi- 21 gations, involving the use of living animals, performed or conducted in 22 laboratories or institutions, which are approved for these purposes by 23 the state commissioner of health. The state commissioner of health shall 24 prescribe the rules under which such approvals shall be granted, includ- 25 ing therein standards regarding the care and treatment of any such 26 animals. Such rules shall be published and copies thereof conspicuously 27 posted in each such laboratory or institution.
