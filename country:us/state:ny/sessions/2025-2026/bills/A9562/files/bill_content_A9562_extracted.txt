Statement of legislative findings and declaration. It is hereby 15 found and declared that New York state needs, as a matter of public 16 policy, to create competitive financial incentives to attract sports and 17 entertainment events that can be located in any state. The empire state 18 sports and entertainment fund is created to help New York compete with 19 other states for the siting of these unique opportunities. 20 This article creates a fund that will make New York venues more 21 competitive when trying to secure these types of events. The sports and 22 entertainment opportunities targeted by this fund will be events that 23 are not currently designated for New York and that are not part of a 24 regular schedule in New York and could reasonably be located in other 25 markets. 26 § 522. Definitions. For the purposes of this article: 27 1. "Qualifying sports or entertainment event" means a sports or enter- 28 tainment event that is not part of a fixed, recurring residency or EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. "Participant" means a business entity that: 19 (a) has completed an application prescribed by the department to be 20 admitted into the program; 21 (b) has been issued a certificate of eligibility by the department; 22 (c) has demonstrated that it meets the eligibility criteria in section 23 five hundred twenty-three and subdivision two of section five hundred 24 twenty-four of this article; and 25 (d) has been certified as a participant by the commissioner. 26 § 523. Eligibility criteria. 1. To be a participant in the empire 27 state sports and entertainment fund, a business entity shall host a 28 qualifying sports or entertainment event in New York state. 29 2. A business entity must be in compliance with all worker protection 30 laws and regulations. In addition, a business entity may not owe past 31 due state taxes. 32 § 524. Application and approval process. 1. A business entity must 33 submit a completed application as prescribed by the commissioner. Such 34 completed application must be submitted to the commissioner at least 35 ninety days prior to the proposed date of such qualifying entertainment 36 or sports event. 37 2. As part of such application, each business entity must: 38 (a) demonstrate that the qualifying sports or entertainment event 39 meets the criteria of such definition. 40 (b) demonstrate the expected economic benefit to the host location. 41 (c) certify, under penalty of perjury, that it is in substantial 42 compliance with all, worker protection, and local, state, and federal 43 tax laws. 44 3. After reviewing a business enterprise's completed application and 45 determining that the business entity will meet the conditions set forth 46 in section five hundred twenty-three of this article, the department may 47 admit the applicant into the fund and provide the applicant with a 48 certificate of eligibility. 49 4. In order to become a participant in the fund, an applicant must 50 submit evidence that it satisfies the eligibility criteria specified in 51 section five hundred twenty-three of this article and subdivision two of 52 this section in such form as the commissioner may prescribe. After 53 reviewing such evidence and finding it sufficient, the department shall 54 certify the applicant as a participant and issue to that participant a 55 certificate of funding for one taxable year.
