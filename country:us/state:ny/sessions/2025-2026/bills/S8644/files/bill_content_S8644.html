    10    § 8. The election law is amended by adding a new section 7-132 to read
    11  as follows:
    12    §  7-132.  Ballots;  form  for  voters  by mail. 1. The state board of
    13  elections shall prescribe a single, uniform ballot form to  be  used  by
    14  early mail, absentee, military and special voters who vote by mail. Such
    15  ballot  shall  satisfy  all  requirements and standards set forth by the
    16  Uniformed and Overseas Citizens Absentee  Voting  Act  (52  U.S.C.  Sec.
    17  20302 et seq.).
    18    2.  The state board of elections shall create a uniform inner affirma-
    19  tion envelope, containing a uniform oath, to be used by county boards of
    20  election into which early mail, absentee, military  and  special  voters
    21  place their voted ballot. In the event that such envelope conflicts with
    22  federal  law,  the state board of elections shall create alternate inner
    23  affirmation envelopes in a manner consistent with federal law.
    24    3. The state board of elections shall promulgate rules and regulations
    25  necessary for the implementation of this section. Such rules  and  regu-
    26  lations  shall include, but not be limited to, provisions that authorize
    27  the state board of elections to:  standardize  and  format  the  single,
    28  uniform  ballot;  provide  such  ballot  in any language required by the
    29  federal Voting Rights Act of 1965 (52 U.S.C. Sec.  10503);  and  consult
    30  with vendors to ensure ballot uniformity.
    31    4. The state board of elections shall promulgate rules and regulations
    32  for  the creation and administration of a statewide online ballot appli-
    33  cation system for early mail, absentee, military and special voters  who
    34  vote by mail pursuant to the provisions of this section.
    35    5.  Nothing  in  this  section shall be construed to alter eligibility
    36  requirements for persons who vote by mail.
    37    6. Nothing in this section shall be  deemed  to  authorize  the  state
    38  board  of  elections  to  contravene the Uniformed and Overseas Citizens
    39  Absentee Voting Act (52 U.S.C. Sec. 20302 et seq.).
