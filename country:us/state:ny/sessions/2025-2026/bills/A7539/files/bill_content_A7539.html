Add §75-0104, amd §§75-0101, 75-0109 & 75-0103, En Con L; amd §51, Pub Serv L; amd §1855, Pub Auth L
 
Establishes a climate action cost council; limits the number of rules and regulations that may be promulgated annually to meet CLCPA goals.

        Introduced  by  M.  of  A.  PALMESANO  --  read once and referred to the
          Committee on Environmental Conservation
 
        AN ACT to amend the  environmental  conservation  law,  in  relation  to
          establishing  a climate action cost council; and to amend the environ-
          mental conservation law, the public service law and the public author-
          ities law, in relation to limiting the number of rules and regulations
          that may be promulgated annually regarding certain climate goals
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The environmental conservation law is amended by adding a
     2  new section 75-0104 to read as follows:
     3  § 75-0104. Climate action cost council.
     4    1. There is hereby established a climate action  cost  council,  which
     5  shall consist of nine members to be appointed as follows:
     6    a. one member appointed by the governor;
     7    b. two members appointed by the temporary president of the senate;
     8    c. two members appointed by the speaker of the assembly;
     9    d. two members appointed by the minority leader of the senate; and
    10    e. two members appointed by the minority leader of the assembly.
    11    2.  The  members  shall  always  include individuals with expertise in
    12  issues relating to energy,  environmental  conservation,  labor,  public
    13  health  and regulated industries, including, but not limited to housing,
    14  agriculture, construction, transportation and manufacturing.
    15    3. Council members shall receive no compensation  for  their  services
    16  but  shall  be  reimbursed for actual and necessary expenses incurred in
    17  the performance of their duties.
    18    4. Each member of the council shall be entitled to one vote. The coun-
    19  cil's approval of regulatory  action  pursuant  to  this  article  shall
    20  require a two-third vote of the council. No action shall be taken by the
    21  council unless there is a quorum, which shall at all times be a majority
    22  of the members of the council.

     1    5. Any vacancies on the council shall be filled in the manner provided
     2  for in the initial appointment.
     3    6. Prior to promulgation of any regulatory measure taken to ensure the
     4  attainment  of the statewide greenhouse gas emissions limits established
     5  pursuant to section 75-0107 of this article taking effect, such  measure
     6  shall be approved by a two-thirds vote of the council.
     7    7.  Each state agency shall notify the council of any regulatory meas-
     8  ure taken to ensure the attainment of the statewide greenhouse gas emis-
     9  sions limits established pursuant to section 75-0107 of this article  no
    10  less than sixty days before the final adoption of a rule, regulation, or
    11  code. The council shall vote on such regulatory measure as soon as prac-
    12  ticable  and  no later than its next quarterly meeting after the council
    13  has been notified. No regulatory measures pursuant to this article shall
    14  take effect unless approved by the council.
    15    8. When determining whether to vote to approve a  regulatory  measure,
    16  council members shall consider:
    17    a. the cost, including, but not limited to, the cost to ratepayers and
    18  businesses across the state;
    19    b.  the  impact to the electrical grid, including, but not limited to,
    20  reliability and safety; and
    21    c. the reduction in greenhouse gas emissions associated with the regu-
    22  latory measure.
    23    9. The council shall meet quarterly. Such meetings shall  be  open  to
    24  the  public  and subject to article seven of the public officers law. No
    25  less than sixty days prior to a meeting  of  the  council,  the  council
    26  shall publish a list of draft regulations, rules, or codes to be consid-
    27  ered  by  the  council,  and  the  council  shall make meaningful public
    28  comment available prior to the meeting.
    29    10. The council shall submit an annual report to the governor and  the
    30  legislature  beginning  on  January  first, two thousand twenty-six, and
    31  annually thereafter. The report shall include a summary of actions taken
    32  by the council, the total costs imposed on  ratepayers  and  businesses,
    33  and  the  amount  of  reduction  in greenhouse gas emissions. The report
    34  shall be made publicly available on the department's, the New York state
    35  energy research and  development  authority's,  and  the  department  of
    36  public service's websites.
    37    11.  The council may request information related to attaining the cost
    38  of regulatory measures on consumers and businesses  from  third  parties
    39  and state agencies.
    40    12.  A  vote  of  the council may be overridden by a roll call vote of
    41  two-thirds of each chamber of the legislature.
    42    § 2. Subdivision 4 of section 75-0101 of the  environmental  conserva-
    43  tion  law,  as  added  by chapter 106 of the laws of 2019, is amended to
    44  read as follows:
    45    4. "Council", except as used in section 75-0104 of this article or  in
    46  reference  to  the  climate  action cost council established thereunder,
    47  means the New York state climate action council established pursuant  to
    48  section 75-0103 of this article.
    49    §  3.  Subdivision 1 of section 75-0109 of the environmental conserva-
    50  tion law, as added by chapter 106 of the laws of  2019,  is  amended  to
    51  read as follows:
    52    1.  No later than four years after the effective date of this article,
    53  the department, after public workshops and consultation with  the  coun-
    54  cil,  the  environmental justice advisory group, and the climate justice
    55  working group established pursuant to section 75-0111 of  this  article,
    56  representatives of regulated entities, community organizations, environ-

    12    § 51. Rules  and  regulations.  The  commission shall adopt such addi-

    23  amended  by  chapter  487  of  the  laws  of 2009, is amended to read as
    24  follows:
    25    4. To make rules and regulations governing the exercise of its  corpo-
    26  rate  powers  and  the  fulfillment of its corporate purposes under this
    27  title and title nine-A of this article, which shall be  filed  with  the
    28  department of state in the manner provided by section one hundred two of
    29  the  executive  law;  provided  however  that, notwithstanding any rule,
    30  regulation, or law to the contrary, the authority shall not adopt  rules
    31  and  regulations  related to compliance with article seventy-five of the
    32  environmental conservation law without prior  approval  of  the  climate
    33  action  cost  council  as established in section 75-0104 of the environ-
    34  mental conservation law except in times of exigent  circumstances  where
    35  there  is an immediate threat to public safety or the reliability of the
    36  grid.
    37    § 6. Subdivision 15 of section 75-0103 of the environmental  conserva-
    38  tion  law,  as  added  by chapter 106 of the laws of 2019, is amended to
    39  read as follows:
    40    15. The council shall update its  plan  for  achieving  the  statewide
    41  greenhouse  gas  emissions  limits at least once every [five years] year
    42  and shall make such updates available to the governor,  the  speaker  of
    43  the  assembly  and  the  temporary president of the senate and post such
    44  updates on its website. The council and the climate action cost  council
    45  as  established  under  section  75-0104  of this article shall hold one
    46  public meeting annually in a city of more than one million  people,  one
    47  public  meeting  annually in Nassau county or Suffolk county, one public
    48  meeting annually in Albany county, and one public  meeting  annually  in
    49  Erie  county.  Such  meetings  shall be for the purpose of informing the
    50  public of the progress of achieving the statewide greenhouse  gas  emis-
    51  sion  limits  as  set  forth  under  this article, and for informing the
    52  public of the costs of rules and regulatory  measures  approved  by  the
    53  climate  action  cost  council established under section 75-0104 of this
    54  article. Such meetings shall be subject to article seven of  the  public
    55  officers law.
    56    § 7. This act shall take effect immediately.
