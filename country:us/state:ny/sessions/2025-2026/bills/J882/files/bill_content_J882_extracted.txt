their past contributions and future participation are a vital part of, and valuable asset to, the fabric of community life and activity; and WHEREAS, This Legislative Body is justly proud to honor Eric Spinner upon the occasion of his designation for special recognition by the New York Office for the Aging on Tuesday, May 27, 2025, at the Plaza-Desmond Hotel in Albany, New York; and WHEREAS, Each May, the New York State Office for the Aging pauses to acknowledge the outstanding contributions made by older citizens during National Older Americans Month; and WHEREAS, Deserving of great praise for his lifework, Eric Spinner is also being honored by the Nassau County Office for the Aging at its Older American's Conference and luncheon on Friday, May 16, 2025, at the Long Island Marriott in Uniondale, New York; and WHEREAS, Members of older generations have helped build and shape our Nation to be the greatest in the world; they have defended our freedoms and values to ensure that those who come after them may have better lives; nowhere in the Nation is the legacy of their efforts more visible and more a part of the lives of all citizens than here in the State of New York; and WHEREAS, Born in Brooklyn in 1943, Eric Spinner moved to Nassau County at age seven; he attended Valley Stream South Jr. and Sr. High Schools; and WHEREAS, Eric Spinner attended NYC Community College where he studied Graphic Arts and Advertising Technology, and later received his AAS in Electrical Technology from New York Institute of Technology; and WHEREAS, In 1965, Eric Spinner joined the New York Army Reserve National Guard and attended Basic Training at Fort Gordon, Georgia and Advanced Training as a Counter-Artillery Radar Operator at Fort Sill, Oklahoma where he attained the rank of Sp-5 and was honorably discharged in July 1971; and WHEREAS, Eric Spinner became a shop teacher in NYC high schools for more than 37 years, the last 10 years of which he was Assistant Director for Virtual Enterprises International, an entrepreneurship training program; and WHEREAS, During his years as an educator, Eric Spinner owned and operated a pest control company in Nassau County for 28 years; and WHEREAS, In July of 1994, Eric Spinner met the lady of his dreams, and they married on December 7, 1997; they remain happily married today and share many common interests; and WHEREAS, Rare indeed is the impressive dedication shown by an individual for the benefit of others which Eric Spinner has displayed throughout his life; and WHEREAS, Eric Spinner has served as a volunteer for the North Hempstead AARP group, New Hyde Park Chamber of Commerce, and Nassau County Veterans Service Agency; and WHEREAS, Eric Spinner's endeavors to further help his community, include taking positions on the Town of North Hempstead Veterans Committee and Nassau County Police Commissioner's Community Council; and WHEREAS, A co-host and co-producer of UVO Veterans News as well as a podcast host for the Holocaust Memorial and Tolerance Center in Glen Cove, New York, Eric Spinner lends his voice and wisdom to the veteran community; and WHEREAS, Eric Spinner, a very active and industrious man, has inspired and enriched the lives of those he serves through his volunteerism, generously sharing the wisdom gained over years of experiencing life to its fullest; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to honor Eric Spinner upon the occasion of his designation for special recognition by the New York State Office for the Aging on May 27, 2025; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Eric Spinner.
