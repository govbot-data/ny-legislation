Directs the gaming commission to implement rules and regulations pertaining to fair play in sports wagering for professional sports where a player is injured early and does not return for the season.

        Introduced  by  COMMITTEE ON RULES -- (at request of M. of A. Wright) --
          read once and referred to the Committee on Racing and Wagering
 
        AN ACT to amend the racing, pari-mutuel wagering and  breeding  law,  in
          relation to the "fair play policy" for sports wagering
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section 1. Subdivision 4 of section 1367 of  the  racing,  pari-mutuel
     2  wagering  and breeding law, as amended by section 3 of part Y of chapter
     3  59 of the laws of 2021, is amended to read as follows:
     4    4. Except as otherwise provided by this article, the commission  shall
     5  have  the  authority  to regulate sports pools and the conduct of sports
     6  wagering under this article to the same extent that the commission regu-
     7  lates other gaming. No casino or mobile sports wagering  licensee  shall
     8  be  authorized  to operate a sports pool unless it has produced informa-
     9  tion, documentation, and assurances concerning its financial  background
    10  and  resources,  including  cash reserves, that are sufficient to demon-
    11  strate that it has the financial stability, integrity, and  responsibil-
    12  ity to operate a sports pool. In developing rules and regulations appli-
    13  cable  to  sports wagering, the commission shall examine the regulations
    14  implemented in other states  where  sports  wagering  is  conducted  and
    15  shall,  as far as practicable, adopt a similar regulatory framework. The
    16  commission shall promulgate  regulations  necessary  to  carry  out  the
    17  provisions  of  this section, including, but not limited to, regulations
    18  governing the:
    19    (a) amount of cash reserves to be maintained  by  casinos  and  mobile
    20  sports wagering licensees to cover winning wagers;
    21    (b) acceptance of wagers on a series of sports events;
    22    (c)  maximum wagers which may be accepted by a casino or mobile sports
    23  wagering licensee from any one patron on any one sports event;
    24    (d) type of wagering tickets which may be used;
    25    (e) method of issuing tickets;
    26    (f) method of accounting to be  used  by  casinos  and  mobile  sports
    27  wagering licensees;
    28    (g) types of records which shall be kept;

     1    (h) use of credit and checks by authorized sports bettors;
     2    (i) the process by which a casino may place a layoff bet;
     3    (j) the use of global risk management;
     4    (k) type of system for wagering; [and]
     5    (l) protections for a person placing a wager; and
     6    (m) fair play policies pursuant to subdivision five of this section.
     7    § 2. Subdivision 5 of section 1367 of the racing, pari-mutuel wagering
     8  and breeding law, as amended by section 3 of part Y of chapter 59 of the
     9  laws of 2021, is amended to read as follows:
    10    5.  (a)  Each  casino  and mobile sports wagering licensee shall adopt
    11  comprehensive house rules and implement a  fair  play  policy  governing
    12  sports  wagering  transactions  with  its authorized sports bettors. The
    13  rules shall specify the amounts to be paid on  winning  wagers  and  the
    14  effect of schedule changes. The house rules, together with the fair play
    15  policy and any other information the commission deems appropriate, shall
    16  be conspicuously displayed in the sports wagering lounge and included in
    17  the  terms  and  conditions  of  the account wagering system, and copies
    18  shall be made readily available to  authorized  sports  bettors.  Mobile
    19  sports  wagering  operators shall develop and prominently display proce-
    20  dures on the main page of such mobile sports wagering  operator's  plat-
    21  form  for  the filing of complaints by authorized sports bettors against
    22  such mobile sports wagering operator. An initial response shall be given
    23  by such mobile sports  wagering  operator  to  such  bettor  filing  the
    24  complaint  within  forty-eight hours. A complete response shall be given
    25  by such mobile sports  wagering  operator  to  such  bettor  filing  the
    26  complaint within ten business days. An authorized sports bettor may file
    27  a  complaint alleging a violation of the provisions of this article with
    28  the commission.
    29    (b) A fair play policy shall establish  categories  of  sports  wagers
    30  eligible  for  adjustment, refund or voiding based on objective criteria
    31  when circumstances beyond the control of  an  authorized  sports  bettor
    32  compromise the validity or settlement of a sports wager.
    33    (i)  Such categories shall include but not be limited to the following
    34  circumstances:
    35    (A) a player or athlete does not participate in or  withdraws  from  a
    36  sports event;
    37    (B) an event is cancelled or suspended;
    38    (C) a statistical error or scoring correction occurs; or
    39    (D) other extraordinary circumstances as determined by the commission.
    40    (ii)  Such  fair  play  policy shall: (A) be applied without requiring
    41  action by an authorized sports bettor where reasonably practicable;  (B)
    42  be  applied  consistently and in a nondiscriminatory manner to similarly
    43  situated authorized sports bettors;  (C)  require  that  the  terms  and
    44  conditions  of  such  policy  are clearly disclosed to authorized sports
    45  bettors prior to sports wager placement; and (D) be  readily  accessible
    46  on a mobile sports wagering licensee's website and platform.
    47    § 3. This act shall take effect on the one hundred eightieth day after
    48  it shall have become a law.
