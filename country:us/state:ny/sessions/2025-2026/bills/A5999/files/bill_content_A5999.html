Amd §6808, Ed L; add §280-d, Pub Health L
 
Directs the commissioner of health, in consultation with the commissioner of education, the commissioner of mental health, the state board of pharmacy and the commissioner of corrections and community supervision, to promulgate and implement rules and regulations authorizing the use of automated patient dispensing and storage systems for distribution of prescription and non-prescription drugs and medical devices.

        AN ACT to amend the education law and the public health law, in relation
          to directing the commissioner of  health,  in  consultation  with  the
          commissioner  of  education,  the  commissioner  of mental health, the
          state board of pharmacy and the commissioner of corrections and commu-
          nity supervision, to promulgate and implement  rules  and  regulations
          authorizing  the  use  of  automated  patient  dispensing  and storage
          systems for distribution of prescription  and  non-prescription  drugs
          and medical devices
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1.  Subdivision 1 of section 6808 of  the  education  law,  as
     2  amended  by  section  2  of part D of chapter 60 of the laws of 2014, is
     3  amended to read as follows:
     4    1. No person, firm, corporation or association  shall  possess  drugs,
     5  prescriptions  or  poisons  for  the purpose of compounding, dispensing,
     6  retailing,  wholesaling,  or  manufacturing,  or  shall   offer   drugs,
     7  prescriptions  or  poisons for sale at retail or wholesale unless regis-
     8  tered by the department  as  a  pharmacy,  wholesaler,  manufacturer  or
     9  outsourcing  facility;  provided,  however,  that the provisions of this
    10  section  shall  not  prohibit   the   dispensing   and/or   storage   of
    11  prescriptions,  drugs or medical devices through the use of an automated
    12  patient dispensing and storage system as provided in section two hundred
    13  eighty-d of the public health law and rules and regulations  promulgated
    14  pursuant to such section.
    15    § 2. The public health law is amended by adding a new section 280-d to
    16  read as follows:
    17    §  280-d.  Automated  patient  dispensing  and storage systems. 1. The
    18  commissioner, in consultation with the commissioner  of  education,  the
    19  commissioner  of  mental  health,  the  state  board of pharmacy and the

     4  shall  include, but not be limited to, requiring that the operator of an

     8    2. Definitions. As used in this section:
     9    (a) "Automated drug delivery system"  or  "ADDS"  means  a  mechanical
    10  system that performs operations or activities, other than compounding or
    11  administration,  relative to the storage, dispensing, or distribution of
    12  drugs. An ADDS shall collect,  control,  and  maintain  all  transaction
    13  information  to  accurately  track the movement of drugs into and out of
    14  the system for security, accuracy, and accountability.
    15    (b) "Automated patient dispensing and storage system" or "APDSS" means
    16  an ADDS for storage and dispensing of drugs directly to patients  or  to
    17  medical  personnel  for  prompt  administration  to patients pursuant to
    18  prior authorization by a pharmacist.
    19    (c) "Covered entity" means:
    20    (i) a "covered entity" as defined in Section 256b of Title 42  of  the
    21  United States Code; and
    22    (ii)  a  correctional  facility  as  defined  in  section  two  of the
    23  correction law.
    24    (d) "Drug" means a controlled substance as defined in section  thirty-
    25  three  hundred six of this chapter, a non-prescription drug or over-the-
    26  counter medication or supply, and/or a medical device.
    27    (e) "Patient"  means  an  individual  for  whom  medication  has  been
    28  prescribed  by any person authorized to prescribe medication pursuant to
    29  article one hundred thirty-one, one hundred thirty-one-b or one  hundred
    30  forty-one of the education law.
    31    3. Pursuant to the rules and regulations authorized by subdivision one
    32  of  this section, if all of the following conditions are met, a pharmacy
    33  located in the state may provide pharmacy services through the use of an
    34  automated patient dispensing system to:  (i) the patients of  a  covered
    35  entity through an APDSS located on the premises of the covered entity or
    36  on  the  premises  of  medical  professional practices under contract to
    37  provide medical services to covered entity patients, which need  not  be
    38  the  same  location  as  the  pharmacy;  and (ii) individual patients as
    39  described in subdivision five of this section:
    40    (a) The pharmacy obtains a license from the state board of pharmacy to
    41  operate the APDSS at the covered entity or an affiliated site, or at  or
    42  adjacent to the pharmacy, as applicable. As part of the application, the
    43  pharmacy  shall  provide  the address at which the APDSS shall be placed
    44  and, if the APDSS is to be installed at a covered entity,  identify  the
    45  covered  entity.  A separate license shall be required for each location
    46  and shall be renewed annually concurrent with the pharmacy license.  The
    47  application  and  renewal  fee shall be three hundred dollars and may be
    48  increased to five hundred dollars.   The  state  board  of  pharmacy  is
    49  authorized to lower the renewal fee to not less than two hundred dollars
    50  if  a  lower  fee level will provide sufficient resources to support the
    51  regulatory activities.
    52    (b) The pharmacy providing the pharmacy services to the patients of  a
    53  covered entity, including, unless otherwise prohibited by any other law,
    54  patients  enrolled in the Medicare and Medicaid programs, shall be under
    55  contract with that covered entity to  provide  those  pharmacy  services
    56  through the use of the APDSS.

     1    (c)  Drugs  stored  in  an APDSS shall be part of the inventory of the
     2  pharmacy providing pharmacy services to the patients and drugs dispensed
     3  from the APDSS shall be considered to have been dispensed by that  phar-
     4  macy.
     5    (d)  The pharmacy shall maintain records of the acquisition and dispo-
     6  sition of drugs  stored  in  the  APDSS  separate  from  other  pharmacy
     7  records.
     8    (e)  The pharmacy shall be solely responsible for the security, opera-
     9  tion, and maintenance of the APDSS; provided,  however,  that  an  APDSS
    10  installed  at  a  correctional  facility shall be deemed to be under the
    11  dual security and control of the pharmacy and the correctional facility.
    12    (f) The pharmacy shall provide training regarding  the  operation  and
    13  use of the APDSS to both pharmacy and covered entity personnel using the
    14  system.
    15    (g)  The  operation  of  the APDSS shall be under the supervision of a
    16  pharmacist licensed pursuant to article one hundred thirty-seven of  the
    17  education law acting on behalf of the pharmacy providing services to the
    18  patients  of  the  covered entity. The pharmacist need not be physically
    19  present at the site of the APDSS and may supervise the system  electron-
    20  ically.
    21    (h)  The state board of pharmacy may issue a license for the operation
    22  of an APDSS at an address for which it has issued another site  license,
    23  including  but  not  limited  to  drive-through pharmacy areas and other
    24  areas adjacent to the physical location occupied by a pharmacy.
    25    (i) The state board of pharmacy, within thirty days after  receipt  of
    26  an  application  for  an  APDSS  license,  shall conduct a pre-licensure
    27  inspection at the proposed location of  the  APDSS.  Relocation  of  the
    28  APDSS  shall require a new application for licensure.  Replacement of an
    29  APDSS shall require notice to such board within thirty days.
    30    (j) The APDSS license shall be canceled by operation  of  law  if  the
    31  underlying  pharmacy  license  is  not  current, valid, and active. Upon
    32  reissuance or reinstatement of the underlying pharmacy  license,  a  new
    33  application  for  an APDSS license shall be submitted to the state board
    34  of pharmacy, if so required by such board.
    35    (k) A pharmacy that holds an APDSS  license  shall  advise  the  state
    36  board  of  pharmacy in writing within thirty days if use of the APDSS is
    37  discontinued.
    38    4. (a) An  automated  patient  dispensing  and  storage  system  shall
    39  collect, control, and maintain all transaction information to accurately
    40  track  the  movement  of  drugs into and out of the system for security,
    41  accuracy, and accountability.
    42    (b) Transaction information shall be made readily available in a down-
    43  loadable format for review and inspection by individuals  authorized  by
    44  law to review such information. These records shall be maintained by the
    45  pharmacy for a minimum of three years.
    46    5.  Drugs  from an automated patient dispensing and storage system may
    47  be dispensed directly to a patient from a drive-through  pharmacy  aisle
    48  or  other  location  adjacent  to  the physical location occupied by the
    49  pharmacy, if all of the following requirements are met:
    50    (a) The pharmacy develops, implements, and  annually  reviews  written
    51  policies and procedures with respect to all of the following:
    52    (i)  the  pharmacy  maintains  the security of the APDSS and the drugs
    53  within that APDSS;
    54    (ii) the pharmacy determines and applies inclusion criteria  regarding
    55  which  drugs  are  appropriate  for placement in the APDSS and for which
    56  patients;

     1    (iii) the pharmacy ensures that patients are aware  that  consultation
     2  with  a pharmacist is available for any prescription medication, includ-
     3  ing those delivered via the APDSS;
     4    (iv) the pharmacy describes the assignment of responsibilities to, and
     5  training  of, pharmacy personnel, and other personnel using the APDSS at
     6  the location where the APDSS is placed, regarding maintenance and  fill-
     7  ing procedures for the APDSS;
     8    (v)  the  pharmacy  orients  participating  patients on the use of the
     9  APDSS, notifying patients when expected prescription medications are not
    10  available in the APDSS, and ensuring that patient use of the APDSS  does
    11  not interfere with delivery of drugs; and
    12    (vi)  the  pharmacy ensures delivery of drugs to patients expecting to
    13  receive them from the APDSS in the event the automated patient  dispens-
    14  ing and storage system is disabled or malfunctions.
    15    (b)  The automated patient dispensing and storage system shall only be
    16  used for patients who have signed a written consent demonstrating  their
    17  informed consent to receive prescribed drugs from an APDSS and whose use
    18  of  the APDSS meet the criteria pursuant to paragraph (a) of this subdi-
    19  vision.
    20    (c) The automated patient dispensing and storage system shall  have  a
    21  means to identify each patient and only release the identified patient's
    22  drugs to the patient or the patient's agent.
    23    (d) A pharmacist shall perform all clinical services conducted as part
    24  of  the dispensing process, including, but not limited to, drug utiliza-
    25  tion review and consultation.
    26    (e) Drugs shall be dispensed from the automated patient dispensing and
    27  storage system only upon authorization from a pharmacist after the phar-
    28  macist has reviewed the  prescription  and  the  patient's  profile  for
    29  potential contraindicated and adverse drug reactions.
    30    (f)  All  drugs  dispensed  from  the automated patient dispensing and
    31  storage system for the first time shall be accompanied by a consultation
    32  conducted by a pharmacist licensed pursuant to article one hundred thir-
    33  ty-seven of the education law in one of the following  manners:  (i)  in
    34  person;  (ii)  through  a  drive-through prescription dispensing window;
    35  (iii) via a telecommunications link that has two-way audio and video; or
    36  (iv) through a telephone call between the pharmacist and the patient  or
    37  the  patient's  appointed  representative with the prescribing physician
    38  present for the duration of the call.
    39    (g) The automated patient dispensing and storage system shall  include
    40  a  notice,  prominently  posted  on the automated patient dispensing and
    41  storage system, that provides the name, address, and telephone number of
    42  the pharmacy that holds the automated  patient  dispensing  and  storage
    43  system license for that APDSS.
    44    (h)  The  labels  on  all  drugs  dispensed  by  the automated patient
    45  dispensing and storage system shall comply with all New York  state  and
    46  federal  requirements for labelling of drugs prescribed and dispensed in
    47  this state.
    48    (i) Any complaint, error, or omission involving the automated  patient
    49  dispensing  and  storage system shall be reviewed as part of the pharma-
    50  cy's quality assurance program.
    51    (j) The state board of pharmacy shall not issue a pharmacy  more  than
    52  fifteen  licenses  for  automated  patient dispensing and storage system
    53  units under this section. The state board of pharmacy  may  adopt  regu-
    54  lations  to  reduce the number of APDSS licenses that may be issued to a
    55  pharmacy.
