NOVAKHOV, BROOK-KRASNY, CHANG, NORBER -- read once and referred to the Committee on Governmental Employees AN ACT to amend the civil service law, in relation to prohibiting the appointment of persons affiliated with terror organizations The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The civil service law is amended by adding a new section 66 2 to read as follows: 3 § 66. Prohibited appointments. 1. Notwithstanding any other provision 4 of this article, no person shall be eligible for permanent, temporary or 5 provisional appointment to any position within the state civil service 6 if such person holds or has held membership or affiliation with an 7 organization that is on the federal terrorism watch list, or such person 8 is listed on the United States department of justice terrorist screening 9 database. Further, any person who carried out, participated in, planned, 10 financed, afforded material support to or otherwise facilitated any of 11 the attacks against Israel initiated by Hamas beginning on October 12 seventh, two thousand twenty-three, shall be ineligible for any perma- 13 nent, temporary or provisional appointment to any position within the 14 state civil service. 15 2. Any person appointed to a position within the state civil service 16 after the effective date of this section shall sign a statement of 17 compliance with this section sworn or subscribed to and bear a form 18 notice that false statements made therein are punishable as a class A 19 misdemeanor pursuant to section 210.45 of the penal law. Such form 20 notice shall include the following language: 21 "I understand that any false statements made herein are punishable as 22 a class A misdemeanor pursuant to section 210.45 of the penal law." 23 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
