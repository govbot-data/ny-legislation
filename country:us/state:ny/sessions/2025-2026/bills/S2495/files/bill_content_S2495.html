Add §355-f, Ed L; add §78-c, St Fin L; amd §5205, CPLR; amd §612, Tax L
 
Establishes the New York state pre-paid tuition plan by which a person may contribute to an account for the pre-payment of college tuition, tax free.

        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  education law, the state finance law, the civil
          practice law and rules and the tax law, in  relation  to  establishing
          the New York state pre-paid tuition plan

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The education law is amended by adding a new section  355-f
     2  to read as follows:
     3    §  355-f. "New York state pre-paid tuition plan". 1. Definitions.  For
     4  the purposes of this section, the following terms shall have the follow-
     5  ing meanings:
     6    a. "Account" or "pre-paid tuition account" shall  mean  an  individual
     7  pre-paid  tuition  account established in accordance with the provisions
     8  of this section.

    10  tuition  agreement pursuant to the provisions of this article, including
    11  a person who enters into such an agreement as a fiduciary  or  agent  on
    12  behalf  of  a trust, estate, partnership, association, company or corpo-
    13  ration. The account owner may also be the designated beneficiary of  the
    14  account.
    15    c. "City university" shall mean the city university of New York.
    16    d. "Comptroller" shall mean the state comptroller.
    17    e.  "Designated beneficiary" shall mean, with respect to an account or
    18  accounts, the individual designated  as  the  individual  whose  tuition
    19  expenses are expected to be paid from the account or accounts.
    20    f.  "Eligible  educational  institution" shall mean any institution of
    21  higher education defined  as  an  eligible  educational  institution  in
    22  section 529(e)(5) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended.
    23    g.  "Financial  organization" shall mean an organization authorized to

    21    3. Plan requirements. Every pre-paid tuition account shall comply with
    22  the provisions of this section.
    23    a.  A pre-paid tuition account may be opened by any person who desires
    24  to enter into a contract for  pre-payment  of  tuition  expenses  at  an
    25  institution  of the state university, the city university or any partic-
    26  ipating eligible educational institution. An account owner may designate
    27  another person as successor owner of the account in  the  event  of  the
    28  death of the original account owner. Such person who opens an account or
    29  any successor owner shall be considered the account owner.
    30    b.  An application for such account shall be in the form prescribed by
    31  the comptroller and contain the following:
    32    (i) the name, address and social security number or employer identifi-
    33  cation number of the account owner;
    34    (ii) the designation of a designated beneficiary;
    35    (iii) the name, address and social security number of  the  designated
    36  beneficiary; and
    37    (iv) such other information as the comptroller may require.
    38    c. The comptroller may establish a nominal fee for such application.
    39    d.  Any person, including the account owner, may make contributions to
    40  an account after the account is opened.
    41    e. Contributions to accounts may be made only in cash.
    42    f. Four years must elapse between  the  establishment  of  a  pre-paid
    43  tuition  account and the time the first qualified withdrawal is made for
    44  the payment of tuition expenses.
    45    g. An account owner may withdraw all or part of the  balance  from  an
    46  account on sixty days notice or such shorter period as may be authorized
    47  under rules governing the plan. Such rules shall include provisions that
    48  will  generally enable the determination as to whether a withdrawal is a
    49  nonqualified withdrawal or a qualified withdrawal.
    50    h. An account owner  may  change  the  designated  beneficiary  of  an
    51  account  to  an  individual  who  is a member of the family of the prior
    52  designated beneficiary in accordance with procedures established by  the
    53  comptroller.
    54    i.  An  account  owner  may transfer all or a portion of an account to
    55  another family tuition account, the subsequent designated beneficiary of
