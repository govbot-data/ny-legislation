BRABENEC -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Education AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to permitting public schools to utilize or display indigenous names The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 305 of the education law is amended by adding a new 2 subdivision 63 to read as follows: 3 63. (a) As used in this subdivision, the following terms shall have 4 the following meanings: 5 (i) "indigenous names" means a name that depicts or refers to indige- 6 nous persons, tribes, nations, individuals, customs, symbols or tradi- 7 tions, including actual or stereotypical aspects of indigenous cultures, 8 used to represent a public school's extracurricular activities, includ- 9 ing but not limited to such school's sports teams. 10 (ii) "indigenous logos or mascots" means a symbol or image that 11 depicts or refers to indigenous persons, tribes, nations, individuals, 12 customs, symbols, or traditions, including actual or stereotypical 13 aspects of indigenous cultures, used to represent a public school's 14 extracurricular activities, including but not limited to such school's 15 sports teams. 16 (b) The commissioner shall adopt regulations to allow public schools 17 to utilize or display an indigenous name and shall repeal any rule or 18 regulation inconsistent with allowing such use. Nothing in this subdivi- 19 sion shall prohibit the commissioner from adopting any rule or regu- 20 lation prohibiting public schools from utilizing or displaying an 21 indigenous logo or mascot other than for purposes of classroom instruc- 22 tion. 23 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
