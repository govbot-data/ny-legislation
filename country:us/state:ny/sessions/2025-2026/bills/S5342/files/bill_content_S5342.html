BILL NOS05342
 
SAME ASSAME AS A07710
 
SPONSORCOMRIE
 
COSPNSR
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Add §2354, amd §2324, Ins L
 
Provides for the use of telematics or usage-based insurance for private passenger or commercial automobile insurance or policies in certain circumstances.

        Introduced  by  Sen.  COMRIE -- read twice and ordered printed, and when

        AN ACT to amend the insurance law, in relation to the use of  telematics
          systems by insurers
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section 1. The insurance law is amended by adding a new  section  2354
     2  to read as follows:
     3    §  2354.  Use of telematics. (a) As used in this section, "telematics"
     4  or "usage-based insurance" means utilizing data  collected  relating  to
     5  any  of  the  following  regarding  a  vehicle  or  driver using vehicle
     6  systems, recording sensors, or a telecommunications device for insurance
     7  operations  including  pricing,  underwriting,  claims,   and   customer
     8  service:
     9    (1) Vehicle speed, location, mileage, and operating hours;
    10    (2) Driver acceleration, braking, and cornering habits;
    11    (3)  Telephone  screen touch-engagement, including but not limited to,
    12  handheld phone call and text usage during vehicle operation;
    13    (4) Road and weather conditions;
    14    (5) Engine diagnostics;
    15    (6) Advanced driver assistance  systems  (ADAS),  including  motorized
    16  seat belts; and
    17    (7)  Any  other  data  point  related  to the use and operation of the
    18  insured vehicle that has been filed with and approved by the superinten-
    19  dent.
    20    (b) An insurer that issues, sells, or delivers  private  passenger  or
    21  commercial  automobile  insurance or policies in the state may, with the
    22  consent of the participating driver, or the named insured if the partic-
    23  ipating driver is a minor, collect telematics data:
    24    (1) For establishing an insurance premium for the policy that  insures
    25  that vehicle and/or operator;
    26    (2) For claims settlement purposes, including investigation of fraud;
    27    (3) For rate filing purposes using aggregated telematics data; and

     1    (4) For other customer service purposes, including but not limited to:
     2  safety,  including  crash  detection;  risk mitigation, including severe
     3  weather warnings; additional insurance protection; and convenience.
     4    (c)  With  respect  to  private  passenger  motor vehicle insurance an
     5  insurer shall not require an applicant or a policyholder to  participate
     6  in  a  program  that  measures  the operation of an insured vehicle as a
     7  condition for pricing or underwriting a private passenger motor  vehicle
     8  insurance risk unless the insurer:
     9    (1)  only  offers  private  passenger motor vehicle insurance products
    10  that require insureds to participate in  a  program  that  measures  the
    11  operation of an insured vehicle, and discloses such requirement to:
    12    (i) an applicant at the time of application; and
    13    (ii) a policyholder at the time of renewal; and
    14    (2) includes the information required to be disclosed in paragraph one
    15  of  this  subsection  in  any  advertising  materials  for the insurance
    16  products offered by the insurer.
    17    (d) (1) This section shall not apply to a premium increase for a driv-
    18  er or vehicle due to a telematics program that measures driving behavior
    19  during the current policy period.
    20    (2) Notice to a participating driver, or named insured if the  partic-
    21  ipating  driver is a minor, of a premium increase resulting from the use
    22  of telematics data shall be made pursuant to regulations promulgated  by
    23  the superintendent in accordance with 15 U.S.C. § 1681m.
    24    (e)  Any  premium  increase  established pursuant to subsection (d) of
    25  this section shall be made pursuant to the provisions of subsection  (b)
    26  of section two thousand three hundred five of this chapter.
    27    (f) An insurer may share data obtained through telematics:
    28    (1) With the named insured;
    29    (2) With a person, natural or legal, other than the named insured only
    30  if the named insured provides consent; or
    31    (3) In accordance with a court order or subpoena.
    32    (g)  Any  insurer  that  offers  the  use of telematics or usage-based
    33  insurance, or any such program that measures the operation of an insured
    34  vehicle during the current policy period, shall do so in accordance with
    35  the provisions of article  twenty-six  of  this  chapter  applicable  to
    36  private passenger auto insurance.
    37    (h)  The  superintendent shall have the power to promulgate, from time
    38  to time, such regulations, not inconsistent with the provisions of  this
    39  chapter,  as  may  be  appropriate  to  carry out the provisions of this
    40  section and, insofar as applicable to this section, other provisions  of
    41  this chapter.
    42    §  2.  Section  2324  of  the insurance law is amended by adding a new
    43  subsection (c-1) to read as follows:
    44    (c-1) This section  shall  not  prohibit  any  insurer  from  offering
    45  participation  in  a loss prevention program that promotes and incentiv-
    46  izes safe driving behavior, with rewards, including through the  use  of
    47  telematics  as  defined  under section two thousand three hundred fifty-
    48  four of this article, so  long  as  participation  in  such  program  is
    49  offered both to members of the general public and to the insurer's poli-
    50  cyholders.
    51    §  3.  This  act shall take effect on the ninetieth day after it shall
    52  have become a law. Effective immediately, the addition, amendment and/or
    53  repeal of any rule or regulation necessary  for  the  implementation  of
    54  this  act  on its effective date are authorized to be made and completed
    55  on or before such effective date.
