Assembly Resolution No. 191
 
BY: M. of A. Buttenschon
 
        COMMENDING  Firefighter  Joseph  T. White upon the
        occasion of his designation for special  recognition
        after  50  years of dedicated service to the Maynard
        Volunteer Fire Department
 
  WHEREAS, The volunteer firefighters of New York State  are  renowned
throughout the Nation; their reputation is synonymous with excellence in
the arduous field of fire protection and prevention; and
 
  WHEREAS,   Volunteer  firefighters  exemplify  the  power  of  human
compassion and the strength of the American spirit  through  actions  of
the   most   heroic  magnitude;  their  sacrifices  and  their  selfless
dedication merit tribute and recognition by all citizens of  this  great
country; and
 
  WHEREAS,   This   Legislative   Body  is  justly  proud  to  commend
Firefighter Joseph T. White upon the occasion  of  his  designation  for
special  recognition  after 50 years of dedicated service to the Maynard
Volunteer Fire Department, to be celebrated  on  Saturday,  February  1,
2025,  at  the  department's  Annual  Installation Banquet in Utica, New
York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Volunteer  firefighters  and  fire  departments  not  only
provide  the  critical  service  of  fire protection, but also engage in
other activities of great benefit and importance to the communities they
serve; and
 
  WHEREAS, Throughout his tenure  of  service,  Joseph  T.  White  has
continually   demonstrated   a   high  knowledge  and  understanding  of
leadership, training, and educational roles with the department and  his
crews; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Born  on  January 16, 1932, Joseph T. White graduated from
Utica Catholic Academy in 1951, before proudly serving his country as  a
member of the United States Army; and
 
  WHEREAS,   Joseph   T.  White  joined  the  Maynard  Volunteer  Fire
Department on January 16, 1975; and
 
  WHEREAS, Alongside his duties with the fire  department,  Joseph  T.
White  dedicated 15 years as the Basketball Coach at St. Peter's Grammar
School and 12 years coaching the Notre Dame Boys Varsity Team; a  member
of  the  Notre  Dame  High School Hall of Fame, he also led his teams to
five Central Oneida League titles, four Tri-Valley League Championships,
four Section Three titles, and a spot in the Final Four of the  NYSPHSAA
Championship in 1991-92; and
 
  WHEREAS, A proud Irishman, Joseph T. White has been an integral part
of local traditions and celebrations, especially the Utica St. Patrick's
Day Parade where he was named Grand Marshal in 1999; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Joseph  T.  White  is widely recognized for his unwavering
commitment to his community, family, and church; with  five  decades  of
 
service  with  the  Maynard Fire Department and a successful career as a
basketball coach, he has made a significant impact on  countless  lives;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  takes  a  special  dedication, a strong desire to help
others, and a tireless sense of community to forsake precious time  with
family  and friends to respond to the signal that a neighbor is in need;
Joseph T. White is one such firefighter; throughout 50 years of  devoted
service,  this  firefighter  heroically  performed, above and beyond the
call of duty, those responsibilities  which  define  the  task  of  fire
protection; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
commend Firefighter Joseph T. White upon the occasion of his designation
for special recognition after 50  years  of  dedicated  service  to  the
Maynard Volunteer Fire Department; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Firefighter Joseph T. White.
