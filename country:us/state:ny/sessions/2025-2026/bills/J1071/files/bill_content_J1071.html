J01071 Text:

 
Senate Resolution No. 1071
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
        COMMEMORATING  the 95th Anniversary of P.J. Green,
        Inc.,   a   business   committed   to    innovation,
        sustainability,  and community service in Utica, New
        York
 
  WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body to  recognize  and
honor those enduring institutions and enterprises which have contributed
significantly  to the economic vitality and cultural enrichment of their
communities and this great Empire State; and
 
  WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and  in  full  accord  with  its
long-standing  traditions,  this  Legislative  Body  is  justly proud to
commemorate the 95th Anniversary of P.J. Green, Inc., located in  Utica,
New  York,  a  distinguished  business  founded  in 1928 by Philip James
Green; and
 
  WHEREAS, What began in  an  East  Utica  garage  as  a  door-to-door
delivery service of printed advertisements and promotional materials has
evolved,  under  four generations of dedicated family leadership, into a
regional  leader  in  printing,  marketing,  and  direct-mail  services,
employing  80  professionals  across  offices  in  Utica,  Syracuse, and
Albany; and
 
  WHEREAS, P.J. Green, Inc. is  currently  co-owned  and  operated  by
Philip  Green's  grandchildren  -  Marianne  Reynolds, Kerry Gradel, and
Terence "Rick" Green - with key  roles  also  held  by  siblings  Sharon
Catlin  and  Kristi  Zbytniewski,  and  great-grandson  Andrew  Reynolds
managing the company's robust printing operations; and
 
  WHEREAS, The company  stands  as  a  certified  Women  and  Minority
Business   Enterprise  (WMBE)  and  has  demonstrated  adaptability  and
resilience in the face of challenges, including those presented  by  the
COVID-19  pandemic,  creatively  addressing  supply  chain  and staffing
issues with innovation and success; and
 
  WHEREAS, Offering a comprehensive array of services such as database
preparation,   targeted   mailing,   commercial   printing,    marketing
consulting,  and  branded  promotional  products,  P.J.  Green, Inc. has
continually embraced technological advancement, including investment  in
cutting-edge   Indigo   presses   and  environmentally  safe  production
equipment; and
 
  WHEREAS, Committed to environmental stewardship, P.J. Green, Inc. is
a  proud  participant  in  the  RecycleOne  program  and   uses   Forest
Stewardship  Council-certified  paper,  environmentally  safe  inks  and
fluids,  and  an  HP  Indigo  oil  recycling  machine,  ensuring   their
operations remain sustainable and waste-conscious; and
 
  WHEREAS,   The   business's   dedication   to  customer  service  is
exemplified by its long-standing partnership with organizations such  as
Hospice  &  Palliative  Care,  whose  leadership  commends  P.J. Green's
unwavering reliability, professionalism, and excellence; and
 
  WHEREAS, On the occasion of its 95th Anniversary and nomination  for
the  2024 Business of the Year by the Greater Utica Chamber of Commerce,
P.J.   Green,  Inc.  continues  to  represent  the  enduring  spirit  of
entrepreneurial vision, familial legacy, and civic contribution; and
 
  WHEREAS,  It  is  the  sense  of  this  Legislative  Body  that when
businesses of such noble purpose and accomplishment are brought  to  our
attention, they should be recognized by all the citizens of the State of
New York; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
commemorate the 95th Anniversary of P.J. Green, Inc., and to extend  its
best wishes for continued success in all its future endeavors; and be it
further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to Marianne Reynolds, President of P.J. Green, Inc.
