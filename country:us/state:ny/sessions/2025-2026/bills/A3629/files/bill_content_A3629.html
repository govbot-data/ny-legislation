        AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to prohibiting sharing or
          selling personal data to third  parties  by  government  entities  and
          contractors
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The executive law is amended by adding a new article 5-A to
     2  read as follows:
     3                                  ARTICLE 5-A
     4                    SHARING AND SELLING OF PERSONAL DATA
     5  Section 81. Definitions.
     6          82. Data collection disclosure.
     7          83. Sharing and selling of personal information prohibited.
     8          84. Limitation on restrictions.
     9    § 81. Definitions. As used in this article, the following terms  shall
    10  have the following meanings unless otherwise specified:
    11    1.  "Aggregate  personal  information"  shall  mean  information  that
    12  relates to a group or category of  individuals,  from  which  individual
    13  identities  have been removed, that is not linked or reasonably linkable
    14  to any individual or  household,  including  via  a  device.  "Aggregate
    15  personal  information"  shall  not mean one or more individual's records
    16  that have been deidentified.
    17    2. "Collects", "collected",  or  "collection"  shall  mean  gathering,
    18  obtaining,  receiving,  or accessing any personal information pertaining
    19  to an individual by any means. This includes receiving information  from
    20  such individual either actively or passively.
    21    3.  "Contractor" means a contractor, or subcontractor of a contractor,
    22  that contracts to process information on behalf of a  government  entity
    23  and  to  which such government entity discloses an individual's personal
    24  information for a legitimate government purpose pursuant  to  a  written

     1  contract,  provided  that  such  contract  prohibits  such contractor or
     2  subcontractor receiving such personal information from retaining, using,
     3  or disclosing such personal information for any purpose other  than  for
     4  the  specific  purpose  of  performing  the  services  specified in such
     5  contract, or as otherwise permitted by this article,  including  retain-
     6  ing,  using,  or  disclosing  such personal information for a commercial
     7  purpose other than providing the services specified in the contract.
     8    4. "Deidentified" shall mean information that cannot reasonably  iden-
     9  tify,  relate  to,  describe, be capable of being associated with, or be
    10  linked to, directly or indirectly,  a  particular  individual,  provided
    11  that a government entity that uses such deidentified information:
    12    (a)  has  implemented technical safeguards and processes that prohibit
    13  reidentification of the individual to whom such information may pertain;
    14    (b) has  implemented  processes  to  prevent  inadvertent  release  of
    15  deidentified information; and
    16    (c) makes no attempt to reidentify such information.
    17    5. "Device" shall mean any physical object that is capable of connect-
    18  ing to the internet, directly or indirectly, or to another device.
    19    6.  "Government entity" or "entity" shall mean any state agency or any
    20  part, body, or subdivision thereof.
    21    7. "Individual" shall mean a person who is  a  resident  of  New  York
    22  state.
    23    8.  (a) "Personal information" shall mean information that identifies,
    24  relates to, describes, is capable of being  associated  with,  or  could
    25  reasonably be linked to, directly or indirectly, a particular individual
    26  or  household. Personal information includes, but is not limited to, the
    27  following:
    28    (i) identifiers such as a real name,  alias,  postal  address,  unique
    29  personal  identifier,  internet  protocol address, email address, social
    30  security number, driver's license number, passport  number,  photograph,
    31  or other similar identifiers;
    32    (ii)  characteristics  of  protected classifications under New York or
    33  federal law;
    34    (iii) commercial information, including records of  real  or  personal
    35  property;
    36    (iv) biometric information;
    37    (v) audio, electronic, visual, or similar information;
    38    (vi) professional or employment-related information;
    39    (vii)  education  information,  defined  as  information  that  is not
    40  publicly available personally identifiable information as defined in the
    41  family educational rights and privacy act (20 USC 1232g);
    42    (viii) inferences drawn from any of the information identified in this
    43  paragraph to create a profile about an individual reflecting such  indi-
    44  vidual's preferences, characteristics, psychological trends, predisposi-
    45  tions, behavior, attitudes, intelligence, abilities, and aptitudes; and
    46    (ix) financial or tax information.
    47    (b) "Personal information" shall not include publicly available infor-
    48  mation.  For these purposes, "publicly available" shall mean information
    49  that is lawfully made available from federal, state, or local government
    50  records, or any conditions associated with such information.   "Publicly
    51  available" shall not include an individual's information that is deiden-
    52  tified or aggregate personal information.
    53    9.  "Probabilistic  identifier"  shall  mean  the identification of an
    54  individual or a device to a degree of certainty of  more  probable  than
    55  not  based  on  any  categories  of personal information included in, or

     1  similar to, the categories  enumerated  in  subdivision  eight  of  this
     2  section.
     3    10. "Process" or "processing" shall mean any operation or set of oper-
     4  ations  that are performed on personal data or on sets of personal data,
     5  whether or not by automated means.
     6    11. (a) "Sell", "selling", "sale", or "sold" shall mean selling, rent-
     7  ing, releasing,  disclosing,  disseminating,  making  available,  trans-
     8  ferring, or otherwise communicating orally, in writing, or by electronic
     9  or  other  means,  an  individual's personal information by a government
    10  entity or contractor to a third party for  monetary  or  other  valuable
    11  consideration.
    12    (b)  A government entity or contractor does not sell personal informa-
    13  tion within the meaning of this article when:
    14    (i) An individual uses or directs such government entity or contractor
    15  to  intentionally  disclose  personal  information  to  a  third  party,
    16  provided  such third party also does not sell such personal information,
    17  unless such disclosure would be consistent with the provisions  of  this
    18  article.
    19    (ii)  Such government entity or contractor uses or shares with a third
    20  party personal information of an individual that is necessary to perform
    21  a legitimate government purpose if both of the following conditions  are
    22  met:
    23    (1)  the  government  entity  or  contractor  has provided notice that
    24  information is being used or shared; and
    25    (2) the third party  does  not  further  collect,  sell,  or  use  the
    26  personal  information  of such individual except as necessary to perform
    27  the business purpose for which it received such information.
    28    (iii) A contractor who transfers to  a  third  party  an  individual's
    29  personal  information as an asset that is part of a merger, acquisition,
    30  bankruptcy, or other transaction in which such contractor or third party
    31  assumes control of all or part of such third party  provided  that  such
    32  information is used or shared consistently with this article. If a third
    33  party materially alters how it uses or shares personal information of an
    34  individual in a manner that is materially inconsistent with the promises
    35  made at the time of collection, it shall provide prior notice of the new
    36  or changed practice to such individual. Such notice shall be sufficient-
    37  ly  prominent  and robust to ensure that individuals can easily exercise
    38  their choices consistently with section eighty-three of this article.
    39    12. "Service" or "services" shall  mean  work,  labor,  and  services,
    40  including  services  furnished  in connection with the sale or repair of
    41  goods.
    42    13. "Third party" shall mean a person or business entity  who  is  not
    43  another government entity or contractor thereof.
    44    14.  "Unique  identifier" or "unique personal identifier" shall mean a
    45  persistent identifier that can be used to  recognize  an  individual,  a
    46  family, or a device that is linked to an individual or family, over time
    47  and  across  different services, including, but not limited to, a device
    48  identifier; an internet protocol address; cookies, beacons, pixel  tags,
    49  or  similar  technology;  unique  pseudonym,  or  user  alias; telephone
    50  numbers, or other forms of persistent or probabilistic identifiers  that
    51  can  be used to identify a particular individual or device. For purposes
    52  of this subdivision, "family" means a custodial parent or  guardian  and
    53  any minor children over which such parent or guardian has custody.
    54    § 82. Data collection disclosure. 1. A government entity or contractor
    55  that  collects  an individual's personal information shall, at or before
    56  the point of collection, inform such individual as to the categories  of

        A. 3629                             4
 
     1  personal  information  to  be  collected and the purposes for which such
     2  categories of personal information shall be used. A government entity or
     3  contractor shall not collect additional categories of personal  informa-
     4  tion or use personal information collected for additional purposes with-
     5  out providing such individual with notice consistent with this article.
     6    2.  This  section  shall not require a government entity or contractor
     7  to:
     8    (a) retain any personal information collected for a  single,  one-time
     9  transaction  if  such  information  is  not  shared  or retained by such
    10  government entity or contractor; or
    11    (b) reidentify or otherwise link information that is not maintained in
    12  a manner that would be considered personal information.
    13    § 83. Sharing and selling of personal information  prohibited.  1.  No
    14  government  entity  or  contractor shall share any individual's personal
    15  information with a contractor or subcontractor unless  such  information
    16  is crucial to the purpose for which such government entity or contractor
    17  has contracted such contractor or subcontractor's services.
    18    2.  No  government  entity  or contractor shall share any individual's
    19  personal information with another government entity or contractor unless
    20  such information is crucial to the performance of such other  government
    21  entity  or  contractor's  duties,  and  such  other government entity or
    22  contractor cannot procure such personal information on its  own  without
    23  serious hardship.
    24    3.  No government entity or contractor shall sell personal information
    25  about an individual.
    26    § 84. Limitation  on  restrictions.  1.  The  obligations  imposed  on
    27  government  entities  and contractors by this article shall not restrict
    28  any government entity or contractor's ability to:
    29    (a) otherwise comply with federal, state, or local laws;
    30    (b) comply with a civil, criminal,  or  regulatory  inquiry,  investi-
    31  gation, subpoena, or summons by federal, state, or local authorities;
    32    (c)  comply  with a request made under the freedom of information law;
    33  or
    34    (d) exercise or defend legal claims.
    35    2. This article shall not apply to the sale of personal information to
    36  or from a consumer  reporting  agency  if  such  information  is  to  be
    37  reported  in,  or  used to generate, a consumer report as defined by the
    38  federal fair credit reporting act (15 USC 1681), and use of that  infor-
    39  mation is limited by such act.
    40    3.  A  government  entity  that  discloses  personal  information to a
    41  contractor shall not be liable under this  article  if  such  contractor
    42  uses  such  personal  information  in  violation of the restrictions set
    43  forth in this article, provided that, at the  time  of  disclosing  such
    44  personal  information, such government entity does not have actual know-
    45  ledge or reason to believe that such contractor intends to commit such a
    46  violation. No contractor shall be liable  under  this  article  for  the
    47  obligations of a government entity for which it provides services as set
    48  forth in this article.
    49    4.  This article shall not be construed to require a government entity
    50  to reidentify or otherwise link information that is not maintained in  a
    51  manner that would be considered personal information.
    52    5.  The  rights afforded to individuals and the obligations imposed on
    53  government entities and contractors by this article shall not  adversely
    54  affect the rights and freedoms of any other person.
    55    §  2. This act shall take effect one year after it shall have become a
    56  law.
