Romero COMMEMORATING the 100th Anniversary of the Fort Orange Stamp Club WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body to recognize and pay tribute to those organizations of high purpose and worthy accomplishment whose endeavors have faithfully preserved the history of this great State and Nation; and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and in full accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to commemorate the 100th Anniversary of the Fort Orange Stamp Club; and WHEREAS, The Fort Orange Stamp Club was established on April 9, 1926; one century ago, the principle founding members of the club met in Albany, New York, to establish this historic organization; and WHEREAS, Since its founding, many notable individuals have joined the Fort Orange Stamp Club; President Franklin D. Roosevelt and Albany Mayor Erastus Corning were among the distinguished members; and WHEREAS, The Fort Orange Stamp Club is dedicated to promoting the preservation and appreciation of historic United States postage stamps; and WHEREAS, The stamps preserved and promoted by the Fort Orange Stamp Club are works of art that depict our Nation's great people, events, places, and culture; and WHEREAS, One of the first "penny postman" in America was Albany's own William B. Winne, a historic figure of the early American postal system who is honored by the Fort Orange Stamp Club; and WHEREAS, In 1936, the Fort Orange Stamp Club became a member in good standing of the American Philatelic Society, and the club has maintained its membership to this day; and WHEREAS, The Fort Orange Stamp Club is dedicated to advancing philately and postal history in the Capital District, New York State, and the Nation; this noble cause is accomplished through the hard work, dedication, and passion of its members over the past 100 years; and WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body that when such notable and accomplished organizations are brought to our attention, they should be celebrated by all the citizens of New York State; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to commemorate the 100th Anniversary of the Fort Orange Stamp Club; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the president of the Fort Orange Stamp Club.
