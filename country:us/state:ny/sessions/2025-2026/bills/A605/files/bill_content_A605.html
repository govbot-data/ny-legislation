Amd §§504 & 490, V & T L
 
Authorizes the issuance of licenses and non-driver identification cards containing a Lifetime Liberty Pass distinguishing mark to certain applicants who were members of the armed forces of the United States.

        Introduced by M. of A. BUTTENSCHON, JACKSON -- read once and referred to
          the Committee on Transportation
 
        AN  ACT to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to authorizing
          the issuance of licenses and non-driver identification cards  contain-
          ing a Lifetime Liberty Pass distinguishing mark
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Subdivision 1 of section 504 of the vehicle and traffic law
     2  is amended by adding a new paragraph (a-2) to read as follows:
     3    (a-2) Every license or renewal thereof issued  to  an  applicant  who:
     4  (i) was a member of the armed forces of the United States; (ii) received
     5  an  honorable discharge or was released therefrom under honorable condi-
     6  tions; and (iii) qualifies for and has received a Lifetime Liberty  Pass
     7  permitting  qualified veterans with disabilities discounted use of state
     8  parks, historic sites,  and  recreational  facilities  operated  by  the
     9  office  of  parks,  recreation  and  historic preservation and the state
    10  department of environmental  conservation,  shall,  upon  such  person's
    11  request  and  submission of proof as set forth herein, contain a distin-
    12  guishing mark, in such form as the commissioner shall  determine,  indi-
    13  cating  that  such  person  is a holder of a Lifetime Liberty Pass. Such
    14  proof shall consist of a certificate of release or discharge from active
    15  duty including but not limited to a DD Form 214, a copy of such person's
    16  Lifetime Liberty Pass or other proof satisfactory to  the  commissioner.
    17  The  commissioner  shall  not  require  fees  for  the  issuance of such
    18  licenses or renewals thereof to persons requesting  a  Lifetime  Liberty
    19  Pass  distinguishing  mark  which  are  different  from  fees  otherwise
    20  required; provided, however, that notwithstanding the provisions of this
    21  section, the commissioner shall not require fees for  a  duplication  or
    22  amendment  of  a  license  prior  to  its renewal if such duplication or
    23  amendment was solely for the purpose of adding to such license.

     1    § 2. Subdivision 3 of section 490 of the vehicle and  traffic  law  is
     2  amended by adding a new paragraph (b-1) to read as follows:
     3    (b-1) Every identification card or renewal thereof issued to an appli-
     4  cant  who:  (i)  was  a member of the armed forces of the United States;
     5  (ii) received an honorable discharge or  was  released  therefrom  under
     6  honorable  conditions;  and (iii) qualifies for and has received a Life-
     7  time  Liberty  Pass  permitting  qualified  veterans  with  disabilities
     8  discounted  use of state parks, historic sites, and recreational facili-
     9  ties operated by the office of parks, recreation and historic  preserva-
    10  tion and the state department of environmental conservation, shall, upon
    11  such  person's  request  and  submission  of  proof as set forth herein,
    12  contain a distinguishing mark, in such form as  the  commissioner  shall
    13  determine, indicating that such person is a holder of a Lifetime Liberty
    14  Pass.  Such proof shall consist of a certificate of release or discharge
    15  from active duty including but not limited to a DD Form 214, a  copy  of
    16  such  person's  Lifetime Liberty Pass or other proof satisfactory to the
    17  commissioner. The commissioner shall not require fees for  the  issuance
    18  of such identification cards or renewals thereof to persons requesting a
    19  Lifetime  Liberty Pass distinguishing mark which are different from fees
    20  otherwise  required;  provided,  however,   that   notwithstanding   the
    21  provisions  of this section, the commissioner shall not require fees for
    22  a duplication or amendment  of  an  identification  card  prior  to  its
    23  renewal  if  such duplication or amendment was solely for the purpose of
    24  adding a Lifetime Liberty Pass distinguishing mark to  such  identifica-
    25  tion card.
    26    §  3.  This  act shall take effect on the ninetieth day after it shall
    27  have become a law.    Effective  immediately,  the  addition,  amendment
    28  and/or repeal of any rule or regulation necessary for the implementation
    29  of  this  act  on  its  effective  date  are  authorized  to be made and
    30  completed on or before such effective date.
