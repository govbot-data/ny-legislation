Add Art 28 §§500 - 506, Ec Dev L
 
Establishes a rural remote recruitment program to provide monetary incentives for remote workers to relocate into rural areas of New York that opt into the program; provides an application process; provides for funding; requires on an annual basis a program report specifying the number of applicants to the program, the number of applicants approved, the names of participants, information as to benefits and the total increase in rural population.

        AN  ACT to amend the economic development law, in relation to establish-
          ing a rural remote recruitment program

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The  economic  development law is amended by adding a new
     2  article 28 to read as follows:
     3                                 ARTICLE 28
     4                      RURAL REMOTE RECRUITMENT PROGRAM
     5  Section 500. Definitions.
     6          501. Rural remote recruitment program.
     7          502. Eligibility criteria.
     8          503. Application and approval process.
     9          504. Powers and duties of the commissioner.
    10          505. Funding.
    11          506. Reporting.
    12    § 500. Definitions. For the purposes of this article:
    13    1. "Rural areas" shall mean any city, town, or village  with  a  popu-
    14  lation  density  less  than  two  hundred  fifty  people per square mile
    15  located within a county with a population of  less  than  three  hundred
    16  thousand  people and a population density of less than two hundred fifty
    17  people per square mile.
    18    2. "Remote worker" shall mean any full time employed person, who works
    19  an average of at least thirty-five hours per week  and  participates  in
    20  the  practice  of  working  from one's home or another designated space,
    21  instead of the traditional office environment.
    22    3. "Acceptance date" shall mean the date upon which  an  applicant  to
    23  the  rural  remote  recruitment program accepts and commits to the terms
    24  and agreements of the program.

     1    4. "Rural remote recruitment program"  or  "program"  shall  mean  the
     2  program established by section five hundred one of this article.
     3    §  501.  Rural  remote  recruitment program. 1. There is hereby estab-
     4  lished within the department a "rural  remote  recruitment"  program  to
     5  provide  monetary  incentives  for remote workers to relocate into rural
     6  areas of New York that opt into the program.
     7    2. The department shall coordinate with the  department  of  labor  to
     8  develop  an application, competitive evaluation process and reporting to
     9  support the rural remote recruitment program.
    10    § 502. Eligibility criteria. In order  to  participate  in  the  rural
    11  remote  recruitment  program,  a  remote worker applicant to the program
    12  must satisfy the following criteria:
    13    1. the applicant must reside in New York outside of a designated rural
    14  area, reside outside of New York state, or currently reside in a  desig-
    15  nated rural area and be working remotely;
    16    2. the applicant must be eighteen years of age or older;
    17    3. the applicant must be employed in a full-time remote position;
    18    4. the applicant must be eligible to work inside the U.S.; and
    19    5. the applicant must be able to relocate into a rural area within six
    20  months  of  the  program and must agree to live in the rural area for at
    21  least twenty-four months.
    22    § 503. Application and approval process. 1. A remote worker  applicant
    23  to the program must submit a completed application in such form and with
    24  such information as prescribed by the commissioner.
    25    2. As part of such application, each applicant must:
    26    (a)  submit  documented  proof  of  income  to New York state annually
    27  during the term of the program;
    28    (b) submit proof of residency annually during the term of the program;
    29    (c) agree to allow the department of taxation  and  finance  to  share
    30  their tax information with the department; provided that any information
    31  shared  as a result of this agreement shall not be available for disclo-
    32  sure or inspection under the state freedom of information law; and
    33    (d) certify, under penalty  of  perjury,  that  the  applicant  is  in
    34  substantial compliance with all local, state, and federal tax laws.
    35    3.  The  commissioner  may approve an application from a remote worker
    36  applicant upon determining that such  applicant  meets  the  eligibility
    37  criteria  established in this article. Following approval by the commis-
    38  sioner of an application to participate in the program, the commissioner
    39  shall issue the applicant a monetary award of up to twenty-six  thousand
    40  dollars. The monetary incentive shall be composed of the following:
    41    (a) a relocation incentive of up to fifteen thousand dollars;
    42    (b) a homebuyer incentive of up to ten thousand dollars;
    43    (c) up to one thousand dollars for travel reimbursement for visiting a
    44  rural region of interest prior to joining; and
    45    (d)  up  to  ten thousand dollars for current remote worker applicants
    46  residing in a designated rural area.
    47    4. Upon acceptance into the program, the incentives are to be paid  as
    48  outlined below:
    49    (a)  within  fourteen days of the department receiving receipts of all
    50  out-of-pocket expenses incurred when visiting the rural region of inter-
    51  est, the recipient will be reimbursed up to  one  thousand  dollars  for
    52  out-of-pocket expenses.
    53    (b)  within  fourteen days of the remote worker applicant's acceptance
    54  date, the remote worker will be paid a one-time payment of  seven  thou-
    55  sand dollars.

    10  ipating  in the program should receive a one-time reimbursement of up to
    11  ten thousand dollars after submitting necessary  proof  of  contract  as
    12  determined by the commissioner.
    13    §  504.  Powers  and  duties  of the commissioner. 1. The commissioner
    14  shall, in consultation with the commissioner of labor, promulgate  regu-
    15  lations consistent with the purposes of this article that, notwithstand-
    16  ing any provisions to the contrary in the state administrative procedure
    17  act,  may  be  adopted  on  an  emergency  basis. Such regulations shall
    18  include, but not be limited to, eligibility criteria for remote  workers
    19  desiring  to  participate in the program, procedures for the receipt and
    20  evaluation of applications from remote workers  to  participate  in  the
    21  program,  and  such  other  provisions  as  the commissioner deems to be
    22  appropriate in order to implement the provisions of this article.
    23    2. The commissioner shall solely  determine  the  eligibility  of  any
    24  applicant  applying  for  entry  into  the  program and shall remove any
    25  participant from the program for failing to meet any of the requirements
    26  set forth in this article or for  making  a  material  misrepresentation
    27  with respect to their participation in the program.
    28    §  505.  Funding.  The  commissioner  of the department of labor shall
    29  transfer any necessary funding from the Federal Workforce Investment Act
    30  Account as funded by grants under the Workforce Investment  Act,  Public
    31  Law  105-220,  and  the Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act, Public
    32  Law 113-128 to the department to support this article.
    33    § 506. Reporting. The commissioner shall prepare on an annual basis  a
    34  program report for posting on the department's website. The first report
    35  will be posted by June thirtieth, two thousand twenty-seven, and by June
    36  thirtieth  every  year thereafter. Such report shall include, but not be
    37  limited to, the following: number of applicants; number of  participants
    38  approved;  names  of  participants;  total amount of benefits certified;
    39  benefits received per participant; total increase in  rural  population;
    40  and such other information as the commissioner determines.
    41    §  2. This act shall take effect on the first of January next succeed-
    42  ing the date upon which it shall have become a law.
