AN ACT to amend chapter 19 of the laws of 1964, relating to incorporat-
ing the Nesconset Volunteer Exempt Firemen's Benevolent Association,
and providing for its powers and duties, in relation to the purpose of
such association and the use of foreign fire insurance premium taxes
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Sections 1, 2, 3 and 7 of chapter 19 of the laws of 1964,
2 relating to incorporating the Nesconset Volunteer Exempt Firemen's
3 Benevolent Association, and providing for its powers and duties, are
4 amended to read as follows:
5 Section 1. Incorporation; membership. A membership corporation by the
6 name of "Nesconset Volunteer Exempt [Firemen's] Firefighter's Benevolent
7 Association" is hereby created. It shall be composed of such persons
8 eligible to membership therein as hereinafter provided as shall (a)
9 notify the secretary of the Nesconset volunteer fire department in writ-
10 ing prior to the organization meeting of such corporation hereinafter
11 provided for of their desire to become members, or (b) shall attend such
12 meeting or an adjournment thereof, and also such persons so eligible as
13 may become members of such corporation pursuant to its by-laws.
14 § 2. Persons eligible to membership. All persons who are now, or who
15 have been, or who hereafter shall be active volunteer members of the
16 Nesconset volunteer fire department, or of any fire corporation in the
17 Nesconset fire district, and who have heretofore acquired or shall here-
18 after acquire the status of exempt volunteer [firemen] firefighters as
19 defined in section two hundred of the general municipal law or any other
20 applicable law by virtue of having rendered service in the Nesconset

21 § 3. Purposes. [The purposes of such corporation shall be the mainte-
22 nance of suitable headquarters for, and the promotion of fraternal
23 intercourse among the members of such corporation, the relief, aid and

28 corporation created by this act shall be: to provide relief, aid and
29 assistance to the volunteer members of such corporation who are disa-
30 bled, indigent or injured in the performance of their duties, and their
31 families; to promote and safeguard the welfare of the volunteer members
32 of such corporation, and their families; to maintain a suitable head-
33 quarters for the members of such corporation; to provide for the health
34 and safety of the volunteer members of such corporation; and to enhance
35 the morale of the volunteer members of such corporation by providing
36 social and recreational activities for the volunteer members of such
37 corporation, and the promotion of the volunteer fire service within the
38 territory protected by the Nesconset volunteer fire department.
39 § 7. Precept for payment of foreign fire insurance premium taxes. Such
40 corporation shall collect and there shall be paid to it all taxes
41 imposed by section [five hundred fifty-three] nine thousand one hundred
42 four of the insurance law, for fire department use and benefit, upon
43 premiums for insurance against loss or damage by fire covering property
44 situated within the territory protected by the Nesconset volunteer fire
45 department, located in the town of Smithtown, Suffolk county, New York,
46 or within any duly organized territory in which the Nesconset volunteer
47 fire department is or may be obligated to render fire protection. The
48 officer of said corporation designated by its by-laws to collect and
49 receive the aforesaid tax shall have all the powers and to be subject to
50 all the provisions of the insurance law, relating to treasurers of fire
51 departments. Such corporation shall also be entitled to receive a share
52 of the tax imposed by section [five hundred fifty-four] nine thousand
53 one hundred five of the insurance law, based upon the business written
54 in the territory with respect to which it is entitled to collect and
55 receive the tax under section [five hundred fifty-three] nine thousand
