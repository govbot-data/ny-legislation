Add §208, Fin Serv L
 
Requires the department of financial services to comply with the provisions of Executive Order 95 of 2013, "Using Technology to Promote Transparency, Improve Government Performance and Enhance Citizen Engagement", and to provide the New York state office of information technology services and the legislature certain necessary data.

        AN ACT to amend the financial services law, in relation to enacting  the
          "department of financial services open data act"
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as the "department
     2  of financial services open data act".
     3    § 2. Legislative findings. The legislature finds that the  release  of
     4  data,  in  a format that is easily accessible, understandable and usable
     5  by the public, can increase civic engagement with government.  In  addi-
     6  tion, in a department providing a public service and operating an enter-
     7  prise  as  vital as that of the Department of Financial Services, trans-
     8  parency in data is in the public's best interest. It is  the  intent  of
     9  the legislature that the department and its subsidiaries release as much
    10  data  as is feasible in open formats, so that the public can access that
    11  data, analyze it, and engage with the department  with  suggestions  and
    12  solutions to improve its operations.
    13    § 3. The financial services law is amended by adding a new section 208
    14  to read as follows:
    15    § 208. Open data reporting. (a) The department shall fully comply with
    16  the  provisions  of  Executive  Order  95  of 2013, "Using Technology to
    17  Promote Transparency, Improve Government Performance and Enhance Citizen
    18  Engagement", and shall provide the New York state office of  information
    19  technology   services  and  the  legislature  all  data  as  defined  in
    20  subsection (b) of this section.
    21    (b) For the purposes of this section, the following terms  shall  have
    22  the following meanings:
    23    (1)  "open  data  website"  shall mean the website data.ny.gov or such
    24  other successor website maintained by, or on behalf of,  the  state,  as
    25  deemed appropriate by the New York state office of information technolo-

     1  gy services under Executive Order 95 of 2013, or any successor agency or

     3    (2)  "data" shall mean final versions of statistical or factual infor-
     4  mation that: (i) are in alphanumeric form reflected in  a  list,  table,
     5  graph,  chart  or other non-narrative form, that can be digitally trans-
     6  mitted or processed; (ii) are regularly created or maintained by  or  on
     7  behalf  of the department and are controlled by the same department; and
     8  (iii) record a measurement, transaction or determination related to  the
     9  mission  of  the  department.    The term "data" shall not include image
    10  files, such as designs, drawings, photos or scanned copies  of  original
    11  documents; provided, however, that the term "data" shall include statis-
    12  tical  or  factual information about image files and geographic informa-
    13  tion system data;
    14    (3) "data set" shall mean a named collection of related records  main-
    15  tained  on  a storage device, with the collection containing data organ-
    16  ized or formatted in a specific or  prescribed  way,  often  in  tabular
    17  form; and
    18    (4)  "publishable  DFS  data" shall mean data that is collected by the
    19  department where the department is permitted, required or able  to  make
    20  the data available to the public, consistent with any and all applicable
    21  laws,  rules,  regulations,  ordinances,  resolutions, policies or other
    22  restrictions, requirements or rights associated with the data, including
    23  but not limited to contractual or other legal  orders,  restrictions  or
    24  requirements. Data shall not be publishable DFS data if making such data
    25  available  on the open data website would violate statute or regulation,
    26  including, but not limited to,  disclosures  that  would  constitute  an
    27  unwarranted  invasion  of  personal privacy, endanger the public health,
    28  safety or welfare, hinder the operation of government, including  crimi-
    29  nal  and civil investigations, or impose an undue financial, operational
    30  or administrative burden on the the department or  its  subsidiaries  or
    31  affiliates.
    32    (c)  As  required  by Executive Order 95 of 2013, the department shall
    33  designate a data coordinator, who shall:
    34    (1) have authority equivalent to that of a deputy commissioner or  the
    35  head of a division or bureau of the department;
    36    (2) have knowledge of data and resources in use by the department; and
    37    (3)  be  responsible for the compliance of the department, its subsid-
    38  iaries and affiliates with the order.
    39    (d) The department and its  subsidiaries  and  affiliates  shall  make
    40  their  publishable  DFS  data available on its website and the open data
    41  website as follows:
    42    (1) the department shall create a catalogue of  publishable  DFS  data
    43  within one hundred eighty days of the effective date of this section;
    44    (2)  the department shall within one hundred eighty days of the effec-
    45  tive date of this section, submit to the legislature and publish on  its
    46  shared  internet  website a schedule for making its publishable DFS data
    47  publicly available. Such schedule shall provide for  updating  the  data
    48  catalogue as appropriate; and
    49    (3) the department shall create schedules for publishing all publisha-
    50  ble DFS data within three years of the effective date of this section.
    51    (e)  The  department  shall  not  be prevented from publishing data in
    52  advance of the dates set in their schedules.
    53    § 4. This act shall take effect immediately.
