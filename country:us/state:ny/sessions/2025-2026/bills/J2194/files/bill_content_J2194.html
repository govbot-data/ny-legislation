Senate Resolution No. 2194
 
BY: Senator MARTINS
 
        CONGRATULATING  James  Condon upon the occasion of
        receiving the distinguished rank of Eagle Scout, the
        most prestigious of Scouting honors, on May 31, 2026
 
  WHEREAS, James Condon, through his diligence and  rigorous  efforts,
has  attained Scouting's highest and most prestigious rank, Eagle Scout;
the Eagle Court of Honor ceremony will be held on Sunday, May 31,  2026,
at Mineola High School, Garden City Park, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS,  James  Condon is a member of Scouts BSA Troop 45, Mineola,
New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, James Condon has  achieved  this  high  honor  through  his
steadfast   commitment   to   the   principles  of  Scouting,  including
leadership, service, citizenship, and personal responsibility; and
 
  WHEREAS, A resident of Williston Park, New York,  James  Condon  was
born on March 1, 2008, and has demonstrated excellence both academically
and within his community; and
 
  WHEREAS, James Condon attended Chaminade High School, Class of 2026,
where   he  distinguished  himself  through  participation  in  numerous
extracurricular  activities,  including  Cross  Country,  Indoor  Track,
Outdoor Track, Speech & Debate, Chorus, and Latin Club; and
 
  WHEREAS,  James  Condon  has  earned recognition as a National Merit
Commended Student, AP Scholar with Distinction, and Scout of  the  Year,
reflecting his academic excellence and leadership within Scouting; and
 
  WHEREAS, As part of the requirements for attaining the rank of Eagle
Scout,  James  Condon completed a meaningful and lasting service project
involving the creation of a sign and custom-built furniture  to  enhance
the courtyard at NYU Langone Medical Center, improving accessibility and
providing   a   welcoming  and  restorative  environment  for  patients,
visitors, and staff alike; and
 
  WHEREAS, James Condon began his Scouting journey with Cub Scout Pack
311 in Williston Park, New York, where he  earned  the  Arrow  of  Light
Award  on  March 28, 2019, before continuing his advancement with Scouts
BSA Troop 45 in Mineola; and
 
  WHEREAS, Through years of perseverance and commitment, James  Condon
advanced  through  the  ranks  of Scout, Tenderfoot, Second Class, First
Class, Star Scout, and Life Scout, ultimately completing his Eagle Scout
project on February 26, 2026, successfully passing his Eagle Scout Board
of Review on April 14, 2026, and earning the rank of Eagle Scout; and
 
  WHEREAS, Throughout his Scouting journey,  James  Condon  served  in
numerous  leadership  roles  including  Scribe, Patrol Leader, Assistant
Senior Patrol Leader, and Junior Assistant Scoutmaster, exemplifying the
ideals of leadership and service central to the Scouting movement; and
 
  WHEREAS, James Condon earned an impressive array  of  merit  badges,
including   required  badges  such  as  Citizenship  in  the  Community,
Citizenship in the Nation, Citizenship  in  the  World,  Citizenship  in
Society,  Communication,  Cooking, Emergency Preparedness, Environmental
Science,  First Aid, Cycling, Personal Management, Personal Fitness, and
Family Life, as well as elective  merit  badges  including  Leatherwork,
Law,  Crime  Prevention, American Heritage, Fingerprinting, Backpacking,
Hiking, and Mammal Studies; and
 
  WHEREAS, James Condon has  demonstrated  exceptional  dedication  to
service,  leadership, and personal excellence, earning the admiration of
his family, friends, fellow Scouts, and community members; and
 
  WHEREAS, With aspirations to pursue a bachelor's degree in  Classics
and  Political  Science  and  a  future  career in government and public
policy, James Condon exemplifies the  values  of  civic  responsibility,
perseverance,  and  leadership  that  define  the  finest  traditions of
Scouting and citizenship; and
 
  WHEREAS, This  Legislative  Body  pauses  in  its  deliberations  to
recognize  that  James Condon is a credit to his family, his school, his
community, his State, and his Nation, and his future is expected  to  be
marked by continued achievement and service; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
congratulate James Condon upon the occasion of  receiving  the  rank  of
Eagle  Scout,  the  highest honor bestowed by the Boy Scouts of America,
and to commend him  for  his  dedication  to  service,  leadership,  and
excellence; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED,  That  a  copy  of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be
transmitted to James Condon.
