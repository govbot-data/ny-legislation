BILL NOS03326

SPONSORASHBY

MLTSPNSR
 
Add §216-a, Art 14 §§400 - 405, Mil L; add §82-a, St Fin L; amd §5205, CPLR; amd §§612 & 658, Tax L; amd §190, Exec L
 
Establishes the "New York's Own combat veterans healthcare choice program act" to establish tax free savings accounts to pay the healthcare costs of combat veterans on active duty during Operation Enduring Freedom or Operation Iraqi Freedom, until covered by the federal government; directs the division of military and naval affairs to establish a registry of Afghanistan and Iraq veterans.

    22    § 400. Program established.  There is hereby established the New  York
    23  combat  veterans  healthcare  choice  program  and such program shall be
    24  known and may be cited as the "New York's Own combat veterans healthcare
    25  choice program".
    26    § 401. Purpose. The purposes of the New York combat  veterans  health-
    27  care  choice  program  are  to  authorize  the establishment of veterans
    28  healthcare choice accounts and provide guidelines for the maintenance of
    29  such accounts to:
    30    1. Enable resident veteran members of the organized  militia  of  this
    31  state who were on active duty in a combat zone, designated by the presi-
    32  dent of the United States during Operation Enduring Freedom or Operation
    33  Iraqi  Freedom,  to obtain healthcare from the provider of their choice,
    34  with funds from a New York combat  veterans  healthcare  choice  account
    35  funded by the state and the service member in accordance with this arti-
    36  cle,  when  after  ninety days of making application to or attempting to
    37  schedule an appointment with the  federal  Veterans  Administration  for
    38  such  care,  the  service  member is unable to secure timely or adequate
    39  healthcare services for injuries and disabilities sustained in the  line
    40  of  active duty in such a combat zone, whenever ordered into such combat
    41  zone for three hundred sixty days or more by the governor, the  command-
    42  ing  general of the New York army national guard, the commanding officer
    43  of the New York air national guard or  the  commanding  officer  of  the
    44  naval militia;
    45    2. Provide funding from the state for New York combat veterans health-
    46  care  choice  accounts  in  an amount of up to five thousand dollars per
    47  calendar year for a period not to exceed three years, when in accordance
    48  with this article, a qualified state resident combat veteran  is  unable
    49  to  secure  healthcare services within ninety days of making application
    50  or scheduling an appointment  to  make  such  an  application  for  such
    51  services from the federal Veterans Administration; and
    52    3.  To  create  a  state  personal  income  tax  deduction for amounts
    53  contributed by qualified resident combat veterans to a New  York  combat
    54  veterans healthcare choice account in an amount not to exceed five thou-
    55  sand dollars per calendar year as provided in this article.
