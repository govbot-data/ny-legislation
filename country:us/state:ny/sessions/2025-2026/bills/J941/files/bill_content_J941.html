Senate Resolution No. 941
 
BY: Senator O'MARA
 
        CONGRATULATING  the GorillaBots Team 5485 upon the
        occasion of capturing First Place at  the  New  York
        Excelsior  FIRST  Tech Challenge Regional Tournament
        and ranking first in the Ochoa Division at the FIRST
        Tech Challenge World Championship in Houston, Texas
 
  WHEREAS, Individual and team  championships  are  vital  in  healthy
competition  among  our  students;  this  Legislative Body commends rare
achievements and pays special  recognition  to  those  who  pursue  such
excellence and become examples for the youth of this great Empire State;
and
 
  WHEREAS,  Competition  helps  prepare  students  for  the  future by
instilling in them the importance of teamwork and developing a sense  of
fair play; and
 
  WHEREAS,  This  Legislative Body is justly proud to congratulate the
GorillaBots Team 5485 upon the occasion of capturing First Place at  the
New York Excelsior FIRST Tech Challenge Regional Tournament; and
 
  WHEREAS,  For  the  first time in five years, the team qualified and
competed in the Robotics FIRST  Tech  Challenge  World  Championship  in
Houston,  Texas,  April 16-19, 2025; the GorillaBots took First Place in
the Ochoa Division and currently rank 26th in the world  (out  of  8,800
teams worldwide); and
 
  WHEREAS,  A  culmination  of months of work, multiple hours per day,
this  prestigious  event  is  such  an  honor  to  the  members  of  the
GorillaBots  Team  5485  which is comprised of students from Horseheads,
Elmira, and Corning, New York; and
 
  WHEREAS, The competition includes qualification matches and alliance
selections; while competing, two teams are paired to form  an  alliance;
one  team's  robot  picks  up blocks and puts them in a basket while the
other team's robot brings blocks to a human who attaches a hook to them;
the robot brings the blocks back to the center of the  floor  and  hangs
the  blocks  on  a  beam, and the alliance with the most points wins the
round; and
 
  WHEREAS, At the Regional Competition, there are eight  qualification
matches,  followed  by  alliance  selection  and  playoff matches, which
determine the teams that participate in the  FIRST  World  Championship;
and
 
  WHEREAS, The GorillaBots Team 5485 prioritizes mentoring new members
to  ensure sufficient hands-on experience; community outreach is another
goal  of  the  team,  spreading  the  concept  of  science,  technology,
engineering, and mathematics (STEM); and
 
  WHEREAS,  The  dedicated  students of the GorillaBots Team 5485 work
with the Science & Discovery  Center  in  Corning,  volunteer  at  their
summer  camps,  mentor  local  middle  school  robotics teams, and build
science kits and distribute  them  at  various  community  events;  they
 
deserve  admiration  for  their  contributions of more than 800 hours to
their communities within New York State; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
congratulate the members of the  GorillaBots  Team  5485:  Jacob  Chung,
Lydia  Chung,  Andrew  Clark,  Kaeden  Martinez, Aidan McGroarty, Niyati
Sharma, Aditya Aiyar, Miroslav Goral, Sriram Aravelli, Frank Chen,  Mark
Mendez, Jennifer Yu, Matthew Han, and Caylee Hinman upon the occasion of
capturing  First  Place  at  the New York Excelsior FIRST Tech Challenge
Regional Tournament; and be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That copies of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted  to  the  members of the GorillaBots Team 5485; and Coaches,
Lauren Chung, Michael Nishimoto, and Mark Joiner.
