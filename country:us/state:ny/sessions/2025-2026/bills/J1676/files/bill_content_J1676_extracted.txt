and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and in full accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to congratulate the St. John's Episcopal Church upon the occasion of its 200th Anniversary; and WHEREAS, On August 21, 1826, several inhabitants of the Town of Sodus met for the purpose of establishing, organizing, and incorporating a Protestant Episcopal Church, marking the beginning of the recorded history of St. John's Episcopal Church; and WHEREAS, The cornerstone of the church was laid on September 26, 1826, and funds to erect the building were raised through the sale of pews; constructed at a cost of $3,000, the church was consecrated on September 8, 1834, and its exterior today remains much as it did at that time; and WHEREAS, The church building, located on Main Street, is the oldest structure in Sodus, New York, that is still used for its original purpose; and WHEREAS, The church has expanded and enhanced its facilities over the years, including the construction of rectories, the addition of a parish hall in 1959, and the installation of accessibility improvements to better serve parishioners and visitors; and WHEREAS, St. John's Episcopal Church has long opened its doors to community organizations and outreach initiatives, providing space for civic groups, youth organizations, educational programs, and charitable activities; and WHEREAS, In 2001, the church commemorated its 175th Anniversary, and in 2026 it will celebrate 200 years since its founding, marking two centuries of continuous worship and service in the Sodus community; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to congratulate the St. John's Episcopal Church upon the occasion of its 200th Anniversary; and be it further RESOLVED, That this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to St. John's Episcopal Church, Sodus, New York.
