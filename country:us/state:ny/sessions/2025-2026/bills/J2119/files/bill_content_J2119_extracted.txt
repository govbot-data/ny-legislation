Volunteer Ski Patroller; and WHEREAS, Georgia Jones was a New York State EMT, a member of the ski patrol for the 1980 Olympics, served on the board of the Lake Placid Olympic Museum for 4 years, is a current Board member of the Lake Placid/North Elba Historical Society, and has been a Board member, President and currently a member of the Advisory Board of the Lake Placid Sinfonietta which has benefited from her efforts for over 30 years; and WHEREAS, A member of the Dark Skies Committee, a sub-committee of the Lake Placid/North Elba Development Commission, for three years, Georgia Jones has been promoting Dark Skies for several years; she also served on the Lake Placid/North Elba Planning Board, the Placid Lake Foundation and the North Elba Land Trust Board; and WHEREAS, Georgia Jones's professionalism, civic duty, and community activities have benefited countless individuals and families throughout the years of her dedicated community service and her efforts have left an indelible mark that will be a positive influence for generations to come; and WHEREAS, Volunteerism promotes family and community solidarity; most importantly, people who volunteer their time in civic and charitable endeavors inspire others to the call of volunteerism; and WHEREAS, Georgia Jones's continued passion for volunteering stands as a shining example of dedication and commitment to community and service; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to commend Georgia Jones, upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of the 2026 Lake Placid Community Day Distinguished Adult Volunteer of the Year Award; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Georgia Jones.
