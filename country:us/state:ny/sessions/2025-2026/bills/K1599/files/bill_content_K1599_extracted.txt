and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and fully in accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to commemorate the 120th Anniversary of the West Winfield Fire Department, also known as the Henry Hiteman Engine and Hose Company, to be celebrated on the occasion of its founding on April 6, 1906; and WHEREAS, For 120 years, the members of the West Winfield Fire Department have faithfully answered the call of duty, providing fire protection, emergency response, and lifesaving services to the residents of the Village of West Winfield, the Town of Winfield, and neighboring communities throughout the region; and WHEREAS, The West Winfield Fire Department was established in 1906 following the vision and leadership of local residents who recognized the need for an organized fire protection service after witnessing the devastating effects of a local business fire; and WHEREAS, Through the determination of community members and the leadership of its first Chief, Norman Churchill, the department was successfully organized and has remained a vital institution within the community ever since; and WHEREAS, A generous contribution from local businessman Henry Hiteman provided the department's first significant firefighting equipment, inspiring the organization's enduring name, the Henry Hiteman Engine and Hose Company; and WHEREAS, Through fundraising efforts, volunteer commitment, and community support, the department constructed its first firehouse in 1910, expanded its facilities in 1970, and in 1992 opened the modern fire station that continues to serve as its headquarters today; and WHEREAS, Over the course of its distinguished history, the West Winfield Fire Department has evolved from hand-drawn apparatus to a modern fleet of firefighting, rescue, emergency medical, and support vehicles, ensuring that its members are equipped to meet the challenges of an ever-changing emergency response environment; and WHEREAS, Throughout its 120 years of service, generations of volunteer firefighters, emergency responders, officers, and community leaders have dedicated countless hours to protecting lives and property, often placing themselves in harm's way for the benefit of others; and WHEREAS, The members of the West Winfield Fire Department have demonstrated an unwavering commitment to professionalism, training, public service, and community involvement, earning the respect and gratitude of the residents they serve; and WHEREAS, Today, under the leadership of Chief Al Moxham Jr. and the department's dedicated officers and members, the West Winfield Fire Department continues the proud traditions established by its founders while preparing for the challenges of the future; and WHEREAS, The 120th Anniversary of the West Winfield Fire Department provides a fitting opportunity to recognize the countless men and women who have served the department since 1906 and to celebrate a legacy of courage, sacrifice, and service that has strengthened the community for generations; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to commemorate the 120th Anniversary of the West Winfield Fire Department and the Henry Hiteman Engine and Hose Company, honoring its remarkable history and celebrating 120 years of dedicated service to the people of the Village of West Winfield, the Town of Winfield, and the State of New York; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the officers and members of the West Winfield Fire Department in recognition of this historic milestone and their extraordinary service to the community.
