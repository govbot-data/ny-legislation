        AN ACT establishing a temporary task force on variable supplements fund
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section 1. Legislative intent. The  variable  supplements  fund  (VSF)
     2  program was enacted in 1970 to provide supplementation to the retirement
     3  allowance  of  New  York city police and fire retirement system members.
     4  Since the enactment of the legislation  establishing  the  VSF  program,
     5  much controversy has been generated by the exclusion of disability reti-
     6  rees  from  eligibility  for the benefit. Although a small number of New
     7  York city disability retirees were permitted to receive VSF supplementa-
     8  tion benefits as a result of technical correction  legislation  approved
     9  during  previous  years, the lack of clarity in defining VSF eligibility
    10  and the continued exclusion of most disabled firefighters  from  receipt
    11  of  this  benefit has been a cause of much frustration and confusion for
    12  disability pensioners.
    13    While it is recognized that any amendment  to  the  VSF  will  require
    14  careful  study  and  consideration  of the issues involved, the legisla-
    15  ture's interest in ensuring fairness  and  equity  demand  such  review.
    16  Because  the legislature does not accept this responsibility lightly, it
    17  hereby declares that the establishment of a temporary task force, to  be
    18  known  as  the temporary task force on the variable supplements fund, is
    19  both warranted and required.
    20    § 2. 1. A temporary task force, to be  known  as  the  temporary  task
    21  force on the variable supplements fund, is hereby created to examine and
    22  investigate  such  issues  as are related to the exclusion of disability
    23  pensioners from the variable supplements fund.
    24    2. The temporary task force shall have the power to:
    25    (a) conduct public hearings in furtherance of the performance  of  its
    26  duties under this act;

     1    (b)  issue subpoenas for and to compel attendance of witnesses and the
     2  production of books, records, documents and other relevant evidence; and
     3    (c)  to  recommend  legislation  consistent  with the purposes of this
     4  section.
     5    3. The task  force  shall  consist  of  eleven  members  appointed  as
     6  follows:
     7    (a) two members who shall be appointed by the governor;
     8    (b)  two  members who shall be appointed by the temporary president of
     9  the senate;
    10    (c) two members who shall be appointed by the speaker of the assembly;
    11    (d) one member who shall be appointed by the minority  leader  of  the
    12  senate;
    13    (e)  one  member  who shall be appointed by the minority leader of the
    14  assembly;
    15    (f) one member who shall be appointed by the mayor of the city of  New
    16  York;
    17    (g) one member who shall be appointed by the state comptroller; and
    18    (h)  one member who shall represent the interest of the affected disa-
    19  bility retirees and who shall be appointed by the chair.
    20    From among such eleven  members,  the  governor  shall  designate  the
    21  chair.   In addition to such eleven voting members, the president of the
    22  uniformed firefighters association of greater New York  or  such  presi-
    23  dent's  designee,  the comptroller of the city of New York or such comp-
    24  troller's designee, a member of the board of  trustees  of  the  transit
    25  police  officer's variable supplements fund or such member's designee, a
    26  member of the board of trustees of the housing police officer's variable
    27  supplements fund or such member's designee, a member  of  the  board  of
    28  trustees  of  the transit police superior officers' variable supplements
    29  fund or such member's designee, a member of the board of trustees of the
    30  housing police superior officers'  variable  supplements  fund  or  such
    31  member's  designee,  and  a  member  of  the  board  of  trustees of the
    32  correction officers' variable supplements fund or such member's designee
    33  shall serve as non-voting members. Six voting members of  the  temporary
    34  task  force  shall,  at  all  times,  constitute a quorum. Members shall
    35  receive no compensation for their services; members (including  non-vot-
    36  ing  members) who are not governmental employees shall be reimbursed for
    37  their reasonable expenses  incurred  during  the  performance  of  their
    38  duties.    Notwithstanding  any  inconsistent  provision of any general,
    39  special or local law, no officer or employee of  the  state  or  of  any
    40  political  subdivision  thereof shall forfeit such officer or employee's
    41  office or employment by reason  of  acceptance  of  appointment  to  the
    42  temporary task force.
    43    4.  Within  one  year of the effective date of this act, the temporary
    44  task force shall issue a report containing such findings and recommenda-
    45  tions regarding the eligibility of disability  pensioners  of  New  York
    46  city to receive supplemental payments from the variable supplements fund
    47  and any other matters relating thereto. Such report shall be transmitted
    48  to  the governor, the comptroller, the chair of the Senate civil service
    49  and pensions committee  and  the  chair  of  the  assembly  governmental
    50  employees committee.
    51    § 3. This act shall take effect immediately.
