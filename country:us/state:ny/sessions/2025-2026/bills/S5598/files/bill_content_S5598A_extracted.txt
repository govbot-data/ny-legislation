40 the time the debt became delinquent or defaulted, even if that person or
41 entity did not originate the private education loan, and where such a
42 debt has not subsequently been sold, transferred or assigned; or
43 (c) a person or entity that purchased a delinquent or defaulted
44 private education loan debt for collection purposes, whether it collects
45 the debt itself, hires a third party for collection, or hires an attor-
46 ney for collection litigation.

3 § 1201. Applicability. 1. Any person or entity that enters into a
4 contract or subcontract with a private education lender or servicer to
5 perform the servicing of a private education loan must fulfill the obli-
6 gations of the private education lender under this article.
7 2. Any private education lender as described in subdivision two of
8 section twelve hundred of this article be jointly and severally liable
9 for the actions of the entity or person in fulfilling the obligations of
10 the private educational lender or servicer under this article.
11 § 1202. Exempt organizations. The following shall be exempt from the
12 provisions of this article only to the extent that state regulation is
13 preempted by federal law:
14 1. Any banking organization, foreign banking corporation, national
15 bank, federal savings association, federal credit union, or any bank,
16 trust company, savings bank, savings and loan association, or credit
17 union organized under the laws of any other state; and
18 2. Any subsidiary of such entities set forth in subdivision one of
19 this section.
20 § 1203. Provisions applicable to cosigners. 1. (a) Prior to the origi-
21 nation of a private education loan, the private education lender shall
22 provide to all cosigner applicants information about the rights and
23 responsibilities of the cosigner of the loan, including:
24 (i) information about how the private education lender will furnish
25 information about the cosigner's private education loan obligation to
26 credit reporting agencies;
27 (ii) information about how the cosigner will be notified if the
28 private education loan becomes delinquent, including how the cosigner
29 can cure the delinquency in order to avoid negative credit furnishing
30 and loss of cosigner release eligibility; and
31 (iii) information about eligibility for release of the cosigner's
32 obligation on the private education loan, including number of on-time
33 payments and any other criteria required to approve the release of
34 cosigner from the loan obligation.
35 (b) Lenders shall send borrowers and cosigners annual written notices
36 containing information about cosigner release, including criteria the
37 lender requires to approve the release of cosigner from the loan obli-
38 gation and the process for applying for cosigner release.
39 (c) Once the borrower has met the applicable consecutive on-time
40 payment requirement to be eligible for cosigner release, the lender
41 shall send the borrower and cosigner a written notification by U.S. mail
42 and by electronic mail, where a borrower has elected to receive elec-
43 tronic communications from the lender, informing the borrower and cosig-
44 ner that such person has met the applicable consecutive, on-time
45 payments requirement to be eligible for cosigner release. The notifica-
46 tion shall also include information about any additional criteria to
47 qualify for cosigner release, and the procedure to apply for cosigner
48 release.
49 (d) Lenders shall provide written notice within fifteen days to any
50 borrower who applies for cosigner release, but whose application is
51 incomplete. The written notice must include a description of the infor-
52 mation needed to consider the application complete and the date by which
53 the applicant should furnish the missing information.
54 (e) After a borrower submits a complete application for cosigner
55 release, within thirty days, the lender shall send the borrower and
56 cosigner a written notice that informs the borrower and cosigner whether

12 § 1204. Prohibition on acceleration of payments on private education
13 loans. 1. Except as provided in subdivision two of this section, a
14 private education loan executed after the effective date of this article
15 may not include a provision that permits the private educational lender
16 to accelerate, in whole or in part, payments on the private education
17 loan.
18 2. A private education loan may include a provision that permits
19 acceleration of the loan in cases of payment default.
20 3. A lender shall not place any loan or account into default or accel-
21 erate a loan for any reason, other than for failure to pay.
22 4. (a) In the event of the death of a cosigner, a lender shall not
23 attempt to collect against the cosigner's estate, other than for failure
24 to pay.
25 (b) Upon receiving notification of the death or bankruptcy of a cosig-
26 ner, when the loan is not more than sixty days delinquent at the time of
27 the notification, a lender shall not change any terms or benefits under
28 the promissory note, repayment schedule, repayment terms, or monthly
29 payment amount or any other provision associated with the loan.
30 (c) A lender shall not place any loan or account into default or
31 accelerate a loan for any reason, other than for failure to pay.
32 § 1205. Required communications from creditors and debt collectors.
33 In addition to any other information required under applicable federal
34 or state law, a creditor or debt collector shall provide, in writing, in
35 the first debt collection communication with the private education loan
36 borrower or cosigner, or within five days thereafter, and at any other
37 time the borrower or cosigner requests such documentation:
38 1. The name of the current owner of the private education loan debt;
39 2. The original creditor's name at the time of origination and, if
40 different, at the time of sale of the loan, if applicable;
41 3. The original creditor's account number used to identify the private
42 education loan debt at the time of sale, if applicable;
43 4. The total outstanding amount owed at the time of default or the
44 amount due to bring the loan current if the loan is delinquent, but not
45 yet in default;
46 5. A schedule of all transactions credited or debited to the private
47 education loan account;
48 6. A copy of all pages of the contract, application or other documents
49 stating all terms and conditions applicable to the private education
50 loan and evidencing the private education loan borrower's or cosigner's
51 liability for the private education loan; and
52 7. A clear and conspicuous statement disclosing that the borrower or
53 cosigner has a right to request all information possessed by the credi-
54 tor related to the private education loan debt, including, but not
55 limited to the information included in section twelve hundred six of
56 this article.

12 § 1207. Enforcement. 1. All private education lenders, creditors and
13 debt collectors shall comply with the provisions of this article.
14 2. No private education lenders, creditors or debt collectors shall
15 engage in unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices.
16 3. Any borrower or cosigner who suffers damage as a result of the
17 failure of a private education lender, creditor, or debt collector
18 covered by the provisions of this article may bring an action on their
19 own behalf and on behalf of a similarly situated class of consumers
20 against that person to recover or obtain any of the following:
21 (a) actual damages, but in no case shall the total award of damages be
22 less than five hundred dollars per person, per violation of this
23 section;
24 (b) punitive damages;
25 (c) correction of any inaccurate, negative reporting by the lender,
26 creditor, or debt collector to any credit reporting agency;
27 (d) injunctive relief; or
28 (e) any other relief that the court deems proper.
29 4. In the case of any successful action to enforce the foregoing
30 liability, a private education lender, creditor, or debt collector shall
31 be liable for the costs of the action, together with reasonable attor-
32 neys' fees as determined by the court.
33 5. The attorney general may bring an action in the name of the people
34 of the state to restrain or prevent any violation of this article or any
35 continuance of any such violation and to obtain restitution of any
36 moneys or property obtained directly or indirectly by any such
37 violation, as well as reasonable attorneys' fees.
38 6. Nothing in this article shall limit any statutory or common law
39 right of any person to bring any action in any court for any act, or the
40 right of the state to punish any person for any violation of any law.
