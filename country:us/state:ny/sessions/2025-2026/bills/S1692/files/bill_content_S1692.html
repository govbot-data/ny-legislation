S01692 Summary:

BILL NOS01692B
 
SAME ASSAME AS A02592-B
 
SPONSORRAMOS
 
COSPNSRBAILEY, BROUK, MYRIE, SANDERS
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Amd §26, Cor L
 
Relates to commissaries and canteens at correctional institutions; provides that items for sale at a commissary or canteen shall be sold for no more than a certain percentage over the purchase price; provides that the department of corrections and community supervisions shall publish on its website the price list for all commissary items for sale at each institution as of June 1, 2026 and on an annual basis thereafter.

        AN ACT to amend the correction  law,  in  relation  to  commissaries  or
          canteens in correctional institutions
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Section 26 of the correction law, as amended by chapter 322
     2  of the laws of 2021, is amended to read as follows:
     3    § 26. [Establishment of  commissaries]  Commissaries  or  canteens  in
     4  correctional institutions. 1. The commissioner may authorize the head of
     5  any institution in the department to establish a commissary or a canteen
     6  in such institution for the use and benefit of incarcerated individuals.
     7  Items  sold  at  the commissary or canteen shall be sold at no more than
     8  three percent over the purchase price for  such  items.  The  department
     9  shall maximize discounts procured by bulk purchasing commissary items or
    10  through  other  contracting  opportunities  that reduce the cost of such
    11  items, and shall not receive commissions, revenue,  or  other  financial
    12  incentives in any contract with a seller, supplier, or vendor of commis-
    13  sary  items.  The  moneys  received  by  the  head of the institution as
    14  profits from the sales of the commissary or canteen shall  be  deposited
    15  in a special fund to be known as the commissary or canteen fund and such
    16  funds  shall be used for the general purposes of the institution subject
    17  to the provisions of section fifty-three of the state finance law.
