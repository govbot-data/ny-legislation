        Introduced by M. of A. DiPIETRO -- read once and referred to the Commit-

        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  environmental  conservation law, in relation to
          authorizing free fishing, hunting, and trapping licenses for qualified
          hunting instructors

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  Subdivision  2  of  section  11-0715 of the environmental
     2  conservation law, as amended by section 4 of part R of chapter 58 of the
     3  laws of 2013, is amended to read as follows:
     4    2. (a) A member of the Shinnecock tribe or the Poospatuck tribe  or  a
     5  member  of the six nations, residing on any reservation wholly or partly
     6  within the state, is entitled  to  receive  free  of  charge  a  fishing
     7  license,  a  hunting  license,  a  muzzle-loading  privilege, a trapping
     8  license, and a bowhunting privilege[;].
     9    [a] (b) A resident of the state who is a member of the  United  States
    10  armed forces in active service who is not stationed within the state and
    11  has  not  been  herein  longer than thirty days on leave or furlough, is
    12  entitled to receive free of charge a fishing license, a hunting license,
    13  and a trapping license[;].
    14    [a] (c) A resident of the state who is an active member of the  organ-
    15  ized  militia  of the state of New York as defined by section one of the
    16  military law, or the reserve components  of  the  armed  forces  of  the
    17  United  States, and excluding members of the inactive national guard and
    18  individual ready reserve, is entitled to receive free of charge a  fish-
    19  ing license, a hunting license, and a trapping license[; and].
    20    [a]  (d)  A  resident  who  is  blind is entitled to receive a fishing
    21  license free of charge. For the purposes of this subdivision a person is
    22  blind only if either: [(a) his or her] (i) such person's central  visual
    23  acuity  does not exceed 20/200 in the better eye with correcting lenses,
    24  or [(b) his or her] (ii) such person's visual  acuity  is  greater  than
 
         EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets
                              [ ] is old law to be omitted.

     1  20/200  but  is  accompanied by a limitation of the field of vision such
     2  that the widest diameter of the visual field subtends an angle no great-
     3  er than 20 degrees.
     4    (e)  A  resident  in the state for a period of thirty days immediately
     5  prior to the date of application who has attained the age of seventy  is
     6  entitled to receive a fishing license, a trapping license, and a hunting
     7  license, at a cost of five dollars for each license.
     8    (f)  A  resident  in the state for a period of thirty days immediately
     9  prior to the date of application who has attained the age of seventy  is
    10  entitled  to receive free of charge a bowhunting privilege and a muzzle-
    11  loading privilege.
    12    (g) A resident of the state who is duly qualified  and  designated  by
    13  the  department to give instruction and issue certification in responsi-
    14  ble hunting, responsible bowhunting or  responsible  trapping  practices
    15  required  for  licensure  under section 11-0713 of this article, and who
    16  provides such instruction without compensation, is entitled  to  receive
    17  free  of  charge  a  fishing  license, a hunting license, and a trapping
    18  license.
    19    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
