SOLAGES -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Local Governments AN ACT to amend the general municipal law, in relation to providing public input on local fire protection in New York state The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The general municipal law is amended by adding a new 2 section 777-a to read as follows: 3 § 777-a. Referendum resolution for dissolution of fire districts. 1. 4 If a dissolution plan calls for the dissolution of a fire district, then 5 contemporaneous with the final approval of the dissolution plan pursuant 6 to subdivision three of section seven hundred seventy-six of this title, 7 the governing body of the fire district shall enact a resolution calling 8 for a referendum on the proposed dissolution by the electors in the fire 9 district. 10 2. The resolution calling for the referendum on the proposed dissol- 11 ution shall: 12 (a) provide: (i) the name of the fire district to be dissolved; and 13 (ii) the date for the referendum, in accordance with subdivision one of 14 section seven hundred eighty of this title; 15 (b) state the substance of the question to be submitted to the elec- 16 tors; and 17 (c) set forth such other matters as may be necessary to call, provide 18 for and give notice of the referendum and to provide for the conduct 19 thereof and the canvass of the returns thereupon. 20 3. The resolution calling for the referendum on the proposed dissol- 21 ution shall have attached to it the final approved version of the 22 dissolution plan. 23 4. A fire district dissolved pursuant to a dissolution plan shall 24 continue to be governed as before dissolution until the effective date 25 of the dissolution specified in the dissolution plan; provided, however, 26 that no dissolution plan for a fire district shall take effect unless 27 approved by a majority of electors of the fire district at a referendum 28 called through a resolution. Notice of the referendum shall be given to the electors of the fire 9 district to be dissolved by publication in a newspaper having a general 10 circulation within the boundaries of the entity at least once a week for 11 four consecutive weeks immediately prior to the referendum. The notice 12 shall include, but not be limited to: 13 (a) a summary of the contents of the resolution and dissolution plan 14 or petition for dissolution, as the case may be; 15 (b) a statement as to where may be examined copies of the resolution 16 and dissolution plan or petition for dissolution, as the case may be; 17 (c) the name of the fire district to be dissolved and a statement 18 fully describing its territory; 19 (d) the time and place or places at which the referendum will be held; 20 and 21 (e) such other matters as may be necessary to call, provide for and 22 give notice of the referendum and to provide for the conduct thereof and 23 the canvass of the returns thereupon. 24 7. The referendum question placed before the electors of the fire 25 district to be dissolved shall be in a form reading substantially as 26 follows: 27 "Shall (insert type and name of fire district) be dissolved? 28 YES ____ 29 NO ____" 30 8. In any referendum held pursuant to this title, the fire district to 31 be dissolved shall bear the costs associated with the conduct of such 32 referendum. 33 9. In any referendum held pursuant to this title, and except as other- 34 wise specified herein, the referendum shall be conducted in the same 35 manner as other municipal elections or referendums for the local govern- 36 ment entity affected by the proposed dissolution. 37 § 2.
