ANDERSON -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Governmental Operations -- committee discharged, bill amended, ordered reprinted as amended and recommitted to said committee -- committee discharged, bill amended, ordered reprinted as amended and recommitted to said committee AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to enacting the "Census Enumerator Navigation Safety for Unimpeded Surveying Act" The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Census 2 Enumerator Navigation Safety for Unimpeded Surveying Act". 3 § 2. The executive law is amended by adding a new section 170-k to 4 read as follows: 5 § 170-k. Census enumerator access. 1. During an official enumeration 6 period established by the United States Census Bureau, enumerators who 7 present valid federal-issued or state-issued credentials shall be 8 afforded reasonable access to common areas of multi-unit residential 9 buildings for the purpose of conducting authorized census activities. 10 Such access shall be granted during the days and hours enumerators are 11 authorized to make in-person inquiries for the purposes of collecting 12 census information. 13 2. (a) The department of state shall prepare and issue guidance to 14 building owners, managing agents, and building staff regarding recogni- 15 tion of valid enumerator credentials. Such guidance shall also be made 16 available to the public on the website of the department of state and in 17 written form upon request. 18 (b) To the extent not in violation of federal law, the department of 19 state shall develop a voluntary state-issued verification resource or 20 training materials to assist building staff in identifying authorized 21 enumerators and reducing confusion during enumeration periods. 22 3. The attorney general shall bring an action in a court of competent 23 jurisdiction to enforce the provisions of this section and to obtain 24 injunctive relief, declaratory relief, or any other appropriate equita- 25 ble relief necessary to ensure compliance.
