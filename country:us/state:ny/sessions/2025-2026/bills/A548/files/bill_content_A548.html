A00548 Summary:

BILL NOA00548
 
SAME ASNo Same As
 
SPONSORPaulin
 
COSPNSRBenedetto
 
MLTSPNSR
 
 
Provides for accountability in state assessments by creating a regents review board to audit assessments used to determine grade promotion, graduation and adequate yearly progress by requiring a written report, implementation of an auditing and review of pilot and/or field testing of the standardized tests used to ensure validity, reliability, alignment to standards and appropriateness of use.

        AN ACT to require annual reports and create a regents  review  board  to
          audit  assessments  used  to  determine grade promotion, graduation or
          adequate yearly progress for validity, reliability, alignment to stan-
          dards and appropriateness of use
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.    Reporting requirements. 1. (a) Not later than September
     2  fifteenth of each year, the commissioner of education  shall  submit  to
     3  the  state  board  of regents, the temporary president of the senate and
     4  the speaker of the assembly:
     5    (i) A written report  that  conclusively  demonstrates  the  validity,
     6  reliability,  alignment  to  the state learning standards established by
     7  the department of education (learning standards) and appropriateness  of
     8  use  for  its intended purposes for each assessment (noting each instru-
     9  ment and the dates of usage) used by  the  department  of  education  to
    10  determine  a  student's eligibility for a high school diploma, promotion
    11  to grade, or measurement of adequate yearly  progress  (AYP)  under  the
    12  federal  No Child Left Behind Act of a school or district in the preced-
    13  ing school year.   Demonstration of  assessment  validity,  reliability,
    14  alignment  to learning standards and appropriateness of use must be made
    15  in accordance with applicable  professional  assessment  guidelines  and
    16  standards  of  the American Educational Research Association (AERA), the
    17  American Psychological Association (APA) and  the  National  Council  on
    18  Measurement  in  Education  (NCME)  (collectively and as may be modified
    19  from time to time, the joint standards), including pedagogic and curric-
    20  ular, as well as psychometric, standards.
    21    (ii) A technical manual for each assessment used by the department  of
    22  education  and  used  to  determine  a  student's eligibility for a high

    24    §  2.  Audit requirements. The state board of regents shall appoint an
    25  independent panel of experts  on  curricular  assessments  qualified  to
    26  determine  whether  or not an assessment meets professional standards of
    27  the measurement industry and scholarly associations for validity,  reli-
    28  ability, alignment to learning standards and appropriateness of use. The
    29  panel  shall be known as the regents review board, hereafter referred to
    30  as the board.  The board shall consist of five members. The  members  of
    31  the  board  will  be  members of the AERA or the APA. Each member of the
    32  board will be a recognized expert in the field of testing  psychometrics
    33  or  curriculum evaluation and will have at least ten years experience in
    34  evaluating validity, reliability, alignment with learning standards  and
    35  appropriateness  of  use  of  tests  at the primary and secondary school
    36  levels.
    37    1. The board shall review each assessment used by  the  department  of
    38  education  to determine a student's eligibility for a high school diplo-
    39  ma, promotion to grade, or measurement of AYP of a school or district to
    40  determine whether the assessment meets the joint standards  for  assess-
    41  ment  validity,  reliability, alignment to learning standards and appro-
    42  priateness of use. The board  shall  also  review  the  commissioner  of
    43  education's annual report and supporting materials concerning the valid-
    44  ity, reliability, alignment to learning standards and appropriateness of
    45  use of the assessments, and the technical manual of each assessment. The
    46  board  shall  have complete access to all documents of the department of
    47  education relating to the assessments reviewed by the  board,  including
    48  pilot  and/or field test results for such assessments and any changes to
    49  materials or practices relating to such  assessments  implemented  as  a
    50  result  of  such  pilot and/or field tests. The board shall maintain the
    51  confidentiality of all documents deemed to contain proprietary  informa-
    52  tion.
    53    2.  The  board  shall determine whether or not each assessment used by
    54  the department of education for graduation, promotion  or  determination
    55  of AYP is valid, reliable, aligned to the learning standards, and appro-
    56  priate for use. The board shall submit, not later than October fifteenth

    25    § 3. Pilot testing. 1. No later than sixty days prior to  the  depart-
    26  ment of education's scheduled administration of any assessment to deter-
    27  mine  a  student's  eligibility  for a high school diploma, promotion to
    28  grade, or measurement of AYP of a school or district  in  the  preceding
    29  school  year,  the  commissioner of education shall submit to the board,
    30  the state board of regents and the temporary president of the senate and
    31  the speaker of the assembly the results of all pilot and/or field  tests
    32  conducted  for  that  assessment  and  make  available  to the board all
    33  records relating to such pilot and/or field  tests  and  results.  Pilot
    34  and/or  field  tests  are  assessments administered by the department of
    35  education that are not being used for decisions of graduation, promotion
    36  to grade, or measurement of AYP but contain  individual  questions  that
    37  are to be used on assessments to determine a student's eligibility for a
    38  high  school  diploma,  promotion  to  grade, or measurement of AYP. For
    39  purposes of this section, results of  pilot  and/or  field  tests  shall
    40  include,  but not be limited to, analysis of answers to questions on the
    41  administered pilot  and/or  field  test  based  on  percentage  answered
    42  correctly,  breakdown of results according to race and gender, an expla-
    43  nation of how the pilot and/or  field  test  was  administered,  and  an
    44  explanation  of  the learning standard a question purports to test.  The
    45  failure to submit and identify pilot and/or field  test  results  or  to
    46  provide  access  to  records  as  required  under this subdivision shall
    47  preclude the department of education from  using  or  administering  the
    48  assessment.
    49    2. Where the board has received from the commissioner of education the
    50  results  of  the  pilot  and/or field test for an assessment as required
    51  under subdivision one of this section, the board shall, not  later  than
    52  thirty  days  prior  to the department of education's scheduled adminis-
    53  tration of such assessment, deliver to the state board  of  regents  and
    54  the  temporary president of the senate and the speaker of the assembly a
    55  written report evaluating the pilot and/or field tests and  the  results
