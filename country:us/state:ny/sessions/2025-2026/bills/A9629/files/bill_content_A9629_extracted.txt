The real property law is amended by adding a new section 467 to 28 read as follows: 29 § 467. Right to inspection. 1. Notwithstanding any law to the contra- 30 ry, except as otherwise provided in this section, a buyer of a home 31 shall have the right to have such home inspected by a home inspector 32 licensed under article twelve-B of this chapter of their choice, within 33 ten business days, not to exceed fourteen calendar days, beginning on 34 the day after the buyer's receipt of a purchase agreement signed by both 35 parties, or longer as the seller and buyer may agree in writing, of 36 receipt by the buyer of a purchase agreement for sale of a seller's 37 home, signed and dated in agreement by both the buyer and seller. Noth- 38 ing in this section shall be construed to prohibit or discourage a buyer 39 from obtaining an inspection or other evaluation of the home prior to 40 execution of a purchase agreement; provided, however, that no seller or 41 agent thereof may condition acceptance of an offer upon waiver, limita- 42 tion, or restriction of the rights established under this section. 43 2. (a) No seller of a home, or agent thereof, shall condition the 44 acceptance of a purchase offer on the buyer's agreement to waive, limit, 45 restrict or otherwise forego the buyer's right to have such home 46 inspected except when the sale of the home is to occur at an auction. 47 (b) No seller shall accept an offer to purchase from any buyer or 48 agent thereof who, in advance of the seller's acceptance of the offer, 49 informs the seller either directly or indirectly that the buyer intends 50 to waive in whole or in part the buyer's right to inspection; provided, 51 however, that the seller may accept such an offer without violating this 52 section if the buyer is: (i) a relative of the seller; or (ii) the 53 former spouse of the seller and the sale of the structure or unit is 54 being made pursuant to a judgment or order. 55 (c) (i) The department of state shall develop a form explaining the 56 inspection right under this section. The following shall be the right to inspection statement: 29 "New York State Right to Home Inspection Advisory: 30 New York follows the rule of caveat emptor, meaning buyers are respon- 31 sible for evaluating the condition of a home before purchase. Under Real 32 Property Law section 467, a buyer has the right to have the home 33 inspected by a licensed home inspector, at the buyer's expense, within 34 the inspection period.
