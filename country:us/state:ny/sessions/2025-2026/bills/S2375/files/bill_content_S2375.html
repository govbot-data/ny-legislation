SPONSORMATTERA

Add Art 19-A §§1115 - 1115-f, Priv Hous Fin L
 
Creates a veteran's home and land ownership loan program to provide loans to veterans to purchase homes or unimproved land suitable for building; requires such loans to be secured by a second mortgage.

        AN ACT to amend the private housing finance law, in relation to creating
          a veteran's home and land ownership loan program

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The private housing finance law is amended by adding a  new
     2  article 19-A to read as follows:
     3                                ARTICLE XIX-A
     4                   VETERAN'S HOME AND LAND OWNERSHIP LOAN
     5  Section 1115. Legislative purpose.
     6          1115-a. Definitions.
     7          1115-b. Creation of the "veteran's home and land ownership
     8                    loan fund".
     9          1115-c. Purpose and terms of loan.
    10          1115-d. Loan repayment.
    11          1115-e. Issuance of bonds.
    12          1115-f. Adoption of regulations.
    13    § 1115. Legislative purpose. It is hereby found and declared that many
    14  New  York residents have served our nation honorably in the armed forces
    15  of the United States, and that many such  New  Yorkers  have  sacrificed
    16  considerably  during their service. It is in the public interest both to
    17  demonstrate our appreciation for that service by providing state  finan-
    18  cial  assistance  to such veterans; in order to encourage home ownership
    19  and the rehabilitation of deteriorating housing,  stabilize  communities
    20  and improve the local tax base.
    21    § 1115-a. Definitions. For the purposes of this article, the following
    22  terms shall have the following meanings:
    23    1.  "Person" means an honorably discharged veteran of the Armed Forces
    24  of the United States, or their surviving unmarried spouse.

     5  ary  medal, and who was discharged or released therefrom under honorable
     6  conditions.

    10  expeditionary medal for participation in operations in Lebanon from June

    16    (b)  The  individual  served on active duty for ninety days or more in
    17  the armed forces of the United States during any one  of  the  following
    18  wars or hostilities:
    19    (i)  in  World  War  I  from  the sixth day of April, nineteen hundred

    23  hundred forty-one to the thirty-first day of December, nineteen  hundred

    25    (iii)  in  the Korean hostilities from the twenty-seventh day of June,
    26  nineteen hundred fifty to the  thirty-first  day  of  January,  nineteen

    28    (iv)  in  the Vietnam conflict from the twenty-second day of December,
    29  nineteen hundred sixty-one to the seventh day of May,  nineteen  hundred
    30  seventy-five; or
    31    (v)  in the Persian Gulf conflict from the second day of August, nine-
    32  teen hundred ninety to the end of such conflict.
    33    4. "Agency" means the New York state housing finance agency created by
    34  section forty-three of this chapter.
    35    § 1115-b. Creation of the "veteran's  home  and  land  ownership  loan
    36  fund".  There is hereby established a "veteran's home and land ownership
    37  loan  fund".  Such  fund  shall be used to make loans authorized by this
    38  article and for expenses incurred by the agency in the implementation of
    39  the program established by this article.
    40    § 1115-c. Purpose and terms of loan. 1. The agency, acting  on  behalf
    41  of the state may in its discretion, enter into a contract with an eligi-
    42  ble  person to provide a loan to assist in the purchase of a dwelling or
    43  the purchase and rehabilitation of a  dwelling  containing  up  to  four
    44  residential  units, provided such person shall reside in at least one of
    45  such units. Such loan may also be made for the  purchase  of  unimproved
    46  real property when such property shall be used for the construction of a
    47  new dwelling.
    48    2.  Such  loan  shall  not  exceed twenty percent of the value for the
    49  purchase of a home, or forty thousand dollars for the purchase of  unim-
    50  proved real property. Such value shall be determined from the appraisal,
    51  if  any, required by the lending institution granting the first mortgage
    52  loan on such dwelling, and if no such appraisal has  been  made  at  the
    53  time  that a contract for loan is entered into pursuant to this article,
    54  the agency shall cause such appraisal to be made.
    55    § 1115-d. Loan repayment. 1. Any loan contracted for pursuant to  this
    56  article  shall  be secured by a second mortgage on the dwelling or unim-

     1  proved real property purchased by the recipient  of  such  loan  if  the
     2  recipient  of  such  loan  assigns,  transfers  or otherwise conveys the
     3  recipient's interest in such dwelling or ceases to occupy such dwelling,
     4  the  unpaid  principal  balance  of  said second mortgage, together with
     5  interest thereon, shall become due and payable. If the recipient of  any
     6  loan  is  unable  to  repay the loan, the agency, at its discretion, may
     7  adjust the interest rate, terms and conditions of the loan to facilitate
     8  repayment.
     9    2. Repayment of any loan provided  in  accordance  with  this  article
    10  shall be subject to an interest rate to be determined in accordance with
    11  terms  and  conditions as the agency may establish. In no case shall the
    12  term exceed the term of the first mortgage obtained for the  purpose  of
    13  purchasing  such  dwelling  except,  in  the case of a graduated payment
    14  mortgage loan, the term of the loan made pursuant to  this  article  may
    15  exceed the expected term of such mortgage loan provided. The term of the
    16  loan made pursuant to this article does not exceed the lesser of:
    17    (a)  The  term  indicated  by the limited amortization schedule of the
    18  graduated payment mortgage loan; or
    19    (b) Thirty years. Payments by homeowners shall be paid to  the  agency
    20  and deposited in the veteran's home and land ownership loan fund.
    21    § 1115-e. Issuance of bonds. 1. The agency shall have the power and is
    22  hereby  authorized  from  time to time to issue its negotiable bonds and
    23  notes in conformity with applicable provisions of the uniform commercial
    24  code in such principal amounts as, in  the  discretion  of  the  agency,
    25  shall be necessary to carry out the purposes of this article, but not in
    26  excess of an aggregate amount of twenty million dollars.
    27    2.  Except  as  may otherwise be expressly provided by the agency, all
    28  bonds and notes issued shall be payable out of  any  moneys,  assets  or
    29  revenues of the state, subject only to any agreement with bondholders or
    30  noteholders pledging any particular moneys, assets or revenues.
    31    3.  Bonds and notes shall be authorized by a resolution or resolutions
    32  of the agency adopted in the manner provided by law.
    33    4. Such bonds or notes shall bear such date or dates, shall mature  at
    34  such  time or times, shall bear interest at such rate or rates, shall be
    35  of such denominations, shall be in such form,  carry  such  registration
    36  privileges,  be  executed  in such manner, be payable in lawful money of
    37  the United States of America at such place or places within  or  without
    38  the  state,  be subject to such terms of redemption prior to maturity as
    39  may be provided by such resolution or resolutions  or  such  certificate
    40  with  respect  to  such  bonds  or  notes, as the case may be; provided,
    41  however, that the maximum maturity of  bonds  shall  not  exceed  thirty
    42  years  from  the  date  thereof and the maximum maturity of notes or any
    43  renewals thereof shall not exceed seven  years  from  the  date  of  the
    44  original issue of such notes.
    45    5.  Any  such  bonds  or notes may be sold at such price or prices, at
    46  public or private sale, in such manner and from time to time as  may  be
    47  determined  by the agency, and the agency may pay all expenses, premiums
    48  and  commissions  which  it  may  deem  necessary  or  advantageous   in
    49  connection  with the issuance and sale thereof. No bonds or notes may be
    50  sold at private sale, however, unless such sale  and  the  term  thereof
    51  have been approved in writing by:
    52    (a) the comptroller, if such sale is not to the comptroller; or
    53    (b)  the  state  director  of the budget, if such sale is to the comp-
    54  troller.
    55    6. The agency is authorized to provide for the issuance of  its  bonds
