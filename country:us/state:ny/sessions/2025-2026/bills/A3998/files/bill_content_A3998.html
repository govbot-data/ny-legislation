        AN ACT to amend the labor law, in relation to employee access to  lacta-
          tion rooms in the workplace
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Subdivisions 2 and 3 of section 206-c of the labor law,  as
     2  added  by  chapter  672  of  the  laws  of  2022, are amended to read as
     3  follows:
     4    2. (a) Upon request of an employee who chooses to express breast  milk
     5  in  the  workplace, an employer shall designate a room or other location
     6  which shall be made available for use by such employee to express breast
     7  milk. Such room or other location shall be a place that is: (i)  hygien-
     8  ic;  (ii)  in  close  proximity to the work area; [(ii)] (iii) well lit;
     9  [(iii)] (iv) shielded from view; and [(iv)] (v) free from intrusion from
    10  other persons in the  workplace  or  the  public.  Such  room  or  other
    11  location shall provide, at minimum, a chair, a working surface, a refri-
    12  gerator that shall be used only for purposes related to lactation, near-
    13  by  access to clean running water and, if the workplace is supplied with
    14  electricity, an electrical outlet. The room or location provided by  the
    15  employer  for  this  purpose shall not be a restroom or toilet stall and
    16  shall be located outside the confines of a public restroom.
    17    (b) If the sole purpose or function of such room or other location  is
    18  not  dedicated for use by employees to express breast milk, such room or
    19  other location shall be made available to such an employee  when  needed
    20  and  shall not be used for any other purpose or function while in use by
    21  such employee. Employers shall provide notice to all employees  as  soon

     1  as  practicable when such room or other location has been designated for
     2  use by employees to express breast milk.
     3    (c) Where compliance with the requirements of paragraphs (a) or (b) of
     4  this subdivision is impracticable because it would impose an undue hard-
     5  ship  on  the employer by causing significant difficulty or expense when
     6  considered in relation to the  size,  financial  resources,  nature,  or
     7  structure  of  the employer's business, such employer shall make reason-
     8  able efforts to provide a room or other location, other than a  restroom
     9  or  toilet  stall,  that is in close proximity to the work area where an
    10  employee can express breast milk in privacy. Provided, however,  nothing
    11  in this subdivision shall otherwise exempt an employer from the require-
    12  ments of subdivision one of this section.
    13    [(d)  If the workplace has access to refrigeration, the employer shall
    14  extend such access to refrigeration for  the  purposes  of  storing  the
    15  expressed milk.]
    16    3.  The  commissioner  shall  develop  and  implement a written policy
    17  regarding the rights of nursing employees to express breast milk in  the
    18  workplace  pursuant  to  the provisions of this section. Employers shall
    19  provide such written policy to each  employee  upon  hire  and  annually
    20  thereafter,  and to employees upon returning to work following the birth
    21  of a child.  Employers shall post such policy in a clear and conspicuous
    22  location and in the employee handbook, to the extent  that  an  employer
    23  provides such handbook to its employees, and in a location accessible on
    24  either  the  employer's website or on the internet accessible by employ-
    25  ees. Such policy shall:
    26    (a) Inform employees of their rights pursuant to this section;
    27    (b) Specify the means by which a  request  may  be  submitted  to  the
    28  employer  for  a  room or other location for use by employees to express
    29  breast milk; and
    30    (c) Require the employer to respond to such request within  a  reason-
    31  able timeframe, but not to exceed five business days.
    32    § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
