Short title. This act shall be known and may be cited as 2 the "empire state performance commission act". 3 § 2. Legislative intent. New York's lack of a comprehensive perfor- 4 mance-budgeting scheme for state-funded programs often leads to irreg- 5 ular appropriation practices and rigid across-the-board cuts, without 6 regard to the programs' effectiveness. A performance-management and 7 performance-budgeting system that ties program funding to the results 8 the programs achieve would provide lawmakers with more flexibility in 9 making budget decisions and provide the public with a means by which 10 they can evaluate how their tax dollars are being spent. 11 The legislature recognizes that implementing and maintaining a compre- 12 hensive system of performance-management and performance-budgeting is a 13 complicated process that will require cooperation between the legisla- 14 tive and executive branches in order to be successful. Unilaterally 15 imposing performance-management and performance-budgeting requirements 16 on executive agencies would likely be unproductive and ineffective. 17 Reforming the state budget process requires careful deliberation that 18 includes feedback from the various stakeholders in the process, includ- 19 ing the public. 20 This legislation creates a blue ribbon commission that will be charged 21 with designing a performance-management and performance-budgeting model EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. The empire state 23 performance commission is established as a blue ribbon commission in the 24 executive department. The purpose of the commission is to design and 25 advise the governor and the legislature on the implementation of a 26 performance-management and performance-budgeting system. When used in 27 this article, the term "performance-management" means a management 28 system consisting of strategic planning, strategic performance and 29 productivity measurement, program evaluation, and performance budgeting, 30 and the term "performance-budgeting" means a systemic incorporation of 31 planning, strategic performance and productivity measurement, and 32 program evaluation information into the budgetary process. 33 § 1001. Membership; terms; chair and vice chair. 1. The commission 34 shall be composed of fifteen members that include six legislative 35 members and nine non-legislative members as follows: 36 (a) the governor; 37 (b) the comptroller; 38 (c) the speaker of the assembly; 39 (d) the minority leader of the assembly; 40 (e) the chair of the assembly ways and means committee; 41 (f) the temporary president of the senate; 42 (g) the minority leader of the senate; 43 (h) the chair of the senate finance committee; 44 (i) one non-legislative citizen member appointed by the speaker of the 45 assembly; 46 (j) one non-legislative citizen member appointed by the temporary 47 president of the senate; 48 (k) the director of the division of the budget; 49 (l) two executive department heads appointed by the governor; and 50 (m) two non-legislative citizen members appointed by the governor. 51 2. The legislative leaders shall submit their appointments to the 52 governor, and the governor shall make appointments, no later than thirty 53 days after the effective date of this article. If any such appointment 54 is not made by such date, the appointing officer may make the appoint- 55 ment after that date, but the vacant appointment shall not count for 56 calculation of a quorum until it is filled. The commission shall have the 21 following duties: 22 1. Design a performance-management and performance-budgeting system 23 for the state which may include, but is not limited to, the following: 24 (a) strategic planning; 25 (b) performance measurement; 26 (c) program evaluation; 27 (d) uniform standards for measuring, compiling, and reporting perform- 28 ance information; 29 (e) a website or other appropriate means for disseminating information 30 about the performance-management and performance-budgeting system to the 31 public; and 32 (f) a continuing oversight and advisory role for the commission. 33 2.
