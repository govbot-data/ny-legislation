        AN  ACT  to  amend the civil service law, in relation to providing addi-
          tional credits to Nassau county law enforcement explorers
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  Short  title. This act shall be known and may be cited as
     2  the "Nassau future protectors act".
     3    § 2. The civil service law is amended by adding a new section 85-e  to
     4  read as follows:
     5    §  85-e.  Additional  credit allowed for Nassau county law enforcement
     6  explorers in competitive  examinations;  preference  in  retention  upon
     7  abolition  of  positions.  1.  Additional  credits  shall be allowed for
     8  participants of the Nassau county law enforcement exploring  program  in
     9  competitive examinations for original appointment. The Nassau county law
    10  enforcement  exploring program shall be defined as a youth police train-
    11  ing program which provides youths between the ages of fourteen and twen-
    12  ty with training and on the  purpose,  mission  and  objectives  of  the
    13  Nassau county police department, public safety and law enforcement.
    14    (a) On all eligible lists resulting from competitive examinations, the
    15  names  of  eligible  persons shall be entered in the order of respective
    16  final earned ratings on examinations, with  the  name  of  the  eligible
    17  person  with  the highest final earned ratings at the head of such list,
    18  provided, however, that for the  purpose  of  determining  final  earned
    19  ratings,  Nassau  county  law enforcement explorers shall be entitled to
    20  receive additional points in  a  competitive  examination  for  original

     1  appointment  in Nassau county. An eligible person who served as a Nassau
     2  county law enforcement explorer  for  a  minimum  of  four  years  shall
     3  receive  an  additional  five points. An eligible person who served as a
     4  Nassau  county  law  enforcement explorer for less than four years shall
     5  receive an additional two points.
     6    (b) Such additional credit shall be added to the final  earned  rating
     7  of  such  Nassau  county  law  enforcement explorer, after such explorer
     8  qualified in the competitive examination and shall be  granted  only  at
     9  the time of establishment of the resulting eligible list.
    10    2.  Any  candidate,  believing themselves to be entitled to additional
    11  credit in a competitive examination as provided  in  this  section,  may
    12  make application for such additional credit at any time between the date
    13  of  application for examination and the date of the establishment of the
    14  resulting eligible list. Such candidates shall be allowed  a  period  of
    15  not  less than two months from the date of the filing of the application
    16  for examination in which to establish by appropriate  documentary  proof
    17  the  eligibility to receive additional credit under this section. At any
    18  time after two months have elapsed  since  the  final  date  for  filing
    19  applications for a competitive examination for original appointment, the
    20  eligible  list  resulting  from  such  examination  may  be established,
    21  notwithstanding the fact that a Nassau county law  enforcement  explorer
    22  who has applied for additional credit has failed to establish the eligi-
    23  bility  to  receive  such  additional  credit.  A candidate who fails to
    24  establish, by appropriate documentary proof, the eligibility to  receive
    25  additional  credit by the time an eligible list is established shall not
    26  thereafter be granted additional credit on such eligible list.
    27    3. (a) Except as otherwise provided in this subdivision, no person who
    28  has received a permanent original appointment in the  civil  service  of
    29  Nassau  county  thereof  from  an eligible list on which such person was
    30  allowed the additional credit granted by this section as a Nassau county
    31  law enforcement explorer, shall thereafter be entitled to any additional
    32  credit under this section.
    33    (b) Where, at the time of establishment of an eligible list, the posi-
    34  tion of a Nassau county law enforcement explorer on such  list  has  not
    35  been affected by the addition of credits granted under this section, the
    36  appointment  of  such  Nassau  county law enforcement explorer from such
    37  eligible list shall not be deemed to have been  made  from  an  eligible
    38  list  on  which  they were allowed the additional credit granted by this
    39  section.
    40    (c) If, at the time of appointment from an  eligible  list,  a  Nassau
    41  county  law  enforcement explorer is in the same relative standing among
    42  the eligible persons who are willing to accept appointment  as  if  such
    43  explorer had not been granted the additional credits as provided by this
    44  section,  the appointment from such eligible persons shall not be deemed
    45  to have been made from an eligible list on which they were allowed  such
    46  additional credits.
    47    (d) Where a Nassau county law enforcement explorer has been originally
    48  appointed  from an eligible list on which such explorer was allowed such
    49  additional credit, but such appointment is thereafter terminated  either
    50  at  the  end of the probationary term or by resignation at or before the
    51  end of the probationary term, such explorer shall not be deemed to  have
    52  been  appointed, as the case may be, from an eligible list on which such
    53  explorer is allowed additional credit, and such  appointment  shall  not
    54  affect  such explorer's eligibility for additional credit in other exam-
    55  inations.

     1    4. An application for additional credit in a  competitive  examination
     2  under  this  section may be withdrawn by the applicant at any time prior
     3  to the establishment of the resulting eligible list. At any time  during
     4  the  term  of existence of an eligible list resulting from a competitive
     5  examination  in  which  a  Nassau  county  law  enforcement explorer has
     6  received the additional credit granted  by  this  section,  such  Nassau
     7  county  law  enforcement explorer may elect, prior to permanent original
     8  appointment, to relinquish the additional credit granted and accept  the
     9  lower position on such eligible list to which such explorer would other-

    11  thereafter be irrevocable. Such election shall be in writing and  signed
    12  by  the  Nassau  county law enforcement explorer, and transmitted to the
    13  department or the appropriate municipal civil service commission.
    14    5. The department and each municipal commission  shall  establish  and
    15  maintain  in  its office a roster of all such Nassau county law enforce-
    16  ment explorers appointed as a result of additional  credits  granted  by
    17  this  section  to positions under its jurisdiction. The appointment of a
    18  Nassau county law enforcement explorer as a result of additional credits
    19  shall be void if such Nassau county law enforcement explorer,  prior  to
    20  such  appointment,  had been appointed as a result of additional credits
    21  granted by this section.
    22    § 3. This act shall take effect immediately.
