"Beverage splash stick" means a device primarily intended to be 11 used to keep heat and liquid from escaping a lidded cup. 12 2. "Beverage stirrer" means a device primarily intended for stirring 13 beverages. 14 3. "Condiment pack" means an individual single-use container, sealed 15 by the manufacturer or a restaurant, containing a condiment. 16 4. "Plastic" means a synthetic material made from organic polymers. 17 5. "Restaurant" means any diner or other eating or beverage establish- 18 ment, which offers for sale food or beverages to the public, guests, 19 members, or patrons, whether consumption occurs on or off the premises. 20 6. "Single-use food service items" shall mean any eating utensil or 21 other item used as part of food or beverage service that is designed and 22 intended by the manufacturer for only one use before being discarded 23 including, but not limited to condiment packs, forks, spoons, sporks, 24 knives, chopsticks, beverage straws, cocktail sticks, toothpicks, non- EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. For purposes of this title, single-use food 4 service items shall not include: (a) a plastic beverage stirrer or a 5 plastic beverage splash stick as defined in this section; or (b) 6 containers actively holding food or beverages for dine-in or take-away 7 customers, such as plates, bowls, and cups. 8 7. "Third-party food delivery service" shall have the same meaning as 9 section three hundred ninety-one-v of the general business law. 10 § 27-3403. Single-use food service items. 11 1. (a) Restaurants and third-party food delivery services shall only 12 provide single-use food service items to customers when explicitly 13 requested by the customer. Restaurants and third-party food delivery 14 services shall provide options to allow a customer to request single-use 15 food service items upon submission of an order. Such options shall be 16 available for all methods of ordering, including but not limited to 17 phone, internet, or mobile phone application orders. The default 18 selected options shall be that no single-use food service items are 19 requested. 20 (b) No inquiry into the reason for the request shall be made by a 21 restaurant, third-party food delivery service, or any of its employees 22 in carrying out the request authorized by paragraph (a) of this subdivi- 23 sion. 24 2. Restaurants and third-party food delivery services are prohibited 25 from providing customers with single-use plastic food service items. 26 This prohibition does not apply to plastic straws, which may be provided 27 by a restaurant or third-party food delivery service, pursuant to subdi- 28 vision one of this section. 29 3. Single-use food service items provided by a restaurant, pursuant to 30 subdivision one of this section, shall not be individually wrapped in 31 plastic. 32 4. (a) If a restaurant fills an order placed through a third-party 33 food delivery service, such restaurant may rely on the information 34 provided by such third-party food delivery service regarding whether the 35 customer has requested single-use food service items. In a proceeding to 36 collect a civil penalty pursuant to section 27-3405 of this title, it 37 shall be a complete defense for a respondent restaurant to establish 38 that such restaurant relied on the information provided to it by the 39 third-party food delivery service regarding the customer's choice pursu- 40 ant to paragraph (a) of subdivision one of this section. 41 (b) A copy or screenshot of a communication by the third-party food 42 delivery service to the restaurant regarding the customer's choice 43 pursuant to paragraph (a) of subdivision one of this section shall 44 constitute prima facie evidence that the restaurant relied on the infor- 45 mation provided to it by the third-party food delivery service. Each 46 third-party food delivery service shall provide such communication in 47 writing to a restaurant within seventy-two hours of such restaurant's 48 request for such communication. 49 § 27-3405. Enforcement. 50 Every local department of health shall ensure compliance with this 51 title as part of an inspection of a food place pursuant to section thir- 52 teen hundred fifty-one of the public health law, the provisions of the 53 sanitary code and provisions of local ordinances or regulations. Any 54 establishment that violates this title shall be liable for a civil 55 penalty in the amount of two hundred dollars for the first violation, 56 three hundred dollars for the second violation committed on a different S. 4059 3 1 day within a period of twelve months, and five hundred dollars for the 2 third and each subsequent violation committed on different days within a 3 period of twelve months, except that the local department of health 4 shall not issue a notice of violation, but shall issue a warning and 5 provide information on compliance with such section, for any violation 6 that occurs within one year of the effective date of this section. 7 § 2.
