"Reverse keyword court order" means any court order, including a 13 search warrant, compelling the disclosure of records or information 14 identifying any unnamed persons, by name or other unique identifier, who 15 electronically searched for particular words, phrases, character 16 strings, or websites, or who visited a particular website through a link 17 generated by such a search, regardless of whether or not the order is 18 limited to a specific geographic area or time frame. 19 4. "Reverse location court order" means any court order, including a 20 search warrant, compelling the disclosure of records or information 21 pertaining to electronic devices or their users or owners, whose scope 22 extends to an unknown number of electronic devices present in a given 23 geographic area at a given time as measured via global positioning 24 system coordinates, cell tower connectivity, Wi-Fi data and/or any other 25 form of location detection. 26 5. "Voluntary reverse keyword request" means any request in the 27 absence of a court order, by any government entity for the provision of 28 records or information identifying any unnamed persons, by name or other 29 unique identifier, who electronically searched for particular words, 30 phrases, character strings, or websites, or who visited a particular 31 website through a link generated by such a search, regardless of whether 32 or not the order is limited to a specific geographic area or time frame. 33 6. "Voluntary reverse location request" means any request in the 34 absence of a court order by any government entity for records or infor- 35 mation pertaining to electronic devices or their users or owners, whose 36 scope extends to an unknown number of electronic devices present in a 37 given geographic area at a given time, whether such device location is 38 measured via global positioning system coordinates, cell tower connec- 39 tivity, Wi-Fi data and/or any other form of location detection. 40 § 695.10 Issuance of reverse location court orders and reverse keyword 41 court orders. 42 No court shall issue a reverse location court order or a reverse 43 keyword court order. 44 § 695.20 Execution of reverse location and reverse keyword searches. 45 1. No government entity shall seek, from any court, a reverse location 46 court order or a reverse keyword court order. 47 2. No government entity shall make a voluntary reverse location 48 request or a voluntary and reverse keyword request. 49 3. No government entity shall seek, secure, obtain, borrow, purchase, 50 use, or review any information or data obtained through a reverse 51 location court order, a reverse keyword court order, a voluntary reverse 52 location request, or a voluntary keyword request. 53 4. Upon motion from a defendant, a court shall order that evidence be 21 suppressed or excluded if the court finds that such evidence: 22 (a) consists of a record acquired via a reverse location court order, 23 reverse keyword court order, voluntary reverse location request, or 24 voluntary reverse keyword request; or 25 (b) was obtained as a result of other evidence obtained under a 26 reverse location court order, reverse keyword court order, voluntary 27 reverse location request, or voluntary reverse keyword request. 28 2. This section shall apply regardless of the court which issued the 29 order and regardless of whether the issuance of the order was permissi- 30 ble under the procedures of that court. 31 3. This section shall apply regardless of any claim that the informa- 32 tion or evidence is attenuated from an unlawful order or request, would 33 inevitably have been discovered, or was simultaneously or subsequently 34 obtained or reobtained through other means. 35 § 695.40 Reverse location and reverse keyword searches; private right of 36 action. 37 1. Any individual whose records were obtained by any government entity 38 in violation of section 695.20 of this article may institute a civil 39 action against such government entity for any or all of the following: 40 (a) One thousand dollars per violation or actual damages, whichever is 41 greater. 42 (b) Punitive damages. 43 (c) Injunctive or declaratory relief. 44 (d) Any other relief the court deems proper. 45 2. In assessing the amount of punitive damages, the court shall 46 consider: 47 (a) the number of people whose information was disclosed; 48 (b) whether the violation directly or indirectly targeted persons 49 engaged in the exercise of activities protected by the constitution of 50 the United States of America or the constitution of the state of New 51 York; and 52 (c) the persistence of violations by the particular government entity. 53 3. In any action brought under this section, the court shall award 54 reasonable attorneys' fees, expenses and costs to a prevailing plain- 55 tiff. 56 § 695.50 Physical searches excluded.
