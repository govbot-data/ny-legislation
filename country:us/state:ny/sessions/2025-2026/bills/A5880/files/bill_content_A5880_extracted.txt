BROWN -- read once and referred to the Commit- tee on Libraries and Education Technology AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to requiring libraries have charging stations for plug-in electric vehicles The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The education law is amended by adding a new section 286 to 2 read as follows: 3 § 286. Electric vehicle charging stations. 1. For the purposes of this 4 section, the term "parking facility" shall mean a parking garage, park- 5 ing structure, or open parking lot that is paved and striped to desig- 6 nate individual parking spaces for vehicles. 7 2. Any parking facility owned and operated by a public library shall 8 include electric vehicle charging stations in at least five percent of 9 the total number of parking spaces offered by such library. 10 3. Each electric vehicle charging station required pursuant to this 11 section shall support electric vehicle charging with a minimum of forty 12 amperes and two hundred eight volts of electrical capacity. Such elec- 13 tric vehicle charging stations and all other components and work appur- 14 tenant thereto shall be in accordance with all applicable state, county, 15 city, town, or village electrical codes. 16 § 2. This act shall take effect three years after it shall have become 17 a law. Effective immediately, the addition, amendment and/or repeal of 18 any rule or regulation necessary for the implementation of this act on 19 its effective date are authorized to be made and completed on or before 20 such effective date.
