Add §4411, amd §§3612, 3004-a, 3033 & 669-f, Ed L
 
Provides for automatic eligibility to teacher support and retention programs for teachers employed by certain schools receiving funding for the provision of special education.

        AN  ACT  to  amend the education law, in relation to access to statewide
          teacher support and retention programs
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  The education law is amended by adding a new section 4411
     2  to read as follows:
     3    § 4411. Access to statewide teacher support and retention programs. 1.
     4  For the purposes of this section, the following  terms  shall  have  the
     5  following meanings:
     6    (a)  "Covered  school"  means  a school or preschool receiving funding
     7  under this article for the provision of special education.
     8    (b) "Teacher support and retention program" means any statewide initi-
     9  ative, policy, funding stream, or award  program  administered,  author-
    10  ized,  or overseen by the department, the regents, the university of the
    11  state of New York, or any other state-affiliated educational entity:
    12    (i) that is designed to:
    13    (1) recruit individuals into the teaching profession;
    14    (2) support teacher preparation, certification, or licensure;
    15    (3) provide professional development, mentorship, or continuing educa-
    16  tion;
    17    (4) recognize teaching excellence or support teacher leadership;
    18    (5)  retain  teachers  in  high-need,  underserved,  or  hard-to-staff
    19  schools or subject areas;
    20    (6) promote teacher diversity, equity, inclusion, and cultural respon-
    21  siveness; or
    22    (7)  offer financial or career-based incentives to enhance recruitment
    23  and retention, including but  not  limited  to  scholarships,  stipends,
    24  housing  assistance,  loan  forgiveness,  tuition reimbursement, or wage
    25  supplements; and
    26    (ii) including, but not limited to:

     1    (1) the teacher opportunity corps (TOC) under section  55.2  of  title
     2  eight of the New York codes, rules and regulations;
     3    (2)  the  empire  state  excellence in teaching awards funded annually
     4  under New York state's aid to localities budget;
     5    (3) the New York state master teacher program;
     6    (4) the teachers of tomorrow teacher recruitment and retention program
     7  under section thirty-six hundred twelve of this chapter;
     8    (5) the Albert Shanker national board for professional teaching stand-
     9  ards certification grant program under section three thousand four-a  of
    10  this chapter;
    11    (6) the New York state mentor teacher-internship program under section
    12  three thousand thirty-three of this chapter;
    13    (7) the New York state masters-in-education teacher incentive scholar-
    14  ship program under section six hundred sixty-nine-f of this chapter; and
    15    (8)  any  successor or future initiative designed to support or retain
    16  qualified teachers within the state of New York.
    17    2. All teachers employed by a covered school shall  be  deemed  to  be
    18  eligible  participants  in  all  teacher support and retention programs.
    19  Such eligibility shall not be denied, limited, or conditioned solely  on
    20  the  basis  that  such  teacher  is employed by a non-district school, a
    21  non-public school, or a program that  contracts  with  a  public  agency
    22  pursuant to this article.
    23    3. The commissioner shall, no later than one hundred twenty days after
    24  the effective date of this section:
    25    (a) update all relevant rules, regulations, administrative procedures,
    26  and  program  eligibility requirements for teacher support and retention
    27  programs to reflect inclusion of teachers employed by covered schools;
    28    (b) ensure application materials, outreach efforts, and guidance docu-
    29  ments for teacher support and retention programs explicitly include such
    30  teachers employed by covered schools;
    31    (c) provide technical assistance  and  outreach  to  approved  covered
    32  schools to promote awareness and access to teacher support and retention
    33  programs; and
    34    (d)  include representatives of covered schools in teacher support and
    35  retention program advisory, design, or evaluation activities, where such
    36  covered school input is solicited.
    37    4. (a) Notwithstanding subdivision two  of  this  section,  a  teacher
    38  support  or retention program may limit eligibility to teachers employed
    39  by public school districts only if:
    40    (i) the department or the administering agency has conducted and docu-
    41  mented a  good  faith  due  diligence  review  to  assess  whether  such
    42  program's legal authority, funding source, or operational design permits
    43  inclusion of educators employed by covered schools; and
    44    (ii)  the department determines, in writing, that such inclusion under
    45  subparagraph (i) of this  paragraph  is  not  legally  or  operationally
    46  feasible due to one or more of the following:
    47    (1) state or federal statutory restrictions;
    48    (2) contractual obligations limiting participation to district employ-
    49  ees; or
    50    (3)  programmatic  design  features  that  are inherently exclusive to
    51  public district-based employment.
    52    (b) A summary of any determination made  under  subparagraph  (ii)  of
    53  paragraph  (a)  of  this  subdivision, including a statement of reasons,
    54  shall be:
    55    (i) made publicly available on the department's website; and

    16    7. Any teacher intern participating in the program under this  section

    20  subdivision 7 to read as follows:
    21    7. Any eligible student who receives an award under this section shall
    22  be  deemed  to  be a part of a teacher support and retention program for
    23  the purposes of section forty-four hundred eleven of this chapter.
    24    § 6. This act shall take effect immediately.
