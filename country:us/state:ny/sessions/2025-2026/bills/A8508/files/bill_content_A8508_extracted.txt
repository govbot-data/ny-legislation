CHANG -- read once and referred to the Committee on Education AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to requiring excess, unused, edible food from meals served at educational facilities be donated to local voluntary food assistance programs The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Subdivision 56 of section 305 of the education law, as 2 added by chapter 316 of the laws of 2017, is renumbered subdivision 63 3 and is amended to read as follows: 4 63. The commissioner, in consultation and cooperation with the commis- 5 sioner of agriculture and markets, [may] shall develop guidelines to 6 [encourage] mandate school districts and institutions of higher educa- 7 tion to donate excess, unused, edible food from meals served at such 8 educational facilities to local voluntary food assistance programs 9 including, but not limited to, community food pantries, soup kitchens, 10 and other community and not-for-profit organizations that distribute 11 food to the poor and disadvantaged. Such guidelines developed concerning 12 donated food shall comply with all federal, state and local public 13 health laws, rules and regulations that govern food safety and food 14 donation. 15 Such guidelines may include, but not be limited to: 16 a. a procedure to provide information to school districts, insti- 17 tutions of higher education and local voluntary food assistance programs 18 of the provisions of such guidelines; 19 b. notification to educational institutions of their ability to elect 20 to donate excess, unused, edible food to local voluntary food assistance 21 programs; and 22 c. information and technical assistance on how school districts and 23 institutions of higher education may best donate excess food in a safe 24 and sanitary manner. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
