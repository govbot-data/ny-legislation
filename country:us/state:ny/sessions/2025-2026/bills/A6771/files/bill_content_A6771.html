Defines the practice of certified registered nurse anesthetist; requires collaboration with a licensed physician qualified to determine the need for anesthesia services; requires licensing; defines qualifications.

        AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to creating  the  profes-
          sion of nurse anesthetist
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The education law is amended by adding a new article  139-A
     2  to read as follows:
     3                                ARTICLE 139-A
     4                   CERTIFIED REGISTERED NURSE ANESTHETIST
     5  Section 6920. Introduction.
     6          6921. Definition  of  practice  of  certified  registered  nurse
     7                  anesthetist.
     8          6922. Practice of certified registered nurse anesthetist and use
     9                  of the title "certified registered nurse anesthetist" or
    10                  "nurse anesthetist".
    11          6923. State board for nursing.
    12          6924. Requirements for a license as a certified registered nurse
    13                  anesthetist.
    14          6925. Limited permits.
    15          6926. Exempt persons.
    16          6927. Special provisions.
    17    § 6920. Introduction. This article applies to the profession of certi-
    18  fied registered  nurse  anesthetist.  The  general  provisions  for  all
    19  professions  contained in article one hundred thirty of this title apply
    20  to this article.

        A. 6771--A                          2
 
     1    § 6921. Definition of practice of certified registered nurse  anesthe-
     2  tist.  1.  In  addition  to  the  provisions  of practice for registered
     3  professional nursing outlined in section sixty-nine hundred two of  this
     4  title,  the practice of a certified registered nurse anesthetist further
     5  includes,  but  is  not  limited  to,  administration  of anesthesia and
     6  sedation to  a  patient;  performing  and  documenting  a  preanesthetic
     7  assessment and evaluation of the patient, including requesting consulta-
     8  tions and diagnostic studies; selecting, obtaining, ordering, and admin-
     9  istering   preanesthetic  medications  and  fluids;  obtaining  informed
    10  consent for anesthesia, developing and implementing an anesthetic  plan;
    11  initiating  the  anesthetic  technique; monitoring the patient including
    12  selecting, applying, and inserting appropriate noninvasive and  invasive
    13  monitoring  modalities  for continuous evaluation of the patient's phys-
    14  ical status; selecting, obtaining, and  administering  the  anesthetics,
    15  adjuvant and accessory drugs, and fluids necessary to manage anesthetics
    16  and acute and chronic pain; ordering and evaluating laboratory and diag-
    17  nostic tests and performing point-of-care testing; ordering and evaluat-
    18  ing  radiographic  imaging  studies;  managing  a  patient's  airway and
    19  cardiopulmonary status using current practice  modalities;  facilitating
    20  emergence and recovery from anesthesia by selecting, obtaining, ordering
    21  and   administering   medications,   fluids,  and  ventilatory  support;
    22  discharging the patient from a postanesthesia care  area  and  providing
    23  postanesthesia  follow-up  evaluation  and  care; implementing acute and
    24  chronic pain management modalities; responding to  emergency  situations
    25  by  providing  airway management, administration of emergency fluids and
    26  drugs, and using basic or advanced cardiac life support techniques;  and
    27  prescribe tests, drugs and other treatments necessary to anesthesia care
    28  provided by a nurse anesthetist.
    29    2.  (a)  A  certified registered nurse anesthetist licensed under this
    30  article and practicing for thirty-six hundred hours or less shall do  so
    31  either  under  the supervision of:   (i) a physician who has completed a
    32  residency program in anesthesiology that is accredited by an accrediting
    33  organization acceptable to the department; (ii) a physician who is board
    34  certified in anesthesiology by a certifying organization  acceptable  to
    35  the  department; (iii) the operating physician who the governing body of
    36  the general hospital or ambulatory surgery center, that  is  subject  to
    37  section  twenty-eight hundred one-b of the public health law, has deter-
    38  mined is qualified to  supervise  anesthesia  services  in  the  general
    39  hospital  or  ambulatory surgery center; or (iv) a dentist or podiatrist
    40  qualified to provide anesthesia services pursuant to sections  sixty-six
    41  hundred  five-a  and seven thousand one of this title, respectively, who
    42  are immediately available as needed.
    43    (b) After practicing for more than thirty-six hundred hours, a  certi-
    44  fied  registered  nurse  anesthetist shall function in an interdependent
    45  role as a member of the patient-centered care team  that  is  performing
    46  the patient's procedure, which shall include a physician, podiatrist, or
    47  dentist.
    48    3. Prescriptions for drugs, devices, and anesthetic agents, anesthesia
    49  related  agents,  and analgesics may be issued by a certified registered
    50  nurse anesthetist under this paragraph and  section  sixty-nine  hundred
    51  twenty-four  of this article, necessary to anesthesia care provided by a
    52  nurse anesthetist.   The certified registered  nurse  anesthetist  shall
    53  obtain  a certificate from the department upon successfully completing a
    54  program including an appropriate pharmacology component, or  its  equiv-
    55  alent,  as  established  by  the  commissioner's  regulations,  prior to
    56  prescribing under this subdivision. The  license  issued  under  section

     1  sixty-nine  hundred  twenty-four of this article shall state whether the
     2  certified registered nurse anesthetist has successfully completed such a
     3  program or equivalent and is authorized to prescribe under this subdivi-
     4  sion.
     5    §  6922. Practice of certified registered nurse anesthetist and use of
     6  the title "certified registered nurse anesthetist"  or  "nurse  anesthe-
     7  tist". Only a person licensed or otherwise authorized under this article
     8  shall  practice  as  a  certified  registered nurse anesthetist or nurse
     9  anesthetist.
    10    § 6923. State board for nursing. The state board for nursing as estab-
    11  lished in section sixty-nine hundred four of this  title  shall  be  the
    12  governing state board for certified registered nurse anesthetists.
    13    §  6924.  Requirements  for  a license as a certified registered nurse
    14  anesthetist. To qualify for a license as a  certified  registered  nurse
    15  anesthetist, an applicant shall fulfill the following requirements:
    16    1. Application: file an application with the department;
    17    2.  License:  be New York state licensed and currently registered as a
    18  registered professional nurse;
    19    3. Education: have received a degree in a program of nurse  anesthesia
    20  from a program registered by the department or determined by the depart-
    21  ment  to be substantially equivalent or meet the requirements of section
    22  sixty-nine hundred twenty-seven of this article;
    23    4. Examination:  pass an examination satisfactory  to  the  department
    24  after  consultation  with  the state board for nursing and in accordance
    25  with the commissioner's regulations;
    26    5. Character: be of good moral character as determined by the  depart-
    27  ment; and
    28    6.  Fees: pay a fee of one hundred fifty dollars to the department for
    29  an initial license and a fee of fifty dollars for each triennial  regis-
    30  tration period.
    31    § 6925. Limited permits. 1.  The department may issue a limited permit
    32  to  an  applicant  who  is  New  York state licensed and registered as a
    33  registered nurse and meets all qualifications for licensure, except  the
    34  examination.
    35    2. Limited permits shall be for two years.
    36    3.  A  limited anesthetist permit shall entitle the holder to practice
    37  as a nurse  anesthetist  only  in  a  health  care  facility  under  the
    38  personal, on-premise supervision of a certified registered nurse anesth-
    39  etist  with more than thirty-six hundred hours of practice experience or
    40  physician who: (a) has completed a residency program  in  anesthesiology
    41  that  is  accredited  by  an  accrediting organization acceptable to the
    42  department; or (b) is board certified in anesthesiology by a  certifying
    43  organization  acceptable  to  the  department;  or (c) has medical staff
    44  privileges to provide anesthesia services at the  health  care  facility
    45  and with the endorsement of the health care facility.
    46    4. Fees. The fee for each limited permit shall be thirty-five dollars.
    47    § 6926. Exempt persons. This article shall not be construed:
    48    1. to limit or diminish the practice of the profession of nursing as a
    49  nurse  practitioner or registered professional nurse pursuant to article
    50  one hundred thirty-nine of this title or any other law, rule, regulation
    51  or certification;
    52    2. as prohibiting the furnishing of  nurse  anesthesia  assistance  in
    53  case of an emergency;
    54    3.  as  prohibiting  such  performance  of nurse anesthesia service by
    55  students enrolled in department registered nurse anesthesia programs  or
    56  students enrolled in other nurse anesthesia programs that are authorized

     1  pursuant  to  section  two  hundred twenty-four of this chapter to place
     2  such students in clinical rotations  in  this  state  under  appropriate
     3  supervision; or
     4    4.  as  prohibiting  or preventing the practice of nurse anesthesia in
     5  this state, in obedience to the requirements of the laws of  the  United
     6  States, by any commissioned nurse anesthetist officer in the armed forc-

    10  for  such  nurse  anesthetist under the United States statutes, provided
    11  such person does not represent or hold themselves  out  as  a  certified
    12  registered nurse anesthetist registered to practice in this state.
    13    §  6927.  Special  provisions.    It shall be lawful for an individual
    14  practicing as a certified registered nurse anesthetist in this state  as
    15  of the effective date of this section that does not meet the educational
    16  requirements,  but  meets  the  other requirements of section sixty-nine
    17  hundred twenty-four of this article, to continue such practice  provided
    18  that such certified registered nurse anesthetist:
    19    1.  submits  evidence of certification as a certified registered nurse
    20  anesthetist by an appropriate national certifying body  to  the  depart-
    21  ment;
    22    2.  has  continually  maintained  active,  up-to-date  recertification
    23  status as a certified registered nurse  anesthetist  by  an  appropriate
    24  national recertifying body;
    25    3.  has  submitted  an application for and obtained licensure as a New
    26  York state certified registered nurse anesthetist within three years  of
    27  the effective date of this article; and
    28    4. nothing in this article shall be construed to authorize an individ-
    29  ual  to  continue  to practice as or use the title "certified registered
    30  nurse anesthetist" or "CRNA" beyond the  three-year  time  frame  unless
    31  such individual obtains a New York state license under this article.
    32    §  2.  Section 6903 of the education law, as amended by chapter 323 of
    33  the laws of 2006, is amended to read  as follows:
    34    § 6903. Practice of nursing and use of title "registered  professional
    35  nurse"  or  "licensed practical nurse". Only a person licensed or other-
    36  wise authorized under this article shall practice  nursing  and  only  a
    37  person  licensed  under  section sixty-nine hundred five of this article
    38  shall use the title "registered professional nurse" and  only  a  person
    39  licensed  under section sixty-nine hundred six of this article shall use
    40  the title "licensed practical nurse". No  person  shall  use  the  title
    41  "nurse"  or  any other title or abbreviation that would represent to the
    42  public that the person is authorized  to  practice  nursing  unless  the
    43  person is licensed or otherwise authorized under this article or article
    44  one hundred thirty-nine-A of this title.
    45    §  3.  Section 6904 of the education law, as amended by chapter 994 of
    46  the laws of 1971 and as renumbered by chapter 50 of the laws of 1972, is

    48    § 6904. State board for nursing.  A state board for nursing  shall  be

    51  matters of professional licensing and professional conduct in accordance
    52  with section sixty-five hundred eight of this title.  The board shall be
    53  composed  of  not  less than [fifteen] seventeen members, eleven of whom
    54  shall be registered professional  nurses  and  four  of  whom  shall  be
    55  licensed  practical nurses and two of whom shall be certified registered
    56  nurse anesthetists all licensed and practicing  in  this  state  for  at
