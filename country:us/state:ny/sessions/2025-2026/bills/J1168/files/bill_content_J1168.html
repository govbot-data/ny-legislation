Senate Resolution No. 1168
 
BY: Senator GRIFFO
 
        HONORING Shari McNamara Gerevics upon the occasion
        of  her  induction into the Rome Sports Hall of Fame
        on July 27, 2025
 
  WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to pay tribute to
outstanding athletes who have  distinguished  themselves  through  their
exceptional performance, attaining unprecedented success and the highest
level of personal achievement; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Attendant  to  such  concern,  and in full accord with its
long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to honor
Shari McNamara Gerevics upon the occasion of her induction into the Rome
Sports Hall of Fame, to be celebrated on July 27, 2025; and
 
  WHEREAS, Few athletes in Rome's history have displayed the level  of
dominance,  discipline, and determination shown by Shari McNamara during
her remarkable high school and collegiate softball careers; and
 
  WHEREAS, As a star pitcher for the Rome Catholic High  School  Girls
Softball Team, Shari McNamara Gerevics compiled an extraordinary record,
throwing  12  no-hitters-including four perfect games-over three varsity
seasons; she led her team to the Section III Championship  in  2000  and
runner-up finishes in the two preceding years; and
 
  WHEREAS,  A  threat  at  the  plate as well, Shari McNamara Gerevics
posted outstanding batting averages of .463, .429, and .417  during  her
high  school  career, earning Center State Conference All-Star honors in
each of her three seasons and being  named  League  MVP  and  First-Team
All-State in her senior year; and
 
  WHEREAS,  A  model  scholar-athlete,  Shari  McNamara  Gerevics also
received  conference  academic  honors  for  three  consecutive   years,
demonstrating excellence in both athletics and academics; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Former  Rome Catholic High Coach Perry Novak praised Shari
McNamara Gerevics as "the  hardest-working,  most  outstanding  softball
player  I  coached  or saw in my 10 years at the varsity level," lauding
not only her skill but her brilliant mind, team spirit, and  exceptional
sportsmanship; and
 
  WHEREAS,   Shari   McNamara  Gerevics  continued  to  excel  at  the
collegiate level, competing for the Rochester  Institute  of  Technology
(RIT), where she was twice named Empire 8 Player of the Year and left an
indelible  mark  on  the  program  by  setting  numerous school records,
including most career wins (55), shutouts (22),  and  strikeouts  (515);
and
 
  WHEREAS,   Alongside   her  athletic  achievements,  Shari  McNamara
Gerevics earned degrees in electrical engineering and  imaging  science,
exemplifying  the  qualities  of  a  true  student-athlete  dedicated to
personal and academic growth; and
 
  WHEREAS, In recognition of her extraordinary collegiate career,  RIT
inducted  Shari  McNamara Gerevics into its Sports Hall of Fame in 2013,
cementing her legacy as one of the institution's greatest athletes; and
 
  WHEREAS,  Shari  McNamara Gerevics stands as an inspiring role model
for young athletes, exemplifying what can be achieved through hard work,
integrity, and perseverance, and her induction into the Rome Sports Hall
of Fame is a fitting tribute to her contributions to  athletics  and  to
the Rome community; now, therefore, be it
 
  RESOLVED,  That  this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to
honor Shari McNamara Gerevics upon the occasion of  her  induction  into
the  Rome  Sports  Hall of Fame on July 27, 2025, and to commend her for
her athletic excellence, academic achievements, and enduring impact; and
be it further
 
  RESOLVED, That a copy of this  Resolution,  suitably  engrossed,  be
transmitted to Shari McNamara Gerevics.
