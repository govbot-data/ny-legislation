        Introduced  by  M.  of  A.  BUTTENSCHON -- read once and referred to the
          Committee on Governmental Operations
 
        AN ACT to amend the public officers law, in  relation  to  the  line  of
          succession to the office of governor; and to repeal article 1-A of the
          New York state defense emergency act relating thereto

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The public officers law is amended by adding a new  section
     2  44 to read as follows:
     3    §  44.  Officials  eligible to act as governor. 1. For the purposes of
     4  article four of the constitution, if  the  office  of  governor  becomes
     5  vacant or if the governor is impeached or unable to discharge the powers
     6  and  duties of the office of governor, and each of the lieutenant gover-
     7  nor, the temporary president of the senate and the speaker of the assem-
     8  bly is unable to act as governor or each  office  is  vacant,  then  the
     9  official  who  is  highest  on  the following list shall act as governor
    10  until a new governor is elected, or the governor or lieutenant  governor
    11  is acquitted or becomes able: attorney general, comptroller, commission-
    12  er  of transportation, commissioner of health, commissioner of financial
    13  services, secretary of state, commissioner of labor and commissioner  of
    14  agriculture,  provided  that  such official otherwise meets the criteria
    15  set forth in section two of article four of  the  constitution  and  was
    16  confirmed  by  the  senate  or elected to lead the official's respective
    17  department.
    18    2. Whenever any official listed in subdivision  one  of  this  section
    19  shall  act  as  governor,  that official shall be required to vacate the
    20  official's existing position and shall remain eligible to act as  gover-
    21  nor  following  such  resignation. Notwithstanding the foregoing, if any
    22  official listed in subdivision one of this section shall act as governor
    23  when the governor or lieutenant governor is under impeachment or  unable
    24  to discharge the powers and duties of office, such official shall not be

     1  required  to  vacate  that  official's existing position until they have
     2  served as acting governor for sixty  consecutive  days.  However,  while
     3  serving  as  acting governor during such sixty-day period, that official
     4  shall  not  be permitted to discharge any powers and duties of the offi-
     5  cial's original position.
     6    3. Any official listed in subdivision one of this section may  decline
     7  to  act as governor, making them unable to act as governor. In the event
     8  any official listed in subdivision one of this section is unable to  act
     9  as  governor or is ineligible to do so, the official next highest on the
    10  list who is eligible and able shall act as governor until a new governor
    11  is elected, or the governor  or  lieutenant  governor  is  acquitted  or
    12  becomes able.
    13    4.  If  an  official acts as governor under this section, the official
    14  shall discharge all the powers and duties of the office of  governor  as
    15  if the official had been elected governor.
    16    §  2. Article 1-A of chapter 784 of the laws of 1951, constituting the
    17  New York state defense emergency act, is REPEALED.
    18    § 3. This act shall take effect on the  same  date  as  a  "CONCURRENT
    19  RESOLUTION  OF THE SENATE AND ASSEMBLY proposing an amendment to article
    20  4 of the constitution, to the line of succession to the  office  of  the
    21  governor"  takes  effect,  in accordance with section 1 of article 19 of
    22  the constitution.
