WIEDER -- read once and referred to the Committee on Real Property Taxation AN ACT in relation to requiring the school district of East Ramapo to refund to taxpayers excess school taxes collected The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Legislative findings and intent. In 2024, the school board 2 of the East Ramapo Central School District voted for an increase of 1.0% 3 for the 2024-2025 school district budget. Relying on an assertion that 4 the district was facing a severe budget deficit, the commissioner of 5 education for the state of New York directed the East Ramapo Central 6 School District to raise the school tax levy by an additional 4.38%, 7 bringing the total tax levy hike to 5.38%. The increase resulted in the 8 collection of more than $6,000,000 in school taxes. A short time later, 9 an external audit of East Ramapo's financing turned up more than 10 $30,000,000 in unrestricted funds, a shock to school district officials, 11 the school board and residents. Subsequent inquiry has revealed that the 12 surplus may be as high as $100,000,000. The legislature finds that the 13 tax levy imposed for the 2024-2025 school taxes was erroneous and 14 declares that the excess funds should be refunded in full to the taxpay- 15 ers who paid them. 16 § 2. Pursuant to the provisions of title 3 of article 5 of the real 17 property tax law, the tax levying body of East Ramapo shall refund to 18 each person the amount of school tax erroneously paid by such person, or 19 that portion thereof in excess of the tax that should have been paid in 20 connection with the 2024-2025 school tax had the increase in such tax 21 been calculated utilizing the originally proposed 1% increase. This 22 refund shall be made available from the surplus funds held by the town 23 and the school district. All refunds shall be paid within ninety days of 24 the effective date of this act. Notwithstanding the provisions of subdi- 25 vision 1 of section 556 of the real property tax law, no person entitled EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
