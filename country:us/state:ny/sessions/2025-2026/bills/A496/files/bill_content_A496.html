Amd §§1504, 1507, 1510 & 1513, N-PC L
 
Relates to certain duties and responsibilities of cemetery corporations; requires disclosure of the dimensions of spacing between plots, lots, or parts thereof; provides for the filing of consumer complaints.

        AN  ACT  to  amend  the  not-for-profit  corporation law, in relation to
          certain duties and responsibilities of cemetery corporations

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1. Paragraph (c) of section 1504 of the not-for-profit corpo-
     2  ration law, as added by chapter 871 of the laws  of  1977,  subparagraph
     3  2-a  as amended and subparagraph 2-b as added by chapter 565 of the laws
     4  of 1980, subparagraph 4 as added by chapter 557 of the laws of 1985, and
     5  subparagraph 5 as renumbered by chapter 557 of  the  laws  of  1985,  is
     6  amended and a new paragraph (e) is added to read as follows:
     7    (c)    Powers  and  duties of the cemetery board.  With respect to any
     8  cemetery or cemetery corporation, the  cemetery  board  shall  have  the
     9  following  duties  and powers:   (1)  To adopt such reasonable rules and
    10  regulations as the cemetery board shall deem necessary  for  the  proper
    11  administration  of  this article.  (2) To order any cemetery corporation
    12  to do such acts as may be necessary to comply  with  the  provisions  of
    13  this  article or any rule or regulation adopted by the cemetery board or
    14  to refrain from doing any act in violation thereof.   (2-a)    To  adopt
    15  reasonable  rules  and regulations to exempt those cemetery corporations
    16  from the provisions of paragraph (h) of section fifteen hundred  ten  of
    17  this [chapter] article which because of a limited number of paid employ-
    18  ees  or  appropriate  resources are unable to carry out such provisions.
    19  (2-b) To adopt reasonable rules and regulations to extend the time peri-
    20  od mandated by the  provisions  of  paragraph  (h)  of  section  fifteen
    21  hundred  ten of this [chapter] article when necessary because compliance
    22  by a cemetery corporation within such time period is impossible. (3)  To
    23  enforce  its orders by mandamus or injunction in a summary proceeding or
    24  otherwise. In connection with such action or  proceeding,  the  attorney

    13    (e) Complaint process. Upon receipt of a consumer complaint form,  the

    18  cemeteries within ten business  days  after  receipt  of  such  consumer

    21  the cemetery board for investigation  and  resolution  thereof.  When  a
    22  complaint  is  escalated,  the board may take any action it deems neces-
    23  sary, consistent with the powers and duties of the cemetery board pursu-

    27  profit  corporation law, as added by chapter 608 of the laws of 1985, is
    28  amended to read as follows:
    29    (1) [A] Any cemetery corporation may, and  all  cemetery  corporations
    30  partially  or  wholly located in a city with a population of one million
    31  or more shall, subject to the approval of the cemetery board,  establish
    32  and maintain a monument maintenance fund. Such a fund is hereby declared
    33  to be and shall be held by the cemetery corporation as a trust fund, for
    34  the purpose of providing notice if such monuments are damaged or defaced
    35  by an act of vandalism and for the restoration of such monuments. Two or
    36  more  cemetery  corporations  may establish a joint monument maintenance
    37  fund.
    38    § 3. Paragraph (a) of section 1510 of the  not-for-profit  corporation
    39  law, as amended by chapter 20 of the laws of 2021, is amended to read as
    40  follows:
    41    (a)  Posting and distribution of rules, regulations, charges and pric-
    42  es. The rules, regulations, charges, and prices of goods, lots, plots or
    43  parts thereof, including the corporation's regulations  of  the  spacing
    44  between  plots,  lots,  or  parts thereof, shall be suitably printed and
    45  shall be conspicuously posted by the corporation in each of its offices,
    46  if any, and conspicuously displayed on any website the corporation main-
    47  tains or through which it conducts business. A printed copy  of  charges
    48  and  prices  of goods, lots, plots or parts thereof shall be made avail-
    49  able upon request by any person for up to the actual price of the print-
    50  ing of the copy. For each day in which the corporation fails to post  or
    51  display the rules, regulations, charges and prices, including the corpo-
    52  ration's regulations of the spacing between plots, lots, or parts there-
    53  of, the corporation shall be subject to a penalty of twenty-five dollars
    54  which may be recovered in a civil action by the cemetery board. For each
    55  instance  in which the corporation fails to make available a copy of the
    56  prices of goods, lots, plots, or parts  thereof,  including  the  corpo-

     1  ration's regulations of the spacing between plots, lots, or parts there-
     2  of, to a person who requests such copy, the corporation shall be subject
     3  to  a  penalty  of twenty-five dollars which may be recovered in a civil
     4  action  by  the cemetery board. The cemetery board may waive the payment
     5  of the penalty or any part thereof.
     6    § 4. Paragraph (b) of section 1513 of the  not-for-profit  corporation
     7  law,  as added by chapter 871 of the laws of 1977, is amended to read as
     8  follows:
     9    (b) Prices for burial rights and instruments of  conveyance.  (1)  The
    10  directors must fix and determine the prices of the burial lots, plots or
    11  parts  thereof, and keep a plainly printed copy of the schedules of such
    12  prices conspicuously posted in each of the offices of  the  corporation,
    13  open at all reasonable times to inspection, and shall file a schedule of
    14  such  prices in the office of the cemetery board. (2) Unless its certif-
    15  icate of incorporation or by-laws otherwise provide, and subject to  its
    16  rules  and  regulations,  the  corporation  shall sell and convey to any
    17  person the use of the lots, plots or parts thereof designated on the map
    18  filed in the office of the corporation, on  payment  of  the  prices  so
    19  fixed  and  determined,  but need not sell and convey more than one lot,
    20  plot or part thereof to any one person. Conveyances of lots,  plots  and
    21  parts  thereof  shall  be  signed by the president or vice-president and
    22  treasurer or assistant treasurer of the corporation. A written  contract
    23  for  the  sale or use of a lot, plot or part thereof shall have attached
    24  thereto and made a part thereof a copy of the rules and  regulations  of
    25  the  cemetery corporation or such parts of such rules and regulations as
    26  relate to the size and placement  of  monuments,  restrictions  on  plot
    27  usage, warranties, obligations of the cemetery corporation and financial
    28  obligations, dimensions of spacing between lots, plots, or parts thereof
    29  as  per  the  corporation's rules and regulations, and duties of the lot
    30  owner. If a lot,  plot  or  part  thereof  is  sold  without  a  written
    31  contract,  the  corporation shall, before any part of the purchase price
    32  is paid by the purchaser, deliver to the purchaser a copy of  the  rules
    33  and  regulations  or  such  parts  thereof  as  would  be required to be
    34  attached to a  written  contract.  Nothing  in  this  subdivision  shall
    35  prevent  the  subsequent  amendment  of  such  rules  and regulations to
    36  increase the charges for services rendered  by  the  corporation  or  in
    37  other  particulars  by  or  with the consent of the cemetery board under
    38  section fifteen hundred nine of this article. (3) A cemetery corporation
    39  that shall sell a lot, plot or part thereof,  in  excess  of  the  price
    40  shown on the schedule filed in the office of the cemetery board, and any
    41  person acting for or on behalf of the cemetery corporation in connection
    42  with  such  sale,  shall  each forfeit to the people of the state of New
    43  York a sum equivalent to three times the excess  amount  so  paid.  Such
    44  penalty  may  be  recovered in a civil action by the cemetery board. (4)
    45  The instrument of conveyance of any burial lot,  plot  or  part  thereof
    46  shall  include the actual amount paid therefor and a description showing
    47  the dimensions of the property conveyed, and the  plot  number,  section
    48  and  block  number  as  they appear on the cemetery map. Such instrument
    49  shall also include the dimensions of spacing  between  plots,  lots,  or
    50  parts thereof as determined by the corporation's rules and regulations.
    51    §  5.  This  act shall take effect on the thirtieth day after it shall
    52  have become a law.
