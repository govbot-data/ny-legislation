COMRIE -- (at request of the NYC Finance) -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Rules AN ACT to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to notices of violation returnable to a parking violations bureau in a city with a population of one million or more The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Subdivision 2 of section 238 of the vehicle and traffic 2 law, as amended by chapter 224 of the laws of 1995, is amended to read 3 as follows: 4 2. A notice of violation shall be served personally upon the operator 5 of a motor vehicle who is present at the time of service, and [his] such 6 operator's name, together with the plate designation and the plate type 7 as shown by the registration plates of said vehicle and the expiration 8 date, provided that in a city with a population of one million persons 9 or more, if such notice is served upon the operator of a vehicle that 10 has no such registration plate issued by the commissioner, the commis- 11 sioner's agent, or the equivalent official or agent from another state, 12 territory, district, province, nation or other jurisdiction, or if such 13 registration plate is concealed or obscured, the vehicle identification 14 number may be inserted in such notice in place of the plate designation 15 and plate type; the make or model, and, provided that a body type is 16 indicated on the registration sticker of said vehicle, the body type of 17 said vehicle; a description of the charged violation, including but not 18 limited to a reference to the applicable traffic rule or provision of 19 this chapter; information as to the days and hours the applicable rule 20 or provision of this chapter is in effect, unless always in effect 21 pursuant to rule or this chapter and where appropriate the word ALL when 22 the days and/or hours in effect are everyday and/or twenty-four hours a 23 day; the meter number for a meter violation, where appropriate; and the EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted.
