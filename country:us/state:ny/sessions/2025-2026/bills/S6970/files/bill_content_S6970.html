Amd §§1604 & 1607, Tax L
 
Requires the return of unsold instant lottery game tickets to the division of the lottery when a licensed agent goes out of business, sells their business or if their license is suspended or revoked.

        AN  ACT to amend the tax law, in relation to unsold instant lottery game
          tickets
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  Subdivision b of section 1604 of the tax law, as added by
     2  chapter 92 of the laws of 1976, is amended to read as follows:
     3    b. 1. The division is authorized to license agents to sell tickets for
     4  the state lottery. The division may require a  bond  from  any  licensed
     5  agent,  in  an  amount  to be determined by the division. Every licensed
     6  agent shall prominently display [his] their license, or a copy  thereof,
     7  as  may  be determined by rules and regulations of the division, in each
     8  place of business or activity where lottery tickets are sold.
     9    2. When a licensed agent goes out of business or sells their  business
    10  to another person as defined in section one thousand six hundred five of
    11  this  article, their license shall expire and any unsold instant lottery
    12  game tickets in their possession shall be returned to the  division  and
    13  redistributed  to  other  licensed  sales  agents,  unless  such instant
    14  lottery game has been terminated by the commission.  For the purposes of
    15  this subdivision, "instant lottery game" shall  mean  any  lottery  game
    16  conducted  on a game ticket authorized or  issued by the commission that
    17  includes one or more instant play  features, such as  rub-off  coatings,
    18  pull-tabs,  or  other concealed play  areas, which, when revealed, indi-
    19  cate whether the player has won a  prize.
    20    § 2. Section 1607 of the tax law, as amended by chapter 2 of the  laws
    21  of  1995,  subdivision  g  as  added by chapter 701 of the laws of 1996,
    22  subdivision h as amended by chapter 162 of the laws of 2002, paragraph 1
    23  of subdivision h as amended by section 7 of part EE of chapter 56 of the
    24  laws of 2020, is amended to read as follows:

     1    § 1607. Suspension and revocation of licenses.  a.  The  division  may
     2  suspend  or revoke, after notice and an opportunity for a hearing pursu-
     3  ant to the state administrative procedure act, any license issued pursu-
     4  ant to this article. Such license may, however, be temporarily suspended
     5  by  the division without prior notice, pending any prosecution, investi-
     6  gation or hearing. A license may be suspended or revoked by the division
     7  for one or more of the following reasons:
     8    [a.] 1. Failure  to  account  for  lottery  tickets  received  or  the
     9  proceeds of the sale of lottery tickets or to file a bond if required by
    10  the  division  or to comply with instructions of the division concerning
    11  the licensed activity;
    12    [b.] 2. Conviction of any offense as defined in the penal law, or  any
    13  felony  under  the  laws  of  the United States or under the laws of the
    14  state of New York or any other state  or  foreign  jurisdiction  or  any
    15  crime relating to gaming activities;
    16    [c.]  3.  Failure  to file any return or report, to keep records or to
    17  pay any tax required by any article of this chapter;
    18    [d.] 4. Fraud, deceit, misrepresentation  or  conduct  prejudicial  to
    19  public confidence in the state lottery;
    20    [e.]  5.  With respect to a lottery sales agent, the number of lottery
    21  tickets sold by the lottery sales agent is insufficient to meet adminis-
    22  trative costs and public  convenience  is  adequately  served  by  other
    23  licensees;
    24    [f.] 6. A material change, since issuance of the license, with respect
    25  to  any matters required to be considered by the division under subdivi-
    26  sion d or e of section sixteen hundred five of this article[.];
    27    [g.] 7. Conviction for misuse of food stamps pursuant to  section  one
    28  hundred forty-seven of the social services law[.]; or
    29    [h.]  8. A violation of article thirteen-F of the public health law as
    30  provided in this subdivision:
    31    [1.] (i) A license shall be suspended for a period of  one  year  upon
    32  notification  to the division by the commissioner of health of a lottery
    33  sales agent's accumulation of three or more points pursuant to  subdivi-
    34  sion  three  of  section  thirteen  hundred ninety-nine-ee of the public
    35  health law.
    36    [2.] (ii) A license shall be revoked for one year upon notification to
    37  the division by the commissioner of health of a  lottery  sales  agent's
    38  fourth violation of article thirteen-F of the public health law within a
    39  three year period.
    40    b.  In any case in which a sales agent's license has been suspended or
    41  revoked, any unsold instant lottery game tickets shall  be  returned  to
    42  the  division  and redistributed to other licensed sales agents for sale
    43  to the public, unless such instant lottery game has been  terminated  by
    44  the commission. For the purposes of this section, "instant lottery game"
    45  shall  mean  any  lottery  game conducted on a game ticket authorized or
    46  issued by  the  commission  that  includes  one  or  more  instant  play
    47  features,  such  as rub-off coatings, pull-tabs, or other concealed play
    48  areas, which, when revealed, indicate  whether  the  player  has  won  a
    49  prize.
    50    § 3. This act shall take effect immediately.
