Add Art 75 §7500, §§7505-a, 7517 & 7518, amd §§7501, 7506, 7507 & 7511, CPLR
 
Requires employment and consumer dispute arbitrations to be submitted to neutral third party arbitrators; establishes prohibited arbitration agreements and provisions; requires disclosure of information by certain arbitrators.

        AN ACT to amend the civil practice law and rules, in relation  to  arbi-
          tration agreements

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Article 75 of the civil practice law and rules  is  amended
     2  by adding a new section 7500 to read as follows:
     3    § 7500. Definitions. As used in this article:
     4    (a) "Arbitration" means a form of dispute resolution that is an alter-
     5  native  to  litigation,  in  which  the parties agree to be bound by the
     6  determination of a neutral third party arbitrator.
     7    (b) "Neutral third party arbitrator" means an arbitrator or  panel  of
     8  arbitrators  each  of whom does not have an undisclosed known, direct or
     9  material interest, including a financial or  personal  interest  in  the
    10  outcome  of  the  arbitration  proceeding,  or a known, existing or past
    11  relationship with any of the parties to the agreement  to  arbitrate  or
    12  the arbitration proceeding, their counsel or representatives, a witness,
    13  or another arbitrator.
    14    (c)  "Employment"  means  a  relationship  between  an employer and an
    15  employee as defined in section three of the Fair Labor Standards Act  of
    16  1938 (29 U.S.C. § 203).
    17    (d) "Consumer" means a natural person residing in this state.
    18    (e)  "Consumer goods" means goods, wares, paid merchandise or services
    19  purchased or paid for by a consumer, the  intended  use  or  benefit  of
    20  which is intended for the personal, family or household purposes of such
    21  consumer.
    22    §  2.  Section 7501 of the civil practice law and rules, as amended by
    23  chapter 532 of the laws of 1963, is amended to read as follows:

     1    § 7501. Effect of arbitration  agreement.    A  written  agreement  to
     2  submit any controversy thereafter arising or any existing controversy to
     3  arbitration  is  enforceable without regard to the justiciable character
     4  of the controversy and confers jurisdiction on the courts of  the  state
     5  to enforce it and to enter judgment on an award; provided, however, that
     6  any  portion  of  an  agreement  or  contract  requiring the controversy
     7  concerning employment or the providing of consumer goods be submitted to
     8  an arbitrator or arbitration organization that is not  a  neutral  third
     9  party  arbitrator,  shall  be  deemed  void.    The requirement that the
    10  controversy be heard by a neutral third  party  arbitrator  may  not  be
    11  waived  by  a  party  prior to the service on such party of a demand for
    12  arbitration. Upon disclosure pursuant to  section  seventy-five  hundred
    13  five-a  of  this  article,  a  party  shall be deemed to have waived any
    14  objection to the arbitrator or composition of any arbitration panel,  by
    15  failing to raise same prior to the commencement of the arbitration hear-
    16  ing.  In  determining  any  matter arising under this article, the court
    17  shall not consider whether the claim with respect to  which  arbitration
    18  is sought is tenable, or otherwise pass upon the merits of the dispute.
    19    Arbitration  of  any employment or consumer dispute shall be conducted
    20  by a neutral third party arbitrator.    Appointment  of  any  arbitrator
    21  shall  reasonably  ensure the personal objectivity of the arbitrator and
    22  the right of each party to present its case, to be in attendance  during
    23  any  presentation  made  by  the other party and to rebut or refute such
    24  presentation.
    25    § 3. The civil practice law and rules  is  amended  by  adding  a  new
    26  section 7505-a to read as follows:
    27    §  7505-a. Disclosure by arbitrator. (a) Before accepting appointment,
    28  an individual who is requested to serve as an arbitrator, after making a
    29  reasonable inquiry, shall disclose to all parties to  the  agreement  to
    30  arbitrate  and  the arbitration proceeding, and to any other arbitrators
    31  any known facts that a reasonable person would consider likely to affect
    32  the impartiality  of  the  arbitrator  in  the  arbitration  proceeding,
    33  including:
    34    (1) a financial or personal interest in the outcome of the arbitration
    35  proceeding; and
    36    (2)  an  existing  or past relationship with any of the parties to the
    37  agreement to arbitrate or the arbitration proceeding, their  counsel  or
    38  representatives, a witness, or another arbitrator.
    39    (b)  An  arbitrator  has  a  continuing  obligation to disclose to all
    40  parties to the agreement to arbitrate and  the  arbitration  proceeding,
    41  and  to any other arbitrators any facts that the arbitrator learns after
    42  accepting appointment which a reasonable person would consider likely to
    43  affect the impartiality of the arbitrator.
    44    (c) If an arbitrator discloses a fact required by subdivision  (a)  or
    45  (b)  of  this  section  and a party timely objects to the appointment or
    46  continued service of the arbitrator based upon the fact  disclosed,  the
    47  objection may be a ground for vacating an award made by the arbitrator.
    48    (d)  If the arbitrator did not disclose a fact as required by subdivi-
    49  sion (a) or (b) of this section, upon timely objection by a  party,  the
    50  court may vacate an award based on such non-disclosure.
    51    (e)  An  arbitrator  appointed  as  a  neutral arbitrator who does not
    52  disclose a known, direct and material interest in  the  outcome  of  the
    53  arbitration proceeding or a known, existing and substantial relationship
    54  with  a party is presumed to act with evident partiality in rendering an
    55  award.

     1    § 4. Section 7506 of the civil practice law and rules  is  amended  to
     2  read as follows:
     3    § 7506. Hearing. (a) Oath of arbitrator. Before hearing any testimony,
     4  an  arbitrator  shall be sworn to hear and decide the controversy faith-
     5  fully and fairly by an officer authorized to administer an oath.
     6    (b) Time and place. The arbitrator shall appoint a time and place  for
     7  the  hearing  and  notify the parties in writing personally or by regis-
     8  tered or certified mail not less than eight days before the hearing. The
     9  arbitrator may adjourn or postpone the hearing. The court, upon applica-
    10  tion of any party, may direct the arbitrator to  proceed  promptly  with
    11  the hearing and determination of the controversy.
    12    (c)  Evidence.  The  parties  are  entitled  to  be  heard, to present
    13  evidence and to cross-examine witnesses. Notwithstanding the failure  of
    14  a  party duly  notified to appear, the arbitrator may hear and determine
    15  the controversy upon the evidence produced.
    16    (d) Postponements and adjournments.  The arbitrator may for good cause
    17  postpone or adjourn the hearing upon request of  a  party  or  upon  the
    18  arbitrator's  own  initiative.  If  a party to an arbitration intends to
    19  present testimony from a witness  at  the  hearing,  absent  good  cause
    20  shown, the identity of the witness must be given to all parties at least
    21  seven calendar days prior to the hearing.
    22    (e)  Representation  by  attorney. A party has the right to be repres-
    23  ented by an attorney and may claim such right at any time as to any part
    24  of the arbitration or hearings which have not taken  place.  This  right
    25  may  not  be waived. If a party is represented by an attorney, papers to
    26  be served on the party shall be served upon [his] the party's  attorney.
    27  It  shall  be discretionary with the arbitrator to permit the attendance
    28  of any other persons.
    29    [(e)] (f) Determination by majority. The hearing shall be conducted by
    30  all the arbitrators, but a  majority  may  determine  any  question  and
    31  render an award.
    32    [(f)]  (g)  Waiver.  Except  as  provided  in  subdivision [(d)](e), a
    33  requirement of this section may be waived  by  written  consent  of  the
    34  parties  and  it  is waived if the parties continue with the arbitration
    35  without objection.
    36    § 5. Section 7507 of the civil practice law and rules, as  amended  by
    37  chapter 952 of the laws of 1981, is amended to read as follows:
    38    §  7507.  Award;  form;  time;  delivery.    (a) Except as provided in
    39  section 7508, the award shall be in writing, signed and affirmed by  the
    40  arbitrator  making it within the time fixed by the agreement, or, if the
    41  time is not fixed, within such time as the court orders.
    42    (b) In a matter involving a consumer  arbitration  or  an  arbitration
    43  between  an employer and an employee, as defined in section three of the
    44  Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938 (29 U.S.C. §  203),  where  arbitration
    45  was  held  pursuant  to  a contract, the award shall state the issues in
    46  dispute and shall contain the arbitrator's findings of fact and  conclu-
    47  sions of law. The award shall contain a decision on all issues submitted
    48  to the arbitrator.
    49    (c)  The parties may in writing extend the time either before or after
    50  its expiration. A party waives the objection that an award was not  made
    51  within  the  time required unless [he] the party notifies the arbitrator
    52  in writing of [his] the party's objection prior to the delivery  of  the
    53  award [to him].
    54    (d)  The arbitrator shall deliver a copy of the award to each party in
    55  the manner provided in the agreement, or, if no provision  is  so  made,
    56  personally or by registered or certified mail, return receipt requested.

     1    §  6.  Subparagraph  (iv) of paragraph 1 of subdivision (b) of section
     2  7511 of the civil practice law and rules is amended and a  new  subpara-
     3  graph (v) is added to read as follows:
     4    (iv) failure to follow the procedure of this article, unless the party
     5  applying  to vacate the award continued with the arbitration with notice
     6  of the defect and without objection[.]; or
     7    (v) the arbitrator evidenced  a  manifest  disregard  of  the  law  in
     8  rendering the award.
     9    §  7.  The  civil  practice law and rules is amended by adding two new
    10  sections 7517 and 7518 to read as follows:
    11    § 7517. Prohibited  predispute  arbitration  agreements.    (a)  Defi-
    12  nitions.  (i)  The  term  "consumer  dispute" means a dispute between an
    13  individual who seeks or acquires real  or  personal  property,  services
    14  (including  services  relating  to  securities  and  other investments),
    15  money, or credit for personal, family  or  household  purposes  and  the
    16  seller or provider of such property, services, money or credit.
    17    (ii) The term "employment dispute" means a dispute between an employer
    18  and employee arising out of the relationship of employer and employee as
    19  defined  in  section  three  of the Fair Labor Standards Act of 1938 (29
    20  U.S.C. § 203).
    21    (iii) The term "predispute arbitration agreement" means any  agreement
    22  to arbitrate a dispute that had not yet arisen at the time of the making
    23  of the agreement.
    24    (b) Prohibition.  Notwithstanding any other provision of this article,
    25  no  predispute arbitration agreement shall be valid or enforceable if it
    26  requires arbitration of an employment dispute or consumer dispute.
    27    § 7518. Prohibited provisions.  (a) Prohibition of effect  of  certain
    28  arbitration  clauses  or  agreements.   Mandatory arbitration clauses or
    29  agreements covering consumers and employees are contrary to  the  estab-
    30  lished  public policy of this state. Because employees and consumers are
    31  forced to assent to these agreements as a condition of being an employee
    32  or consumer before any dispute has arisen with the employer or merchant,
    33  these agreements do not  offer  employees  and  consumers  a  meaningful
    34  choice  about  how  to  resolve  their  disputes  with  the  employer or
    35  merchant. In addition, mandatory arbitration agreements prevent  employ-
    36  ees  and consumers from effectively vindicating their rights under state
    37  law. For these reasons, except when inconsistent with federal  law,  the
    38  state  prohibits  the formation and enforcement of mandatory arbitration
    39  agreements in employment and consumer contracts.
    40    (b) Prohibition of arbitration clauses  in  employment  contracts  for
    41  workers  exempted  from  the Federal Arbitration Act.  A mandatory arbi-
    42  tration agreement within or part of any written contract  of  employment
    43  of  seamen,  railroad employees or any other class of workers engaged in
    44  foreign or interstate commerce is unenforceable and void. Any such arbi-
    45  tration  agreement  shall  be  considered  severable,  and   all   other
    46  provisions  of  the employment contract shall remain in effect and given
    47  full force.
    48    (c) Prohibition of arbitration clauses that are not governed by feder-
    49  al law.  Any mandatory arbitration agreement, or portion thereof, in  an
    50  employment or consumer contract is invalid, unenforceable and void, when
    51  the  enforceability  of  such  arbitration  agreement, or the portion at
    52  issue, is governed by state law. Any such arbitration agreement shall be
    53  considered  severable,  and  all  other  provisions  of  the  employment
    54  contract shall remain in effect and given full force.

     1    (d)  Exclusion.  The  provisions  of  this  section shall not apply to
     2  agreements negotiated with any labor union through  collective  bargain-
     3  ing.
     4    §  8.  Enforcement.   Any private person and any enforcement agency or
     5  official responsible for enforcing the provisions of this act may  bring
     6  suit  for  injunctive  relief  against  an  entity  that  violates  such
     7  provisions, and may recover reasonable attorney fees and other costs  if
     8  an  injunction  or equivalent relief is awarded. Injunctive relief shall
     9  be the only relief available in a suit arising from  failure  to  comply
    10  with this act.
    11    §  9.  Severability.   If any provision of this act or the application
    12  thereof to any person or circumstance is held invalid,  such  invalidity
    13  shall  not  affect other provisions or applications of this act that can
    14  be given effect without the invalid provision  or  application,  and  to
    15  that end the provisions of this act are declared to be severable.
    16    § 10. This act shall take effect on the first of January next succeed-
    17  ing the date on which it shall have become a law.
