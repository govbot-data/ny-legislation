AN ACT to amend the transportation law, in relation to establishing the
New York road salt reduction council and the New York road salt
reduction advisory committee
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. The transportation law is amended by adding a new article
2 23 to read as follows:
3 ARTICLE 23
4 NEW YORK ROAD SALT REDUCTION PROGRAM
5 Section 500. Legislative findings.
6 501. Definitions.
7 502. New York road salt reduction council.
8 503. New York road salt reduction advisory committee.
9 § 500. Legislative findings. The legislature finds that the report of
10 the Adirondack road salt reduction task force complies with the Randy
11 Preston road salt reduction act, and that its recommendations should be
12 implemented to protect public health and the environment from contam-
13 ination by road salt. The legislature further finds that effective
14 implementation requires interagency cooperation and coordination, atten-
15 tion extending beyond the boundaries of the Adirondack park. A mechanism
16 for such does not currently exist but should be created. The legislature
17 further recognizes that both state and local agencies need the expertise
18 and experience of scientists, engineers, nongovernmental institutions
19 advocating for clean water, highway users, and others in developing

7 § 501. Definitions. As used in this article the following terms shall
8 have the following meanings:
9 1. "Advisory committee" means the New York road salt reduction advi-

13 section five hundred two of this article.
14 3. "Pilot program" means the road salt reduction pilot studies
15 described in the Report.
16 4. "Report" means the two thousand twenty-three report of the task
17 force to the governor and legislature, including its recommendations and
18 background and technical appendix.
19 5. "Task force" means the Adirondack road salt reduction task force
20 established under the Randy Preston road salt reduction act.
21 § 502. New York road salt reduction council. 1. There is hereby estab-
22 lished the New York road salt reduction council. Such council shall
23 consist of a total of fifteen members and shall include:
24 (a) the commissioner;
25 (b) the commissioner of environmental conservation;
26 (c) the commissioner of health;
27 (d) the chairperson of the New York state thruway authority;
28 (e) the commissioner of the New York city department of environmental
29 protection;
30 (f) the presidents of the New York state association of counties, New
31 York association of towns and the New York conference of mayors;
32 (g) three appointees upon recommendation of the senate majority;
33 (h) three appointees upon recommendation of the assembly majority; and
34 (i) the chair of the road salt reduction advisory committee.
35 With the exception of the chair of the road salt reduction advisory
36 committee and any members appointed by the legislature, all other
37 members may appoint a designee to serve on their behalf.
38 2. The commissioner and the commissioner of environmental conservation
39 or their designees shall serve as joint chairs of the council.
40 3. The members of the council shall include individuals with expertise
41 in the science of road salt contamination and the best practices of road
42 management. Council members shall receive no compensation for their
43 services but shall be reimbursed for actual and necessary expenses
44 incurred in the performance of their duties, to the extent funds are
45 appropriated for such purpose. Each member of the council shall be enti-
46 tled to one vote. Any approvals or adoptions of recommendations by the
47 council shall require a supermajority of its members. No action may be
48 taken by the council unless there is a quorum, which shall be a majority
49 of the members of the council. Any vacancies on the council shall be
50 filled in the manner provided for the initial appointment. The council
51 shall meet at least quarterly.
52 4. The council shall, without limitation:
53 (a) From time to time assess the nature, scope and magnitude of the
54 environmental, ecological, agricultural, economic, recreational, and
55 social impacts caused by the use of road salt in the state;

36 § 503. New York road salt reduction advisory committee. 1. There is
37 hereby established the New York road salt reduction advisory committee
38 that is comprised of individuals representing nongovernmental organiza-
39 tions with expertise in public health and environmental impacts of road
40 salt use, expertise in data collection and analysis of water quality
41 standards or drinking water maximum contaminant levels, or technical
42 proficiency in safely reducing overall road salt use. Such advisory
43 committee shall have up to twenty-five members, with up to seven members
44 selected by the commissioner and commissioners of environmental conser-
45 vation and health, up to six members selected by the senate majority, up
46 to six members selected by the assembly majority, up to three members
47 selected by the senate minority and up to three members selected by the
48 assembly minority, with at least one member from each of the following:
49 (a) Adirondack watershed institute at Paul Smith's college;
50 (b) College of agriculture and life sciences of Cornell university;
51 (c) Riverkeeper;
52 (d) Lake George association;
53 (e) any New York based branch of the American Automobile Association;
54 (f) Snow and ice management association;
55 (g) the New York section of the American water works association;
56 (h) the New York state drinking water quality council;
