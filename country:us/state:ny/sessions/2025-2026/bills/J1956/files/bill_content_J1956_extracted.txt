Caldwell upon the occasion of his retirement after many years of distinguished service to the people of the State of New York WHEREAS, It is the sense of this Legislative Body to recognize and honor those distinguished citizens who have devoted their lives to public service and, through their dedication and professionalism, have enhanced the institutions they serve; and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and in full accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to congratulate Randal B. Caldwell upon the occasion of his retirement, marking the culmination of a long and exemplary career in public service; and WHEREAS, Randal B. Caldwell has rendered faithful, conscientious, and valuable service to the State of New York and to Oneida County over the course of decades, earning the admiration and respect of colleagues, litigants, and the community at large; and WHEREAS, Randal B. Caldwell served with distinction as a Family Court Judge, Acting Supreme Court Justice, and Supervising Family Court Judge for the Fifth Judicial District, presiding over complex and sensitive matters with fairness, integrity, and compassion; and WHEREAS, During his judicial tenure, he presided over the Juvenile Treatment Court from 2008 to 2015 and served as Lead Judge for Oneida County in the Child Welfare Court Improvement Project's Initiative to Enhance Court Practices in Child Welfare Proceedings, demonstrating a deep commitment to improving outcomes for children and families; and WHEREAS, He further served as Chairman of the Oneida County Arrest Diversion Governing Board, as a Family Court representative on the Fifth Judicial District State Standards and Goals Committee, and as Judicial Representative on the Oneida County Courthouse Renovation Committee; and WHEREAS, Randal B. Caldwell also served as Acting Surrogate Judge of Oneida County in 2009, and as Acting Supreme Court Justice presiding over matrimonial matters, the Integrated Domestic Violence Court, and as Youth Part Judge for Oneida County in Raise the Age matters; and WHEREAS, In addition to his judicial service, he shared his knowledge and experience as an Adjunct Professor at Mohawk Valley Community College from 2007 to 2015, and at Utica College from 2013 to 2014, teaching courses in government, law, and criminal justice; and WHEREAS, Prior to his judicial career, Randal B. Caldwell served as County Attorney for Oneida County from 2000 to 2006, acting as Chief Legal Officer and providing counsel to county government while supervising a wide range of legal matters; and WHEREAS, Randal B. Caldwell's distinguished legal career also includes service as Town Attorney for the Towns of Marcy and Deerfield, private practice as a general partner and sole practitioner, and as an Assistant District Attorney in Oneida County, where he prosecuted numerous serious criminal cases; and WHEREAS, He began his legal career as a Law Clerk to the Honorable Edward L. Snyder in the Court of Common Pleas in Brookville, Pennsylvania; and WHEREAS, Randal B. Caldwell earned his Juris Doctor from Ohio Northern University Law School, where he served as Associate Editor of the Law Review, and received his Bachelor of Arts in Political Science from Utica College of Syracuse University; and WHEREAS, His commitment to service extends beyond the courtroom, as evidenced by his decades-long membership in the Maynard Volunteer Fire Department, where he has held numerous leadership roles and currently serves as Assistant Chief, EMT, and RIT Commander; and WHEREAS, Randal B. Caldwell has generously provided legal counsel to several firefighter organizations and has been actively involved in youth athletics as a coach and board member in local baseball and hockey associations; and WHEREAS, Furthermore, in 2005, he was the recipient of a Letter of Appreciation from the New York State Police for his assistance in a crisis negotiation situation, further exemplifying his dedication to public safety; and WHEREAS, Throughout his illustrious career, Randal B. Caldwell has demonstrated an unwavering commitment to justice, public service, and the betterment of his community, leaving an enduring legacy of professionalism and integrity; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to congratulate Randal B. Caldwell upon the occasion of his retirement, and to express its deepest gratitude for his outstanding service to the State of New York; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Randal B.
