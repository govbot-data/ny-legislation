WALCZYK -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Ethics and Internal Gover- nance AN ACT to amend the public officers law, in relation to crimes against public trust by state officers The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited as "the halt 2 crimes against public trust act". 3 § 2. The public officers law is amended by adding a new section 79-a 4 to read as follows: 5 § 79-a. Crimes against public trust by state officers. 1. (a) Any 6 state officer indicted for a crime involving public trust shall have 7 their salary for such official position suspended pending such indict- 8 ment. 9 (b) Where the indictment results in a conviction of a crime against 10 public trust, all monies earned by such state officer for such official 11 position beginning with the pay period immediately proceeding the date 12 upon which the first convicted offense occurred shall be paid back to 13 the state by such state officer. 14 (c) Where the indictment does not result in a conviction of a crime 15 against public trust, such suspension shall cease and such state officer 16 shall be paid back all monies not paid to such state officer during such 17 suspension. 18 2.
