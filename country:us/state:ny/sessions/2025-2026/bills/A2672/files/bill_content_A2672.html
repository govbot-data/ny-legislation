        AN ACT to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to including if
          a  person  has autism spectrum disorder on their driver's license; and
          to repeal certain provisions of such law relating thereto
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  Section  490 of the vehicle and traffic law is amended by
     2  adding a new subdivision 2-a to read as follows:
     3    2-a. Autism spectrum disorder. In addition to the provisions of subdi-
     4  vision two of this section, the commissioner shall ensure that space  is
     5  provided on the application so that the applicant shall indicate if they
     6  have  autism  spectrum disorder and that the following is typed in clear
     7  and conspicuous type:
     8    "You must fill out the following section: Are you a person with autism
     9  spectrum disorder? Check box for 'yes' or 'skip this question'."
    10    The commissioner shall  make  whatever  provisions  are  feasible  and
    11  desirable  to  maintain  and  issue  all  suitable  documents  regarding
    12  advanced directives at driver's license examining offices.
    13    § 2.  Paragraph (b) of subdivision 3 of section 490 of the vehicle and
    14  traffic law, as amended by section 97 of part PP of chapter  56  of  the

    19  and  shall  contain  such  other information and shall be issued in such

    21  tification card or renewal thereof issued to a person with autism  spec-

    23  identification  card or renewal thereof issued to a person under the age

    29  REPEALED and a new subdivision 1 is added to read as follows:
    30    1. Application for license. Application for a driver's  license  shall
    31  be  made to the commissioner. The fee prescribed by law may be submitted
    32  with such application. The applicant shall furnish such proof of identi-
    33  ty, age, and fitness as may be  required  by  the  commissioner.    With
    34  respect  to  a non-commercial driver's license or learner's permit which
    35  does not meet federal standards for identification, in addition  to  the
    36  acceptable proofs of age and identity approved by the commissioner as of
    37  January  first,  two  thousand twenty-five, acceptable proof of identity
    38  shall also include, but not be limited to, a  valid,  unexpired  foreign
    39  passport  issued  by the applicant's country of citizenship (which shall
    40  also be eligible as proof of age), a valid, unexpired consular identifi-
    41  cation document issued by a consulate from the  applicant's  country  of
    42  citizenship,  or  a valid foreign driver's license that includes a photo
    43  image of the applicant and which is unexpired or expired for  less  than
    44  twenty-four  months  of its date of expiration, as primary forms of such
    45  proof. Nothing contained in this subdivision shall be deemed to preclude
    46  the commissioner from approving additional proofs of identity  and  age.
    47  The  commissioner  may also provide that the application procedure shall
    48  include the taking of a photo  image  or  images  of  the  applicant  in
    49  accordance with rules and regulations prescribed by the commissioner. In
    50  addition, the commissioner also shall require that the applicant provide
    51  their  social  security  number  or, in lieu thereof, with respect to an
    52  application for a non-commercial driver's license  or  learner's  permit
    53  which  does  not meet federal standards for identification, an affidavit
    54  signed by such applicant that they have not been issued a social securi-
    55  ty number. The commissioner also shall provide space on the  application
    56  so  that  the  applicant  may  register  in the New York state organ and
