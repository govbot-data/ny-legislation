        AN  ACT  to amend the education law, in relation to the requirements for
          certification for certified interior designers
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1. Section 8305 of the education law, as added by chapter 905
     2  of the laws of 1990, paragraph (b) of subdivision 2 as amended by  chap-
     3  ter 653 of the laws of 1993, is amended to read as follows:
     4    § 8305. Requirements  for  certification.    1. To qualify for certif-
     5  ication to use the title "certified  interior  designer",  an  applicant
     6  shall fulfill all the following requirements:
     7    (a) Application: file an application with the department;
     8    (b)  Education:  have  [received  at least seven years of professional
     9  training consisting of academic study and work  experience  relating  to
    10  interior  design  and in accordance with the commissioner's regulations.
    11  These seven years shall contain at least two  but  not  more  than  five
    12  years  of post secondary education, including an associate degree or the
    13  equivalent, in an approved program of interior design]  met  the  educa-
    14  tional  requirements  required  to  sit  for an examination offered by a
    15  national certifying  organization  for  interior  design  professionals,
    16  provided  that  such  certifying organization is approved by the commis-
    17  sioner;
    18    (c) Experience: have met experience [satisfactory to the board and  in
    19  accordance  with  the  commissioner's  regulations]  requirements deemed
    20  necessary to sit for an examination offered  by  a  national  certifying
    21  organization for interior design professionals provided that such certi-
    22  fying organization is approved by the commissioner;

     1    (d)  Examination: (1) pass an examination satisfactory to the board in
     2  accordance with the commissioner's regulations, and (2) [pass a separate
     3  examination satisfactory to the board relative to the fire,  safety  and
     4  building  codes  of  the  state]  provide a verification from a national
     5  certifying organization for interior design professionals that an appli-
     6  cant  has  passed an examination offered by such certifying organization
     7  for interior design professionals, and that the applicant is  a  certif-
     8  icate  holder  in  good  standing  from such certifying organization for
     9  interior design professionals, provided that such  certifying  organiza-
    10  tion is approved by the commissioner;
    11    (e) Age: be at least twenty-one years of age;
    12    (f) Citizenship: meet no requirements as to United States citizenship;
    13    (g) Character: be of good moral character as determined by the depart-
    14  ment; and
    15    (h)  Fees:  pay  a fee of [three hundred dollars to the department for
    16  admission to the  two  department  conducted  examinations  and  for  an
    17  initial  license, a fee of one hundred fifty dollars for each reexamina-
    18  tion, a fee of] one hundred thirty-five dollars for an  initial  license
    19  [for  persons not requiring admission to a department conducted examina-
    20  tion,] and a fee of two hundred ten dollars for each triennial registra-
    21  tion period.
    22    2. On recommendation of the board, the department may exempt:
    23    (a) from the requirement of  subparagraph  one  of  paragraph  (d)  of
    24  subdivision  one  of  this  section, an applicant who holds a license of
    25  certification to practice as an interior designer issued to [him or her]
    26  the applicant upon examination by a legally constituted board of examin-
    27  ers in any other state or political subdivision of  the  United  States,
    28  provided  that at the time the license or certificate was issued by such
    29  state or political subdivision the applicant  met  the  requirements  of
    30  paragraph  (b)  of  subdivision one of this section for certification to
    31  use the title "certified interior designer" established by this article;
    32  or
    33    (b) from the requirements specified in paragraph  (b)  of  subdivision
    34  one  of  this  section  provided the applicant submits, prior to January
    35  first, nineteen  hundred  ninety-six,  satisfactory  evidence  that  the
    36  applicant  has  held  [himself  or  herself] themself out as an interior
    37  designer and has prepared interior design documents in  accordance  with
    38  the requirements set forth in section eighty-three hundred three of this
    39  article  for  a  minimum of seven years, has received a secondary school
    40  diploma or equivalent, and has passed the  examinations  required  under
    41  paragraph (d) of subdivision one of this section.
    42    § 2. This act shall take effect on the one hundred eightieth day after
