McGreevy, distinguished veteran, revered citizen, and devoted member of his community WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to pay tribute to citizens of the State of New York whose lifework and civic endeavor served to enhance the quality of life in their communities and the great State of New York; and WHEREAS, William W. McGreevy of Carmel, New York, died on Wednesday, August 6, 2025, at the age of 91; and WHEREAS, Born in the Bronx, New York, William W. McGreevy later became employed with the Verizon Corporation in White Plains, New York for numerous years where he worked in management until his retirement; and WHEREAS, A proud veteran, William W. McGreevy served in the United States Army during the Korean conflict; his military service was a testament to his dedication to his country and he carried the discipline and camaraderie learned through his military service throughout his life; and WHEREAS, William W. McGreevy shared the pleasures of life with his cherished wife, Ellen, whom he married on February 27, 1960; and WHEREAS, Family was of utmost importance to William W. McGreevy; the core of his existence and his role as a husband, father, and grandfather was where he found his greatest joy and fulfillment; and WHEREAS, William W. McGreevy loved golf and sailing with his brother-law Frank on the Long Island sound; he also loved dancing at parties and rooting for the New York Giants; and WHEREAS, William W. McGreevy distinguished himself in his profession and by his sincere dedication and substantial contribution to the welfare of his community; and WHEREAS, William W. McGreevy's commitment to excellence and spirit of humanity carried over into all fields of enterprise, including charitable and civic endeavors; and WHEREAS, William W. McGreevy is survived by his wife, Ellen; his children, Maryanne and John; grandchildren, Michael, Christina, Alec, Elizabeth, and Katherine; and two great-grandsons, Cole and Shane; he is predeceased by his son, William Jr.; parents, John and Loretta; and siblings, Christine, Jack, and Mary; and WHEREAS, Armed with a humanistic spirit and imbued with a sense of compassion, William W. McGreevy leaves behind a legacy which will long endure the passage of time and will remain as a comforting memory to all he served and befriended; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of William W. McGreevy; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of William W. McGreevy. Go to top Sitemap Request Information Directions and Maps
