Amd §6802, add §6833, Ed L
 
Establishes and authorizes telepharmacy in this state; defines terms; creates telepharmacy satellite consultation sites and telepharmacy in hospitals; authorizes the filling of prescriptions at remote sites connected to central pharmacies via computer link, videolink and audiolink; makes exceptions.

        AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to establishing the prac-
          tice of telepharmacy

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Section 6802 of the education law is amended by adding four
     2  new subdivisions 31, 32, 33 and 34 to read as follows:
     3    31. "Remote site" means a  pharmacy  staffed  by  registered  pharmacy
     4  technicians  with access to its main pharmacy and registered pharmacists
     5  by computer link, video link and audio link while open.
     6    32. "Satellite consultation  site"  means  a  telepharmacy  where  any
     7  filled  prescriptions ready for dispensing are prepared at another phar-
     8  macy and delivered to the satellite for dispensing  via  computer  link,
     9  video  link  and  audio link to the main pharmacy and a licensed pharma-
    10  cist.
    11    33. "Telepharmacy" means a central pharmacy with one  or  more  remote
    12  sites in which all sites are connected via computer link, video link and
    13  audio link.
    14    34. "Telepharmacy in hospitals" means a central hospital pharmacy with
    15  one  or  more remote sites in which all sites are connected via computer
    16  link, video link and audio link.
    17    § 2. The education law is amended by adding a new section 6833 to read
    18  as follows:
    19    § 6833. Telepharmacy. 1. A  remote  site  shall  comply  with  section
    20  sixty-eight  hundred  eight  of this article, governing requirements for
    21  permit to operate a pharmacy. The remote site is considered to be  under
    22  the personal charge of the pharmacist at the central pharmacy.

     1    2.  A  remote  site  shall  be  connected  to its central pharmacy via
     2  computer link, video link and audio link. A remote site  shall  use  its
     3  central  pharmacy's  central processing unit, including, but not limited
     4  to:
     5    (a)  consecutive  prescription  numbers  and  all prescription records
     6  shall be maintained at the central pharmacy;
     7    (b) prescriptions filled at the remote site shall  be  distinguishable
     8  on records from those filled at the central pharmacy;
     9    (c)  daily  reports must be separated for the central pharmacy and the
    10  remote site, but shall be maintained at the central pharmacy;
    11    (d) pharmacies shall be able to generate labels from the central phar-
    12  macy or at the remote site; and
    13    (e) all prescriptions distributed at the  remote  site  shall  have  a
    14  label  that  meets requirements set forth in section sixty-eight hundred
    15  eleven-a of this article attached to the final drug container before the
    16  pharmacist verifies the dispensing process.
    17    3.  A  pharmacist  at  the  central  pharmacy   shall   approve   each
    18  prescription before it leaves the remote site, including, but not limit-
    19  ed to:
    20    (a) dispensing is considered to be done at the central pharmacy;
    21    (b)  both  the pharmacist's and the technician's initials shall appear
    22  on the fill screen, patient profile and label; and
    23    (c) a pharmacist shall compare via video link the stock  bottle,  drug
    24  dispensed  and  strength. The entire label shall be checked for accuracy
    25  on the video link.
    26    4. Counseling shall be accomplished by  a  pharmacist  via  video  and
    27  audio  link.  The  pharmacist shall counsel the patient or the patient's
    28  agent on all new prescriptions and refills.
    29    5. A pharmacist shall complete monthly inspections of the remote site.
    30  Inspection criteria shall be included in the policies and procedures for
    31  the site. The inspection reports shall  be  maintained  until  the  next
    32  state board of pharmacy inspection.
    33    6.  The  remote  site  shall have a prescription inventory. Controlled
    34  substances shall be kept at the remote site.
    35    (a) If controlled substances are kept, the remote site shall be regis-
    36  tered with the drug enforcement administration and shall obtain its  own
    37  drug enforcement administration number.
    38    (b)  All records shall be stored at the central pharmacy, except those
    39  required by drug enforcement administration to be at the  drug  enforce-
    40  ment administration registered site.
    41    (c)  Policies  and  procedures shall be implemented to ensure the safe
    42  and effective distribution of pharmaceutical products  and  delivery  of
    43  required  pharmaceutical  care; including, but not limited to an ongoing
    44  review of incident reports and  outcomes,  with  appropriate  corrective
    45  action taken when necessary, to ensure there is no abnormal frequency of
    46  errors in dispensing drugs or devices.
    47    7. Dispensing and consultation may be done when the registered pharma-
    48  cy technician is not present, under the following circumstances:
    49    (a)  The  prescription  has  been  prepared by the registered pharmacy
    50  technician and checked by the licensed pharmacist;
    51    (b) The prescription area is locked;
    52    (c)  A  separate  locked  drawer  or   cabinet   is   maintained   for
    53  prescriptions ready for dispensing;
    54    (d)  A  log  is  maintained  by  the registered pharmacy technician of
    55  prescriptions placed in the locked drawer or cabinet;

     1    (e) A record shall be made by the pharmacist as to the date  and  time
     2  at which dispensing and counseling occurs;
     3    (f)  Supportive  personnel,  trained  in the use of the audio link and
     4  video link, to the licensed pharmacist,  are  on  hand,  to  assist  the
     5  patients; and
     6    (g)  The  patients  receive  their  prescriptions as such patients are
     7  being counseled by the licensed pharmacist.
     8    8. The permitholder or the pharmacist in charge of the central pharma-
     9  cy shall apply for a permit for the remote site. A  telepharmacy  permit
    10  is hereby established for the purpose of conducting a telepharmacy. Such
    11  permits  are issued to a remote site connected to a central pharmacy via
    12  computer link, video link and audio link.
    13    9. To the extent of a conflict with any provision of this article, the
    14  provisions of this article govern with respect  to  a  telepharmacy  and
    15  remote site operating in compliance with this chapter.  With the follow-
    16  ing conditions, this section is an exception to the following rules:
    17    (a)  The remote site shall have a registered pharmacy technician pres-
    18  ent and a working computer, video and audio link to a pharmacist at  the
    19  central  pharmacy  to have the prescription area open. The communication
    20  link shall be checked daily and the remote site pharmacy shall be closed
    21  if such link malfunctions, unless a pharmacist is at the remote site.
    22    (i) The technician shall be registered with the state board of pharma-
    23  cy.
    24    (ii) The technician shall be a graduate of an approved pharmacy  tech-
    25  nician  education program or must make application to the state board of
    26  pharmacy, which demonstrates knowledge and experience in preparation  of
    27  prescriptions for dispensing and working with  patients.
    28    (iii)  A  pharmacist  may  oversee  no more than four remote sites. As
    29  dispensing is considered done by the pharmacist, the pharmacist shall be
    30  held responsible for and held accountable for the remote site.
    31    (b) The pharmacy technician may unlock the  prescription  and  storage
    32  areas.    While  the  technician  is  on duty, the prescription area may
    33  remain open.
    34    (c) The input of drug information shall be done by a pharmacist at the
    35  main pharmacy or if entered by the technician at the remote site,  shall
    36  be verified by a pharmacist.
    37    (i)  New  prescriptions  may  be  received at the central pharmacy and
    38  entered there with a label printing at the remote site; and
    39    (ii) New prescriptions received at the remote site may be entered into
    40  the computer system there with all  verification,  interaction  checking
    41  and  profile  review the responsibility of the pharmacist at the central
    42  pharmacy.
    43    10. The state board of pharmacy may suspend immediately the permit  of
    44  any telepharmacy if a danger to the public exists.
    45    11.  There  shall  be  established telepharmacy satellite consultation
    46  sites which shall:
    47    (a) have no prescription inventory;
    48    (b) allow only filled prescriptions, filled at the  central  pharmacy,
    49  with final patient labeling attached;
    50    (c) be controlled by supportive personnel who have been trained in the
    51  use  of the patient counseling audio links and video links necessary for
    52  the dispensing and consultation to occur;
    53    (d) have the supportive personnel necessary to assist the  patient  in
    54  accessing the pharmacist via the audio link and video link;
    55    (e)  accept  prescription refill requests communicated to such site by
    56  the patient or the patient's agent;

     1    (f) accept original written prescriptions brought to such sites by the

     4    (g) not receive a prescription or refill communicated from practition-

     6    (h)  maintain  by the pharmacist security of filled prescriptions by a

     8    (i) have a record by the pharmacist as to the date and time  at  which
     9  dispensing and counseling occurred.

    11  section, no prescription order may be released for administration  to  a
    12  patient  until  approved  by a pharmacist via the audio link, video link
    13  and computer link. The policy and procedures of  the  hospital  pharmacy
    14  shall  address all aspects of the telepharmacy operation, including, but
    15  not limited to, the control of the pharmacy by the  registered  pharmacy
    16  technician in the absence of the pharmacist.
    17    13.  Telepharmacy satellite remote dispensing machine sites shall have
    18  prescription inventory, which is  secured  in  an  automated  dispensing
    19  device connected to the central processing unit at the central pharmacy.
    20    A  pharmacist  shall  approve  all  prescription  orders  before  such
    21  prescriptions are released from the automated dispensing device.
    22    Dispensing and counseling are performed by the licensed pharmacist  at
    23  the central site via audio link and video link.

    26  sary  for  the  implementation  of  this  act  on its effective date are
    27  authorized to be made and completed on or before such effective date.
