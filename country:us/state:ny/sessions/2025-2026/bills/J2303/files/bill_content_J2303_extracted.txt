and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and in full accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to pay tribute to Tony Cristello upon the occasion of being honored as part of a special Memorial Tribute during the 28th Annual Schenectady City School District Athletic Hall of Fame and Reunion Dinner on Monday, September 14, 2026, at the Event Center at Rivers Casino & Resort in Schenectady, New York; and WHEREAS, Excellence and success photographing competitive sports can be achieved only through strenuous attention to detail, effort to develop one's craft, and an immense pride in surrounding communities, nurtured by dedicated practice and strategic planning; and WHEREAS, Tony Cristello was born on September 28, 1942, in the City of Schenectady, and attended Mont Pleasant High School where he made friends who lasted him throughout the rest of his life, often coming back together to the Schenectady City School District Athletic Hall of Fame and Reunion Dinners; and WHEREAS, Tony Cristello pursued his interest in art at the New York State University at Albany, prior to helping his mother operate their family restaurant, Cristello's, a staple in the Mont Pleasant neighborhood; and WHEREAS, Following working at the family restaurant, Tony Cristello spent many years working as a draftsman at the General Electric company; and WHEREAS, Throughout Cristello's life, he produced oil paintings and pastels, many of which featured horses and are currently on display all around the Saratoga region; and WHEREAS, Tony Cristello's artwork was not only confined to horse's, as he produced many of the collages to honor inductees in the Schenectady City School District Athletic Hall of Fame, created tribute painting which brought recognition to Schenectady High School State Championship basketball teams, and put countless hours decorating the trophy cases at Schenectady High School; and WHEREAS, Much of Tony Cristello's work is still on display throughout the Schenectady City School District, including numerous pieces in Schenectady High School and Woodlawn Elementary; and WHEREAS, Tony Cristello passed away peacefully on October 23, 2025, with his loving family by his side, and is remembered fondly by all those who were fortunate enough to be a part of his life; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to pay tribute to Tony Cristello upon the occasion of being honored as part of a special Memorial Tribute during the 28th Annual Schenectady City School District Athletic Hall of Fame and Reunion Dinner; and be it further RESOLVED, That copies of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family members of Tony Cristello.
