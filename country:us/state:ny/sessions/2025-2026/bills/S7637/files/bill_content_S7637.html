S07637 Summary:

SAME ASNo Same As

Add §141, Ed L
 
Establishes the commission for agricultural education excellence to assist in developing a statewide plan for increasing agricultural literacy and supporting agricultural education; provides for the membership and powers and duties of such commission; requires the commission to file an annual report with the governor and the legislature.

        AN ACT to amend the education  law,  in  relation  to  establishing  the
          commission for agricultural education excellence
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. The education law is amended by adding a new section 141 to
     2  read as follows:
     3    § 141. Commission for agricultural education excellence. 1.  There  is
     4  hereby established within the department the commission for agricultural
     5  education  excellence  to  assist  in  developing  a  statewide plan for
     6  increasing agricultural literacy and supporting agricultural  education.
     7  The  commissioner  shall  implement  the  requirements of the commission
     8  within funds available to the department and  in  conjunction  with  the
     9  department  of  agriculture  and markets, and the department of labor as
    10  provided for under this section.
    11    2. (a)  The  commission  shall  consist  of  nineteen  members  to  be
    12  appointed as follows:
    13    (i) the commissioner, or their designee;
    14    (ii) the commissioner of agriculture and markets, or their designee;
    15    (iii) the commissioner of labor, or their designee;
    16    (iv)  the  dean  of  the  college  of agriculture and life sciences of
    17  Cornell university, or their designee;
    18    (v) the executive director of agriculture education  and  outreach  at
    19  Cornell university;
    20    (vi)  a  member  of the New York agriculture in the classroom advisory
    21  committee appointed by the chair;
    22    (vii) the president of New York FFA;
    23    (viii) the director of the Cornell  cooperative  extension,  or  their
    24  designee;
    25    (ix) the president of New York State 4-H;

     1    (x)  ten  members  who shall be jointly appointed by the commissioner,
     2  the commissioner of agriculture and markets,  and  the  commissioner  of
     3  labor  from lists submitted by the temporary president of the senate and
     4  the speaker of the assembly, in consultation with the minority leader of
     5  the senate and the majority leader and the minority leader of the assem-
     6  bly as follows:
     7    (1) two farmers;
     8    (2)  a  representative  of  the  agricultural processing and marketing
     9  industries;
    10    (3) two secondary teachers of agriculture, one from a board of cooper-
    11  ative educational services career and technical center and  one  from  a
    12  school  district  appointed by the president of the New York Association
    13  of Agricultural Educators;
    14    (4) a representative of the state university of New York  agricultural
    15  and  technical  colleges  that offer agriculture majors, who has a back-
    16  ground in or knowledge of agricultural education;
    17    (5) a secondary school administrator from a school  that  conducts  an
    18  agricultural education program;
    19    (6)  one  member  from any college or university that offers state-ap-
    20  proved agricultural science teacher preparation  programs  or  a  public
    21  community  college  in  this state that provides an articulated agricul-
    22  tural science teacher education course of study; and
    23    (7) two members of the business community with knowledge  of  agricul-
    24  tural education.
    25    (b)  Each  member  of  the  commission shall serve for a term of three
    26  years, except that of the initial members so  appointed:  seven  members
    27  shall  serve  for  a  term of three years, six members shall serve for a
    28  term of two years, and six members shall serve a term of one year.  Such
    29  initial terms shall be randomly determined among the members. Any vacan-
    30  cy  in the commission shall be filled for the unexpired term in the same
    31  manner as the original appointment.
    32    (c) The chair of the  commission  shall  rotate  on  an  annual  basis
    33  between  the  commissioner, the commissioner of agriculture and markets,
    34  and the commissioner of labor, with the commissioner of agriculture  and
    35  markets chairing the first annual rotation.
    36    (d)  A  majority of the members of the commission then in office shall
    37  constitute a quorum for the transaction of any business or the  exercise
    38  of any power or function of the commission.
    39    (e) Members of the commission shall not receive compensation for their
    40  services  as  members,  but  shall be allowed their actual and necessary
    41  expenses reasonably incurred in the  performance  of  their  duties.  No
    42  member  shall  be  disqualified  from holding any other public office or
    43  employment by reason of such person's appointment or service as a member
    44  notwithstanding the provisions of any other law to the contrary.
    45    (f) The department, in conjunction with the department of  agriculture
    46  and  markets  and  the department of labor, shall provide the commission
    47  with such staff, equipment, facilities and such other resources  as  the
    48  commission may need to carry out its powers and duties.
    49    (g)  The  commission shall keep a record of all of its proceedings and
    50  actions.
    51    3. The commission shall:
    52    (a) make recommendations on policy, programs,  and  initiatives  aimed
    53  at:
    54    (i)  deepening  the public understanding of the agriculture, food, and
    55  natural resources industry and the importance of  locally  sourced  food
    56  supplies;

     1    (ii)  educating the public on the ability of the agricultural industry
     2  to assist the state in achieving the goals set by the Climate Leadership
     3  and Community Protection Act;
     4    (iii)  increasing the effectiveness and accountability of agricultural
     5  education programs;
     6    (iv) addressing industry workforce  needs,  including  attracting  and
     7  preparing young people for agricultural careers;
     8    (v)  providing  in-service  training  for  agricultural workers in the
     9  agricultural industry; and
    10    (vi) promoting technology focused jobs and careers in the agricultural
    11  industry;
    12    (b) consult with secondary schools and boards  of  cooperative  educa-
    13  tional services to facilitate articulation agreements with postsecondary
    14  institutions of higher education;
    15    (c)  provide  support  and technical assistance to supervised agricul-
    16  tural experience, also known as work-based learning programs,  based  on
    17  student and industry needs;
    18    (d)  provide  support and technical assistance for K-12 programs using
    19  food and agriculture as context for learning;
    20    (e) offer support and coordination for statewide and local  activities
    21  related to FFA programs;
    22    (f)  offer support and coordination for statewide and local activities
    23  related to 4-H programs;
    24    (g) investigate, review, and issue an annual report on the  status  of
    25  agricultural  literacy  and  education in the state required pursuant to
    26  subdivision four of this section  that  includes  the  location  of  all
    27  resources provided by the commission;
    28    (h)  serve  as a link between the agribusiness sector and the agricul-
    29  tural literacy and education  system  to  communicate  mutual  concerns,
    30  needs, and projections;
    31    (i) establish and maintain a system of increased awareness of agricul-
    32  tural  literacy education and its continued need to all residents of New
    33  York; and
    34    (j) study and make recommendations on model curriculum or  instruction
    35  on  agricultural  literacy  and education in an appropriate place in the
    36  curriculum of elementary, middle and high school students.
    37    4. By May first, two thousand twenty-six, and by  May  first  of  each
    38  year  thereafter,  the  commission shall submit a report to the governor
    39  and the legislature on the status of agricultural education in the state
    40  of New York. The report shall:
    41    (a) outline agricultural literacy and education programs and  achieve-
    42  ments;
    43    (b)  assess  the  trends  and  needs  in secondary and both formal and
    44  informal postsecondary agricultural education and training;
    45    (c) investigate and assess workforce trends of the agriculture,  food,
    46  and natural resources industry;
    47    (d) assess and make programming recommendations for meeting the train-
    48  ing needs for individuals not pursuing formal postsecondary education;
    49    (e) identify critical needs, challenges and opportunities in the agri-
    50  culture,  food,  and  natural resources industry in the state, its work-
    51  force, and agricultural communities, and identify how to best strengthen
    52  the industry for growth and profitability;
    53    (f) consider the manner in  which  funds  are  acquired  and  used  to
    54  support agricultural education activities;
    55    (g) highlight new initiatives and recommend future program needs;

     1    (h)  make  recommendations on model curriculum or instruction on agri-
     2  cultural literacy and education in an appropriate place in  the  curric-
     3  ulum of elementary, middle and high schools;
     4    (i) make recommendations to the governor and the legislature regarding
