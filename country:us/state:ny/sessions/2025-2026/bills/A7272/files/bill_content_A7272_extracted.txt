CHANDLER-WATERMAN -- read once and referred to the Committee on Governmental Operations AN ACT to amend the executive law, in relation to requiring the instal- lation and periodic inspection of water detector alarms in residential dwellings and certain commercial buildings The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Section 378 of the executive law is amended by adding two 2 new subdivisions 5-e and 5-f to read as follows: 3 5-e. a. Standards for installation of water detector alarms requiring 4 that every one or two-family dwelling, or any dwelling accommodation 5 located in a building owned as a condominium or cooperative in the state 6 or any multiple dwellings shall have installed an operable water detec- 7 tor alarm of such manufacture, design and installation standards as are 8 established and determined to be necessary by the council. For the 9 purposes of this subdivision, "water detector alarm" means an electronic 10 device that detects the presence of water and sets off an audible and 11 visual alarm when its sensors are triggered. 12 b. Standards for inspections of water detector alarms requiring that 13 an appropriate and qualified inspector, as determined by the local 14 government, shall perform a periodic inspection to ensure that every 15 dwelling required to have a water detector alarm pursuant to paragraph a 16 of this subdivision has such water detector alarm installed and it is in 17 operable condition. 18 5-f. a. Standards for installation of water detector alarms requiring 19 that a commercial building in the state that may be subject to flooding 20 shall have installed an operable water detector alarm of such manufac- 21 ture, design and installation standards as are established and deter- 22 mined to be necessary by the council.
