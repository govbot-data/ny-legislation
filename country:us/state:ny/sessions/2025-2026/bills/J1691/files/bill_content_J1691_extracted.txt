Bill No.: Summary   Actions   Committee&nbspVotes   Floor&nbspVotes   Memo   Text   LFIN   Chamber&nbspVideo/Transcript J01691 Summary:BILL NOJ01691  SAME ASNo Same As SPONSORMARTINS COSPNSR MLTSPNSR  Go to topJ01691 Text: Senate Resolution No. 1691 BY: Senator MARTINS HONORING Dr. Dorothy Bruckstein upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of the Distinguished Faculty Award by the North Shore Hebrew Academy at its 71st Annual Dinner Celebration on March 22, 2026 WHEREAS, Individuals who give of their time and energies and serve the best interests of their communities are an asset beyond remuneration and cannot be sufficiently extolled; and WHEREAS, Dr. Dorothy Bruckstein has given not only her time and energy, but also her competence, intelligence, and leadership, and consequently has been designated for this special honor; and WHEREAS, This Legislative Body is justly proud to honor Dr. Dorothy Bruckstein upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of the Distinguished Faculty Award by the North Shore Hebrew Academy (NSHA) at its 71st Annual Dinner Celebration at the Old Westbury Hebrew Congregation on Sunday, March 22, 2026; and WHEREAS, Dr. Dorothy Bruckstein has been called upon to contribute her time and talents to countless civic and charitable endeavors and has always given of herself unstintingly; and WHEREAS, Dr. Dorothy Bruckstein began her professional career with earning a bachelor's degree in nursing from Lehman College and a master's degree in nursing from New York University before going on to receive her PhD in clinical/school psychology from Hofstra University; and WHEREAS, With an initial focus on clinical practice, Dr. Dorothy Bruckstein found her true passion for school psychology and has served the students of NSHA faithfully and with expertise for the past 25 years; and WHEREAS, Dr. Dorothy Bruckstein treasures the sense of family, respect, and kindness that defines NSHA High School and feels privileged to work closely with students and families to support their growth and well-being; and WHEREAS, Exemplifying her passion for her work and commitment to upholding the highest standards of practice, Dr. Bruckstein is a member of the American Psychological Association, National Association of School Psychologists, and NEFESH International (Network of Orthodox Mental Health Professionals); and WHEREAS, With her throughout has been her husband, Alex; her children, Tammy, Shavy, Sarah, and Moishe; her twelve grandchildren; and her four great-grandchildren, all of whom feel privileged to be a part of her fruitful life and rejoice in her many achievements; and WHEREAS, Throughout the entire period of her service, a period of constructive involvement, Dr. Dorothy Bruckstein has stood constant in dignity, good grace, and humor; and WHEREAS, Rare indeed is the impressive dedication shown by individuals for the benefit of others which Dr. Dorothy Bruckstein has displayed throughout her life; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to Legislative Body honor Dr. Dorothy Bruckstein upon the occasion of her designation as recipient of the Distinguished Faculty Award by the North Shore Hebrew Academy at its 71st Annual Dinner Celebration; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Dr. Dorothy Bruckstein.
