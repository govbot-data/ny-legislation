Add Art 131-D §§6549-j - 6549-p, Ed L
 
Relates to licensure of anesthesiologist assistants; establishes requirements for such licensure; establishes a state committee for anesthesiologist assistants; allows for limited permits lasting one year.

        Introduced  by  M.  of  A.  BUTTENSCHON -- read once and referred to the

        AN ACT to amend the education law, in relation to licensure of  anesthe-
          siologist assistants
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section 1. Legislative findings.  The  legislature  hereby  finds  and
     2  declares  that  the public interest requires the regulation of the prac-
     3  tice of anesthesiologist assistant and the establishment of clear licen-
     4  sure standards for anesthesiologist assistants; and that the health  and
     5  welfare  of  the residents of the state will be protected by identifying
     6  to the public those individuals who are qualified and legally authorized
     7  to practice as anesthesiologist assistants.
     8    § 2. The education law is amended by adding a  new  article  131-D  to
     9  read as follows:
    10                                ARTICLE 131-D
    11                         ANESTHESIOLOGIST ASSISTANTS
    12  Section 6549-j. Definitions.
    13          6549-k. Practice  of anesthesiologist assistant and use of title
    14                    "anesthesiologist assistant".
    15          6549-l. Requirements  for  licensure  as   an   anesthesiologist
    16                    assistant.
    17          6549-m. Special provisions.
    18          6549-n. State committee for anesthesiologist assistants.
    19          6549-o. Limited permits.
    20          6549-p. Exempt persons.
    21    §  6549-j.  Definitions.  As used in this article, the following terms
    22  shall have the following meanings: 1. "Anesthesiologist assistant" means
    23  an individual who is licensed to practice as an anesthesiologist assist-
    24  ant pursuant to this article and provides anesthesia  care  pursuant  to

     1  the  order  and  direction  of,  and  under the direct supervision of, a
     2  licensed physician anesthesiologist.
     3    2.  "Committee" means the state committee for anesthesiologist assist-
     4  ants created under section sixty-five hundred forty-nine-n of this arti-

     6    3. "NCCAA" means the national commission for certification of anesthe-
     7  siologist assistants.
     8    § 6549-k. Practice of anesthesiologist  assistant  and  use  of  title
     9  "anesthesiologist  assistant".  Only  an  individual  licensed or exempt
    10  under this article shall practice as an anesthesiologist assistant. Only
    11  individuals licensed as anesthesiologist assistants may  use  the  title
    12  "anesthesiologist assistant".
    13    § 6549-l. Requirements for licensure as an anesthesiologist assistant.
    14  To  qualify for licensure as an anesthesiologist assistant, an applicant
    15  shall fulfill the following requirements:
    16    1. Application: file an application with the department;
    17    2. Education: successfully complete (a) (i)  an  accredited  baccalau-
    18  reate or higher accredited educational program; and (ii) an anesthesiol-
    19  ogist assistant training program accredited by the commission on accred-
    20  itation of allied health education programs; or (b) a program determined
    21  by  the  department to be equivalent to the requirements under paragraph
    22  (a) of this subdivision and in accordance with the commissioner's  regu-
    23  lations;
    24    3.  Credentials: (a) complete and maintain the credential of certified
    25  anesthesiologist assistant from a national certifying body  specializing
    26  in the certification of anesthesiologist assistants such as the NCCAA or
    27  one  approved  by  the  department; or (b) submit evidence of license or
    28  certification, the educational preparation for which  is  determined  by
    29  the  department  to be equivalent to the credentials under paragraph (a)
    30  of this subdivision, from any state  or  country,  satisfactory  to  the
    31  department and in accordance with the commissioner's regulations;
    32    4.  Age:  at  the  time of application is at least twenty-one years of
    33  age;
    34    5. Character: be of good moral character as determined by the  depart-
    35  ment; and
    36    6.  Fees:  pay  a  fee of one hundred dollars to the department for an
    37  initial license and a fee of fifty dollars for each triennial  registra-
    38  tion period.
    39    § 6549-m. Special provisions. An individual who meets the requirements
    40  for  a  license as an anesthesiologist assistant except for examination,
    41  experience, and education, and who  meets  the  requirements  enumerated
    42  under  subdivisions  one  or two of this section may be licensed without
    43  meeting additional requirements, provided that such  individual  submits
    44  an  application to the department within two years of the effective date
    45  of this section:
    46    1. Applicants may be licensed if  they  have  been  practicing  as  an
    47  anesthesiologist assistant in another jurisdiction for five years out of
    48  the  prior  ten years in an inpatient unit that provides anesthesia care
    49  services in a hospital approved by the department of health and accepta-
    50  ble to the committee and the department, at least three of such years of
    51  experience having occurred during the prior five years; or
    52    2. Applicants who possess certification from a national  certification
    53  organization  such as the NCCAA or one found acceptable to the committee
    54  and the department may be licensed if they  have  been  employed  as  an
    55  anesthesiologist  assistant  in  another  jurisdiction  for three of the
    56  prior five years.

     1    § 6549-n. State committee for anesthesiologist assistants. 1. A  state
     2  committee  for  anesthesiologist  assistants  shall  be appointed by the
     3  board of regents upon  the  recommendation  of  the  commissioner  as  a
     4  committee of the board for medicine to advise solely in matters relating
     5  to  the  practice  of  anesthesiologist  assistant  and  shall assist on
     6  matters of licensure and professional conduct.
     7    2. The committee shall consist of no fewer than eight individuals,  to
     8  be composed of a minimum of the following:
     9    a. four licensed anesthesiologist assistants;
    10    b.  two  licensed  physicians  board  certified in anesthesiology by a
    11  national certifying body recognized by the department; and
    12    c. two representatives of the public at large.
    13    § 6549-o. Limited permits. 1. Eligibility. A person who  fulfills  all
    14  requirements  for  licensure as an anesthesiologist assistant under this
    15  article except those relating to the examination shall be eligible for a
    16  limited permit.
    17    2. Limit of practice. A permittee shall be authorized to  practice  as
    18  an  anesthesiologist  assistant only pursuant to the order and direction
    19  and under the direct supervision of a licensed physician board certified
    20  in anesthesiology by a national certifying  organization  recognized  by
    21  the department.
    22    3.  Duration.  A limited permit shall expire one year from the date of
    23  issuance. A limited permit may be extended for one additional  year  for
    24  good cause as determined by the department.
    25    4. Fees. The fee for each limited permit shall be forty dollars.
    26    §  6549-p.  Exempt  persons.  This  article shall not prohibit: 1. The
    27  anesthesiology care services by any student who is engaged  in  clinical
    28  training in a general hospital licensed pursuant to article twenty-eight
    29  of  the  public  health  law  and who is enrolled in an anesthesiologist
    30  assistant program found acceptable  by  the  department,  provided  such
    31  practice is limited to such clinical training which shall be carried out
    32  pursuant  to the order and direction and under the direct supervision of
    33  a licensed physician board certified in  anesthesiology  by  a  national
    34  certifying body recognized by the department;
    35    2. The performance of any of the tasks or responsibilities included in
    36  the  definition  of  the  practice  of anesthesiologist assistant by any
    37  other individual licensed under this title, provided that such tasks  or
    38  responsibilities  are authorized by the article governing the profession
    39  pursuant to which such individual is licensed; or
    40    3. The practice  of  anesthesiology  care  by  any  legally  qualified
    41  anesthesiologist  assistant  of any other jurisdiction who is serving in
    42  the armed forces or the public health service of the  United  States  or
    43  who  is  employed  by  the veterans administration, while engaged in the
    44  performance of such anesthesiologist assistant's duties.
    45    § 3. This act shall take effect immediately.
