PARKER -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Cities 1 AN ACT to amend the administrative code of the city of New York, in relation to the establishment of a precinct commander appointment board in each police precinct in the city of New York for the purpose of evaluating and selecting the precinct commander for such police precinct The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The administrative code of the city of New York is amended 2 by adding a new section 14-198 to read as follows: 3 § 14-198 Precinct commander appointment board. 1. Notwithstanding the 4 provisions of section seventy of the civil service law, the borough 5 president of each borough in the city shall establish in each police 6 precinct of the city a precinct commander appointment board ("the 7 board") for the purpose of evaluating and selecting the precinct comman- 8 der for such police precinct. The board will establish appropriate 9 procedures and policies for the conduct of its affairs. 10 2. (a) Subject to the provisions of paragraph (b) of this subdivision, 11 each precinct commander appointment board shall be composed of eight 12 members as follows: 13 (i) the chair of the community board for the district in which the 14 respective police precinct is located; 15 (ii) the president of the community council for the district in which 16 the respective police precinct is located; 17 (iii) three police captains appointed by the commissioner, none of 18 whom shall be stationed at the respective precinct; 19 (iv) one person appointed by the borough president for the borough in 20 which the respective police precinct is located; and EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. (a) Upon the occurrence of a vacancy in a police precinct commander 21 position, the commissioner shall notify the secretary of the precinct 22 commander appointment board for the respective precinct of the vacancy. 23 The commissioner or such commissioner's delegee shall supply the board 24 with the names of prospective candidates for the position and with 25 employment and background information regarding each such candidate, as 26 deemed necessary by the board. The board, in its discretion, may consid- 27 er other candidates for the position. The board, through the secretary 28 of the board, shall immediately notify the commissioner of the name of 29 any alternative candidate. Any person whose name is put forth by the 30 commissioner or by the precinct commander appointment board shall have 31 attained a minimum rank of sergeant not less than one year prior to the 32 selection process to fill the precinct commander vacancy. 33 (b) In selecting the new precinct commander, the precinct commander 34 appointment board will give consideration to the examination from which 35 a candidate received permanent civil service status, the length of time 36 served in the candidate's present position and other related factors 37 which will be established and promulgated by the board. 38 (c) The precinct commander appointment board shall select the new 39 precinct commander by a majority vote of the entire board. A member of 40 the board may cast their vote by video teleconference or conference call 41 upon notice to the other members of the board by mail, email, text or 42 telephone call at least twelve hours before such meeting. 43 (d) Within thirty days of being notified of a vacancy in the precinct 44 commander position, the precinct commander appointment board shall 45 select the new precinct commander. The secretary of the board shall 46 immediately provide written notice to the commissioner of the board's 47 selection to fill the position. Such notice may be provided electron- 48 ically by email or facsimile. 49 4. Meetings of the precinct commander appointment board will be 50 convened by the chair of the board, who will provide the other members 51 of the board with not less than five days' notice of the date, time and 52 place of the meeting. Meetings may be conducted in full or in part by 53 video teleconference or conference call upon notice to all members of 54 the board and to the secretary of the board by mail, email, text or 55 telephone call at least twelve hours before such meeting.
