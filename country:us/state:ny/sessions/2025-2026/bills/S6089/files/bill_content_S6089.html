Add §14-198, NYC Ad Cd
 
Directs the borough president of each borough in the city of New York to establish a precinct commander appointment board in each police precinct in the city of New York for the purpose of evaluating and selecting the precinct commander for such police precinct.

        Introduced  by  Sen.  PARKER -- read twice and ordered printed, and when
          printed to be committed to the Committee on Cities 1
 
        AN ACT to amend the administrative code of the  city  of  New  York,  in
          relation  to  the  establishment  of  a precinct commander appointment
          board in each police precinct in the city of New York for the  purpose
          of  evaluating  and  selecting  the precinct commander for such police
          precinct
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1. The administrative code of the city of New York is amended
     2  by adding a new section 14-198 to read as follows:
     3    § 14-198 Precinct commander appointment board. 1. Notwithstanding  the
     4  provisions  of  section  seventy  of  the civil service law, the borough
     5  president of each borough in the city shall  establish  in  each  police
     6  precinct  of  the  city  a  precinct  commander  appointment board ("the
     7  board") for the purpose of evaluating and selecting the precinct comman-
     8  der for such police  precinct.  The  board  will  establish  appropriate
     9  procedures and policies for the conduct of its affairs.
    10    2. (a) Subject to the provisions of paragraph (b) of this subdivision,
    11  each  precinct  commander  appointment  board shall be composed of eight
    12  members as follows:
    13    (i) the chair of the community board for the  district  in  which  the
    14  respective police precinct is located;
    15    (ii)  the president of the community council for the district in which
    16  the respective police precinct is located;
    17    (iii) three police captains appointed by  the  commissioner,  none  of
    18  whom shall be stationed at the respective precinct;
    19    (iv)  one person appointed by the borough president for the borough in
    20  which the respective police precinct is located; and

     1    (v) two persons appointed by a majority of the  city  council  members
     2  representing  the  district  in  which the respective police precinct is
     3  located.
     4    (b)  In  areas  in which the New York City Housing Authority operates,
     5  the precinct commander appointment board shall include  a  ninth  member
     6  who  shall  be  the  chair of the New York City Housing Authority Tenant
     7  Association for the area in which  the  respective  police  precinct  is
     8  located.
     9    (c)  The  chair  of  the community board for the district in which the
    10  respective police precinct is  located  shall  serve  as  chair  of  the
    11  precinct commander appointment board.
    12    (d) A representative of the New York state department of civil service
    13  shall  serve  as  secretary to the precinct commander appointment board;
    14  provided that such secretary shall not vote in the proceedings.
    15    (e) All members of the precinct commander appointment board  shall  be
    16  residents of the borough in which the precinct is located.
    17    (f)  Any  vacancy in the membership of the precinct commander appoint-
    18  ment board shall be filled in the same  manner  in  which  the  original
    19  appointment was made.
    20    3. (a) Upon the occurrence of a vacancy in a police precinct commander
    21  position,  the  commissioner  shall notify the secretary of the precinct
    22  commander appointment board for the respective precinct of the  vacancy.
    23  The  commissioner  or such commissioner's delegee shall supply the board
    24  with the names of prospective  candidates  for  the  position  and  with
    25  employment  and background information regarding each such candidate, as
    26  deemed necessary by the board. The board, in its discretion, may consid-
    27  er other candidates for the position. The board, through  the  secretary
    28  of  the  board, shall immediately notify the commissioner of the name of
    29  any alternative candidate. Any person whose name is  put  forth  by  the
    30  commissioner  or  by the precinct commander appointment board shall have
    31  attained a minimum rank of sergeant not less than one year prior to  the
    32  selection process to fill the precinct commander vacancy.
    33    (b)  In  selecting  the new precinct commander, the precinct commander
    34  appointment board will give consideration to the examination from  which
    35  a  candidate received permanent civil service status, the length of time
    36  served in the candidate's present position  and  other  related  factors
    37  which will be established and promulgated by the board.
    38    (c)  The  precinct  commander  appointment  board shall select the new
    39  precinct commander by a majority vote of the entire board. A  member  of
    40  the board may cast their vote by video teleconference or conference call
    41  upon  notice  to  the other members of the board by mail, email, text or
    42  telephone call at least twelve hours before such meeting.
    43    (d) Within thirty days of being notified of a vacancy in the  precinct
    44  commander  position,  the  precinct  commander  appointment  board shall
    45  select the new precinct commander. The  secretary  of  the  board  shall
    46  immediately  provide  written  notice to the commissioner of the board's
    47  selection to fill the position. Such notice may  be  provided  electron-
    48  ically by email or facsimile.
    49    4.  Meetings  of  the  precinct  commander  appointment  board will be
    50  convened by the chair of the board, who will provide the  other  members
    51  of  the board with not less than five days' notice of the date, time and
    52  place of the meeting. Meetings may be conducted in full or  in  part  by
    53  video  teleconference  or  conference call upon notice to all members of
    54  the board and to the secretary of the board  by  mail,  email,  text  or
    55  telephone call at least twelve hours before such meeting.

        S. 6089                             3
 
     1    5. No member of the precinct commander appointment board shall receive
     2  any  additional  compensation  from  the state of New York or any of its
     3  political subdivisions for any services rendered to the  board  nor  any
     4  expenses incurred in attendance at board meetings.
     5    6.  No  member  of  a  precinct  commander  appointment board shall be
     6  disqualified from holding any other public  office  or  employment,  nor
     7  shall  they  forfeit  any  such  office or employment by reason of their
     8  membership on a precinct commander  appointment  board,  notwithstanding
     9  the  provisions of any general, special or local law, ordinance, or city
    10  charter.
    11    § 2. This act shall take effect on the ninetieth day  after  it  shall
    12  have become a law.
