A03650 Summary:

BILL NOA03650
 
SAME ASSAME AS S00344
 
SPONSORSolages
 
COSPNSR
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Add Art 34-E §§1290 - 1298, §§383-a & 301-c, V & T L
 
Provides that a person may operate a fully autonomous vehicle on the public roads of this state without a human driver provided that the automated driving system is engaged and the vehicle meets certain conditions; defines terms; requires insurance and that such vehicle is registered as a fully autonomous vehicle; makes related provisions.

    17    § 1290. Definitions. For purposes of this article the following  terms

    20  that are collectively capable of performing the entire  dynamic  driving
    21  task  on  a  sustained  basis,  regardless of whether it is limited to a
    22  specific operational design domain;
    23    2. "Dynamic driving task or DDT" shall mean all of the real-time oper-
    24  ational and tactical functions required to operate a vehicle in  on-road
    25  traffic,  excluding  the strategic functions such as trip scheduling and

    14    4. "Fully autonomous vehicle" shall mean a motor vehicle equipped with

    16  as a level 4 or 5 system under SAE J3016;
    17    5. "Human driver" shall mean a natural person in the  vehicle  with  a

    30    8. "Operational design domain or ODD" shall mean operating  conditions
    31  under which a given automated driving system is specifically designed to

    33  and time-of-day restrictions, and/or the requisite presence  or  absence
    34  of certain traffic or roadway characteristics;
    35    9.  "Person" shall mean a natural person, corporation, business trust,
    36  estate, trust,  partnership,  limited  liability  company,  association,
    37  joint  venture,  governmental  agency,  public corporation, or any other
    38  legal or commercial entity;
    39    10. "Request to intervene" shall mean  notification  by  an  automated
    40  driving  system to a human driver, that the human driver should promptly
    41  begin or resume performance of part or all of the dynamic driving  task;
    42  and
    43    11.  "SAE  J3016"  shall  mean  the Taxonomy and Definitions for Terms
    44  Related  to  Driving  Automation  Systems  for  On-Road  Motor  Vehicles
    45  published by SAE International in April 2021.
    46    § 1291. Operation of fully autonomous vehicles without a human driver.
    47  1.  A  person may operate a fully autonomous vehicle on the public roads
    48  of this state without a human driver provided that the automated driving
    49  system is engaged and the vehicle meets the following conditions:
    50    (a) If a failure of the automated driving system occurs  that  renders
    51  that  system  unable to perform the entire dynamic driving task relevant
    52  to its intended operational design domain, the fully autonomous  vehicle
    53  shall achieve a minimal risk condition;
    54    (b) The fully autonomous vehicle is capable of operating in compliance
    55  with  the  applicable  traffic  and  motor vehicle safety laws and regu-

     2  been granted by the department; and
     3    (c)  When  required  by  federal  law,  the vehicle bears the required
     4  manufacturer's certification label indicating that at the  time  of  its
     5  manufacture  it has been certified to be in compliance with all applica-

    20    § 1292. Licensing. Notwithstanding the provisions of article  nineteen
    21  of  this  chapter, when an automated driving system installed on a motor
    22  vehicle is engaged:
    23    1. The automated driving system is considered the driver or  operator,
    24  for the purpose of assessing compliance with applicable traffic or motor
    25  vehicle  laws and shall be deemed to satisfy electronically all physical
    26  acts required by a driver or operator of the vehicle; and
    27    2. The automated driving system is considered to be licensed to  oper-
    28  ate the vehicle.
    29    §  1293.  Insurance.  Before  operating  a fully autonomous vehicle on
    30  public roads in this state, a person shall  submit  proof  of  financial
    31  responsibility  satisfactory to the department that the fully autonomous
    32  vehicle is covered by insurance or proof of self-insurance  that  satis-
    33  fies the requirements of article six of this chapter.
    34    §  1294. Duties following crashes involving fully autonomous vehicles.
    35  In the event of a crash:
    36    1. The fully autonomous vehicle shall remain on the scene of the crash
    37  when required by article twenty-two of this chapter; and
    38    2. The owner of the fully autonomous vehicle, or a person on behalf of
    39  the vehicle owner, shall  promptly  report  any  crashes  or  collisions
    40  consistent with article twenty-two of this chapter.
    41    §  1295. On-demand autonomous vehicle network. An on-demand autonomous
    42  vehicle network shall be permitted to operate  pursuant  to  state  laws
    43  governing  the  operation of transportation network companies, taxis, or
    44  any other ground transportation for-hire of passengers, with the  excep-
    45  tion  that  any  provision  of article forty-four-B of this chapter that
    46  reasonably applies only to a human driver does not apply to  the  opera-
    47  tion  of  fully  autonomous  vehicles  with the automated driving system
    48  engaged on an on-demand autonomous vehicle network.
    49    § 1296. Registration and title. 1. A fully autonomous vehicle shall be
    50  properly registered in accordance with title four of  this  chapter  and
    51  the  vehicle  shall  be identified on the registration as a fully auton-
    52  omous vehicle.
    53    2. A fully autonomous vehicle shall be properly titled  in  accordance
    54  with  article forty-six of this chapter and the vehicle shall be identi-
    55  fied on the title as a fully autonomous vehicle.

     1    § 1297. Controlling authority. Notwithstanding any law, rule or  regu-
     2  lation to the contrary:
     3    1.  Unless  otherwise provided in this chapter and notwithstanding any
     4  other provision of law, fully autonomous vehicles and automated  driving

    10  twenty-six of this chapter; and
    11    3.  No  state  agency,  political  subdivision, municipality, or local
    12  entity may prohibit the operation of fully  autonomous  vehicles,  auto-
    13  mated  driving  systems,  or  on-demand  autonomous vehicle networks, or
    14  otherwise enact or keep in force rules or ordinances that  would  impose
    15  taxes,  fees,  or  other  requirements  including performance standards,
    16  specific to the operation of fully autonomous vehicles, automated  driv-
    17  ing systems, or on-demand autonomous vehicle networks in addition to the
    18  requirements of this article.
    19    4. Notwithstanding subdivision one, two, or three of this section, any
    20  city with a population of five million or more may establish local rules
    21  and  regulations governing for-hire vehicle services for trips originat-
    22  ing in such city, when such services are provided using fully autonomous
    23  vehicles,  including,  as  applicable,  on-demand   autonomous   vehicle
    24  networks,   tax,   limousine,  paratransit  vehicle,  and  commuter  van
    25  services.
    26    § 1298. Operation of a motor vehicle with an automated driving  system
    27  by  a  human  driver.   1. A person may operate a motor vehicle equipped
    28  with an automated driving system capable of performing the entire dynam-
    29  ic driving task if:
    30    (a) such automated driving system will issue a  request  to  intervene
    31  whenever  the  automated driving system is not capable of performing the
    32  entire dynamic driving task with the expectation that  the  person  will
    33  respond appropriately to such a request; and
    34    (b)  the  automated  driving  system  is  capable of being operated in
    35  compliance with title seven of this chapter when  reasonable  to  do  so
    36  unless an exemption has been granted by the department.
    37    2.  Nothing in this article prohibits or restricts a human driver from
    38  operating a fully autonomous vehicle equipped with controls  that  allow
    39  for the human driver to control all or part of the dynamic driving task.
