Romero) -- read once and referred to the Committee on Real Property Taxation AN ACT to amend the real property tax law, in relation to authorizing the city of Albany to add unpaid housing code violation penalties, costs and fines to such city's annual tax levy The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. The real property tax law is amended by adding a new 2 section 901-a to read as follows: 3 § 901-a. Collection of unpaid housing code violation penalties in the 4 city of Albany; levy. 1. Authorization. In addition to and not in limi- 5 tation of any power otherwise granted by law, the city of Albany is 6 hereby authorized to collect any unpaid housing, building and fire code 7 violation penalties, costs and fines through placement by the city's 8 treasurer on the city's annual tax levy in accordance with the 9 provisions of this section. 10 2. Eligibility. In order to be eligible for placement on the city of 11 Albany's annual tax levy such unpaid code violation penalties, costs and 12 fines shall have been adjudicated and imposed through a judgment in a 13 court of competent jurisdiction on an owner of real property within the 14 city and recorded by the county clerk, as certified by the city's corpo- 15 ration counsel and have remained unpaid for one year after the final 16 adjudication and exhaustion of all appeals relating to the imposition of 17 the fines for a code violation preceding the placement on the city's tax 18 levy. 19 3. Minimum amount owed. To qualify for placement on the tax levy the 20 amount owed for unpaid code violations must be at least five percent of 21 the amount of the tax assessed value of the property. 22 4. Levy. Such code violation penalty, cost or fine as set forth in a 23 copy of the judgment certified by the corporation counsel shall be set 24 down in the annual tax levy under the heading uncollected fines and 25 penalties and in accordance with this section shall be levied, enforced 26 and collected in the same manner, by the same proceedings, at the same EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. The city of Albany shall notify all owners or known inter- 4 ested parties of record of the placement of the code violations on the 5 municipal tax levy as uncollected fines and penalties within thirty days 6 of placement, pursuant to section three hundred eight of the civil prac- 7 tice law and rules. The notice shall include the date or dates of such 8 violations, the description of the violations, the amount owed, a state- 9 ment detailing the foreclosure process that will occur if the violations 10 remain unpaid, the process to claim any surplus funds and the contact 11 information for the city's office in charge of receiving payments. 12 6. Tax year. Any unpaid code violations shall be placed on the tax 13 roll the city of Albany is currently in and shall not be placed on a 14 list, roll or levy of delinquent taxes. 15 7. Owner-occupied. Notwithstanding any other applicable provisions of 16 law, nothing in this section shall be applied to a residential dwelling 17 that is owner-occupied or is the primary residence of a homeowner. 18 8. Tenants. Prior to the placement of any property with unpaid code 19 violations on the tax levy, the city of Albany shall develop a program 20 to assist tenants residing in a dwelling at risk for tax foreclosure due 21 to unpaid code violations. Such program shall include housing counseling 22 assistance or other support in relocating the tenants to suitable hous- 23 ing prior to the tax foreclosure. 24 9. Payment plan. Nothing in this section shall preclude an owner or 25 landlord from entering into a payment plan with the city of Albany for 26 past amounts due for code violations. 27 10. Curing code violations. (a) If all of the violations for which the 28 penalties, fees and costs have been assessed are cured, removed or 29 corrected prior to the expiration of the period for redemption set forth 30 by the city of Albany for the city's annual tax levy, the property shall 31 be removed from the levy and auction and the balance of the amount owed 32 shall be placed as a lien on the property pursuant to applicable laws 33 for debt collection and an action for foreclosure of the property shall 34 not be maintained for the amount owed. 35 (b) The determination of whether or not the code violations have been 36 cured shall be made by the city of Albany's enforcing officer in charge 37 of ensuring compliance with applicable housing, building, and fire codes 38 such as a code enforcement officer. An appeal of this determination may 39 be made to the city's zoning board of appeals or other local administra- 40 tive body as provided for in local law. The final determination made by 41 the administrative body shall be reviewable pursuant to article seven- 42 ty-eight of the civil practice law and rules. 43 (c) This section shall not be applicable to any cause of action 44 brought for money due based on the curing of code violations under any 45 form for receivership or a mechanics lien. 46 11. Payment prior to auction. (a) If the balance owed for code 47 violations placed on the tax levy is paid prior to the expiration of the 48 period for redemption set forth by the city of Albany for the city's 49 annual tax levy and there is no balance due for unpaid real property 50 taxes, the property may not be auctioned, and the property shall be 51 removed from the tax levy. 52 (b) The owner shall have the right to pay the full balance prior to 53 the expiration of the period for redemption set forth by the city of 54 Albany for the city's annual tax levy in order to redeem the property. 55 12. Surplus. Any surplus funds remaining after the sale of a property 56 at a tax foreclosure for unpaid code violations shall be returned to the A. 11470 3 1 former owner of the property in a manner provided under local law. For 2 the purpose of this section, "surplus funds" shall mean the balance of 3 money received after auction of a property at a tax foreclosure sale 4 minus the amount owed for code violations and the costs and attorneys' 5 fees incurred in the collection of the fees by the city. 6 13. Balance due. If after an auction a balance is due for code 7 violations, the city of Albany may proceed with any action against the 8 former owner pursuant to applicable laws. 9 14. Exclusions. The provisions of this section shall not apply if the 10 city of Albany sells their tax liens in a tax lien sale. 11 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
