3 § 6921. Definition of practice of certified registered nurse anesthe-
4 tist. 1. In addition to the provisions of practice for registered
5 professional nursing outlined in section sixty-nine hundred two of this
6 title, the practice of a certified registered nurse anesthetist further
7 includes, but is not limited to, administration of anesthesia and
8 sedation to a patient; performing and documenting a preanesthetic
9 assessment and evaluation of the patient, including requesting consulta-
10 tions and diagnostic studies; selecting, obtaining, ordering, and admin-
11 istering preanesthetic medications and fluids; obtaining informed
12 consent for anesthesia, developing and implementing an anesthetic plan;
13 initiating the anesthetic technique; monitoring the patient including
14 selecting, applying, and inserting appropriate noninvasive and invasive
15 monitoring modalities for continuous evaluation of the patient's phys-
16 ical status; selecting, obtaining, and administering the anesthetics,
17 adjuvant and accessory drugs, and fluids necessary to manage anesthetics
18 and acute and chronic pain; ordering and evaluating laboratory and diag-
19 nostic tests and performing point-of-care testing; ordering and evaluat-
20 ing radiographic imaging studies; managing a patient's airway and
21 cardiopulmonary status using current practice modalities; facilitating
22 emergence and recovery from anesthesia by selecting, obtaining, ordering
23 and administering medications, fluids, and ventilatory support;
24 discharging the patient from a postanesthesia care area and providing
25 postanesthesia follow-up evaluation and care; implementing acute and
26 chronic pain management modalities; responding to emergency situations
27 by providing airway management, administration of emergency fluids and
28 drugs, and using basic or advanced cardiac life support techniques; and
29 prescribe tests, drugs and other treatments necessary to anesthesia care
30 provided by a nurse anesthetist.
31 2. (a) A certified registered nurse anesthetist licensed under this
32 article and practicing for thirty-six hundred hours or less shall do so
33 either under the direction of: (i) a physician who has completed a
34 residency program in anesthesiology that is accredited by an accrediting
35 organization acceptable to the department; (ii) a physician who is board
36 certified in anesthesiology by a certifying organization acceptable to
37 the department; (iii) the operating physician who the governing body of
38 the general hospital or ambulatory surgery center, that is subject to
39 section twenty-eight hundred one-b of the public health law, has deter-
40 mined is qualified to supervise anesthesia services in the general
41 hospital or ambulatory surgery center; or (iv) a dentist or podiatrist
42 qualified to provide anesthesia services pursuant to sections sixty-six
43 hundred five-a and seven thousand one of the education law, respective-
44 ly. For purposes of this section, direction shall mean patient care
45 guidance and advice, coordination and communication from the physician,
46 dentist or podiatrist to the certified registered nurse anesthetist
47 practicing for less than thirty-six hundred hours, as needed for the
48 care of the patient.
49 (b) After practicing for more than thirty-six hundred hours, a certi-
50 fied registered nurse anesthetist shall function in an interdependent
51 role as a member of a patient-centered care team, which shall include a
52 physician, podiatrist, or dentist.
53 3. Prescriptions for drugs, devices, and anesthetic agents, anesthesia
54 related agents, and analgesics may be issued by a certified registered
55 nurse anesthetist under this paragraph and section sixty-nine hundred
56 twenty-four of this article, necessary to anesthesia care provided by a

10 § 6922. Practice of certified registered nurse anesthetist and use of
11 the title "certified registered nurse anesthetist" or "nurse anesthe-
12 tist". Only a person licensed or otherwise authorized under this article
13 shall practice as a certified registered nurse anesthetist or nurse
14 anesthetist.
15 § 6923. State board for nursing. The state board for nursing as estab-
16 lished in section sixty-nine hundred four of this title shall be the
17 governing state board for certified registered nurse anesthetists.
18 § 6924. Requirements for a license as a certified registered nurse
19 anesthetist. To qualify for a license as a certified registered nurse
20 anesthetist, an applicant shall fulfill the following requirements:
21 1. Application: file an application with the department;
22 2. License: be New York state licensed and currently registered as a
23 registered professional nurse;
24 3. Education: have received a degree in a program of nurse anesthesia
25 from a program registered by the department or determined by the depart-
26 ment to be substantially equivalent or meet the requirements of section
27 sixty-nine hundred twenty-seven of this article;
28 4. Examination: pass an examination satisfactory to the department
29 after consultation with the state board for nursing and in accordance
30 with the commissioner's regulations;
31 5. Character: be of good moral character as determined by the depart-
32 ment; and
33 6. Fees: pay a fee of one hundred fifty dollars to the department for
34 an initial license and a fee of fifty dollars for each triennial regis-
35 tration period.
36 § 6925. Limited permits. 1. The department may issue a limited permit
37 to an applicant who is New York state licensed and registered as a
38 registered nurse and meets all qualifications for licensure, except the
39 examination.
40 2. Limited permits shall be for two years.
41 3. A limited anesthetist permit shall entitle the holder to practice
42 as a nurse anesthetist only in a health care facility under the
43 personal, on-premise supervision of a certified registered nurse anesth-
44 etist with more than thirty-six hundred hours of practice experience or
45 physician who: (a) has completed a residency program in anesthesiology
46 that is accredited by an accrediting organization acceptable to the
47 department; or (b) is board certified in anesthesiology by a certifying
48 organization acceptable to the department; or (c) has medical staff
49 privileges to provide anesthesia services at the health care facility
50 and with the endorsement of the health care facility.
51 4. Fees. The fee for each limited permit shall be thirty-five dollars.
52 § 6926. Exempt persons. This article shall not be construed:
53 1. to limit or diminish the practice of the profession of nursing as a
54 nurse practitioner or registered professional nurse pursuant to article
55 one hundred thirty-nine of this title or any other law, rule, regulation
56 or certification;

18 § 6927. Special provisions. It shall be lawful for an individual
19 practicing as a certified registered nurse anesthetist in this state as
20 of the effective date of this section that does not meet the educational
21 requirements, but meets the other requirements of section sixty-nine
22 hundred twenty-four of this article, to continue such practice provided
23 that such certified registered nurse anesthetist:
24 1. submits evidence of certification as a certified registered nurse
25 anesthetist by an appropriate national certifying body to the depart-
26 ment;
27 2. has continually maintained active, up-to-date recertification
28 status as a certified registered nurse anesthetist by an appropriate
29 national recertifying body;
30 3. has submitted an application for and obtained licensure as a New
31 York state certified registered nurse anesthetist within three years of
32 the effective date of this article; and
33 4. nothing in this article shall be construed to authorize an individ-
34 ual to continue to practice as or use the title "certified registered
35 nurse anesthetist" or "CRNA" beyond the three-year time frame unless
36 such individual obtains a New York state license under this article.
