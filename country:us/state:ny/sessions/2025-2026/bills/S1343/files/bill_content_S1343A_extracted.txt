AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law, in relation to
establishing the "clean fuel standard of 2025"
The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
bly, do enact as follows:
1 Section 1. Legislative findings and declarations:
2 1. The transportation sector in New York is a leading source of crite-
3 ria pollutants and the leading source of greenhouse gas emissions that
4 endanger public health and welfare by causing and contributing to
5 increased air pollution and dangerous climate change. Meeting the
6 pollution reduction requirements of the Climate Leadership and Community
7 Protection Act will require sharp decreases in transportation-related
8 emissions.
9 2. Shifting from today's petroleum-based transportation fuels to
10 alternative fuels has the potential to significantly reduce transporta-
11 tion emissions of air pollutants and greenhouse gases and is recommended
12 by the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change as an important pathway
13 for holding global warming to 1.5 degrees Celsius.
14 3. The Climate Leadership and Community Protection Act directs the
15 Department of Environmental Protection to promulgate regulations that
16 will reduce greenhouse gas emissions, including from on-road vehicles.
17 4. New York signed a 15-state MOU to develop an action plan to reduce
18 toxic diesel emissions from medium and heavy-duty vehicles by 2050.
19 5. A clean fuels standard regulation would promote innovation
20 production and use of non-petroleum fuels that reduce vehicle and fuel-
21 related air pollution that endangers public health and welfare and
22 disproportionately impacts disadvantaged communities.

5 § 19-0333. Clean fuel standard.
6 1. A clean fuel standard is hereby established. The clean fuel stand-
7 ard is intended to reduce greenhouse gas intensity from the on-road
8 transportation sector by twenty percent by two thousand thirty-three.
9 In advance of two thousand thirty-three and every five years thereaft-
10 er, the department shall promulgate regulations determining the minimum
11 additional greenhouse gas intensity reduction to be achieved over the
12 following five years, with further reductions to be implemented based
13 upon advances in technology and to support achieving the requirements of
14 the New York state climate leadership and community protection act and
15 the goals of the scoping plan established pursuant to section 75-0103 of
16 this chapter, as determined by the commissioner. Fuels which provide
17 net human health benefits through overall air quality improvements rela-
18 tive to diesel and gasoline usage shall be eligible. Aviation fuels
19 shall be exempted from the clean fuel standard due to federal
20 preemption, but sustainable aviation fuel shall be eligible to generate
21 credits on an opt-in basis to help encourage development of a viable
22 sustainable aviation fuel market.
23 2. The clean fuel standard shall apply to all providers of transporta-
24 tion fuels, including electricity, in New York, shall be measured on a
25 full fuels lifecycle basis and may be met through market-based methods
26 by which providers exceeding the performance required by the clean fuel
27 standard shall receive credits that may be applied to future obligations
28 or traded to providers not meeting the clean fuel standard. The gener-
29 ation of credits must use a lifecycle emissions performance-based
30 approach that is technology and feedstock neutral to achieve greenhouse
31 gas reductions. In addition, the department shall consider other envi-
32 ronmental impacts of fuels in determining credits, including but not
33 limited to crop displacement impacts and forms of pollution other than
34 greenhouse gas emissions, whether or not occurring within the United
35 States.
36 3. For purposes of this section the term "providers" shall include,
37 but shall not be limited to, all refiners, blenders, producers or
38 importers of transportation fuels, or enablers of electricity used as
39 transportation fuel, "greenhouse gas intensity" means the quantity of
40 lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions per unit of fuel energy, and "full
41 fuels lifecycle" means the aggregate greenhouse gas emissions, including
42 direct emissions and indirect emissions, such as emissions from direct
43 or indirect land use changes, whether or not such emissions occur within
44 the United States, measured in carbon dioxide equivalents, as such term
45 is defined in subdivision two of section 75-0101 of this chapter, with
46 aggregate per unit fuel energy emissions determined by the commissioner
47 after consideration of all relevant factors, including as contemplated
48 by subdivision four of this section.
49 4. The full fuels lifecycle shall be assessed annually and such
50 assessment shall include all stages of fuel and feedstock production and
51 distribution, including but not limited to feedstock generation or
52 extraction through the distribution, delivery, and use of the finished
53 fuel by the ultimate consumer.
54 5. a. Within twenty-four months following the effective date of this
55 section, the commissioner, in consultation with the New York state ener-
56 gy research and development authority, shall promulgate regulations
