Levenberg MOURNING the death of Lisa K. Nestor, distinguished citizen and devoted member of the Ossining community WHEREAS, There are certain outstanding members of our community who, through their selfless commitment and dedication, have served to better the quality of life in our community and have had a measurable positive impact on the lives of its residents; Lisa K. Nestor was one such individual; and WHEREAS, It is with great sorrow and deep regret that this Legislative Body records the passing of Lisa K. Nestor, a lifelong resident of Ossining, New York, who died on Sunday, June 1, 2025, noting the significance of her purposeful life and accomplishments; and WHEREAS, Born on July 9, 1959, in North Tarrytown, Lisa K. Nestor was the cherished daughter of A. Richard and Helen (Carlson) Kamp; and WHEREAS, A woman of integrity and dedication, Lisa K. Nestor was a proud graduate of Ossining High School, Class of 1977, where she completed her studies with honors; she went on to graduate from the Westchester Business Institute in White Plains, New York, in 1979; and WHEREAS, On August 1, 1981, Lisa married the love of her life, James P. Nestor, at St. Augustine's Church in Ossining, and together they built a life centered on faith, family, and service to their community; and WHEREAS, Lisa K. Nestor dedicated many years of faithful service to the Village of Ossining before her retirement in December 2024; her professional life also included time at the bank then known as Wachovia Bank in Ossining, and during her high school years she worked at Bethel Nursing Home, demonstrating early her strong work ethic and compassion for others; and WHEREAS, Lisa K. Nestor is survived by her devoted husband, James P. Nestor; her beloved sons, James C. Nestor and his wife Suzanne (Moyer), and Ryan M. Nestor and his wife Jessica (Summers); her adored grandchildren, Jackson, Cameron, and Camden; her loving mother, Helen Kamp; her dear siblings, Richard Kamp, David Kamp and his wife Karen, and Brenda Dias; as well as many nieces and nephews and a host of treasured friends; and WHEREAS, Lisa K. Nestor will long be remembered as a loving wife, mother, grandmother, daughter, sister, and friend, whose strength, grace, and generosity profoundly touched the lives of all who knew her; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the death of Lisa K. Nestor, and to extend sincere condolences to her family and friends; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Lisa K. Nestor.
