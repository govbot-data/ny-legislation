        AN ACT to amend the public lands law  and  the  real  property  law,  in
          relation to establishing central depository databases for governmental
          real property transaction records

          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Subdivision 1 of section 2  of  the  public  lands  law  is
     2  amended  by  adding  five  new  paragraphs  c, d, e, f, and g to read as
     3  follows:
     4    c. "Distributed ledger technology" shall mean:
     5    (i) a technology deployed in a distributed fashion and shared across a
     6  network that, when used in  conjunction  with  cryptographic  solutions,
     7  enable  auditable  data  sharing  across  a network of untrusted partic-
     8  ipants; and
     9    (ii) a system that allows multiple  parties  to  share  and  update  a
    10  common  data  source  without  requiring the party to rely on any single
    11  party to maintain and validate the data.
    12    d. "Tokenization" shall mean the process of creating digital ownership
    13  of real world assets.
    14    e. "Digitalization" shall mean the process of  converting  information
    15  into a digital format.
    16    f. "Blockchain" shall mean any technology where data is:
    17    (i)  shared  across  a  network  to create a public ledger of verified
    18  transactions or information among participants;
    19    (ii) linked using cryptography to maintain the integrity of the public
    20  ledger and to execute other functions;
    21    (iii) distributed among network participants in an  automated  fashion
    22  to  concurrently  update network participants on the state of the public
    23  ledger and any other functions; and

     1    (iv) composed of source code that is publicly available.
     2    g.  "Federated  blockchain" shall mean a distributed ledger technology
     3  system:
     4    (i) in which a pre-selected group of entities, rather  than  a  single

    14    §  2.  The  public lands law is amended by adding a new section 2-c to
    15  read as follows:
    16    § 2-c. Central depository database for governmental property.  1.  The
    17  commissioner  of  general  services may establish and maintain a central
    18  depository database for governmental real property  to  compile,  store,
    19  maintain, and secure government real estate records. Such database shall
    20  provide  redundant,  immutable, and permissioned data controlled through
    21  distributed ledger technology, increase efficiency for  state  agencies,
    22  and create transparency while maintaining proper security.
    23    2.  The commissioner of general services, in conjunction with relevant
    24  state agencies, may create and implement a strategy to adopt distributed
    25  ledger technology that uses tokenization and digitalization  within  the
    26  agency  whereby  data  is stored securely and permanently on a federated
    27  blockchain for the purpose of properly securing government  real  estate
    28  records and preventing improper access to and hacking of such records.
    29    3.  The  data  stored  in  such distributed ledger technology shall be
    30  accessible to state agencies, municipalities, and taxpayers.
    31    § 3. Subdivision 12 of section  290  of  the  real  property  law,  as
    32  amended  by  chapter  549  of  the  laws  of 2011, is amended to read as
    33  follows:
    34    12. "Distributed ledger technology" shall mean:
    35    (a) a technology deployed in a distributed fashion and shared across a
    36  network that, when used in  conjunction  with  cryptographic  solutions,
    37  enable  auditable  data  sharing  across  a network of untrusted partic-
    38  ipants; and
    39    (b) a system that allows multiple parties to share and update a common
    40  data source without requiring the party to rely on any single  party  to
    41  maintain and validate the data.
    42    13.  "Tokenization"  shall mean the process of creating digital owner-
    43  ship of real world assets.
    44    14. "Digitalization" shall mean the process of converting  information
    45  into a digital format.
    46    15. "Blockchain" shall mean any technology where data is:
    47    (a)  shared  across  a  network  to create a public ledger of verified
    48  transactions or information among participants;
    49    (b) linked using cryptography to maintain the integrity of the  public
    50  ledger and to execute other functions;
    51    (c)  distributed among network participants in an automated fashion to
    52  concurrently update network participants on  the  state  of  the  public
    53  ledger and any other functions; and
    54    (d) composed of source code that is publicly available.
    55    16.  "Federated blockchain" shall mean a distributed ledger technology
    56  system:

    18    § 291-k. Central depository database for real  property  transactions.
    19  1.    The office of the clerk of the county or city registrar may, where
    20  applicable, establish and maintain a  central  depository  database  for
    21  governmental  real property to compile, store, maintain, and secure real
    22  estate records. Such database shall provide  redundant,  immutable,  and
    23  permissioned  data  controlled  through  distributed  ledger technology,
    24  increase efficiency for state agencies, and  create  transparency  while
    25  maintaining proper security.
    26    2.  The office of the clerk of the county or city registrar may, where
    27  applicable, create and implement a strategy to adopt distributed  ledger
    28  technology  that  uses tokenization and digitalization within the agency
    29  whereby data is stored securely and permanently on  a  federated  block-
    30  chain  for  the  purpose  of  properly  securing  government real estate
    31  records and preventing improper access to and hacking of such records.
