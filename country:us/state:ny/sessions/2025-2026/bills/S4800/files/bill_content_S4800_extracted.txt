Resolved (if the Assembly concur), That section 3 of arti- 2 cle 13 of the constitution be amended to read as follows: 3 § 3. (a) The legislature shall provide for filling vacancies in 4 office, and in case of elective officers, no person appointed to fill a 5 vacancy shall hold his or her office by virtue of such appointment long- 6 er than the commencement of the political year next succeeding the first 7 annual election after the happening of the vacancy; provided, however, 8 that nothing contained in this article shall prohibit the filling of 9 vacancies on boards of education, including boards of education of 10 community districts in the city school district of the city of New York, 11 by appointment until the next regular school district election, whether 12 or not such appointment shall extend beyond the thirty-first day of 13 December in any year. 14 (b) In the case of a special election to fill a vacancy of a federal 15 officer: 16 (i) a vacancy occurring three months before the general election in 17 any year in any office authorized to be filled at a general election, 18 shall be filled at the general election held next thereafter, unless 19 otherwise provided by the constitution, or unless previously filled at a 20 special election. 21 (ii) A vacancy occurring by the expiration of term at the end of an 22 even numbered year in an office which may not under the provisions of 23 the constitution be filled for a full term at the general election held 24 prior to the expiration of such term, shall be filled at said general 25 election for a term ending with the commencement of the political year EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. If such vacancy occurs in any 30 even numbered calendar year on or before the sixtieth day prior to an 31 annual primary election, the governor shall make a temporary appointment 32 to fill such vacancy until the third day of January in the next calendar 33 year. If a vacancy occurs in the office of United States senator from 34 this state in any odd numbered calendar year, the governor shall make a 35 temporary appointment to fill such vacancy until the third day of Janu- 36 ary in the next odd numbered calendar year. Such an appointment shall be 37 evidenced by a certificate of the governor which shall be filed in the 38 office of the state board of elections. At the time for filing such 39 certificate, the governor shall issue and file in the office of the 40 state board of elections a writ of election directing the election of a 41 United States senator to fill such vacancy for the unexpired term at the 42 general election next preceding the expiration for the term of such 43 appointment. 44 § 2.
