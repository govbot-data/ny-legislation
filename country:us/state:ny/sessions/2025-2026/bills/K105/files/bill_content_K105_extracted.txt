Conrad CONGRATULATING Mary Alyce Burns upon the occasion of celebrating her 100th Birthday WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to commemorate and recognize certain milestones celebrated by citizens of this great Empire State; and WHEREAS, Senior citizens bring a wealth of experience and knowledge to the increasingly active roles they play in today's society; their past contributions and future participation are a vital part of, and valuable asset to, the fabric of community life and activity; and WHEREAS, Attendant to such concern, and in full accord with its long-standing traditions, this Legislative Body is justly proud to congratulate Mary Alyce Burns upon the occasion of her 100th Birthday to be celebrated on Wednesday, March 12, 2025; and WHEREAS, Mary Alyce Burns was born on March 12, 1925, in Buffalo, New York; she attended Lafayette High School and later graduated from D'Youville College with a bachelor's degree in history; and WHEREAS, On December 27, 1947, Mary Alyce married the late Edward B. Burns at Holy Angels Church in Buffalo; she then left the workforce to focus on raising their children: Alice, John, Joanne, Lois, and Mary Ann; through the years, their family grew to include 10 grandchildren, 17 great-grandchildren, and 1 great-great-grandchild, all of whom she deeply cherishes; and WHEREAS, Mary Alyce Burns had always been active in her community, serving on several Women's Organizations boards, including the Customs and Immigration Wives Club, New York State Federation of Women's Clubs, Honorarians and Pro-To; she was twice honored with the esteemed position of Board President for the Buffalo Federation of Women's Clubs and St. John the Baptist's Neumann Guild; and WHEREAS, Furthermore, Mary Alyce Burns was a devoted parishioner at St. John the Baptist in the Town of Tonawanda where she served for several years on the Church Council and as a Eucharist Minister; she also spent many years as a volunteer for Channel Four's Call 4 Action; and WHEREAS, Mary Alyce Burns, with her zest for life, has inspired and enhanced the lives of her family and friends; and WHEREAS, In her leisure time, Mary Alyce Burns enjoyed meeting up with her eight friends from college, a group known between them as "The Girls," for dinners and lunches; four of these beloved friends took pleasure in watching theater, and carried season tickets to Shaw Festival in Niagara on the Lake and to Studio Arena; and WHEREAS, Mary Alyce Burns cherishes all the time she has spent, and continues to spend, with her family and friends over her storied life; she reflects fondly of the time spent square dancing with her husband, and now loves playing games such as Scrabble and Rumikub, as well as reading with the book club she started at her senior apartment complex; and WHEREAS, This exceptional centenarian has experienced the incredible joys and sorrows characteristic of and reserved for those with the stamina and courage to savor a full life; and WHEREAS, Mary Alyce Burns has enriched the lives of those around her through her joyous and sincere love for others and through the quiescent charm and wisdom which comes only from a fullness of years; and WHEREAS, It is the intent of this Legislative Body to publicly recognize those who have reached such a remarkable age and who have witnessed and celebrated the innovations, cultural developments and magnificent achievements of this country during the last century, while themselves contributing to the growth and excellence of this great Empire State; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to congratulate Mary Alyce Burns upon the occasion of celebrating her 100th Birthday; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to Mary Alyce Burns.
