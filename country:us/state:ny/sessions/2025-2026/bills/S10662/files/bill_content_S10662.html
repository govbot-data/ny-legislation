Amd §3-304, El L
 
Prohibits a board of elections employee or election commissioner from being employed by or controlling any political action committee that is organized for the purpose of any election administered by the board at which such employee is employed or which such commissioner oversees.

        AN  ACT  to amend the election law, in relation to conflicts of interest
          of boards of elections employees and election commissioners
 
          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:

     1    Section  1.  Section 3-304 of election law, as added by chapter 639 of
     2  the laws of 2024, subdivision 2 as amended by chapter 105 of the laws of
     3  2025, is amended to read as follows:
     4    § 3-304. Board employees and commissioners; conflicts of interest.  1.
     5  As used in this section, the following terms shall  have  the  following
     6  meanings:
     7    (a)  "Board  of  elections  employee"  shall  mean any person directly
     8  compensated with wages or salary by a board of elections for their full-
     9  time or part-time work, excluding election inspectors, poll clerks,  and
    10  election  coordinators  compensated  solely for their work at a general,
    11  primary, or special election.
    12    (b) "Election commissioner" shall mean as provided in subdivision  two
    13  of section 3-200 of this article.
    14    (c)  "Political action committee" shall mean as defined in subdivision
    15  sixteen of section 14-100 of this chapter.
    16    (d) "Operational control" shall mean actual and strategic control over
    17  the day-to-day affairs of a political action committee.
    18    2. No board of elections employee shall engage in  or  participate  in
    19  any trade or business which creates, or may tend to create, an actual or
    20  potential  conflict  of  interest.  No board of elections employee shall
    21  maintain a direct financial interest in or be employed by a vendor or  a
    22  company  providing  services to a candidate who has an election overseen
    23  by such employee's office, including but not limited to, printing compa-
    24  nies, election consulting companies, direct mail companies  and  digital
    25  marketing  companies.  No  board  of elections employee shall maintain a
    26  direct financial interest in or be employed by a vendor or company  that
    27  sells  to  the board voting machines, electronic poll books, printers or
    28  other technical or electronic equipment.  A  violation  of  any  of  the
