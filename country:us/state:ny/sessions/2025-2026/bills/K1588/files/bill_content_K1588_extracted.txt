O'Pharrow MOURNING the untimely death of Edeedson Cine Jr., beloved son, brother, grandson, and member of his community WHEREAS, It is the custom of this Legislative Body to honor and pay tribute to individuals whose lives exemplified kindness, promise, devotion to family, and commitment to their community, and to express its deepest sympathy upon their untimely passing; and WHEREAS, Edeedson Cine Jr., affectionately known as "Eddy," of West Babylon, New York, died tragically on December 25, 2025, at the age of 23; and WHEREAS, Edeedson Cine Jr. was born on July 29, 2002, at North Shore LIJ University Hospital in Manhasset, New York, the cherished son of Edeedson Cine, Sr. and Marie Rooseveline Cine; and WHEREAS, Eddy attended Parliament Place Elementary School and Robert Moses Middle School before graduating from North Babylon High School in 2020; and WHEREAS, Following his graduation, he pursued higher education at Nassau Community College before continuing his studies at Hunter Business School, where he successfully completed the Computer Technician Program; and WHEREAS, A hardworking and determined young man, Eddy held positions with several employers throughout his young career and was employed as a Customer Service Clerk and Associate at CVS in Lindenhurst, New York; and WHEREAS, Eddy was known by all who knew him as a caring, thoughtful, and compassionate individual whose warm spirit and gentle nature left a lasting impression on family members, friends, coworkers, and neighbors alike; and WHEREAS, He possessed many talents and passions, including a love of cooking and baking for those he cared about most, especially preparing his favorite meals of pasta, T-bone steak, and late-night eggs; and WHEREAS, Eddy also loved music of many genres, sang in youth and children's choirs, played the drums, and brought joy to others through his creativity, energy, and enthusiasm for life; and WHEREAS, He took pride in his appearance, valued self-expression, and was admired for his sense of style, while remaining grounded by his loving heart and devotion to family and friends; and WHEREAS, Though his years were far too few, Eddy's life was filled with love, laughter, faith, and meaningful connections that will continue to inspire all who were blessed to know him; and WHEREAS, He is survived by his loving parents, Edeedson Cine Sr. and Marie Rooseveline Cine; his brother, Jonathan S. Cine; his sisters, Edelyne Cine and Faith R. Cine; his maternal grandmother, Jacqueline Beaubrun; his paternal grandparents, Joseph Cine and Rose I. Cine; and a large and devoted extended family of aunts, uncles, cousins, and countless friends who mourn his passing and cherish his memory; and WHEREAS, The passing of Edeedson Cine Jr. leaves a profound sorrow in the hearts of his family, friends, and the entire community, yet his legacy of kindness, love, perseverance, and faith will forever endure; now, therefore, be it RESOLVED, That this Legislative Body pause in its deliberations to mourn the untimely death of Edeedson Cine Jr., to honor and celebrate his life and legacy, and to extend its deepest condolences to his family and loved ones; and be it further RESOLVED, That a copy of this Resolution, suitably engrossed, be transmitted to the family of Edeedson Cine Jr. Go to top Sitemap Request Information Directions and Maps
