BILL NOS01500

COSPNSRWEBER
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Amd §1180-a, V & T L
 
Authorizes an increase in the maximum speed limit to 70 mph for travel on roadways that currently have a maximum speed limit of 65 mph.

        AN  ACT to amend the vehicle and traffic law, in relation to authorizing
          an increase in the maximum speed limit for travel on certain roadways

          The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and  Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section  1.  Section 1180-a of the vehicle and traffic law, as amended
     2  by chapter 464 of the laws of 2003, is amended to read as follows:
     3    § 1180-a. Maximum speed limits. 1. Notwithstanding any other provision
     4  of law, no city, village,  town,  county,  public  authority,  division,
     5  office or department of the state shall maintain or create (a) any speed
     6  limit in excess of fifty-five miles per hour on any road, highway, park-
     7  way  or  bridge  or (b) any speed limit on any other portion of a public
     8  highway, which is not uniformly applicable to all types of  motor  vehi-
     9  cles  using  such  portion  of  highway,  if on November first, nineteen
    10  hundred seventy-three, such portion of highway had a speed  limit  which
    11  was  uniformly  applicable  to  all  types  of  motor vehicles using it;
    12  provided however, a lower speed limit may be established for any vehicle
    13  operating under a special permit because of any weight or  dimension  of
    14  such  vehicle,  including  any  load  thereon, and (c) provided further,
    15  paragraph (b) of this subdivision shall not apply to any  portion  of  a
    16  highway  during such time that the condition of the highway, weather, an
    17  accident, or other condition creates a temporary hazard to the safety of
    18  traffic on such portion of a highway. However, the commissioner  of  the
    19  department  of transportation may establish a maximum speed limit of not
    20  more than [sixty-five] seventy miles per hour on any state roadway which
    21  meets department criteria for such maximum speed.
    22    2. Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraphs (a) and (b) of  subdi-
    23  vision one of this section, upon The Governor Thomas E. Dewey Thruway as
    24  such  term  is  defined in section three hundred fifty-six of the public
    25  authorities law, the New York state thruway authority  may  establish  a
    26  maximum speed limit of not more than [sixty-five] seventy miles per hour
    27  provided  that  such  maximum  allowable  speed  limit is established in
    28  accordance with all applicable rules and regulations.
