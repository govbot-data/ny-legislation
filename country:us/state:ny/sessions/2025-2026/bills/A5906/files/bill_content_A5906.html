        AN  ACT  to  amend  the labor law, in relation to requiring employers to
          disclose information about benefits and other compensation,  including
          bonuses, stock options, and commissions
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. This act shall be known and may be cited  as  the  "benefit
     2  transparency act".
     3    §  2.  Section 194-b of the labor law, as amended by chapter 94 of the
     4  laws of 2023, is amended to read as follows:
     5    §  194-b.  Mandatory  disclosure   of   compensation   or   range   of
     6  compensation, other compensation, benefits, and position description. 1.
     7  a.    No  employer,  employment agency, employee, or agent thereof shall
     8  advertise a job, promotion, or transfer opportunity that will physically
     9  be performed, at least in part, in the state of New  York,  including  a
    10  job,   promotion,  or  transfer  opportunity  that  will  physically  be
    11  performed outside of New York but reports to a  supervisor,  office,  or
    12  other work site in New York without disclosing the following:
    13    (i)  the  compensation  or  a  range  of  compensation  for  such job,
    14  promotion, or transfer opportunity; [and]
    15    (ii) any other forms of compensation not required to be  disclosed  in
    16  subparagraph  (i) of this paragraph for which the position may be eligi-
    17  ble, including bonuses, stocks, bonds, options, and equity or ownership;

     1    (iii) a general description of all benefits that an  employer  reason-
     2  ably  expects in good faith to offer for such job, promotion or transfer
     3  opportunity, including health insurance benefits, paid  time  off,  paid
     4  disability insurance, and paid family leave; and
     5    (iv) the job description for such job, promotion, or transfer opportu-
     6  nity[, if such description exists].
     7    b.  If  an employer offers a base salary outside the advertised range,
     8  the employer shall retain a  written  record  listing  the  reasons  for
     9  offering  a  salary  outside the advertised range for no less than three
    10  years after the date the position is filled.
    11    c. An employer, employment agency, employee, or agent  thereof  adver-
    12  tising  for  a  job,  promotion,  or transfer opportunity paid solely on
    13  commission shall maintain compliance with subparagraph (i) of  paragraph
    14  a  of  this  subdivision  by disclosing a general statement that compen-
    15  sation shall be based on commission.
    16    d. An employment agency, employer, or employee or agent thereof  shall
    17  disclose  to current employees upon request, but no more frequently than
    18  annually, the current range of compensation, including base  salary  and
    19  other  forms  of monetary and non-monetary compensation for such employ-
    20  ee's current job title  and  any  other  positions  requiring  equal  or
    21  substantially   similar  skill,  effort,  and  responsibility  that  are
    22  performed under similar working conditions. For any  other  non-monetary
    23  compensation,  the  range  shall  be  calculated using the units of such
    24  non-monetary compensation.
    25    e. An employment agency, employer, or employee or  agent  thereof  who
    26  does not issue an advertisement for a job, promotion, or transfer oppor-
    27  tunity  shall  disclose  the  minimum  and maximum wages, including base
    28  compensation, non-wage compensation, and benefits which may  be  offered
    29  for  such  position upon the request of any prospective candidate at any
    30  point during the hiring process.
    31    f. Within five business days of making an offer of  employment  for  a
    32  job,   promotion,  or  transfer  opportunity  that  will  physically  be
    33  performed, at least in part, in the state of New York, an employer shall
    34  provide the offeree with information related to  current  benefit  plans
    35  that  such  offeree  would be eligible for if such offeree were to imme-
    36  diately commence employment, if such offeree is eligible for any benefit
    37  plans through the employer or through a union, including:
    38    (i) the identity of any available insurance plans;
    39    (ii) premium costs attributable to the employee  in  a  specified  pay
    40  period;
    41    (iii) annual deductibles;
    42    (iv) coinsurance;
    43    (v) copayments;
    44    (vi) annual or lifetime caps on benefits;
    45    (vii) coverage of preventive services;
    46    (viii) coverage for prenatal and reproductive health care;
    47    (ix) coverage of existing and new drugs;
    48    (x) coverage for medical tests, devices, and procedures;
    49    (xi) a link to search for in-network providers, if available; and
    50    (xii) out-of-network coverage provisions.
    51    2.  No  employer  shall  refuse to interview, hire, promote, employ or
    52  otherwise retaliate against an applicant or current employee  for  exer-
    53  cising any rights under this section.
    54    3. The commissioner shall promulgate rules and regulations to effectu-
    55  ate the provisions of this section.

        A. 5906--C                          3
 
     1    4.  The department shall conduct a public awareness outreach campaign,
     2  which shall include making information  available  on  its  website  and
     3  otherwise informing employers of the provisions of this section.
     4    5.  a.  Any  person  claiming  to  be aggrieved by a violation of this
     5  section may file  with  the  commissioner  a  complaint  regarding  such
     6  alleged  violation  for an investigation of such complaint and statement
     7  setting the appropriate remedy, if any, pursuant to  the  provisions  of
     8  section one hundred ninety-six-a of this article.
     9    b.  An  employer  who  fails  to  comply  with any requirement of this
    10  section or any  regulation  published  thereunder  shall  be  deemed  in
    11  violation  of  this  section  and shall be subject to a civil penalty in
    12  accordance with section two hundred eighteen of this chapter.
    13    6. For the purposes of this section the following terms shall have the
    14  following meanings:
    15    a. "range of compensation" shall mean the minimum and  maximum  annual
    16  salary or hourly range of compensation for a job, promotion, or transfer
    17  opportunity  that  the employer in good faith believes to be accurate at
    18  the time of the posting of an advertisement for such  opportunity  based
    19  on  factors  such as relevant qualifications, the budgeted amount avail-
    20  able for the position, applicable pay scale or compensation model relied
    21  upon by the  employer,  the  actual  range  of  compensation  for  those
    22  currently  holding the position or equivalent positions, or other opera-
    23  tional considerations.
    24    b. "employer" shall mean:
    25    (i) any person, corporation, limited liability  company,  association,
    26  labor  organization  or  entity  employing four or more employees in any
    27  occupation, industry, trade, business or service, or any agent  thereof;
    28  and
    29    (ii)  any  person, corporation, limited liability company, association
    30  or entity acting as an  employment  agent  or  recruiter,  or  otherwise
    31  connecting applicants with employers, provided that "employer" shall not
    32  include  a  temporary  help  firm as such term is defined by subdivision
    33  five of section nine hundred sixteen of this chapter.
    34    c. "advertise" shall mean to make available to  a  pool  of  potential
    35  applicants  for  internal or public viewing, including electronically, a
    36  written description of an employment opportunity.
    37    d. "offeree" shall mean any individual with a pending offer of employ-
    38  ment for a job, promotion, or transfer opportunity.
    39    7. The provisions of this section shall not be construed or interpret-
    40  ed to supersede or preempt any provisions of local law, rules, or  regu-
    41  lations, or to narrow in any way the scope of the definition of wages in
    42  subdivision one of section one hundred ninety of this article.
    43    §  3.  Severability.  If any clause, sentence, paragraph, subdivision,
    44  section or part of this act shall be adjudged by any court of  competent
    45  jurisdiction  to  be invalid, such judgment shall not affect, impair, or
    46  invalidate the remainder thereof, but shall be confined in its operation
    47  to the clause, sentence, paragraph, subdivision, section or part thereof
    48  directly involved in the controversy in which such judgment  shall  have
    49  been rendered. It is hereby declared to be the intent of the legislature
    50  that  this  act  would have been enacted even if such invalid provisions
    51  had not been included herein.
    52    § 4. This act shall take effect on  the  two  hundred  seventieth  day
    53  after it shall have become a law.
