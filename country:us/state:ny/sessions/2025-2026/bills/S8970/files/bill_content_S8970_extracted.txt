ASHBY -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Environmental Conservation AN ACT to amend the environmental conservation law, in relation to establishing a municipal solar planning grant program The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Article 54 of the environmental conservation law is amended 2 by adding a new title 17 to read as follows: 3 TITLE 17 4 MUNICIPAL SOLAR PLANNING GRANT PROGRAM 5 Section 54-1701. Municipal solar planning grant program. 6 54-1702. Municipal solar planning grant program database. 7 § 54-1701. Municipal solar planning grant program. 8 1. For the purposes of this title, the term "solar energy system" 9 shall mean a renewable energy system, as defined by section sixty-six-p 10 of the public service law, that generates electricity through solar 11 thermal technology or photovoltaics technology. 12 2. The department, in conjunction with the New York state energy 13 research and development authority, shall establish the municipal solar 14 planning grant program to provide financial assistance to municipalities 15 that submit a plan for the development of solar energy systems. Such 16 submitted plans shall include a ratio, as determined by the New York 17 state energy research and development authority, of solar energy systems 18 housed on former agricultural land and open space land compared to 19 commercial land, rooftops and parking lots. 20 3. Such program shall determine the following: 21 (a) definitions of agricultural land and commercially-zoned properties 22 for the purposes of the program; 23 (b) eligible municipalities under the program; 24 (c) maximum per-municipality awards under the program; and 25 (d) any other standards, guidelines or rules as may be necessary to 26 administer the program. 27 § 54-1702. Municipal solar planning grant program database. EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. LBD14536-02-6 S. 8970 2 1 The department, in consultation with relevant state agencies, munici- 2 palities and the federal government, shall establish a database for 3 municipalities to track their distribution of solar energy systems on 4 agricultural land versus commercially-zoned properties, including, but 5 not limited to, the address, square footage and approximate age of such 6 solar energy systems. Such database shall be designed to enable munici- 7 palities to regularly update the information therein. 8 § 2. This act shall take effect immediately.
