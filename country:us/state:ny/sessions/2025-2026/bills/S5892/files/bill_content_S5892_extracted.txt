LANZA -- read twice and ordered printed, and when printed to be committed to the Committee on Codes AN ACT to amend the penal law and the criminal procedure law, in relation to revocation of probation and re-sentencing upon conviction of a felony while under probation supervision for a felony conviction The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Subdivision 4 of section 60.01 of the penal law, as amended 2 by chapter 548 of the laws of 1984, is amended to read as follows: 3 4. In any case where a person has been sentenced to a period of 4 probation imposed pursuant to section 65.00 of this [chapter] title, if 5 the part of the sentence that provides for probation is revoked, the 6 court must sentence such person to imprisonment or to the sentence of 7 imprisonment and probation as provided for in paragraph (d) of subdivi- 8 sion two of this section. Provided, however, that where a person who 9 has been sentenced to a period of probation upon conviction of a felony 10 is subsequently convicted of a felony or is convicted of an offense in 11 another jurisdiction which if committed in this state would constitute a 12 felony, which subsequent felony is committed while under such person's 13 present probation supervision, and a new indeterminate or determinate 14 sentence is imposed for such subsequent felony, the part of the sentence 15 that provided for probation shall be revoked and such person shall be 16 sentenced to a term of imprisonment of over one year. Such term shall 17 run consecutively to the term of imprisonment imposed for the subsequent 18 felony, unless the court determines that mitigating circumstances exist, 19 in which case the court must place such circumstances on the record and 20 a concurrent sentence may be imposed. 21 § 2. Section 70.25 of the penal law is amended by adding a new subdi- 22 vision 6 to read as follows: 23 6.
