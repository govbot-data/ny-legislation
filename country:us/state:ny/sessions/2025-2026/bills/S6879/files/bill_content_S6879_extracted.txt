Legislative findings. 1. The legislature recognizes that 2 climate change and environmental degradation are two of the greatest 3 challenges facing the state and the world today, and that we must mobi- 4 lize New York's workforce and youth to create a clean energy economy and 5 strengthen our communities and ecosystems in the face of climate 6 impacts. 7 2. The legislature finds that service provides a unique opportunity to 8 mobilize young adults to build clean energy and climate-resilient commu- 9 nities, economies, and ecosystems. Growing equity-centered, climate-re- 10 lated service programs and addressing critical gaps in service opportu- 11 nities will broaden access to service, ensuring that young adults of all 12 backgrounds, especially from overburdened communities and vulnerable 13 populations, can serve. Doing so will also ensure that service programs 14 address the needs of communities across the state, especially those 15 communities disproportionately impacted by environmental and health 16 burdens. 17 3. The legislature further finds that as our state transitions away 18 from a fossil fuel-based economy, we must do so in a way that fosters 19 innovation, investment, and growth in clean energy technology sectors 20 and jobs so our businesses, workforce, and communities can thrive. As 21 state, federal, local, and tribal governments implement policies to 22 mitigate the destructive forces of climate change, there will be conse- 23 quences for New York's businesses, workers, and communities. Accomplish- 24 ing an equitable transition will require identification of future indus- 25 try occupations and skill needs, the existing workforce's transferable EXPLANATION--Matter in italics (underscored) is new; matter in brackets [ ] is old law to be omitted. Establishment. 37 The New York youth climate corps is established to support and grow 38 climate-related service opportunities for young adults with the objec- 39 tive of building low-carbon and climate-resilient communities, ecosys- 40 tems, and economies while providing education, workforce development, 41 and career pathways to service members, with a focus on disadvantaged 42 communities as defined in section 75-0111 of this chapter. The New York 43 youth climate corps shall be an office within the New York commission on 44 national and community service, a governmental entity created by execu- 45 tive order one hundred seventy-nine, signed December thirtieth, nineteen 46 hundred ninety-three. 47 § 59-0105.
