A07595 Summary:

BILL NOA07595
 
SAME ASSAME AS S06210
 
SPONSORRomero
 
COSPNSR
 
MLTSPNSR
 
Ren Art 32 §670 to be Art 32-A §676, add §677, Gen Bus L
 
Directs the department of health to establish a right to preventative maintenance of wheelchairs program, after consultation with certain other offices, under which owners of manual and powered wheelchairs shall be entitled to reimbursement of certain preventative maintenance costs for such wheelchairs.

        Introduced by M. of A. ROMERO -- read once and referred to the Committee

        AN  ACT  to amend the general business law, in relation to directing the
          department of health to establish a right to preventative  maintenance
          of wheelchairs program
 
          The  People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem-
        bly, do enact as follows:
 
     1    Section 1. Article 32 of the general business law, as amended by chap-
     2  ter 219 of the laws of 2006, is renumbered  article  32-A,  and  section
     3  670,  as  amended  by  chapter  219  of  the laws of 2006, is renumbered
     4  section 676.
     5    § 2. The general business law is amended by adding a new  section  677
     6  to read as follows:
     7    §  677.  Preventative maintenance of wheelchairs. 1. No later than one
     8  year after the effective date of this section,  and  after  consultation
     9  with  the deputy secretary for human services and mental hygiene and the
    10  office of the chief disability officer, the department of  health  shall
    11  establish  a  "preventative  maintenance  of  wheelchairs program" under
    12  which owners and lessors  of  wheelchairs  shall  be  afforded  biannual
    13  appointments  for  preventative  maintenance  in  accordance  with  this
    14  section.
    15    2. The commissioner of health shall promulgate rules, regulations, and
    16  reporting requirements to establish the program pursuant to  subdivision
    17  one of this section. Factors considered shall include, but not be limit-
    18  ed  to,  length of enrollment in the program and ways to certify author-
    19  ized repair providers.
    20    3. (a) The commissioner of social  services  shall  maintain  specific
    21  reimbursement  and billing procedures under this section for wheelchairs
    22  to ensure that Medicaid payments for wheelchair repairs permit  adequate
    23  access to such products and services, and takes into account the signif-
    24  icant resources, infrastructure, and staff needed.
    25    (b)  The commissioner of social services shall monitor the addition of
    26  new billing codes for wheelchair repairs by  the  Medicare  program  and

     1  shall  expeditiously  incorporate  such  codes  in  accordance with this

     3    (c)  Where reimbursement rates are determined by a managed care organ-
     4  ization, such rates shall be determined consistent  with  this  subdivi-

     6  mark reimbursement rates to be paid by managed care organizations  under
     7  this paragraph.
     8    4. As used in this section, the following terms shall have the follow-
     9  ing meanings:
    10    (a)  "Authorized repair provider" means an individual or business that
    11  is unaffiliated with an original equipment manufacturer and that has  an
    12  arrangement with such original equipment manufacturer, for a definite or
    13  indefinite  period,  under  which  such  original equipment manufacturer
    14  grants to such individual or business a license to  use  a  trade  name,
    15  service mark, or other proprietary identifier for the purposes of offer-
    16  ing  the  services  of  inspection, diagnosis, maintenance, or repair of
    17  wheelchairs under the name of such original equipment  manufacturer,  or
    18  other  arrangement  with  such  original equipment manufacturer to offer
    19  such services on behalf of  such  original  equipment  manufacturer.  An
    20  original  equipment manufacturer that offers the services of inspection,
    21  diagnosis, maintenance, or repair of its own wheelchairs, and that  does
    22  not  have  an arrangement described under this subdivision with an unaf-
    23  filiated individual or  business,  shall  be  considered  an  authorized
    24  repair provider with respect to such wheelchairs.
    25    (b)  "Independent  repair  provider"  means an individual or business,
    26  other than the original equipment manufacturer, that is engaged  in  the
    27  services of inspection, diagnosis, maintenance, or repair of wheelchairs
    28  for purposes of returning such wheelchairs to the safety and performance
    29  specifications  established  by such original equipment manufacturer and
    30  to meet its original intended use.
    31    (c) "Original equipment manufacturer" means a business engaged in  the
    32  selling,  leasing, or otherwise supplying new wheelchairs or parts manu-
    33  factured by, or on behalf of, itself, to any individual or business.
    34    (d) "Preventative maintenance" means an assessment of a wheelchair and
    35  its use by an  authorized  repair  provider  or  an  original  equipment
    36  manufacturer to ensure that such wheelchair maintains its originally-de-
    37  signed  quality,  function, and utility, and that includes an assessment
    38  of:
    39    (i) the physical condition of the complex rehabilitation technology;
    40    (ii) a review of all maintenance that is listed in  such  wheelchair's
    41  owner's manual; and
    42    (iii)  any  needed  repairs  that  such  authorized independent repair
    43  provider or original equipment manufacturer is able to complete.
    44    (e) "Repair" means the repair or replacement of a  deficient,  broken,
    45  or otherwise malfunctioning part, component, hardware, or software, when
    46  such  deficient, broken, or otherwise malfunctioning state of such part,
    47  component, hardware, or software results in the incapacity of or  other-
    48  wise diminished capacity for use of a wheelchair.
    49    (f)  "Wheelchair"  means a manual or motorized wheeled device designed
    50  for use by a person with a physical disability, and shall apply to  such
    51  devices either owned or leased.
    52    §  3.  This  act shall take effect on the ninetieth day after it shall
    53  have become a law. Effective immediately, the addition, amendment and/or
    54  repeal of any rule or regulation necessary  for  the  implementation  of
    55  this  act  on its effective date are authorized to be made and completed
    56  on or before such effective date.
