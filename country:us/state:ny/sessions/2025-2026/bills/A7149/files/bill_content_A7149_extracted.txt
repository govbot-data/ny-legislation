BICHOTTE HERMELYN, GIBBS, KIM -- read once and referred to the Committee on Health AN ACT to amend the social services law, in relation to requiring managed care providers that provide coverage for prescription drugs to provide coverage for home delivery of any covered prescription drug by any non-mail-order retail pharmacy in the managed care provider network The People of the State of New York, represented in Senate and Assem- bly, do enact as follows: 1 Section 1. Paragraph (u) of subdivision 4 of section 364-j of the 2 social services law, as amended by section 12 of part A of chapter 56 of 3 the laws of 2013, is amended to read as follows: 4 (u) A managed care provider that provides coverage for prescription 5 drugs shall permit each participant to fill any mail order covered 6 prescription, at [his or her] the participant's option, at any mail 7 order pharmacy or non-mail-order retail pharmacy in the managed care 8 provider network. A managed care provider that provides coverage for 9 prescription drugs shall also provide coverage for home delivery of any 10 covered prescription drug by any non-mail-order retail pharmacy in the 11 managed care provider network. If the managed care provider has desig- 12 nated one or more pharmacies for filling prescriptions for a particular 13 drug or drugs, then such prescriptions may be filled, at the partic- 14 ipant's option, at any other pharmacy in the network, if the network 15 pharmacy chosen by the participant offers to accept a price that is 16 comparable to that of the pharmacy designated by the managed care 17 provider. For the purposes of this section, "mail order pharmacy" means 18 a pharmacy whose primary business is to receive prescriptions by mail, 19 telefax or through electronic submissions, and to dispense medication to 20 patients through the use of the United States mail or other common or 21 contract carrier services, and provides any consultation with patients 22 electronically rather than face to face.
